# Herbalism Research Sources - Comprehensive Guide

Research compiled for the folkprotocol project to identify authoritative, accessible sources for herbs/materia that balance traditional knowledge with modern evidence.

---

## 1. Clinical/Scientific Databases

### PubMed
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
- **Type**: Clinical research, peer-reviewed studies
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - 35+ million citations for biomedical research
  - Gold standard for medical literature
  - Excellent MeSH terms for herbal medicine searches
  - Cross-references with other databases
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Mostly abstracts, not always full text
  - Clinical/reductionist focus
- **Best for**: Finding RCTs, systematic reviews, safety data, mechanism studies
- **Search tips**: Use MeSH terms like "Complementary therapies", "Drugs Chinese herbal", "Herbal medicines", "Herb-drug interactions", "Pharmacognosy"

### PubMed Central (PMC)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
- **Type**: Full-text clinical research
- **Access**: Free (open access)
- **Strengths**:
  - Full-text versions of papers indexed in PubMed
  - Completely free access
  - NIH-maintained archive
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Not all PubMed papers are in PMC
- **Best for**: Reading complete research papers, methodology details

### Cochrane Library
- **URL**: https://www.cochranelibrary.com/
- **Type**: Systematic reviews, meta-analyses
- **Access**: Free (varies by country, some abstracts only)
- **Strengths**:
  - Gold standard for systematic reviews
  - High-quality evidence synthesis
  - 58+ reviews on Chinese herbal medicines alone
  - Covers herbs for specific conditions
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Limited to systematic review format
  - Conservative/skeptical bias toward herbs
- **Best for**: Evidence-based practice, understanding quality of evidence for specific herbs/conditions

## 2. Traditional Medicine Databases

### TCMBank
- **URL**: (Multiple TCM databases - see below)
- **Type**: Traditional Chinese Medicine
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Largest non-commercial TCM database
  - Herb-ingredient-target-disease mapping
  - Includes modern molecular data
- **Weaknesses**:
  - May require technical knowledge to navigate
- **Best for**: Understanding TCM patterns, formula composition, molecular mechanisms

### LTM-TCM
- **Type**: Traditional Chinese Medicine with modern integration
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Most comprehensive TCM database
  - 41,025 clinical treatment records
  - 213 ancient Chinese medical books
  - Integrates 14 authoritative TCM databases
- **Weaknesses**:
  - May be academically oriented
- **Best for**: Historical TCM usage, clinical treatment patterns

### TCMID 2.0
- **URL**: http://www.megabionet.org/tcmid/
- **Type**: TCM integrative database
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - 18,203 herbal ingredients
  - Target and disease mapping
  - Drug interaction data
- **Best for**: Ingredient research, mechanism analysis

### ETCM v2.0
- **URL**: http://www.tcmip.cn/ETCM2/front/#/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia of Traditional Chinese Medicine
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - 48,442 TCM formulas from ancient texts
  - 9,872 Chinese patent drugs
  - 2,079 Chinese medicinal materials
  - 38,298 ingredients
- **Best for**: Formula research, traditional preparation methods

### TCM Database@Taiwan
- **URL**: http://tcm.cmu.edu.tw/
- **Type**: TCM for drug discovery
- **Access**: Free download
- **Strengths**:
  - World's largest TCM database
  - Virtual screening capabilities
  - Small molecule component data
- **Best for**: In silico screening, molecular research

### YaTCM
- **URL**: http://cadd.pharmacy.nankai.edu.cn/yatcm/home
- **Type**: TCM with analysis tools
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Web-based toolkit
  - Built-in analysis tools
  - Comprehensive TCM information
- **Best for**: Interactive research, data analysis

### Ayurvedic Pharmacopoeia of India (API)
- **Type**: Official Ayurvedic monographs
- **Access**: Free (PDFs available through various institutions)
- **Strengths**:
  - Official government-sanctioned resource
  - Mono-monographs and compound formulations
  - Standardized quality parameters
  - Properties, therapeutic uses, dosing
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Can be technical/regulatory focused
- **Best for**: Authentic Ayurvedic preparations, traditional formulations, dosing guidelines

### Evolutionary Herbalism Materia Medica Monthly
- **URL**: https://www.evolutionaryherbalism.com/mmm-details/
- **Type**: Ayurveda + Western + Medical astrology + Alchemy
- **Access**: Paid subscription
- **Strengths**:
  - Massive digital library
  - Integrates multiple traditions
  - More depth than most sources
  - Quick reference for clinical cases
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Requires subscription
- **Best for**: Multi-tradition perspective, clinical practice

## 3. Regulatory/Professional Sources

### Commission E Monographs (American Botanical Council)
- **URL**: http://cms.herbalgram.org/commissione/index.html
- **Type**: German government-sanctioned monographs
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - 380 monographs on herbs and combinations
  - Approved uses, dosage, contraindications
  - Side effects and drug interactions
  - Professional and public-friendly
- **Weaknesses**:
  - From 1978-1994, somewhat dated
  - Conservative European perspective
- **Best for**: Safety data, approved uses, professional practice guidelines

### Commission E - Internet Archive
- **URL**: https://archive.org/details/completegermanco0000unse
- **Type**: Complete and expanded monographs
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Downloadable PDF versions
  - Both complete and expanded editions
- **Best for**: Offline reference, comprehensive reading

### European Medicines Agency (EMA) - HMPC Monographs
- **URL**: https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/human-regulatory-overview/herbal-medicinal-products/european-union-monographs-list-entries
- **Type**: EU herbal medicine monographs
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Current, regularly updated
  - Scientific opinion on safety and efficacy
  - Assessment reports included
  - Both traditional use and well-established use
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Regulatory focus may be conservative
- **Best for**: European herbs, current safety data, regulatory standards

### WHO Herbal Monographs
- **Type**: International monographs on selected medicinal plants
- **Access**: Generally free through WHO website
- **Strengths**:
  - International authority
  - Multiple volumes
  - Global perspective
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Limited number of herbs covered
- **Best for**: International standards, developing world herbs

## 4. Meta-Aggregators & Professional Databases

### Natural Medicines (NatMed Pro)
- **URL**: https://naturalmedicines.therapeuticresearch.com/
- **Type**: Professional evidence-based database
- **Access**: Paid subscription (often available through university/hospital libraries)
- **Strengths**:
  - Most authoritative professional resource
  - Scientifically accurate, regularly updated
  - Supplements, natural medicines, CAM therapies
  - Evidence grading system
  - Interaction checker
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Expensive subscription
  - May be conservative on traditional use
- **Best for**: Clinical practice, evidence-based decisions, safety

### NCCIH Herbs at a Glance
- **URL**: https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/herbsataglance
- **Type**: Government fact sheets
- **Access**: Free (website + eBook + mobile app)
- **Strengths**:
  - ~50 popular herbs
  - Science-based summaries
  - Safety and cautions
  - Free HerbList mobile app
  - Easy to read, consumer-friendly
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Limited number of herbs
  - Basic information only
- **Best for**: Quick safety checks, patient education, mobile reference

### Memorial Sloan Kettering - About Herbs
- **Type**: Evidence-based herb database
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Cancer-focused perspective (interactions with chemo)
  - Evidence-based
  - Major cancer center authority
  - Brand names included
  - Contraindications and side effects
  - Metabolic pathway interactions
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Cancer-treatment focus
- **Best for**: Herb-drug interactions, safety in cancer patients

### HerbMed
- **Type**: Research citation database
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Links to scientific data and research
  - Evidence-based
  - For professionals and public
- **Weaknesses**:
  - More of a research portal than monograph database
- **Best for**: Finding primary research, literature review

### ConsumerLab.com
- **Type**: Independent product testing
- **Access**: Paid subscription
- **Strengths**:
  - Tests actual products for quality
  - Identifies best brands
  - Tests for contaminants, potency
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Subscription required
  - Product-focused, not plant-focused
- **Best for**: Choosing high-quality commercial products

### USP Herbal Medicines Compendium
- **URL**: https://hmc.usp.org/
- **Type**: Standards for herbal ingredients
- **Access**: Free online resource
- **Strengths**:
  - U.S. Pharmacopeial Convention standards
  - Quality standards and testing
- **Best for**: Quality control, standardization

## 5. Hidden Gems & Specialized Sources

### Southwest School of Botanical Medicine (Michael Moore)
- **URL**: https://swsbm.org/ and https://www.swsbm.com/
- **Type**: Western herbalism, clinical practice
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - 30+ years of collected material by "godfather of American herbalism"
  - Extensive plant monographs
  - Photos, illustrations, maps
  - Historical Eclectic and Physiomedicalist materials
  - Teaching materials from SWSBM
  - Regional focus (Southwest, Mountain West, Pacific West)
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Website navigation can be challenging
  - Some content is dated (Moore died 2009)
- **Best for**: Southwest US plants, clinical Western herbalism, energetic approaches, Michael Moore's unique perspective

### Plants For A Future (PFAF)
- **URL**: https://pfaf.org/
- **Type**: Edible and useful plants database
- **Access**: Free (plus paid downloadable versions)
- **Strengths**:
  - 8,000+ plant profiles
  - Medicinal, edible, and other uses
  - Comprehensive search facilities
  - Cultivation details
  - Known hazards
  - Range and habitat info
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Variable depth across entries
  - Not clinically focused
- **Best for**: Permaculture perspective, edible-medicinal overlap, cultivation info, traditional uses

### Henriette's Herbal Homepage
- **URL**: https://www.henriettes-herb.com/
- **Type**: Classic herbal texts + plant database
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Online since 1995, one of oldest herb sites
  - **Classic Eclectic texts digitized**: King's American Dispensatory, Felter's Eclectic Materia Medica, Ellingwood's American Materia Medica
  - Dozens of texts from 1700s-early 1900s
  - Plant name database
  - Run by experienced herbalist (Henriette Kress, Finland)
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Website undergoing transition, some database issues
  - Historical texts may have outdated dosing/practices
- **Best for**: Historical perspective, Eclectic herbalism, classic monographs, plant identification

### King's American Dispensatory (Felter & Lloyd, 1898)
- **URL**: https://www.henriettes-herb.com/eclectic/kings/index.html
- **URL**: https://archive.org/details/kingsamericandis02kinguoft
- **Type**: Eclectic herbal text
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Comprehensive 19th century herbal
  - Eclectic medicine perspective
  - Detailed preparations and uses
  - Available on both Henriette's site and Internet Archive
- **Weaknesses**:
  - 1898 edition - historical dosing/safety standards
- **Best for**: Eclectic herbalism, historical preparations, depth on American herbs

### Northeast School of Botanical Medicine (7Song)
- **URL**: https://7song.com/
- **Type**: Clinical herbalism resources
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Generous free resource collection
  - Clinical presentations from Ithaca Free Clinic
  - Herbal first aid materials
  - Charts for anxiety, depression, sleep
  - 281-page handout binder (2024)
  - Videos on plants and first aid
  - Outlines from classes/presentations
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Northeast US/clinical focus
- **Best for**: First aid, clinical practice, free clinic model, handouts for patients

### University of Kansas - Prairie Ethnobotany Database
- **URL**: https://nativeplants.ku.edu/ethnobotany-database
- **Type**: Ethnobotanical database
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - 1,600+ species
  - Native American traditional knowledge
  - Regional focus (Plains/Prairie)
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Regional limitation
- **Best for**: Native American herbalism, prairie/plains plants, ethnobotanical research

### University Herbaria (various)
- **Type**: Specimen collections with ethnobotanical data
- **Access**: Varies, often underutilized
- **Strengths**:
  - Voucher specimens with traditional use notes
  - Often overlooked source of ethnobotanical data
  - Research shows 17% of medicinal species documented only on herbarium labels
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Not aggregated in one place
  - Requires knowing which herbaria to contact
- **Best for**: Regional ethnobotany, verification of plant identification

## 6. Safety & Interaction Checkers

### Drugs.com Drug Interaction Checker
- **URL**: https://www.drugs.com/drug_interactions.html
- **Type**: Drug-herb-food interaction checker
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Includes herbs, food, alcohol
  - Easy to use
  - Shows harmful combinations
- **Best for**: Quick interaction checks

### WebMD Drug Interaction Checker
- **URL**: https://www.webmd.com/interaction-checker/default.htm
- **Type**: Drug-herb-supplement-food interactions
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Consumer-friendly interface
  - Comprehensive coverage
- **Best for**: Patient education, quick checks

### Medscape Drug Interaction Checker
- **URL**: https://reference.medscape.com/drug-interactionchecker
- **Type**: Professional interaction checker
- **Access**: Free (registration may be required)
- **Strengths**:
  - Includes herbal supplements
  - Professional-grade
- **Best for**: Clinical practice

### Evaluate My Meds
- **URL**: https://evaluatemymeds.com/
- **Type**: Herb-medication interaction checker
- **Access**: Free for personal use
- **Strengths**:
  - Specifically designed for herb-drug interactions
  - Includes prescription, OTC, herbs, supplements, vitamins
- **Best for**: Comprehensive personal medication reviews

### NCCIH - Herb-Drug Interactions Resources
- **URL**: https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/providers/digest/herb-drug-interactions
- **Type**: Educational resource on interactions
- **Access**: Free
- **Strengths**:
  - Evidence-based overview
  - NIH authority
- **Best for**: Understanding interaction mechanisms, provider education

## 9. Books & Monograph Series (Not Free, But Essential)

### American Herbal Pharmacopoeia (AHP)
- **URL**: https://herbal-ahp.com/
- **Type**: Professional monographs
- **Access**: Paid ($1,478 for complete 45-monograph set)
- **Strengths**:
  - Highest quality herbal monographs available
  - Quality standards
  - Microscopy, HPTLC, chemistry
  - Therapeutic monographs
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Expensive
- **Best for**: Professional practice, quality control, serious study

### Matthew Wood's Works
- The Practice of Traditional Western Herbalism (2004)
- The Earthwise Herbal Vol I & II (Old World & New World)
- The Book of Herbal Wisdom (1998)
- **Strengths**:
  - Energetic approach to Western herbalism
  - Clinical experience since 1982
  - Integrates TCM, homeopathy, shamanic botany
  - Case histories
- **Best for**: Energetic/constitutional herbalism, clinical practice

## Research Strategy Recommendations

### For Folkprotocol Project:

**1. Start Broad (Free Sources):**
- PFAF for initial plant overview (uses, cultivation, hazards)
- Henriette's Herbal for historical Eclectic perspective
- Michael Moore's SWSBM for Western clinical approach

**2. Get Traditional Context:**
- ETCM/TCM databases for Chinese medicine perspective
- API for Ayurvedic perspective
- Look for convergent use across traditions

**3. Find Modern Evidence:**
- PubMed/PMC for clinical studies
- Cochrane for systematic reviews
- Focus on safety data, mechanisms, clinical trials

**4. Check Safety/Regulations:**
- Commission E for approved uses
- EMA HMPC for current EU standards
- Interaction checkers for contraindications
- NCCIH for basic safety overview

**5. Get Clinical Perspective:**
- 7Song's materials for first aid/clinical
- Matthew Wood's books for energetics
- AHP monographs if budget allows

**6. Verify & Cross-Reference:**
- Use multiple sources
- Check historical use + modern evidence
- Look for traditional convergence (multiple systems using similarly)
- Verify Latin names across sources

### Prioritized Source List (for initial research on each herb):

1. **PFAF** - Quick overview
2. **PubMed** - Safety and mechanism
3. **ETCM or TCM database** - Traditional patterns
4. **Commission E or EMA** - Professional standards
5. **Michael Moore's SWSBM** or **Henriette's Eclectic texts** - Western tradition
6. **7Song's handouts** - Practical clinical use
7. **Interaction checkers** - Safety verification
