## What The Evidence Says

Valerian is one of the most-studied herbal sleep aids, but the evidence is frustratingly mixed. Meta-analyses generally show:

- **Modest improvement in sleep quality** (subjective ratings)
- **Some reduction in time to fall asleep**
- **Highly variable between studies**

The variability may come from different preparations, doses, and study populations. This suggests valerian might work well for some people and not others - making it a good candidate for n=1 experimentation.

## Traditional Use

Valerian has been used in Europe for centuries. The consistency across traditions (Germanic, British, Mediterranean) for similar uses (sleep, nervousness, restlessness) is notable. When multiple unrelated traditions independently arrive at similar uses, it's worth paying attention.

## How To Try It

**Tincture** is probably the easiest:
- Start with 2ml about 30 minutes before bed
- Give it 2-3 weeks - some say effects build over time
- Track your sleep onset time and morning feeling

**What to look for:**
- Falling asleep faster
- Feeling calmer at bedtime
- Note any grogginess or vivid dreams

## The Smell

Valerian smells... distinctive. Like old socks or wet dog. This is normal. It's the isovaleric acid. Some tinctures mask it better than others. The smell is part of the active compounds, so don't be alarmed - this is what valerian is supposed to smell like.

If you can't tolerate the smell, capsules are a reasonable alternative, though some practitioners prefer tinctures for faster onset.
