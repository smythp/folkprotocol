## Overview

Sleep is one of the better-studied areas for herbal interventions. Several herbs have moderate-to-strong evidence for various aspects of sleep support. Unlike pharmaceutical sleep aids, many herbs work gently and don't typically cause morning grogginess or dependency.

## The Landscape

**Well-evidenced:**
- [Valerian](/materia/valerian) (most studied, mixed but positive results)
- Passionflower
- Magnolia bark

**Traditional with some support:**
- Chamomile
- Lemon balm
- Lavender

**Interesting but early:**
- Ashwagandha (for stress-related sleep issues)
- Reishi

## What We're Looking For

When evaluating sleep herbs, research typically measures:

- **Reduced time to fall asleep** (sleep onset latency)
- **Fewer night wakings**
- **Subjective sleep quality**
- **Next-day function** (no grogginess)

These are all things you can track in your own experiments.

## N=1 Considerations

Sleep is one of the more measurable areas for self-experimentation. You can track:

- **Time to fall asleep** (subjective estimate - note when you get in bed and when you think you fell asleep)
- **Wake times** (if you notice them - how many times, how long awake)
- **Morning energy/grogginess** (rate on a scale)
- **Sleep tracker data** (optional - take with grain of salt, but can show patterns)

### A/B Testing Protocol

A simple way to test a sleep herb:

1. **Baseline week**: Track sleep metrics without any intervention
2. **Trial week**: Take the herb consistently (same dose, same timing)
3. **Washout week**: Stop the herb, continue tracking
4. **Repeat**: Try another week on to confirm

Look for consistent patterns rather than single good/bad nights.

## Combining Herbs

Many traditional formulas combine multiple sleep herbs. Common pairings:

- **Valerian + Passionflower**: Complementary mechanisms
- **Valerian + Lemon balm**: For anxiety-related sleep issues
- **Chamomile + Lavender**: Gentle, suitable for ongoing use

Start with single herbs to understand your response before combining.

## When to Seek Professional Help

While herbs can support sleep, some sleep issues need professional attention:

- Suspected sleep apnea (snoring, gasping, daytime exhaustion)
- Severe chronic insomnia (>3 nights/week for >3 months)
- Sleep issues from underlying conditions (depression, anxiety disorders, pain)

Herbs work best for occasional sleep issues or as part of broader sleep hygiene improvements.
