## Overview

A tickly cough—that maddening dry irritation that won't quit—is one of the most common reasons people reach for remedies. Unlike a productive cough that brings up mucus, a tickly cough is dry, persistent, and often worst at night or when talking.

The frustrating reality: most tickly coughs are either post-viral (lingering 3-8 weeks after a cold or flu) or triggered by something else entirely—silent reflux, allergies, or simple irritation. Herbs can't cure the underlying cause, but they excel at what matters most when you're desperate for relief: soothing irritated throat tissues and breaking the cough cycle long enough to sleep, speak, or function.

This protocol focuses on **comfort and symptom management** using demulcent herbs (plants that coat and soothe), combined with practical strategies that address the most common patterns. Think of this as a toolkit for the 2am coughing fit, the presentation that triggers a coughing jag, or the weeks-long post-viral cough that's driving you (and your partner) to exhaustion.

## The Landscape

**Strongest evidence for tickly cough:**
- **Honey** - Outperforms conventional OTC cough medicines for nighttime cough in multiple trials
- **Marshmallow root** (*Althaea officinalis*) - Approved by German Commission E and EMA for throat irritation, works "very quickly" for dry coughs
- **Position/elevation** - Elevating the head 6-8 inches is the single most effective non-drug intervention for night cough

**Traditional demulcents with support:**
- **Licorice root** (*Glycyrrhiza glabra*) - Fast-acting anti-inflammatory and cough suppressant (short-term use only due to blood pressure concerns)
- **Slippery elm** (*Ulmus rubra*) - Traditionally used but **avoid due to sustainability concerns** (Dutch Elm Disease has decimated wild populations)

Note: Detailed monographs for marshmallow, licorice, and honey forthcoming in the materia section.

**Environmental strategies:**
- **Humidification** - Cool-mist helps soothe irritated airways
- **Steam** - Mixed evidence but many report subjective relief

**When herbs help vs. when they don't:**
Demulcents work by coating irritated tissues (temporary, 2-4 hours), reducing inflammation locally, and soothing the cough reflex. They do NOT cure GERD, replace allergy management, treat infections, or fix medication side effects. Best use: supportive care during diagnosis, symptom relief while the body heals, or management of uncomplicated post-viral cough.

## What We're Looking For

When evaluating whether your interventions are working, track these markers:

**Frequency:**
- Count coughs in 1 hour (morning + evening) - 55% reduction = "much improved", 30% = "minimally improved"
- Sleep interruptions per night (can you sleep through the night vs. waking 5x?)

**Quality of life:**
- Can you complete sentences/presentations without interruption?
- Can you laugh without triggering a cough fit?
- Cough severity on 0-10 scale (1.3-point reduction = meaningful improvement)

**Pattern recognition:**
- Time of day cough is worst
- Activities that trigger (talking, lying down, eating, laughing)
- Foods eaten 0-3 hours before worse episodes
- Environmental changes (indoors/outdoors, weather, pets)

Track for at least 3 days before starting interventions to establish your baseline, then track for 7 days during treatment to see if there's real improvement vs. placebo or natural resolution.

## N=1 Considerations

### Quick Start: First 24 Hours

You or your partner has that annoying tickle starting. Do this NOW:

1. **Start marshmallow cold infusion tonight** - 2-4 tbsp dried root in quart jar, fill with cold water, leave overnight (4-12 hours)
2. **Grab honey** - Take 1-2 tsp immediately, keep it handy for frequent dosing
3. **Elevate the bed head** - Add wedge pillow or raise bed 6-8 inches (not just extra pillows—these can kink your neck)
4. **Turn on humidifier** - Cool-mist in bedroom
5. **Track the pattern** - Note: time of day, after eating what, lying down or upright?

**Why this order?** These give the fastest relief with strongest evidence, while you figure out what type of cough this is.

### Decision Tree: What Kind of Tickly Cough Is This?

**Post-Viral (After Cold/Flu)**
- **Pattern:** Started with cold/flu, now just the cough remains, dry and tickly, no colored mucus
- **Timeline:** 3-8 weeks is normal, can last up to 2 months
- **Strategy:** Patience + symptom management. Marshmallow root (fast-acting demulcent), honey (evidence beats OTC cough meds), humidification, elevated sleep position
- **When to worry:** Not improving after 8 weeks, or worsening

**GERD-Related (Silent Reflux)**
- **Pattern:** Dry, hacking (no mucus), worse at night or after eating, worse lying flat, may have ZERO heartburn
- **Hidden triggers:** High-fat foods, spicy/acidic foods (citrus, tomatoes), coffee, alcohol, late-night eating
- **Strategy:** Elevate bed head 6-8 inches (CRITICAL), eat dinner 2-3 hours before bed, marshmallow for symptom relief, food diary to identify triggers
- **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks to see dietary improvement

**Allergy-Related**
- **Pattern:** Seasonal or environmental (dust, pets, mold), worse in certain locations/times
- **Track:** Where (indoors/outdoors?), when (time of year, after pets?), pattern (weather changes?)
- **Strategy:** Identify and avoid triggers, clean bedsheets regularly, pets out of bedroom, air purifier, shower before bed (removes pollen)

**Talking/Irritation Triggered**
- **Pattern:** Worse when speaking a lot (presentations, teaching), after laughing
- **Immediate relief:** Small sips of water, hard swallow (deliberate, forceful), non-medicated lozenge, relaxed-throat breathing
- **Long-term:** Consider behavioral cough suppression therapy (2-4 sessions with speech therapist can give 90% control)

### The Marshmallow Protocol

**Why marshmallow wins:**
- Works "very quickly" for dry coughs
- 20% mucilage content forms protective coating over irritated throat tissues
- Sustainable and ethical (widely cultivated)
- Pleasant taste (mild, slightly sweet)
- No safety concerns (use freely)

**Cold Infusion (BEST METHOD):**

Why cold water? Extracts pure mucilaginous polysaccharides (the healing part). Hot water extracts starches and breaks down delicate polysaccharides.

Recipe:
1. Fill jar 1/4 way with dried marshmallow root (or 2-4 tbsp per quart)
2. Add cold or room-temperature water to fill
3. Steep 4-12 hours (overnight is ideal)
4. Strain and drink

What to expect: Slippery, slightly thick, mildly sweet liquid. The sliminess is therapeutic—it's what soothes your throat.

**Dosing:** 4-8 oz every 2-3 hours while awake during acute cough

**Storage:** Keep in fridge, make fresh batch each night

**Marshmallow-Honey Syrup (Concentrated for Nighttime):**

Recipe:
1. Simmer 1/2 oz dried marshmallow root in 1 quart water
2. Reduce liquid by half (concentrated)
3. Strain out herb
4. Add equal volume raw honey (1:1 ratio)
5. Stir gently (don't overheat honey)
6. Store in fridge up to 1 month

**Dosing:** 1-2 tsp every 2-3 hours, especially before bed

Why this works: Combines marshmallow's coating with honey's antimicrobial + demulcent properties. Evidence shows honey outperforms OTC cough medicines for nighttime cough.

### The Night Protocol: For Sleep-Disrupting Cough

The problem: Tickly cough is WORST at night. Lying flat triggers it, disrupts sleep, makes everything worse.

**30-60 Minutes Before Bed:**
1. Steam shower (loosens secretions, moistens airways)
2. 2 tsp marshmallow-honey syrup OR 8-12 oz cold infusion
3. Elevate bed head 6-8 inches
4. Turn on humidifier (cool-mist in bedroom)

**At Bedside (For Middle-of-Night Wake-Ups):**
- Jar of marshmallow cold infusion or bottle of syrup
- Glass of water
- Lozenges (non-medicated or menthol)

**Environmental:**
- Cool (not cold) air
- Side sleeping instead of back
- Consider extra elevation if GERD suspected

**Why this works:** Demulcent coating + gravity (elevation prevents mucus pooling/reflux) + moisture (humidifier) = breaks the cough cycle

### About Licorice Root

**Strengths:**
- Fast-acting anti-inflammatory
- Anti-tussive (cough suppressant) properties
- Very sweet (50x sweeter than sugar)—excellent in formulas to sweeten bitter herbs

**CRITICAL SAFETY ISSUE:**
⚠️ As little as 100mg/day glycyrrhizic acid increases blood pressure significantly
⚠️ Short-term use ONLY (4-6 weeks max)
⚠️ NOT for people with high blood pressure, heart disease, kidney disease, pregnancy

**Safe dosing:** 0.5-1g powdered root 1-3x daily—count ALL sources (teas, lozenges, candy)

**Bottom line:** Use as supporting herb in small amounts (1/4 of marshmallow amount), NOT solo remedy

### Expected Improvement Timelines

| Intervention | When to Expect Relief |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Honey, position change | Immediate to 1-2 days |
| Marshmallow infusion | Within hours (coating effect) |
| Humidification | 1-3 days |
| GERD dietary changes | 2-4 weeks |
| Post-viral natural resolution | Week-over-week improvement, 3-8 weeks total |
| Behavioral cough suppression therapy | Noticeable in 2-4 sessions |

### Progressive Protocol: What to Try When

**Week 1: First-Line Interventions**
- Marshmallow cold infusion (3-6x daily)
- Honey (1-2 tsp every 2-3 hrs)
- Elevate sleep position
- Humidifier at night
- Track pattern

If improving: Continue until resolved
If not improving: Move to Week 2

**Week 2-3: Add Pattern-Specific Interventions**

If GERD pattern suspected:
- Strict meal timing (3 hours before bed)
- Food diary (identify triggers)
- Avoid high-fat, spicy, acidic foods

If allergy pattern suspected:
- Clean bedroom thoroughly
- Change bedsheets 2x/week
- Pets out of bedroom
- Shower before bed

If post-viral pattern (no other clear trigger):
- Continue symptom management
- Be patient (can take 8 weeks)
- Watch for improvement week-over-week

**Week 4-6: Escalate If Needed**

Still not improving?
- See doctor (rule out asthma, chronic bronchitis, medication side effects)
- Re-evaluate herb dosing (are you dosing frequently enough?)
- Consider adding small amount of licorice to marshmallow (short-term only)

**Week 8+: Medical Evaluation Required** (see below)

## Travel-Friendly Preparations

Can't make fresh cold infusions while traveling? Try:

**1. Marshmallow-Honey Syrup (BEST)**
- Pre-make before trip
- Transfer to 2-4 oz leak-proof bottle (TSA compliant)
- Lasts 1 month refrigerated, 1-2 weeks unrefrigerated
- Take 1 tsp as needed, no preparation required

**2. Marshmallow Root Powder in Capsules**
- Pre-fill size 00 capsules with marshmallow powder
- Take 2-4 capsules with water, 3-6x daily
- Less effective than liquid, but very portable

**3. Small Jar of Raw Honey**
- 2-4 oz jar (TSA compliant if <3.4 oz)
- Take by spoonful or add to any tea/water

## When to Seek Professional Help

**Seek medical care if:**

**Timeline:**
- Cough lasting >8 weeks (chronic cough threshold)
- Not improving after 2 months despite interventions
- Worsening instead of improving

**Symptoms:**
- Fever >101°F or persistent low-grade fever
- Coughing up colored mucus (green, yellow, brown, blood)
- Chest pain or tightness
- Shortness of breath or wheezing
- Difficulty breathing
- Significant unintentional weight loss
- Night sweats

**Impact:**
- Severely disrupting sleep (can't sleep more than 1-2 hours at a time)
- Preventing normal activities (can't work, can't speak without coughing fit)
- Causing rib pain, vomiting, or passing out from coughing

**Medical history concerns:**
- You have asthma, COPD, heart disease, or immunocompromised status
- You're on ACE inhibitor medication (common cough side effect)

**Top 3 causes of chronic cough (90%):**
1. Postnasal drip / Upper airway cough syndrome
2. Asthma (may have NO wheezing—cough-variant asthma)
3. GERD (may have NO heartburn—silent reflux)

**Treatments doctor may offer:**
- Prescription PPI for GERD
- Inhaled corticosteroids for asthma
- Antihistamines for postnasal drip
- Change medication if ACE inhibitor
- Speech therapy referral for behavioral cough suppression (surprisingly effective—90% control achievable)

## Behavioral Cough Suppression: The Hidden Game-Changer

**What is it?** Speech therapy techniques to retrain the cough reflex

**Evidence:**
- 2014 systematic review: Significant reduction in cough sensitivity
- Case study: Patient with 3-year chronic cough achieved 90% suppression after 5 weeks
- 2-4 sessions typically needed

**Techniques:**
1. **Distraction** - Sipping water, chewing gum when urge starts
2. **Effortful swallow** - Deliberate hard swallow instead of coughing
3. **Relaxed throat breathing** - Keep throat, jaw, tongue relaxed (prevents vocal cord squeeze)
4. **Cough-control breathing** - Specific breathing pattern to interrupt reflex

**Who benefits:** People with chronic cough (>8 weeks), especially if cough is partly habitual/reflex-driven

**How to access:** Ask doctor for referral to speech-language pathologist (SLP) with cough suppression experience

This is NOT "just stop coughing." It's retraining a hypersensitive reflex through specific techniques. Surprisingly effective when other treatments fail.

## For Partners: Support Tips

**DO:**
- Keep honey, cold infusion, lozenges stocked and visible
- Remind about elevation/humidifier before bed
- Help track patterns (outside observer notices things sufferer doesn't)
- Be patient—chronic cough is exhausting and frustrating
- Encourage doctor visit if >8 weeks or worsening

**DON'T:**
- Say "just stop coughing" (it's involuntary, unhelpful)
- Suggest "you're not drinking enough water" repeatedly (unless actually dehydrated)
- Insist on steam if it doesn't help them (individual variation is real)
- Add stress about "trying everything"—systematic is better than shotgun

**REALITY CHECK:** Sometimes it just takes time (post-viral can be 8 weeks). Support ≠ fixing.

## Final Thoughts: Realistic Expectations

**Herbs work, but they're not magic:**
- Demulcents soothe and coat (temporary, 2-4 hours)
- Honey reduces cough frequency (evidence-based)
- They provide comfort and symptom relief
- They do NOT cure underlying disease

**Time is often the real healer:**
- Post-viral cough can take 8 weeks (this is NORMAL)
- GERD treatment takes 2-4 weeks minimum
- Allergies require identifying and avoiding triggers

**Individual variation is real:**
- What works for one person may not work for another
- Track, test, learn YOUR pattern and YOUR effective remedies
- Don't compare your timeline to someone else's

**When to escalate:**
- Don't suffer needlessly for months
- If >8 weeks or significantly impacting life, see doctor
- Behavioral therapy is surprisingly effective (ask for referral)
- There ARE medical treatments that work when herbs don't

**Bottom line:** Marshmallow + honey + elevation + humidification will help most acute tickly coughs. Track the pattern, intervene early, be patient, and escalate to medical care if not resolving.
