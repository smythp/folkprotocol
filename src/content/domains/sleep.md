## Overview

Sleep is one of the better-studied areas for herbal interventions. Several herbs have moderate-to-strong evidence for various aspects of sleep support.

## The Landscape

**Well-evidenced:**
- Valerian (most studied, mixed results)
- Passionflower
- Magnolia bark

**Traditional with some support:**
- Chamomile
- Lemon balm
- Lavender

**Interesting but early:**
- Ashwagandha (for stress-related sleep issues)
- Reishi

## What We're Looking For

- Reduced time to fall asleep (sleep onset latency)
- Fewer night wakings
- Subjective sleep quality
- Next-day function

## N=1 Considerations

Sleep is measurable. You can track:
- Time to fall asleep (subjective estimate)
- Wake times (if you notice them)
- Morning energy/grogginess
- Optional: sleep tracker data (take with grain of salt)

A/B testing is possible: week on, week off, compare.
