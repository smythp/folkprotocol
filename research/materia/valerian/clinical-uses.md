# Valerian - Clinical Uses and Dosing

## Primary Clinical Indications

### Insomnia and Sleep Disorders

**Evidence Level**: Moderate for subjective improvement, limited for objective parameters

**Meta-Analysis Findings**:
- **Bent et al. 2006** (16 studies, n=1,093): RR 1.8 (95% CI: 1.2-2.9) for improved sleep
- **Fernández-San-Martín et al. 2010** (18 RCTs): RR 1.37 for subjective quality improvement; no significant objective improvement (sleep latency MD 0.70 min, 95% CI: -3.44 to 4.83)
- **Shinjyo et al. 2020** (60 studies, n=6,894): Meta-analysis of 10 studies (n=1,065) showed improvements in subjective sleep quality; more reliable effects from whole root/rhizome preparations

**Specific Clinical Trial Dosing**:

1. **Shekhar et al. 2024** - Most Recent RCT
   - **Population**: Adults with sleep complaints (n=80, 72 completed)
   - **Dose**: Standardized Valeriana officinalis extract (specific dose not disclosed)
   - **Duration**: 8 weeks
   - **Frequency**: Daily
   - **Outcomes**:
     - PSQI Total Score decreased significantly (p<0.05) vs placebo on days 14, 28, 56
     - Reduced sleep latency
     - Improved sleep efficiency
     - Decreased anxiety scores
     - Lower daytime drowsiness
     - Enhanced feeling of refreshment upon waking
   - **Safety**: No adverse events reported

2. **Zare Elmi et al. 2021** - Post-Surgical Sleep
   - **Population**: Post-CABG surgery patients (n=72)
   - **Dose**: 530 mg valerian extract capsules
   - **Duration**: 30 nights
   - **Frequency**: Daily
   - **Outcomes**:
     - Significant improvements in total sleep quality
     - Reduced sleep latency
     - Increased sleep duration
     - Improved sleep efficiency
     - Reduced daytime dysfunction
   - **Conclusion**: Safe and effective for post-operative sleep recovery

### Anxiety Disorders

**Evidence Level**: Preliminary/supportive

**Meta-Analysis Findings**:
- **Shinjyo et al. 2020**: Meta-analysis of 8 studies (n=535) for anxiety reduction

**Specific Clinical Trial**:

**Roh et al. 2019** - Neurophysiological Study
- **Population**: Nonclinical volunteers with psychological stress (n=64)
- **Dose**: 100 mg valerian root extract
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Frequency**: Three times daily (TID)
- **Measurement**: EEG functional brain connectivity
- **Outcomes**:
  - Significantly greater increases in frontal alpha coherence (4 electrode pairs) vs placebo
  - Significantly greater decreases in theta coherence (4 electrode pairs)
  - Alpha coherence changes correlated with anxiety reduction
  - Distinctive neurophysiological changes measurable via EEG
- **Significance**: Provides objective neurophysiological evidence of anxiolytic effects

## Dosing Guidelines

### Traditional/Regulatory Dosing

**From German Commission E Monograph**:
- **Tincture (1:5, 45% ethanol)**: 1-3 mL single dose
- **Fluid extract**: 2-3 mL single dose
- **Dried root/rhizome**: 2-3 g multiple times daily or single dose before bedtime
- **Aqueous extract**: 400-900 mg

### Clinical Trial Dosing Range

- **Low dose**: 100 mg TID (300 mg/day total) - Roh 2019
- **Medium dose**: 530 mg once daily - Zare Elmi 2021
- **High dose**: 600 mg single dose - Anderson 2005 (PK study)
- **Standardized extract**: Variable (manufacturer-specific) - Shekhar 2024

### Timing Recommendations

**Based on Pharmacokinetics** (Anderson et al. 2005):
- **Tmax**: 1-2 hours in 5 of 6 subjects
- **Recommendation**: Take 30 minutes to 2 hours before bedtime for sleep
- **Rationale**: Peak valerenic acid levels align with sleep onset window

### Duration of Treatment

**Clinical Trials**:
- **Short-term**: 4 weeks (Roh 2019) - effective for anxiety/stress
- **Medium-term**: 8 weeks (Shekhar 2024) - progressive improvement in sleep quality (significant effects by day 14)
- **Extended**: 30 days post-surgery (Zare Elmi 2021) - sustained benefit

**Research Gaps**: Long-term safety studies (>6 months) lacking

## Factors Affecting Clinical Outcomes

### Extract Quality and Standardization

**Key Issues** (Shinjyo et al. 2020):
- Inconsistent outcomes possibly due to variable quality of herbal extracts
- More reliable effects from whole root/rhizome preparations vs isolated extracts
- Variable valepotriate content by preparation method and stability

**Quality Recommendations**:
- Use standardized preparations from reputable manufacturers
- Follow pharmacopoeial quality standards (USP, EP, BP)
- Consider whole root preparations for more consistent effects
- European Pharmacopoeia method preferred for valerenic acid analysis

### Pharmacokinetic Variability

**Anderson et al. 2010** - Key Finding:
- Large inter- and intra-subject variability in all PK parameters
- Body weight inversely correlated with concentration and half-life
- **Clinical Implication**: Individual differences may explain variable clinical responses

**Valerenic Acid PK Parameters**:
- **Tmax**: 1-2 hours
- **Cmax**: 0.9-2.3 ng/mL (considerable inter-subject variability)
- **T½**: 1.1 ± 0.6 hours
- **AUC**: 4.80 ± 2.96 µg/mL·h
- **No accumulation** with repeated dosing

## Clinical Context Summary

### When to Consider Valerian

**Appropriate Uses**:
- Mild to moderate insomnia (subjective sleep quality improvement)
- Sleep disorders secondary to stress/anxiety
- Post-operative sleep disturbances
- Mild anxiety with psychological stress
- Preference for herbal over synthetic sleep aids

**Therapeutic Expectations**:
- Subjective improvement in sleep quality more likely than objective polysomnographic changes
- Effects may be gradual; allow 2-4 weeks for full benefit
- Individual response variable (consider PK variability)

### Clinical Study Quality Notes

**Methodological Concerns** (Bent et al. 2006):
- Most studies had methodological weaknesses
- High variability in preparations and dosing
- Evidence of publication bias

**High-Quality Evidence**: Fernández-San-Martín et al. 2010 identified only 8 high-quality studies (Jadad score 5) out of 18 included trials

### Safety Profile in Clinical Trials

- **Age Range Studied**: 7-80 years (Shinjyo et al. 2020)
- **Severe Adverse Events**: None reported in systematic reviews
- **Short-term Safety**: Well-established
- **Long-term Safety**: Data lacking (>6 months)
