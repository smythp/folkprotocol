# Valerian - Preparations and Dosing

## Overview of Preparation Types

Valerian is available in multiple preparation forms, each with distinct characteristics, dosing, and regulatory standards. This document provides detailed preparation and dosing information extracted from clinical trials, regulatory monographs, and pharmacopoeial standards.

## Tinctures

### Standardized Tincture (Commission E, European Pharmacopoeia)

**Ratio**: 1:5 (drug:menstruum)
**Menstruum**: 45% ethanol
**Dose**: 1-3 mL single dose
**Timing**: Typically before bedtime for sleep; multiple times daily for anxiety

**Regulatory Status**:
- European Pharmacopoeia monograph
- EMA European Union herbal monograph
- German Commission E monograph

**Preparation Details**:
- 1 part dried valerian root/rhizome
- 5 parts 45% ethanol (by volume)
- Maceration or percolation method (specific details in pharmacopoeial monographs)

**Clinical Context**:
- Traditional preparation with long history of use
- Alcohol content consideration for specific populations (pregnancy, children, recovering alcoholics)

### Alcohol-Free Alternatives

**Note**: Glycerite or other non-alcoholic tinctures not specifically detailed in clinical/regulatory bibliography. For alcohol-free preparations, consider:
- Aqueous extracts (see below)
- Standardized dry extracts in capsules
- Glycerin-based tinctures (consult manufacturer specifications)

## Fluid Extracts

**Dose**: 2-3 mL single dose (Commission E)
**Concentration**: More concentrated than tinctures (typically 1:1 or 1:2 drug:extract ratio)
**Timing**: Before bedtime for sleep

**Difference from Tincture**:
- Higher concentration of active constituents
- Smaller volume for equivalent dose
- May have different extraction parameters (solvent strength, time, temperature)

**Note**: Specific extraction parameters not detailed in available bibliography; consult pharmacopoeial monographs for manufacturing standards.

## Aqueous Extracts

### Dosing Range
**Standard Dose**: 400-900 mg
**Frequency**: Variable (typically once daily before bedtime or divided doses for anxiety)

### Safety Profile (Bao et al. 2024)
Aqueous extract extensively studied for safety:
- **Genotoxicity**: None detected
- **Acute Toxicity**: No deaths or behavioral abnormalities at 96 g/kg (mice)
- **Subchronic Toxicity**: NOAEL ≥14 g/kg in 90-day rat study
- **Teratogenicity**: No adverse effects on embryonic formation or fetal development
- **Conclusion**: Excellent safety profile

### Preparation Methods
**Note**: Specific aqueous extraction parameters (temperature, time, solid:liquid ratio) not detailed in clinical bibliography. Refer to:
- Pharmacopoeial monographs (USP, EP, BP)
- WHO Monographs on Selected Medicinal Plants (1999)
- Manufacturer specifications for standardized products

## Standardized Dry Extracts (Capsules/Tablets)

### Clinical Trial Dosing

#### Shekhar et al. 2024 - Most Recent RCT
- **Preparation**: Standardized Valeriana officinalis extract
- **Dose**: Not specifically disclosed in trial publication
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Frequency**: Daily
- **Outcomes**: Significant improvement in PSQI scores by day 14
- **Safety**: No adverse events
- **Note**: Represents current standard for clinical research

#### Zare Elmi et al. 2021 - Post-CABG Trial
- **Preparation**: Valerian extract capsules
- **Dose**: 530 mg
- **Duration**: 30 nights
- **Frequency**: Once daily
- **Population**: Post-surgical patients
- **Outcomes**: Significant improvements in sleep quality, latency, duration, efficiency
- **Safety**: Safe in vulnerable post-operative population

#### Roh et al. 2019 - Anxiety/EEG Study
- **Preparation**: Valerian root extract (VRE)
- **Dose**: 100 mg
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Frequency**: Three times daily (TID) = 300 mg/day total
- **Population**: Nonclinical volunteers with psychological stress
- **Outcomes**: Significant EEG changes (alpha/theta coherence), anxiety reduction
- **Note**: Lower dose with multiple daily administration

#### Anderson et al. 2005 - Pharmacokinetic Study
- **Preparation**: Valerian capsules
- **Dose**: 600 mg single dose
- **Purpose**: PK parameter determination
- **Key Finding**: Tmax 1-2 hours, supporting 30 min-2 h pre-bedtime administration

### Dosing Range Summary

**Low Dose**: 100 mg TID (300 mg/day) - for anxiety/stress
**Medium Dose**: 530 mg once daily - for sleep
**High Dose**: 600 mg once daily - upper end of typical range

**Timing Recommendations**:
- **For sleep**: 30 minutes to 2 hours before bedtime (based on PK data)
- **For anxiety**: Divided doses throughout day (e.g., TID)

### Standardization Considerations

**Marker Compound**: Valerenic acid (primary standardization target)

**Quality Issues** (Shinjyo et al. 2020):
- Variable extract quality affects clinical outcomes
- Standardization across products inconsistent

**Recommendations**:
- Choose products standardized to valerenic acid content
- Prefer manufacturers following pharmacopoeial standards (USP, EP, BP)
- Look for products with certificates of analysis

## Dried Root/Rhizome (Whole Herb)

### Traditional Dosing
**Dose**: 2-3 g dried root/rhizome
**Frequency**: Multiple times daily or single dose before bedtime
**Preparation**: Typically as tea/infusion or powdered in capsules

### Tea Preparation

**Method** (inferred from traditional dose):
- 2-3 g (approximately 1-1.5 teaspoons) dried, cut valerian root
- 150-250 mL (1 cup) hot water
- Steep 10-15 minutes (covered to preserve volatile compounds)
- Strain and drink

**Timing**: 30 minutes to 2 hours before bedtime for sleep

**Note**: Specific tea preparation parameters (water temperature, steep time, covering) not explicitly detailed in clinical bibliography. Consult traditional herbal texts or Commission E monograph expanded version for traditional preparation methods.

### Advantages of Whole Root Preparations (Shinjyo et al. 2020)

**Key Finding**: "More reliable effects from whole root/rhizome preparations"

**Rationale**:
- Inconsistent outcomes possibly due to variable quality of isolated extracts
- Whole herb contains full spectrum of constituents
- Natural buffering/synergy among compounds

**Clinical Implication**: Consider whole root tea or powdered root capsules for potentially more consistent effects vs. isolated extracts

## Duration of Treatment

### Evidence-Based Timelines

**Short-Term** (4 weeks):
- Roh et al. 2019: Significant anxiety/EEG changes
- Minimum duration to assess benefit for anxiety

**Medium-Term** (8 weeks):
- Shekhar et al. 2024: Progressive improvement with significance by day 14
- Recommended trial period for sleep disorders

**Extended** (30 days):
- Zare Elmi et al. 2021: Sustained benefit in post-operative patients
- Appropriate for specific clinical contexts (post-surgery recovery)

**Long-Term** (>6 months):
- **Research Gap**: Safety and efficacy data lacking
- **Recommendation**: Periodically reassess need for continued use

### Clinical Dosing Strategy

**Initial Trial**:
1. Start with standardized preparation for consistency
2. Use evidence-based dose (e.g., 400-600 mg for sleep, 100 mg TID for anxiety)
3. Allow 2-4 weeks for full effect (some benefit may appear earlier)

**Timing Optimization**:
- For sleep: 30 min-2 hours before bedtime (based on valerenic acid Tmax)
- For anxiety: Divided doses (e.g., TID) or single morning/afternoon dose

**Duration**:
- Assess response at 4 weeks
- Continue if beneficial up to 8 weeks for sleep trials
- If effective and well-tolerated, may continue with periodic reassessment
- Insufficient data for long-term (>6 months) continuous use

## Special Populations

### Pediatric Dosing
- **Age Range in Studies**: Children as young as 7 years included (Shinjyo et al. 2020)
- **Specific Pediatric Doses**: Not systematically reported in available bibliography
- **Recommendation**: Reduce adult dose proportionally by body weight; consult regulatory monographs for specific guidance

### Geriatric Dosing
- **PK Consideration** (Anderson et al. 2010): Body weight inversely correlated with concentration and half-life
- **Implication**: Adjust dose based on body weight, not age per se
- **Safety**: Well-tolerated in older adults (studied in older women)

### Post-Surgical Patients
- **Evidence**: Zare Elmi et al. 2021 (post-CABG)
- **Dose**: 530 mg once daily for 30 nights
- **Safety**: No concerns in this vulnerable population

## Pharmacopoeial Standards Reference

Products meeting the following standards ensure minimum quality:

- **United States Pharmacopeia (USP)**: Valerian, Powdered Valerian, Powdered Valerian Extract, Valerian Tablets
- **European Pharmacopoeia (Ph. Eur.)**: Valerian Root, Valerian Dry Hydroalcoholic Extract, Valerian Tincture
- **British Pharmacopoeia (BP)**: Valerian

**Recommendation**: Look for products stating compliance with USP, EP, or BP on label.

## Practical Preparation Selection Guide

### For Sleep (Primary Indication)

**First-Line**:
- Standardized dry extract capsules: 400-600 mg once daily, 30 min-2 h before bedtime
- Duration: 4-8 weeks trial

**Alternative**:
- Tincture (1:5, 45% EtOH): 1-3 mL before bedtime
- Dried root tea: 2-3 g steeped 10-15 min, before bedtime

**Rationale**: Standardized preparations have strongest clinical trial evidence (Shekhar 2024); whole root preparations may offer more consistent effects (Shinjyo 2020)

### For Anxiety/Stress

**First-Line**:
- Standardized dry extract: 100 mg TID (300 mg/day total)
- Duration: 4 weeks minimum

**Alternative**:
- Tincture: 1-3 mL up to three times daily
- Aqueous extract: 400-900 mg divided into 2-3 doses

**Rationale**: Roh et al. 2019 demonstrated objective EEG changes with TID dosing

### For Alcohol-Sensitive Populations

**Recommended**:
- Aqueous extracts: 400-900 mg
- Standardized dry extract capsules: 400-600 mg
- Avoid tinctures and fluid extracts (alcohol content)

## Combination Products

**Note**: Many commercial products combine valerian with other herbs (e.g., hops, passionflower, lemon balm, chamomile).

**Considerations**:
- Combination products not systematically studied in clinical trials reviewed
- Difficult to attribute effects to valerian specifically
- Hepatotoxicity cases often involved herbal blends (valerian with skullcap, black cohosh)

**Recommendation**: Single-herb products preferred for initial trial to assess individual response and tolerance

## Storage and Stability

**Valepotriate Considerations**:
- Valepotriate content varies by preparation method and stability (Yao et al. 2007)
- Storage conditions may affect potency over time

**General Recommendations**:
- Store in cool, dry place away from light
- Check expiration dates
- Follow manufacturer storage instructions

**Note**: Specific stability data not systematically reported in clinical bibliography; consult pharmacopoeial monographs for detailed stability information

## Research Gaps

1. **Tea Preparation Parameters**: Optimal water temperature, steep time, effects of covering/not covering
2. **Comparative Effectiveness**: Head-to-head trials of different preparation types
3. **Standardization Targets**: Beyond valerenic acid - which compounds matter most?
4. **Combination Products**: Systematic study of valerian + hops, passionflower, etc.
5. **Long-Term Use**: Safety and efficacy beyond 8 weeks

## Summary: Preparation Selection Strategy

1. **Choose based on evidence**: Standardized dry extracts have most clinical trial support
2. **Consider whole root for consistency**: Whole root/rhizome preparations may be more reliable (Shinjyo 2020)
3. **Match dose to indication**: 400-600 mg QD for sleep; 100 mg TID for anxiety
4. **Time appropriately**: 30 min-2 h before bedtime for sleep based on PK
5. **Ensure quality**: Look for pharmacopoeial compliance (USP, EP, BP)
6. **Allow adequate trial**: Minimum 4 weeks, ideally 8 weeks for sleep
7. **Monitor for rare adverse effects**: Hepatotoxicity rare but documented
