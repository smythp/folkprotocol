## Quick Reference Summary

### Evidence-Based Dosing

**For Insomnia**:
- **Dose**: 400-600 mg standardized extract OR 2-3 g dried root
- **Timing**: 30 minutes to 2 hours before bedtime
- **Duration**: 4-8 weeks trial
- **Evidence**: RR 1.8 for improved sleep (Bent 2006)

**For Anxiety/Stress**:
- **Dose**: 100 mg TID (300 mg/day) OR 1-3 mL tincture TID
- **Duration**: 4 weeks minimum
- **Evidence**: Objective EEG changes, anxiety reduction (Roh 2019)

### Safety Highlights

✅ **Well-Established**:
- No severe adverse events in large systematic review (n=6,894)
- Excellent toxicology profile (NOAEL ≥14 g/kg in rats)
- No reproductive/developmental toxicity at high doses
- No genotoxicity

⚠️ **Caution**:
- Rare hepatotoxicity (likelihood score C - probable rare cause)
- Long-term safety data lacking (>6 months)
- Pregnancy/lactation: insufficient human data
- Pre-existing liver disease: monitor liver function

### Quality Recommendations

1. **Choose standardized preparations** meeting pharmacopoeial standards (USP, EP, BP)
2. **Consider whole root preparations** for potentially more consistent effects
3. **Look for valerenic acid standardization** on product labels
4. **Prefer reputable manufacturers** with certificates of analysis

### Research Quality Assessment

**Strengths**:
- Large systematic reviews (Shinjyo 2020: 60 studies, n=6,894)
- Multiple meta-analyses consistent in findings
- Recent high-quality RCT (Shekhar 2024)
- Comprehensive safety/toxicology data

**Limitations**:
- High variability in preparations across studies
- Most studies had methodological weaknesses (Bent 2006)
- Publication bias documented
- Objective sleep parameter improvements not demonstrated
- Long-term studies lacking

## Key Clinical Questions Answered

### 1. Does valerian improve sleep?
**Answer**: Moderate evidence for **subjective** sleep quality improvement (RR 1.8); limited evidence for **objective** polysomnographic changes.

### 2. How long before it works?
**Answer**: Some benefit may appear within 2 weeks; full effect by 4-8 weeks. Pharmacokinetics show peak levels at 1-2 hours (supports 30 min-2h pre-bedtime dosing).

### 3. Is it safe?
**Answer**: Generally yes for short-term use (≤8 weeks). Rare hepatotoxicity documented. Long-term safety (>6 months) unknown.

### 4. Why do clinical outcomes vary?
**Answer**: Two main factors:
1. **Extract quality variability** - inconsistent standardization across products
2. **Pharmacokinetic variability** - large inter- and intra-subject differences in absorption/metabolism

### 5. Which preparation is best?
**Answer**:
- **Best evidence**: Standardized dry extracts (most clinical trial data)
- **Potentially most reliable**: Whole root/rhizome preparations (less processing variability)
- **Most convenient**: Capsules/tablets
- **Most traditional**: Tincture or tea

## Research Priorities (Identified Gaps)

1. **Long-term safety studies** (>6 months continuous use)
2. **Standardization optimization** (beyond valerenic acid alone)
3. **Head-to-head comparisons** (whole root vs. extracts)
4. **Pharmacokinetic studies** to address variability
5. **Hepatotoxicity mechanism** and risk factor identification
6. **Drug interaction studies** (currently very limited)
7. **Adequate sample sizes** with rigorous methodology
8. **Objective sleep parameters** in well-designed trials

## Regulatory Status Summary

- ✅ **WHO**: Monograph in WHO Monographs on Selected Medicinal Plants, Volume 1 (1999)
- ✅ **EMA**: European Union herbal monograph (traditional use pathway)
- ✅ **Commission E**: Approved monograph (Germany)
- ✅ **Pharmacopoeias**: USP, EP, BP, plus Belgium, France, Germany, Italy, Switzerland, Japan
- ✅ **Health Canada**: Natural Health Product monograph

**Implication**: Valerian is well-established in regulatory frameworks globally, indicating recognized safety and traditional use.
