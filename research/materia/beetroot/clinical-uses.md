# Beetroot - Clinical Uses

## Blood Pressure Reduction (Hypertension)

### Meta-Analysis Evidence

**Benjamim et al., 2022** - 7 studies, 218 participants
- **Dosing**: 70-250 mL daily beetroot juice
- **Duration**: 3-60 days
- **Results**:
  - Systolic BP: **-4.95 mmHg** (95% CI: -8.88 to -1.01, p < 0.001)
  - Diastolic BP: -0.90 mmHg (95% CI: -3.16 to 1.36, p = 0.06, not significant)
- **Quality**: Moderate GRADE rating

**Grönroos et al., 2024** - 11 studies, 349 participants
- **Dosing**: 200-800 mg nitrate/day from beetroot juice, daily
- **Results**:
  - Clinical systolic BP: **-5.31 mmHg** (95% CI: -7.46 to -3.16)
  - 24-hour ambulatory BP: No significant effect
- **Key finding**: Daily ingestion reduces clinical systolic BP with no tolerance development
- **Quality**: Low certainty due to moderate-to-high heterogeneity

**Siervo et al., 2013** - 16 studies, 254 participants (7-30 per trial)
- **Duration range**: 2 hours to 15 days
- **Dose-response**: Significant dose-response relationship (p < 0.05)
- **Results**:
  - Systolic BP: **-4.4 mmHg** (95% CI: -5.9 to -2.8, p < 0.001)
  - Diastolic BP: -1.1 mmHg (95% CI: -2.2 to 0.1, p = 0.06)

**Bahadoran et al., 2017** - 22 studies, 1,248 participants (650 intervention, 598 control)
- **Optimal duration**: ≥14 days
- **Optimal dose**: 500 mL/day
- **Results**:
  - Systolic BP: **-3.55 mmHg** (95% CI: -4.55 to -2.54)
  - Diastolic BP: **-1.32 mmHg** (95% CI: -1.97 to -0.68)
- **Key findings**:
  - Greater effects with longer duration and higher doses
  - Paradoxically, higher nitrate concentrations showed smaller BP reductions
  - Nitrate-depleted placebo showed weaker effects, suggesting nitrate-independent mechanisms (polyphenols, betalains)

### RCT Evidence - Postmenopausal Women with Hypertension

**Benjamim et al., 2024** - 14 postmenopausal women with systemic arterial hypertension
- **Population**: Mean age 59±4 years, BMI 29.2±3.1 kg/m²
- **Dosing**:
  - Acute: 800 mg dietary nitrate via beetroot juice
  - Chronic: 400 mg daily for 6 days
  - Exercise timing: 2.5 hours post-ingestion
- **Results**:
  - Post-exercise systolic BP (acute): **-9.28 mmHg** (95% CI: -1.68 to -16.88)
  - Flow-mediated dilation (acute): **+3.18%** (p = 0.031)
  - Flow-mediated dilation (after 1 week): **+4.5%** (p = 0.005)
  - Enhanced parasympathetic modulation observed

### Clinical Dosing Guidelines for Hypertension
- **Nitrate dose**: 200-800 mg/day
- **Beetroot juice volume**: 70-250 mL/day
- **Duration**: 3-60 days for clinical effects
- **Timing**: Daily consumption
- **Tolerance**: No tolerance development observed

## Athletic Performance

### Muscular Endurance and Strength

**Evangelista et al., 2024** - 27 studies, healthy male individuals
- **Dosing**: 316-985 mg nitrate/day
- **Timing**: 2-3 hours before exercise
- **Results**:
  - **Muscular endurance**: SMD 0.31 (95% CI: 0.10-0.51, p < 0.01) - small-to-medium effect
  - **Overall muscular strength**: SMD 0.26 (95% CI: 0.03-0.48, p < 0.05)
  - **Strength in fatigued state**: SMD 0.64 (95% CI: 0.25-1.03, p < 0.01) - medium effect
  - **Strength at rest**: No significant effect

### Cardiorespiratory Endurance

**Domínguez et al., 2017** - 23 studies, athletes
- **Key findings**:
  - Improves cardiorespiratory endurance by increasing efficiency
  - Improves performance at various distances
  - **Increases time to exhaustion** at submaximal intensities (up to 16% in trained cyclists)
  - May improve performance at anaerobic threshold
  - Possible improvements in VO2max
  - **Caution**: Combining with caffeine may undermine ergogenic benefits
  - Contradictory data exists across studies

### Athletic Performance Dosing Guidelines
- **Nitrate dose**: 316-985 mg/day (optimal: 8.3-16.4 mmol)
- **Beetroot juice volume**: 70-140 mL
- **Timing**: 2-3 hours before exercise, OR chronic supplementation ≥3 days
- **Duration**: ≥3 days for chronic loading protocol

## Metabolic Effects

### Insulin Sensitivity

**Pinheiro et al., 2024** - 12-week RCT in older adults
- **Dosing**: 20g/day standardized beetroot extract
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Results**:
  - **Insulin levels**: Statistically significant decrease (p = 0.026, effect size f = 0.59)
  - **Plasma nitrate**: Notable elevation (p = 0.076, effect size f = 0.50)
  - HOMA-IR and HOMA-β: No significant alterations
  - No changes in anthropometric, biochemical, or hemodynamic measurements

## Cognitive Function

### Long-term Supplementation

**Babateen et al., 2022** - 13-week RCT, 47 overweight/obese older adults (60-75 years)
- **Dosing**:
  - High nitrate: 2 × 70 mL beetroot juice/day
  - Medium nitrate: 70 mL beetroot juice/day
  - Low nitrate: 70 mL beetroot juice on alternate days
  - Placebo: 70 mL nitrate-depleted juice on alternate days
- **Duration**: 13 weeks
- **Outcomes**:
  - **Cognitive function**: No significant differences (COMPASS battery, p > 0.05)
  - **Cerebral blood flow**: No significant differences (near-infrared spectroscopy, p > 0.05)
- **Conclusion**: NO3-rich beetroot juice does not modify cognitive function or cerebral blood flow in chronic supplementation

## Pharmacokinetics and Bioavailability

### Blood Nitrate/Nitrite Levels

**Jakubcik et al., 2021** - 11 healthy adults, 250 mL beverages
- **Dosing tested**:
  - High nitrate/low nitrite: 572 mg NO3-, 32 mg NO2-
  - Medium nitrate/medium nitrite: 280 mg NO3-, 237 mg NO2-
  - Low nitrate/medium nitrite: 43 mg NO3-, 262 mg NO2-
  - Placebo: 8 mg NO3-, 5.8 mg NO2-
- **Measurement period**: Baseline to 6 hours
- **Results**:
  - High-nitrate beverages increased plasma NO2- and NO3- at 2h, remained elevated at 6h (p < 0.05)
  - Mean arterial pressure decreased at 4-6h with high-nitrate treatment
  - Systolic/diastolic BP reduction not consistently observed
  - **Inorganic nitrate critical** for elevating plasma concentrations

### Bioavailability Data

**Nitrate absorption**:
- Cooked beetroot: **106±15% bioavailability** (near-complete absorption)
- Beetroot juice and whole beetroot: **~100% bioavailability**
- Nitrate from vegetables absorbed very effectively

**Betanin (betalain pigment)**:
- Urinary excretion: 0.5-0.9% of ingested betacyanins in 12 hours (from 300 mL juice)
- Limited absorption but detectable

**Key references**:
- "The plasma bioavailability of nitrate and betanin from Beta vulgaris rubra in humans" (PMC5346430)
- "The oral bioavailability of nitrate from nitrate-rich vegetables in humans" (PMID: 18723086)

## Dose-Dependent Relationships

### Feasibility Study - Incremental Dosing

**Babateen et al., 2021** - 13-week pilot RCT, 62 enrolled, 50 completed (80.6%)
- **Population**: Overweight and obese older adults
- **Dosing**:
  - High: 2 × 70 mL beetroot juice/day (800 mg NO3-/day)
  - Medium: 70 mL beetroot juice/day (400 mg NO3-/day)
  - Low: 70 mL beetroot juice on alternate days (200 mg NO3-/day)
  - Placebo: 70 mL nitrate-depleted juice on alternate days
- **Duration**: 13 weeks
- **Results**:
  - Self-reported compliance: **>90%**
  - **Dose-dependent relationship** between nitrate intake and plasma/urinary nitrate
  - **Feasibility confirmed** for prolonged supplementation in older overweight/obese adults

## Comparative Bioavailability

**McIlvenna et al., 2017** - 9 healthy volunteers, beetroot juice vs. chard gel
- **Key findings**:
  - Plasma NO3- and NO2- increased similarly in both groups
  - **Greater increase in RXNO with beetroot juice** vs. chard gel (likely due to higher polyphenol content)
  - Systolic BP reduced similarly in both groups
  - **Considerable inter-individual variation** observed

## Summary of Clinical Outcomes

### Blood Pressure
- **Systolic reduction**: -3.55 to -5.31 mmHg
- **Diastolic reduction**: -0.90 to -1.32 mmHg (often non-significant)
- **Population**: Hypertensive adults
- **Quality**: Moderate evidence, some heterogeneity

### Exercise Performance
- **Muscular endurance**: SMD 0.31 (small-to-medium effect)
- **Time to exhaustion**: Up to 16% improvement in trained cyclists
- **Strength when fatigued**: SMD 0.64 (medium effect)
- **Strength at rest**: No significant effect

### Cognitive Function
- **Long-term (13 weeks)**: No effect in older adults
- **Cerebral blood flow**: No effect in chronic supplementation
- **Acute effects**: Some positive findings in acute studies (requires further research)

### Vascular Function
- **Flow-mediated dilation**: +3.18% to +4.5% improvement
- **Population**: Postmenopausal women with hypertension
