# Beetroot - Preparations and Dosing

## Clinical Preparations Used in Trials

### Beetroot Juice

**Standard preparation**: Fresh or concentrated beetroot juice

#### Cardiovascular Health Dosing
- **Volume**: 70-250 mL/day
- **Nitrate content**: 200-800 mg NO3-/day
- **Duration**: 3-60 days for clinical effects
- **Frequency**: Daily
- **Timing**: Any time of day (consistent daily intake)
- **Evidence**: 7-22 studies, 218-1,248 participants across meta-analyses

**Specific protocols from trials**:

**High dose** (Babateen et al., 2021):
- 2 × 70 mL beetroot juice/day (140 mL total)
- Provides: 800 mg NO3-/day
- Duration: 13 weeks
- Compliance: >90%

**Medium dose** (Babateen et al., 2021):
- 70 mL beetroot juice/day
- Provides: 400 mg NO3-/day
- Duration: 13 weeks
- Compliance: >90%

**Low dose** (Babateen et al., 2021):
- 70 mL beetroot juice on alternate days
- Provides: 200 mg NO3-/day (averaged)
- Duration: 13 weeks
- Compliance: >90%

**Optimal dose for BP** (Bahadoran et al., 2017):
- 500 mL/day
- Duration: ≥14 days for maximal effect

#### Athletic Performance Dosing
- **Volume**: 70-140 mL
- **Nitrate content**: 316-985 mg/day (optimal: 8.3-16.4 mmol)
- **Timing**: 2-3 hours before exercise for acute effects
- **Chronic loading**: Daily intake for ≥3 days before competition
- **Evidence**: 23-27 studies, multiple systematic reviews

**Acute protocol** (Benjamim et al., 2024):
- 800 mg dietary nitrate via beetroot juice
- Timing: 2.5 hours before exercise
- Population: Postmenopausal women with hypertension
- Result: -9.28 mmHg systolic BP post-exercise

**Chronic loading protocol** (Benjamim et al., 2024):
- 400 mg nitrate daily
- Duration: 6 days
- Result: +4.5% flow-mediated dilation

#### Pharmacokinetic Considerations
- **Peak plasma levels**: 2 hours post-ingestion
- **Duration of elevation**: Plasma NO3- and NO2- remain elevated at 6 hours
- **Blood pressure effects**: Most pronounced at 4-6 hours post-ingestion
- **Reference**: Jakubcik et al., 2021

### Standardized Beetroot Extract

**Form**: Dried extract powder, tablets, or capsules

#### Dose Used in Safety Study
**Pinheiro et al., 2024**:
- **Dose**: 20g/day standardized beetroot extract
- **Form**: Extract (form not specified - likely powder)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Population**: Older adults
- **Safety**: No serious adverse events
- **Effects**: Insulin reduction (p = 0.026), plasma nitrate elevation (p = 0.076)

**Standardization**: Not specified in study, but likely standardized for nitrate or betalain content

**Commercial preparation**: Dehydrated beetroot ground into powder

**Dose** (from literature):
- **15g beetroot powder**
- **Provides**: 200 mg nitrate, 1.2 mg nitrite
- **Reference**: PMID 33207606

**Equivalent to**: ~200 mg nitrate/day (low-dose clinical range)

**Usage**: Can be mixed in water, smoothies, or food

## Traditional Preparations

### Whole Cooked Beetroot

**Traditional food preparation**:
- Boiled, roasted, or steamed beetroot
- **Bioavailability**: 106±15% for nitrate (near-complete absorption)
- **Advantage**: Cooking does not significantly degrade nitrate content
- **Serving size**: Typically 100-200g cooked beetroot

**Estimated nitrate content**: Varies widely (50-300 mg per serving depending on growing conditions)

---

### Beetroot Juice (Traditional/Folk)

**Folk remedy preparation**:
- Fresh-pressed raw beetroot juice
- Historically used in Eastern European folk medicine
- **Dose**: Variable, typically 100-250 mL/day

**Modern commercial beetroot juice**: Standardized for nitrate content, more consistent than homemade

---

### Fermented Beetroot (Kvass)

**Traditional Eastern European preparation**:
- Lacto-fermented beetroot beverage
- **Preparation**: Beetroot + water + salt, fermented for several days to weeks
- **Nitrate content**: Unknown, likely reduced by fermentation
- **Traditional use**: Nutritional beverage, digestive tonic
- **Clinical evidence**: None

**Note**: Fermentation may alter nitrate content and bioavailability; not studied in clinical trials

---

### Dried Beetroot

**Traditional preservation method**:
- Dehydrated beetroot slices or powder
- **Use**: Reconstituted in water or added to food
- **Nitrate stability**: Likely preserved in dried form
- **Clinical evidence**: None for this specific preparation

---

## Herbal Preparation Formats

### Tincture (Alcohol Extract)

**Note**: No clinical trials using beetroot tincture identified in reviewed literature

**Theoretical preparation** (extrapolated from general herbalism):
- **Ratio**: 1:5 (w/v) - 1 part dried beetroot to 5 parts menstruum
- **Menstruum**: 25-40% alcohol (low alcohol due to water-soluble actives)
- **Dose**: Not established; would need to be calculated based on nitrate content
- **Limitation**: Nitrate is highly water-soluble; alcohol extraction may not be optimal

**Recommendation**: Juice or water-based preparations preferred over alcohol tinctures for nitrate content

---

### Tea/Decoction

**Preparation method**: Dried beetroot simmered in water

**Not found in clinical literature**. Theoretical preparation:
- **Ratio**: 5-10g dried beetroot per cup (240 mL) water
- **Method**: Simmer for 10-15 minutes, strain
- **Dose**: 1-3 cups/day
- **Nitrate content**: Unknown; would depend on extraction efficiency

**Limitation**: No standardization for nitrate content; difficult to achieve clinical doses used in trials

**Recommendation**: Juice or standardized extracts preferred for therapeutic dosing

---

### Capsules/Tablets

**Commercial preparations**: Encapsulated beetroot powder or extract

**Typical commercial doses**: 500-1,000 mg beetroot powder per capsule
- **Nitrate content**: Varies widely; check product label
- **Dose**: Variable; aim for 200-800 mg nitrate/day based on clinical evidence
- **Standardization**: Look for products standardized to nitrate content

**Advantage**: Convenient, avoids beetroot taste, shelf-stable

**Disadvantage**: May be lower bioavailability than juice (requires dissolution); check for standardization

## Dosing Equivalencies

### Nitrate Content Comparison

**To achieve 400 mg nitrate/day** (moderate clinical dose):
- **Beetroot juice**: ~70-100 mL (depending on concentration)
- **Beetroot powder**: ~30g (based on 15g = 200 mg nitrate)
- **Whole cooked beetroot**: Variable, ~150-300g (high variability in nitrate content)
- **Standardized extract**: Depends on product; check label for nitrate content

**Note**: Nitrate content varies significantly based on:
- Growing conditions (soil nitrate, fertilization)
- Beetroot variety
- Processing method
- Storage conditions

**Recommendation**: Use products with verified nitrate content for therapeutic dosing

## Preparation Recommendations by Indication

### Blood Pressure Reduction (Hypertension)
**Preferred preparation**: Beetroot juice or standardized extract
- **Dose**: 200-800 mg nitrate/day
- **Format**: 70-250 mL juice/day OR equivalent standardized extract
- **Duration**: Minimum 14 days; continuous daily use
- **Timing**: Consistent daily intake; time of day not critical

**Evidence level**: High (multiple meta-analyses, 7-22 RCTs)

---

### Athletic Performance Enhancement
**Preferred preparation**: Beetroot juice (acute or chronic protocol)

**Acute protocol**:
- **Dose**: 400-800 mg nitrate
- **Format**: 70-140 mL concentrated beetroot juice
- **Timing**: 2-3 hours before exercise or competition
- **Use**: One-time boost for performance

**Chronic loading protocol**:
- **Dose**: 400-800 mg nitrate/day
- **Format**: 70-140 mL juice/day
- **Duration**: Minimum 3 days before event
- **Timing**: Daily, any time of day
- **Use**: Sustained performance enhancement

**Evidence level**: High (multiple systematic reviews, 23-27 RCTs)

---

### General Cardiovascular Health
**Preferred preparation**: Beetroot juice, powder, or extract
- **Dose**: 200-400 mg nitrate/day (moderate dose)
- **Format**: 70-140 mL juice/day OR equivalent powder/extract
- **Duration**: Long-term daily use (safe up to 13 weeks studied)
- **Timing**: Consistent daily intake

**Evidence level**: Moderate (extrapolated from hypertension trials)

---

### Metabolic Support (Insulin Sensitivity)
**Preferred preparation**: Standardized beetroot extract
- **Dose**: 20g/day standardized extract
- **Duration**: 12 weeks (studied duration)
- **Timing**: Daily

**Evidence level**: Low (single RCT showing insulin reduction)

## Nitrate-Depleted Placebo (Control)

**Used in clinical trials**: Beetroot juice with nitrate removed
- **Purpose**: Control for nitrate-independent effects (polyphenols, betalains)
- **Finding**: Weaker but present BP-lowering effects
- **Implication**: Non-nitrate compounds contribute to benefits

**Not commercially available** for consumer use; used in research only

---

## Storage and Stability

### Beetroot Juice
- **Fresh**: Refrigerate, use within 3-5 days
- **Commercial concentrated**: Follow manufacturer guidelines
- **Nitrate stability**: Relatively stable; refrigeration recommended

### Beetroot Powder/Extract
- **Storage**: Cool, dry place; sealed container
- **Stability**: Stable for extended periods (months to years) when stored properly
- **Advantage**: More shelf-stable than fresh juice

### Whole Beetroot
- **Fresh**: Refrigerate, use within 1-2 weeks
- **Cooked**: Refrigerate, use within 3-5 days
- **Nitrate content**: Stable in whole beetroot; not degraded by cooking

---

## Preparation Quality Considerations

### Factors Affecting Nitrate Content

1. **Growing conditions**: Soil nitrate, fertilization practices
2. **Beetroot variety**: Red beetroot generally used; other varieties not studied
3. **Processing**: Juicing, drying, extraction methods
4. **Storage**: Nitrate relatively stable, but degradation possible over time

### Quality Markers for Commercial Products

✅ **Look for**:
- **Nitrate content listed** on label (mg NO3- per serving)
- **Standardization**: To nitrate or betalain content
- **Third-party testing**: Verification of nitrate content
- **Organic certification**: Reduces pesticide exposure

⚠️ **Avoid**:
- Products without listed nitrate content (cannot dose accurately)
- Non-standardized "beetroot blend" products
- Products with added sugars (for cardiovascular health applications)

---

## Contraindicated Combinations

### Antibacterial Mouthwash
- **Effect**: Kills oral bacteria needed for NO3- → NO2- conversion
- **Result**: Reduces beetroot efficacy
- **Recommendation**: Avoid mouthwash within 2-3 hours of beetroot intake, or avoid altogether during supplementation

### Caffeine (for Athletic Performance)
- **Effect**: May undermine ergogenic benefits
- **Recommendation**: Separate beetroot intake from caffeine consumption, especially before athletic events

---

## Summary - Preparation Guidelines

### Evidence-Based Preparations

**Highest quality evidence**:
1. **Beetroot juice**: 70-250 mL/day (200-800 mg nitrate)
2. **Standardized extract**: 20g/day (or dose providing 200-800 mg nitrate)
3. **Beetroot powder**: Dose to provide 200-800 mg nitrate/day

**Limited or no evidence**:
- Tinctures (alcohol extracts)
- Teas/decoctions
- Fermented beetroot (kvass)
- Non-standardized capsules

### Dosing by Indication

| Indication | Dose (Nitrate) | Format | Duration | Timing |
|------------|---------------|---------|----------|---------|
| **Hypertension** | 200-800 mg/day | Juice (70-250 mL) or extract | ≥14 days, ongoing | Daily, consistent |
| **Athletic performance (acute)** | 400-800 mg | Juice (70-140 mL) | Single dose | 2-3h pre-exercise |
| **Athletic performance (chronic)** | 400-800 mg/day | Juice (70-140 mL) | ≥3 days | Daily |
| **General cardiovascular** | 200-400 mg/day | Juice, powder, or extract | Ongoing | Daily |

### Key Preparation Principles

1. **Standardize to nitrate content**: Dose by mg NO3-, not volume or weight
2. **Choose evidence-based formats**: Juice or standardized extracts
3. **Ensure consistent daily intake**: Effects build over time; missed doses reduce efficacy
4. **Verify product quality**: Third-party testing, nitrate content listed
5. **Avoid mouthwash**: Impairs nitrate conversion
6. **Refrigerate fresh preparations**: Maintains stability
7. **Monitor blood pressure**: If hypertensive or on BP medications

### Bioavailability Ranking

1. **Beetroot juice**: ~100% nitrate bioavailability, rapid absorption
2. **Cooked whole beetroot**: 106±15% bioavailability (near-complete)
3. **Beetroot powder**: Likely similar to juice when dissolved; requires dissolution
4. **Standardized extract**: Depends on extraction method; verify nitrate content
5. **Tincture**: Unknown; likely suboptimal for nitrate (water-soluble compound)

**Recommendation**: Beetroot juice or whole beetroot (cooked) for maximum bioavailability
