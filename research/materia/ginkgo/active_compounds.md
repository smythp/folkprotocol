# Ginkgo biloba: Active Compounds and Standardization

## STANDARDIZED EXTRACT: EGb 761®

### Source Material and Extraction Process

**Plant Material:** Ginkgo biloba leaves

**Extraction Ratio:** 35-67:1 (35-67 kg of leaves per 1 kg of extract)

**Extraction Solvent:** Acetone 60% (weight/weight)

**Result:** Highly concentrated standardized extract with consistent marker compound percentages

---

## STANDARDIZATION MARKERS BY PHARMACOPOEIA

### European Pharmacopoeia Standards (EGb 761®)

**Flavonoid Content:**
- **22.0-27.0%** ginkgo flavonoids (calculated as ginkgo flavone glycosides)

**Terpene Lactone Content:**
- **5.4-6.6%** total terpene lactones
  - **2.8-3.4%** ginkgolides A, B, C
  - **2.6-3.2%** bilobalide

**Ginkgolic Acids (toxic component):**
- **<5 ppm** (parts per million) - strictly limited due to potential toxicity

---

### US Pharmacopoeia Standards

**Flavonoid Content:**
- **22.0-27.0%** ginkgo flavonoids

**Terpene Lactone Content:**
- **5.4-12.0%** total terpene lactones (wider range than European standards)

**Ginkgolic Acids:**
- **<5 ppm**

---

### Chinese Pharmacopoeia Standards

**Terpene Lactone Content:**
- **≥6%** (lower limit only, no upper limit specified)

**Flavonoid Content:**
- **≥24%** (lower limit only)

**Ginkgolic Acids:**
- Not specified in Chinese standards

## KEY BIOACTIVE CONSTITUENTS

### 1. Flavonoid Fraction (22-27% of extract)

**Primary Compounds:**
- **Quercetin** - potent antioxidant flavonol
- **Kaempferol** - antioxidant flavonol with anti-inflammatory properties
- **Isorhamnetin** - methylated flavonol with antioxidant activity

**Primary Actions:**
- Free radical scavenging (antioxidant activity)
- Protection against oxidative stress
- Modulation of inflammatory pathways
- Vascular protection

**Clinical Relevance:** The flavonoid fraction contributes to Ginkgo's antioxidant and neuroprotective effects

---

### 2. Terpene Lactone Fraction (5.4-6.6% of extract)

**Ginkgolides (2.8-3.4%):**
- **Ginkgolide A** - platelet-activating factor (PAF) antagonist
- **Ginkgolide B** - most potent PAF antagonist of the ginkgolides
- **Ginkgolide C** - PAF antagonist with anti-inflammatory effects

**Bilobalide (2.6-3.2%):**
- Neuroprotective sesquiterpene
- Mitochondrial protection
- Anti-apoptotic effects
- Modulation of neurotransmitter systems

**Primary Actions:**
- PAF receptor antagonism (anti-platelet, anti-inflammatory)
- Neuroprotection and neuronal survival
- Mitochondrial stabilization
- Modulation of cerebral blood flow

**Clinical Relevance:** The terpene lactone fraction is responsible for many of Ginkgo's cognitive and circulatory effects

---

### 3. Ginkgolic Acids (<5 ppm)

**Nature:** Alkylphenolic compounds naturally present in Ginkgo leaves

**Toxicity:** Can cause allergic reactions and have cytotoxic properties

**Standardization Requirement:** Must be reduced to <5 ppm in pharmaceutical-grade extracts through processing

**Clinical Significance:** Proper standardization to remove ginkgolic acids is critical for safety

---

## PHARMACOKINETICS AND BIOAVAILABILITY

### Absorption and Distribution

**Ginkgolide Half-Lives:**
- **1.7-2.2 hours** for major ginkgolide compounds

**Maximum Plasma Concentrations:**
- **40-159 ng/ml** depending on specific compound
- Reached within 1-3 hours after oral administration

**Clearance Values:**
- **24.2-37.6 ml/min/kg** for ginkgolides

---

### Time Course of Effects

**Acute Effects (Single Dose):**
- **Onset:** 2.5 hours post-dosing
- **Peak:** 2.5-4 hours
- **Duration:** Effects persist through 6 hours
- **Doses showing acute effects:** 240-360 mg

**Chronic Effects (Repeated Dosing):**
- **Cognitive and functional benefits:** Require 22-26 weeks minimum
- **Neuropsychiatric benefits in MCI:** Demonstrable at 24 weeks
- **Cerebral blood flow changes:** Detectable at 4 weeks

---

### Dose-Response Relationships

**Kennedy et al. 2000 Dose-Response Study (20 participants, acute dosing):**
- **120 mg:** Minimal acute cognitive effects
- **240 mg:** Significant improvement in speed of attention at 2.5-6 hours
- **360 mg:** Enhanced attention effects, but not linear increase from 240 mg

**Conclusion:** Effects are dose-dependent but not in a linear manner

---

## METABOLIC INTERACTIONS

### Cytochrome P450 Enzyme Effects

**Study Design (Zadoyan et al. 2012):**
- 18 healthy adults
- Cocktail phenotyping trial (tests multiple CYP enzymes simultaneously)
- Comparison of 120 mg BID vs 240 mg QD vs placebo

**Key Findings:**
- **EGb 761/placebo ratios:** Close to unity for all CYPs
- **90% confidence intervals:** Largely within 0.70-1.43 range
- **Conclusion:** No relevant effect on in vivo activity of major CYP enzymes
- **Drug interaction potential:** No relevant potential to cause metabolic drug-drug interactions

**Safety Threshold:** Maximum consumption of EGb 761 should not exceed 240 mg daily to maintain pharmacokinetic herb-drug interactions at an acceptable limit

**Source:** Zadoyan et al. 2012, European Journal of Clinical Pharmacology (PMID: 22189672)

## QUALITY CONTROL AND STANDARDIZATION

### Critical Quality Factors

**1. Extraction Solvent:** Must use 60% acetone (w/w) to ensure consistent phytochemical profile

**2. Extraction Ratio:** 35-67:1 ensures adequate concentration of active constituents

**3. Marker Compound Ranges:**
- Flavonoids: Must fall within 22-27%
- Terpene lactones: Must fall within 5.4-6.6% (European) or 5.4-12.0% (US)
- Ginkgolic acids: Must be <5 ppm

**4. Batch-to-Batch Consistency:** Critical for ensuring reproducible clinical effects across different manufacturers and production batches

---

### Clinical Significance of Standardization

**Why Standardization Matters:**
- Most clinical trials use standardized extracts (particularly EGb 761®)
- Non-standardized Ginkgo products may not contain therapeutic levels of active compounds
- Inadequate removal of ginkgolic acids poses safety risks
- Consistency ensures predictable dosing and effects

**Recommendation:** Only use Ginkgo products that meet European or US Pharmacopoeia standards and specify:
- Flavonoid content (22-27%)
- Terpene lactone content (5.4-6.6% or 5.4-12.0%)
- Ginkgolic acid content (<5 ppm)
- Extraction ratio and solvent

---

## COMPARATIVE BIOAVAILABILITY

### Extract vs Raw Plant Material

**Standardized Extract (EGb 761):**
- 35-67:1 concentration ratio
- Consistent bioavailable flavonoid and terpene content
- Controlled ginkgolic acid levels
- Predictable pharmacokinetics

**Raw Ginkgo Leaves (Tea/Decoction):**
- Variable phytochemical content
- Unknown levels of ginkgolic acids (potentially high)
- Poor standardization
- Unpredictable clinical effects

**Clinical Evidence:** All high-quality RCTs and meta-analyses use standardized extracts. There is minimal clinical evidence supporting use of raw leaf preparations.

---

## MECHANISM OF ACTION SUMMARY

### Proposed Mechanisms Based on Bioactive Compounds

**Flavonoid-Mediated Effects:**
1. Free radical scavenging (quercetin, kaempferol, isorhamnetin)
2. Lipid peroxidation inhibition
3. Vascular endothelial protection
4. Anti-inflammatory signaling modulation

**Terpene Lactone-Mediated Effects:**
1. PAF receptor antagonism (ginkgolides A, B, C) → reduced platelet aggregation and inflammation
2. Mitochondrial protection (bilobalide) → enhanced cellular energy metabolism
3. Neuroprotection via anti-apoptotic signaling
4. Cerebral blood flow enhancement
5. Neurotransmitter modulation (dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine systems)

**Combined Effects:**
- Improved cerebral circulation
- Enhanced neuronal survival in ischemic/oxidative conditions
- Cognitive enhancement via multiple pathways
- Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant neuroprotection

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Standardization is essential** - Only extracts meeting pharmacopoeia standards (22-27% flavonoids, 5.4-6.6% terpene lactones, <5 ppm ginkgolic acids) have clinical evidence

2. **Bioavailability is dose-dependent** - Acute effects at 240-360 mg; chronic effects require 120-240 mg daily for 22-26 weeks

3. **No significant drug interactions** - At doses ≤240 mg daily, no relevant CYP450 interactions

4. **Multiple mechanisms of action** - Both flavonoid and terpene fractions contribute to clinical effects through complementary pathways

5. **Quality control matters** - Extraction ratio (35-67:1), solvent (60% acetone), and ginkgolic acid removal are critical for safety and efficacy
