# Ginkgo biloba: Preparations and Dosing

## STANDARDIZED EXTRACT (EGb 761® and Equivalents)

### Pharmaceutical-Grade Extract Specifications

**Extraction Process:**
- **Plant Material:** Ginkgo biloba leaves (dried)
- **Extraction Ratio:** 35-67:1 (35-67 kg of leaves per 1 kg of extract)
- **Extraction Solvent:** Acetone 60% (weight/weight)
- **Result:** Highly concentrated, standardized extract

**Required Standardization Markers:**
- **Flavonoid glycosides:** 22-27%
- **Terpene lactones:** 5.4-6.6% (European) or 5.4-12.0% (US)
  - Ginkgolides A, B, C: 2.8-3.4%
  - Bilobalide: 2.6-3.2%
- **Ginkgolic acids:** <5 ppm (critical safety requirement)

**Available Forms:**
- Tablets (most common)
- Capsules
- Liquid extracts (less common for standardized extracts)

## CLINICAL DOSING PROTOCOLS

### Evidence-Based Dosing by Indication

**1. Alzheimer's Disease and Dementia**
- **Dose:** 240 mg daily
- **Formulation:** Standardized extract (EGb 761®)
- **Administration:** Can be divided (120 mg BID) or once daily
- **Duration:** Minimum 22-26 weeks for clinical benefits
- **Evidence:** 18 RCTs, 1,642 participants (Li et al. 2023)

**2. Mild Cognitive Impairment with Neuropsychiatric Symptoms**
- **Dose:** 240 mg daily
- **Formulation:** EGb 761® standardized extract
- **Administration:** Once daily
- **Duration:** 24 weeks
- **Evidence:** 160 participants, RCT (Gavrilova et al. 2014)

**3. General Cognitive Support (Mild Cognitive Impairment)**
- **Dose:** 120-240 mg daily
- **Formulation:** Standardized extract
- **Administration:** Divided (60 mg BID for 120 mg total) or once daily
- **Duration:** Minimum 22-26 weeks
- **Evidence:** 2,561 participants across 9 RCTs (Tan et al. 2015)

**4. Cerebral Blood Flow Enhancement**
- **Dose:** 120 mg daily
- **Formulation:** Ginkgo biloba extract
- **Administration:** 60 mg twice daily
- **Duration:** 4 weeks minimum
- **Evidence:** 9 participants, pilot study (Mashayekh et al. 2011)

**5. Tinnitus**
- **Dose:** 240 mg daily
- **Formulation:** EGb 761® standardized extract
- **Administration:** 120 mg twice daily
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Evidence:** 197 participants, RCT (Procházková et al. 2018)

**6. Acute Cognitive Enhancement**
- **Dose:** 240-360 mg
- **Formulation:** Standardized extract (GK501)
- **Administration:** Single acute dose
- **Time to effect:** 2.5 hours
- **Duration of effect:** Up to 6 hours
- **Evidence:** 20 participants, dose-response study (Kennedy et al. 2000)

**7. Intermittent Claudication (Peripheral Arterial Disease)**
- **Dose:** 120-240 mg daily (based on meta-analysis)
- **Formulation:** Standardized extract
- **Administration:** Divided doses typical
- **Duration:** Variable (trials ranged from weeks to months)
- **Evidence:** 8 RCTs, meta-analysis (Pittler & Ernst 2000)
- **Note:** Modest benefit; 300 mg daily showed no benefit in Gardner et al. 2008

## DOSING SCHEDULE OPTIONS

### Once Daily Dosing

**Advantages:**
- Improved compliance
- Simpler dosing schedule
- Tested in clinical trials (240 mg QD)

**Dosing:**
- Take in the morning with or without food
- Consistent timing each day

**Evidence:** Zadoyan et al. 2012 showed 240 mg once daily had equivalent safety to 120 mg BID

---

### Twice Daily Dosing (Divided Dose)

**Advantages:**
- May provide more consistent plasma levels
- Traditional dosing in many clinical trials

**Dosing:**
- 60 mg BID (120 mg total) or 120 mg BID (240 mg total)
- Morning and evening doses
- With or without food

**Evidence:** Used in cerebral blood flow study (Mashayekh et al. 2011) and tinnitus trial (Procházková et al. 2018)

---

## MAXIMUM SAFE DOSES

**Chronic Daily Use:**
- **Maximum recommended:** 240 mg daily
- **Studied up to:** 300 mg daily (Gardner et al. 2008 PAD trial)
- **Safety data:** Excellent safety profile up to 240 mg daily; 300 mg well tolerated but not more effective

**Acute Single Dose:**
- **Studied up to:** 360 mg (Kennedy et al. 2000)
- **Well tolerated:** Yes, but effects not linear above 240 mg
- **Recommendation:** 240-360 mg for acute cognitive effects

**Drug Interaction Threshold:**
- **No CYP450 interactions:** Up to 240 mg daily (Zadoyan et al. 2012)
- **Doses >240 mg:** Not studied for drug interaction potential

## TINCTURE PREPARATIONS

### Important Note on Tinctures

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **No high-quality clinical trials** have used Ginkgo tinctures
- All RCTs and meta-analyses use standardized dry extracts (tablets/capsules)
- Tinctures are traditional preparations but lack clinical validation

**Challenges with Tinctures:**
- Difficult to achieve standardization (22-27% flavonoids, 5.4-6.6% terpene lactones)
- Unknown ginkgolic acid content (safety concern)
- Variable extraction efficiency depending on solvent
- No pharmacopoeia specifications for tinctures

---

### Theoretical Tincture Ratios (For Traditional Herbalism)

**If making a tincture (not evidence-based):**

**Fresh Leaf Tincture:**
- **Ratio:** 1:2 (herb:menstruum)
- **Solvent:** 50-70% alcohol
- **Dosage:** Unknown (no clinical data)

**Dried Leaf Tincture:**
- **Ratio:** 1:5 (herb:menstruum)
- **Solvent:** 50-70% alcohol
- **Dosage:** Unknown (no clinical data)

**Problems:**
- Ginkgolic acids likely NOT adequately removed (safety risk)
- Flavonoid and terpene content unknown and likely inadequate
- No clinical evidence supporting efficacy
- Potential for allergic reactions

**Recommendation:** Do NOT use tinctures for clinical/therapeutic purposes. Use only standardized extracts meeting pharmacopoeia standards.

---

## TEA PREPARATIONS (DECOCTION/INFUSION)

### Important Note on Tea

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **No high-quality clinical trials** have used Ginkgo tea
- All evidence supports standardized extracts only

**Challenges with Tea:**
- Very low concentration of active compounds
- Ginkgolic acids NOT removed (safety concern)
- Water extraction inefficient for flavonoids and terpene lactones
- No standardization possible
- Unknown and likely inadequate bioavailability

---

### Theoretical Tea Dosing (Not Recommended)

**If making tea (not evidence-based and NOT recommended):**

**Dried Leaf Infusion:**
- **Amount:** 2-3 grams dried leaves
- **Water:** 200-250 ml boiling water
- **Steep time:** 10-15 minutes
- **Dosage:** Unknown (no clinical data)

**Problems:**
- To match 240 mg of 50:1 extract, would need ~12 grams of dried leaf
- Even 12 grams unlikely to provide equivalent bioavailable compounds
- High ginkgolic acid content (allergic risk)
- Water extraction inefficient for lipophilic terpene lactones

**Recommendation:** Do NOT use tea for therapeutic purposes. The extraction ratio and standardization cannot match pharmaceutical-grade extracts.

## CAPSULE/TABLET PREPARATIONS (RECOMMENDED)

### How to Choose a Quality Product

**1. Standardization Specifications (MUST be listed on label):**
- Flavonoid glycosides: 22-27%
- Terpene lactones: 5.4-6.6% (or 5.4-12.0%)
- Ginkgolic acids: <5 ppm

**2. Extract Ratio:**
- Should list 35:1, 40:1, 50:1, or similar high-concentration ratio
- Example: "50:1 extract" means 50 kg of leaves per 1 kg of extract

**3. Dosage per Capsule/Tablet:**
- Common: 40 mg, 60 mg, 80 mg, 120 mg per capsule
- For 240 mg daily dose: Take 2x120 mg, 3x80 mg, 4x60 mg, or 6x40 mg capsules

**4. Third-Party Testing:**
- USP Verified
- NSF Certified for Sport
- ConsumerLab approved
- Ensures purity, potency, and absence of contaminants

**5. Manufacturer Reputation:**
- Established herbal supplement companies
- GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) certified facilities
- Transparent about sourcing and testing

---

### Example Product Labels (What to Look For)

**Good Label Example:**
```
Ginkgo Biloba Extract 120 mg
Standardized to 24% ginkgo flavone glycosides (28.8 mg)
Standardized to 6% terpene lactones (7.2 mg)
Ginkgolic acids <5 ppm
Extract ratio: 50:1
Directions: Take 1-2 capsules daily
```

**Bad Label Example (Avoid):**
```
Ginkgo Biloba Leaf Powder 500 mg
(No standardization information)
(No extract ratio)
(No ginkgolic acid specification)
```

## ADMINISTRATION GUIDELINES

### Timing and Food

**With or Without Food:**
- Clinical trials have not specifically tested food effects
- Both approaches appear acceptable
- Some clinicians recommend with food to reduce potential GI upset

**Time of Day:**
- **Once daily:** Morning administration typical
- **Twice daily:** Morning and evening
- Consistency is more important than specific timing

---

### Duration of Treatment

**Acute Effects:**
- Single doses show effects within 2.5 hours (Kennedy et al. 2000)
- Duration: Up to 6 hours

**Chronic Effects (Cognitive/Functional Benefits):**
- **Minimum duration:** 22-26 weeks (5-6 months)
- Benefits accumulate over time
- Most trials studied 24-26 week durations

**Long-Term Use:**
- Safety data available up to 26 weeks
- Longer-term safety not extensively studied
- No evidence of tolerance or diminishing effects within studied timeframes

---

## DOSE TITRATION

### Starting Dose

**Option 1 - Start Low:**
- Begin with 120 mg daily for 1-2 weeks
- Assess tolerance
- Increase to 240 mg daily if well tolerated

**Option 2 - Standard Dose:**
- Begin with 240 mg daily (the clinically studied dose)
- Most patients tolerate this dose without titration

**For Elderly or Sensitive Patients:**
- May start with 60 mg daily
- Increase by 60 mg every 1-2 weeks
- Target: 120-240 mg daily

---

## COMBINATION WITH OTHER THERAPIES

### Ginkgo + Donepezil (Alzheimer's Disease)

**Evidence:** Li et al. 2023 meta-analysis specifically studied this combination

**Dosing:**
- Ginkgo biloba extract 240 mg daily + donepezil (standard dose)
- Superior efficacy compared to donepezil alone
- No increased adverse events

**Clinical Use:**
- Safe and effective combination
- Additive benefits for cognitive function
- Well tolerated

---

### Ginkgo + Other Supplements

**No high-quality data on combinations with:**
- Other nootropics
- Omega-3 fatty acids
- B vitamins
- Other herbal supplements

**Theoretical Considerations:**
- Avoid combinations with other PAF antagonists
- Caution with other herbs affecting bleeding (garlic, ginger, turmeric at high doses)
- No evidence of negative interactions with standard multivitamins

## BIOEQUIVALENCE AND GENERIC PRODUCTS

### EGb 761® vs Generic Extracts

**EGb 761®:**
- Specific proprietary extract with extensive clinical trial data
- Manufactured by Dr. Willmar Schwabe Pharmaceuticals
- Gold standard for Ginkgo research

**Generic Extracts:**
- Must meet same pharmacopoeia standards (22-27% flavonoids, 5.4-6.6% terpene lactones, <5 ppm ginkgolic acids)
- If properly standardized, should be bioequivalent
- Look for third-party testing to ensure quality

**Recommendation:**
- Either EGb 761® or properly standardized generic extract acceptable
- Ensure generic product lists standardization specifications
- Avoid products without standardization information

## STORAGE AND STABILITY

**Storage Conditions:**
- Store in cool, dry place
- Protect from light and moisture
- Keep in original container with desiccant if provided

**Shelf Life:**
- Typically 2-3 years for tablets/capsules
- Check expiration date on product
- Discard if product appears discolored or has unusual odor

**Travel:**
- Stable at room temperature
- No refrigeration needed
- Keep in original labeled container when traveling

## PREPARATION SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS

### RECOMMENDED (Evidence-Based)

**Standardized Extract Tablets/Capsules:**
- **Dose:** 120-240 mg daily (240 mg for cognitive impairment/dementia)
- **Formulation:** 22-27% flavonoids, 5.4-6.6% terpene lactones, <5 ppm ginkgolic acids
- **Extract ratio:** 35:1 to 67:1
- **Administration:** Once daily or divided doses (BID)
- **Duration:** Minimum 22-26 weeks for cognitive benefits
- **Evidence:** 18+ RCTs, 2,500+ participants, excellent safety profile

---

### NOT RECOMMENDED (Lack of Evidence)

**Tinctures:**
- No clinical trial evidence
- Inadequate standardization
- Ginkgolic acid content unknown (safety risk)
- Cannot achieve therapeutic levels of active compounds

**Teas/Decoctions:**
- No clinical trial evidence
- Very low concentration of active compounds
- High ginkgolic acid content (allergic risk)
- Water extraction inefficient for terpene lactones

**Raw Leaf Powder:**
- Not standardized
- Unknown ginkgolic acid content
- Variable phytochemical composition
- No clinical evidence

---

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Use only standardized extracts** meeting European or US Pharmacopoeia standards (22-27% flavonoids, 5.4-6.6% terpene lactones, <5 ppm ginkgolic acids)

2. **Dose:** 240 mg daily for cognitive impairment/dementia; 120-240 mg for other indications

3. **Duration:** Minimum 22-26 weeks for chronic cognitive benefits; 2.5-6 hours for acute effects

4. **Administration:** Once daily or divided (BID) dosing both acceptable

5. **Avoid tinctures and teas** - no clinical evidence, safety concerns, inadequate standardization

6. **Look for third-party testing** (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab) to ensure product quality

7. **Maximum dose:** 240 mg daily for chronic use (no CYP450 interactions, excellent safety data)
