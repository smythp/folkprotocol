# Ginkgo biloba: Clinical Uses

## Evidence-Based Clinical Applications

### 1. ALZHEIMER'S DISEASE AND DEMENTIA

**Dosage in Trials:** 240 mg daily of standardized Ginkgo biloba extract (EGb 761) combined with donepezil hydrochloride

**Duration:** 22-26 weeks typical trial duration

**Effect Sizes:**
- **Clinical effectiveness rate:** 23% improvement (RR 1.23, 95% CI 1.13-1.34) - 1,642 participants across 18 RCTs
- **Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE):** Improved by 3.02 points (95% CI 2.14-3.89) - 1,642 participants
- **Activity of Daily Living Scale:** Decreased by 4.56 points (95% CI -5.09 to -4.03), indicating functional improvement - 1,642 participants
- **Hasegawa Dementia Scale:** Improved by 2.04 points (95% CI 1.74-2.34) - 1,642 participants
- **Montreal Cognitive Assessment:** 2.38-point improvement (95% CI 0.72-4.06) - 1,642 participants

**Safety:** No statistically significant difference in adverse reactions between Ginkgo + donepezil groups and donepezil alone (RR 0.91, 95% CI 0.58-1.42)

**Source:** Li et al. 2023, Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience (PMID: 36960422)

---

### 2. MILD COGNITIVE IMPAIRMENT (MCI) WITH NEUROPSYCHIATRIC SYMPTOMS

**Dosage in Trials:** 240 mg EGb 761 daily

**Duration:** 24 weeks

**Effect Sizes (160 participants, randomized double-blind trial):**
- **NPI composite score reduction:** 7.0 ± 4.5 points (EGb 761) vs 5.5 ± 5.2 (placebo); p=0.001
- **Clinical improvement rate:** 78.8% on EGb 761 achieved ≥4 point NPI decrease vs 55.7% on placebo (p=0.002)
- **Anxiety scores:** Statistically significant superiority for EGb 761
- **Trail-Making Test (processing speed):** Statistically significant improvement

**Safety:** Safe and well tolerated

**Source:** Gavrilova et al. 2014, International Journal of Geriatric Psychiatry (PMID: 24633934)

---

### 3. COGNITIVE IMPAIRMENT AND DEMENTIA (META-ANALYSIS)

**Dosage in Trials:** 240 mg/day EGb 761

**Duration:** 22-26 weeks

**Effect Sizes (2,561 participants across 9 RCTs):**
- **Primary Finding:** Able to stabilize or slow decline in cognitive impairment and dementia over 22-26 weeks
- **Benefits:** Improvements in cognition, function, and behavior mainly associated with EGb 761 at 240 mg/day
- **Limitation:** Could not demonstrate statistically significant reduction in clinically relevant symptoms compared with placebo

**Safety:** Excellent safety profile; no important safety concerns revealed

**Source:** Tan et al. 2015, Journal of Alzheimer's Disease (PMID: 25114079)

---

### 4. IDIOPATHIC PULMONARY FIBROSIS

**Dosage in Trials:** Ginkgo biloba extract (dose not specified in meta-analysis)

**Duration:** Variable across 14 RCTs

**Effect Sizes (1,043 participants across 14 RCTs):**
- **Arterial oxygen partial pressure:** Increased
- **Arterial oxygen saturation:** Increased
- **Forced vital capacity:** Enhanced
- **Forced expiratory volume in one second:** Enhanced
- **Maximum voluntary ventilation:** Enhanced
- **6-minute walk test:** Performance improved
- **Interleukin-4 levels:** Reduced (inflammatory marker)
- **Hyaluronan levels:** Decreased (fibrosis marker)
- **Laminin levels:** Decreased (fibrosis marker)
- **Arterial carbon dioxide partial pressure:** Reduced

**Conclusion:** GBE may offer therapeutic benefits in IPF by improving respiratory function, modulating inflammation, and affecting fibrosis markers

**Recommendation:** Larger multicenter trials recommended

**Source:** Sun et al. 2025, Frontiers in Pharmacology (PMID: 40110130)

---

### 5. CEREBRAL BLOOD FLOW ENHANCEMENT

**Dosage in Trials:** 60 mg twice daily (120 mg total daily)

**Duration:** 4 weeks

**Effect Sizes (9 healthy elderly men, mean age 61±10 years):**
- **White matter CBF:** 15% global increase (P≤0.0001)
- **Gray matter CBF:** 13% global increase (P≤0.0001)
- **Left parietal-occipital white matter:** Mild localized increase
- **Intrasubject reproducibility:** Standard deviations of 7% in gray matter and 4% in white matter regions

**Study Type:** Pilot study using dynamic susceptibility contrast-enhanced MRI

**Source:** Mashayekh et al. 2011, Neuroradiology (PMID: 21061003)

---

### 6. TINNITUS

**Dosage in Trials:** 120 mg EGb 761 twice daily (240 mg total daily)

**Duration:** 12 weeks

**Effect Sizes (197 participants - 99 EGb 761, 98 pentoxifylline 600 mg BID):**
- **Efficacy:** Both treatments produced significant improvements in tinnitus questionnaire scores
- **Improvements seen in:** Loudness/annoyance scales, anxiety, disability measures
- **Comparative efficacy:** No meaningful difference between EGb 761 and pentoxifylline
- **Adverse events:** 20 in ginkgo group vs 36 in pentoxifylline group
- **Serious adverse events:** None reported

**Safety:** Better tolerability profile for EGb 761 compared to pentoxifylline

**Source:** Procházková et al. 2018, International Journal of Clinical Pharmacy (PMID: 29855986)

---

### 7. INTERMITTENT CLAUDICATION (PERIPHERAL ARTERIAL DISEASE)

**Dosage in Trials:** Variable (meta-analysis of 8 RCTs)

**Duration:** Variable across trials

**Effect Sizes (8 randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind trials):**
- **Pain-free walking distance:** Weighted mean difference of 34 meters (95% CI: 26 to 43 meters) favoring Ginkgo biloba
- **Clinical significance:** Modest and of uncertain clinical relevance
- **Conclusion:** Superior to placebo but effect size is modest

**Source:** Pittler & Ernst 2000, American Journal of Medicine (PMID: 11014719)

---

### 8. PERIPHERAL ARTERY DISEASE (PAD) - NEGATIVE TRIAL

**Dosage in Trials:** 300 mg/day EGb 761

**Duration:** 4 months

**Effect Sizes (62 participants - 31 ginkgo, 31 placebo; mean age 70±8 years):**
- **Maximal walking time:** Placebo increased by 20 ± 80 seconds, Ginkgo by 91 ± 242 seconds (P=0.12, not significant)
- **Pain-free walking time:** Placebo increased by 15 ± 31 seconds, Ginkgo by 21 ± 43 seconds (P=0.28, not significant)
- **Conclusion:** Data do not support use of Ginkgo biloba as effective therapy for PAD

**Source:** Gardner et al. 2008, Journal of Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation and Prevention (PMID: 18628657)

---

### 9. ACUTE COGNITIVE EFFECTS (DOSE-RESPONSE)

**Dosages Tested:** 120 mg, 240 mg, and 360 mg (acute single doses)

**Duration:** Acute administration with assessments at baseline and 1, 2.5, 4, and 6 hours post-dosing

**Effect Sizes (20 healthy young volunteers, placebo-controlled crossover):**
- **Speed of attention factor:** Dose-dependent improvement at 240 mg and 360 mg doses
- **Time course:** Improvement demonstrable at 2.5 hours and persisting through 6 hours compared to placebo
- **Other cognitive factors:** Time- and dose-specific changes (both positive and negative effects)
- **Conclusion:** Acute Ginkgo administration produced sustained improvement in attention in healthy young volunteers

**Dosing Conclusions:** Effects are dose-dependent though not in a linear manner

**Source:** Kennedy et al. 2000, Psychopharmacology (PMID: 11026748)

## AUTHORITATIVE INDICATIONS

### German Commission E (1998)
**Recognized indications for standardized Ginkgo biloba extract:**
- Cerebral insufficiency
- Intermittent claudication
- Vertigo

**Status:** Approved for medical use in Germany as a recognized medicine

---

### World Health Organization (1999)
**Recognized indications for standardized Ginkgo biloba leaf extract:**
- Symptomatic treatment of mild to moderate cerebrovascular insufficiency
- Improvement of pain-free walking distance in patients with peripheral arterial occlusive disease (PAOD)
- Treatment of inner ear problems including tinnitus

**Status:** WHO-recognized medicinal plant monograph, widely cited as authoritative source

---

## EVIDENCE QUALITY SUMMARY

### High-Quality Evidence
- **Cognitive function in dementia:** Multiple large RCTs, meta-analyses with >2,500 participants
- **Safety profile:** Extensive RCT data, >2,500 participants across multiple trials

### Moderate-Quality Evidence
- **Cerebral blood flow improvements:** Pilot studies, small samples (n=9)
- **Neuropsychiatric symptoms in MCI:** Limited RCTs (n=160)

### Low-Quality Evidence
- **Peripheral arterial disease:** Mixed results, modest effect sizes, conflicting findings
- **Tinnitus:** Conflicting results, unclear clinical significance

### Limitations
- Many trials have unclear or high risk of bias
- Heterogeneous protocols across studies
- Limited independent replications of single studies
- Most prevention trials show negative results

---

## CLINICAL DOSING RECOMMENDATIONS

**Primary indication (Cognitive impairment/dementia):** 240 mg daily, divided or once daily, for minimum 22-26 weeks

**MCI with neuropsychiatric symptoms:** 240 mg daily for 24 weeks

**Cerebral blood flow support:** 60 mg twice daily (120 mg total)

**Tinnitus:** 120 mg twice daily (240 mg total)

**Acute cognitive enhancement:** 240-360 mg single dose (effects at 2.5-6 hours)

**Maximum studied dose:** 360 mg (acute), 300 mg daily (chronic)

**Critical requirement:** Use standardized extract meeting European or US Pharmacopoeia standards (EGb 761 or equivalent)
