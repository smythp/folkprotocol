# American Ginseng - Active Compounds and Bioavailability

## Primary Active Constituents: Ginsenosides

### Major Ginsenosides in American Ginseng

**Primary Standardization Markers**:

1. **Ginsenoside Re**
   - One of two main saponins in American ginseng
   - **Higher in P. quinquefolius than P. ginseng** (distinguishing characteristic)
   - Bioavailability: 7.1%
   - Tmax: 1 hour
   - Used for quality control and authentication

2. **Ginsenoside Rb1**
   - One of two main saponins in American ginseng
   - Bioavailability: 1.2-4.3%
   - Tmax: 1.5 hours
   - Used for quality control and authentication

**Other Significant Ginsenosides**:

3. **Ginsenoside Rg1**
   - Bioavailability: 18.4% (highest of the major ginsenosides)
   - Tmax: 1 hour

4. **Ginsenoside Rd**
   - Bioavailability: <5%

5. **Ginsenoside Rb2**
   - Bioavailability: <5%

6. **Ginsenoside Rc**
   - Present in root extracts

7. **Ginsenoside Rf**
   - Present in root extracts

8. **Pseudoginsenoside F11**
   - Found in leaf and flower extracts
   - Identified via LC-QTOF MS/MS analysis

**Source**: LC/MS/MS analysis (PMID: 10221076); Review (PMID: PMC12365553)

## Ginsenoside Profile by Plant Part

### Distribution Analysis (2023)

**LC-QTOF MS/MS Analysis** identified seven main ginsenosides from different plant parts:

- **Root**: Re, Rb1, Rb2, Rb3, Rd, F2
- **Leaf**: Re, Rb1, pseudoginsenoside F11, Rd
- **Flower**: Re, pseudoginsenoside F11

**Clinical Significance**: Root extracts contain the most diverse ginsenoside profile, supporting traditional focus on root preparations.

**Source**: PMID: 36940929

## Standardization in Clinical Products

### Typical Standardization
- **Standard Percentage**: 10% total ginsenosides for extracts
- **Quality Control Markers**: Rb1 and Re (the two main saponins)
- **Clinical Trial Standardization Examples**:
  - Cereboost™: 10.65% ginsenosides (cognitive study)
  - CVT-E002: Proprietary standardized extract (respiratory infection studies)
  - Diabetes studies: 10% ginsenosides

### Importance of Standardization
- American ginseng is "one of the most widely adulterated herbs on market" (AHP, 2012)
- Ginsenoside profile distinguishes American from Asian ginseng
- Re:Rb1 ratio differs between species
- Authentication critical for clinical efficacy

## Bioavailability Data

### Parent Ginsenosides

**Major Limitation**: Low oral bioavailability of most parent compounds

| Ginsenoside | Bioavailability | Tmax | Notes |
|-------------|-----------------|------|-------|
| **Rb1** | 1.2-4.3% | 1.5 h | One of main markers; very low absorption |
| **Rb2** | <5% | - | Poor absorption |
| **Rd** | <5% | - | Poor absorption |
| **Re** | 7.1% | 1 h | Main marker; low but better than Rb ginsenosides |
| **Rg1** | 18.4% | 1 h | Highest bioavailability of major ginsenosides |

**Source**: J Ginseng Res. 2025; PMID: PMC12365553

### Metabolites

**Protopanaxadiol (PPD)**:
- Bioavailability: **36.8%** (significantly higher than parent compounds)
- Metabolite of Rb1, Rb2, Rc, Rd ginsenosides
- May be responsible for therapeutic effects despite low parent compound absorption

**Clinical Implication**: Therapeutic effects may depend on gut bacterial conversion of parent ginsenosides to more bioavailable metabolites.

## Factors Affecting Bioavailability

### Processing Methods

**Effect on Pharmacokinetics** (2018 study):
- Different processing methods affect pharmacokinetics and oral bioavailability
- Processing impacts tissue concentrations of Rg1, Re, Rd, and Rb1
- **Clinical Significance**: Processing method matters for bioavailability

**Source**: PMID: 29348719

### Formulation Studies

**Ultrafine Granular Powder Study** (2016):

**Study Design**: Systematic study comparing dissolution and bioavailability in beagles
- Ultrafine granular powder
- Common powder
- Traditional pieces

**Results**:
- **Ultrafine granular powder had highest bioavailability**
- Greatest extent and fastest dissolution in vitro
- Improved formulations could facilitate dissolution and promote absorption

**Clinical Implication**: Particle size and formulation significantly impact absorption; explains variability in clinical outcomes with different products.

**Source**: J Ethnopharmacol. 2016; https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0378874116301386

## Analytical Methods for Quality Control

### HPLC Methods

**Standard Method** (2005):
- **Method**: HPLC with UV photodiode array detection
- **Ginsenosides Analyzed**: Rb1, Rb2, Rc, Rd, Re, Rf, Rg1
- **Results**:
  - Gradient HPLC method developed for separation and quantification
  - All ginseng saponins baseline-resolved
  - Detection limits of 1.0 µg/mL or less
- **Application**: Standard analytical method for American ginseng quality control

**Source**: PMID: 16042154

### LC-MS/MS Methods

**Ginsenoside Identification** (1999):
- **Method**: LC/MS/MS
- **Key Finding**: Ginsenosides Re and Rb1 identified as two main ginseng saponins in American ginseng root
- **Application**: Product authentication and quality control

**Source**: PMID: 10221076

### Authentication Methods

**Differentiation from Asian Ginseng** (2000):
- **Method**: HPLC/MS
- **Purpose**: Differentiate P. ginseng from P. quinquefolius
- **Importance**: Detecting adulteration (widespread problem)
- **Application**: Addresses widespread adulteration issue in American ginseng products

**Source**: PMID: 10740871

### Comprehensive LC-QTOF MS/MS Analysis

**Multi-Part Analysis** (2023):
- **Method**: LC-QTOF MS/MS
- **Plant Parts Analyzed**: Root, leaf, flower
- **Ginsenosides Identified**: Re, Rb1, pseudoginsenoside F11, Rb2, Rb3, Rd, F2
- **Additional Finding**: Coronary disease improvement potential assessed

**Source**: PMID: 36940929

## Mechanisms of Action (Based on Active Compounds)

### Ginsenoside-Mediated Effects

**Nitric Oxide Production**:
- Increase in serum NOx: +1.85±2.13 µmol/L (p<0.03) in diabetic patients
- Significant increase in endothelium-derived nitric oxide levels (2025 meta-analysis)
- Correlates with improved flow-mediated dilation (FMD)
- Mechanism for cardiovascular benefits

**Glucose Metabolism**:
- Effects independent of blood glucose changes in cognitive studies
- Postprandial glucose reduction in time-dependent manner
- Suggests multiple mechanisms beyond simple glucose modulation

**Anti-Inflammatory Properties**:
- Traditional "cooling" property
- May relate to modulation of inflammatory pathways

## Clinical Implications of Bioavailability Data

### Low Parent Compound Bioavailability

**Challenge**: "Low bioavailability of ginsenosides is major hurdle for clinical advancement"

**Explanations for Clinical Efficacy Despite Low Bioavailability**:

1. **Metabolite Activity**: PPD (36.8% bioavailability) may be primary active form
2. **Gut Microbiome Conversion**: Individual differences in gut bacteria may explain variable responses
3. **Local Gut Effects**: Low systemic absorption doesn't preclude local GI activity
4. **Formulation Quality**: Clinical trials used specific standardized extracts; commercial products vary widely

### Product Selection Considerations

**For Clinical Use**:
1. **Standardization**: Look for 10% ginsenosides (matches clinical trial products)
2. **Markers**: Rb1 and Re content specified
3. **Form**: Extracts show better bioavailability than crude powder
4. **Processing**: Ultrafine powder > standard powder > whole pieces
5. **Authentication**: Third-party testing for adulteration
6. **Species Verification**: Ensure P. quinquefolius (not P. ginseng substitution)
