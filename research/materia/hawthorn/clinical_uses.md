# Hawthorn (Crataegus spp.) - Clinical Uses

## Heart Failure (NYHA Class II-III)

### Meta-Analysis Evidence (Pittler 2003)
- **Study details:** 8 RCTs analyzed, 632 patients total
- **Patient population:** Chronic heart failure NYHA classes I-III
- **Key findings:**
  - Maximal workload increased by weighted mean of **7 Watts** (95% CI: 3-11 Watts; P < 0.01; n=310)
  - Pressure-heart rate product decreased by weighted mean of **-20** (95% CI: -32 to -8; n=264)
  - Dyspnea and fatigue improved significantly with hawthorn vs placebo
  - Adverse events infrequent, mild, and transient (nausea, dizziness, GI complaints)
- **Conclusion:** Significant benefit from hawthorn extract as adjunctive treatment for chronic heart failure
- **Source:** Pittler MH, Schmidt K, Ernst E. Am J Med. 2003;114(8):665-674. PMID: 12798455

### SPICE Trial (Holubarsch 2008) - Largest RCT
- **Study design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter
- **Sample size:** 2,681 patients (1,338 active, 1,343 placebo)
- **Patient population:** NYHA class II or III heart failure with LVEF ≤35%
- **Dosing:**
  - Crataegus Special Extract WS 1442: **900 mg/day**
  - Standardization: 84.3 mg oligomeric procyanidins
  - Duration: **24 months**
- **Primary endpoint:** Time to first cardiac event
  - WS 1442: 620 days average; **27.9% event rate**
  - Placebo: 606 days average; **28.9% event rate**
  - Hazard ratio: 0.95 (95% CI: 0.82-1.10), p=0.476
  - **Result:** Not statistically significant
- **Secondary findings:**
  - Cardiac mortality trended lower with WS 1442 (9.7% at month 24) but not significant (p=0.269)
  - **In patients with LVEF ≥25%:** WS 1442 reduced sudden cardiac death by **39.7%** (HR 0.59; p=0.025)
- **Safety:** Adverse events comparable in both groups
- **Source:** Holubarsch CJF, et al. Eur J Heart Fail. 2008;10(12):1255-1263. PMID: 19019730

### HERB CHF Trial (Zick 2009) - Negative Results
- **Study design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Sample size:** 120 patients
- **Patient population:** Ages ≥18 years with NYHA class II-III heart failure on standard medical therapy
- **Dosing:**
  - Hawthorn extract: **450 mg twice daily**
  - Duration: **6 months**
- **Primary outcome:** Change in 6-minute walk distance at 6 months
  - **Result:** No significant difference (P = 0.61)
- **Secondary outcomes:**
  - Quality of life: no significant differences
  - Functional capacity: no significant differences
  - Neurohormones: no significant differences
  - Oxidative stress: no significant differences
  - Inflammation: no significant differences
  - Modest improvement in LVEF favored hawthorn (P = 0.04)
- **Adverse events:** More adverse events in hawthorn group (P = 0.02), though most non-cardiac
- **Conclusion:** Hawthorn provides no symptomatic or functional benefit when given with standard medical therapy
- **Note:** This negative trial represents contemporary evidence-based heart failure therapy context
- **Source:** Zick SM, et al. Eur J Heart Fail. 2009;11(10):990-999. PMID: 19789403

### Degenring 2003 - Fresh Berry Extract
- **Study design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Sample size:** 143 patients (69 active, 74 placebo)
- **Demographics:** 72 men, 71 women; mean age 64.8 ± 8.0 years
- **Patient population:** NYHA class II heart failure
- **Dosing:**
  - Preparation: Fresh Crataegus berry extract (Crataegisan)
  - Dose: **3 times 30 drops daily**
  - Duration: **8 weeks**
- **Primary outcome:** Exercise tolerance via bicycle exercise testing
  - **Result:** **8.3-watt advantage** over placebo (p = 0.045)
  - Clinical effect: Dyspnoea and fatigue occur at significantly higher wattage
- **Secondary outcomes:** Blood pressure-heart rate product showed improvement favoring Crataegus (not statistically significant)
- **Tolerability:** Good tolerability and patient acceptance
- **Conclusion:** NYHA II patients may expect improvement in heart failure condition under long-term therapy
- **Source:** Degenring FH, et al. Phytomedicine. 2003;10(5):363-369. PMID: 12833999

### Leuchtgens 1993 - WS 1442 Study
- **Study design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Sample size:** 30 patients
- **Patient population:** Stage NYHA II cardiac insufficiency
- **Dosing:**
  - Crataegus Special Extract WS 1442: **1 capsule twice daily**
  - Duration: **8 weeks**
- **Primary measures:**
  - Pressure-rate product changes during bicycle ergometer testing (50W load)
  - Subjective symptom improvement score from questionnaire
- **Key findings:**
  - Statistically significant advantage over placebo in PRP changes
  - Improvements in heart rate
  - Mild reductions in systolic and diastolic blood pressure in both groups
- **Adverse events:** None in either group
- **Source:** Leuchtgens H. Fortschr Med. 1993;111(20-21):352-354. PMID: 8375791

### WS 1442 Benefit-Risk Assessment (Holubarsch 2018)
- **Patients exposed:** >1,300 in clinical trials (includes 2-year mortality study)
- **Patient population:** Heart failure NYHA classes II-III
- **Efficacy outcomes:**
  - Increased functional capacity
  - Alleviated disabling symptoms
  - Improved health-related quality of life
  - Enhanced maximum workload vs placebo
  - Reduced von Zerssen Complaints scores
  - Improved Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire metrics
- **Safety:**
  - Drug interactions: None observed
  - Specific adverse reactions: None known to date
  - Profile: Very favorable safety profile as monotherapy and add-on therapy
- **Conclusion:** Benefit-risk assessment for WS 1442 is positive
- **Source:** Holubarsch CJF, Colucci WS, Eha J. Am J Cardiovasc Drugs. 2018;18(1):25-36. PMID: 29080984

## Hypertension (Mild)

### Blood Pressure Meta-Analysis (Szikora 2025)
- **Study details:** 6 RCTs, 428 patients total
- **Registration:** PROSPERO ID CRD42024557170 (June 13, 2024)
- **Key findings:**
  - Evaluated antihypertensive effects and safety of hawthorn in RCTs
  - Demonstrated **clinically significant blood pressure reduction**
- **Source:** Szikora Z, et al. Pharmaceuticals. 2025;18(7):1027. DOI: 10.3390/ph18071027

### Systematic Review (Cloud 2020)
- **Databases searched:** AMED, CINAHL, EMBASE, PubMed, Cochrane Central (up to September 2016)
- **Key findings:**
  - Hawthorn can significantly lower blood pressure in people with mild hypertension
  - **Minimum treatment duration of 12 weeks required for effect**
  - Studies were few and too heterogeneous to allow meta-analysis
- **Source:** Cloud AME, Vilcins D, McEwen BJ. Adv Integr Med. 2020;7(3):167-175

## Dosing Ranges from Clinical Studies

### Heart Failure Dosing
- **WS 1442:**
  - 450 mg twice daily (900 mg/day)
  - 900 mg/day for 24 months (SPICE trial)
- **LI 132:**
  - 600 mg daily (3 dragées)
  - 160-1800 mg daily range across studies
- **Fresh berry extract (Crataegisan):**
  - 3 times 30 drops daily for 8 weeks

### Treatment Duration
- **Minimum effective:** 12 weeks for blood pressure effects
- **Typical trials:** 8 weeks to 6 months
- **Long-term studies:** Up to 24 months
- **Onset:** Slow-acting herb requiring at least 4-8 weeks for full benefit

## Regulatory Approvals

### German Commission E (1978)
- **Approved indication:** Mild congestive heart failure and nervous heart complaints
- **Specific approval:** Decreasing cardiac output as described in functional stage II of NYHA
- **Approved parts:** Leaf with flower (combined)
- **Note:** Limited scientific support for berry, flower, or leaves alone (as of 1993)

## Clinical Evidence Summary

### Efficacy
- Benefit demonstrated in older trials and meta-analyses for NYHA I-II heart failure
- Recent HERB CHF trial (2009) showed **no benefit with contemporary standard therapy**
- SPICE trial showed no significant benefit on primary endpoint but subgroup benefits (LVEF ≥25%)
- Blood pressure reduction in mild hypertension with ≥12 weeks treatment

### Safety Profile
- **Total patients in safety studies:** >7,000 across multiple studies
- Very favorable safety profile
- Well-tolerated in studies up to 24 months
- Adverse events infrequent, mild to moderate
- No drug interactions observed in trials
- No reproductive toxicity in animal studies at 56x human dose

### Adverse Events (Daniele 2006 Safety Review)
- **Study details:** 24 trials, 5,577 patients, 3 to 24 weeks duration
- **Dose range:** 160-1800 mg daily
- **Total adverse events:** 166
- **Event breakdown:**
  - Gastrointestinal: 24
  - Dizziness/vertigo: 15
  - Palpitations: 11
  - Headache: 9
  - Migraine: 8
  - Severity: Mostly mild to moderate; 8 severe events with LI 132 extract
- **Conclusion:** Hawthorn is well tolerated; some severe adverse events reported suggest further studies needed
- **Source:** Daniele C, et al. Drug Saf. 2006;29(6):523-535. PMID: 16752934

## Research Gaps

1. Efficacy in context of contemporary evidence-based heart failure therapy unclear
2. Optimal effective dosage not fully established
3. Need for more studies on blood pressure effects with adequate sample sizes
4. Standardized extracts (WS 1442, LI 132) show more consistent results than unstandardized preparations
