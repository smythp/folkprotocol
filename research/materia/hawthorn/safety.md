# Hawthorn (Crataegus spp.) - Safety Profile

## Overview

Hawthorn has a very favorable safety profile based on extensive clinical research. Over 7,000 patients have been studied across multiple clinical trials with durations up to 24 months.

## Comprehensive Safety Evidence

### Systematic Safety Review (Daniele 2006)
**Study scope:**
- Trials analyzed: 24
- Total patients: 5,577
- Duration range: 3 to 24 weeks
- Dose range: 160-1800 mg daily

**Total adverse events:** 166

**Adverse event breakdown:**
- Gastrointestinal: 24
- Dizziness/vertigo: 15
- Palpitations: 11
- Headache: 9
- Migraine: 8

**Severity:**
- Mostly mild to moderate
- 8 severe events reported with LI 132 extract

**Conclusion:** Hawthorn is well tolerated; some severe adverse events reported suggest further studies needed

**Source:** Daniele C, et al. Drug Saf. 2006;29(6):523-535. PMID: 16752934

### WS 1442 Safety Profile (Holubarsch 2018)
**Study population:**
- Patients exposed: >1,300 in clinical trials
- Includes 2-year mortality study
- Patient population: Heart failure NYHA classes II-III

**Safety findings:**
- **Drug interactions:** None observed
- **Specific adverse reactions:** None known to date
- **Profile:** Very favorable safety profile as monotherapy and add-on therapy

**Conclusion:** Benefit-risk assessment for WS 1442 is positive

**Source:** Holubarsch CJF, et al. Am J Cardiovasc Drugs. 2018;18(1):25-36. PMID: 29080984

### Meta-Analysis Safety Data (Pittler 2003)
**Study details:**
- 8 RCTs analyzed, 632 patients
- Patient population: Chronic heart failure NYHA classes I-III

**Adverse events:**
- Infrequent, mild, and transient
- Common events: nausea, dizziness, GI complaints

**Source:** Pittler MH, et al. Am J Med. 2003;114(8):665-674. PMID: 12798455

### SPICE Trial Safety (Holubarsch 2008)
**Study details:**
- Largest trial: 2,681 patients
- Duration: 24 months
- Dose: 900 mg/day WS 1442

**Safety findings:**
- Adverse events comparable in both active and placebo groups
- No significant safety concerns identified

**Source:** Holubarsch CJF, et al. Eur J Heart Fail. 2008;10(12):1255-1263. PMID: 19019730

### HERB CHF Trial Safety (Zick 2009)
**Study details:**
- 120 patients
- Duration: 6 months
- Dose: 450 mg twice daily

**Adverse events:**
- More adverse events in hawthorn group (P = 0.02)
- Most adverse events were non-cardiac

**Source:** Zick SM, et al. Eur J Heart Fail. 2009;11(10):990-999. PMID: 19789403

### Leuchtgens 1993 Safety
**Study details:**
- 30 patients, 8 weeks, WS 1442

**Adverse events:** None in either hawthorn or placebo group

**Source:** Leuchtgens H. Fortschr Med. 1993;111(20-21):352-354. PMID: 8375791

## Reproductive Toxicity Studies

### Yao 2008 Reproductive Screening
**Animal model:** Pregnant rats

**Dosing:**
- Dose level: 56 times the human dose
- Route: Daily gavage
- Test periods:
  - GD 1-8 (early pregnancy)
  - GD 8-15 (organogenesis period)

**Evaluation:**
- In vivo: Fetuses examined GD 20 for external, internal, skeletal malformations
- In vitro: Rat embryos cultured with hawthorn extract for 26 hours

**Results:** Hawthorn did not have adverse effect on embryonic development in vivo or in vitro

**Interpretation:** Safety at recommended doses may be due to low bioavailability of some constituents when taken orally

**Recommendation:** Pharmacokinetic studies recommended to verify safety in humans

**Source:** Yao M, et al. J Ethnopharmacol. 2008;118(1):127-132. PMID: 18485639

## Contraindications

### Pregnancy
- **Status:** Clinically contraindicated (precautionary)
- **Rationale:** Despite no reproductive toxicity at 56x human dose in rats, insufficient human safety data
- **Recommendation:** Avoid use during pregnancy until human pharmacokinetic and safety studies completed

### Lactation
- **Status:** Insufficient data
- **Recommendation:** Avoid use during breastfeeding due to lack of safety data

### Cardiac Conditions
- **Consideration:** While hawthorn is used for heart conditions, medical supervision recommended for:
  - Severe heart failure (NYHA IV)
  - Acute cardiac events
  - Recent myocardial infarction
  - Unstable angina

### Known Hypersensitivity
- Avoid in patients with known allergies to Crataegus species or related plants (Rosaceae family)

## Drug Interactions

### Clinical Trial Evidence
**WS 1442 Studies (Holubarsch 2018):**
- **Drug interactions observed:** None
- **Patient population:** >1,300 patients on standard cardiac medications
- **Duration:** Up to 24 months
- **Conclusion:** No drug interactions observed in clinical trials

### Theoretical Interactions (Caution Advised)

#### Cardiovascular Medications
**Consideration:** Hawthorn has cardiovascular effects; theoretical potential for interactions with:

1. **Cardiac glycosides (Digoxin)**
   - Theoretical concern: Additive positive inotropic effects
   - Clinical evidence: None observed in trials
   - Recommendation: Monitor if used concurrently

2. **Beta-blockers**
   - Theoretical concern: Additive cardiac effects
   - Clinical evidence: Used safely in combination in trials (SPICE, HERB CHF)
   - Recommendation: Generally considered safe under medical supervision

3. **ACE inhibitors**
   - Theoretical concern: Additive blood pressure effects
   - Clinical evidence: Used safely in combination in heart failure trials
   - Recommendation: Generally considered safe under medical supervision

4. **Antihypertensive medications**
   - Theoretical concern: Additive blood pressure lowering
   - Clinical evidence: Blood pressure effects require ≥12 weeks; gradual onset
   - Recommendation: Monitor blood pressure if used concurrently

5. **Nitrates**
   - Theoretical concern: Additive vasodilatory effects
   - Clinical evidence: Limited data
   - Recommendation: Use with caution; monitor for hypotension

#### P-Glycoprotein Substrates
- Vitexin-2''-O-rhamnoside absorption mediated by P-glycoprotein
- Theoretical potential for interactions with P-gp substrates
- Clinical significance unknown

### No Interactions Found
Based on clinical trial data (>7,000 patient exposures):
- No significant drug interactions documented in clinical trials
- Patients typically on standard heart failure medications (ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, diuretics)

## Adverse Events by Frequency

### Common (>1%)
Based on pooled safety data from 5,577 patients (Daniele 2006):
- Gastrointestinal symptoms: 0.43% (24/5577)
- Dizziness/vertigo: 0.27% (15/5577)
- Palpitations: 0.20% (11/5577)
- Headache: 0.16% (9/5577)
- Migraine: 0.14% (8/5577)

### Severity Classification
- **Mild to moderate:** Majority of adverse events
- **Severe:** 8 events total with LI 132 extract (specific events not detailed in available data)

### Comparison to Placebo
- SPICE trial (2,681 patients): Adverse events comparable in hawthorn and placebo groups
- HERB CHF trial (120 patients): More adverse events in hawthorn group (P = 0.02), but most non-cardiac
- Leuchtgens 1993 (30 patients): No adverse events in either group

## Toxicology

### General Toxicity
**Cui 2024 Review:** "Hawthorn lacks toxicity"
- Based on comprehensive review of 337 identified compounds (1952-2023)
- No significant toxicity identified in pharmacological studies

**Source:** Cui M, et al. J Ethnopharmacol. 2024;319(Pt 2):117229. PMID: 37788786

### Reproductive Toxicity
- **No adverse effects** at 56x human dose in pregnant rats
- No external, internal, or skeletal malformations observed
- No embryotoxicity in cultured rat embryos

## Long-Term Safety

### Duration of Safe Use
- **Up to 24 months:** Well-tolerated in SPICE trial (2,681 patients)
- **6 months:** Safe in HERB CHF trial (120 patients)
- **8 weeks:** Multiple trials show good tolerability

### Chronic Use Considerations
- Slow-acting herb requiring 4-8 weeks for full benefit
- Long-term use appears safe based on clinical trial data
- No cumulative toxicity observed

## Special Populations

### Elderly
- **Heart failure trials:** Included elderly patients (mean age ~65 years in Degenring 2003)
- **Safety:** No age-related safety concerns identified
- **Efficacy:** Primary population for heart failure studies

### Pediatric
- **Data:** Insufficient safety data in children
- **Recommendation:** Not recommended for pediatric use without medical supervision

### Hepatic Impairment
- **Data:** Limited specific data
- **Pharmacology:** Hepatoprotective properties noted in comprehensive review
- **Recommendation:** Use with caution; no dose adjustment guidance available

### Renal Impairment
- **Data:** Limited specific data
- **Bioavailability:** Low oral bioavailability of most compounds suggests limited renal concerns
- **Recommendation:** Use with caution; no dose adjustment guidance available

## Safety Summary

### Well-Established Safety Profile
✓ Over 7,000 patients studied in clinical trials
✓ Duration up to 24 months well-tolerated
✓ Adverse events infrequent, mild to moderate
✓ No drug interactions observed in clinical trials
✓ No reproductive toxicity at high doses (animal studies)
✓ "Lacks toxicity" per comprehensive phytochemical review

### Precautions
⚠ Contraindicated in pregnancy (precautionary, insufficient human data)
⚠ Not recommended during lactation (insufficient data)
⚠ Medical supervision advised for severe cardiac conditions
⚠ Monitor for additive effects with cardiovascular medications (theoretical)
⚠ Avoid in known hypersensitivity to Rosaceae family

### Areas Needing More Research
- Human pharmacokinetic data (mostly rat studies)
- Safety in pregnancy and lactation (human data)
- Pediatric safety data
- Long-term safety beyond 24 months
- Specific drug interaction studies

## Comparison: WS 1442 vs LI 132 Safety

### WS 1442
- Very favorable safety profile
- No specific adverse reactions known
- No drug interactions observed
- Studied in >1,300 patients
- Largest trial: 2,681 patients for 24 months

### LI 132
- Generally well-tolerated
- 8 severe adverse events reported (details not fully specified)
- Dose range: 160-1800 mg daily
- Higher doses may be associated with increased adverse events

### Recommendation
WS 1442 has more extensive and consistent safety data compared to LI 132.
