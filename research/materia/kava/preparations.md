# Kava (Piper methysticum) - Preparations

## Standardized Extract Doses

### Clinical Trial Dosing Regimens

Clinical evidence for specific standardized extract doses:

#### Aqueous Extracts

**Sarris et al. 2013 - Positive Results:**
- **Extract Type:** Aqueous kava extract
- **Dosing:** 120-240 mg kavalactones per day (response-adjusted)
- **Administration:** Divided doses throughout the day
- **Duration:** 6 weeks
- **Results:** Significant anxiety reduction (p=0.046, d=0.62), 26% remission vs 6% placebo

**Sarris et al. 2020 - Negative Results:**
- **Extract Type:** Aqueous extract of dried kava root
- **Dosing:** 120 mg kavalactones twice daily (240 mg/day total)
- **Administration:** Twice daily
- **Duration:** 16 weeks
- **Results:** No significant advantage over placebo, increased adverse events

**KADSS Trial 2009 - Strong Positive Results:**
- **Extract Type:** Aqueous extract
- **Dosing:** Five tablets daily containing 250 mg kavalactones per day
- **Administration:** Divided doses (five tablets)
- **Duration:** 3 weeks per phase
- **Results:** Highly significant (p<0.0001, d=2.24), Hamilton Anxiety Scale reduction -9.9 to -10.3 points

#### WS1490 Acetone Extract (Pharmaceutical Preparation)

**Gastpar & Klimm 2003 - Multicenter Trial:**
- **Extract Type:** WS 1490
- **Dosing:** 3 × 50 mg per day (150 mg/day total)
- **Administration:** Three times daily
- **Duration:** 4 weeks
- **Results:** 73% improved vs 56% placebo (p<0.01)

**Lehrl 2004 - Sleep Study:**
- **Extract Type:** WS 1490
- **Dosing:** 200 mg daily
- **Administration:** Once daily (timing not specified)
- **Duration:** 4 weeks
- **Results:** Significant improvement in sleep quality (p=0.007) and recuperative effect (p=0.018)

**Volz & Kieser 1997 - Long-Term Trial:**
- **Extract Type:** WS 1490
- **Dosing:** Not specified in abstract
- **Administration:** Not specified
- **Duration:** 25 weeks
- **Results:** Significant superiority starting week 8, no tolerance

### Standardization Requirements

**General Requirements:**
- Products should be standardized to total kavalactone content
- Typical standardization: 30-70% kavalactones
- Individual kavalactone profile varies by cultivar

**Quality Markers:**
- Total kavalactone content (primary marker)
- Profile of six major kavalactones (kavain, dihydrokavain, methysticin, dihydromethysticin, yangonin, desmethoxyyangonin)
- Source cultivar documentation
- Plant part verification (rhizomes and roots only)

## Traditional Tea Preparation

### Aqueous (Water) Extraction Method

**Traditional Preparation:**
- **Plant Material:** Peeled and dried rhizomes and roots of noble kava cultivar
- **Method:** Grinding or pounding the root, then mixing with cold water
- **Extraction:** Cold water extraction (hot water may degrade some kavalactones)
- **Straining:** Traditionally strained through cloth to remove fibrous material

**Modern Tea Preparation:**
Based on traditional methods and clinical evidence:

1. **Material Amount:** Calculate based on desired kavalactone content
   - For 120 mg kavalactones from 30% standardized material: ~400 mg root material
   - For 250 mg kavalactones from 30% standardized material: ~830 mg root material

2. **Water Temperature:** Cold to room temperature (traditional method)
   - Hot water may reduce kavalactone content
   - Some modern preparations use warm water for palatability

3. **Steeping Time:** Variable, typically 10-30 minutes with agitation

4. **Consumption:** Consumed as beverage, typically on empty stomach for better absorption

**Dosing Considerations:**
- Kavalactone content varies widely based on:
  - Source cultivar and growing conditions
  - Plant age (at least 5 years old preferred)
  - Extraction efficiency
  - Storage conditions of plant material

**Traditional Beverage Use:**
- FAO/WHO 2016 review: Traditional and recreational beverage consumption has long safety record
- Traditional Pacific Island preparation: Variable strength depending on cultural context
- Modern therapeutic use: Requires standardization for consistent dosing

## Tincture Preparations

### Alcohol Extraction Ratios

**Note:** The bibliography does not provide specific tincture ratios from clinical evidence. Traditional herbal medicine practice and the pharmacokinetic data suggest the following:

**Typical Herbal Medicine Tincture Ratios:**
- 1:2 (herb:alcohol) for fresh root
- 1:5 (herb:alcohol) for dried root
- Alcohol percentage: 40-60% typically used for kava

**Safety Considerations:**
- **Ethanolic extracts may have higher hepatotoxicity risk**
- Sarris et al. 2011 recommends aqueous extracts over ethanolic
- Alcohol consumption should be avoided with kava use
- Tinctures introduce alcohol, which is specifically contraindicated

**Dosing Tinctures:**
- Must be standardized to kavalactone content for consistent therapeutic dosing
- Calculate dose based on total kavalactone content
- Example: If tincture contains 30 mg kavalactones per ml, 4-8 ml would provide 120-240 mg kavalactones

**Clinical Evidence Gap:**
- Most positive clinical trials used aqueous or acetone extracts, not alcohol tinctures
- Ethanolic preparations less studied in modern clinical trials
- Safety profile of ethanolic extracts less favorable than aqueous

## Capsule and Tablet Preparations

### Standardized Extract Capsules

**Common Formats:**
- Capsules containing standardized extract powder
- Tablets containing compressed standardized extract
- Standardized to specific kavalactone content per unit

**Typical Commercial Preparations:**
- 50-100 mg kavalactones per capsule/tablet
- 2-3 capsules/tablets per day to achieve therapeutic dose (120-250 mg total)

**Clinical Trial Formats:**

**KADSS 2009:**
- Five tablets daily
- Each tablet portion providing ~50 mg kavalactones
- Total 250 mg kavalactones per day

**Sarris et al. 2020:**
- Twice daily dosing
- 120 mg kavalactones per dose
- Total 240 mg per day

**Gastpar & Klimm 2003:**
- Three times daily
- 50 mg per dose
- Total 150 mg per day

### Powder Preparations

**Root Powder:**
- Ground dried kava root
- Variable kavalactone content (typically 3-10% in raw root)
- Requires larger doses to achieve therapeutic kavalactone levels
- Example: 4-8 grams root powder for 120-250 mg kavalactones (assuming 3% content)

**Standardized Extract Powder:**
- Concentrated extract standardized to 30-70% kavalactones
- Can be encapsulated or mixed with liquid
- Easier to achieve consistent therapeutic dosing

## Administration Timing and Frequency

### Timing Relative to Food

**Kanumuri et al. 2022 Pharmacokinetic Study:**
- **Finding:** Food significantly reduced absorption extent
- **Recommendation:** Consider taking on empty stomach for optimal absorption
- **Clinical Implication:** Take 30-60 minutes before meals or 2 hours after meals

### Dosing Frequency

**Clinical Trial Patterns:**

**Once Daily:**
- Lehrl 2004: 200 mg WS1490 once daily for sleep
- Simpler dosing schedule
- May have less stable blood levels

**Twice Daily:**
- Sarris et al. 2020: 120 mg twice daily (240 mg total)
- Balanced dosing schedule
- More stable blood levels throughout day

**Three Times Daily:**
- Gastpar & Klimm 2003: 50 mg three times daily (150 mg total)
- Kanumuri et al. 2022: 75 mg three times daily in PK study
- Most frequent dosing
- Most stable blood levels but less convenient

**Five Times Daily:**
- KADSS 2009: Five tablets daily (250 mg total)
- Highest frequency
- May be impractical for long-term use

### Single Dose vs. Divided Dosing

**Kanumuri et al. 2022 Findings:**
- Higher overall exposure observed with single 225 mg dose versus distributed 3 × 75 mg dosing
- Single dose may be more efficient for immediate effects
- Divided dosing may provide more sustained effects throughout day

## Onset and Duration of Effects

### Time to Peak Plasma Levels

**Kanumuri et al. 2022:**
- **Time to Maximum Concentration:** 1-3 hours after oral administration
- Immediate pharmacological effects within this timeframe

### Time to Clinical Effects

**Clinical Trial Evidence:**
- **Onset of anxiolytic effect:** Typically 1-8 weeks of regular use
- **Volz & Kieser 1997:** Significant superiority starting from week 8
- **White 2018:** Results don't appear immediately; optimal dose and dosing schedule needs determination

**Implication:**
- Acute anxiolytic effects may occur within hours
- Maximum therapeutic benefit requires sustained use over weeks
- Not suitable for immediate anxiety relief like benzodiazepines
- Better suited for ongoing anxiety management

## Preparation Quality Factors

### Source Material Standards

**Teschke & Lebot 2011 Six-Point Plan:**

1. **Cultivar:** Noble kava cultivar (e.g., Borogu)
2. **Plant Age:** At least 5 years old at harvest
3. **Plant Parts:** Peeled and dried rhizomes and roots only
4. **Extraction:** Aqueous extraction recommended
5. **Dosage:** ≤250 mg kavalactones per day
6. **Research:** Systematic rigorous future research

### Extraction Method Comparison

**Aqueous (Water) Extraction:**
- **Advantages:** Traditional method, best safety profile, recommended by experts
- **Disadvantages:** Variable kavalactone content, mixed clinical efficacy results
- **Best For:** Safety-conscious use, traditional preparation alignment

**Acetone Extraction (WS1490):**
- **Advantages:** Most studied formulation, consistent positive results, standardized pharmaceutical preparation
- **Disadvantages:** Not traditional method, requires pharmaceutical manufacturing
- **Best For:** Evidence-based therapeutic use with established efficacy

**Ethanolic (Alcohol) Extraction:**
- **Advantages:** Common in commercial products, efficient extraction
- **Disadvantages:** May have higher hepatotoxicity risk, alcohol contradicts kava safety guidelines
- **Best For:** Not recommended as first choice; aqueous or acetone preferred

**CO2 Extraction:**
- Not extensively studied in clinical trials
- May offer purity advantages
- Insufficient evidence to assess efficacy and safety

### Storage and Stability

**Plant Material:**
- Store dried rhizomes and roots in cool, dark, dry conditions
- Avoid exposure to light, heat, and moisture
- Kavalactones may degrade over time

**Prepared Extracts:**
- Follow manufacturer storage instructions
- Generally store in cool, dark, dry place
- Check expiration dates

## Dosing Protocols Based on Clinical Evidence

### For Generalized Anxiety Disorder

**Conservative Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 120 mg kavalactones per day
- **Duration:** 6 weeks
- **Administration:** Divided doses, on empty stomach
- **Based on:** Sarris et al. 2013 (positive results, good safety)

**Standard Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 150-250 mg kavalactones per day
- **Duration:** 4-6 weeks
- **Administration:** Divided into 2-3 doses daily, on empty stomach
- **Based on:** Multiple positive trials (KADSS, Gastpar & Klimm, meta-analyses)

**Response-Adjusted Protocol:**
- **Starting Dose:** 120 mg kavalactones per day
- **Adjustment:** Increase to 240 mg per day based on response after 2-3 weeks
- **Duration:** 6 weeks
- **Based on:** Sarris et al. 2013 protocol

### For Sleep Disturbances with Anxiety

**Protocol:**
- **Dose:** 200 mg WS1490 daily
- **Duration:** 4 weeks
- **Administration:** Single daily dose (evening timing suggested though not specified)
- **Based on:** Lehrl 2004

### Maximum Safe Dosing

**Conservative Maximum:**
- **Dose:** ≤120 mg kavalactones per day
- **Duration:** ≤3 months
- **Based on:** Teschke et al. 2008 (safest parameters from hepatotoxicity analysis)

**Standard Maximum:**
- **Dose:** ≤250 mg kavalactones per day
- **Duration:** ≤3 months
- **Based on:** Teschke & Lebot 2011 recommendations

## Preparation Selection Guide

### For Maximum Efficacy (Evidence-Based)

**Recommendation:** WS1490 acetone extract
- Most consistent positive clinical results
- Standardized pharmaceutical preparation
- Well-studied in multiple RCTs and meta-analyses
- Doses: 150-200 mg per day

### For Maximum Safety

**Recommendation:** Aqueous extract from noble cultivar
- Traditional preparation method
- Best safety profile
- Recommended by experts (Sarris et al. 2011, Teschke & Lebot 2011)
- Doses: 120-250 mg kavalactones per day
- Variable efficacy in trials

### For Traditional Use

**Recommendation:** Cold water extraction of dried peeled rhizomes and roots
- Aligns with traditional Pacific Island preparation
- Noble cultivar (e.g., Borogu)
- At least 5 years old at harvest
- Variable kavalactone content requires careful calculation

### Preparations to Avoid

**Not Recommended:**
1. Products containing aerial parts (leaves, stems)
2. Products from non-noble cultivars or unknown source
3. Products without kavalactone standardization
4. Alcohol extracts (tinctures) due to safety concerns
5. Products with unclear extraction methods
6. Products from plants <5 years old
7. Products with unpeeled or unprepared root material

## Special Considerations

### Synergistic Effects

**Whole Extract vs. Isolated Compounds:**
- Drug Metabolism and Disposition Study (2005): Coadministration of kawain with full kava extract tripled kawain AUC and doubled C(max)
- Whole extract more effective than isolated kavalactones
- Synergistic pharmacokinetic interactions between kavalactones
- Use whole extract preparations rather than isolated compounds

### Bioavailability Optimization

**Factors Enhancing Bioavailability:**
1. **Empty stomach:** Take 30-60 minutes before meals
2. **Whole extract:** Use complete kavalactone profile, not isolated compounds
3. **Proper extraction:** Ensure efficient extraction method
4. **Quality source:** Noble cultivar, mature plants, proper plant parts

**Factors Reducing Bioavailability:**
1. **Food:** Significantly reduces absorption
2. **Poor quality material:** Degraded or improperly stored root
3. **Inefficient extraction:** Improper preparation methods
4. **Inappropriate plant parts:** Aerial parts or unpeeled material
