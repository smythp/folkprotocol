# Kava - Preparations and Dosing

## Overview of Preparation Types

Clinical research has examined various kava preparations:
1. **Aqueous (water) extracts** - Traditional method
2. **Acetone extracts** - Pharmaceutical standardized (WS1490)
3. **Ethanolic (alcohol) extracts** - Commercial products
4. **Traditional beverage** - Pacific Island ceremonial use

## Standardized Pharmaceutical Extracts

### WS1490 (Acetone Extract)

**Description:**
- Acetonic extraction of kava rhizome/root
- Most studied pharmaceutical kava extract in clinical trials
- German standardized preparation

**Clinical Studies Using WS1490:**

1. **Witte et al. 2005** - Meta-analysis of 6 studies
   - Effective success rate OR=3.3 (95% CI: 2.09-5.22)
   - Hamilton Anxiety Rating Scale improvement: 5.94 points vs controls

2. **Gastpar & Klimm 2003**: 141 participants, 4 weeks
   - **Dose: 3 × 50 mg/day = 150 mg/day total**
   - 73% showed >5 point anxiety improvement vs 56% placebo (p < 0.01)
   - Well-tolerated, no adverse liver effects

3. **Volz & Kieser 1997**: 101 participants, 25 weeks
   - Significant superiority from week 8
   - Long-term efficacy demonstrated
   - No tolerance problems

4. **Lehrl 2004**: 61 participants, 4 weeks
   - **Dose: 200 mg daily**
   - Quality of sleep significantly improved (p = 0.007)
   - Recuperative effect improved (p = 0.018)

5. **Malsch & Kieser 2001**: 40 participants, 5 weeks
   - **Dose: Increased from 50 mg to 300 mg daily over first week, then 3 weeks monotherapy**
   - Post-benzodiazepine treatment
   - Superior to placebo (p = 0.01)

6. **Kinzler et al. 1991**: 58 participants, 4 weeks
   - **Dose: WS 1490 (Laitan) 3 × 100 mg/day = 300 mg/day**
   - Improvements evident after one week
   - No adverse effects

**WS1490 Dosing Summary:**
- **Range: 50-300 mg/day of extract**
- **Most common: 150-200 mg/day**
- **Typical regimen: 50 mg three times daily (150 mg/day)**
- **Duration: 4-25 weeks in studies**
- **Note:** WS1490 dose refers to extract weight, which is standardized to kavalactone content

**White et al. 2018** review:
- **Acetone extract most commonly studied formulation** for anxiety treatment

## Aqueous (Water-Based) Extracts

### Clinical Aqueous Extracts

**Description:**
- Water extraction of kava rhizome/root
- Traditional preparation method adapted for standardization
- Recommended for safety by multiple authorities

**Clinical Studies Using Aqueous Extracts:**

1. **Sarris et al. 2009 (KADSS)**: 60 participants, 3 weeks
   - **Dose: 5 tablets daily containing 250 mg kavalactones per day**
   - Aqueous extract preparation
   - Hamilton Anxiety Scale reduction: -9.9 (kava) vs -0.8 (placebo) phase one
   - Pooled effect: p < 0.0001, Cohen's d = 2.24 (very large effect)
   - No serious adverse effects or hepatotoxicity

2. **Sarris et al. 2013**: 75 participants, 58 completers, 6 weeks
   - **Dose: Aqueous kava extract providing 120-240 mg kavalactones per day depending on response**
   - Dose-flexible design
   - Significant anxiety reduction (p = 0.046, d = 0.62)
   - Remission rates: 26% vs 6% placebo (p = 0.04)
   - No significant liver function abnormalities

3. **Sarris et al. 2020**: 171 participants, 16 weeks
   - **Dose: Aqueous extract of dried kava root, twice daily, 120 mg kavalactones per dose (240 mg/day total)**
   - No significant advantage over placebo (p = 0.25)
   - Liver function abnormalities more frequent (but no clinically significant hepatic injury)
   - Note: Longest duration study (16 weeks) may explain different results

**Aqueous Extract Dosing Summary:**
- **Range: 120-250 mg kavalactones per day**
- **Most common: 240 mg/day (120 mg twice daily)**
- **Duration: 3-16 weeks in studies**
- **Administration: Divided doses (2-3 times daily)**

**Sarris et al. 2011** comprehensive review:
- **Recommends traditional water-soluble extracts of rhizome from appropriate cultivars**

**Teschke & Lebot 2011** standardization plan:
- **Recommendation #3: Aqueous extraction** (traditional method)

### Traditional Aqueous Beverage

**Aporosa et al. 2022** - Traditional consumption study:
- **Dose: 3.6 liters of beverage kava during typical 6-hour sessions**
- Traditional preparation method
- Much higher kavalactone content than medicinal use
- Recreational/ceremonial pattern, not therapeutic

**Traditional Preparation Method:**
1. Ground, pounded, or chewed root/rhizome
2. Mixed with cold water
3. Strained through fibrous material
4. Consumed as beverage

**Note:** Traditional ceremonial doses much higher than therapeutic doses. Not recommended for medicinal use.

## Ethanolic (Alcohol) Extracts

**Description:**
- Alcohol extraction of kava rhizome/root
- Common in commercial dietary supplements
- Less studied in clinical trials than aqueous or acetone extracts

**Safety Considerations:**
- **Teschke 2010** review: Hepatotoxicity cases with ethanolic extracts (n=5 of 14 cases)
- **However:** Teschke et al. 2012 concluded hepatotoxicity occurred regardless of solvent
- Poor quality raw material, not extraction method, is primary concern

**Limited Clinical Trial Data:**
- Fewer published RCTs using ethanolic extracts compared to aqueous or WS1490
- Commercial products variable in quality and standardization

## Dosing Guidelines by Indication

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**Effective Doses from Positive Trials:**

**High-Effect Trials:**
- **250 mg kavalactones/day** - KADSS trial (Sarris 2009): Cohen's d = 2.24
- **120-240 mg kavalactones/day** - Sarris 2013: Cohen's d = 0.62-0.82

**Moderate-Effect Trials:**
- **150 mg/day WS1490** - Gastpar & Klimm 2003
- **WS1490 extract** - Volz & Kieser 1997 (25 weeks)

**Administration:**
- Divided into **2-3 doses per day**
- Example: 120 mg twice daily (240 mg/day total)
- Example: 50 mg three times daily (150 mg/day WS1490)

**Duration:**
- Short-term: **4-8 weeks** typical
- Onset of effect: **1-8 weeks** after starting
- Maximum recommended: **≤3 months** continuous use

### Sleep Disturbances with Anxiety

**Lehrl 2004**: WS 1490, 61 participants, 4 weeks
- **Dose: 200 mg daily**
- Quality of sleep significantly improved (p = 0.007)
- Recuperative effect improved (p = 0.018)

### Perimenopausal/Postmenopausal Anxiety

**Cagnacci et al. 2003**: 68 participants, 3 months
- **Doses tested: 100 mg/day or 200 mg/day (plus calcium)**
- Both doses effective
- Anxiety declined at 1 and 3 months (p < 0.001)

**De Leo et al. 2001**: 6 months
- Kava extract combined with HRT
- Specific dose not detailed in bibliography
- More pronounced HAMA score reduction with kava

### Post-Benzodiazepine Anxiety

**Malsch & Kieser 2001**: 40 participants, 5 weeks
- **Dose: WS1490 increased from 50 mg to 300 mg daily over first week**
- **Then 3 weeks monotherapy**
- Superior to placebo (p = 0.01)
- Further symptom reduction possible after benzodiazepine change-over

### Nervous Tension and Restlessness

**Kuchta et al. 2021**: 156 participants, observational, median 28 days
- Dose not specified in detail
- All symptoms showed significant improvement
- Most effective for nervous tension and restlessness

## Maximum Safe Dosing

**Teschke & Lebot 2011** standardization plan:
- **≤250 mg kavalactones per day** for medicinal use

**Teschke et al. 2008** hepatotoxicity analysis:
- **≤120 mg kavapyrones daily, ≤3 months duration** = safest recommendation
- Only 1 patient following these guidelines had probable kava-related liver injury

**Smith & Leiras 2018** systematic review:
- **120-280 mg/day kavalactones** for short-term (4-8 weeks) therapeutic use
- Safe and well-tolerated at this range

**Conservative Recommendation:**
- **120-250 mg kavalactones per day**
- **Maximum 250 mg/day**
- **Duration ≤3 months** without break

## Administration Timing and Food Effects

### Food Interactions

**Kanumuri et al. 2022** pharmacokinetic study:
- **Food significantly reduced absorption extent** in multiple-dose study
- Higher overall exposure with single 225 mg dose vs distributed 3 × 75 mg dosing

**Recommendation:**
- **Take on empty stomach** for optimal absorption
- Or at least avoid large, fatty meals around dosing time

### Timing of Doses

**Divided Dosing:**
- Most clinical trials used **2-3 divided doses per day**
- Examples:
  - 120 mg twice daily (morning and evening)
  - 50 mg three times daily (morning, midday, evening)

**Single Dosing:**
- **Kanumuri et al. 2022**: Higher bioavailability with single 225 mg dose vs divided 3 × 75 mg
- However, clinical trials primarily used divided dosing
- **For sleep:** Evening dose may be preferred

**Time to Peak Plasma Concentration:**
- **Tmax: 1-3 hours** (Kanumuri et al. 2022)
- Plan dosing accordingly for desired effect timing

### Evening/Bedtime Dosing

**For Sleep Disturbances:**
- **Lehrl 2004**: 200 mg daily improved sleep quality
- **Smith et al. 2024**: 225 mg kavalactones nightly for military training study

**Recommendation:**
- For sleep issues: Consider single evening dose or weighted toward evening
- For anxiety throughout day: Divided doses

## Preparation Quality Standards

### Raw Material Requirements

**Teschke & Lebot 2011** six-point plan:

1. **Cultivar:**
   - **Noble kava cultivars** (e.g., Borogu)
   - Avoid "tudei" varieties

2. **Plant Age:**
   - **At least 5 years old** at harvest

3. **Plant Parts:**
   - **Peeled and dried rhizomes and roots** only
   - **NEVER aerial parts** (leaves, stems)

### Extraction Method

**Recommended:**
- **Aqueous extraction** (traditional, recommended for safety)
- **Acetone extraction (WS1490)** (well-studied, standardized)

**Less Recommended:**
- Ethanolic extraction (less safety data, though Teschke et al. 2012 showed solvent not primary factor)

### Standardization

**Typical Standardization:**
- **30-70% kavalactones** in extract
- Products should state total kavalactone content per dose
- Look for third-party verification

**Major Kavalactones:**
- Kavain
- Dihydrokavain
- Methysticin
- Dihydromethysticin
- Yangonin
- Desmethoxyyangonin

**Bioavailability Ranking:**
- Dihydrokavain > Dihydromethysticin > Kavain > Methysticin > Yangonin

### Contamination Testing

**Critical Quality Control:**

**Teschke et al. 2012:**
- Test for **aflatoxins** (Aspergillus)
- Test for **ochratoxin A**
- Test for **bacterial contamination** (Bacillus cereus)

**Poor quality raw material = primary hepatotoxicity risk**

## Tincture Preparations

**Note:** The bibliography does not contain detailed information on specific tincture ratios or dosing for kava tinctures. Clinical trials primarily used standardized extracts in capsule/tablet form.

**General Tincture Information (Not from Bibliography):**
- Tinctures would need to be standardized to kavalactone content
- Dosing would aim for same kavalactone amounts as clinical trials (120-250 mg/day)
- Alcohol-based tinctures may have different bioavailability than aqueous extracts
- **Concern:** Sarris et al. 2011 recommends "avoid concurrent alcohol use"
- Small amounts in tinctures likely acceptable, but aqueous preparations preferred

**For Specific Tincture Protocols:** Consult product labeling showing kavalactone content per mL or other professional herbalist resources not included in this research bibliography.

## Tea Preparations

**Traditional Method:**
- Ground or powdered kava root/rhizome
- Mixed with cold or warm (not boiling) water
- Strained

**Dosing for Tea:**
- Would need to calculate kavalactone content
- Aim for therapeutic range: 120-250 mg kavalactones per day
- Divided into 2-3 cups if desired

**Preparation Notes:**
- Kavalactones are lipophilic (fat-soluble) but also water-soluble to degree
- Traditional aqueous preparations effective
- Some sources recommend adding small amount of fat (coconut milk) to increase extraction

**Modern "Instant" Kava:**
- Some products offer instant kava powder
- Should be standardized to kavalactone content
- Follow product labeling for dosing

## Capsule/Tablet Preparations

**Most Common Form in Clinical Trials:**
- Standardized extract in capsules or tablets
- Clearly labeled kavalactone content
- Easier to dose accurately than teas or tinctures

**Dosing:**
- Follow clinical trial guidelines: 120-250 mg kavalactones/day
- Typically 2-3 capsules per day depending on strength
- Take on empty stomach for best absorption

**Example Regimens:**
- 2 capsules of 120 mg kavalactones each = 240 mg/day (120 mg morning, 120 mg evening)
- 3 capsules of 50 mg kavalactones each = 150 mg/day (50 mg three times daily)

## Special Preparations

### Kava + HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy)

**De Leo et al. 2001**: Postmenopausal women, 6 months
- Kava extract combined with HRT
- More pronounced anxiety reduction than HRT alone
- Specific kava dose not detailed in bibliography

### Kava + Calcium

**Cagnacci et al. 2003**: Perimenopausal women, 3 months
- Control: Calcium alone
- Intervention: Calcium plus kava 100 mg/day or 200 mg/day
- Both kava doses effective for anxiety

## Onset of Effect and Duration

**Onset:**
- **Kinzler et al. 1991**: Improvements evident after **one week**
- **Volz & Kieser 1997**: Significant superiority starting from **week 8**
- **General pattern: 1-8 weeks** for full anxiolytic effect
- Not immediate like benzodiazepines

**Duration of Treatment:**
- **Short-term: 4-8 weeks** typical and safest
- **Medium-term: Up to 3 months** recommended maximum
- **Long-term: 25 weeks** studied by Volz & Kieser 1997 without tolerance
- **16 weeks** (Sarris et al. 2020) showed increased liver function abnormalities

**Recommendation:**
- Start with 4-8 week trial
- If beneficial and well-tolerated, may continue up to 3 months
- Monitor liver function
- Take breaks between courses of treatment

## Product Selection Recommendations

**Choose Products That:**
1. Specify **total kavalactone content** per dose
2. Use **noble kava cultivars**
3. Use **rhizome and root only** (no aerial parts)
4. Use **aqueous or acetone extraction**
5. Have **third-party testing** for:
   - Kavalactone content verification
   - Contaminant testing (aflatoxins, ochratoxin, bacteria)
6. Preferably **USP verified** or similar quality certification
7. From **reputable manufacturers** with quality control

**Avoid Products That:**
1. Don't specify kavalactone content
2. Use "proprietary blends" without amounts
3. Contain aerial parts (leaves, stems)
4. Have unknown or "tudei" cultivars
5. Lack third-party testing
6. Have no quality certifications

## Practical Preparation Protocols

### Protocol 1: Standardized Extract (Most Evidence-Based)

**Product:** Standardized kava extract capsules (aqueous or WS1490)
**Dose:** 120 mg kavalactones per capsule
**Regimen:**
- Morning: 1 capsule (120 mg) on empty stomach
- Evening: 1 capsule (120 mg) on empty stomach
- **Total: 240 mg/day**

**Duration:** 6-8 weeks initially
**Monitoring:** Liver function tests at baseline and 4-6 weeks

### Protocol 2: Lower Dose Conservative Approach

**Product:** Standardized kava extract capsules
**Dose:** 50 mg kavalactones per capsule
**Regimen:**
- Morning: 1 capsule (50 mg)
- Midday: 1 capsule (50 mg)
- Evening: 1 capsule (50 mg)
- **Total: 150 mg/day**

**Duration:** 4 weeks initially
**Monitoring:** Liver function tests at baseline and 4 weeks

### Protocol 3: Sleep-Focused

**Product:** Standardized kava extract
**Dose:** 200 mg kavalactones
**Regimen:**
- Evening: 200 mg, 1-2 hours before bed, on empty stomach

**Duration:** 4 weeks
**Monitoring:** Liver function tests, sleep quality tracking

### Protocol 4: Traditional Tea (Less Precise Dosing)

**Product:** Powdered noble kava root (standardized if possible)
**Preparation:**
- 2-3 tablespoons kava powder
- Mix with 8-12 oz cold/warm water
- Strain through cloth
- Consume 1-2 cups per day

**Note:** Difficult to standardize kavalactone content. Prefer standardized extracts for medicinal use.

## Conclusion

**Best-Supported Preparations:**
1. **Aqueous extracts** standardized to 120-250 mg kavalactones/day
2. **WS1490 acetone extract** 150-200 mg/day

**Optimal Dosing:**
- **240 mg kavalactones/day** (120 mg twice daily) - best balance of efficacy and safety
- Divided doses, on empty stomach
- 4-8 weeks duration initially
- Maximum ≤250 mg/day for ≤3 months

**Critical Quality Factors:**
- Noble cultivar, ≥5 years old
- Rhizome/root only (no aerial parts)
- Tested for contamination
- Standardized kavalactone content

**Administration:**
- Empty stomach for best absorption
- Divided doses (2-3 times daily) for anxiety
- Evening dose for sleep
- Allow 1-8 weeks for full effect
