# Rhodiola rosea - Clinical Uses

*Extracted from: bibliographies/rhodiola.yaml*
*Date: 2025-12-31*

## Well-Supported Clinical Indications

### 1. Stress-Related Fatigue and Burnout

**Evidence Level:** Multiple RCTs, systematic reviews

**Key Studies:**

- **Olsson 2009 (SHR-5)** - Double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group study
  - **Dose:** 576 mg/day (4 tablets)
  - **Duration:** Study duration (not specified in detail)
  - **Findings:** Exerts anti-fatigue effect, increases mental performance (especially concentration), decreases cortisol response to awakening stress in burnout patients
  - **Biomarkers:** Decreased cortisol response to awakening stress
  - **Safety:** Well-tolerated

- **Edwards 2012 (WS® 1375)** - Open-label study
  - **Dose:** 200 mg twice daily (400 mg/day total)
  - **Duration:** 4 weeks
  - **Findings:** Safe and effective in improving life-stress symptoms to clinically relevant degree
  - **Safety:** Safe, well-tolerated

- **Lekomtseva 2017 (WS® 1375)** - Open-label clinical trial
  - **Dose:** 400 mg daily (2 × 200 mg)
  - **Duration:** 8 weeks
  - **Findings:** Greatest improvement after 1 week, with statistically significant continuing improvement at week 8
  - **Indication:** Prolonged or chronic fatigue

**Effective Dosing Range:** 400-576 mg/day
**Typical Duration:** 4-8 weeks

### 2. Mild to Moderate Depression

**Evidence Level:** RCTs, systematic reviews

**Key Studies:**

- **Darbinyan 2007 (SHR-5)** - Phase III RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled
  - **Sample Size:** 89 patients
  - **Duration:** 6 weeks
  - **Dosing:**
    - Group A (n=31): 340 mg/day SHR-5
    - Group B (n=29): 680 mg/day SHR-5
    - Group C (n=29): placebo
  - **Findings:** Both Groups A and B showed significant improvement in:
    - Overall depression
    - Insomnia
    - Emotional instability
    - Somatization
  - **Safety:** No serious side effects in any group
  - **Note:** No significant difference between 340 mg vs 680 mg doses

- **Mao 2015** - Randomized placebo-controlled trial vs sertraline
  - **Sample Size:** 57 subjects
  - **Duration:** 12 weeks
  - **Arms:** Standardized R. rosea extract vs Sertraline vs Placebo
  - **Findings:** R. rosea produced less antidepressant effect vs sertraline, but resulted in significantly fewer adverse events and was better tolerated
  - **Clinical Note:** Fewer side effects than conventional antidepressants

**Effective Dosing Range:** 340-680 mg/day
**Typical Duration:** 6-12 weeks
**Key Advantage:** Fewer adverse effects than conventional antidepressants

### 3. Physical and Mental Fatigue

**Evidence Level:** Multiple RCTs, systematic reviews

**Key Studies:**

- **Shevtsov 2003 (SHR-5)** - RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled
  - **Sample Size:** 161 cadets, ages 19-21
  - **Dosing:** Standard dose vs 50% higher dose vs placebo
  - **Findings:** Both doses showed statistically highly significant antifatigue effect (p<0.001)
  - **Dose Response:** No significant difference between dosage groups, with possible trend favoring lower dose
  - **Indication:** Mental work capacity and fatigue

- **Jówko 2018** - Chronic supplementation study
  - **Sample Size:** Healthy male students
  - **Dose:** 600 mg/day
  - **Duration:** 4 weeks
  - **Findings:** Benefits for mental performance, physical capacity, and oxidative stress biomarkers

**Effective Dosing Range:** 200-600 mg/day
**Typical Duration:** 4-8 weeks

**Systematic Review Evidence:**
- **Ishaque 2012:** Mixed results - 2 of 6 physical fatigue trials showed benefit; 3 of 5 mental fatigue trials demonstrated effectiveness
- **Hung 2011:** Beneficial effects on physical performance, mental performance, and certain mental health conditions across 11 RCTs

### 4. Endurance Exercise Performance

**Evidence Level:** Multiple RCTs, meta-analysis

**Acute Dosing Protocols:**

- **Noreen 2013**
  - **Dose:** 3 mg/kg body weight (~200-300 mg for average adult)
  - **Timing:** 60 minutes before exercise
  - **Findings:** Subjects completed 6-mile time trial significantly faster after R. rosea vs placebo. Lower mean perceived exertion in R. rosea trial

- **De Bock 2004**
  - **Dose:** ~200 mg (containing ~1% salidroside and ~3% rosavin)
  - **Timing:** 60 minutes before exercise
  - **Findings:** Acute R. rosea intake can improve endurance exercise capacity. Response not altered by prior 4-week daily intake

**Meta-Analysis Evidence:**

- **Wang 2025** - 26 RCTs, 668 participants
  - **Findings:** Significantly improved endurance-related outcomes including:
    - VO2max
    - Time to exhaustion
    - Time trial performance
    - Enhanced antioxidant enzymes
    - Reduced oxidative stress
  - **Note:** No significant effect on inflammatory markers
  - **Conclusion:** Rhodiola functions as effective ergogenic aid for endurance performance

**Effective Acute Dosing:** 200-400 mg, 60 minutes pre-exercise
**Effect:** Improves VO2max, time to exhaustion, reduces perceived exertion

## Moderately Supported Clinical Indications

### 5. Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**Evidence Level:** Pilot studies

**Key Study:**

- **Bystritsky 2008** - Pilot study
  - **Duration:** 10 weeks
  - **Dose:** 340 mg total daily dose
  - **Findings:** Significant improvement in GAD symptoms

**Effective Dosing:** 340 mg/day
**Typical Duration:** 10 weeks
**Note:** Limited evidence; more research needed

### 6. Mental Work Capacity and Concentration

**Evidence Level:** RCTs

**Key Studies:**

- **Shevtsov 2003** (see above under fatigue)
- **Olsson 2009** (see above under stress-related fatigue)

**Effective Dosing Range:** 170-340 mg/day
**Note:** Antifatigue effect in healthy individuals, improves concentration

## Dose-Response Findings

### Lower Doses May Be Equally Effective

Evidence from multiple studies suggests that lower-to-moderate doses (340-600 mg/day) are effective for most indications:

- **Darbinyan 2007:** 340 mg/day as effective as 680 mg/day for depression
- **Shevtsov 2003:** Standard dose comparable to 50% higher dose, with possible trend favoring lower dose

**Clinical Implication:** Start with lower doses (340-400 mg/day) and increase only if needed.

## Safety Profile

### General Safety

- **Lu 2022:** No obvious adverse reactions reported in athletic/sports use
- **Hung 2011:** Only few mild adverse events reported across 11 RCTs
- **Edwards 2012:** Most adverse events of mild intensity, not related to study drug. No serious adverse events
- **Mao 2015:** Significantly fewer adverse events vs sertraline, better tolerated
- **Darbinyan 2007:** No serious side effects in any group (340 mg, 680 mg, or placebo)

### Comparative Safety

In contrast to most conventional antidepressants, R. rosea extract appears well-tolerated in short-term studies with favorable safety profile.

---

## Clinical Notes

1. **Standardized extracts recommended:** Look for products standardized to 3% rosavins and 1% salidroside (similar to well-studied SHR-5 extract)

2. **Onset of action:** Some studies show greatest improvement after 1 week, with continuing improvement over 4-8 weeks

3. **Quality matters:** Standardized, authenticated products essential due to quality and authenticity concerns in commercial market

4. **Sustainability:** Consider sourcing from sustainable/cultivated sources due to conservation concerns for wild populations
