# Rhodiola rosea - Active Compounds and Standardization

*Extracted from: bibliographies/rhodiola.yaml*
*Date: 2025-12-31*

## Primary Active Constituents

Rhodiola rosea contains approximately **140 isolated compounds** including:
- Monoterpenes
- Glycosides
- Flavonoids
- Phenolic derivatives

*(Source: Panossian 2010)*

---

## Standardization Markers

The following compounds are used as markers for standardization and quality control:

### Primary Markers

1. **Salidroside** (phenylpropanoid glycoside)
2. **Rosavin** (phenylpropanoid)
3. **Rosarin** (phenylpropanoid)
4. **Rosin** (phenylpropanoid)

**Note:** Phenylpropanoids comprise rosavin, rosarin, and rosin collectively.

---

## Pharmacopoeia Requirements

### United States Pharmacopoeia (USP)

| Compound | Minimum Requirement |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Salidroside** | ≥0.08% |
| **Phenylpropanoids** (rosavin + rosarin + rosin) | ≥0.3% |

### Russian Pharmacopoeia

| Compound | Minimum Requirement |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Salidroside** | >0.8% |
| **Phenylpropanoids** (rosavin + rosarin + rosin) | >1.0% |

**Key Difference:** Russian Pharmacopoeia requirements are significantly higher:
- **10× higher salidroside** (0.8% vs 0.08%)
- **3× higher phenylpropanoids** (1.0% vs 0.3%)

### European Pharmacopoeia

**Requirement:** THR (Traditional Herbal Registration) products must be:
- Manufactured in licensed GMP facility
- Conform to European Pharmacopoeia requirements

## Clinical Trial Standardized Extracts

### SHR-5 (Swedish Herbal Institute)

**Standardization:** 3% rosavins, 1% salidroside

**Manufacturer:** Swedish Herbal Institute (proprietary standardized extract)

**Clinical Studies Using SHR-5:**
- Darbinyan 2007: Mild to moderate depression (340 mg and 680 mg doses)
- Shevtsov 2003: Mental work capacity and fatigue (161 cadets)
- Olsson 2009: Stress-related fatigue and burnout (576 mg/day)

**Key Point:** SHR-5 is the most extensively studied standardized extract, with multiple Phase III RCTs demonstrating efficacy.

---

### WS® 1375 (Dr. Willmar Schwabe)

**Manufacturer:** Dr. Willmar Schwabe GmbH & Co. KG

**Clinical Studies Using WS® 1375:**
- Edwards 2012: Life-stress symptoms (200 mg twice daily)
- Lekomtseva 2017: Prolonged or chronic fatigue (400 mg daily)

**Key Point:** WS® 1375 is a well-studied proprietary extract with documented effectiveness in stress and fatigue.

---

## Recommended Standardization for Clinical Use

Based on the clinical trial evidence, look for products standardized to:

**Standard:** **3% rosavins and 1% salidroside**

This standardization matches the well-studied SHR-5 extract and exceeds USP minimum requirements.

## Pharmacokinetics of Salidroside

### Key Compound: Salidroside

**Study:** Zhang 2013 (Planta Medica) - Pharmacokinetic study in rats

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Bioavailability** | 32.1% |
| **Half-life** | ~1 hour |
| **Pharmacokinetics** | Linear |
| **Plasma concentration** | Declined rapidly after IV dosing |

**Excretion after 48 hours (IV injection at 15 mg/kg):**
- Urine: 53.67 ± 12.03%
- Bile: 0.09 ± 0.03%
- Feces: 0.18 ± 0.18%

**Key Takeaways:**
- Moderate bioavailability (about 1/3 of oral dose absorbed)
- Short half-life (~1 hour) - suggests need for divided doses or sustained-release formulations
- Primarily excreted via urine
- Linear pharmacokinetics (dose-proportional)

## Mechanisms of Action

### Primary Pathways

Based on Panossian 2010 comprehensive review:

1. **HPA axis modulation**
   - Reduces cortisol response to stress
   - Documented in Olsson 2009 clinical trial

2. **Protein kinase activation**
   - Cellular signaling pathways

3. **Cellular defense mechanisms**
   - Stress resistance at cellular level

4. **Antioxidant enzyme enhancement**
   - Increases antioxidant capacity
   - Documented in multiple exercise trials (Wang 2025 meta-analysis)

5. **Oxidative stress reduction**
   - Reduces markers of oxidative stress
   - Consistent finding across exercise performance studies

### Documented Pharmacological Effects

- **Adaptogenic effects** (primary)
- **Neuroprotective**
- **Cardioprotective**
- **Anti-fatigue**
- **Antidepressive**
- **Anxiolytic**
- **Cognitive enhancement**

## Clinical Dosing Context

### Relationship Between Standardization and Dosing

When clinical studies report doses (e.g., "340 mg SHR-5"), this refers to:
- 340 mg of the **total extract**
- Which contains 3% rosavins = ~10.2 mg rosavins
- And 1% salidroside = ~3.4 mg salidroside

**Key Point:** Clinical efficacy is based on total extract dose, not isolated compounds. The standardization ensures consistent levels of active markers, but the full spectrum of compounds in the extract likely contributes to efficacy.

## Active Compound Summary

### What We Know

1. **Primary active markers:** Salidroside, rosavin, rosarin, rosin
2. **~140 total compounds** present in whole extract
3. **Clinical efficacy** demonstrated with extracts standardized to 3% rosavins, 1% salidroside
4. **Short half-life** of salidroside (~1 hour) suggests divided daily dosing may be beneficial
5. **Moderate bioavailability** (32%) - oral dosing effective despite incomplete absorption

### What Remains Unclear

- Relative contribution of individual compounds vs whole extract
- Whether isolated salidroside or rosavin alone produces same effects as full extract
- Optimal ratio of phenylpropanoids to glycosides
- Role of minor constituents in overall efficacy

**Clinical Implication:** Use standardized whole extracts rather than isolated compounds, as clinical trials demonstrate efficacy with multi-compound extracts.
