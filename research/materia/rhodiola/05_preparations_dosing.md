# Rhodiola rosea - Preparations and Dosing

*Extracted from: bibliographies/rhodiola.yaml*
*Date: 2025-12-31*

## Plant Part Used

**Official:** Rhizome and root (*rhizoma et radix*)

Consistent across traditional use, clinical trials, and pharmacopoeia monographs.

## Standardized Extracts (Clinical Evidence)

### SHR-5 Extract

**Standardization:** 3% rosavins, 1% salidroside

**Manufacturer:** Swedish Herbal Institute (proprietary)

**Clinical Dosing Protocols:**

| **Study** | **Indication** | **Dose** | **Duration** | **Efficacy** |
|-----------|----------------|----------|--------------|--------------|
| Darbinyan 2007 | Mild-moderate depression | 340 mg/day or 680 mg/day | 6 weeks | Both doses effective; no significant difference |
| Shevtsov 2003 | Mental work capacity | Standard vs 50% higher dose | Study duration | Both effective; trend favoring lower dose |
| Olsson 2009 | Stress-related fatigue | 576 mg/day (4 tablets) | Study duration | Effective for burnout |

**Key Takeaway:** SHR-5 is the most extensively studied extract. Dosing range: 340-576 mg/day.

---

### WS® 1375 Extract

**Manufacturer:** Dr. Willmar Schwabe GmbH & Co. KG

**Clinical Dosing Protocols:**

| **Study** | **Indication** | **Dose** | **Duration** | **Efficacy** |
|-----------|----------------|----------|--------------|--------------|
| Edwards 2012 | Life-stress symptoms | 200 mg twice daily (400 mg/day) | 4 weeks | Effective |
| Lekomtseva 2017 | Chronic fatigue | 400 mg/day (2 × 200 mg) | 8 weeks | Greatest improvement week 1; continued improvement to week 8 |

**Key Takeaway:** WS® 1375 effective at 400 mg/day divided into two doses.

---

### Non-Proprietary Standardized Extracts

**Typical Standardization:** 3% rosavins, 1% salidroside (matching SHR-5)

**Clinical Studies Using Non-Proprietary Extracts:**

| **Study** | **Indication** | **Dose** | **Duration** | **Efficacy** |
|-----------|----------------|----------|--------------|--------------|

## Dosing Protocols by Indication

### Stress-Related Fatigue and Burnout

**Effective Dose:** 400-576 mg/day
**Duration:** 4-8 weeks
**Timing:** Daily (morning or divided doses)
**Evidence:** Multiple RCTs (SHR-5, WS® 1375)

**Sample Protocol:**
- Week 1-8: 400 mg daily (200 mg twice daily with breakfast and lunch)
- Or: 576 mg daily (4 × 144 mg tablets)

---

### Mild to Moderate Depression

**Effective Dose:** 340-680 mg/day
**Duration:** 6-12 weeks
**Timing:** Daily
**Evidence:** RCTs (Darbinyan 2007, Mao 2015)

**Sample Protocol:**
- Start: 340 mg daily for 6 weeks
- If inadequate response: Increase to 680 mg daily
- Note: Lower dose (340 mg) as effective as higher dose (680 mg) in Darbinyan 2007

---

### Physical and Mental Fatigue

**Effective Dose:** 200-600 mg/day
**Duration:** 4-8 weeks
**Timing:** Daily
**Evidence:** Multiple RCTs, systematic reviews

**Sample Protocol:**
- Standard: 400 mg daily (200 mg twice daily)
- Higher intensity: 600 mg daily (200 mg three times daily)

---

### Endurance Exercise Performance (Acute Use)

**Effective Dose:** 200-400 mg single dose
**Timing:** 60 minutes before exercise
**Evidence:** Multiple RCTs, meta-analysis (Wang 2025)

**Sample Protocol:**
- 200-300 mg (~3 mg/kg body weight)
- Take 60 minutes before endurance activity
- Effects: Improved VO2max, time to exhaustion, reduced perceived exertion

**Note:** Response not altered by prior chronic supplementation (De Bock 2004)

---

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder

**Effective Dose:** 340 mg/day
**Duration:** 10 weeks
**Evidence:** Pilot study (Bystritsky 2008)

**Sample Protocol:**
- 340 mg daily for 10 weeks
- Note: Limited evidence; more research needed

---

### Mental Work Capacity and Concentration

**Effective Dose:** 170-340 mg/day
**Duration:** 4+ weeks
**Evidence:** RCTs (Shevtsov 2003, Olsson 2009)

**Sample Protocol:**
- 170-340 mg daily
- For healthy individuals during periods of high cognitive demand

---

## Dose-Response Relationships

### Lower Doses May Be Equally Effective

**Evidence:**

1. **Darbinyan 2007:** 340 mg/day as effective as 680 mg/day for depression

2. **Shevtsov 2003:** No significant difference between standard dose and 50% higher dose; possible trend favoring lower dose

**Clinical Implication:** Start with lower doses (340-400 mg/day). Increase only if inadequate response after 4-6 weeks.

---

## Timing of Doses

### Single Daily Dose vs Divided Doses

**Pharmacokinetic Context:**
- Salidroside half-life: ~1 hour (Zhang 2013)
- Bioavailability: 32.1%

**Clinical Trial Protocols:**

| **Dosing Schedule** | **Study Examples** | **Indication** |
|---------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Once daily** | Not common in reviewed trials | - |
| **Twice daily** | Edwards 2012 (200 mg × 2), Lekomtseva 2017 (200 mg × 2) | Stress, fatigue |
| **Multiple times daily** | Olsson 2009 (4 tablets/day) | Burnout |
| **Acute single dose** | Noreen 2013, De Bock 2004 (60 min pre-exercise) | Endurance performance |

**Recommendation:** Given short half-life of salidroside, **divided doses** (2-3 times daily) may maintain more consistent plasma levels. However, clinical trials show efficacy with various schedules.

**Typical Schedule:**
- Morning dose (with breakfast)
- Midday dose (with lunch)
- Avoid evening doses (potential for activation/alertness)

## Extract Equivalency Considerations

### Standardized Extract vs Crude Herb

**Challenge:** Clinical trials use standardized extracts (3% rosavins, 1% salidroside). Equivalency to crude herb is unclear.

**Why This Matters:**
- Crude root may have 0.5-2% salidroside (natural variation)
- Standardized extracts concentrate active compounds
- Extraction method affects compound availability

**Rough Estimation (theoretical):**
- If crude root contains ~1% salidroside and ~3% rosavins naturally
- 400 mg standardized extract (1% salidroside) = 4 mg salidroside
- Crude root equivalent: ~400 mg to ~800 mg depending on root quality

**Recommendation:** For clinical applications, use standardized extracts with verified content to match evidence-based dosing.

## Product Selection Guidelines

### What to Look For

1. **Standardization:** 3% rosavins, 1% salidroside (matches SHR-5)

2. **Quality Markers:**
   - USP verification or third-party testing
   - GMP-certified facility
   - Authentic *R. rosea* species (not substitutes)
   - Sustainable/cultivated sourcing

3. **Clinical-Grade Products:**
   - Products matching clinical trial extracts when available
   - Pharmaceutical-grade standardization

4. **Avoid:**
   - Unstandardized products
   - Products without salidroside/rosavin content listed
   - Suspiciously low prices (quality concerns)

## Onset and Duration of Effects

### Onset of Action

**Acute Use (Endurance Performance):**
- Timing: 60 minutes before exercise
- Onset: Effects apparent during exercise session

**Chronic Use (Fatigue, Depression, Stress):**
- **Week 1:** Initial improvements may be noticeable (Lekomtseva 2017 showed greatest change after 1 week)
- **Weeks 4-6:** Continued improvement (typical trial duration)
- **Weeks 6-12:** Full effects (particularly for depression)

### Duration of Treatment

**Short-term conditions:**
- Acute fatigue/stress: 4-8 weeks
- Life-stress symptoms: 4 weeks

**Longer-term conditions:**
- Depression: 6-12 weeks
- Chronic fatigue: 8 weeks
- GAD: 10 weeks

**Maintenance:** Data on long-term use (>12 weeks) limited in clinical trials.

## Special Considerations

### Taking with Food

**Not specifically addressed in clinical trial protocols.**

**General Recommendation:** Take with meals to:
- Improve gastrointestinal tolerance
- Consistent absorption
- Avoid potential stomach upset

### Time of Day

**Recommendation:** Morning and/or midday dosing

**Rationale:**
- Adaptogenic/stimulating effects may interfere with sleep if taken in evening
- Clinical trials generally used morning/daytime dosing

### Cycling On/Off

**No specific data in clinical trials.**

**Theoretical Consideration:** Some herbalists recommend periodic breaks (e.g., 5 days on, 2 days off, or 8 weeks on, 2 weeks off) for adaptogens, but this is not evidence-based for Rhodiola.

## Dose Adjustment Factors

### Body Weight

**Noreen 2013:** Used weight-based dosing for acute endurance protocol (3 mg/kg)

**For 70 kg adult:** ~210 mg
**For 90 kg adult:** ~270 mg

**Other Studies:** Fixed dosing (not weight-adjusted)

**Recommendation:** Fixed dosing (340-600 mg/day) appears effective across body weights for chronic use. Weight-based dosing may be considered for acute performance use.

### Age

**No age-specific dosing in clinical trials.**

**Trial Age Ranges:**
- Young adults: Ages 19-21 (Shevtsov 2003)
- Adults: Up to age 65 (various trials)

**Recommendation:** Standard adult dosing for ages 18-65. Use caution in elderly due to potential drug interactions and polypharmacy.

### Sex

**No sex-specific dosing recommendations.**

**Trial Populations:**
- Mixed (male and female): Most trials
- Male-only: Jówko 2018 (healthy male students)

**Recommendation:** No sex-based dose adjustment needed.

## Clinical Bottom Line: Preparations and Dosing

### Evidence-Based Recommendations

1. **Use standardized extracts:** 3% rosavins, 1% salidroside

2. **Starting dose:** 340-400 mg/day for most indications

3. **Timing:** Divided doses (morning and midday), with meals

4. **Duration:** 4-12 weeks depending on indication

5. **Adjustment:** Increase to 600 mg/day if inadequate response after 4-6 weeks

6. **Acute use:** 200-400 mg, 60 minutes pre-exercise for endurance performance

7. **Quality matters:** Choose authenticated, standardized products from reputable sources
