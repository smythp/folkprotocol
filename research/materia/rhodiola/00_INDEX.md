### 03_active_compounds.md
**Active Compounds and Standardization**

- Primary active constituents (salidroside, rosavin, rosarin, rosin)
- Pharmacopoeia requirements (USP, Russian, European)
- Clinical trial standardized extracts (SHR-5, WS® 1375)
- Recommended standardization: 3% rosavins, 1% salidroside
- Pharmacokinetics of salidroside (32.1% bioavailability, ~1 hr half-life)
- Mechanisms of action (HPA axis modulation, antioxidant effects, protein kinase activation)
- Quality and authenticity concerns

**Key Takeaway:** Use products standardized to 3% rosavins and 1% salidroside to match well-studied clinical extracts. ~140 compounds present; whole extract likely more effective than isolated compounds.

### 04_safety_interactions.md
**Safety, Contraindications, and Drug Interactions**

- General safety profile (well-tolerated, few adverse events)
- **CYP2C9 inhibition (~21%)** - clinically relevant
  - Warfarin interaction concern
  - Phenytoin interaction concern
- Antidepressant interactions (case report with paroxetine)
- Pregnancy/lactation (insufficient safety data)
- Comparative safety vs conventional antidepressants (fewer adverse effects)
- Quality and authenticity concerns
- Safety monitoring recommendations

**Key Takeaway:** Generally safe, but caution needed with CYP2C9 substrates (especially warfarin, phenytoin) and when combining with antidepressants.

**Preparations and Dosing Protocols**

- Standardized extracts (SHR-5, WS® 1375)
- Dosing protocols by indication (detailed tables)
- Dose-response relationships (lower doses often as effective as higher)
