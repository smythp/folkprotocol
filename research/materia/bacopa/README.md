# Bacopa monnieri Research Summary

## Overview

This directory contains comprehensive research summaries for **Bacopa monnieri** (Brahmi), an Ayurvedic herb with strong clinical evidence for cognitive enhancement and memory support.

**Source Data**: All summaries extracted from bibliographies/bacopa.yaml (18 clinical studies from PubMed, including 11 RCTs, 4 systematic reviews, 1 clinical trial, and 2 reviews, dated 2002-2025).

**Last Updated**: 2026-01-08

## Quick Reference

**Primary Clinical Use**: Memory enhancement and cognitive function improvement

**Evidence Base**: Moderate to strong (meta-analysis with 518 participants, multiple RCTs)

**Standard Dose**: 300-450 mg daily of standardized extract (20-50% bacosides)

**Duration**: Minimum 12 weeks for cognitive effects

**Safety**: Generally well-tolerated; mild GI effects most common

**Contraindications**: Avoid in pregnancy/lactation; caution with cholinergic/thyroid medications

## Evidence Hierarchy

### Strong Evidence (Multiple RCTs + Meta-Analysis)
- Memory enhancement in adults
- Cognitive function improvement
- Verbal learning and delayed recall

### Moderate Evidence (Multiple RCTs or Systematic Review)
- Cognitive benefits in elderly
- Memory and language improvements in children
- General cognitive enhancement

### Preliminary Evidence (Limited Studies)
- Anxiety reduction (elderly)
- Depression scores improvement
- Anhedonia treatment
- ADHD symptoms (mixed results)

### Insufficient Evidence (Theoretical or Single Study)
- Parkinson's disease (pilot only)
- Schizophrenia augmentation (under review)
- Neuroprotection in humans (preclinical data)

## Clinical Bottom Line

**Who benefits most**: Healthy adults and elderly seeking evidence-based cognitive enhancement; children with attention/memory issues (under medical supervision)

**What to use**: 300-450 mg daily of standardized extract (20-50% bacosides) from reputable manufacturer (CDRI 08, Bacumen, or equivalent)

**How to use**: Daily with meals (especially fatty meals) for minimum 12 weeks; start lower (150 mg) if GI sensitive

**Safety**: Generally well-tolerated; avoid in pregnancy/lactation; caution with cholinergic or thyroid medications; choose third-party tested products

**Evidence strength**: Moderate to strong for memory and cognitive enhancement based on meta-analysis (518 participants) and multiple RCTs (2002-2025)

## Research Gaps

1. Long-term safety (>12 weeks, especially >1 year)
2. Pregnancy and lactation safety data
3. Specific drug interaction studies (beyond theoretical)
4. Optimal dosing strategies (higher doses? divided doses?)
5. Bioavailability-enhanced formulation efficacy and safety
6. Mechanisms of thyroid medication interaction
7. Efficacy in diagnosed cognitive disorders (MCI, dementia, Alzheimer's)
8. Female pediatric population safety and efficacy
