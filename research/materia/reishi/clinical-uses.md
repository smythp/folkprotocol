# Reishi (Ganoderma lucidum) - Clinical Uses

## Immune Modulation

### Meta-Analysis Evidence (Cochrane 2016)
- **Study**: Jin et al., Cochrane systematic review
- **Sample**: 5 RCTs analyzed
- **Context**: Cancer patients receiving chemo/radiotherapy
- **Findings**:
  - Increased CD3 percentage by 3.91%
  - Increased CD4 percentage by 3.05%
  - Increased CD8 percentage by 2.02%
  - Patients more likely to respond positively to treatment (RR 1.50; 95% CI 0.90 to 2.51, P = 0.02)
  - 4 studies showed improved quality of life vs controls

### RCT: β-Glucan in Healthy Adults (Lin 2023)
- **Sample**: Healthy adults aged 18-55
- **Duration**: 84 days
- **Dose**: 200 mg daily (purified β-1,3; 1,6 D-Glucan)
- **Findings**:
  - Significant enhancement in CD3+ T-lymphocytes
  - Increased CD4+ T-lymphocytes
  - Increased CD8+ T-lymphocytes
  - Improved CD4/CD8 ratio
  - Increased natural killer cell counts
- **Note**: Lower dose effective due to purified concentrated fraction

### RCT: Yogurt-Based Delivery in Children (Vetvicka 2018)
- **Sample**: Children aged 3-5 years
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Delivery**: Yogurt enriched with β-glucans from G. lucidum
- **Findings**:
  - Significantly higher absolute counts of peripheral blood total lymphocytes
  - Increased CD3+ T cells
  - Increased CD4+ T cells
  - Increased CD8+ T cells compared to placebo

## Cancer-Related Fatigue

### RCT: Breast Cancer Patients (Zhao 2012)
- **Sample**: 48 breast cancer patients with cancer-related fatigue during endocrine therapy
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Dose**: 1,000 mg three times daily (3,000 mg/day total spore powder)
- **Findings**:
  - Statistically significant improvements in physical well-being
  - Reduced fatigue subscale scores
  - Less anxiety and depression
  - Better quality of life
  - **TNF-α decreased from 128.70 to 71.89 pg/mL (P < 0.01)** - 44% reduction
  - **IL-6 decreased from 62.43 to 37.62 pg/mL (P < 0.05)** - 40% reduction
  - No significant adverse effects
- **Note**: Demonstrates measurable anti-inflammatory effects via cytokine modulation

## Dyslipidemia & Cardiovascular Health

### RCT: Spore Oil for Dyslipidemia (Geng 2025)
- **Sample**: 110 participants
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Intervention**: Ganoderma lucidum spore oil (dose not specified)
- **Findings**:
  - Significantly lower total cholesterol after 12 weeks
  - Significantly lower triglycerides
  - Significantly lower LDL-C
  - Significantly higher HDL-C compared to placebo

### Meta-Analysis: Metabolic Effects (Jafari 2025)
- **Type**: GRADE-assessed systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Sample**: 17 RCTs, 971 participants
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, Scopus, EMBASE, Cochrane, Web of Science (up to August 2024)
- **Findings**:
  - Significant reduction in body mass index (BMI)
  - Significant reductions in creatinine levels
  - Reduced glutathione peroxidase
  - Reduced heart rate
- **Quality**: GRADE methodology for evidence quality assessment

### NEGATIVE TRIAL: Metabolic Syndrome (Gao 2016)
- **Sample**: 84 participants with type 2 diabetes and metabolic syndrome
- **Duration**: 16 weeks
- **Dose**: 3 g/day G. lucidum (with or without Cordyceps sinensis)
- **Findings**:
  - Evidence does NOT support use for cardiovascular risk factors in diabetes or metabolic syndrome
  - 52 and 35 adverse events reported in G. lucidum groups
  - Two serious events (stroke, breast cancer diagnosis) not attributed to intervention
  - No significant improvements in cardiovascular markers
- **Clinical Significance**: Important negative trial; effectiveness may depend on baseline metabolic status

## Hepatoprotection & Antioxidation

### RCT: Crossover Design in Mild Liver Dysfunction (Hsu 2008)
- **Sample**: 42 healthy middle-aged subjects (40-54 years, 22 male/20 female) with mild liver dysfunction
- **Duration**: 6 months treatment, 1 month washout, 6 months crossover
- **Dose**: 225 mg twice daily (triterpenoids and polysaccharide peptides-enriched extract)
- **Findings**:
  - Substantial escalation in SOD, CAT, GPx, and G-6-PDH levels (antioxidant enzymes)
  - Substantial decrement in GOT and GPT activities (liver enzymes)
  - Demonstrated antioxidation, anti-aging and hepatoprotective effects
  - Effective in subjects with elevated liver enzymes and fatty liver
- **Note**: Crossover design strengthens findings; standardized extract with both triterpenoids and polysaccharides

### Mechanistic Review: Hepatoprotective Pathways (Henao 2023)
Key mechanisms identified:
- Modulation of liver Phase I and II enzymes
- Suppression of β-glucuronidase
- Antifibrotic and antiviral actions
- Regulation of nitric oxide (NO) production
- Maintenance of hepatocellular calcium homeostasis
- Immunomodulatory activity
- Free radical scavenging
- **Applications**: NAFLD, alcohol-induced liver disease, hepatitis B, fibrosis

## Neurasthenia & Fatigue

### RCT: Ganopoly for Neurasthenia (Tang 2005)
- **Sample**: 132 patients with neurasthenia
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Dose**: 1,800 mg three times daily (5,400 mg/day total polysaccharide extract - Ganopoly)
- **Findings**:
  - Ganopoly treatment resulted in significantly lower CGI severity scores: **15.5% reduction vs 4.9% placebo**
  - Reduced sense of fatigue by **28.3% from baseline (vs 20.1% placebo)**
  - Statistically significant improvements in well-being
  - Well tolerated
- **Note**: High dose study; Ganopoly is a standardized polysaccharide preparation

## Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms

### RCT: Men with LUTS (Noguchi 2008)
- **Sample**: 88 men over age 49 with slight-to-moderate lower urinary tract symptoms
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Dose**: 6 mg once daily (ethanol extract)
- **Findings**:
  - Extract well tolerated
  - Improved International Prostate Symptom Score (IPSS)
  - Significant improvement in urinary symptoms
  - No severe adverse effects
- **Note**: Much lower dose (6 mg) suggests high concentration/potency; ethanol extraction emphasizes triterpenoid content (ganoderic acids)

## Cancer Adjunct Therapy

### Cochrane Review Summary (Jin 2016)
- **Conclusion**: G. lucidum could be administered as adjunct to conventional cancer treatment for:
  - Enhancing tumor response
  - Stimulating host immunity
  - Improving quality of life
- **Not recommended for**: First-line cancer treatment or improving survival
- **Best evidence**: Supportive care during chemo/radiotherapy

### Ongoing Trial: NSCLC with EGFR Mutation (Sanchez 2024)
- **Status**: Protocol published, trial ongoing
- **Sample**: 140 participants planned (1:1 randomization)
- **Intervention**: De-walled GLSP with third-generation EGFR-TKIs
- **Goal**: Evaluate synergy with targeted cancer therapy

## Spore-Specific Research

### Systematic Review: Spores (Jafari 2023)
- **Sample**: 90 studies on G. lucidum spores
- **Databases**: PubMed, VHL, GHL, SIGLE, Google Scholar (searched September 2021)
- **Findings**:
  - Spores showed antitumor, immunomodulatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective activities
  - Generally well-tolerated in clinical applications
  - Safety profile comparable to whole fruiting body
- **Note**: Spores have different bioactive profile than fruiting body; require wall-breaking for bioavailability

## Clinical Dosing Summary

### Evidence-Based Dose Ranges
- **Purified β-glucan**: 200 mg daily (Lin 2023)
- **Concentrated extracts**: 225-450 mg daily (Hsu 2008)
- **Ethanol extract**: 6 mg once daily (Noguchi 2008) - highly concentrated
- **Standard extract range**: 1,400-3,000 mg daily (most common in trials)
- **Spore powder**: 3,000 mg daily (Zhao 2012)
- **High dose polysaccharide**: 5,400 mg daily (Tang 2005)
- **Chinese Pharmacopoeia**: 6-12 g extract daily
- **Very high dose**: Up to 11,200 mg/day reported in some studies

### Typical Duration
- **Most common**: 8-16 weeks
- **Range**: 1-24 weeks depending on indication
- **Safety data**: Up to 1 year in some studies

### Dosing by Indication

| Indication | Dose | Duration | Preparation Type |
|------------|------|----------|------------------|
| Immune modulation | 200 mg daily | 84 days | Purified β-glucan |
| Cancer fatigue | 3,000 mg daily (1,000 mg × 3) | 4 weeks | Spore powder |
| Neurasthenia | 5,400 mg daily (1,800 mg × 3) | 8 weeks | Polysaccharide extract (Ganopoly) |
| Hepatoprotection | 450 mg daily (225 mg × 2) | 6 months | Triterpenoid + polysaccharide extract |
| LUTS | 6 mg once daily | 12 weeks | Ethanol extract |
| Dyslipidemia | Not specified | 12 weeks | Spore oil |
| Metabolic syndrome | 3,000 mg daily | 16 weeks | Whole extract (NEGATIVE trial) |

## Conditions with Strongest Evidence

1. **Immune modulation** (CD3, CD4, CD8, NK cell enhancement) - Multiple RCTs, Cochrane review
2. **Adjunct cancer therapy** (quality of life, immune support) - Cochrane review, meta-analysis
3. **Cancer-related fatigue** (particularly breast cancer) - RCT with measurable cytokine changes
4. **Lipid profile improvement** (total cholesterol, triglycerides, LDL/HDL) - 2025 RCT, meta-analyses
5. **Hepatoprotection and antioxidation** - Crossover RCT, mechanistic review
6. **Neurasthenia and fatigue reduction** - RCT with significant effect sizes

## Conditions with Moderate Evidence

1. **Lower urinary tract symptoms** - Single RCT
2. **Body mass index reduction** - Meta-analysis
3. **Anti-inflammatory effects** (cytokine modulation) - RCT with biomarker data

## Insufficient or Negative Evidence

1. **First-line cancer treatment** - Cochrane review conclusion
2. **Long-term cancer survival** - No evidence
3. **Cardiovascular risk factors in diabetes/metabolic syndrome** - Negative RCT (Gao 2016)
4. **Radioprotection in humans** - Preclinical only, no human trials

## Research Gaps

1. **Long-term safety data**: Most trials 12-16 weeks; >1 year data limited
2. **Optimal dosing by indication**: Wide ranges (200 mg to 11,200 mg/day); optimal doses unclear
3. **Bioavailability enhancement**: Low polysaccharide bioavailability, first-pass metabolism of triterpenoids
4. **Standardization**: No universal standards; unclear optimal polysaccharide:triterpenoid ratio
5. **Diabetes management**: Negative human RCT despite promising preclinical data
6. **Species identification**: Commercial products may contain different Ganoderma species with unclear pharmacological equivalence
