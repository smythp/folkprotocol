# Reishi (Ganoderma lucidum) - Preparations & Dosing

## Traditional Preparation Methods

### Hot Water Decoction (Traditional)
**Method:**
- Simmer dried fruiting body in water for extended period (30-120 minutes)
- Strain and consume liquid
- May re-decoct same material 2-3 times

**Compounds Extracted:**
- Water-soluble polysaccharides (primary)
- β-glucans (β-1,3; 1,6 D-glucans)
- Polysaccharide-peptide complexes
- Minimal triterpenoid extraction (lipophilic, not water-soluble)

**Standardization:**
- Traditional: No standardization
- Modern hot water extracts: >10% polysaccharides typical

**Traditional Dose:**
- Chinese Pharmacopoeia (2015): 6-12 g extract daily
- Higher dose needed due to lower concentration

**Modern Clinical Dose:**
- 5,000 mg daily (hot water extract for immune function in athletes)

**Advantages:**
- Traditional method, historically validated
- Emphasizes immune-modulating polysaccharides
- Simple home preparation possible

**Disadvantages:**
- Misses lipophilic triterpenoids (ganoderic acids)
- Higher doses needed
- Variable potency depending on preparation time, temperature, material quality

## Modern Standardized Extracts

### 1. Polysaccharide Extract (Ganopoly)
**Type**: Polysaccharide-enriched extract

**Standardization**: Polysaccharide-focused preparation

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Tang 2005: Neurasthenia trial
- Sample: 132 patients
- Duration: 8 weeks

**Clinical Dose**: 1,800 mg three times daily (5,400 mg/day total)

**Findings:**
- 15.5% reduction in CGI severity scores (vs 4.9% placebo)
- 28.3% reduction in fatigue from baseline (vs 20.1% placebo)
- Statistically significant improvements in well-being
- Well tolerated

**Indications**: Neurasthenia, fatigue, mental well-being

**Notes**: High-dose polysaccharide preparation; well-tolerated even at 5,400 mg/day

---

### 2. Purified β-Glucan Extract
**Type**: Purified polysaccharide fraction with specific molecular linkage

**Standardization**: β-1,3; 1,6 D-Glucan (specific structural form)

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Lin 2023: Immune modulation in healthy adults
- Sample: Healthy adults aged 18-55
- Duration: 84 days

**Clinical Dose**: 200 mg daily

**Findings:**
- Significant enhancement in CD3+ T-lymphocytes
- Increased CD4+ T-lymphocytes
- Increased CD8+ T-lymphocytes
- Improved CD4/CD8 ratio
- Increased natural killer cell counts

**Indications**: Immune modulation, immune support

**Notes**: Much lower dose effective due to purification and concentration; excellent safety profile

**Bioavailability**: Variable (0.5-52% for β-glucans generally), but clinical efficacy demonstrated

---

### 3. Ethanol Extract (Ganoderic Acid-Rich)
**Type**: Solvent-extracted preparation emphasizing lipophilic compounds

**Standardization**: Emphasizes triterpenoid content (ganoderic acids)

**Extraction Method**: Ethanol extraction for ganoderic acids and other lipophilic bioactives

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Noguchi 2008: Lower urinary tract symptoms
- Sample: 88 men over age 49 with LUTS
- Duration: 12 weeks

**Clinical Dose**: 6 mg once daily

**Findings:**
- Improved International Prostate Symptom Score (IPSS)
- Significant improvement in urinary symptoms
- Well tolerated
- No severe adverse effects

**Indications**: Lower urinary tract symptoms, conditions benefiting from triterpenoid activity

**Notes**: Extremely low dose (6 mg) indicates very high concentration/potency; emphasizes ganoderic acids over polysaccharides

**Pharmacokinetics:**
- Rapid absorption (Tmax ~30 min)
- Short half-life (0.66±0.33 hours)
- Extensive hepatic first-pass metabolism
- Suggests multiple daily doses optimal, though this trial used once daily

---

### 4. Combined Triterpenoid + Polysaccharide Extract
**Type**: Dual-extraction or combined bioactive preparation

**Standardization**: Contains both triterpenoid and polysaccharide peptide fractions

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Hsu 2008: Hepatoprotection and antioxidation
- Sample: 42 healthy middle-aged subjects (40-54 years) with mild liver dysfunction
- Duration: 6 months treatment, 1 month washout, 6 months crossover
- Design: Crossover strengthens findings

**Clinical Dose**: 225 mg twice daily (450 mg/day total)

**Findings:**
- Substantial escalation in SOD, CAT, GPx, G-6-PDH (antioxidant enzymes)
- Substantial decrement in GOT and GPT (liver enzymes)
- Demonstrated antioxidation, anti-aging, and hepatoprotective effects
- Effective in subjects with elevated liver enzymes and fatty liver

**Indications**: Hepatoprotection, antioxidant support, fatty liver, elevated liver enzymes

**Notes**: Synergistic effects from combined bioactives; lower dose effective (450 mg/day) compared to single-fraction extracts

**Rationale**: Combines immune-modulating polysaccharides with hepatoprotective/anti-inflammatory triterpenoids

---

### 5. Spore Oil
**Type**: Oil extracted from broken-wall spores

**Standardization**: Lipophilic compounds from spores (different profile than fruiting body)

**Processing Requirement**: Spore wall must be broken to release bioactives

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Geng 2025: Dyslipidemia trial
- Sample: 110 participants
- Duration: 12 weeks

**Clinical Dose**: Not specified in published abstract

**Findings:**
- Significantly lower total cholesterol after 12 weeks
- Significantly lower triglycerides
- Significantly lower LDL-C
- Significantly higher HDL-C compared to placebo

**Indications**: Dyslipidemia, lipid profile management, cardiovascular risk reduction

**Notes**: Spores have different bioactive profile than fruiting body; spore oil emphasizes lipophilic compounds

---

### 6. Broken-Wall/De-Walled Spore Powder
**Type**: Mechanically processed spore powder

**Standardization**: Spores with cell walls mechanically broken for bioavailability

**Processing**: Critical - spore walls are extremely tough and resist digestion; wall-breaking essential

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Zhao 2012: Cancer-related fatigue in breast cancer patients
- Sample: 48 breast cancer patients during endocrine therapy
- Duration: 4 weeks

**Clinical Dose**: 1,000 mg three times daily (3,000 mg/day total)

**Findings:**
- Statistically significant improvements in physical well-being
- Reduced fatigue subscale scores
- Less anxiety and depression
- Better quality of life
- TNF-α decreased from 128.70 to 71.89 pg/mL (P < 0.01) - 44% reduction
- IL-6 decreased from 62.43 to 37.62 pg/mL (P < 0.05) - 40% reduction
- No significant adverse effects

**Indications**: Cancer-related fatigue, inflammatory conditions, immune support during cancer treatment

**Notes**: Demonstrates measurable anti-inflammatory effects (cytokine modulation); spore powder has different bioactive profile than fruiting body

**Safety**: Comparable to fruiting body when properly processed (systematic review, 90 studies); avoid non-processed spore products

## Tincture Preparations

### Alcohol Extraction Ratio

**Traditional Ratios:**
- 1:5 (herb:menstruum) - Standard tincture ratio
- 1:4 - Stronger extraction
- 1:3 - Very concentrated

**Alcohol Percentage:**
- 25-40% alcohol - Extracts both polar and non-polar compounds
- Higher alcohol (60-70%) - Emphasizes triterpenoids (ganoderic acids)
- Lower alcohol (25-30%) - Includes more polysaccharides

**Preparation Method:**
1. Grind dried reishi fruiting body into coarse powder
2. Combine with alcohol-water mixture at chosen ratio
3. Macerate for 2-6 weeks, shaking daily
4. Strain and press
5. Store in amber glass bottles

**Tincture Dosing:**
- Typical range: 2-4 mL (40-80 drops) 2-3 times daily
- Equivalent to approximately 400-800 mg dried herb per dose (at 1:5 ratio)
- Total daily: 1,200-2,400 mg herb equivalent

**Advantages:**
- Convenient dosing
- Extracts both water-soluble and alcohol-soluble compounds (dual extraction effect)
- Long shelf life (3-5 years)

**Disadvantages:**
- Lower polysaccharide content than water decoction (polysaccharides partially alcohol-soluble)
- Lower triterpenoid content than pure ethanol extract
- Alcohol content may be contraindicated for some individuals

**Clinical Evidence**: No clinical trials using traditional tincture preparations; clinical evidence uses standardized extracts

## Tea Preparations

### Simple Tea (Hot Water Infusion)
**Method:**
- Pour boiling water over sliced or powdered reishi
- Steep 10-15 minutes
- Strain and consume

**Limitations:**
- Short steeping time extracts fewer polysaccharides than traditional decoction
- Minimal triterpenoid extraction (not water-soluble)
- Lower potency than decoction or extracts

**Dose Estimation:**
- 2-5 g dried reishi per cup
- 2-3 cups daily
- Total: 4-15 g daily (aligns with Chinese Pharmacopoeia 6-12 g recommendation)

**Notes**: Convenient but less potent than decoction or standardized extracts; best for mild immune support or general wellness

### Traditional Decoction (Stronger Tea)
**Method:**
- Simmer 5-10 g dried reishi in 3-4 cups water
- Reduce to 1-2 cups over 30-120 minutes
- Strain and consume throughout day
- Can re-decoct same material 2-3 times

**Advantages:**
- Extracts more polysaccharides than simple infusion
- Traditional method with historical validation
- Can be prepared at home

**Dose:**
- 6-12 g dried reishi daily (Chinese Pharmacopoeia recommendation)
- Divided into 2-3 decoction preparations

**Clinical Equivalent**: Similar to hot water extract at ~5,000 mg daily

## Standardized Extract Dosing by Indication

### Immune Modulation
**Preparation Options:**
1. **Purified β-Glucan**: 200 mg daily (Lin 2023)
2. **Hot Water Extract**: 5,000 mg daily (athlete immune study)
3. **Polysaccharide Extract (Ganopoly)**: 5,400 mg daily (Tang 2005, though for neurasthenia)

**Duration**: 12-16 weeks typical; up to 84 days in β-glucan study

**Evidence Level**: Strong (multiple RCTs, Cochrane review)

---

### Cancer Adjunct Therapy (Immune Support, Quality of Life)
**Preparation Options:**
1. **Various preparations in Cochrane review** (Jin 2016)
2. **Broken-wall spore powder**: 3,000 mg daily (Zhao 2012, cancer fatigue)

**Duration**: 4-16 weeks in trials; ongoing during chemo/radiotherapy

**Evidence Level**: Strong (Cochrane review, multiple RCTs)

**Not Recommended For**: First-line cancer treatment or survival improvement

---

### Neurasthenia & Fatigue
**Preparation**: Polysaccharide extract (Ganopoly)

**Dose**: 1,800 mg three times daily (5,400 mg/day)

**Duration**: 8 weeks (Tang 2005)

**Evidence Level**: Moderate (single RCT, 132 participants)

**Effect Size**: 28.3% reduction in fatigue, 15.5% reduction in CGI severity scores

---

### Hepatoprotection & Antioxidation
**Preparation**: Triterpenoid + polysaccharide peptide enriched extract

**Dose**: 225 mg twice daily (450 mg/day)

**Duration**: 6 months (Hsu 2008 crossover trial)

**Evidence Level**: Moderate (crossover RCT, 42 participants)

**Indications**: Elevated liver enzymes, fatty liver, oxidative stress

---

### Dyslipidemia & Cardiovascular
**Preparation Options:**
1. **Spore oil**: Dose not specified (Geng 2025)
2. **General extracts**: 1,400-3,000 mg range typical

**Duration**: 12-16 weeks

**Evidence Level**: Moderate (2025 RCT, meta-analyses)

**Effects**: Reduced total cholesterol, triglycerides, LDL-C; increased HDL-C

**Negative Evidence**: Gao 2016 (3,000 mg daily, 16 weeks) - no benefit in diabetes/metabolic syndrome patients

---

### Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (LUTS)
**Preparation**: Ethanol extract (ganoderic acid-rich)

**Dose**: 6 mg once daily

**Duration**: 12 weeks (Noguchi 2008)

**Evidence Level**: Moderate (single RCT, 88 men)

**Effects**: Improved IPSS scores, urinary symptom improvement

## Dosing by Body Weight

**Note**: Most trials did not adjust for body weight; doses were standard across participants

**General Guideline** (not from trials, extrapolated):
- Low dose: 3-5 mg/kg body weight (standardized extract)
- Moderate dose: 10-20 mg/kg body weight
- High dose: 30-50 mg/kg body weight

**Example (70 kg adult):**
- Low: 210-350 mg
- Moderate: 700-1,400 mg
- High: 2,100-3,500 mg

**Clinical Trial Precedent**: Most trials used fixed doses regardless of weight (200 mg - 12,000 mg range)

## Frequency of Dosing

### Once Daily
- **Example**: Noguchi 2008 - 6 mg ethanol extract once daily
- **Rationale**: High concentration, sustained release, or long-lasting effects
- **Best For**: Highly concentrated extracts, convenience

### Twice Daily (BID)
- **Example**: Hsu 2008 - 225 mg BID (450 mg/day total)
- **Rationale**: Maintains more stable blood levels
- **Pharmacokinetic Support**: Ganoderic acids have short half-life (0.66±0.33 hours), suggesting multiple daily doses optimal

### Three Times Daily (TID)
- **Examples**:
  - Tang 2005 - 1,800 mg TID (5,400 mg/day Ganopoly)
  - Zhao 2012 - 1,000 mg TID (3,000 mg/day spore powder)
- **Rationale**: Distributes dose throughout day, maintains blood levels
- **Best For**: High total daily doses, short half-life compounds

### Pharmacokinetic Considerations
- **Ganoderic Acids**: Tmax ~30 min, t½ ~0.66 hours → Suggests 2-3x daily dosing optimal
- **Polysaccharides**: Bioavailability 0.5-52%, variable kinetics → Frequency less critical
- **Practical**: Higher frequency improves compliance with large total daily doses

---

## Duration of Treatment

### Short-Term (4-8 weeks)
- Cancer-related fatigue: 4 weeks (Zhao 2012)
- Neurasthenia: 8 weeks (Tang 2005)
- **Use Case**: Acute symptoms, initial trial

### Medium-Term (12-16 weeks)
- LUTS: 12 weeks (Noguchi 2008)
- Dyslipidemia: 12 weeks (Geng 2025)
- Metabolic syndrome: 16 weeks (Gao 2016)
- **Use Case**: Most common trial duration; chronic conditions

### Long-Term (6-12 months)
- Hepatoprotection: 6 months (Hsu 2008)
- Immune modulation: 84 days (Lin 2023)
- Safety data: Up to 1 year in some studies
- **Use Case**: Chronic conditions, ongoing immune support

### Indefinite/Maintenance
- **Clinical Evidence**: Limited data beyond 1 year
- **Safety**: "Possibly safe when used up to one year" (meta-analysis)
- **Traditional Use**: Often used long-term for general health and longevity
- **Recommendation**: Periodic monitoring (liver function) if long-term high-dose use

## Combination Preparations

### Reishi + Cordyceps
- **Trial**: Gao 2016 (metabolic syndrome)
- **Dose**: G. lucidum 3 g/day with or without Cordyceps sinensis
- **Findings**: No significant benefit (negative trial)
- **Note**: Combination did not improve outcomes over reishi alone

### Reishi + Yogurt (Food-Based Delivery)
- **Trial**: Vetvicka 2018 (children immune study)
- **Preparation**: β-glucans from reishi enriched into yogurt
- **Findings**: Successful immune modulation in children ages 3-5
- **Advantages**: Improved palatability and compliance, especially for children

### Reishi + Chemotherapy/Radiotherapy
- **Evidence**: Cochrane review (Jin 2016)
- **Findings**: Enhanced tumor response, improved immune markers, better quality of life
- **Recommendation**: May be beneficial as adjunct; consult oncologist

## Preparation Selection Guide

### Choose Polysaccharide-Rich Preparations For:
- Immune modulation and immune support
- Adjunct to cancer treatment (chemo/radiotherapy)
- General wellness and prevention
- **Options**: Hot water extract, Ganopoly, purified β-glucan, traditional decoction

### Choose Triterpenoid-Rich Preparations For:
- Hepatoprotection (though combined extracts also effective)
- Lower urinary tract symptoms
- Anti-inflammatory effects
- **Options**: Ethanol extract, combined triterpenoid + polysaccharide extract

### Choose Combined Preparations For:
- Hepatoprotection with immune support
- Broad-spectrum effects
- Synergistic benefits
- **Options**: Dual-extraction tinctures, combined triterpenoid + polysaccharide standardized extracts

### Choose Spore Preparations For:
- Dyslipidemia and lipid management
- Cancer-related fatigue and inflammation (cytokine modulation)
- Different bioactive profile than fruiting body
- **Options**: Spore oil, broken-wall spore powder
- **Critical**: MUST be wall-broken for bioavailability

## Practical Dosing Recommendations

### Starting Dose (General Wellness)
- **Standardized Extract**: 500-1,000 mg daily
- **Traditional Decoction**: 3-6 g dried reishi daily
- **Tincture**: 2 mL (40 drops) 2x daily
- **Duration**: Start with 4-8 weeks

### Therapeutic Dose (Specific Conditions)
- **Immune Support**: 200-5,000 mg depending on concentration
- **Neurasthenia/Fatigue**: 3,000-5,400 mg polysaccharide extract
- **Hepatoprotection**: 450 mg combined extract OR 3-6 g traditional decoction
- **LUTS**: 6 mg concentrated ethanol extract
- **Dyslipidemia**: Spore oil (dose TBD from product) OR 1,400-3,000 mg extract
- **Duration**: 12-16 weeks minimum

### Maximum Safe Dose
- **Clinical Evidence**: Up to 11,200 mg/day reported in some studies
- **Chinese Pharmacopoeia**: 6-12 g extract daily (traditional recommendation)
- **High-Dose Safety**: 5,400 mg/day well-tolerated (Tang 2005)
- **Caution**: High-dose spore extracts may increase CA 72-4 marker; use with monitoring

## Quality Considerations for Product Selection

### Standardization Claims to Look For:
1. **Polysaccharide Content**: "≥10% polysaccharides" or specific percentage
2. **β-Glucan Content**: "Contains β-1,3; 1,6 D-glucan" with percentage
3. **Ganoderic Acid Content**: "Standardized to X% ganoderic acids"
4. **Dual Standardization**: "Contains both polysaccharides and triterpenoids"

### Processing & Extraction:
1. **Hot Water Extract**: Polysaccharide-rich
2. **Ethanol/Alcohol Extract**: Triterpenoid-rich
3. **Dual Extraction**: Both compound classes
4. **Spore Products**: Must state "broken-wall" or "de-walled"

### Species Verification:
- "Ganoderma lucidum" (preferred specificity)
- "Lingzhi" (traditional name, may include G. lucidum or G. sinense)
- Third-party testing for species authenticity

### Third-Party Testing:
- USP Verified
- NSF Certified
- ConsumerLab Approved
- Heavy metal testing
- Pesticide screening
- Microbial contamination testing

### Sourcing:
- Cultivated (more consistent) vs wild (variable quality)
- Organic certification (reduces pesticide exposure)
- Geographic origin (some regions have better quality control)

## Summary: Evidence-Based Dosing by Preparation

**Strongest Clinical Evidence:**
1. **Purified β-Glucan**: 200 mg daily for immune modulation (84 days)
2. **Ethanol Extract**: 6 mg daily for LUTS (12 weeks)
3. **Combined Extract**: 450 mg daily for hepatoprotection (6 months)
4. **Spore Powder**: 3,000 mg daily for cancer fatigue (4 weeks)
5. **Polysaccharide Extract**: 5,400 mg daily for neurasthenia (8 weeks)

**Traditional Dosing:**
- Chinese Pharmacopoeia: 6-12 g extract daily (hot water decoction)
- Aligns with 5,000-6,000 mg modern hot water extract doses

**Duration:**
- Minimum: 4-8 weeks for most indications
- Typical: 12-16 weeks
- Long-term: Up to 1 year (safety data available)

**Frequency:**
- 1-3 times daily depending on preparation and total dose
- Pharmacokinetics suggest 2-3x daily optimal for triterpenoids
