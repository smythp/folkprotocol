# Butterbur (Petasites hybridus) - Clinical Uses

## Migraine Prophylaxis

### Adult Studies

**Lipton 2004** - Large 3-arm RCT (Neurology)
- Sample: 245 patients with migraine
- Duration: 4 months
- Dosing groups:
  - High dose: 75 mg twice daily (150 mg/day) - ~82 patients
  - Mid dose: 50 mg twice daily (100 mg/day) - ~82 patients
  - Placebo: ~81 patients
- Primary outcome (migraine attack frequency reduction):
  - High dose: 48% reduction (p=0.0012 vs placebo)
  - Mid dose: 36% reduction (p=0.127 vs placebo, not significant)
  - Placebo: 26% reduction
- Secondary outcome (≥50% reduction in attack frequency):
  - High dose: 68% of patients (p < 0.05)
  - Placebo: 49% of patients
- Adverse effects: Mild gastrointestinal events, predominantly burping
- Conclusion: Only 75 mg BID dose demonstrated statistically significant superiority over placebo

**Grossmann 2000** - RCT (Int J Clin Pharmacol Ther)
- Sample: 60 patients
- Duration: 12 weeks (plus 4-week run-in phase)
- Dose: Petadolex - 2 capsules twice daily, each containing 25 mg (100 mg/day total)
- Primary outcome: Migraine attack frequency decreased by maximum of 60% compared to baseline (p < 0.05 vs placebo)
- Adverse effects: No adverse events reported
- Tolerability: Exceptional
- Conclusion: High efficacy combined with excellent tolerance

**Agosti 2006** - Systematic Review (Phytomedicine)
- Included: 2 RCTs with 293 patients total (60 and 233 patients)
- Treatment duration: 3-4 months
- Dosages studied: 150 mg/day, 100 mg/day, placebo
- Key findings:
  - Higher dose (150 mg/day) showed greater decreased frequency of migraine attacks than lower dose (100 mg/day) and placebo
  - Greater number of responders (improvement >50%) with 150 mg dose after 3-4 months treatment
  - Moderate evidence of effectiveness for 150 mg dose in migraine prevention
- Product: Petadolex (proprietary Petasites root extract)

### Pediatric Studies

**Pothmann 2005** - Open-label trial (Headache)
- Sample: 108 children and adolescents (ages 6-17)
- Duration: 4 months
- Dosage: 50-150 mg butterbur root extract
- Primary outcomes:
  - ≥50% reduction in migraine attack frequency: 77% of participants
  - Overall attack frequency reduction: 63%
  - Substantial or slight improvement: 91% reported improvement
  - Well-being or improved well-being: 90% (both physicians and patients)
- Safety:
  - Adverse effect rate: 7.4% of patients
  - Primary adverse effect: Eructation (burping)
  - Serious adverse effects: None documented
  - Premature discontinuations: None due to side effects

### Clinical Dosing Recommendations - Migraine

**Effective dose:** 75 mg twice daily (150 mg/day total)
- Strongest evidence level
- Statistically significant vs placebo
- Duration: 4 months

**Lower dose:** 50 mg twice daily (100 mg/day total)
- Some evidence
- Not statistically significant vs placebo

**Pediatric:** 50-150 mg daily for 4 months

## Allergic Rhinitis (Seasonal)

### Comparative Trials

**Schapowal 2002** - RCT vs Cetirizine (BMJ)
- Sample: 125 patients (butterbur: 61; cetirizine: 64)
- Setting: Four outpatient clinics in Switzerland and Germany
- Duration: Two consecutive weeks
- Interventions:
  - Butterbur: CO₂ extract tablets (Ze 339), one tablet four times daily
  - Cetirizine: One tablet in evening
- Outcomes:
  - SF-36 questionnaire scores: Comparable efficacy between groups
  - Clinical global impression scale: Median global improvement scores: 3 in both groups
- Adverse events:
  - Cetirizine: 8 of 12 adverse events involved sedative effects (despite cetirizine's non-sedating classification)
- Conclusion: Butterbur showed similar effectiveness to cetirizine, useful when avoiding antihistamine sedation

**Thomet 2002** - Clinical trial with inflammatory mediator measurements (Int Immunopharmacol)
- Extract: Ze339 standardized to 8 mg petasins
- Dosing: Three times daily for one week
- Primary outcomes:
  - Day- and nighttime nasal symptoms: Significantly improved after 5 days
  - Nasal resistance (rhinomanometry):
    - Baseline: 403.5±62.0 ml
    - Post-treatment: 844.8±38.8 ml (significant increase in airflow)
- Inflammatory mediator reductions at 5 days:
  - Histamine: 153.7±32.1 pg/ml → 53.0±8.4 pg/ml
  - LTB4: 313.1±46.5 pg/ml → 180.6±32.2 pg/ml
  - Cysteinyl-LT: 137.0±42.2 pg/ml → 70.1±16.5 pg/ml
- Secondary outcomes:
  - Quality-of-life scores: Significantly improved
  - Lymphocyte distribution: No effects observed
  - Immune cell mediator generation: No effects observed

### Observational Studies

**Käufeler 2006** - Postmarketing surveillance (Advances in Therapy)
- Sample: 580 patients with seasonal allergic rhinitis
- Duration: 2 weeks
- Dosage: Average of 2 tablets daily (8 mg petasines per tablet)
- Extract: Carbon dioxide extract from Petasites hybridus L. leaves
- Outcomes:
  - Symptom improvement: 90% of patients
  - Efficacy positively rated: 80% of participants
  - Tolerability positively rated: 92%
  - Quality of life improvement: 80%
- Combination therapy:
  - 44% received additional antiallergic medication
  - Combination therapy did not exceed monotherapy benefits
- Adverse events: 3.8% of cases (predominantly nonspecific gastrointestinal complaints)

**Blosa 2021** - Observational study in Switzerland (Pharmaceuticals)
- Sample: 226 patients from Swiss outpatient clinics
- Duration: Mean of 63 days; ~75% treated for at least 4 weeks
- Treatment groups:
  - Monotherapy with Ze 339: 58.5%
  - Ze 339 + other antiallergic/sympathomimetic drugs: 41.5%
- Dosing: 1-3 tablets daily at baseline
- Primary outcomes:
  - Allergic symptom scores: Statistically significant reduction (p < 0.001)
  - Inflammatory symptom scores: Statistically significant reduction (p < 0.001)
  - Quality of life scores: Improved
- Safety:
  - Tolerability: Excellent
  - Adverse events: Only 3 mild gastrointestinal events reported among all participants

**Guo 2007** - Systematic review of herbal medicines (Ann Allergy Asthma Immunol)
- Included: 16 RCTs testing 10 different herbal products
- Butterbur trials: 6 RCTs on Petasites hybridus
- Key findings:
  - P. hybridus is superior to placebo or similarly effective compared with nonsedative antihistamines for intermittent allergic rhinitis
  - Methodological quality: median score 4 out of 5
  - Limitation: 3 large trials had financial support from manufacturer
  - Recommendation: Larger, independently-funded trials needed for confirmation

### Clinical Dosing Recommendations - Allergic Rhinitis

**Standard dose:** One tablet (Ze 339) 3-4 times daily
- Standardization: 8 mg petasines per tablet
- Duration: 2 weeks typical
- Extract: Carbon dioxide extract from leaves

## Asthma

**Danesch 2004** - Open trial (Alternative Medicine Review)
- Sample: 80 participants (64 adults and 16 children/adolescents)
- Treatment: Petadolex (butterbur root extract)
- Duration: 4 months total (2 months with extract, 2 months optional intake)
- Key findings:
  - Asthma attack frequency, duration, and severity all decreased
  - Peak flow and forced expiratory volume (FEV1) improved
  - All measured symptoms improved during therapy
  - More than 40% of patients using asthma medications at baseline reduced intake by study end
- Safety: Effective and safe therapy
- Notes: Concomitant asthma medications permitted during study

### Clinical Dosing Recommendations - Asthma

**Standard dose:** 50 mg/day
- Duration: 1-2 months
- Note: Improved bronchial hyperresponsiveness in small trial (N=16)

## Clinical Outcomes Summary

### Migraine Prophylaxis
**Efficacy:**
- 48% reduction in attack frequency with 75 mg BID (vs 26% placebo)
- 68% of patients achieve ≥50% reduction (vs 49% placebo)
- Effective dose: 150 mg/day (75 mg BID)

**Pediatric:**
- 77% achieve ≥50% reduction in attack frequency
- 63% overall reduction in attacks

### Allergic Rhinitis
**Efficacy:**
- Similar effectiveness to cetirizine
- 90% symptom improvement in postmarketing study
- Significant reduction in inflammatory mediators (histamine, leukotrienes)

**Advantages:**
- Non-sedating (vs antihistamines)
- Addresses both early allergic and late inflammatory symptoms

### Tolerability Across Indications
- Excellent tolerability in most studies
- Most common adverse effect: mild gastrointestinal (burping, eructation)
- Adverse event rates: 3.8-7.4% in clinical trials

## Mechanism of Action (Borlak 2022)

**Nociception modulation:**
- Petasins dampen nociception by desensitizing calcium-conducting TRP ion channels

**Anti-inflammatory:**
- Suppress inflammatory mediators through enzyme inhibition

**Migraine-specific:**
- Block calcitonin-gene related peptide (CGRP) signaling - central to migraine pathophysiology

**Clinical efficacy confirmation:**
- Migraine attack frequency reduced significantly at 150 mg/day in RCTs
- No clinically relevant hepatic dysfunction reported in RCTs using Petadolex®

---

## Research Gaps

- Need for larger, independently-funded trials (many trials had manufacturer support)
- Long-term safety studies (>16 weeks) needed
- Head-to-head comparisons with standard migraine prophylaxis medications
- No dedicated WHO monograph found for Petasites
