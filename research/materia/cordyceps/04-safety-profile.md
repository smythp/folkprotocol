# Cordyceps - Safety Profile

Comprehensive safety data including contraindications, drug interactions, pregnancy/lactation guidance, and adverse events from clinical trials.

---

## Regulatory Safety Status

### FDA Classification
**Status:** Designated as **"Generally Regarded as Safe" (GRAS)**

**Implication:** Recognized as safe for use in dietary supplements in the United States

**Source:** NCBI LiverTox 2023 ([NCBI Books NBK615916](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK615916/))

---

### Chinese Regulatory Status
**Chinese Pharmacopoeia:** Official herbal drug since 1964

**Chinese FDA Approval (since 2002):**
- Over **50 medicines** with cultured Cordyceps approved
- **2 dietary supplements** approved
- Widespread clinical use throughout China

**Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph 2011

---

## Hepatotoxicity (Liver Safety)

### Clinical Evidence
**Hepatotoxicity:** **NOT associated with:**
- Serum aminotransferase elevations during therapy
- Clinically apparent liver injury
- ALT (alanine aminotransferase) elevations

**Clinical Trials:** Multiple short- and long-term trials show adverse effects are uncommon and minimal with **no mention of hepatotoxicity**

**Source:** NCBI LiverTox 2023

---

### Animal Studies (Liver Tissue Analysis)
**Extended Use (up to 3 weeks in animals):**
- **No hepatic steatosis** (fatty liver)
- **No necrosis** (tissue death)
- **No inflammation**
- **No fibrosis** (scarring)

**Conclusion:** Safe for liver tissue even with extended use

**Source:** NCBI LiverTox 2023

---

## Preclinical Toxicology Studies

### Acute Toxicity (ICR Mice)
**Maximum Dose Tested:** 20 g/kg oral administration

**Results:**
- **No mortality** within 14 days
- **No tissue abnormalities** detected

**LD50 (Lethal Dose 50%):** **>5 g/kg**
- **Classification:** Non-toxic

**Maximum Tolerable Dose:** **>20 g/kg**

**Source:** Long et al. 2021 ([PubMed 33220357](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33220357/))

---

### Extreme Dose Testing
**Dose:** 80 g/kg/day for 7 days (intraperitoneal injection in mice)

**Result:** **No fatality**

**Implication:** Exceptionally wide safety margin; dose far exceeds any clinical use

**Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph 2011

---

### Genotoxicity Testing

**Chromosomal Aberration Test (Chinese hamster lung cells):**
- **No dose-dependent increase** detected
- Doses tested: Up to 5,000 μg per plate

**Ames Bacterial Mutation Assay (5 bacterial strains):**
- **No mutagenic activity**
- Doses tested: 128-5,000 μg per plate

**Micronucleus Testing (bone marrow cells):**
- **No significant differences** versus negative controls

**Conclusion:** No genetic toxicity or mutagenicity

**Source:** Long et al. 2021

---

## Clinical Trial Safety Data

### Long COVID Study (110 participants, 12 weeks)
**Adverse Events:**
- **Total patients with adverse events:** 10 (17.2%)
- **Cordyceps group:** 6 patients (20.0%) with 12 total TEAEs (treatment-emergent adverse events)
- **Placebo group:** 4 patients (14.3%) with 4 TEAEs
- **Severe events:** None

**Conclusion:** No severe adverse events related to interventions; similar rates to placebo

**Source:** Acta Materia Medica 2025 Long COVID trial

---

### Chronic Bronchitis Study (240 participants, 48 weeks)
**Dosing:** Bailing capsule 6 g/day for 48 weeks

**Adverse Events:**
- **Bailing group:** 29.6%
- **Placebo group:** 30.4%

**Conclusion:** **Similar adverse event rates between groups**; no signal for Cordyceps-specific toxicity

**Source:** Shu et al. 2024 ([PubMed 39193337](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39193337/))

---

### Immune Function Study (20 participants, 8 weeks)
**Dosing:** Fermented C. militaris beverage (2.85 mg cordycepin per serving) for 8 weeks

**Safety Parameters Monitored:**
- Liver function markers: **No toxicity**
- Kidney function markers: **No toxicity**
- Blood components: **No toxicity**
- Blood sugar indices: **No differences** between groups
- Lipid indices: **No differences** between groups

**Conclusion:** No liver, kidney, or blood component toxicity

**Source:** Ontawong et al. 2024 ([PubMed 38580687](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38580687/))

---

### Cordyceps cicadae Safety Assessment (49 participants, 3 months)
**Dosing:** 1.05 g freeze-dried C. cicadae mycelium once daily for 3 months

**Safety Parameters:**
- **Kidney function:** No clinically significant changes
- **Liver function:** No changes in markers
- **Blood electrolytes:** Stable levels
- **Lipid profiles:** Stable

**Adverse Events:** **No side effects or adverse feelings reported** by participants

**Conclusion:** Safe for development as functional health food

**Source:** Tsai et al. 2021 ([PubMed 32702252](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32702252/))

---

### Exercise Performance Study (20 participants, 12 weeks)
**Dosing:** Cs-4 1,000 mg/day for 12 weeks (elderly 50-75 years)

**Adverse Events:** **None reported**

**Source:** Chen et al. 2010 ([PubMed 20804368](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20804368/))

---

### COPD Systematic Review (15 studies, 1,238 participants)
**Adverse Events:** **No serious adverse events reported** across all studies

**Limitation:** No studies were placebo-controlled or of high methodological quality

**Source:** Yu et al. 2019 ([PubMed 31073318](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31073318/))

### Hemodialysis Systematic Review (12 studies, 655 participants)
**Adverse Events:** **No explicit side effects or complications mentioned**

## Contraindications

### Evidence-Based Contraindications
**Status:** **No specific contraindications identified** in scientific literature

**Rationale:**
- FDA GRAS designation supports general safety
- Extensive clinical trial data shows minimal adverse events
- No documented serious reactions or interactions

---

### Theoretical Precautions (Based on Pharmacology)

**Bleeding Disorders:**
- Traditional use includes "stop bleeding," but no clinical evidence of anticoagulant or antiplatelet effects
- **No documented bleeding events** in clinical trials
- **Theoretical caution** if combining with anticoagulants (warfarin, heparin) due to lack of interaction studies

**Autoimmune Diseases:**
- Cordyceps has **immunomodulatory effects** (enhances NK cells, CD4+ cells, cytokine production)
- **Theoretical concern** for autoimmune conditions (lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, MS)
- **However:** No clinical evidence of harm; immunomodulation appears balanced (both pro- and anti-inflammatory cytokines affected)

**Organ Transplant Recipients:**
- **Paradox:** Bailing Capsule specifically indicated for post-renal transplant support in China
- Used to **improve function and reduce infection** after transplant
- Suggests immunomodulation does not cause rejection
- **Recommendation:** Use only under medical supervision if on immunosuppressants

---

## Drug Interactions

### Evidence-Based Interactions
**Status:** **No documented drug interactions** in clinical literature

**Clinical Trial Context:**
- Many trials involved patients on concurrent medications (chemotherapy, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, dialysis medications)
- **No interactions reported**

---

### Theoretical Interactions (Requiring Monitoring)

**Immunosuppressants:**
- Cordyceps enhances immune function (NK cells, T cells)
- **Theoretical concern:** May counteract immunosuppressive drugs (cyclosporine, tacrolimus, prednisone)
- **Clinical context:** Bailing Capsule used in transplant patients without reported rejection issues
- **Recommendation:** Use under medical supervision; monitor immunosuppressive drug levels

**Anticoagulants/Antiplatelets:**
- **No documented interaction**
- Traditional use includes hemostatic properties
- **Theoretical caution** with warfarin, heparin, clopidogrel, aspirin
- **Recommendation:** Monitor INR if combining with warfarin

**Antidiabetic Drugs:**
- Cordyceps may affect blood glucose metabolism (preclinical evidence)
- **No hypoglycemia reported in clinical trials**
- **Recommendation:** Monitor blood glucose if diabetic and on insulin or oral hypoglycemics

**Chemotherapy Agents:**
- **Clinical evidence:** Cordyceps used as adjuvant with lung cancer chemotherapy (928 participants)
- **Result:** Reduced adverse reactions (myelosuppression, leukopenia) without compromising efficacy
- **Conclusion:** Likely safe and potentially beneficial as adjuvant
- **Recommendation:** Use under oncologist supervision

## Pregnancy and Lactation

### Evidence Status
**Human Studies:** **No clinical data available** on use during pregnancy or lactation

**Animal Studies:** No reproductive toxicity studies identified in reviewed literature

---

### Recommendations (Conservative Approach)

**Pregnancy:**
- **Avoid use** due to lack of safety data
- **No evidence of harm**, but also no evidence of safety
- **Traditional use:** Not specifically documented for pregnancy in TCM texts

**Lactation:**
- **Avoid use** due to lack of safety data
- Unknown if bioactive compounds (polysaccharides, cordycepin, adenosine) pass into breast milk
- Unknown effects on infant

**Rationale:** Standard precautionary principle for herbs lacking specific pregnancy/lactation safety studies

---

## Pediatric Use

**Evidence:** No clinical trials in pediatric populations identified

**Recommendation:** Insufficient data to recommend use in children

**Traditional Context:** Chinese Pharmacopoeia does not specify pediatric dosing or contraindications

---

## Geriatric Use

**Evidence:** Multiple studies specifically in elderly populations (50-75+ years)

**Safety Profile:**
- **Chen et al. 2010:** 20 healthy elderly (50-75 years), 12 weeks, 1,000 mg/day Cs-4 - **No adverse events**
- **Chinese 2003 study:** 37 elderly, 6 weeks, 3 g/day Cs-4 - Aerobic capacity improved, **safe**

**Conclusion:** **Safe for elderly populations** based on clinical evidence

---

## Long-Term Safety

### Human Studies
**Longest Duration:** 48 weeks (chronic bronchitis study, 240 participants)

**Dosing:** 6 g/day Bailing capsule

**Results:** Adverse events similar to placebo (29.6% vs 30.4%)

**Conclusion:** Safe for at least 12 months at therapeutic doses

---

### Animal Studies
**Duration:** Up to 3 weeks

**Findings:** No hepatic, renal, or systemic toxicity

**Limitation:** Longer-term animal studies (>3 months) not identified in literature

## Dosing Safety Thresholds

### Clinical Dosing Range (Safe)
**General tonification:** 3-9 g/day (traditional decoction)

**Standardized extracts:** 1-6 g/day

**Specific conditions:**
- Exercise performance: 1-3 g/day
- Renal dysfunction: 3-6 g/day
- Lung cancer adjuvant: 6 g/day
- Chronic bronchitis: 6 g/day

**Duration:** Up to 48 weeks documented as safe

---

### Animal Safety Thresholds
**LD50:** >5 g/kg (non-toxic classification)

**Maximum tolerable dose:** >20 g/kg (single dose in mice)

**Extreme test:** 80 g/kg/day × 7 days (no fatality)

**Human Equivalent (70 kg adult, based on LD50):** >350 g single dose (far exceeds any clinical use)

## Quality and Purity Concerns

### Contamination Risks (Not Cordyceps-Specific)

**Heavy Metals:**
- Wild-harvested herbs may contain heavy metals (lead, cadmium, mercury) from environmental contamination
- **Recommendation:** Use certified, tested products from reputable manufacturers

**Microbial Contamination:**
- Cultured mycelium products should meet microbial standards
- **Recommendation:** Use products with third-party testing (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab)

**Adulteration:**
- Substitution with cheaper species or synthetic compounds
- **Recommendation:** Choose products with authentication (HPLC-MS/MS chemical fingerprinting, novel glycoside markers)

## Summary: Safety Profile

**Overall Safety:** **Excellent**

**FDA Status:** Generally Regarded as Safe (GRAS)

**Clinical Trial Safety:** Adverse events minimal and similar to placebo across multiple trials (up to 48 weeks, up to 6 g/day)

**Hepatotoxicity:** None documented

**Nephrotoxicity:** None documented (used therapeutically for kidney support)

**Genotoxicity:** None (Ames test, chromosomal aberration, micronucleus all negative)

**Acute Toxicity:** LD50 >5 g/kg (non-toxic); extreme doses (80 g/kg/day) caused no fatality

**Contraindications:** No specific contraindications identified; theoretical caution with immunosuppressants, autoimmune diseases, bleeding disorders (no documented cases)

**Drug Interactions:** None documented; theoretical monitoring recommended with immunosuppressants, anticoagulants, antidiabetic drugs

**Pregnancy/Lactation:** Avoid due to lack of safety data (no evidence of harm, but no evidence of safety)

**Pediatric Use:** Insufficient data

**Geriatric Use:** Safe and effective based on multiple trials

**Long-Term Safety:** Safe for at least 48 weeks at 6 g/day

**Quality Concerns:** Ensure third-party tested products to avoid heavy metal contamination and adulteration

---

## Recommendations for Clinical Practice

1. **General Use:** Safe for most adults at dosages 1-6 g/day
2. **Medical Supervision Recommended:**
   - Organ transplant recipients on immunosuppressants
   - Patients on anticoagulants (monitor INR)
   - Diabetics on insulin/oral hypoglycemics (monitor glucose)
   - Cancer patients on chemotherapy (coordinate with oncologist)
3. **Avoid:**
   - Pregnancy
   - Lactation
   - Pediatric use (insufficient data)
4. **Product Quality:** Choose standardized extracts (Cs-4, JinShuiBao, Bailing) with third-party testing over crude herb
5. **Monitoring:** No specific laboratory monitoring required for healthy adults; consider periodic liver/kidney function tests if using long-term (>6 months) at high doses (>6 g/day)
