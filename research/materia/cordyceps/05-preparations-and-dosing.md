# Cordyceps - Preparations and Dosing

Comprehensive guide to traditional preparations, standardized extracts, and evidence-based dosing regimens.

---

## Traditional Preparations (Chinese Medicine)

### Decoction (Tang 汤)

**Form:** Whole dried fungus-larva complex boiled in water

**Traditional Dosing:**
- **3-9 g crude drug** as decoction
- Standard traditional dose range from Chinese Pharmacopoeia

**Preparation Method:**
1. Add 3-9 g whole Cordyceps to water
2. Boil/simmer to extract active compounds
3. Strain and drink as medicinal tea

**Source:** Chinese Pharmacopoeia traditional dosing; Lin & Li 2011

**Note:** Traditional preparation used wild-harvested Cordyceps sinensis (now prohibitively expensive and nearly extinct). Modern practice uses cultivated alternatives.

## Modern Standardized Extracts

### Cs-4 (CordyMax)

**Source:** Fermented mycelium of Paecilomyces hepiali (now classified as Hirsutella sinensis)

**Recognition:** Well recognized by Chinese Pharmacopoeia

**Clinical Studies:** Over 30 clinical trials

**Standardization:**
- Polysaccharides
- Adenosine
- Mannitol

**Evidence-Based Dosing:**

**Exercise Performance (Elderly 50-75 years):**
- **Dose:** 333 mg capsules, **3 times daily** (1,000 mg/day total)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Effect:** 10.5% increase in metabolic threshold, 8.5% increase in ventilatory threshold
- **Study:** Chen et al. 2010 ([PubMed 20804368](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20804368/))

**Alternative Elderly Dosing:**
- **Dose:** 3 g/day
- **Duration:** 6 weeks
- **Effect:** Enhanced aerobic capacity and respiratory function
- **Study:** Chinese investigators 2003

**Long COVID:**
- **Dose:** Cs-4 extract (specific dose not published)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Effect:** -10.1 point improvement in Long COVID symptoms (p < 0.001)
- **Study:** Hong Kong 2025 ([ScienceOpen](https://www.scienceopen.com/hosted-document?doi=10.15212/AMM-2024-0089))

**Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph; clinical trials

---

### Bailing Capsule

**Source:** C. sinensis mycelia preparation

**Primary Indication:** Post-renal transplant support (improved function, reduced infection)

**Additional Uses:** Chronic bronchitis, renal dysfunction

**Evidence-Based Dosing:**

**Chronic Bronchitis:**
- **Dose:** 2.0 g **3 times daily** (6 g/day total)
- **Duration:** 48 weeks
- **Effect:** 72% reduction in acute exacerbations (0.43 vs 1.56 in placebo, p < 0.001)
- **Study:** Shu et al. 2024 ([PubMed 39193337](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39193337/))

**Renal Dysfunction:**
- **Dose:** Typically 3-6 g/day
- **Duration:** Variable (weeks to months)
- **Effect:** Reduced creatinine, improved urine osmolality, increased creatinine clearance
- **Study:** Wu et al. 2025 systematic review ([PubMed 39839641](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39839641/))

**Source:** Clinical trials; Chinese clinical practice

---

### JinShuiBao

**Source:** Commercial Cs-4 capsule product

**Clinical Use:** Throughout China

**Revenue:** Several million US dollars annually

**Indications:**
- Immunosuppression support
- Renal function improvement
- Diabetic nephropathy

**Diabetic Nephropathy Dosing:**
- **Dose:** 0.5-2 g **3 times daily** (1.5-6 g/day total)
- **Combination:** With ACE inhibitors or ARBs
- **Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph recommendations

**Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph; Chinese Pharmacopoeia

---

### Fermented Cordyceps militaris Beverage

**Evidence-Based Dosing:**

**Immune Function Enhancement:**
- **Dose:** 2.85 mg cordycepin per serving (in fermented beverage)
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Effect:**
  - Males: NK cell activity increase at 4 weeks (p = 0.049)
  - Females: NK cell activity increase at 8 weeks (p = 0.023)
- **Study:** Ontawong et al. 2024 ([PubMed 38580687](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38580687/))

**Source:** Clinical trial

---

### CBG-CS-2 Extract

**Source:** Paecilomyces hepiali mycelium extract

**Evidence-Based Dosing:**

**Immune Modulation (Healthy Adults):**
- **Dose:** 1.68 g/day
- **Form:** Capsules
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Effect:** 38.8 ± 17.6% enhancement of NK cell activity from baseline (p < 0.019)
- **Study:** Jung et al. 2019 ([PubMed 30925876](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30925876/))

**Source:** Clinical trial

---

### Cordyceps cicadae Mycelium

**Evidence-Based Dosing:**

**Safety Assessment (Healthy Adults):**
- **Dose:** 1.05 g freeze-dried mycelium
- **Frequency:** Once daily
- **Duration:** 3 months
- **Safety:** No adverse effects; no changes in kidney, liver, electrolytes, lipids
- **Study:** Tsai et al. 2021 ([PubMed 32702252](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32702252/))

**Source:** Clinical trial

## Condition-Specific Dosing Guidelines

### Renal Dysfunction and Kidney Disease

**Chronic Renal Failure:**
- **Dose:** 3-6 g/day
- **Form:** Crude drug, Bailing, JinShuiBao, or Zhiling formulations
- **Duration:** Weeks to months
- **Evidence:** Systematic review (15 studies, 1,310 participants)
- **Source:** Wu et al. 2025; Chinese Pharmacopoeia

**Diabetic Nephropathy:**
- **Dose:** 0.5-2 g **3 times daily** (1.5-6 g/day total)
- **Combination:** With ACE inhibitors or ARBs
- **Form:** JinShuiBao or similar standardized extract
- **Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph

**Post-Renal Transplant:**
- **Form:** Bailing Capsule
- **Dose:** Typically 3-6 g/day (specific dosing per medical supervision)
- **Purpose:** Improve function, reduce infection
- **Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph

---

### Respiratory Conditions

**Chronic Bronchitis:**
- **Dose:** 6 g/day (2 g × 3 times daily)
- **Form:** Bailing capsule
- **Duration:** Minimum 12 weeks; optimal 48 weeks for sustained exacerbation reduction
- **Evidence:** RCT (240 participants)
- **Source:** Shu et al. 2024

**COPD (Stable, GOLD Stages 2-3):**
- **Dose:** Variable across studies (typically 3-6 g/day)
- **Form:** Oral Cordyceps preparations
- **Duration:** Weeks to months
- **Evidence:** Systematic review (15 studies, 1,238 participants)
- **Limitations:** No high-quality placebo-controlled studies
- **Source:** Yu et al. 2019

---

### Cancer Support (Adjuvant Therapy)

**Lung Cancer:**
- **Dose:** 6 g/day
- **Schedule:** 21 days per cycle, **3-4 cycles**
- **Form:** Cordyceps sinensis (various preparations)
- **Evidence:** Systematic review (12 trials, 928 participants)
- **Effects:** Improved tumor response (RR 1.17), enhanced immune function, reduced chemotherapy side effects
- **Source:** Wang et al. 2024

---

### Long COVID

**Dose:** Cs-4 extract (specific dose not published in abstract)

**Duration:** 12 weeks

**Evidence:** RCT (110 participants)

**Effects:**
- Long COVID symptoms: -10.1 points (p < 0.001)
- Fatigue: -8.1 points (p = 0.011)
- Improved insomnia, respiratory symptoms, anxiety/depression

**Source:** Hong Kong 2025

---

### Immune Function Enhancement

**General Immune Support:**
- **Dose:** 1.68 g/day (CBG-CS-2 extract)
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Evidence:** 38.8% NK cell enhancement
- **Source:** Jung et al. 2019

**Alternative (Cordyceps militaris beverage):**
- **Dose:** 2.85 mg cordycepin per serving
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Evidence:** Significant NK cell increase, reduced inflammatory cytokines
- **Source:** Ontawong et al. 2024

---

### Exercise Performance and Anti-Fatigue

**Healthy Elderly (50-75 years):**
- **Dose:** 1,000 mg/day Cs-4 (333 mg × 3) **OR** 3 g/day
- **Duration:** 6-12 weeks
- **Evidence:** Improved metabolic threshold, ventilatory threshold, aerobic capacity
- **Source:** Chen et al. 2010; Chinese 2003 study

**Body Composition (with Training):**
- **Dose:** 20 mg/kg/day (Rhodiola/Cordyceps combination)
- **Duration:** 8 weeks with endurance training
- **Evidence:** Reduced body weight, BMI, fat mass; increased muscle
- **Source:** Liao et al. 2019

**Sufficient Dosing Guidance:**

## Tincture Preparation (Not Evidence-Based)

**Note:** No clinical trials identified using alcohol tinctures of Cordyceps. The following is extrapolated from general herbalism principles and traditional use.

**Theoretical Tincture Ratios:**
- **1:5 (dried herb:menstruum)** in 25-40% alcohol
- Polysaccharides are **not alcohol-soluble**; tinctures would primarily extract nucleosides, sterols, and smaller compounds

**Limitation:**
- Polysaccharides (3-8% dry weight) are major active constituents
- Alcohol tinctures would **miss** this key component
- **Not recommended** as primary preparation method

**Preferred:** Water-based extracts or dual extraction (hot water + alcohol, then combined)

## Tea/Infusion Dosing (Water Extract)

**Traditional Method:** Simmer 3-9 g whole Cordyceps in water for 20-30 minutes

**Modern Equivalent (Using Powder):**
- 1-3 g powdered extract per cup
- Steep or simmer for 10-15 minutes
- Consume 1-3 times daily

**Total Daily Dose:** 3-9 g (matches traditional decoction dosing)

**Advantage:** Extracts polysaccharides effectively (water-soluble)

---

## Standardized Extract Equivalency

### Common Commercial Standardization
**Standard:**
- 40% polysaccharides
- 8% mannitol
- 0.2% adenosine

**Extract Ratio:** Typically 10:1 or 20:1 (meaning 10-20 g crude herb → 1 g extract)

**Dosing Conversion:**
- **Traditional 6 g crude herb ≈ 300-600 mg standardized extract** (depending on ratio)
- However, clinical trials used **higher doses** (1-6 g standardized extract), suggesting conservative conversion

**Recommendation:** Follow clinical trial dosing for standardized extracts (1-6 g/day) rather than attempting equivalency calculations

---

## Bioavailability Considerations for Dosing

### Cordycepin Challenge
**Issue:** Rapid deamination by adenosine deaminase (half-life ~30 minutes)

**Implication:**
- Intact cordycepin not absorbed orally
- Metabolite 3'-deoxyinosine absorbed instead
- Clinical efficacy likely depends on **polysaccharides and nucleosides** more than cordycepin

**Dosing Strategy:**
- **Divided doses** (2-3 times daily) may maintain more consistent levels
- **Standardize to polysaccharides** rather than cordycepin for C. sinensis products

---

### Polysaccharide Bioavailability
**Issue:** Low bioavailability from whole mushroom material (25.9 mg/100 g after digestion)

**Solution:**
- Use **hot water extracts** or fermented mycelium (pre-digested)
- Standardized extracts more bioavailable than crude herb

## Duration of Use

### Short-Term (Acute Conditions)
**Duration:** 6-12 weeks

**Applications:**
- Immune support during illness
- Exercise performance enhancement
- Post-surgical recovery

**Evidence:** Multiple RCTs show effects within 4-12 weeks

---

### Medium-Term
**Duration:** 3-6 months

**Applications:**
- COPD management
- Diabetic nephropathy
- Long COVID recovery

**Evidence:** Long COVID study (12 weeks), chronic bronchitis (48 weeks)

---

### Long-Term (Chronic Conditions)
**Duration:** 6-12+ months

**Applications:**
- Chronic renal failure
- Chronic bronchitis
- Cancer adjuvant therapy (cycles)

**Evidence:** Chronic bronchitis RCT (48 weeks, safe and effective)

**Safety:** No serious adverse events in trials up to 48 weeks

---

## Timing of Administration

### General Recommendations
**Frequency:** 1-3 times daily (depending on total dose)

**Timing:**
- **With or without food** (not specified in most trials)
- **Divided doses** preferred for bioavailability (e.g., 2 g × 3 times daily rather than 6 g once daily)

**Example from Trials:**
- Bailing capsule: 2 g three times daily (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
- Cs-4: 333 mg three times daily

---

### Special Considerations
**Renal Patients:** No specific timing restrictions; safe with dialysis

**Cancer Patients:** Coordinate with chemotherapy cycles (typically 21-day cycles with Cordyceps daily)

**Immunosuppressed:** Use under medical supervision; timing relative to immunosuppressant doses not specified in literature

## Quality Control for Dosing Accuracy

### Choosing High-Quality Products

**Standardization Markers:**
- **Polysaccharides:** 40% (most important for C. sinensis)
- **Mannitol:** 8%
- **Adenosine:** 0.2%
- **Cordycepin:** 2-3% (for C. militaris products)

**Authentication Methods:**
- HPLC-MS/MS chemical fingerprinting
- Novel glycoside markers (Phe-o-glucose, Cyclo-Ala-Leu-rhamnose) for C. sinensis
- Third-party testing (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab)

---

### Species Considerations for Dosing

**C. sinensis (Ophiocordyceps sinensis):**
- Higher polysaccharides and mannitol
- Low/absent cordycepin
- **Preferred for:** Kidney support, respiratory conditions, general tonification
- **Dosing:** 3-6 g/day

**C. militaris:**
- 5-15× more cordycepin than C. sinensis
- Lower polysaccharides
- **Preferred for:** Immune enhancement (NK cells), anticancer support
- **Dosing:** 1-3 g/day (lower due to higher cordycepin content)

**Note:** Both species show clinical efficacy; choice depends on indication and desired active compounds.

## Summary: Practical Dosing Recommendations

### General Health and Tonification
- **Dose:** 1-3 g/day standardized extract (or 3-6 g crude)
- **Form:** Cs-4, JinShuiBao, or similar
- **Frequency:** 1-2 times daily
- **Duration:** Continuous or cycled (8 weeks on, 2 weeks off)

### Kidney Support (Chronic Renal Failure, Diabetic Nephropathy)
- **Dose:** 3-6 g/day
- **Form:** Bailing, JinShuiBao
- **Frequency:** 2-3 times daily
- **Combination:** With ACE inhibitors/ARBs for diabetic nephropathy
- **Duration:** Months to years (under medical supervision)

### Respiratory Conditions (COPD, Chronic Bronchitis)
- **Dose:** 6 g/day
- **Form:** Bailing capsule or similar
- **Frequency:** 2 g × 3 times daily
- **Duration:** Minimum 12 weeks; optimal 48 weeks for chronic bronchitis

### Cancer Support (Adjuvant)
- **Dose:** 6 g/day
- **Schedule:** 21 days per cycle, 3-4 cycles
- **Coordination:** With chemotherapy (under oncologist supervision)

### Immune Enhancement
- **Dose:** 1.68-2.85 mg cordycepin (C. militaris) OR 1-3 g Cs-4
- **Frequency:** Once daily
- **Duration:** 8 weeks

### Exercise Performance (Elderly)
- **Dose:** 1-3 g/day Cs-4
- **Frequency:** Divided into 2-3 doses
- **Duration:** 6-12 weeks

### Long COVID Recovery
- **Dose:** Cs-4 extract (1-3 g/day estimated based on other trials)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks

---

## Final Recommendations

1. **Prefer Standardized Extracts** (Cs-4, Bailing, JinShuiBao) over crude herb for consistent dosing and bioavailability
2. **Divide Daily Dose** into 2-3 administrations for better bioavailability
3. **Match Species to Indication:** C. sinensis for kidney/respiratory; C. militaris for immune/cancer support
4. **Use Therapeutic Doses:** 3-6 g/day for clinical effects (1-3 g/day for general wellness)
5. **Ensure Quality:** Third-party tested, standardized to polysaccharides and adenosine
6. **Allow Adequate Duration:** Minimum 8-12 weeks for most conditions
7. **Medical Supervision:** For kidney disease, cancer, immunosuppression, or doses >6 g/day
