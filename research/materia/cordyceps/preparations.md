# Cordyceps - Preparations and Dosing

## Standardized Commercial Extracts (Clinical Trial Evidence)

### Cs-4 (CordyMax)

**Source**: Fermented mycelium of Paecilomyces hepialid

**Regulatory Status**:
- Recognized by Chinese Pharmacopoeia
- Over 30 clinical trials
- FDA GRAS (Generally Regarded As Safe)

**Standardization**:
- Polysaccharides: 40%
- Mannitol: 8%
- Adenosine: 0.2%

**Clinical Evidence**:
1. **Exercise Performance (Chen 2010)**
   - Dose: 333 mg capsules, 3× daily (1,000 mg/day total)
   - Duration: 12 weeks
   - Population: Healthy elderly (ages 50-75)
   - Results: Metabolic threshold +10.5%, ventilatory threshold +8.5%
   - Source: [PubMed 20804368](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20804368/)

2. **Aerobic Capacity (Chinese 2003)**
   - Dose: 3 g/day
   - Duration: 6 weeks
   - Population: Healthy elderly Chinese
   - Results: Enhanced aerobic capacity and respiratory function

**Typical Dosing**: 1-3 g/day for general health, exercise, and immune support

---

### JinShuiBao

**Source**: Commercial Cs-4 capsule product

**Clinical Use**: Throughout China; several million USD in annual revenue

**Indications**:
- Immunosuppression
- Renal function support
- Diabetic nephropathy

**Standardization**: Similar to Cs-4 (40% polysaccharides, 8% mannitol, 0.2% adenosine)

**Clinical Evidence**:
- Included in renal dysfunction meta-analysis (Wu 2025, 1,310 participants)
- Diabetic nephropathy trials

**Typical Dosing**: 3-6 g/day for renal conditions

---

### Bailing Capsule

**Source**: C. sinensis mycelia preparation

**Primary Indication**: Post-renal transplant support

**Clinical Evidence**:
- Improved kidney function
- Reduced infection rates post-transplant
- Included in renal dysfunction meta-analysis

**Population**: Renal transplant patients (necessarily on immunosuppressants)

**Typical Dosing**: As directed for renal support (dosing range not specified in sources)

---

### Fermented C. militaris Beverage

**Source**: Fermented Cordyceps militaris functional beverage

**Standardization**: 2.85 mg cordycepin per serving

**Clinical Evidence** (Ontawong 2024):
- 20 participants, 8 weeks
- NK cell activity significantly increased (males p=0.049, females p=0.023)
- Reduced IL-1β (males, p=0.049) and IL-6 (females, p=0.047)
- No toxicity (liver, kidney, blood, metabolic markers)
- Source: [PubMed 38580687](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38580687/)

**Dosing**: One serving daily (2.85 mg cordycepin) for immune support

**Note**: C. militaris contains 5-15× more cordycepin than C. sinensis but has bioavailability challenges (see Active Compounds document)

---

## Condition-Specific Dosing (Evidence-Based)

### Chronic Renal Failure
**Dose**: 3-6 g/day
**Preparations**: Bailing Capsule, JinShuiBao, Zhiling formulations
**Evidence**: Meta-analysis, 15 RCTs, 1,310 participants
- Pooled sensitivity 0.89, specificity 0.69
- Reduced creatinine, improved clearance
- Source: [PubMed 39839641](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39839641/)

**Duration**: Ongoing as adjunctive therapy

---

### Hemodialysis Support
**Dose**: Not specified in meta-analysis
**Preparations**: Various Cs-4 formulations
**Evidence**: 12 trials, 655 participants
- CRP -3.44 mg/L, albumin +3.07 g/L, hemoglobin +9.56 g/L
- Source: [PubMed 32186018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32186018/)

**Quality Note**: Evidence insufficient to recommend use due to limited trials and unclear methodological quality

---

### Lung Cancer Adjuvant
**Dose**: 6 g/day
**Schedule**: 21 days per cycle, 3-4 cycles
**Evidence**: Meta-analysis, 12 RCTs, 928 participants
- Tumor response RR 1.17, CD4+ +4.98%, NK cells +4.17%
- Reduced myelosuppression (RR 0.38), leukopenia (RR 0.76), radiation pneumonitis (RR 0.74)
- Source: [PubMed 38484953](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38484953/)

**Usage**: Concurrent with chemotherapy/radiation

---

### COPD (GOLD Stages 2-3)
**Dose**: Not specified in meta-analysis
**Preparations**: Oral Cordyceps sinensis (various forms)
**Evidence**: 15 trials, 1,238 participants
- Improved FEV1%, 6MWD, SGRQ scores
- No serious adverse events
- Source: [PubMed 31073318](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31073318/)

**Quality Note**: No placebo-controlled studies of high methodological quality

---

### Long COVID
**Dose**: Cs4 extract (specific dose not published)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Evidence**: RCT, 110 participants
- Symptom improvement MD -10.1 (p<0.001), fatigue -8.1 (p=0.011)
- Source: [Acta Materia Medica](https://www.scienceopen.com/hosted-document?doi=10.15212/AMM-2024-0089)

**Estimated Dosing**: Likely in 3-4.5 g/day range based on other Cs4 studies

---

### Exercise Performance (Elderly)
**Dose**: 1-3 g/day Cs-4
**Duration**: 6-12 weeks
**Evidence**:
- 1 g/day × 12 weeks: Metabolic threshold +10.5% (Chen 2010, 20 participants)
- 3 g/day × 6 weeks: Enhanced aerobic capacity (Chinese 2003, 37 participants)

**Population**: Ages 50-75, healthy elderly

---

### Immune Support
**Dose**: 2.85 mg cordycepin/day (C. militaris beverage) OR 1.5 g/day C. militaris powder
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Evidence**:
- 2.85 mg cordycepin: NK cell activity increased (Ontawong 2024, 20 participants)
- 1.5 g/day C. militaris: Enhanced cell-mediated immunity (Korean study)

**Note**: C. militaris-specific preparations; different profile from C. sinensis

---

### Diabetic Nephropathy
**Dose**: 0.5-2 g three times daily (1.5-6 g/day total)
**Combination**: With ACE inhibitors or ARBs
**Source**: [NCBI Bookshelf](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK92758/)

**Evidence Level**: Monograph recommendation; specific RCT data not detailed in bibliography

---

## Traditional Preparations

### Decoction (Traditional Chinese Medicine)

**Source**: Chinese Pharmacopoeia (since 1964)

**Preparation Method**:
1. Measure 3-9 g crude dried cordyceps (whole fungus-caterpillar complex)
2. Simmer in water for extended period (traditionally 1-2 hours)
3. Strain and consume liquid

**Traditional Dosing**: 3-9 g crude drug daily

**Traditional Schedule**: Daily or as needed for tonification

**Traditional Applications**:
- Fatigue and weakness
- Chronic cough
- Hyposexuality
- Post-illness recovery
- Renal dysfunction

**Modern Note**: Due to wild C. sinensis scarcity (>90% yield decline) and extreme cost (~$25,000/kg in 2007), traditional decoction rarely used with authentic wild material today.

---

### Duck or Chicken Soup (Traditional Chinese Preparation)

**Traditional Method**:
1. Add 3-5 g dried cordyceps to whole duck or chicken
2. Slow-cook for several hours
3. Consume meat and broth

**Traditional Use**: Nourishing recovery meal, post-illness tonification

**Modern Status**: Cultural practice continues but typically with cultivated cordyceps due to cost

---

## Modern Extract Forms (Not Directly Studied in RCTs)

### Tincture (Alcohol Extract)

**Typical Ratios**: 1:3 to 1:5 (dried herb to menstruum)

**Preparation Method**:
1. Macerate dried cordyceps in 40-60% alcohol
2. Steep for 2-4 weeks with periodic agitation
3. Press and filter

**Theoretical Dosing** (extrapolated from crude drug equivalents):
- For 3-4.5 g crude drug equivalent:
- 1:3 tincture: 9-13.5 mL/day
- 1:5 tincture: 15-22.5 mL/day

**Evidence Level**: **None** - no clinical trials using tincture form
**Bioavailability Note**: Alcohol extraction may not optimally extract polysaccharides (water-soluble); may favor nucleosides and small molecules

**Recommendation**: Standardized extracts (Cs-4, JinShuiBao) preferred due to clinical evidence

---

### Powdered Mushroom (Whole Fruiting Body or Mycelium)

**Dosing**: 3-6 g/day (based on crude drug traditional dosing)

**Preparation**:
- Ground whole dried cordyceps
- Encapsulated or mixed in beverages/food

**Evidence Level**: Traditional use basis; no specific RCTs using simple powdered form
**Quality Concern**: Wild vs. cultivated; species authentication; standardization lacking

**Bioavailability**: Lower than standardized extracts due to:
- Cell wall chitin not fully broken down
- Polysaccharides bound in matrix
- Cordycepin rapidly degraded by adenosine deaminase (if present)

**Recommendation**: If using powdered form, choose products with:
- Third-party testing for authenticity
- Heavy metal and contaminant screening
- Clear species identification (C. sinensis vs. C. militaris)

---

### Hot Water Extract (Non-Alcohol)

**Typical Preparation**:
1. Simmer dried cordyceps or mycelium in hot water (80-95°C)
2. Extract for 1-2 hours
3. Strain and concentrate or spray-dry

**Advantages**:
- Optimal polysaccharide extraction (water-soluble)
- Mimics traditional decoction method
- Preserves heat-stable bioactives

**Commercial Products**: Many standardized extracts (Cs-4, JinShuiBao) use hot water extraction followed by concentration

**Dosing**: Depends on concentration ratio; 3-6 g crude drug equivalent per day

**Evidence Level**: Hot water extraction is the basis for most clinical trial preparations (Cs-4, etc.)

---

### Dual Extraction (Water + Alcohol)

**Method**:
1. Hot water extraction first (polysaccharides, water-soluble compounds)
2. Alcohol extraction of marc (lipid-soluble compounds, nucleosides)
3. Combine extracts

**Theoretical Advantage**: Captures both water-soluble polysaccharides and alcohol-soluble nucleosides, sterols

**Evidence Level**: No specific RCTs comparing dual to single extraction

**Dosing**: Depends on concentration; aim for 3-6 g crude drug equivalent

---

## Tea Preparation (Home Use)

### Simple Hot Water Infusion

**Method**:
1. Place 3-6 g dried cordyceps in teapot or cup
2. Add 500 mL hot water (80-90°C, not boiling)
3. Steep for 20-30 minutes
4. Strain and drink
5. May re-steep 1-2 additional times

**Dosing**: 3-6 g dried cordyceps daily

**Traditional Practice**: Sometimes the softened cordyceps bodies are consumed after steeping

**Limitations**:
- Incomplete extraction compared to prolonged decoction or commercial extraction
- Polysaccharide extraction limited by short steep time
- Variable potency
- Quality and species authentication critical

**Evidence Level**: None - traditional practice without clinical validation

---

### Prolonged Decoction (Traditional Method)

**Method**:
1. Place 3-6 g dried cordyceps in pot with 750 mL-1 L water
2. Bring to gentle boil, then reduce to simmer
3. Simmer covered for 1-2 hours
4. Strain and consume liquid (warm or room temperature)
5. Optionally re-decoct the marc with fresh water

**Dosing**: 3-6 g dried cordyceps daily

**Traditional Schedule**: Daily for chronic conditions; as needed for tonification

**Advantages Over Simple Infusion**:
- More complete polysaccharide extraction
- Higher bioactive yield
- Closer to traditional preparation

**Limitations**:
- Time-intensive
- Requires authentic, quality cordyceps
- Standardization difficult

**Evidence Level**: Traditional practice; modern RCTs use standardized extracts rather than home decoctions

## Capsule and Tablet Forms (Standardized Extracts)

### Cs-4 (CordyMax) Capsules

**Typical Product**: 333 mg capsules

**Dosing**:
- General health: 333-666 mg daily (1-2 capsules)
- Exercise performance: 1,000 mg daily (3 capsules) - evidence-based (Chen 2010)
- Therapeutic use: 3,000 mg daily (9 capsules) - evidence-based (Chinese 2003)

**Administration**: With or without food (not specified in trials; standardized extracts generally food-independent)

**Clinical Trial Schedule**: Typically once daily or divided doses (3× daily in Chen 2010)

---

### Higher-Dose Capsules (Renal/Cancer Support)

**Dosing for Therapeutic Conditions**:
- Chronic renal failure: 3-6 g/day (equivalent to 9-18 capsules of 333 mg, or 6-12 capsules of 500 mg)
- Lung cancer adjuvant: 6 g/day for 21 days per cycle

**Practical Consideration**: Higher doses often require larger capsules (500-1,000 mg) or multiple daily administrations

**Evidence-Based Products**: Bailing Capsule, JinShuiBao (dosing per manufacturer, based on standardization)

### Frequency of Administration

**Once Daily**: Suitable for general health, immune support, maintenance dosing
- Convenient for compliance
- Supported by some trials (e.g., C. militaris beverage study)

**Divided Doses (2-3× daily)**: May be preferable for higher therapeutic doses
- Chen 2010: 333 mg × 3 daily (1,000 mg total)
- Diabetic nephropathy: 0.5-2 g × 3 daily
- Reduces single-dose burden
- May improve absorption and sustained blood levels

**Cyclical Dosing**: Lung cancer adjuvant
- 21 days on per chemotherapy cycle
- 3-4 cycles total
- Coordinated with conventional treatment schedule

---

### Duration of Use

**Short-Term (6-12 weeks)**: Evidence-based for specific outcomes
- Exercise performance trials: 6-12 weeks
- Immune function: 8 weeks
- Long COVID: 12 weeks

**Long-Term (Months to Years)**: Based on traditional use
- Chronic renal failure: Ongoing adjunctive therapy
- Hemodialysis support: Ongoing
- COPD management: Extended use (trial durations vary)
- Traditional tonification: As needed, often extended periods

**Evidence Gap**: Systematic long-term safety data (>3 months) limited; traditional use and short-term trial safety support extended use

**Clinical Guidance**: Long-term use reasonable for chronic conditions; periodic reassessment and monitoring appropriate

## Bioavailability Optimization

**From Active Compounds Analysis**:

### Challenges
- Cordycepin: Low oral bioavailability, rapid deamination by adenosine deaminase
- Intact cordycepin: NOT absorbed orally (animal studies)
- Polysaccharides: Only ~25.9 mg/100 g dry weight absorbed after intestinal digestion

### Practical Strategies (Evidence-Based)

1. **Use Standardized Whole Extracts** (Not Isolated Cordycepin)
   - Clinical trial evidence uses whole extracts (Cs-4, JinShuiBao)
   - Synergistic effects likely more important than single compounds
   - Polysaccharides, nucleosides, and other compounds contribute to efficacy

2. **Hot Water Extraction Products**
   - Optimal for polysaccharide extraction (primary bioactive)
   - Mimics traditional preparation
   - Basis for most clinical trial products

3. **Take on Empty Stomach or With Meals?**
   - **Not specified in clinical trials** - suggests food-independent absorption
   - **Practical recommendation**: Either approach acceptable; consistency matters more than timing

4. **Avoid Relying on Cordycepin Content Alone**
   - Marketing often emphasizes cordycepin despite poor bioavailability
   - Polysaccharide content (40% in standardized products) more relevant
   - Choose products with clinical trial evidence, not just high cordycepin claims

## Product Selection Criteria

### Choose Products With:

✓ **Clinical Trial Evidence**
- Cs-4 (CordyMax): >30 clinical trials
- JinShuiBao: Multiple trials, Chinese FDA approved
- Bailing Capsule: Renal transplant evidence

✓ **Standardization Markers**
- 40% polysaccharides
- 8% mannitol
- 0.2% adenosine
- OR clear cordycepin content (if C. militaris)

✓ **Third-Party Testing**
- Heavy metal screening
- Microbial contamination testing
- Species authentication

✓ **Regulatory Recognition**
- Chinese Pharmacopoeia recognition (for Cs-4)
- Chinese FDA approval (JinShuiBao, Bailing)
- FDA GRAS status (general category)

✓ **Clear Species Identification**
- C. sinensis (Ophiocordyceps sinensis) vs. C. militaris
- Mycelium vs. fruiting body
- Wild vs. cultivated (cultivated preferred for sustainability and consistency)

---

### Avoid Products With:

✗ **Vague labeling** ("Cordyceps blend," unclear species)
✗ **Unrealistic claims** (cure-all marketing)
✗ **Only cordycepin standardization** (ignores bioavailability issues and polysaccharide importance)
✗ **No third-party testing** (heavy metals, contaminants)
✗ **Suspiciously low price** (authentic cordyceps expensive; very cheap products likely adulterated)

## Practical Recommendations by User Type

### General Health & Immune Support (Healthy Adults)
**Dose**: 1-3 g/day Cs-4 standardized extract
**Form**: Capsules (333-500 mg), 2-6 capsules daily
**Schedule**: Once daily or divided doses
**Duration**: 6-12 weeks initially, then as needed
**Expected Benefits**: Immune support, general vitality (based on traditional use and limited RCT data)

---

### Athletes & Active Elderly (Exercise Performance)
**Dose**: 1-3 g/day Cs-4 (CordyMax)
**Form**: Capsules, 333 mg × 3 daily OR 1,000 mg once daily
**Schedule**: Daily for 6-12 weeks
**Duration**: Continuous during training periods
**Evidence**: Metabolic threshold +10.5%, ventilatory threshold +8.5% (elderly 50-75 years)
**Source**: Chen 2010 (n=20), Chinese 2003 (n=37)

---

### Chronic Kidney Disease (Adjunctive Therapy)
**Dose**: 3-6 g/day
**Form**: Bailing Capsule, JinShuiBao, or Cs-4 capsules
**Schedule**: Daily, divided doses recommended
**Duration**: Ongoing as adjunctive to conventional treatment
**Medical Supervision**: Essential; coordinate with nephrologist
**Evidence**: Meta-analysis (n=1,310), sensitivity 0.89, reduced creatinine
**Source**: Wu 2025

---

### Cancer Patients (Adjuvant with Chemotherapy/Radiation)
**Dose**: 6 g/day
**Schedule**: 21 days per cycle, 3-4 cycles (coordinate with chemo cycles)
**Form**: Standardized capsules or tablets
**Medical Supervision**: **Essential** - coordinate with oncologist
**Expected Benefits**: Improved tumor response, enhanced immune function, reduced chemo side effects
**Evidence**: Meta-analysis (n=928), RR 1.17 tumor response, reduced myelosuppression (RR 0.38)
**Source**: Wang 2024

---

### COPD Patients
**Dose**: 3-4.5 g/day (estimated from meta-analysis)
**Form**: Oral C. sinensis capsules
**Schedule**: Daily
**Duration**: Extended use (months)
**Medical Supervision**: Coordinate with pulmonologist
**Expected Benefits**: Improved FEV1%, exercise endurance, quality of life
**Evidence**: Meta-analysis (n=1,238), no serious adverse events
**Source**: Yu 2019

---

### Long COVID & Post-Viral Fatigue
**Dose**: 3-4.5 g/day Cs4 extract (estimated)
**Schedule**: Daily for 12 weeks minimum
**Form**: Cs4 mycelium extract capsules
**Expected Benefits**: Reduced overall symptoms (MD -10.1), fatigue (MD -8.1), improved sleep and respiratory symptoms
**Evidence**: RCT (n=110), no severe adverse events
**Source**: Acta Materia Medica 2025

---

## Cost-Effectiveness Considerations

### Wild C. sinensis
- **Cost**: ~$25,000/kg (2007 pricing)
- **Availability**: Extremely limited; >90% yield decline
- **Recommendation**: Not cost-effective or sustainable for regular use

### Cultivated Cs-4/JinShuiBao
- **Cost**: Moderate (specific pricing varies by manufacturer and region)
- **Availability**: Widely available
- **Clinical Evidence**: Extensive (>30 trials for Cs-4)
- **Recommendation**: **Best choice** for evidence-based use

### Generic "Cordyceps" Supplements
- **Cost**: Wide range (often cheaper than branded Cs-4)
- **Quality**: Variable; authentication and standardization concerns
- **Evidence**: Minimal to none for specific products
- **Recommendation**: Only if third-party tested with clear standardization; branded products with clinical evidence preferred

### C. militaris Products
- **Cost**: Moderate
- **Cordycepin Content**: High (5-15× more than C. sinensis)
- **Bioavailability Issue**: Cordycepin poorly absorbed; polysaccharides more relevant
- **Evidence**: Limited (one RCT with fermented beverage, 20 participants)
- **Recommendation**: May be suitable for immune support; fewer clinical trials than Cs-4

---

## Summary: Preparation Recommendations

### Strongest Clinical Evidence
1. **Cs-4 (CordyMax)**: 1-3 g/day for general use, up to 6 g/day for therapeutic conditions
2. **JinShuiBao**: 3-6 g/day for renal conditions
3. **Bailing Capsule**: For post-transplant and renal support

### Traditional Preparation (Historical Context)
- **Decoction**: 3-9 g crude drug; impractical today due to cost and scarcity
- **Modern Alternative**: Standardized extracts provide consistent, affordable, evidence-based option

### Home Preparation (Limited Evidence)
- **Simple tea**: 3-6 g dried cordyceps, steep 20-30 minutes (incomplete extraction)
- **Prolonged decoction**: 3-6 g dried cordyceps, simmer 1-2 hours (better extraction, time-intensive)
- **Quality Critical**: Authentication and heavy metal testing essential for raw materials

### Not Recommended (Insufficient Evidence)
- Tinctures (no RCT data; poor polysaccharide extraction)
- Unauthenticated "cordyceps blend" products
- Products standardized only to cordycepin (ignores bioavailability issues)

**Final Recommendation**: For evidence-based use, choose standardized extracts (Cs-4, JinShuiBao) with clinical trial support at appropriate doses (1-6 g/day depending on indication).
