# Cordyceps - Clinical Uses

Evidence-based clinical applications with dosing, duration, and effect sizes from randomized controlled trials and systematic reviews.

---

## Renal Dysfunction and Kidney Disease

### Chronic Renal Failure
**Evidence:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (15 studies, 1,310 participants)

**Dosing:**
- **3-6 g/day** of crude drug or standardized extract
- Common preparations: Bailing Capsule, Jinshuibao, Zhiling formulations

**Effect Sizes:**
- Pooled sensitivity: **89%** (95% CI: 84-93%)
- Pooled specificity: **69%** (95% CI: 59-77%)
- Diagnostic odds ratio: **18.0** (95% CI: 8.0-39.0)
- Area under curve: **0.88** (95% CI: 0.85-0.90)

**Clinical Improvements:**
- Significantly reduced blood creatinine levels
- Shortened oliguria period
- Increased urine osmolality
- Improved creatinine clearance rate
- Reduced blood urea nitrogen (BUN)

**Source:** Wu et al. 2025 ([PubMed 39839641](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39839641/))

---

### Diabetic Nephropathy
**Dosing:**
- **0.5-2 g** orally three times daily (1.5-6 g/day total)
- Used in combination with ACE inhibitors or ARBs

**Source:** Chinese Pharmacopoeia recommendations, NCBI Herbal Drug monograph

---

### Hemodialysis Patients
**Evidence:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (12 studies, 655 participants)

**Effect Sizes:**
- C-reactive protein: **SMD -0.61** (reduced inflammation)
- High-sensitivity CRP: **-3.44 mg/L reduction**
- Serum albumin: **+3.07 g/L increase**
- Malondialdehyde: **-1.95 nmol/L reduction** (oxidative stress)
- Hemoglobin: **+9.56 g/L increase**

**No Significant Changes:**
- Serum creatinine
- LDL cholesterol

**Conclusion:** Evidence insufficient to recommend use due to limited trial numbers and unclear methodological quality, but anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects observed.

**Source:** Ong & Aziz 2019 ([PubMed 32186018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32186018/))

## Respiratory Conditions

### Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
**Evidence:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (15 studies, 1,238 participants)

**Clinical Improvements:**
- FEV1% predicted (lung function)
- 6-minute walking distance (exercise endurance)
- St. George's Respiratory Questionnaire scores (quality of life)
- Significant respiratory symptom improvements

**Safety:** No serious adverse events reported

**Limitations:** No placebo-controlled studies of high methodological quality

**Source:** Yu et al. 2019 ([PubMed 31073318](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31073318/))

---

### Chronic Bronchitis
**Evidence:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (240 participants, 238 analyzed)

**Dosing:**
- **Bailing capsule: 2.0 g three times daily** (6 g/day total)
- **Duration: 48 weeks**

**Effect Sizes:**
- Acute exacerbation frequency (Bailing): **0.43**
- Acute exacerbation frequency (placebo): **1.56**
- **p < 0.001** (72% reduction in exacerbations)

**Additional Improvements:**
- Improved expectoration and wheezing during follow-up
- Overall chronic bronchitis severity significantly improved (p = 0.035)

**Safety:**
- Adverse events (Bailing): 29.6%
- Adverse events (placebo): 30.4%
- Similar adverse event rates between groups

**Source:** Shu et al. 2024 ([PubMed 39193337](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39193337/))

## Lung Cancer (Adjuvant Treatment)

**Evidence:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (12 trials, 928 participants)

**Optimal Dosing:**
- **6 g/day for 21 days per cycle, 3-4 cycles**

**Effect Sizes:**

**Tumor Response:**
- Relative risk: **1.17** (95% CI: 1.05-1.29) - improved tumor response

**Immune Function:**
- CD4+ cells: **+4.98 percentage points** (95% CI: 1.49-8.47)
- NK cells: **+4.17 percentage points** (95% CI: 2.26-6.08)
- Immunoglobulin IgG: **+3.95 g/L** (95% CI: 0.98-6.92)

**Quality of Life:**
- Karnofsky Performance Status: **+8.20 points** (95% CI: 6.87-9.53)

**Adverse Reaction Reductions:**
- Myelosuppression: **RR 0.38** (95% CI: 0.19-0.75) - 62% reduction
- Leukopenia: **RR 0.76** (95% CI: 0.63-0.92) - 24% reduction
- Radiation pneumonitis: **RR 0.74** (95% CI: 0.62-0.88) - 26% reduction

**Source:** Wang et al. 2024 ([PubMed 38484953](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38484953/))

## Long COVID

**Evidence:** Randomized, waitlist-controlled trial (110 participants: 55 treatment, 55 control)

**Dosing:**
- **Cs-4 extract** (Cordyceps sinensis mycelium culture extract)
- **Duration: 12 weeks**

**Effect Sizes:**

**Primary Outcome (Long COVID symptoms):**
- Mean difference: **-10.1** (95% CI: -14.1 to -6.1, p < 0.001)
- Scale: Modified COVID-19 Yorkshire Rehabilitation Scale

**Fatigue Improvement:**
- Mean difference: **-8.1** (95% CI: -14.2 to -2.0, p = 0.011)
- Scale: Brief Fatigue Inventory Form

**Additional Improvements:**
- Insomnia (Insomnia Severity Index)
- Respiratory symptoms (St. George's Respiratory Questionnaire)
- Anxiety/depression (Hospital Anxiety and Depression Scale)
- Quality of life (Short Form 12 health survey)

**Safety:** No severe adverse events related to interventions

**Source:** Hong Kong study 2025, Acta Materia Medica ([ScienceOpen](https://www.scienceopen.com/hosted-document?doi=10.15212/AMM-2024-0089))

## Immune Function Enhancement

### Healthy Adults
**Evidence:** RCT (79 participants: 39 intervention, 40 control)

**Dosing:**
- **CBG-CS-2 extract (Paecilomyces hepiali mycelium): 1.68 g/day**
- **Form:** Capsules
- **Duration:** 8 weeks

**Effect Sizes:**
- NK cell activity enhancement: **38.8 ± 17.6% from baseline** (p < 0.019)

**Conclusion:** Safe and effective for enhancing cell-mediated immunity in healthy adults

**Source:** Jung et al. 2019 ([PubMed 30925876](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30925876/))

---

### Fermented Cordyceps militaris Beverage
**Evidence:** RCT (20 participants: 10 males, 10 females)

**Dosing:**
- **Cordycepin content: 2.85 mg per serving**
- **Duration:** 8 weeks

**Effect Sizes (Sex-Specific):**

**Males:**
- NK cell activity: Significant increase baseline to 4 weeks (p = 0.049)
- IL-1β: Decreased (p = 0.049)

**Females:**
- NK cell activity: Significant increase at 8 weeks vs placebo (p = 0.023)
- IL-6: Decreased from baseline (p = 0.047)

**Safety:** No liver, kidney, or blood component toxicity; blood sugar and lipid indices normal

**Source:** Ontawong et al. 2024 ([PubMed 38580687](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38580687/))

## Exercise Performance and Aerobic Capacity

### Healthy Elderly (50-75 years)
**Evidence:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT (20 participants)

**Dosing:**
- **Cs-4 (CordyMax): 333 mg capsules, three times daily** (1,000 mg/day total)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks

**Effect Sizes:**

**Metabolic Threshold:**
- Increase: **10.5%** (p < 0.02)
- Baseline: 0.83 ± 0.06 L/min
- Post-treatment: 0.93 ± 0.08 L/min

**Ventilatory Threshold:**
- Increase: **8.5%** (p = 0.031)
- Baseline: 1.25 ± 0.11 L/min
- Post-treatment: 1.36 ± 0.15 L/min

**VO2 Max:** No changes in either group

**Safety:** No adverse events reported

**Source:** Chen et al. 2010 ([PubMed 20804368](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20804368/))

---

### Alternative Dosing for Elderly
**Evidence:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT (37 participants)

**Dosing:**
- **Cs-4: 3 g/day**
- **Duration:** 6 weeks

**Results:**
- Aerobic capacity: Significantly enhanced
- Respiratory function: Significantly improved

**Source:** Chinese investigators 2003, Chinese Journal of Integrative Medicine

## Body Composition (with Endurance Training)

**Evidence:** Double-blind RCT (14 participants: 8 male, 6 female young sedentary adults)

**Dosing:**
- **Rhodiola crenulata/Cordyceps sinensis combination: 20 mg/kg/day**
- **Duration:** 8 weeks with endurance training

**Effect Sizes:**
- Body weight: Reduced (p = 0.044)
- BMI: Lower (p = 0.003)
- Upper extremity fat: Decreased (p = 0.032)
- Lower extremity muscle: Increased (p = 0.029)
- Trunk fat: Reduced (p = 0.011)

**No Changes:** Blood lipid profile and oxidative stress markers unchanged

**Source:** Liao et al. 2019 ([PubMed 31623349](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31623349/))

## Negative Trials (Important for Balanced View)

### Depression/Insomnia
**Evidence:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT (59 participants: 28 C. militaris, 31 placebo; 11 withdrew)

**Duration:** 6 weeks

**Results:**
- Sleep outcomes: **NO significant improvement** in Athens Insomnia Scale scores vs placebo
- Depression outcomes: **NO significant differences** in depression severity scores
- Adverse events: Incidences not significantly different between groups
- Safety: Safe with rare side effects

**Conclusion:** C. militaris at tested dose/duration did not improve insomnia in depression patients

**Source:** Zhou et al. 2021 ([PubMed 34858228](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34858228/))

## Traditional Chinese Medicine Applications

**Source:** Chinese Pharmacopoeia (official since 1964)

**Traditional Properties:**
- Taste: Sweet
- Nature: Neutral
- Meridians: Lung and Kidney

**Traditional Uses:**
- Replenish kidney (补肾)
- Soothe lung (润肺)
- Stop bleeding (止血)
- Eliminate phlegm (化痰)

**Clinical Applications (Traditional):**
- Fatigue and weakness
- Chronic cough
- Hyposexuality
- Asthenia after severe illness
- Renal dysfunction and renal failure

**Traditional Dosing:**
- **3-9 g crude drug as decoction**

**Source:** NCBI Herbal Drug monograph ([NCBI Books NBK92758](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK92758/))

## Summary of Evidence Quality

**Strong Evidence (Systematic Reviews/Meta-Analyses):**
- Renal dysfunction (1,310 participants)
- Lung cancer adjuvant therapy (928 participants)
- COPD (1,238 participants)
- Hemodialysis (655 participants)

**Moderate Evidence (RCTs):**
- Chronic bronchitis (240 participants, 48 weeks)
- Long COVID (110 participants)
- Exercise performance (20-37 participants)
- Immune function (79-80 participants)

**Limitations:**
- Limited placebo-controlled studies of high methodological quality for COPD
- Insufficient evidence for hemodialysis due to limited trials
- Need for larger, well-designed RCTs with standardized preparations
- Most studies use different preparations, making direct comparisons difficult
