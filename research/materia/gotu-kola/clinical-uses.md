# Gotu Kola (Centella asiatica) - Clinical Uses

## Venous Insufficiency & Circulatory Conditions

### Chronic Venous Hypertension
**Evidence strength:** Strong (multiple RCTs, consistent positive findings)

**Dosage:** 60-180 mg TTFCA (Total Triterpenic Fraction) daily
- **Lower dose:** 60 mg daily (Incandela et al., 2001)
- **Standard dose:** 120 mg daily (60 mg twice daily)
- **High dose:** 180 mg daily (60 mg three times daily)

**Duration:** 2-6 months typical treatment period

**Clinical findings:**
- **Incandela et al., 2001 - Venous Hypertension (PMID: 11666126):**
  - Higher dose (120 mg daily) more effective than lower dose (60 mg)
  - Decreased resting flux: 29%
  - Improved venoarteriolar response: 52%
  - Decreased leg volume: 66 mL
  - Both doses safe; 120 mg daily recommended

- **De Sanctis et al., 2001 - Dose-Ranging Trial (PMID: 11666125):**
  - Sample: 62 participants (20 at 180 mg/day, 20 at 90 mg/day, 12 placebo, 10 normal subjects)
  - 180 mg/day more effective than 90 mg/day in improving symptoms and capillary filtration rate
  - Demonstrated clear dose-dependent response
  - Significant improvement in edema and capillary filtration

### Diabetic Microangiopathy
**Evidence strength:** Moderate to strong (RCTs showing microcirculatory improvements)

**Dosage:** 60 mg TTFCA twice daily (120 mg total daily)

**Duration:** 6-12 months

**Clinical findings:**
- **Incandela et al., 2001 - 12-Month Study (PMID: 11666119):**
  - Sample: Diabetic patients with microangiopathy
  - Flux decreased by 38% with corresponding 38% improvement in venoarteriolar response
  - Rate of ankle swelling decreased by 28% (p<0.05)
  - In patients without neuropathy: flux reduction 22%, venoarteriolar response increase 22.7%, ankle swelling decrease 9.5%
  - Significant reductions in capillary filtration and edema with symptomatic improvement

- **Cesarone et al., 2001 - 6-Month Study (PMID: 11666124):**
  - Sample: 50 participants (30 TTFCA, 10 placebo, 10 untreated control)
  - Dosage: 60 mg TTFCA twice daily
  - Decreased resting skin blood flow (initially abnormally elevated)
  - Improved venoarteriolar response (impaired at baseline)
  - Elevated transcutaneous oxygen levels
  - Reduced carbon dioxide measurements
  - Decreased capillary permeability
  - Protective effects against microcirculation deterioration

## Pain Management

### Temporomandibular Pain
**Evidence strength:** Preliminary (positive pilot RCT)

**Dosage:** 500 mg ECa233 (standardized extract) twice daily

**Duration:** 14 days

**Clinical findings:**
- **Potewiratnanond et al., 2025 - Pilot RCT (PMID: 40253389):**
  - Sample: 23 participants
  - At day 7: significantly decreased pain intensity (p=0.016)
  - Highest percentage reduction in self-reported pain and TMJ palpation pain
  - By day 14: no significant differences among groups
  - Suggests efficacy for acute pain relief in temporomandibular disorders

## Wound Healing

### Diabetic Wound Healing
**Evidence strength:** Moderate (RCT with positive findings)

**Dosage:** 50 mg asiaticoside per capsule, three times daily (150 mg total/day)

**Duration:** 21 days

**Clinical findings:**
- **Paocharoen, RCT (no PMID listed):**
  - Sample: 170 participants (84 treatment, 86 placebo)
  - Wound contraction better in study group than placebo
  - Granulation tissue formation better in placebo group
  - Overall promoted wound healing in diabetic patients

### Postlaser Resurfacing (Topical)
**Evidence strength:** Moderate (positive RCT)

**Dosage:** 0.05% w/w ECa 233 gel (topical)

**Clinical findings:**
- **Damkerngsuntorn et al., 2020 - Split-Face RCT:**
  - Sample: 30 participants
  - Indication: Postlaser resurfacing for acne scars (Er:YAG laser)
  - ECa 233 treated side showed significantly less erythema index by 0.03 U (p=0.046)
  - Improved wound healing after laser treatment

### Scar Prevention (Topical)
**Evidence strength:** Moderate (positive RCT)

**Dosage:** 7% C. asiatica extract cream

**Duration:** 12 weeks

**Clinical findings:**
- **Jenwitheesuk et al., 2018 - Double-Blind RCT:**
  - Sample: 30 enrolled, 23 completed
  - Indication: Split-thickness skin graft scar prevention
  - Improved scar results overall
  - Significant improvement in pigmentation from week 8 vs placebo (which improved from week 12)

## Mood & Anxiety

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder
**Evidence strength:** Preliminary (small clinical trial with positive findings)

**Dosage:** 500 mg twice daily after meals (1000 mg total/day)

**Duration:** 60 days minimum

**Clinical findings:**
- **Jana et al., 2010 - Clinical Trial (PMID: 20677602):**
  - Sample: 33 participants (18 male, 15 female, mean age 33 years)
  - Significantly attenuated anxiety-related disorders (p<0.01)
  - Significantly reduced stress phenomenon and correlated depression (p<0.01)
  - Improvements measured by standard psychological rating scales at baseline, day 30, and day 60

### Acute Anxiolytic Effects
**Evidence strength:** Preliminary (positive RCT with acoustic startle response)

**Dosage:** Single 12 g oral dose (acute study)

**Clinical findings:**
- **Bradwejn et al., 2000 - Double-Blind RCT (PMID: 11106141):**
  - Sample: 40 participants (20 treatment, 20 placebo)
  - Significantly attenuated peak acoustic startle response amplitude at 30 and 60 minutes post-treatment vs placebo
  - No significant effect on self-rated mood, heart rate, or blood pressure
  - Suggests anxiolytic properties without cardiovascular or mood impacts

### Mood Enhancement
**Evidence strength:** Moderate (meta-analysis with positive findings for specific mood parameters)

**Dosage:** Various (250-500 mg standardized extract typical)

**Clinical findings:**
- **Puttarak et al., 2017 - Meta-Analysis (PMID: 28878245):**
  - Analysis: 11 RCTs (5 C. asiatica alone, 6 combination products)
  - Alert scores increased by 0.71 SD (95% CI 0.01-1.41)
  - Anger scores decreased by 0.81 SD at 1 hour (95% CI -1.51 to -0.09)
  - Improved alertness and relieved anger in meta-analysis

## Cognitive Function

### Memory & Cognition
**Evidence strength:** Weak to moderate (no significant improvements vs placebo in meta-analysis despite traditional use)

**Dosage:**
- Standard extract: 250-500 mg twice daily
- CAP (Centella asiatica aqueous extract): 2-4 g daily

**Duration:** 2-6 months

**Clinical findings:**
- **Puttarak et al., 2017 - Meta-Analysis (PMID: 28878245):**
  - Analysis: 11 RCTs (5 C. asiatica alone, 6 combination products)
  - No significant differences in cognitive function domains vs placebo
  - Need for better-standardized future trials
  - Traditional use as "brain tonic" lacks strong clinical support

- **Wright et al., 2022 - Phase 1 PK Study in Cognitively Impaired (PMID: 35204098):**
  - Sample: 4 cognitively impaired older adults
  - Dosage: 2 g CAP, 4 g CAP (10-hour observation per dose)
  - CAP safe and well-tolerated in cognitively impaired older adults on cholinesterase inhibitors
  - Metabolites showed detectable pharmacokinetic profiles
  - Triterpene aglycones (asiatic acid, madecassic acid) identified in plasma and urine

## Summary of Strongest Clinical Evidence

1. **Venous insufficiency:** Multiple positive RCTs with 50-87 patients showing 29-52% improvements in microcirculatory parameters
2. **Diabetic microangiopathy:** 38% improvement in flux, 28% reduction in ankle swelling (12-month RCT)
3. **Mood:** Meta-analysis showed increased alertness (0.71 SD) and reduced anger (0.81 SD)
4. **Wound healing:** Moderate evidence for topical use; preliminary evidence for oral use in diabetic wounds
5. **Anxiety:** Preliminary positive findings in small trials
6. **Cognitive function:** Traditional use not well-supported by clinical evidence; no significant improvements vs placebo

## Standardized Preparations Used in Clinical Trials

- **ECa 233:** Standardized extract used in multiple trials (cognitive, pain, wound healing)
- **TTFCA:** Total Triterpenic Fraction of Centella asiatica - extensively used in vascular trials
- **CAP:** Centella asiatica aqueous extract product
- **Asiaticoside preparations:** Isolated triterpene glycoside used in some wound healing studies
