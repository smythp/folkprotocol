# Gotu Kola - Traditional Uses

Historical and traditional medicine applications of Centella asiatica.

---

## Ayurvedic Medicine

### Historical References
- **Traditional Name:** Mandukaparni
- **Historical Source:** Susruta Samhita (2000-3000 years ago)
- **Classification:** Rasayana (rejuvenation)

### Meridians Influenced
- Liver
- Spleen
- Kidney

### Traditional Ayurvedic Uses
- **Brain tonic:** Enhancement of cognitive function and mental clarity
- **Memory enhancement:** Specifically valued for improving memory and learning
- **Mental and physical health:** Overall wellness and vitality
- **Rejuvenation in debilitated conditions:** Restoration of health in weakened states
- **Longevity:** Promotion of long life and healthy aging

### Traditional Ayurvedic Dosing
**Decoction (tea/infusion):**
- **Dry herb:** 15-30 grams
- **Fresh herb:** 30-60 grams

## Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)

### Historical References
- **Historical Source:** Shennong Herbal (2000-3000 years ago)
- **Pharmacopoeia:** Listed in Chinese Pharmacopoeia

### Traditional TCM Uses
- **Mental exhaustion:** Treatment of mental fatigue and cognitive depletion
- **Physical exhaustion:** Restoration of physical energy and vitality

## European Traditional Use

### Pharmacopoeia Recognition

**French Pharmacopoeia:**
- **Year Added:** 1884
- One of the earliest Western pharmacopoeia inclusions

**Commission E (German Ministry of Health):**
- **Recognized Effects:**
  - Wound healing
  - Memory enhancement

**European Pharmacopoeia:**
- **Monograph Revision:** EDQM (Pharmeuropa 24.1, 2012)
- **Minimum Standards:** ≥6.0% of total triterpenoid derivatives expressed as asiaticoside (Polish and European Pharmacopoeias)

## Traditional Preparation Methods

### Ayurvedic Preparations
- **Decoction (Kashaya):** Boiling dried or fresh herb in water
  - Dry: 15-30 grams per preparation
  - Fresh: 30-60 grams per preparation
- **Fresh juice:** Extraction from fresh plant material
- **Ghrita (Ghee preparations):** Herb infused in clarified butter for enhanced absorption

### TCM Preparations
- **Decoction:** Boiled with other herbs in multi-ingredient formulas
- **Powder:** Dried and ground for consumption
- **Fresh plant:** Used in poultices and external applications

### European Traditional Preparations
- **Infusion:** Dried leaf steeped in hot water
- **Tincture:** Alcohol-based extracts
- **Ointments and creams:** For topical wound healing applications

## Traditional Wisdom Validated by Modern Research

### Memory & Cognitive Function
- **Traditional claim:** Brain tonic, memory enhancement (Ayurveda, TCM)
- **Modern evidence:** RCT showing working memory enhancement at 750 mg (Wattanathorn 2008)
- **Status:** Partially validated - effects present but modest

### Wound Healing
- **Traditional claim:** Topical wound healing (all traditions)
- **Modern evidence:** RCT in 200 diabetic patients showing improved wound contraction (Paocharoen 2010)
- **Status:** Well-validated

### Anxiety & Mental Calm
- **Traditional claim:** Mental health, stress reduction (Ayurveda)
- **Modern evidence:** RCT showing reduced anxiety, stress, depression (Jana 2010); Acoustic startle response reduced (Bradwejn 2000)
- **Status:** Well-validated

### Longevity & Vitality
- **Traditional claim:** Rejuvenation, longevity (Ayurveda as Rasayana)
- **Modern evidence:** Mechanisms include anti-oxidative stress, anti-inflammatory, mitochondrial function improvement
- **Status:** Mechanistic support, lacks direct longevity trials

## Notes on Traditional Dosing vs. Modern Clinical Doses

**Traditional Ayurvedic doses (15-60 grams fresh/dry herb) are significantly higher than modern standardized extract doses (250-4000 mg).** This reflects:

1. **Concentration differences:** Modern extracts are highly concentrated (80-85% triterpenes in ECa 233 vs. ~2-6% in crude herb)
2. **Standardization:** Modern extracts guarantee specific active compound levels
3. **Bioavailability:** Extraction methods may enhance bioavailability of active compounds

**Conversion estimate:**
- 15-30 grams crude herb (at ~6% triterpenes) = ~900-1800 mg triterpenes
- Modern ECa 233 at 500 mg (at 80% triterpenes) = ~400 mg triterpenes

Traditional doses thus provided roughly 2-4x the triterpene content of typical modern doses, though bioavailability differences make direct comparison difficult.
