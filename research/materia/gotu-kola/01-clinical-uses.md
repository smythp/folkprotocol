# Gotu Kola - Clinical Uses

Evidence from randomized controlled trials and clinical studies.

---

## Cognitive Function & Memory

### Elderly Cognitive Enhancement
**Study:** Wattanathorn et al. 2008 ([PubMed 18191355](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18191355/))

- **Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial
- **Sample:** 28 healthy elderly participants
- **Doses:** 250 mg, 500 mg, or 750 mg once daily
- **Duration:** 2 months with assessments at baseline, after single dose, 1 month, and 2 months
- **Results:**
  - **Working memory:** Enhanced at highest dose (750 mg)
  - **Brain activity:** Increased N100 component amplitude of event-related potential at 750 mg
  - **Mood:** Self-rated mood improvements across treatment conditions
- **Safety:** No adverse effects reported

### Cognitively Impaired Older Adults
**Study:** Wright et al. 2022 ([PubMed 35204098](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35204098/))

- **Design:** Phase 1, double-blind, randomized crossover trial
- **Sample:** 4 cognitively impaired older adults on cholinesterase inhibitor therapy
- **Doses:** 2 grams or 4 grams CAP (Centella asiatica aqueous extract)
- **Duration:** Single dose with 10-hour post-dose monitoring
- **Key Findings:**
  - Oral bioavailability confirmed for active compounds
  - NRF2 target engagement demonstrated
  - Acute safety confirmed
  - Supports validity for future clinical studies

### Meta-Analysis: Cognitive Function & Mood
**Study:** Puttarak et al. 2017 ([PubMed 28878245](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28878245/))

- **Design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies:** 11 RCTs (5 examining C. asiatica alone, 6 examining combination products)
- **Results:**
  - **Cognitive function:** No significant differences vs placebo across all domains
  - **Mood - Alertness:** Increased [SMD: 0.71 (95% CI; 0.01 to 1.41)]
  - **Mood - Anger:** Decreased at 1 hour post-treatment [SMD: -0.81 (95% CI; -1.51 to -0.09)]
  - **Safety:** None of the studies reported adverse effects
- **Conclusion:** Need for future trials using standardized preparations emphasized

## Anxiety & Stress

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder
**Study:** Jana et al. 2010 ([PubMed 20677602](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20677602/))

- **Design:** Clinical trial
- **Sample:** 33 participants (18 male, 15 female; mean age 33 years)
- **Dose:** 500 mg per capsule, twice daily with meals (1000 mg/day total)
- **Duration:** 60 days
- **Assessment:** Hamilton's Brief Psychiatric Rating Scale at baseline (day 0), mid-term (day 30), and final (day 60)
- **Results:** All statistically significant (p<0.01)
  - **Anxiety:** Significantly attenuated anxiety-related disorders
  - **Stress:** Significantly reduced stress phenomenon
  - **Depression:** Significantly reduced correlated depression
  - **Cognition:** Significantly improved willingness for adjustment and cognition
- **Safety:** No adverse effects reported

### Acute Anxiolytic Activity
**Study:** Bradwejn et al. 2000 ([PubMed 11106141](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11106141/))

- **Design:** Double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled trial
- **Sample:** 40 healthy subjects (20 gotu kola, 20 placebo)
- **Dose:** Single 12-gram oral dose
- **Assessment:** 30 and 60 minutes post-treatment
- **Measurements:** Acoustic startle response, heart rate, blood pressure, self-rated mood
- **Results:**
  - **Startle response:** Significantly attenuated peak ASR amplitude at both 30 and 60 minutes vs placebo
  - **Cardiovascular:** No significant effects on heart rate or blood pressure
  - **Mood:** No significant effects on self-rated mood
- **Conclusion:** Findings suggest gotu kola has anxiolytic activity in humans

## Chronic Venous Insufficiency & Microcirculation

### Meta-Analysis: Venous Insufficiency
**Study:** Chong & Aziz 2013 ([PubMed 23533507](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23533507/))

- **Design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies:** 8 RCTs from 13 databases
- **Results:**
  - **Microcirculation:** Significantly improved transcutaneous partial pressure of CO2 and O2, rate of ankle swelling, and venoarteriolar response
  - **Symptoms:** 3 studies reported significant improvement in CVI signs (leg heaviness, pain, edema)
- **Limitations:** Most studies had inadequate reporting and unclear risks of bias
- **Conclusion:** Results need cautious interpretation due to methodological limitations

### Venous Hypertension - Dose-Ranging Trial
**Study:** Incandela et al. 2001 ([PubMed 11666126](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11666126/))

- **Design:** Single-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized trial
- **Doses:** 60 mg TTFCA daily, 120 mg TTFCA daily, or placebo
- **Results:**
  - **Laser Doppler:** Significant difference between drug-treated groups and placebo
  - **Transcutaneous gases:** Significant modification of oxygen and carbon dioxide measurements with drug vs placebo
  - **Symptoms:** Important symptomatologic effects, especially at higher dose (120 mg)
  - **Dose comparison:** 120 mg daily showed greater effects than 60 mg daily
- **Conclusion:** 120 mg daily safely used in venous hypertension

### Diabetic Microangiopathy
**Study:** Cesarone et al. 2001 ([PubMed 11666124](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11666124/))

- **Design:** Prospective randomized trial
- **Sample:** 50 patients (30 TTFCA treatment, 10 placebo, 10 untreated control)
- **Dose:** 60 mg TTFCA twice daily (120 mg/day total)
- **Duration:** 6 months
- **Results:**
  - **Skin blood flow:** Abnormally elevated resting skin blood flow decreased significantly in treated group
  - **Venoarteriolar response:** Improved after treatment
  - **Transcutaneous oxygen:** Increased in treated group
  - **Transcutaneous CO2:** Decreased in treated group
  - **Capillary permeability:** Abnormally increased capillary permeability improved (decreased)
  - **Control groups:** No significant changes over study period
- **Conclusion:** Protective effects against microcirculation deterioration in diabetic microangiopathy

### Edema & Capillary Filtration
**Study:** De Sanctis et al. 2001 ([PubMed 11666125](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11666125/))

- **Design:** Randomized, placebo-controlled, dose-ranging trial
- **Sample:** 62 participants
  - Group A: 20 patients - 60 mg TTFCA TID (180 mg/day)
  - Group B: 20 patients - 30 mg TTFCA TID (90 mg/day)
  - Group C: 12 patients - placebo
  - Group D: 10 normal subjects
- **Duration:** 4 weeks
- **Results:**
  - **Capillary filtration rate:** Significant decrease after active treatment
  - **Ankle circumference:** Significant decrease with active treatment
  - **Ankle edema:** Significant decrease with active treatment
  - **Dose response:** Higher dose group (180 mg/day) showed greater improvement than lower dose (90 mg/day)
  - **Symptoms:** Significantly improved in both active groups according to dose
  - **Placebo/normal:** No significant changes

## Wound Healing

### Diabetic Wound Healing
**Study:** Paocharoen 2010 ([PubMed 21298840](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21298840/))

- **Design:** Randomized controlled trial
- **Sample:** 200 diabetic patients with wounds
- **Dose:** 50 mg asiaticoside per capsule, 2 capsules three times daily after meals (300 mg asiaticoside/day total)
- **Assessment:** Days 7, 14, and 21
- **Results:**
  - **Wound contraction:** Study group showed better wound contraction than placebo (p ≤ 0.001 at all time points)
  - **Granulation tissue:** Better in placebo group (unexpected finding)
  - **Scar suppression:** Effective in wound healing promotion and scar suppression
- **Safety:** No serious adverse reactions in either group
- **Conclusion:** Centella asiatica extract effective in wound healing promotion and scar suppression in diabetic wound patients

## Scar Management

### Keloid Prevention Post-Surgery
**Study:** Cotellese et al. 2018 ([PubMed 29623705](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29623705/))

- **Design:** Registry study
- **Sample:** 129 subjects (64 controls, 65 treatment group)
- **Product:** Centellicum®
- **Dose:** Two 225 mg capsules daily (450 mg/day total)
- **Timing:** Weeks 2-6 following surgery
- **Assessment:** Ultrasound evaluation of scar tissue
- **Results:**
  - **Scar regularity:** Significantly higher in supplemented subjects than controls (P<0.05)
  - **Uniformity:** Treated group demonstrated significantly more uniform scar appearance
  - **Tolerability:** Excellent with no reported adverse effects
  - **Compliance:** >98% compliance rates

## Dosing Summary by Indication

### Cognitive Enhancement
- **General:** 250-750 mg once daily for 2 months (optimal: 750 mg) - Wattanathorn 2008
- **Cognitive impairment:** 2-4 grams aqueous extract - Wright 2022

### Anxiety & Stress
- **Generalized anxiety:** 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg/day) for 60 days - Jana 2010
- **Acute anxiolytic effect:** Single 12-gram dose - Bradwejn 2000

### Venous Insufficiency
- **General venous hypertension:** 60-120 mg TTFCA daily (optimal: 120 mg) - Incandela 2001
- **Diabetic microangiopathy:** 60 mg TTFCA twice daily (120 mg/day) for 6 months - Cesarone 2001
- **Edema & capillary filtration:** 30-60 mg TTFCA three times daily (90-180 mg/day) for 4 weeks - De Sanctis 2001

### Wound Healing
- **Diabetic wounds:** 300 mg asiaticoside daily (50 mg capsule, 2 caps TID) for up to 21 days - Paocharoen 2010

### Scar Management
- **Keloid prevention:** 450 mg daily (225 mg capsule, 2 caps once daily) weeks 2-6 post-surgery - Cotellese 2018

### Topical Applications
- **Post-laser wound healing:** 0.05% w/w ECa 233 gel
- **Wound formation support:** 1% cutaneous cream
- **Cutaneous ulcerations:** 2% powder
- **Scar prevention:** 7% C. asiatica extract cream

## Research Gaps & Limitations

- Large-scale, long-term RCTs needed for cognitive enhancement in Alzheimer's disease
- Better standardization of preparations across studies (meta-analyses emphasize this limitation)
- More rigorously controlled trials for dermatological applications
- Many studies characterized by inadequate reporting and unclear risks of bias (particularly venous insufficiency studies)
