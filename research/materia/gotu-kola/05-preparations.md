# Gotu Kola - Preparations & Dosing

Practical guidance on forms, doses, and preparation methods.

---

## Standardized Extracts (Evidence-Based Doses)

### ECa 233 (80-85% Triterpenoid Glycosides)

**Composition:**
- ≥80-85% total triterpenoid glycosides
- Madecassoside: 53.1%
- Asiaticoside: 32.3%
- Ratio maintained: 1.5 ± 0.5:1 (madecassoside:asiaticoside)

**Dosing by Indication:**

| Indication | Dose | Frequency | Duration | Evidence |
|------------|------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Cognitive enhancement | 250-500 mg | Once daily | 2 months | Phase 1 trials (Songvut 2019, 2021) |
| General wellness | 250 mg | Once daily | Ongoing | Safety established for long-term use |

**Advantages:**
- Most extensively studied modern extract
- Consistent standardization
- Well-characterized pharmacokinetics
- Excellent safety profile

**Availability:** Commercial supplement market (look for "ECa 233" on label or ≥80% triterpene standardization)

---

### TTFCA/TECA (Total Triterpenic Fraction)

**Composition:**
- Asiaticoside: 40%
- Asiatic acid: 30%
- Madecassic acid: 30%

**Dosing by Indication:**

| Indication | Dose | Frequency | Total Daily | Duration | Evidence |
|------------|------|-----------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Venous insufficiency (general) | 60-120 mg | Once daily | 60-120 mg | 4-6 months | Incandela 2001 |
| Venous insufficiency (optimal) | 60 mg | Twice daily | 120 mg | 6 months | Cesarone 2001 |
| Edema (moderate) | 30 mg | Three times daily | 90 mg | 4 weeks | De Sanctis 2001 |
| Edema (high dose) | 60 mg | Three times daily | 180 mg | 4 weeks | De Sanctis 2001 |
| Diabetic microangiopathy | 60 mg | Twice daily | 120 mg | 6 months | Cesarone 2001 |

**Advantages:**
- Strong evidence for vascular conditions
- Contains both glycosides and active aglycones
- Dose-ranging data available

**Availability:** May be labeled as "TTFCA," "TECA," or "total triterpenic fraction" - verify composition matches 40/30/30 ratio

---

### Non-Specific Standardized Extracts

**For products not explicitly labeled ECa 233 or TTFCA:**

**Minimum Quality Standards:**
- European Pharmacopoeia: ≥6% total triterpenoid derivatives (as asiaticoside)
- WHO: ≥2% triterpene ester glycosides

**General Dosing (Based on Triterpene Content):**

| % Triterpenes | Target Daily Triterpenes | Dose Needed | Use |
|---------------|--------------------------|-------------|-----|
| 80% (high potency) | 400 mg | 500 mg extract | Cognitive, anxiety |
| 40% (moderate) | 400 mg | 1000 mg extract | Cognitive, anxiety |
| 10% (standard) | 400 mg | 4000 mg extract | Traditional equivalent |
| 6% (minimum) | 400 mg | ~6700 mg extract | Not practical for capsules |

**Practical Recommendation:** Look for extracts standardized to at least 40% triterpenes for practical dosing.

## Crude Herb Preparations

### Traditional Ayurvedic Decoction

**Dose (Traditional):**
- **Dry herb:** 15-30 grams
- **Fresh herb:** 30-60 grams

**Preparation Method:**
1. Measure dried or fresh gotu kola leaves
2. Add to water (ratio: 1 part herb to 16 parts water)
3. Bring to boil, then reduce heat
4. Simmer until reduced to 1/4 original volume
5. Strain and consume warm

**Frequency:** Once or twice daily

**Triterpene Content (Estimated):**
- 15-30 grams dry herb at 6% triterpenes = 900-1800 mg triterpenes
- Roughly equivalent to 2-4.5 grams of 40% standardized extract

**Modern Context:** Traditional decoctions provide higher absolute triterpene content than typical modern doses, but bioavailability may be lower.

---

### Tea/Infusion (Gentler Extraction)

**Dose:**
- **Dried leaf:** 2-4 grams (approximately 1-2 teaspoons)
- **Fresh leaf:** 4-8 grams

**Preparation Method:**
1. Place dried or fresh leaves in cup or teapot
2. Pour 8 oz (240 ml) boiling water over herbs
3. Steep covered for 10-15 minutes
4. Strain and drink

**Frequency:** 1-3 cups daily

**Triterpene Content (Estimated):**
- 2-4 grams dry herb at 6% triterpenes = 120-240 mg triterpenes per cup
- Lower extraction efficiency than decoction (water temperature and time limited)

**Use Case:** Mild cognitive support, relaxation, traditional wellness use

**Taste Profile:** Mildly bitter, slightly earthy; can be combined with other herbs or honey

---

### Fresh Juice

**Traditional Ayurvedic Method:**

**Dose:** 10-20 ml fresh juice

**Preparation:**
1. Harvest fresh gotu kola leaves and stems
2. Wash thoroughly
3. Blend or grind with minimal water to extract juice
4. Strain through cloth
5. Consume immediately

**Frequency:** Once or twice daily

**Advantages:**
- Full spectrum of fresh plant compounds
- Traditional preparation method
- May include compounds not present in dried herb

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires fresh plant material
- Short shelf life
- Taste can be intensely bitter

**Modern Context:** Less practical for most users; fresh herb access limited

---

## Tincture (Alcohol Extract)

**Standard Ratio:** 1:5 (herb:menstruum) at 25-40% alcohol

**Preparation Method (Folk Method):**
1. Combine 1 part dried gotu kola (by weight) with 5 parts menstruum (by volume)
2. Menstruum: 25-40% alcohol (vodka or grain alcohol diluted with water)
3. Macerate in sealed jar for 4-6 weeks, shaking daily
4. Strain and bottle in dark glass

**Dosing:**
- **Standard dose:** 2-4 ml (40-80 drops) three times daily
- **Daily total:** 6-12 ml

**Triterpene Content (Estimated):**
- Difficult to estimate; depends on extraction efficiency
- Alcohol extracts triterpenes moderately well

**Advantages:**
- Long shelf life (2-5 years)
- Concentrated form
- Rapid absorption (sublingual)

**Disadvantages:**
- Alcohol content (contraindicated for some users)
- Less clinical data than standardized extracts

**Commercial Products:** Look for 1:5 or 1:2 ratio tinctures from reputable herbalists

## Topical Preparations

### Post-Laser Wound Healing
- **Concentration:** 0.05% w/w ECa 233 gel
- **Application:** Apply thin layer to affected area 2-3 times daily

### General Wound Healing
- **Concentration:** 1% cutaneous cream
- **Application:** Apply to wound edges and surrounding skin 2-3 times daily

### Cutaneous Ulcerations
- **Concentration:** 2% powder
- **Application:** Dust directly onto ulcer after cleaning, cover with sterile dressing

### Scar Prevention
- **Concentration:** 7% C. asiatica extract cream
- **Application:** Massage into scar tissue 2-3 times daily for 8-12 weeks

**Preparation of Home Topical (Simple Method):**
1. Mix gotu kola powder (standardized extract preferred) with base cream
2. Target concentration: 5-10% for scar management
3. Example: 5 grams standardized powder + 95 grams unscented cream base = 5% cream
4. Store in cool, dark place; use within 3 months

## Dosing by Indication (Summary)

### Cognitive Enhancement & Memory
- **Evidence-based:** 250-750 mg/day standardized extract (optimal: 750 mg)
- **Traditional equivalent:** 2-4 grams dried herb as decoction
- **Duration:** Minimum 2 months for effects
- **Timing:** Once daily, morning or early afternoon

### Anxiety & Stress
- **Evidence-based:** 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg/day total) standardized extract
- **Acute anxiety:** 12 grams single dose (high dose; consult practitioner)
- **Traditional equivalent:** 15-30 grams dried herb as decoction
- **Duration:** 60 days for sustained effects
- **Timing:** With meals

### Venous Insufficiency & Edema
- **Evidence-based:** 60-180 mg TTFCA daily (optimal: 120-180 mg)
- **Dosing schedule:** Divided doses (2-3x daily with meals)
- **Duration:** 4-6 months
- **Note:** Requires standardized TTFCA extract

### Wound Healing (Oral)
- **Evidence-based:** 300 mg asiaticoside daily (specific to diabetic wounds)
- **Dosing schedule:** 50 mg asiaticoside capsule, 2 capsules three times daily after meals
- **Duration:** Until wound closure (up to 21 days in studies)
- **Note:** Requires asiaticoside-standardized product

### Scar Management (Oral)
- **Evidence-based:** 450 mg/day standardized extract
- **Timing:** Weeks 2-6 post-surgery
- **Dosing schedule:** 225 mg twice daily
- **Note:** Combine with topical application for enhanced effect

### Wound Healing & Scar Prevention (Topical)
- **Concentration:** 1-7% cream/gel
- **Frequency:** 2-3 times daily
- **Duration:** 8-12 weeks for scars; until wound closure for acute wounds

## Special Dosing Considerations

### Accumulation Effect
- Active metabolites (asiatic acid, madecassic acid) **accumulate with repeated dosing**
- Peak effects may not appear until 7-14 days of consistent use
- Implication: Start at lower dose, titrate up if needed after 2 weeks

### With or Without Food?
- Clinical trials used dosing **with meals**
- May reduce potential GI upset
- No evidence that food significantly affects absorption

### Time of Day
- **Cognitive/mood effects:** Morning or early afternoon (avoid evening due to potential alertness effects)
- **Venous insufficiency:** Divided doses with meals (2-3x daily)
- **Anxiolytic effects:** Evening dosing may be beneficial for stress/anxiety

### Duration of Treatment
- **Acute:** Single dose to 21 days (wound healing, acute anxiety)
- **Short-term:** 2-3 months (cognitive enhancement, scar management)
- **Long-term:** 6 months (venous insufficiency, diabetic microangiopathy)
- **Safety:** Well-tolerated for up to 6 months in clinical trials; traditional use suggests safe for longer periods with monitoring

## Practical Product Selection Guide

### What to Look For on Labels

**High-Quality Standardized Extract:**
1. **Triterpene content:** Minimum 40%, ideally 80-85%
2. **Specific compounds listed:** Asiaticoside and/or madecassoside percentages
3. **Third-party testing:** USP, NSF, or ConsumerLab certification
4. **Batch number and expiration date**
5. **Genus and species:** "Centella asiatica" (not just "gotu kola")

**Red Flags:**
- No standardization information
- "Proprietary blend" without specific amounts
- Extremely low price (suggests low quality or adulteration)
- Claims that seem too good to be true
- No contact information for manufacturer

### Comparing Products

**Example Product Comparison:**

| Product | Standardization | Dose per Capsule | Capsules Needed for 400mg Triterpenes | Cost Efficiency |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|---------------------------------------|-----------------|
| Product A | 80% triterpenes | 500 mg | 1 capsule | Best |
| Product B | 40% triterpenes | 500 mg | 2 capsules | Moderate |
| Product C | 10% triterpenes | 500 mg | 8 capsules | Poor |
| Product D | "Whole herb" unstandardized | 500 mg | Unknown (assume 6%) = 13 capsules | Very poor |

**Cost Calculation:**
- Calculate cost per mg of triterpenes
- Example: $20 for 60 capsules of 500mg at 80% = 24,000 mg triterpenes total = $0.00083 per mg triterpene
- Compare across brands using this metric

## Preparation Forms: Pros & Cons Summary

| Form | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|------|------|------|----------|
| **ECa 233 (80%+)** | Strong clinical evidence, consistent quality, convenient | Cost, less vascular data | Cognitive, anxiety, general wellness |
| **TTFCA** | Best evidence for vascular conditions, dose-ranging data | Specific indication, harder to find | Venous insufficiency, edema, microangiopathy |
| **Standardized extract (40%+)** | Good balance of potency and cost | Less clinical data than ECa 233 | General use, budget-conscious |
| **Traditional decoction** | Traditional method, full-spectrum | Time-consuming, bitter taste, inconsistent potency | Traditional wellness, those preferring whole-herb |
| **Tea/infusion** | Gentle, pleasant ritual, low cost | Low potency, less bioavailable | Mild relaxation, daily wellness |
| **Tincture** | Long shelf life, rapid absorption, concentrated | Alcohol content, less clinical data | Quick absorption needed, alcohol tolerance |
| **Topical** | Direct application, no systemic effects | Limited to skin conditions | Wounds, scars, skin conditions |
| **Fresh juice** | Traditional, full-spectrum, fresh compounds | Requires fresh plant, very bitter, short shelf life | Traditional Ayurvedic practice, garden access |

## Storage & Shelf Life

### Dried Herb
- **Storage:** Airtight container, cool, dark, dry location
- **Shelf life:** 1-2 years (potency degrades over time)
- **Signs of degradation:** Color fading (from green to brown), loss of aroma

### Standardized Extracts (Capsules/Tablets)
- **Storage:** Original container, cool, dry location (avoid bathroom humidity)
- **Shelf life:** 2-3 years (check expiration date)
- **Signs of degradation:** Discoloration, unusual odor, capsule deterioration

### Tinctures
- **Storage:** Dark glass bottle, cool location (room temperature acceptable)
- **Shelf life:** 2-5 years (alcohol preserves well)
- **Signs of degradation:** Cloudiness, sediment, off odor

### Topical Preparations
- **Storage:** Refrigerate if homemade; cool, dry location if commercial
- **Shelf life:** 3-6 months (homemade); 1-2 years (commercial with preservatives)
- **Signs of degradation:** Separation, mold, rancid smell, color change

## Key Takeaways for Practical Use

1. **For evidence-based cognitive/anxiety support:** Choose ECa 233 or high-triterpene extract (≥40%), dose 500-1000 mg/day

2. **For vascular conditions:** Seek TTFCA specifically, dose 60-180 mg/day

3. **For traditional wellness:** Decoction or tea of whole herb, 2-4 grams dried herb per serving

4. **For topical use:** 5-10% cream for scars/wounds

5. **Quality matters:** Standardization ensures consistent dosing and replicates clinical trial conditions

6. **Duration matters:** Minimum 2 months for cognitive effects; up to 6 months for vascular effects

7. **Accumulation occurs:** Effects build over 1-2 weeks; don't judge efficacy on day 1
