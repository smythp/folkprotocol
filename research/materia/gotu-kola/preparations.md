# Gotu Kola (Centella asiatica) - Preparations & Dosing

## Standardized Extract Preparations

### ECa 233 (Standardized Extract)

**Standardization:**
- Consistent triterpene content
- Standardized to asiaticoside equivalents
- Available in both oral and topical formulations

**Oral Dosing (from clinical trials):**
- **250 mg capsules:** Once or twice daily (250-500 mg/day)
- **500 mg capsules:** Once or twice daily (500-1000 mg/day)

**Clinical applications:**
- Cognitive support: 250-500 mg twice daily
- Temporomandibular pain: 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg total)
- Anxiety/mood: 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg total)

**Duration:**
- Acute effects: Single doses studied for anxiolytic effects
- Cognitive/mood: 2-6 months
- Pain: 14 days studied (benefits at day 7)

**Safety profile:**
- Well-tolerated at 250-500 mg doses in Phase 1 trials
- No discontinuations due to adverse effects
- Safe for 7-day repeated dosing

**Topical Formulations:**
- **0.05% w/w ECa 233 gel:** For wound healing (post-laser resurfacing)
- **7% C. asiatica extract cream:** For scar prevention

### TTFCA (Total Triterpenic Fraction of Centella asiatica)

**Standardization:**
- Concentrated triterpene preparation
- Removes non-triterpene components
- Higher potency than crude extracts

**Dosing for Vascular Conditions:**

**Low dose:**
- **30 mg twice daily (60 mg total/day)**
- Used in some venous insufficiency trials
- Less effective than higher doses

**Standard dose:**
- **60 mg twice daily (120 mg total/day)**
- Most commonly used dose in clinical trials
- Optimal balance of efficacy and safety

**High dose:**
- **60 mg three times daily (180 mg total/day)**
- More effective than 90 mg/day for venous insufficiency
- Dose-dependent response demonstrated

**Clinical applications:**
- Chronic venous insufficiency: 60-180 mg/day
- Diabetic microangiopathy: 120 mg/day (60 mg twice daily)
- Venous hypertension: 120-180 mg/day

**Duration:**
- Vascular conditions: 2-6 months typical
- Diabetic microangiopathy: 6-12 months studied

**Dose-response relationship:**
- Clear dose-dependent effects demonstrated
- 120-180 mg/day more effective than 30-90 mg/day
- Higher doses (120 mg) more effective than lower dose (60 mg) for venous hypertension

### CAP (Centella asiatica Aqueous Extract Product)

**Extraction method:** Water-based (aqueous) extraction

**Composition:**
- Contains triterpenes
- Also contains caffeoylquinic acids (with delayed absorption)
- Broader phytochemical profile than TTFCA

**Dosing:**
- **2 g daily:** Lower dose studied
- **4 g daily:** Higher dose studied

**Clinical application:**
- Cognitive support in elderly populations
- Studied specifically in cognitively impaired older adults on cholinesterase inhibitors

**Safety:**
- Well-tolerated in elderly
- Safe in combination with cholinesterase inhibitors

### Asiaticoside (Isolated Triterpene)

**Preparation:** Isolated triterpene glycoside

**Dosing:**
- **50 mg per capsule, three times daily (150 mg total/day)**

**Clinical application:**
- Diabetic wound healing (oral use)

**Duration:**
- 21 days studied

## Traditional Crude Herb Preparations

### Dried Herb Powder

**Dosing range:**
- **1-4 g daily** (referenced in clinical summary as traditional dose range)

**Standardization challenge:**
- Variable triterpene content in crude herb
- 2% minimum triterpenes (WHO standard) for quality crude material
- Less precise dosing than standardized extracts

**Traditional preparation methods:**
- Encapsulated powder
- Mixed with water or other liquids
- Incorporated into traditional formulations

### Tincture Preparations

**Extraction ratio (from USP monograph):**
- **30:1 to 65:1 plant material to extract**
- Typical tincture ratios would be inverse (e.g., 1:30 to 1:65)

**Solvents used:**
- Alcohol (ethanol)
- Methanol (pharmaceutical/research use)
- Acetone (pharmaceutical/research use)
- Mixtures of solvents

**Note on traditional tinctures:**
While the bibliography provides USP extraction ratios for standardized extracts, it does not specify traditional folk tincture ratios (such as 1:5 or 1:2 fresh herb tinctures common in Western herbalism).

**Triterpene content in tinctures:**
- Should contain 90-110% of labeled triterpene amount (USP standard)
- Quality tinctures should specify triterpene content

**Approximate tincture dosing (extrapolated from extract data):**
If using a tincture standardized to contain equivalent triterpenes to clinical doses:
- For ECa 233 equivalent (250-500 mg extract): Would need tincture volume delivering equivalent triterpene content
- Calculation requires knowing exact triterpene percentage in tincture

### Tea/Infusion Preparations

**Note:** The bibliography does not provide specific data on traditional tea/infusion dosing.

**Considerations for tea preparation:**
- **Crude herb dose:** 1-4 g daily suggests this amount could be used for tea
- **Bioavailability concern:** Water extraction may not efficiently extract triterpene glycosides
- **Traditional context:** Used in Ayurvedic and Asian traditions but specific preparation ratios not detailed in bibliography

**Estimated tea preparation (not from bibliography data):**
- 1-4 g dried herb per cup
- Steep duration not specified
- May require multiple cups daily to achieve therapeutic dose

**Limitation:** Aqueous extraction may have lower triterpene content than alcohol extracts or standardized preparations

### Fresh Juice

**Traditional preparation:** Fresh leaf juice used in Ayurvedic tradition

**Note:** Bibliography does not provide specific dosing for fresh juice preparations

**Consideration:**
- Fresh herb has higher water content than dried
- Triterpene content per gram of fresh material lower than dried
- Would require larger volume than dried herb

## Topical Preparations

### Wound Healing Formulations

**ECa 233 Gel:**
- **Concentration:** 0.05% w/w
- **Application:** Post-laser resurfacing for acne scars
- **Clinical findings:** Significantly less erythema, improved wound healing (Damkerngsuntorn et al., 2020)

**C. asiatica Extract Cream:**
- **Concentration:** 7% C. asiatica extract
- **Application:** Scar prevention (split-thickness skin graft scars)
- **Clinical findings:** Improved pigmentation from week 8 (Jenwitheesuk et al., 2018)

**Duration of topical use:**
- Wound healing: 3 weeks to 6 months
- Scar treatment: 12 weeks studied

### Cosmetic Formulations

**Safety for cosmetic use (Johnson et al., 2023):**
- Generally regarded as safe for cosmetic use
- Centella Asiatica Extract (30%) classified as weak sensitizer
- Patch testing advisable for sensitive skin

**Common cosmetic concentrations:**
- Widely variable (0.05% to 30%)
- Lower concentrations for sensitive skin products
- Higher concentrations for targeted treatment products

## Comparative Bioavailability of Preparations

### Poor Bioavailability Challenge

**All oral preparations face bioavailability limitation:**
- Parent glycosides (asiaticoside, madecassoside): <1% oral bioavailability
- Require conversion to active metabolites (asiatic acid, madecassic acid)

### Formulation Strategies to Improve Bioavailability

**Modified ECa 233 (Songvut et al., 2021):**
- Improved dissolution properties
- Higher plasma levels of active metabolites at same dose
- Formulation modifications can enhance bioavailability

**Aqueous vs. Alcohol Extracts:**
- **Aqueous extracts (CAP):** Broader phytochemical profile, includes caffeoylquinic acids
- **Alcohol extracts (TTFCA, ECa 233):** More concentrated triterpene content
- Both achieve detectable plasma metabolite levels

### Accumulation with Repeated Dosing

**All preparations show accumulation:**
- **Single dose:** Initial plasma levels
- **7-day repeated dosing:** Significantly higher peak plasma concentrations, AUC, and half-life
- **Clinical implication:** Steady-state achieved with regular dosing; effects may increase over first week

## Dosing by Indication

### Venous Insufficiency & Vascular Conditions
**Preparation:** TTFCA
**Dose:** 60-180 mg/day (60 mg twice daily to 60 mg three times daily)
**Duration:** 2-6 months

### Diabetic Microangiopathy
**Preparation:** TTFCA
**Dose:** 120 mg/day (60 mg twice daily)
**Duration:** 6-12 months

### Anxiety & Stress
**Preparation:** Standardized extract (ECa 233 or equivalent)
**Dose:** 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg total/day)
**Duration:** 60 days minimum

### Acute Anxiolytic Effect
**Preparation:** Gotu Kola extract
**Dose:** Single 12 g oral dose
**Duration:** Single dose (effects at 30-60 minutes)
**Note:** This very high single dose used in research; not standard clinical practice

### Cognitive Support
**Preparation:** ECa 233 or CAP
**Dose:**
- ECa 233: 250-500 mg twice daily
- CAP: 2-4 g daily
**Duration:** 2-6 months
**Note:** Evidence for cognitive benefits is weak despite traditional use

### Temporomandibular Pain
**Preparation:** ECa 233
**Dose:** 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg total/day)
**Duration:** 14 days (benefits seen at day 7)

### Diabetic Wound Healing (Oral)
**Preparation:** Asiaticoside
**Dose:** 50 mg three times daily (150 mg total/day)
**Duration:** 21 days

### Wound Healing & Scars (Topical)
**Preparation:**
- ECa 233 gel (0.05% w/w) for post-procedure healing
- 7% extract cream for scar prevention
**Duration:** 3 weeks to 12 weeks depending on indication

## Quality Considerations

### Standardization Requirements

**Minimum standards:**
- **WHO:** 2% total triterpene ester glycosides
- **USP:** 90-110% of labeled triterpene amount
- **European Pharmacopoeia:** Specific marker compounds (asiaticoside, madecassoside, asiatic acid, madecassic acid)

### Choosing Quality Preparations

**Look for:**
- Standardized triterpene content (percentage stated on label)
- Third-party testing certification
- Adherence to pharmacopeial standards
- Consistent batch-to-batch composition

**Clinical trial preparations (ECa 233, TTFCA):**
- Highest confidence for clinical efficacy
- Consistent composition across studies
- May not be available in consumer market under these exact names

### Crude Herb Quality

**Quality markers:**
- Minimum 2% triterpenes (WHO standard)
- Proper botanical identification (Centella asiatica (L.) Urb.)
- Avoid confusion with Bacopa monnieri (also called "Brahmi")
- Proper harvesting and drying conditions

## Timing & Administration

### With or Without Food

**Jana et al., 2010 anxiety study:**
- 500 mg twice daily **after meals**
- Suggests taking with food may be preferable

**Other studies:**
- Many trials do not specify food requirements
- No specific food interactions noted

**Recommendation:** Take with food if gastrointestinal upset occurs (GI upset is common mild side effect)

### Time of Day

**Not specified in clinical trials**

**Considerations:**
- **Anxiolytic effects:** May prefer evening dosing if sedation occurs
- **Alertness effects:** Meta-analysis showed increased alertness; may prefer morning dosing
- **Twice daily dosing:** Morning and evening to maintain steady plasma levels

### Duration Before Effects

**Varies by indication:**
- **Acute anxiolytic:** 30-60 minutes (single high dose study)
- **Pain relief:** 7 days (temporomandibular pain study)
- **Vascular conditions:** 2-6 months
- **Cognitive/mood:** 30 days to 6 months
- **Wound healing:** 21 days to 12 weeks

**Accumulation period:** First 7 days for steady-state plasma levels
