# Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis L.) - Clinical Uses

## Anxiety & Depression

### Meta-Analysis Evidence (Ghazizadeh et al., 2021)
- **Effect on Anxiety**: SMD -0.98 (95% CI: -1.63 to -0.33; p=0.003)
- **Effect on Depression**: SMD -0.47 (95% CI: -0.73 to -0.21; p=0.0005)
- **Safety**: No serious adverse effects reported
- **Note**: High heterogeneity between studies - interpret with caution

### Pilot Trial (Cases et al., 2011)
- **Sample Size**: 20 participants with mild-to-moderate anxiety and sleep disturbances
- **Dose**: 600 mg daily (300 mg twice daily, Cyracos® standardized extract)
- **Duration**: 15 days
- **Results**:
  - Anxiety manifestations reduced by 18% (p<0.01)
  - Anxiety-associated symptoms ameliorated by 15% (p<0.01)
  - Insomnia lowered by 42% (p<0.01)
  - Response rate: 95% of participants
  - Complete remission: 70% for anxiety, 85% for insomnia

## Sleep Disorders

### Sleep Quality Cross-Over Study (Di Pierro et al., 2024)
- **Formulation**: Melissa officinalis Phytosome (MOP)
- **Duration**: Two-week treatment periods
- **Sample Size**: Not specified
- **Results**:
  - ISI score: 6.8±4.1 in treatment vs 9.7±3.7 in placebo (reduction of 2.9 points, p=0.003)
  - Slow-wave sleep (SWS) phase duration increased by 15%
  - REM phase decreased by 10%
  - Subjective sleep quality improvement: 87% in treatment vs 30% in placebo (p=0.0003)

### Menopausal Sleep Disorder (Shirazi et al., 2021)
- **Sample Size**: 60 postmenopausal women with sleep disturbance
- **Dose**: 500 mg daily
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Comparators**: Citalopram 30 mg daily, placebo
- **Results (all domains p<0.001)**:
  - Vasomotor: 2.2±0.84 (M.off) vs 0.56±0.58 (cit) vs 0.36±0.55 (pla)
  - Psychomotor-social: 1.02±0.6 vs 0.28±0.2 vs 0.17±0.1
  - Physical: 0.76±0.4 vs 0.25±0.1 vs 0.11±0.1
  - Sexual: 2.3±1.0 vs 0.35±0.5 vs 0.41±0.5
- **Conclusion**: Superior improvements across all quality-of-life domains compared to antidepressant and placebo

## Cognitive Function & Alzheimer's Disease

### Alzheimer's Disease Treatment (Akhondzadeh et al., 2003)
- **Sample Size**: 42 participants (18 women, 24 men, aged 65-80 years)
- **Diagnosis**: Mild to moderate Alzheimer's disease
- **Dose**: 60 drops daily of Melissa officinalis extract (fixed dose)
- **Duration**: 4 months
- **Results**:
  - ADAS-cog score: significantly better cognitive function vs placebo (F=6.93, p=0.01)
  - Clinical Dementia Rating (CDR): significantly better (F=16.87, p<0.0001)
  - Agitation: more common in placebo group (p=0.03)
- **Safety**: No significant differences in side effects between groups except agitation

### Alzheimer's Prevention (Noguchi-Shinohara et al., 2023)
- **Sample Size**: 323 participants with subjective or mild cognitive impairment
- **Dose**: 500 mg rosmarinic acid daily
- **Duration**: 96 weeks treatment + 24 weeks washout
- **Primary Outcome**: No significant differences in ADAS-cog scores between groups at 96 weeks
- **Secondary Finding**: In hypertension-free participants, CDR scores increased 0.006 in treatment group vs decreased 0.085 in placebo (p=0.036)
- **Conclusion**: Potential preventive benefits in hypertension-free subgroup

### Alzheimer's Neuropsychiatric Symptoms (Noguchi-Shinohara et al., 2020)
- **Sample Size**: 23 participants with mild dementia due to probable Alzheimer's disease
- **Dose**: 500 mg rosmarinic acid daily
- **Duration**: 24 weeks
- **Results**:
  - No significant differences in cognitive measures
  - NPI-Q scores improved by 0.5 points in treatment vs worsened by 0.7 in placebo (p=0.012)
- **Safety**: No serious adverse events; safe and well-tolerated
- **Conclusion**: May help prevent worsening of AD-related neuropsychiatric symptoms

### Acute Cognitive Effects (Kennedy et al., 2003)
- **Sample Size**: 20 healthy young participants
- **Study Design**: Randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind, balanced crossover trial
- **Doses**: 600 mg, 1000 mg, 1600 mg encapsulated dried leaf, plus placebo
- **Duration**: Single doses at 7-day intervals
- **Assessment Times**: Predose, 1h, 3h, 6h postdose
- **Results**:
  - **1600 mg dose**: improved memory performance and increased calmness at all postdose times
  - **Lower doses (600-1000 mg)**: decrements in speed of timed memory tasks and rapid visual information processing
- **Conclusion**: May be valuable adjunct in treatment of Alzheimer's disease

### Alzheimer's Agitation (Burns et al., 2011)
- **Sample Size**: 114 participants
- **Completers**: 94 at week 4; 81 at week 12
- **Groups**:
  - Placebo medication + active aromatherapy (Melissa oil)
  - Active medication (donepezil) + placebo aromatherapy
  - Placebo of both
- **Duration**: 12 weeks (primary assessment at 4 weeks)
- **Results**:
  - No significant differences between aromatherapy, donepezil and placebo at week 4 and week 12
  - 18% improvement in PAS and 37% improvement in NPI over 12 weeks across all groups
- **Conclusion**: Substantial placebo effects; no treatment-specific benefits demonstrated for aromatherapy route

## Type 2 Diabetes

### Glycemic Control (Asadi et al., 2019)
- **Sample Size**: 62 participants (31 treatment, 31 placebo)
- **Dose**: 700 mg/day (350 mg twice daily)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Glycemic Results**:
  - Fasting blood sugar: significant difference (p=0.007)
  - HbA1c: significant difference (p=0.002)
  - β-cell activity: significant difference (p=0.05)
  - HOMA-IR and insulin: no significant changes
- **Cardiovascular Results**:
  - Triglycerides: significant difference (p=0.04)
  - HDL-cholesterol: significant improvement (p=0.05 between groups; p=0.009 within treatment)
  - Total cholesterol and LDL-cholesterol: no significant changes
  - High-sensitivity CRP: significant reduction (p=0.001)
  - Systolic blood pressure: significant reduction (p=0.04)

### Apolipoproteins & Lipid Ratios (Asadi et al., 2018)
- **Sample Size**: 70 type 2 diabetic patients aged 20-65 years
- **Dose**: 700 mg/day hydroalcoholic extract (350 mg twice daily)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Results**:
  - ApoA-I: significant increase in treatment group (p=0.003); significant reduction in placebo (p=0.001)
  - ApoB: no significant differences between groups
  - TC/HDL-c and LDL-c/HDL-c ratios: significant improvements (p<0.05)
  - TG/HDL-c ratio: significant reduction in treatment group (p=0.05)
  - ICAM-1: both groups showed reduction, but no significant differences between groups
- **Safety**: No significant differences in liver enzymes (AST, ALT, ALP) between groups

### Metabolic Parameters (Nayebi et al., 2019)
- **Sample Size**: 37 participants (32 completers: 16 treatment, 16 placebo)
- **Product**: Melissa officinalis L. combined with Rosa damascena Mill.
- **Dose**: Two 500 mg capsules daily
- **Duration**: 3 months
- **Results**:
  - Triglycerides: significant decrease in all patients after 2 months (p=0.02)
  - Triglycerides in patients with baseline TG≥200 mg/dl: significant decrease after 3 months (p=0.04)
  - Systolic BP (baseline ≥130 mmHg): significant reduction (p=0.02)
  - Diastolic BP (baseline ≥85 mmHg): significant reduction (p=0.02)
  - Overall metabolic changes: no significant changes compared to control group

## Cardiovascular Health

### Lipid Profile Meta-Analysis (Shahsavari et al., 2024)
- **Studies Included**: 5 RCTs
- **Sample Size**: 302 total participants
- **Results**:
  - Triglycerides decreased: SMD -0.396 (95% CI: -0.620, -0.173; p=0.001)
  - Total cholesterol decreased: SMD -0.416 (95% CI: -0.641, -0.192; p<0.001)
  - LDL cholesterol decreased: SMD -0.23 (95% CI: -0.45, -0.008; p<0.05)
  - HDL cholesterol: no significant effect, SMD 0.336 (95% CI: -0.091, 0.767; p=0.123)
- **Conclusion**: May be administered as safe and beneficial herbal medicine for reducing TC, TG and LDL

### Cardio-Metabolic Outcomes Meta-Analysis (Heshmati et al., 2020)
- **Studies Included**: 7 RCTs
- **Results**:
  - Total cholesterol reduced: SMD -0.26 (95% CI: -0.52, -0.01; I²=13.7%; k=6)
  - Systolic blood pressure reduced: SMD -0.56 (95% CI: -0.85, -0.27; I²=0.0%; k=3)
  - No significant effects on: triglycerides, LDL, diastolic BP, CRP, FBS, HbA1c, insulin, or HDL
- **Safety**: No serious adverse events reported
- **Limitations**: Low number of included studies and serious risk of bias

### Heart Palpitations (Alijaniha et al., 2015)
- **Sample Size**: 55 participants (71 enrolled)
- **Product**: Lyophilized aqueous extract of M. officinalis leaves
- **Dose**: 500 mg twice daily
- **Duration**: 14 days
- **Results**:
  - Reduced frequency of palpitation episodes compared to placebo
  - Significantly reduced number of anxious patients vs placebo (p=0.004)
  - No serious side effects reported
- **Conclusion**: Safe herbal drug for treatment of benign palpitations

## Clinical Dosing Summary by Indication

### Anxiety & Depression
- 300 mg twice daily (600 mg/day) for 15 days (Cyracos® standardized extract)

### Sleep Disorders
- 300 mg twice daily (600 mg/day) for 15 days
- 500 mg daily for 8 weeks (menopausal women)
- Melissa officinalis Phytosome formulation (2-week treatment periods)

### Cognitive Function
- **Single doses**: 600-1600 mg (1600 mg most effective for acute effects)
- **Alzheimer's disease**: 60 drops daily of extract for 4 months
- **Long-term cognitive studies**: 500 mg rosmarinic acid daily (up to 96 weeks)

### Type 2 Diabetes
- 700 mg/day (350 mg twice daily) for 12 weeks

### Cardiovascular (Heart Palpitations)
- 500 mg twice daily for 14 days

### Traditional Use
- 1.5-4.5 g cut herb several times daily (Commission E recommendation)
