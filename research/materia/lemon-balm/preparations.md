# Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis L.) - Preparations & Dosing

## Standardized Extract Preparations

### European Pharmacopoeia Standard Extracts

**Dried Herbal Substance**:
- **Definition**: Whole or fragmented dried leaves of Melissa officinalis L.
- **Minimum rosmarinic acid**: 1%
- **Quality control**: HPLC quantification of rosmarinic acid

**Dried Extract**:
- **Minimum rosmarinic acid**: 2-3%
- **Hydroxycinnamic acid derivatives**: ≥10% (UV spectrophotometry)
- **Extraction solvent**: Typically 30-70% ethanol in water
- **Concentration ratio**: Variable, typically 4:1 to 10:1 (herb:extract)

### Cyracos® Standardized Extract

**Specifications**:
- **Extraction solvent**: 30% ethanol
- **Rosmarinic acid content**: >7%
- **Hydroxycinnamic acid derivatives**: >15%
- **Clinical evidence**: Used in published clinical trials (Cases et al., 2011)

**Dosing with Cyracos®**:
- **Standard dose**: 300 mg twice daily (600 mg/day)
- **Duration**: Minimum 15 days for anxiety/sleep effects
- **Rosmarinic acid delivery**: Approximately 42 mg/day at 7% concentration

### Generic Standardized Extracts (Market Standard)

**Typical Specifications**:
- **Rosmarinic acid**: 2-5% (minimum 2% per EMA requirements)
- **Extraction ratio**: 4:1 to 7:1 (herb:extract)
- **Hydroxycinnamic acids**: ≥10%

**Dose Equivalency**:
- 500 mg standardized extract (5% rosmarinic acid) = 25 mg rosmarinic acid
- Roughly equivalent to 2-3 g dried herb (assuming 1-1.5% rosmarinic acid in herb)

## Traditional Tea Preparations

### Commission E Recommended Tea Preparation

**Dosage**: 1.5-4.5 g dried leaf per serving
**Frequency**: Several times daily (typically 3-4 times)
**Daily total**: 4.5-18 g dried herb

### Standard Infusion Method

**Preparation**:
1. Use 1.5-4.5 g (approximately 1-2 teaspoons) dried lemon balm leaves
2. Add 150-200 mL (approximately 3/4 cup) boiling water
3. Steep covered for 5-10 minutes
4. Strain

**Dosing Schedule**:
- **Anxiety/stress**: 1 cup 2-3 times daily
- **Sleep**: 1 cup 30-60 minutes before bedtime
- **Digestive**: 1 cup before or after meals

**Active Compound Delivery**:
- Variable rosmarinic acid extraction (depends on steep time, temperature)
- Estimated 5-20 mg rosmarinic acid per cup (from 1.5 g herb at 0.5-1% content)

## Tincture Preparations

### Traditional Tincture Ratios

**Standard Preparations**:
- **1:5 (w/v)**: 1 part herb to 5 parts menstruum (most common)
- **1:10 (w/v)**: 1 part herb to 10 parts menstruum (weaker preparation)
- **Alcohol strength**: 30-70% ethanol

**Extraction Method** (1:5 preparation):
1. 200 g dried lemon balm leaf
2. 1000 mL menstruum (45-70% ethanol in water)
3. Macerate for 14 days with occasional agitation
4. Press and filter

### Traditional Tincture Dosing

**Standard Dose**:
- **40-80 drops** (approximately 2-4 mL) three times daily
- **Daily total**: 6-12 mL tincture

**Modern Clinical Correlation**:
- Akhondzadeh et al. 2003 (Alzheimer's): "60 drops daily" of extract
- Suggests traditional tincture dosing aligns with modern clinical evidence

**Active Compound Delivery** (estimated for 1:5, 50% alcohol tincture):
- 3 mL tincture contains approximately 600 mg herb equivalent
- At 1% rosmarinic acid: ~6 mg rosmarinic acid per 3 mL dose
- Daily total (12 mL): ~24 mg rosmarinic acid

## Fresh Herb Preparations

### Fresh Leaf Tea

**Preparation**:
- Use 3-6 g fresh leaves (approximately 2-3 tablespoons chopped)
- Fresh herb equivalent: approximately 3-4x weight of dried herb
- Steep 5-10 minutes in boiling water

**Advantages**:
- Full spectrum of volatile compounds
- Pleasant lemony flavor
- Traditional European household preparation

**Limitations**:
- Variable rosmarinic acid content by season
- Reduced potency compared to standardized extracts
- Limited shelf life

### Fresh Leaf Topical Applications

**Traditional Use**:
- Fresh leaves crushed and applied to herpes lesions (cold sores)
- Fresh leaf poultices for wounds, insect bites

**Note**: Topical applications not covered in detail in this bibliography (focus on oral administration)

## Phytosome Formulations

### Melissa officinalis Phytosome (MOP)

**Technology**: Phospholipid complex for enhanced bioavailability

**Clinical Evidence** (Di Pierro et al., 2024):
- **Improved sleep quality**: ISI score reduction of 2.9 points (p=0.003)
- **Slow-wave sleep**: Increased by 15%
- **Subjective improvement**: 87% vs 30% placebo (p=0.0003)

**Mechanism**:
- Phospholipid (typically phosphatidylcholine) complex with active compounds
- Enhanced membrane permeability and cellular uptake
- Potentially lower dose requirements for equivalent effects

**Dosing**: Not specified in study, but typically lower than standard extracts due to enhanced absorption

## Clinical Trial Dosing by Indication

### Anxiety & Depression

**Cases et al. 2011**:
- **Product**: Cyracos® standardized extract
- **Dose**: 300 mg twice daily (600 mg/day)
- **Duration**: 15 days minimum
- **Results**: 18% reduction in anxiety manifestations, 42% reduction in insomnia

### Sleep Disorders

**Multiple formulations effective**:

1. **Cyracos® extract** (Cases et al., 2011):
   - 300 mg twice daily (600 mg/day)
   - 15 days treatment
   - 85% complete remission for insomnia

2. **Standard extract** (Shirazi et al., 2021):
   - 500 mg daily
   - 8 weeks treatment
   - Menopausal women with sleep disturbance

3. **Phytosome formulation** (Di Pierro et al., 2024):
   - Dose not specified
   - 2-week treatment periods
   - 87% subjective improvement

### Cognitive Function & Alzheimer's Disease

**Acute cognitive effects** (Kennedy et al., 2003):
- **Single dose**: 600-1600 mg dried leaf
- **Optimal dose**: 1600 mg for memory improvement and calmness
- **Lower doses (600-1000 mg)**: May cause sedation without cognitive benefit

**Alzheimer's disease treatment** (Akhondzadeh et al., 2003):
- **Dose**: 60 drops daily of extract (approximately 3-4 mL)
- **Duration**: 4 months
- **Results**: Significant improvements in ADAS-cog and CDR scores

**Long-term cognitive studies** (Noguchi-Shinohara et al., 2020, 2023):
- **Dose**: 500 mg rosmarinic acid daily (from M. officinalis extract)
- **Duration**: 24-96 weeks
- **Note**: Requires highly standardized extract (approximately 7-10% rosmarinic acid)

### Type 2 Diabetes

**Asadi et al. 2018, 2019**:
- **Dose**: 700 mg/day hydroalcoholic extract (350 mg twice daily)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Results**: Improved glycemic control, lipid profile, blood pressure

### Cardiovascular (Heart Palpitations)

**Alijaniha et al. 2015**:
- **Product**: Lyophilized aqueous extract
- **Dose**: 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg/day)
- **Duration**: 14 days
- **Results**: Reduced palpitation frequency, reduced anxiety

## Dose Equivalency Calculations

### Dried Herb to Standardized Extract

**Assumptions**:
- Dried herb: 1% rosmarinic acid
- Standardized extract: 5% rosmarinic acid

**Equivalencies**:
- 1 g dried herb (10 mg rosmarinic acid) ≈ 200 mg standardized extract (10 mg rosmarinic acid)
- 3 g dried herb ≈ 600 mg standardized extract
- 4.5 g dried herb (Commission E maximum) ≈ 900 mg standardized extract

### Tea to Capsule Conversion

**Traditional tea preparation** (1.5 g herb per cup, 3 cups daily):
- Total herb: 4.5 g/day
- Rosmarinic acid: ~22-45 mg/day (assuming 0.5-1% content)

**Equivalent capsule dose**:
- 600-900 mg standardized extract (5% rosmarinic acid)
- Provides 30-45 mg rosmarinic acid/day

### Tincture to Capsule Conversion

**Traditional tincture** (12 mL/day of 1:5 preparation):
- Herb equivalent: ~2.4 g/day
- Rosmarinic acid: ~12-24 mg/day

**Equivalent capsule dose**:
- 400-500 mg standardized extract (5% rosmarinic acid)

## Administration Guidelines

### Timing Considerations

**For anxiety/stress**:
- Divide daily dose into 2-3 administrations
- Take throughout day as needed

**For sleep**:
- Single dose 30-60 minutes before bedtime
- Or 300 mg twice daily (late afternoon and bedtime)

**For cognitive function**:
- **Acute effects**: Single dose 1 hour before cognitive demand
- **Long-term**: Daily dosing with meals

**For diabetes/metabolic effects**:
- Twice daily with meals (morning and evening)

### Food Effects

**Pharmacokinetic data** (Noguchi-Shinohara et al., 2015):
- Food increases AUC (total absorption) of rosmarinic acid
- Food delays Tmax (time to peak concentration)

**Clinical recommendation**: Take with food to enhance bioavailability, unless acute effects desired (take on empty stomach for faster onset)

## Quality Considerations for Preparations

### Selecting Standardized Extracts

**Minimum quality markers**:
- Rosmarinic acid: ≥2% (EMA standard)
- Hydroxycinnamic acid derivatives: ≥10%

**Optimal specifications**:
- Rosmarinic acid: 5-7% (aligns with clinical trial products)
- GMP manufacturing (Good Manufacturing Practices)
- Third-party testing for purity

### Selecting Dried Herb

**Quality indicators**:
- Rosmarinic acid: ≥1% (European Pharmacopoeia standard)
- Appearance: Green leaves, not brown (oxidation indicator)
- Aroma: Fresh, lemony scent
- Source: Certified organic or tested for pesticides

### Storage Recommendations

**Dried herb**:
- Store in airtight container
- Protect from light and moisture
- Use within 1 year of harvest

**Standardized extracts** (capsules/tablets):
- Store in cool, dry place
- Protect from light
- Follow expiration date

**Tinctures**:
- Store in dark glass bottle
- Keep tightly capped
- Shelf life: 3-5 years if properly stored

## Preparation-Specific Safety Notes

### Alcohol-Based Tinctures
- **Alcohol content**: Typically 30-70%
- **Contraindications**: Avoid in individuals with alcohol use disorders
- **Pediatric use**: Not recommended due to alcohol content
- **Alternative**: Glycerin-based tinctures (glycerites) for alcohol-free option

### Standardized Extracts
- **Quality control**: Use products from reputable manufacturers with third-party testing
- **Adulteration risk**: Lower with certified products meeting European Pharmacopoeia standards

### Tea Preparations
- **Variable potency**: Active compound content varies by plant source, growing conditions, storage
- **Benefit**: Generally gentler, suitable for traditional use and mild symptoms
- **Limitation**: May not provide therapeutic doses for clinical conditions

## Cost-Effectiveness Considerations

### Relative Costs (Approximate)

**Dried herb (tea)**:
- Lowest cost per dose
- Suitable for: Mild symptoms, traditional use, daily wellness

**Tincture**:
- Moderate cost
- Suitable for: Traditional dosing, alcohol-based extraction benefits

**Standardized extract capsules**:
- Higher cost
- Suitable for: Clinical conditions, consistent dosing, convenience

**Phytosome formulations**:
- Highest cost
- Suitable for: Enhanced bioavailability, potentially lower doses needed

## Summary: Preparation Selection Guide

### For Mild Anxiety/Stress or General Wellness
- **Recommendation**: Tea (1.5-3 g dried herb, 2-3 times daily)
- **Alternative**: Standardized extract 300 mg twice daily

### For Moderate Anxiety or Sleep Disorders
- **Recommendation**: Standardized extract 300 mg twice daily (minimum 2% rosmarinic acid)
- **Optimal**: Cyracos® 300 mg twice daily (clinical evidence)
- **Duration**: Minimum 15 days

### For Cognitive Support
- **Acute**: 1600 mg dried leaf single dose, or equivalent extract (approximately 600-800 mg at 2:1 concentration)
- **Long-term**: 500 mg rosmarinic acid daily (requires high-potency standardized extract)

### For Type 2 Diabetes (Adjunct)
- **Recommendation**: 700 mg/day standardized extract (350 mg twice daily)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks minimum
- **Note**: Use under medical supervision with glucose monitoring

### For Cardiovascular Support
- **Recommendation**: 500 mg twice daily standardized extract
- **Duration**: Based on clinical need and provider guidance

### For Traditional/Historical Use
- **Recommendation**: Follow Commission E guidelines (1.5-4.5 g dried herb several times daily)
- **Forms**: Tea or tincture (traditional preparations)
