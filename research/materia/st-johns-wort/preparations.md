# St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) - Preparations & Dosing

## Standardized Extracts (Most Evidence-Based)

Standardized extracts have the most robust clinical evidence for efficacy in mild-to-moderate depression.

### WS 5570 (Most Extensively Studied)

**Manufacturer**: Dr. Willmar Schwabe Pharmaceuticals

**Description**: Hydroalcoholic extract with defined specifications for hypericin, hyperforin, and flavonoids

**Source**: Hypericum perforatum aerial parts (flowering tops)

**Standardization**:
- Hypericin: Standardized content
- Hyperforin: Standard content (high-hyperforin formulation)
- Flavonoids: Defined specifications

**Clinical Evidence**:
- Kasper 2006: 332 patients, major depression (PMID: 16796730)
- Lecrubier 2002: 375 patients, mild to moderate depression (PMID: 12153829)
- Multiple other clinical trials

**Dosing**:
- **Mild-to-Moderate Depression**: 600-1200 mg/day
- **Typical Dosing**: 300 mg three times daily (900 mg/day) OR 600 mg once daily
- **Administration**: With or without food; consistency important

**Efficacy**: Superior to placebo, comparable to SSRIs for mild to moderate depression

**Safety**: Well tolerated at 600-1200 mg/day doses

**Drug Interactions**: Standard hyperforin content - use caution with medications metabolized by CYP3A4

---

### WS 5572 (Low-Hyperforin Formulation)

**Manufacturer**: Dr. Willmar Schwabe Pharmaceuticals

**Description**: Low-hyperforin standardized St. John's Wort extract

**Source**: Hypericum perforatum

**Standardization**:
- Hypericin: 0.1-0.3%
- **Hyperforin: Low content (≤1 mg/day)** to minimize drug interactions

**Clinical Significance**: Developed to reduce CYP3A4 induction while maintaining antidepressant efficacy

**Advantages**:
- Maintains antidepressant effects
- Significantly reduced drug interaction risk
- Suitable for patients on medications metabolized by CYP3A4

**When to Use**:
- Patients on oral contraceptives
- Patients on cardiovascular medications (warfarin, digoxin, statins)
- Patients requiring reduced drug interaction risk
- NOT for transplant patients or those on critical medications (still some interaction risk)

**Dosing**: 900 mg/day (typical), following same schedule as WS 5570

---

### LI 160

**Manufacturer**: Lichtwer Pharma

**Description**: Standardized St. John's Wort extract

**Source**: Hypericum perforatum

**Standardization**:
- **Hypericin**: 0.3%
- **Hyperforin**: Defined content (standard formulation)

**Clinical Significance**: One of the earliest extensively studied standardized extracts

**Clinical Evidence**: Multiple early RCTs on depression

**Historical Importance**: Established efficacy of standardized St. John's Wort extracts in clinical trials

**Dosing**: 900 mg/day (typical), 300 mg three times daily

---

### Ze 117

**Manufacturer**: Max Zeller Söhne AG

**Description**: St. John's Wort extract for long-term use

**Source**: Hypericum perforatum

**Clinical Evidence**:
- Brattström 2009: 440 patients, 1-year safety study (PMID: 19299116)
- Demonstrated long-term safety profile

**Dosing**: **500 mg daily**

**Long-Term Safety**:
- No changes in clinical chemistry over 1 year
- No ECG changes
- Stable body mass index
- Safe for up to 1 year of continuous use

**Indication**: Mild to moderate depression with long-term treatment goal

## Standard Clinical Dosing Recommendations

### For Depression (Mild-to-Moderate)

**Standard Dose**: 900 mg/day of standardized extract
- **Standardization**: 0.3% hypericin, 2-4% hyperforin (high-hyperforin formulations)

**Administration Options**:
1. **300 mg three times daily** (with meals for GI tolerance)
2. **450 mg twice daily** (morning and evening)
3. **600 mg once daily** (if using certain formulations)

**Titration**:
- Start: 900 mg/day
- If inadequate response after 4 weeks: May increase to 1200-1800 mg/day
- Maximum studied dose: 1800 mg/day

### Duration of Treatment

**Initial Trial**: 4-6 weeks to assess response
- **Onset of Action**: 2-4 weeks (similar to synthetic antidepressants)
- **Assessment**: Re-evaluate depression severity at 4-6 weeks using HAM-D or similar scale

**Continuation**: If effective, continue for at least 6 months to prevent relapse
- Maintenance therapy recommended for patients with history of recurrent depression

**Long-Term Use**: Safe for up to 1 year based on clinical trials
- Brattström 2009 demonstrated safety for continuous use up to 1 year

### Low-Hyperforin Formulations (When Drug Interactions Are a Concern)

**Indication**: When patient is on medications metabolized by CYP3A4

**Hyperforin Threshold**: ≤1 mg/day to minimize CYP3A4 induction

**Dosing**: 900 mg/day (same as standard formulations)

**Affected Medications** (when low-hyperforin is preferred):
- Oral contraceptives
- Warfarin
- Digoxin
- Statins
- Certain benzodiazepines

## Traditional Preparations

### Tincture (Alcohol Extract)

#### Traditional Folk Preparation
**Ratio**: 1:5 (herb to alcohol)
- 1 part dried flowering tops (by weight)
- 5 parts alcohol solution

**Alcohol Strength**: 40-60% alcohol (80-120 proof)
- Vodka (40%) or grain alcohol diluted to 50-60%

**Preparation Method**:
1. Coarsely chop dried flowering tops
2. Place herb in glass jar
3. Cover with alcohol (1:5 ratio by weight)
4. Seal jar and store in cool, dark place
5. Shake daily for 2-4 weeks
6. Strain through cheesecloth or fine mesh
7. Store in dark glass bottles (amber or cobalt blue)

**Traditional Dose**: 20-30 drops (approximately 1-1.5 mL), 2-3 times daily
- Modern recommendation: 2-4 mL, 2-3 times daily (approximately 40-80 drops per dose)

**Administration**: Dilute in small amount of water or take directly under tongue

**Storage**: Dark glass bottles, cool location, shelf life 2-5 years

**Clinical Note**: Tinctures are less standardized than commercial extracts; hyperforin and hypericin content varies

---

#### Commercial Standardized Tincture (1:2 Ratio)
**Ratio**: 1:2 (herb to alcohol) - more concentrated

**Standardization**: Some commercial tinctures standardized to 0.3% hypericin

**Dose**: 1-2 mL, 2-3 times daily (more concentrated than 1:5 folk preparations)

**Advantages**:
- Higher concentration of active constituents
- Standardized hypericin content (some brands)
- Quality-controlled manufacturing

---

### Tea (Infusion)

#### Traditional Tea Preparation
**Amount**: 2-4 grams dried herb per day
- 1-2 teaspoons dried flowering tops per cup

**Preparation**:
1. Boil water (approximately 8 oz / 240 mL per cup)
2. Add 1-2 teaspoons dried St. John's Wort flowering tops
3. Cover and steep for 10-15 minutes (covering prevents loss of volatile oils)
4. Strain and drink

**Frequency**: 1-3 cups daily

**Timing**: Between meals or with light meals

**Traditional Uses**:
- Mild depression or low mood
- Nervous exhaustion
- Sleep disturbances
- Digestive complaints

**Clinical Note**:
- Tea is **significantly less concentrated** than standardized extracts
- Hyperforin content may be lower (hyperforin is sensitive to heat and light)
- Traditional preparation for mild symptoms
- Not equivalent to 900 mg standardized extract doses used in clinical trials

**Taste**: Mildly bitter, slightly astringent, pleasant herbal flavor

---

### St. John's Wort Oil (Oleum Hyperici) - External Use

#### Traditional Folk Preparation
**Purpose**: Topical application for wounds, burns, muscle pain, nerve pain

**Plant Material**: Fresh flowers (preferred) or dried flowers
- Harvest at peak bloom (late June-July, around St. John's Day)

**Carrier Oil**: Olive oil or sunflower oil (organic, cold-pressed preferred)

**Traditional Method**:
1. Harvest fresh St. John's Wort flowers in morning after dew has dried
2. Fill a clean glass jar with flowers (loosely packed)
3. Cover completely with olive oil or sunflower oil
4. Place jar in sunny location (windowsill or outdoors)
5. Allow to macerate for 3-6 weeks
6. Oil will turn deep red (hypericin extraction)
7. Strain through cheesecloth
8. Store in dark glass bottles

**Color**: Characteristic deep red color indicates proper hypericin extraction

**Storage**: Dark glass bottles (amber or cobalt blue), cool location, shelf life 1-2 years

**Application**:
- Apply directly to affected area
- Use 2-3 times daily
- Massage gently into skin for muscle/nerve pain

**Traditional Uses**:
- Minor burns and sunburn
- Wounds and cuts (after initial healing has begun)
- Bruises and muscle pain
- Nerve pain (neuralgia)
- Muscle tension

**Caution**: **Do not apply before sun exposure** - risk of photosensitivity and severe skin reactions

---

#### Commercial St. John's Wort Oil
**Advantages**:
- Quality-controlled manufacturing
- Standardized hypericin content (some brands)
- Sterile preparation

**Use**: Follow manufacturer's instructions (typically 2-3 applications daily)

---

### Capsules/Tablets (Dried Herb Powder - Non-Standardized)

**Form**: Dried flowering tops powdered and encapsulated

**Dose**: Variable, typically 300-500 mg per capsule, 2-3 times daily

**Clinical Note**:
- **Not equivalent to standardized extracts**
- Hypericin and hyperforin content varies widely
- Less reliable for clinical depression
- Suitable for very mild symptoms or general wellness

**Recommendation**: Use standardized extracts for depression treatment

## Dosing by Indication

### Mild-to-Moderate Depression (Primary Indication)
**Preparation**: Standardized extract (0.3% hypericin, 2-4% hyperforin)
**Dose**: 900 mg/day
- 300 mg three times daily, OR
- 450 mg twice daily
**Titration**: May increase to 1200-1800 mg/day after 4 weeks if needed
**Duration**: At least 4-6 weeks for initial assessment; 6 months if effective

### Nervous Exhaustion / Mental Fatigue (Traditional Use)
**Preparation**: Tea or tincture
**Tea Dose**: 1-2 cups daily (1-2 teaspoons dried herb per cup)
**Tincture Dose**: 2-4 mL, 2-3 times daily
**Duration**: 2-4 weeks

### Topical Use - Wounds, Burns, Muscle Pain
**Preparation**: St. John's Wort oil (Oleum Hyperici)
**Application**: Apply to affected area 2-3 times daily
**Duration**: Until healed or symptoms resolve
**Caution**: Avoid sun exposure after application (photosensitivity risk)

---

## Special Populations - Dose Adjustments

### Geriatric Patients
**Dose**: Standard dose (900 mg/day) generally well-tolerated
**Consideration**: Increased risk of polypharmacy - screen for drug interactions
**Monitoring**: More frequent follow-up recommended

### Hepatic Impairment
**Dose**: No established guidelines; use with caution
**Monitoring**: Monitor for adverse effects

### Renal Impairment
**Dose**: No dose adjustment required
**Safety**: Generally considered safe

## Preparation Selection Guide

### For Clinical Depression (Evidence-Based)
**Best Choice**: Standardized extract (WS 5570, LI 160, Ze 117)
- 900 mg/day, standardized to 0.3% hypericin, 2-4% hyperforin
- Supported by multiple RCTs and meta-analyses

### For Drug Interaction Concerns
**Best Choice**: Low-hyperforin standardized extract (WS 5572)
- 900 mg/day, ≤1 mg/day hyperforin
- Maintains efficacy with reduced interaction risk

### For Mild Symptoms / Traditional Use
**Options**:
- Tea: 1-3 cups daily (traditional, less concentrated)
- Tincture: 2-4 mL, 2-3 times daily (more concentrated than tea)

### For Topical Use (Wounds, Pain)
**Best Choice**: St. John's Wort oil (Oleum Hyperici)
- Apply 2-3 times daily to affected area
- Avoid sun exposure after application

---

## Quality Considerations

### Selecting Standardized Extracts
1. **Standardization**: Verify hypericin content (0.3%) and hyperforin content (2-4% for standard, <1% for low-hyperforin)
2. **Manufacturer Reputation**: Choose reputable manufacturers (Schwabe, Lichtwer Pharma, Max Zeller)
3. **Third-Party Testing**: Look for USP, ConsumerLab, or NSF certification
4. **Clinical Evidence**: Prefer extracts used in clinical trials (WS 5570, LI 160, Ze 117)

### Selecting Traditional Preparations
1. **Source**: Organic, wildcrafted, or sustainably cultivated Hypericum perforatum
2. **Harvest Time**: Flowering tops harvested at peak bloom (June-July)
3. **Storage**: Properly dried and stored in cool, dark, dry conditions
4. **Color**: St. John's Wort oil should be deep red (indicates hypericin extraction)

### Avoiding Adulteration
- **Botanical Identity**: Ensure Hypericum perforatum (not other Hypericum species)
- **Purity Testing**: Third-party testing for heavy metals, pesticides, microbial contamination
- **Expiration Date**: Check expiration date; active constituents degrade over time

---

## Storage and Stability

### Standardized Extracts (Tablets/Capsules)
**Storage**: Cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight
**Temperature**: Room temperature (15-25°C / 59-77°F)
**Humidity**: Low humidity (protect from moisture)
**Shelf Life**: 2-3 years (check expiration date)

### Tinctures
**Storage**: Dark glass bottles, cool location
**Temperature**: Room temperature or refrigerator
**Shelf Life**: 2-5 years (alcohol preserves)

### Dried Herb
**Storage**: Airtight container, cool, dark, dry location
**Shelf Life**: 1-2 years (quality degrades over time)
**Color**: Should retain green color; brown indicates oxidation

### St. John's Wort Oil
**Storage**: Dark glass bottles (amber or cobalt blue), cool location
**Refrigeration**: Recommended for extended shelf life
**Shelf Life**: 1-2 years
**Color**: Deep red (hypericin); color fades with oxidation

---

## Administration Tips

### Standardized Extracts
- **Consistency**: Take at same time each day for optimal steady-state levels
- **With Food**: May reduce GI side effects
- **Timing**: Morning and/or evening doses; avoid late evening if causes restlessness

### Tinctures
- **Dilution**: Dilute in small amount of water if too strong
- **Sublingual**: For faster absorption, hold under tongue for 30-60 seconds before swallowing
- **Taste**: Bitter; may mix with juice if needed

### Tea
- **Covering**: Always cover while steeping to retain volatile compounds
- **Temperature**: Use boiling water (100°C / 212°F)
- **Timing**: Between meals or with light meals

### Topical Oil
- **Cleansing**: Clean affected area before application
- **Amount**: Small amount massaged into skin
- **Frequency**: 2-3 times daily
- **Sun Avoidance**: Wait at least 4-6 hours before sun exposure, or cover treated area

## Clinical Monitoring

### Initial Assessment (Before Starting)
1. Depression severity (HAM-D or equivalent scale)
2. Medication review (screen for drug interactions)
3. Medical history (rule out contraindications)
4. Sun exposure habits (photosensitivity risk assessment)

### Follow-Up Schedule
- **Week 2-4**: Check for early adverse effects, reinforce compliance
- **Week 4-6**: Re-assess depression severity, evaluate response
- **Month 3**: If continuing, assess for long-term adverse effects
- **Month 6**: Re-evaluate need for continued treatment

### Monitoring Parameters
1. **Efficacy**: Depression severity scores (HAM-D, PHQ-9, etc.)
2. **Safety**: GI symptoms, dizziness, skin reactions
3. **Drug Interactions**: Monitor drug levels if on narrow therapeutic index medications
4. **Compliance**: Assess adherence to dosing schedule
