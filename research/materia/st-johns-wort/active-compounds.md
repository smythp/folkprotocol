# St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) - Active Compounds & Bioavailability

## Primary Active Constituents

### Hypericin
**Chemical Class**: Naphthodianthrone

#### Standardization
- **Typical Content**: 0.1-0.3% in standardized extracts
- **Marker Compound**: Primary standardization marker for St. John's Wort preparations
- **Color**: Red pigment (gives St. John's Wort oil its characteristic deep red color)

#### Pharmacokinetic Data (Schulz 2005, PMID: 15727160)
**Single Dose Study**: 612 mg dry extract in 36 healthy male volunteers

- **AUC** (Area Under Curve): 75.96 h × ng/ml
- **Cmax** (Maximum Plasma Concentration): 3.14 ng/ml
- **Tmax** (Time to Maximum Concentration): 8.1 hours
- **Half-life (t½)**: 23.76 hours
- **Bioavailability**: Low, approximately 15-20%

**Multiple Dose Regimen**: Once-daily dosing for 14 consecutive days
- Steady-state levels achieved with daily dosing
- Longer elimination half-life supports once-daily dosing

#### Mechanism of Action
- **Photosensitization**: Absorbs light energy, may contribute to antidepressant effects through modulation of melatonin
- **Protein Kinase C Inhibition**: Inhibits protein kinase C enzyme
- **Antiviral Activity**: Documented antiviral effects
- **Antidepressant Effects**: Contributes to overall therapeutic activity (mechanism not fully elucidated)

### Pseudohypericin
**Chemical Class**: Naphthodianthrone (related to hypericin)

#### Pharmacokinetic Data (Schulz 2005, PMID: 15727160)
**Single Dose Study**: 612 mg dry extract

- **AUC**: 93.03 h × ng/ml
- **Cmax**: 8.50 ng/ml (higher than hypericin)
- **Tmax**: 3.0 hours (faster absorption than hypericin)
- **Half-life (t½)**: 25.39 hours
- **Bioavailability**: Low, approximately 15-20%

#### Clinical Significance
- Often co-standardized with hypericin
- Similar pharmacological properties to hypericin
- Contributes to red pigmentation of St. John's Wort oil

### Hyperforin
**Chemical Class**: Phloroglucinol derivative

#### Standardization
- **High-Hyperforin Extracts**: 2-4% hyperforin content
- **Low-Hyperforin Extracts**: <1% hyperforin (typically ≤1 mg/day)
- **Role**: Major active constituent for antidepressant activity

#### Pharmacokinetic Data (Schulz 2005, PMID: 15727160)
**Single Dose Study**: 612 mg dry extract

- **AUC**: 1009.0 h × ng/ml (much higher than hypericin compounds)
- **Cmax**: 83.5 ng/ml (highest plasma concentration of all constituents)
- **Tmax**: 4.4 hours
- **Half-life (t½)**: 19.64 hours (single dose)
- **Half-life (repeat dose)**: 9 hours
- **Steady-State Plasma Level**: ~100 ng/mL at 900 mg/day dosing
- **Bioavailability**: Low, approximately 15-20%

#### Mechanism of Action - Monoamine Reuptake Inhibition
Hyperforin is the **primary active constituent** responsible for antidepressant effects:

- **Mechanism**: Non-competitive reuptake inhibition through elevation of intracellular sodium
- **Neurotransmitters Affected**:
  - Serotonin (5-HT)
  - Norepinephrine (NE)
  - Dopamine (DA)
  - GABA
  - Glutamate
- **Broad Spectrum**: Unlike SSRIs (which target only serotonin), hyperforin inhibits reuptake of multiple neurotransmitters

#### Drug Interaction Risk (Nicolussi 2020, PMID: 31742659)
**CYP3A4 Induction**: Hyperforin is a potent inducer of cytochrome P450 3A4 enzyme

- **Mechanism**: Activates pregnane X receptor (PXR), leading to induction of CYP3A4 and P-glycoprotein
- **Threshold for Drug Interactions**:
  - **Low-Hyperforin (≤1 mg/day)**: No clinically relevant pharmacokinetic interactions observed
  - **High-Hyperforin (>1 mg/day)**: Significant drug interactions possible

**Affected Medications**:
- Immunosuppressants: cyclosporine, tacrolimus
- Cardiovascular: digoxin, warfarin
- HIV medications: indinavir
- Oral contraceptives
- Benzodiazepines: alprazolam
- Statins: simvastatin

**Clinical Significance**:
- Acute rejection episodes documented in two heart transplant patients due to reduced cyclosporine levels
- Low-hyperforin preparations (≤1 mg/day) developed to minimize interactions while maintaining efficacy

### Flavonoids

#### Quercetin
**Chemical Class**: Flavonol

- **Role**: Antioxidant properties
- **Contribution**: Contributes to overall therapeutic effect
- **Standardization**: Present in standardized extracts, though not typically used as primary marker

**Pharmacokinetic Data (Schulz 2005)**: Measured in bioavailability study, contributes to antioxidant activity

#### Isorhamnetin
**Chemical Class**: Flavonoid glycoside

- **Role**: Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties
- **Contribution**: Synergistic effects with other constituents

### Other Active Constituents

#### Xanthones
- **Examples**: 1,3,6,7-tetrahydroxyxanthone
- **Activity**: Antioxidant and neuroprotective properties

#### Procyanidins
- **Chemical Class**: Oligomeric proanthocyanidins
- **Activity**: Antioxidant effects

#### Essential Oil
- **Components**: Contains various volatile compounds
- **Activity**: May contribute to overall therapeutic activity

## Bioavailability Summary

### Schulz 2005 Study Design (PMID: 15727160)
- **Study Type**: Phase I Clinical Trial
- **Sample Size**: 36 healthy male volunteers (18 per trial)
- **Dosing**:
  - Single dose: 612 mg dry extract
  - Multiple dose: Once-daily for 14 consecutive days

### Key Bioavailability Findings

#### General Observations
- **Oral Bioavailability**: Low for all major constituents (15-20%)
- **Absorption**: Variable absorption rates (Tmax ranges from 3.0 to 8.1 hours)
- **Plasma Concentrations**: Hyperforin achieves highest plasma levels (~83.5 ng/ml Cmax)
- **Steady-State**: All constituents reach steady-state with daily dosing

#### Clinical Implications
1. **Low Bioavailability**: Despite low bioavailability (15-20%), clinical efficacy is well-established
2. **Multiple Dosing**: Daily administration ensures steady-state plasma levels
3. **Hyperforin Concentration**: Higher plasma concentrations suggest hyperforin is the primary active constituent
4. **Elimination Half-Lives**: Long half-lives (19-25 hours) support once-daily or twice-daily dosing

## Standardization Requirements

### Clinical Extracts

#### High-Hyperforin Formulations
- **Hypericin**: 0.1-0.3%
- **Hyperforin**: 2-4%
- **Clinical Dose**: 900 mg/day (typical)
- **Indication**: Mild-to-moderate depression
- **Caution**: Risk of drug interactions via CYP3A4 induction

#### Low-Hyperforin Formulations
- **Hypericin**: 0.1-0.3%
- **Hyperforin**: ≤1 mg/day total (typically <1%)
- **Clinical Dose**: 900 mg/day (typical)
- **Advantage**: Minimizes drug interactions while maintaining antidepressant efficacy
- **Example**: WS 5572 extract

### Extract Types Used in Clinical Trials

#### WS 5570 (Most Extensively Studied)
- **Type**: Hydroalcoholic extract with defined specifications
- **Standardization**: Hypericin, hyperforin, and flavonoids standardized
- **Studies**: Kasper 2006 (332 patients), Lecrubier 2002 (375 patients)
- **Manufacturer**: Dr. Willmar Schwabe Pharmaceuticals
- **Source**: Hypericum perforatum aerial parts (flowering tops)

#### WS 5572 (Low-Hyperforin)
- **Type**: Low-hyperforin standardized extract
- **Purpose**: Developed to reduce CYP3A4 induction and minimize drug interactions
- **Clinical Significance**: Maintains antidepressant efficacy with improved safety profile
- **Manufacturer**: Dr. Willmar Schwabe Pharmaceuticals

#### LI 160
- **Standardization**: 0.3% hypericin and defined hyperforin content
- **Manufacturer**: Lichtwer Pharma
- **Historical Significance**: One of the earliest extensively studied standardized extracts

#### Ze 117
- **Clinical Dose**: 500 mg daily
- **Long-Term Safety**: Demonstrated safe for up to 1 year continuous use (Brattström 2009, 440 patients)
- **Manufacturer**: Max Zeller Söhne AG

## Mechanisms of Action Summary

### Primary Antidepressant Mechanism
**Hyperforin-Mediated Monoamine Reuptake Inhibition**:
- Non-competitive inhibition of serotonin, norepinephrine, dopamine, GABA, and glutamate reuptake
- Mechanism involves elevation of intracellular sodium
- Broad-spectrum effect (unlike selective SSRIs)

### Neuroendocrine Effects
- **HPA Axis Modulation**: Affects hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis
- **Cortisol Regulation**: May normalize cortisol response to stress

### Receptor Interactions
- **Sigma Receptors**: Agonist activity at sigma receptors
- **GABA Receptors**: GABAergic effects contribute to anxiolytic properties

### Additional Mechanisms
- **Anti-Inflammatory**: Inhibition of pro-inflammatory cytokines
- **Neuroprotection**: Antioxidant properties (flavonoids, xanthones)
- **Protein Kinase C Inhibition**: Hypericin inhibits PKC enzyme

### Importance of Standardization
1. **Consistent Clinical Effects**: Ensures reproducible therapeutic outcomes
2. **Active Constituent Levels**: Guarantees minimum levels of hypericin and hyperforin
3. **Drug Interaction Risk**: Allows selection of low-hyperforin formulations when needed
4. **Batch-to-Batch Consistency**: Maintains quality across different production batches

## Clinical Recommendations

### Extract Selection
- **General Use**: Standard high-hyperforin extract (2-4% hyperforin) for depression
- **Drug Interaction Concerns**: Low-hyperforin extract (≤1 mg/day) when patient takes:
  - Immunosuppressants
  - Anticoagulants
  - Oral contraceptives
  - Narrow therapeutic index medications

### Dosing Based on Bioavailability
- **Standard Dose**: 900 mg/day standardized extract
- **Rationale**: Compensates for low bioavailability (15-20%)
- **Administration**: Once daily or divided doses (300 mg three times daily)
- **Steady-State**: Achieved after 4-6 days of regular dosing

### Monitoring
- **Onset**: 2-4 weeks for antidepressant effects
- **Duration**: At least 4-6 weeks to assess response
- **Drug Interactions**: Monitor patients on medications metabolized by CYP3A4
- **Photosensitivity**: Advise caution with sun exposure (hypericin-related)
