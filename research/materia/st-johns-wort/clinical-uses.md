# St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) - Clinical Uses

## Depression: Mild-to-Moderate

### Evidence Summary
St. John's Wort demonstrates significant efficacy for mild-to-moderate depression with evidence comparable to SSRIs and superior tolerability.

### Clinical Trial Results

#### Kasper 2006 (WS 5570 Extract)
- **Sample Size**: 332 participants randomized
- **Design**: 6-week double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter trial
- **Doses Tested**:
  - 600 mg once daily (n=123)
  - 1200 mg in two divided doses (n=127)
  - Placebo (n=82)
- **Primary Outcome**: Hamilton Rating Scale for Depression (HAM-D, 17-item)
- **Results**:
  - HAM-D decrease with 600mg: 11.6 ± 6.4 points
  - HAM-D decrease with 1200mg: 10.8 ± 7.3 points
  - HAM-D decrease with placebo: 6.0 ± 8.1 points
  - Both treatment groups statistically superior to placebo
  - Dose-dependent response pattern observed
- **PMID**: 16796730

#### Lecrubier 2002 (WS 5570 Extract)
- **Sample Size**: 375 participants (186 treatment, 189 placebo)
- **Design**: 6-week double-blind, placebo-controlled trial
- **Dose**: 300 mg three times daily (900 mg/day total)
- **Results**:
  - Significantly greater reduction in HAM-D total score vs placebo
  - Significantly more patients achieved treatment response or remission
  - Well-tolerated with safe profile
- **PMID**: 12153829

### Meta-Analyses

#### Ng 2017 - Comparison to SSRIs
- **Trials**: 27 clinical trials
- **Sample Size**: 3,808 patients total
- **Findings**:
  - Response rate: pooled RR 0.983 (95% CI 0.924-1.042) - no significant difference from SSRIs
  - Remission rate: pooled RR 1.013 (95% CI 0.892-1.134) - comparable to SSRIs
  - **Discontinuation rate: pooled OR 0.587 (95% CI 0.478-0.697) - significantly lower than SSRIs**
  - HAM-D score improvement: pooled SMD -0.068 (95% CI -0.127 to 0.021)
- **Conclusion**: Comparable efficacy to SSRIs with notably better retention rates
- **PMID**: 28064110

#### Zhao 2023 - Efficacy vs SSRIs
- **Trials**: 14 randomized clinical trials (2000-2022)
- **Sample Size**: 2,270 participants
- **Finding**: Pooled OR of 2.44 (95% CI 1.33-4.45) favoring St. John's wort
- **Outcome**: Reduced number of depressive patients and HAMD scores
- **Safety**: Fewer risks and side effects than conventional SSRIs
- **Heterogeneity**: I² = 77%, p = 0.004
- **PMID**: 36226689

#### Rahimi 2009 - Tolerability Comparison
- **Trials**: 13 placebo-controlled trials comparing St. John's wort to SSRIs
- **Findings**:
  - Clinical response: non-significant RR of 0.99 (comparable efficacy)
  - **Adverse events causing withdrawal: significant RR of 0.53 (53% fewer dropouts than SSRIs)**
  - Superior tolerability profile
- **PMID**: 19028540

#### Kim 1999 - Efficacy vs Placebo and TCAs
- **Finding**: St. John's wort was **1.5 times more likely** to result in an antidepressant response than placebo
- **Comparison to TCAs**:
  - Equivalent efficacy for mild to moderate depression in short-term use
  - TCA group showed higher dropout rates
  - TCAs were nearly twice as likely to cause side effects
- **PMID**: 10496508

### Linde 2005 - Depression Severity Matters
- **Trials**: 37 double-blind RCTs
- **Key Finding**: Effectiveness varies by depression severity and trial design
  - Larger, recent trials restricted to major depression showed only minor effects over placebo
  - Older and smaller trials not restricted to major depression showed marked effects
  - Compared to standard antidepressants, Hypericum extracts had similar effects
- **Interpretation**: Most effective for mild-to-moderate depression, less effective for severe/major depression
- **PMID**: 15684231

## Depression: Severe/Major

### Shelton 2001 - Negative Trial in Major Depression
- **Sample Size**: 200 adult outpatients
- **Population**: 67% female, mean age 42.4 years, 85.9% white
- **Design**: 8 weeks following 1-week placebo run-in
- **Dose**: 900 mg/day for 4 weeks, increased to 1200 mg/day if inadequate response
- **Results**:
  - St. John's wort was not effective for treatment of major depression
  - Remission rates: 14.3% (St. John's wort) vs 4.9% (placebo) - both remained low
  - No significant improvement over placebo in this severely depressed population
- **Adverse Effect**: Headache more frequent with St. John's wort (41%) than placebo (25%)
- **Interpretation**: Limited efficacy in severe cases
- **PMID**: 11308434

## Standard Dosing Protocols

### Clinical Dose Range
- **Standard Dose**: 900 mg/day of standardized extract (0.3% hypericin, 2-4% hyperforin)
- **Administration**:
  - 300 mg three times daily, OR
  - 600 mg once or twice daily
- **Titration**: May increase to 1200-1800 mg/day if inadequate response after 4 weeks

### Duration of Treatment
- **Initial Trial**: 4-6 weeks to assess response
- **Onset of Action**: 2-4 weeks (similar to synthetic antidepressants)
- **Continuation**: If effective, continue for at least 6 months to prevent relapse
- **Long-term Use**: Safe for up to 1 year based on clinical trials

### Standardized Extracts Used in Trials
- **WS 5570**: Most extensively studied (Kasper 2006, Lecrubier 2002)
- **WS 5572**: Low-hyperforin formulation (reduces drug interactions)
- **LI 160**: Early standardized extract
- **Ze 117**: Long-term safety demonstrated (Brattström 2009)

## Long-Term Safety

### Brattström 2009 - 1-Year Safety Study
- **Sample Size**: 440 outpatients with mild to moderate depression
- **Duration**: Up to 1 year
- **Extract**: Ze 117, 500 mg daily
- **Findings**:
  - 217 patients (49%) reported 504 adverse events
  - 30 adverse events (6%) possibly or probably related to treatment
  - Most common: gastrointestinal and skin complaints
  - No changes in clinical chemistry and ECG recordings
  - Body mass index remained stable
- **Conclusion**: Safe and effective for long-term treatment (up to 1 year)
- **PMID**: 19299116

## Summary: Clinical Efficacy

### Mild-to-Moderate Depression
- **Effectiveness**: Significantly more effective than placebo
- **Comparison to SSRIs**: Comparable efficacy with better tolerability and lower dropout rates
- **Comparison to TCAs**: Equivalent efficacy with fewer side effects and lower dropout rates
- **Evidence Quality**: Multiple meta-analyses, systematic reviews, and large-scale RCTs
- **Optimal Dose**: 900-1200 mg/day of standardized extract

### Major/Severe Depression
- **Effectiveness**: Mixed evidence - larger trials show only minor effects over placebo
- **Interpretation**: May be less effective in severe/major depression compared to mild-to-moderate cases

### Response Rates
- **vs Placebo**: 1.5-1.98 times more likely to achieve antidepressant response
- **vs SSRIs**: Comparable response and remission rates (RR ~0.98-1.01)
- **Dropout Rates**: Significantly lower than SSRIs (OR 0.53-0.59)

## Regulatory Approval

### German Commission E (1984)
- **Approved Use**: Depression
- **Dose**: 900 mg standardized extract per day
- **External Use**: St. John's wort oil for acute injuries, myalgia, first-degree burns

### EMA/HMPC (2018)
- **Well-Established Use**: Treatment of mild to moderate depressive episodes
- **Traditional Use**: Relief of temporary mental exhaustion

### WHO (2002)
- **Recognized Uses**: Antibacterial, antiviral, antidepressant, protein kinase-C inhibitor, wound-healing effects
