# Clinical Uses of Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis L.)

## Cognitive Function and Memory

### Elderly Population - Memory Enhancement

**Study**: Pengelly et al. 2012 (RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover)
- **Sample**: 28 older adults, mean age 75 years
- **Formulation**: Dried rosemary leaf powder
- **Optimal Dose**: 750 mg single dose
- **Primary Outcome**: Speed of memory
- **Effect Size**: Statistically significant improvement vs placebo (p=0.01)
- **Duration**: Single dose assessment
- **Safety Note**: High dose of 6000 mg showed significant impairing effects on speed of memory (p<0.01) and other cognitive measures
- **Clinical Recommendation**: Lower doses approximating normal culinary consumption (750 mg) show promise for memory enhancement in elderly

### University Students - Comprehensive Cognitive Support

**Study**: Nematolahi et al. 2018 (RCT, double-blind)
- **Sample**: 68 university students
- **Formulation**: Rosemary capsules
- **Dosing**: 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg/day total)
- **Duration**: 1 month
- **Outcomes**:
  - **Memory**: Significant improvement in both prospective and retrospective memory
  - **Anxiety**: Significantly reduced anxiety scores vs control
  - **Depression**: Significantly reduced depression scores vs control
  - **Sleep Quality**: Significantly improved overall sleep quality (all subscales except sleep latency and duration)

### Working-Age Men - Mental Energy and Mood

**Study**: Araki et al. 2020 (RCT post-hoc analysis)
- **Sample**: 21 working-age Japanese men with elevated baseline mood disturbance scores (8 rosemary, 13 control)
- **Formulation**: Rosemary extracts
- **Duration**: 4 weeks continuous intake
- **Between-Group Improvements**:
  - Total mood disturbance scores significantly decreased (p < 0.05)
  - Confusion-Bewilderment decreased (p < 0.05)
  - Vigor-Activity increased (p < 0.01)
- **Within-Group Rosemary Improvements**:
  - Tension-anxiety reduced (p < 0.05)
  - Vigor-activity improved (p < 0.05)
  - Fatigue on awakening reduced (p < 0.05)
  - Daytime sleepiness reduced (p < 0.05)
  - Psychomotor speed improved (p < 0.05)
- **Clinical Application**: Effective for improving mental energy and sleep quality in work-age men with poor mental health

### Meta-Analysis - Animal Studies Evidence

**Study**: Hussain et al. 2022 (Systematic review and meta-analysis, 23 studies, 35 comparisons)
- **Normal Animals Effect Size**: 1.19 (95% CI: 0.74-1.64)
- **Impaired Animals Effect Size**: 0.57 (95% CI: 0.19-0.96)
- **Robustness**: Positive effect across species, extract type, treatment duration, and memory type
- **Limitation**: Substantial unexplained heterogeneity among studies

## Depression and Anxiety

### Major Depressive Disorder - Adjunctive Treatment

**Study**: Azizi et al. 2022 (RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled)
- **Population**: Patients with major depressive disorder
- **Formulation**: Rosemary capsules (dried plant material)
- **Dosing**: 1 capsule twice daily (2 capsules/day total)
- **Rosmarinic Acid Content**: 21.13 ± 0.56 mg/g dried plant
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Assessment Points**: Baseline, week 4, week 8
- **Outcome Measures**:
  - Beck Depression Inventory—Second Edition
  - Hospital Anxiety and Depression Scale (anxiety subscale)
- **Results at 8 Weeks**:
  - Anxiety subscale scores significantly decreased vs control
  - Beck Depression Inventory scores significantly decreased vs control
  - Memory improvement observed during trial
- **Clinical Role**: Effective as adjunctive treatment for depression

### Stress and Anxiety - General Population

**Study**: Kuwata et al. 2025 (Two-part: open-label safety + RCT crossover)
- **Safety Study (Part 1)**:
  - Sample: 22 adults (21 completed)
  - Duration: 4 weeks
  - Dosing: 10 capsules daily containing 25 mg/day rosmarinic acid + 40 mg/day diterpenes (4 mg carnosic acid + carnosol per capsule)
  - Findings: No clinically relevant adverse events or abnormal laboratory findings

- **Efficacy Study (Part 2)**:
  - Sample: 40 randomized (38 analyzed)
  - Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover
  - Duration: 4 weeks continuous intake with 4-week washout
  - Dosing: 2 capsules daily containing 5 mg/day rosmarinic acid + 8 mg/day diterpenes (4 mg carnosic acid + carnosol per capsule)
  - **Outcomes**:
    - **Trait Anxiety**: Significant reduction in rosemary group vs placebo after 4 weeks (p=0.014)
    - **State Anxiety**: Significantly lower anxiety after single dose before work task (p=0.041)
    - **Heart Rate Variability**: Significant reduction in LF/HF ratio with rosemary vs placebo (p=0.019)
    - **Salivary Cortisol**: No significant differences
  - **Conclusion**: Rosemary extract safe and effective for reducing stress and anxiety markers

## Hair Growth and Androgenetic Alopecia

### Rosemary Oil vs Minoxidil 2%

**Study**: Panahi et al. 2015 (RCT, comparative trial)
- **Sample**: 100 participants (50 rosemary oil, 50 minoxidil 2%)
- **Formulation**: Rosemary essential oil (topical)
- **Duration**: 6 months
- **Assessment Points**: Baseline, 3 months, 6 months
- **Evaluation**: Standardized professional microphotographic assessment
- **Results**:
  - **Month 3**: No significant change in hair count in either group (p > 0.05)
  - **Month 6**: Both groups experienced significant increase in hair count vs baseline and 3-month (p < 0.05)
  - **Comparison**: No significant difference between rosemary oil and minoxidil groups at months 3 or 6
  - **Adverse Effects**: Scalp itching increased significantly in both groups by months 3 and 6 (p < 0.05), but MORE FREQUENT in minoxidil group
  - **Other Effects**: Dry hair, greasy hair, and dandruff showed no significant changes
- **Clinical Conclusion**: Rosemary oil as effective as minoxidil 2% for androgenetic alopecia with LESS scalp itching

### Rosemary-Lavender Oil (Rosmagain™) for Hair Health

**Study**: Patel et al. 2025 (RCT, double-blind, three-armed, placebo-controlled)
- **Sample**: 90 healthy participants aged 18-55 years, equally randomized to three arms
- **Formulation**: Rosmagain™ (rosemary-lavender oil formulation)
- **Duration**: 90 days
- **Trial Registration**: CTRI/2024/07/071115
- **Results** (all p<0.0001):
  - **Hair Growth Rate**: Increased from 0.22 ± 0.04 mm/day to 0.34 ± 0.05 mm/day (57.73% change from baseline)
  - **Hair Thickness**: 68.70% improvement
  - **Hair Density**: 32.21% increase
  - **Hair Length**: 28.78% increase
  - **Hair Fall**: Reduction exceeded 40%
- **Clinical Application**: Rosemary-lavender oil significantly effective for hair regrowth and scalp health

## Skin Health

### Facial Skin Quality - Internal Supplementation

**Study**: Draelos et al. 2025 (RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled)
- **Sample**: 104 women aged 40-65 years (52 rosemary extract, 52 placebo)
- **Formulation**: BioR rosemary extract dietary supplement
- **Dosing Schedule**:
  - Weeks 1-4: 2 capsules three times daily (6 capsules/day)
  - Weeks 5-8: 2 capsules twice daily (4 capsules/day)
  - Weeks 9-12: 1 capsule twice daily (2 capsules/day)
  - All capsules taken with food
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Primary Outcomes**: Visible facial skin quality parameters
- **Week 12 Improvements vs Placebo**:
  - Skin dullness: Significant improvement (p=0.04)
  - Roughness/texture: Significant improvement (p=0.001)
  - Erythema: Significant improvement (p=0.05)
  - Pore size: Significant improvement (p=0.04)
  - Global skin quality: Significant improvement (p=0.002)
  - Uneven pigmentation: No significant difference
- **Safety**: Well tolerated; one participant discontinued due to moderate GI upset possibly related to treatment
- **Clinical Application**: Effective for improving facial skin quality in middle-aged and older women

### Scalp Seborrheic Dermatitis - Topical Treatment

**Study**: Sadati et al. 2024 (RCT, double-blind)
- **Sample**: 42 patients with scalp seborrheic dermatitis (21 rosemary extract lotion, 21 ketoconazole 2% lotion)
- **Formulation**: Rosemary extract lotion (topical)
- **Dosing**: Both lotions applied twice daily
- **Duration**: 2 months
- **Assessment Points**: Baseline, 1 month, 2 months
- **Outcome Measures**: Flaking scores, itching severity, quality of life (DLQI)
- **Results**:
  - **Ketoconazole Superiority**: Better reduction in flaking scores (p=0.011)
  - **Rosemary Superiority**: Significantly greater itching reduction at 1 and 2 months (p<0.001)
  - **Quality of Life**: No significant difference between treatments at 1 and 2 months
- **Clinical Application**: Rosemary extract superior for itching relief; ketoconazole superior for flaking reduction

## Aromatherapy Applications

**Study**: Aćimović & Miljković 2025 (Systematic review, 39 references)
- **Formulation**: Rosemary essential oil
- **Documented Benefits**:
  - Cognitive function
  - Sleep quality
  - Mood enhancement
  - Pain management
- **Safety Considerations**:
  - Contact dermatitis from prolonged topical application
  - Hypersensitivity reactions
  - Rare anaphylaxis cases

## Digestive Health - Traditional Medicinal Use

**Source**: EMA/HMPC Community Herbal Monograph (2010)
- **Traditional Internal Uses**:
  - Dyspeptic complaints
  - Digestive support
  - Gastrointestinal comfort
- **Evidence Level**: Well-established traditional use

## Key Clinical Insights

1. **Dose-Response Relationship**: Lower doses (750 mg dried leaf) show cognitive benefits in elderly, while very high doses (6000 mg) impair cognitive function
2. **Standardization Matters**: Clinical trials used varied formulations; standardized extracts with known rosmarinic acid (5-25 mg/day) and diterpene content (8-40 mg/day) show reliable effects
3. **Duration for Effect**: Most cognitive and mental health benefits appear within 1-4 weeks; hair growth requires 6 months; skin quality improvements visible at 12 weeks
4. **Topical vs Internal**: Both routes effective; topical for dermatological conditions, internal for cognitive/mental health
5. **Safety Profile**: Generally safe at therapeutic doses; one GI upset case in 104-person trial; high doses (>4000 mg/kg/day in animal studies) show hepatorenal and reproductive toxicity
