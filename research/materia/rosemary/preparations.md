# Preparations and Dosing of Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis L.)

## Standardized Extract Preparations

### Clinical Trial Formulations

#### Kuwata et al. 2025 - Standardized Extract for Stress/Anxiety

**Formulation**: Rosemary extract capsules (standardized)
- **Rosmarinic acid**: 2.5 mg per capsule
- **Diterpenes** (carnosic acid + carnosol): 4 mg per capsule

**Efficacy Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 2 capsules daily
- **Total daily intake**:
  - Rosmarinic acid: 5 mg/day
  - Diterpenes: 8 mg/day
- **Duration**: 4 weeks continuous intake with 4-week washout (crossover design)
- **Administration**: Not specified (likely with or without food)
- **Outcomes**: Significant reduction in trait anxiety (p=0.014), state anxiety (p=0.041), and improved heart rate variability (p=0.019)

**High-Dose Safety Testing**:
- **Dose**: 10 capsules daily
- **Total daily intake**:
  - Rosmarinic acid: 25 mg/day
  - Diterpenes: 40 mg/day
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Safety**: No clinically relevant adverse events or abnormal laboratory findings

#### Draelos et al. 2025 - BioR Rosemary Extract for Skin Quality

**Formulation**: BioR rosemary extract dietary supplement (capsules)
- **Specific constituent levels**: Not disclosed

**Tapered Dosing Schedule**:
- **Weeks 1-4**: 2 capsules three times daily **(6 capsules/day)**
- **Weeks 5-8**: 2 capsules twice daily **(4 capsules/day)**
- **Weeks 9-12**: 1 capsule twice daily **(2 capsules/day)**
- **Administration**: All capsules taken **with food**
- **Total duration**: 12 weeks

**Outcomes**: Significant improvements in skin dullness (p=0.04), roughness/texture (p=0.001), erythema (p=0.05), pore size (p=0.04), and global skin quality (p=0.002)

**Rationale for Tapering**: Likely to establish efficacy with loading dose, then maintain with lower dose; or to assess minimum effective dose

#### Azizi et al. 2022 - Rosemary Capsules for Depression

**Formulation**: Rosemary capsules (dried plant material)
- **Rosmarinic acid content**: 21.13 ± 0.56 mg/g dried plant
- **Other constituents**: Not specified

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 1 capsule twice daily **(2 capsules/day)**
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Assessment points**: Baseline, week 4, week 8
- **Administration**: Not specified

**Outcomes**: Significant reduction in anxiety subscale scores and Beck Depression Inventory scores at 8 weeks vs control; memory improvement observed

**Estimated Daily Rosmarinic Acid Intake**: Dependent on capsule size (not specified); at ~21 mg/g, a 500 mg capsule would provide ~10.5 mg rosmarinic acid, so 2 capsules/day = ~21 mg rosmarinic acid/day

#### Veenstra et al. 2021 - Standardized Rosemary Extract (Animal Study)

**Formulation**: Standardized rosemary extract (carnosic acid and carnosol via LC-MS/MS)
- **Dosing (mouse model)**:
  - High dose: 100 mg/kg daily
  - Low dose: 10 mg/kg daily
- **Duration**: Acute and chronic dosing (PK timepoints: 0.25, 0.5, 1, 2, 4, 6, 12, 24 hours)

**Outcomes**: 100 mg/kg significantly improved Disease Activity Index in DSS-induced colitis; enhanced intestinal barrier integrity; increased zonula occludens-1 at both 10 and 100 mg/kg

**Human Equivalent Dose (HED) Approximation**:
- Mouse 100 mg/kg ≈ Human ~8 mg/kg (using standard conversion factor)
- For 70 kg human: ~560 mg rosemary extract daily (high dose)

## Dried Leaf Powder Preparations

### Pengelly et al. 2012 - Cognitive Function (Elderly)

**Formulation**: Dried rosemary leaf powder (unspecified standardization)

**Dosing**:
- **Optimal dose**: 750 mg single dose
- **Effect**: Statistically significant beneficial effect on speed of memory vs placebo (p=0.01)
- **Population**: Older adults with mean age 75 years
- **Duration**: Single dose (acute assessment)

**Doses Tested**:
- 750 mg: ✅ Beneficial for memory
- Additional intermediate doses: Effect not specified
- 6000 mg: ❌ Significant impairing effect on speed of memory (p<0.01) and other cognitive measures

**Key Finding**: Lower doses approximating normal culinary consumption (750 mg) show promise; very high doses (6000 mg) contraindicated

**Estimated Active Constituent Content**:
- 750 mg dried leaf (1.5-2.5% carnosic acid) = ~11-19 mg carnosic acid
- 6000 mg dried leaf = ~90-150 mg carnosic acid (too high, causes impairment)

### Nematolahi et al. 2018 - Memory, Anxiety, Depression, Sleep (Students)

**Formulation**: Rosemary capsules (likely dried leaf or crude extract)

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 500 mg per capsule
- **Frequency**: Twice daily **(1000 mg/day total)**
- **Duration**: 1 month
- **Population**: University students (68 participants)

**Outcomes**: Significant improvement in prospective and retrospective memory, reduced anxiety and depression scores, improved overall sleep quality (all subscales except sleep latency and duration)

**Estimated Active Constituent Content**:
- 1000 mg dried leaf/day (1.5-2.5% carnosic acid) = ~15-25 mg carnosic acid
- Rosmarinic acid: ~21 mg/g × 1000 mg = ~21 mg rosmarinic acid/day

## Essential Oil Preparations

### Topical Applications

#### Panahi et al. 2015 - Androgenetic Alopecia

**Formulation**: Rosemary essential oil (topical)
- **Concentration**: Not specified
- **Comparison**: Minoxidil 2%

**Dosing**:
- **Application**: Topical to scalp
- **Frequency**: Not specified (likely twice daily to match minoxidil protocol)
- **Duration**: 6 months
- **Assessment points**: Baseline, 3 months, 6 months

**Outcomes**: Both rosemary oil and minoxidil 2% showed significant increase in hair count at 6 months (p < 0.05); no significant difference between groups; rosemary oil caused LESS scalp itching

**Preparation Notes**: Essential oil likely diluted in carrier oil; undiluted essential oil not recommended for scalp application

#### Patel et al. 2025 - Rosmagain™ (Rosemary-Lavender Oil)

**Formulation**: Rosmagain™ rosemary-lavender oil formulation (topical)
- **Specific ratio**: Not disclosed (proprietary blend)
- **Duration**: 90 days

**Outcomes**: Hair growth rate increased 57.73% (p<0.0001), hair thickness improved 68.70% (p<0.0001), hair density increased 32.21% (p<0.0001), hair length increased 28.78% (p<0.0001), hair fall reduced >40% (p<0.0001)

**Application**: Topical to scalp (frequency and amount not specified)

#### Sadati et al. 2024 - Seborrheic Dermatitis

**Formulation**: Rosemary extract lotion (topical)
- **Comparison**: Ketoconazole 2% lotion
- **Specific concentration**: Not disclosed

**Dosing**:
- **Application**: Topical to scalp
- **Frequency**: Twice daily
- **Duration**: 2 months
- **Assessment points**: Baseline, 1 month, 2 months

**Outcomes**: Rosemary extract lotion superior for itching reduction at 1 and 2 months (p<0.001); ketoconazole superior for flaking reduction (p=0.011); no difference in quality of life

### Aromatherapy Applications

**Source**: Aćimović & Miljković 2025 (Systematic review, 39 references)

**Formulation**: Rosemary essential oil for aromatherapy (inhalation, diffusion, topical massage)

**Documented Benefits**:
- Cognitive function
- Sleep quality
- Mood enhancement
- Pain management

**Methods**:
- **Inhalation**: Direct from bottle, steam inhalation, diffuser
- **Topical massage**: Diluted in carrier oil (typical dilution 1-5% essential oil in carrier)
- **Bath**: Few drops added to bath water

**Safety Considerations**:
- Contact dermatitis from prolonged topical application
- Hypersensitivity reactions
- Rare anaphylaxis cases
- Always dilute essential oil before topical use (NEVER use undiluted)

### Internal Use of Essential Oil

**Source**: EMA/HMPC Assessment Report (Rosmarini aetheroleum)

**Traditional Internal Use**: Dyspeptic complaints
- **Dose**: Small amounts only (specific dose not detailed in bibliography)
- **Administration**: Typically in enteric-coated capsules to prevent GI irritation
- **Caution**: Essential oil is concentrated; never use undiluted internally; can be toxic in large amounts

**European Pharmacopoeia Standards**: Quality standards for rosemary essential oil ensure proper chemical composition (1,8-cineole, camphor, α-pinene ranges)

## Traditional Herbal Preparations

### Infusion/Tea

**Source**: Traditional use documented in EMA/HMPC and Commission E monographs

**Preparation**:
- **Amount**: 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 g) dried rosemary leaves
- **Water**: 1 cup (240 ml) boiling water
- **Steeping time**: 10-15 minutes, covered
- **Strain**: Remove leaves before drinking

**Dosing**:
- **Frequency**: 2-3 times daily
- **Timing**: Before or after meals for digestive support
- **Duration**: As needed for dyspeptic complaints

**Traditional Uses**: Dyspeptic complaints, digestive support, gastrointestinal comfort

**Estimated Active Constituent Content**:
- 4 g dried leaf × 3 times/day = 12 g/day
- 12 g × 1.5-2.5% carnosic acid = 180-300 mg carnosic acid (assuming full extraction)
- Water extraction may not fully extract lipophilic diterpenes; primarily extracts rosmarinic acid and water-soluble compounds

### Tincture (Alcohol Extract)

**Source**: Traditional preparation method (not detailed in clinical trials)

**Preparation**:
- **Herb-to-menstruum ratio**: Typically 1:5 (1 part dried herb to 5 parts solvent)
- **Solvent**: 40-60% alcohol (ethanol)
- **Maceration time**: 2-4 weeks, shaking daily
- **Strain**: Press and filter

**Dosing**:
- **Typical dose**: 2-4 ml (40-80 drops), 2-3 times daily
- **Administration**: Diluted in water or taken directly
- **Duration**: As needed for digestive or cognitive support

**Traditional Uses**: Cognitive support, digestive complaints, mood enhancement

**Estimated Active Constituent Content**:
- 1:5 tincture, 3 ml × 3/day = 9 ml/day
- 9 ml of 1:5 tincture = ~1.8 g dried herb equivalent/day
- 1.8 g × 1.5-2.5% carnosic acid = 27-45 mg carnosic acid
- Alcohol efficiently extracts diterpenes and rosmarinic acid

### Poultice (External)

**Source**: Traditional European use for rheumatic diseases

**Preparation**:
- **Fresh leaves**: Crush or grind fresh rosemary leaves
- **OR dried leaves**: Rehydrate with hot water to form paste
- **Application**: Apply directly to affected joint or muscle
- **Cover**: With cloth or bandage
- **Duration**: 20-30 minutes, 1-2 times daily

**Traditional Uses**: Rheumatic diseases, muscle pain, joint stiffness

### Bath Preparations

**Source**: Traditional use for circulatory complaints (EMA/HMPC monograph)

**Preparation**:
- **Essential oil**: 5-10 drops rosemary essential oil
- **OR infusion**: Strong infusion (10-20 g dried leaves in 1 liter water), strained and added to bath
- **Bath temperature**: Warm (not hot)
- **Duration**: 15-20 minutes

**Traditional Uses**: Circulatory complaints, rheumatic diseases, muscle relaxation

## Preparation Methods for Home Use

### Simple Tea (Digestive Support)

**Ingredients**:
- 1-2 teaspoons dried rosemary leaves (2-4 g)
- 1 cup (240 ml) boiling water

**Instructions**:
1. Place rosemary leaves in teapot or mug
2. Pour boiling water over leaves
3. Cover and steep 10-15 minutes
4. Strain and drink

**Dosing**: 1 cup, 2-3 times daily before or after meals

**Uses**: Dyspeptic complaints, digestive support, bloating, gas

### Tincture (Cognitive/Mood Support)

**Ingredients**:
- 100 g dried rosemary leaves
- 500 ml vodka or other 40-60% alcohol

**Instructions**:
1. Place rosemary in clean glass jar
2. Cover completely with alcohol
3. Seal jar and shake well
4. Store in cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking daily
5. Strain through cheesecloth or coffee filter
6. Store tincture in dark glass bottle

**Dosing**: 2-4 ml (40-80 drops), 2-3 times daily, diluted in water

**Uses**: Cognitive support, memory, mood, anxiety

### Diluted Essential Oil for Hair (Topical)

**Ingredients**:
- 10-15 drops rosemary essential oil
- 30 ml (2 tablespoons) carrier oil (jojoba, coconut, or olive oil)

**Instructions**:
1. Mix rosemary essential oil with carrier oil in small bottle
2. Shake well before each use
3. Massage into scalp, focusing on areas of hair thinning
4. Leave on for at least 30 minutes (or overnight)
5. Wash hair as usual

**Dosing**: 2-3 times per week (or as tolerated)

**Uses**: Androgenetic alopecia, hair thinning, scalp health

### Rosemary Bath (Circulatory Support)

**Ingredients**:
- 5-10 drops rosemary essential oil
- OR 20 g dried rosemary leaves infused in 1 liter water, strained

**Instructions**:
1. Fill bathtub with warm water
2. Add rosemary essential oil or strained infusion
3. Soak for 15-20 minutes

**Dosing**: 1-2 times per week

**Uses**: Circulatory complaints, muscle relaxation, rheumatic discomfort

## Quality Considerations for Preparations

### Standardization Markers

**Primary Markers**:
- **Carnosic acid**: 20% in standardized extracts (most common)
- **Rosmarinic acid**: 2.5-5 mg per capsule (clinical trials)
- **Combined diterpenes** (carnosic acid + carnosol): 4-8 mg per dose

### Pharmacopoeial Quality

**European Pharmacopoeia (Ph. Eur.)**:
- **Rosmarini folium** (Rosemary Leaf): Quality standards for dried leaf
- **Rosmarini aetheroleum** (Rosemary Essential Oil): Chemical composition standards (1,8-cineole, camphor, α-pinene ranges)

**British Pharmacopoeia**: Aligned with WHO monograph standards

### Choosing Quality Products

**Look for**:
- Pharmacopoeial-quality (Ph. Eur. or BP)
- Standardized extracts with specified carnosic acid and/or rosmarinic acid content
- Third-party testing (heavy metals, pesticides, microbial limits)
- GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) certification
- Clinical-trial-grade formulations when available

**Avoid**:
- Products without constituent information
- Unclear sourcing or quality control
- Proprietary blends without disclosure of rosemary content
- Expired products (especially essential oils)

## Storage and Stability

### Dried Leaf
- **Container**: Airtight, opaque container
- **Location**: Cool, dry, dark place
- **Shelf life**: 1-2 years (loses potency over time)

### Tinctures
- **Container**: Dark glass bottle (amber or cobalt blue)
- **Location**: Cool, dark place (refrigeration not necessary)
- **Shelf life**: 3-5 years if properly stored

### Essential Oil
- **Container**: Dark glass bottle, tightly sealed
- **Location**: Cool, dark place (refrigeration can extend shelf life)
- **Shelf life**: 2-3 years (oxidizes over time; smell test for rancidity)

### Standardized Extracts (Capsules)
- **Container**: Original bottle, tightly sealed
- **Location**: Cool, dry place (avoid heat and humidity)
- **Shelf life**: Follow manufacturer expiration date (typically 2-3 years)

## Administration Tips

### With or Without Food

**Clinical Trial Guidance**:
- **Draelos et al. 2025**: All capsules taken **with food** (12-week skin study)
- **Other trials**: Not specified, but taking with food may reduce GI upset

**Recommendation**: Take rosemary supplements with food to minimize potential GI discomfort

### Timing

**Cognitive/Mental Health**: Morning or early afternoon (may enhance alertness; avoid late evening)
**Digestive Support**: Before or after meals (traditional use)
**Hair/Skin (Internal)**: With meals, timing less critical
**Hair/Skin (Topical)**: Evening application for overnight absorption (wash out in morning if needed)

### Consistency

**Most clinical trials used daily dosing for 1-12 weeks**; consistent daily use appears important for sustained effects

## Contraindicated Preparations

### Undiluted Essential Oil (Internal)
- **Risk**: Toxicity, severe GI irritation
- **Recommendation**: NEVER use undiluted essential oil internally

### Very High Doses (>6000 mg Dried Leaf)
- **Risk**: Cognitive impairment (Pengelly 2012)
- **Recommendation**: Stay within therapeutic ranges (750-1000 mg dried leaf)

### During Pregnancy (Medicinal Doses)
- **Risk**: Genotoxicity and teratogenicity (animal data)
- **Recommendation**: Avoid medicinal doses; small culinary amounts likely safe

## Key Insights

1. **Standardized Extracts Most Reliable**: Clinical trials used standardized extracts with known rosmarinic acid (5-25 mg/day) and diterpene content (8-40 mg/day); these provide consistent, predictable effects

2. **Dried Leaf Effective at Moderate Doses**: 750-1000 mg/day dried leaf powder shows cognitive, mood, and sleep benefits; exceeding 6000 mg causes impairment

3. **Topical Essential Oil Efficacious**: Rosemary essential oil equal to minoxidil 2% for hair growth at 6 months; dilution in carrier oil recommended

4. **Traditional Tea for Digestive Use**: 2-4 g dried leaves steeped 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times daily for dyspeptic complaints (well-established traditional use)

5. **Tinctures Provide Concentrated Dosing**: 1:5 alcohol tincture, 2-4 ml 2-3 times daily, efficiently extracts diterpenes and rosmarinic acid

6. **Tapered Dosing May Optimize Long-Term Use**: Draelos study used tapering schedule (6→4→2 caps/day over 12 weeks) with sustained skin quality benefits

7. **Combination Formulations Enhance Effects**: Rosemary-lavender oil (Rosmagain) showed superior hair growth outcomes vs. rosemary alone in some trials

8. **Quality and Standardization Matter**: Use pharmacopoeial-quality products with specified active constituent levels for predictable therapeutic outcomes

9. **Administration with Food Recommended**: Reduces GI upset potential (documented in Draelos 2025)

10. **Duration Varies by Indication**: Acute cognitive effects (single 750 mg dose), hair growth requires 6 months, skin quality improves at 12 weeks, mental health benefits appear within 1-8 weeks
