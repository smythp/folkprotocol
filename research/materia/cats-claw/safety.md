# Cat's Claw - Safety Profile

## Overall Safety Assessment

**LiverTox Score**: E (unlikely cause of clinically apparent liver injury)
**Source**: LiverTox - Clinical and Research Information on Drug-Induced Liver Injury, National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases

### Key Safety Finding
- Cat's claw has **not been implicated in causing liver injury**
- **No reported cases of clinically apparent hepatotoxicity**
- No liver enzyme elevations documented across reviewed studies

## Clinical Trial Safety Data

### Hepatotoxicity Assessment

**Osteoarthritis Studies**:
- **Sample Size**: 45-107 patients
- **Finding**: No serious adverse events or changes in ALT/AST

**Rheumatoid Arthritis Trial** (Mur et al. 2002, PMID: 11950006):
- **Sample Size**: 40 patients
- **Dose**: 30 mg daily (pentacyclic alkaloid-chemotype)
- **Duration**: 52 weeks
- **Finding**: No laboratory changes, only minor side effects observed

**Osteoarthritis Trial** (Piscoya et al. 2001, PMID: 11603848):
- **Sample Size**: 45 patients
- **Finding**: No deleterious effects on blood or liver function vs placebo
- **Finding**: No significant side effects vs placebo

**Cancer Quality of Life Trial** (de Paula et al. 2015, PMID: 25495394):
- **Sample Size**: 51 patients
- **Dose**: 300 mg/day
- **Finding**: Well tolerated by most patients, no serious adverse events

**Breast Cancer Adjuvant Trial** (Araújo et al. 2012, PMID: 22811748):
- **Dose**: 300 mg daily
- **Duration**: 6 chemotherapy cycles
- **Finding**: Positive protective effects, reduced neutropenia

**Colorectal Cancer Trial** (Farias et al. 2012, PMID: 21869902):
- **Sample Size**: 43 patients
- **Dose**: 300 mg daily
- **Duration**: 6 cycles FOLFOX4
- **Finding**: No toxic effects observed

## Common Minor Side Effects

**Frequency**: Uncommon to rare in clinical trials

1. **Gastrointestinal**:
   - Nausea
   - Gastrointestinal upset
   - Diarrhea

2. **Other** (Uncommon):
   - Dyspepsia
   - Pruritus

## Contraindications

### Absolute Contraindications

1. **Pregnancy**
   - **Source**: LiverTox monograph, WHO guidelines
   - **Rationale**: Insufficient safety data

2. **Lactation**
   - **Source**: LiverTox monograph, WHO guidelines
   - **Rationale**: Insufficient safety data, potential transfer to breast milk

3. **Organ Transplant Recipients**
   - **Source**: LiverTox monograph
   - **Rationale**: Immunomodulatory effects may interfere with immunosuppressive therapy

### Relative Contraindications (Use with Caution)

4. **Anticoagulant Use**
   - **Source**: LiverTox monograph
   - **Rationale**: Potential interaction, theoretical bleeding risk
   - **Recommendation**: Use with caution, monitor closely

## Drug Interactions

### CYP 3A4 Inhibition

**Mechanism**: Cat's claw inhibits CYP 3A4 activity
**Source**: LiverTox monograph
**Clinical Concern**: Potential herb-drug interactions with medications metabolized via CYP 3A4 pathway

**Common CYP 3A4 Substrates** (Potential Interactions):
- Statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin)
- Calcium channel blockers (amlodipine, nifedipine)
- Benzodiazepines (alprazolam, midazolam)
- Immunosuppressants (cyclosporine, tacrolimus)
- Chemotherapy agents (various)
- Antiretrovirals
- Macrolide antibiotics

**Clinical Recommendation**:
- Monitor for increased drug effects/toxicity when combining cat's claw with CYP 3A4 substrates
- Consider dose adjustment or alternative therapy
- Particularly important for narrow therapeutic index drugs (e.g., immunosuppressants)

- **Status**: No toxic symptoms observed

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy and Lactation
- **Status**: Contraindicated
- **Data**: Insufficient safety data
- **Recommendation**: Avoid use

### Organ Transplant Recipients
- **Status**: Contraindicated
- **Rationale**: Immunomodulatory effects (IL-6 reduction SMD -0.72, NF-κB reduction SMD -1.19) may interfere with immunosuppressive therapy needed to prevent rejection
- **Recommendation**: Avoid use

### Cancer Patients on Chemotherapy
- **Breast Cancer Evidence** (Araújo et al. 2012):
  - **Dose**: 300 mg daily
  - **Regimen**: FAC chemotherapy
  - **Finding**: Reduced neutropenia, restored cellular DNA damage
  - **Safety**: Positive protective effects

- **Colorectal Cancer Evidence** (Farias et al. 2012):
  - **Dose**: 300 mg daily
  - **Regimen**: FOLFOX4
  - **Finding**: No benefit, but no toxic effects observed

- **Advanced Solid Tumors** (de Paula et al. 2015):
  - **Dose**: 300 mg/day
  - **Finding**: Improved quality of life, well tolerated

**Clinical Recommendation**:
- May be safe as adjuvant in cancer care at 300 mg/day
- Discuss with oncologist due to CYP 3A4 inhibition (potential chemotherapy drug interactions)
- Monitor for interactions with specific chemotherapy agents

### Autoimmune Conditions
- **Rheumatoid Arthritis**:
  - Evidence of safety and efficacy in 52-week trial (40 patients)
  - Used in combination with conventional DMARDs (sulfasalazine, hydroxychloroquine)

- **Other Autoimmune Conditions**:
  - Insufficient data
  - Theoretical concern: Immunomodulatory effects (both stimulatory and suppressive mechanisms observed)
  - **Recommendation**: Use with caution, monitor closely

## Toxicity Profile

### Acute Toxicity
- **Clinical Trials**: No serious adverse events at doses up to 350 mg/day for 6 weeks
- **Systematic Review Finding** (Arado et al. 2024): "Low toxicity" in animal studies

### Normal Cell Toxicity (In Vitro)
**Source**: Lopes et al. 2025 (PMID: 40438596) - Systematic review of cytotoxic effects

**General Finding**: Most extracts non-toxic to normal cell lines

**Exception**:
- Crude aqueous bark extract toxic to keratinocytes
- **IC50**: <10 μg/mL on HaCaT cells (human keratinocyte cell line)
- **Implication**: Standardized extracts preferred over crude aqueous bark extracts

### Hepatotoxicity
**LiverTox Assessment**:
- **Score**: E (unlikely cause of liver injury)
- **Reported Cases**: None
- **Clinical Trial Evidence**: No changes in ALT/AST across multiple trials
- **Conclusion**: Not associated with hepatotoxicity

## Comparative Safety: U. tomentosa vs U. guianensis

### Osteoarthritis Trial (Piscoya et al. 2001)
**Species Used**: Uncaria guianensis
- No deleterious effects on blood or liver function vs placebo
- No significant side effects vs placebo

### Rheumatoid Arthritis Trial (Mur et al. 2002)
**Species Used**: Uncaria tomentosa (pentacyclic alkaloid-chemotype)
- Only minor side effects observed
- No laboratory changes

**Conclusion**: Both species appear similarly safe in clinical use

## Dosing and Safety

### Clinical Trial Dose Range
- **Low Dose**: 30 mg/day (RA trial, 52 weeks, safe)
- **Medium Dose**: 100 mg 3x daily = 300 mg/day (cancer trials, safe)
- **High Dose**: 350 mg/day (6 weeks, no toxic symptoms)

### Traditional Dosing
- **1 gram root bark** 2-3 times daily (traditional use)
- **20-30 mg root bark extract** daily

### LiverTox Typical Dosing
- **30-300 mg extract once daily** (varies by preparation)

## Monitoring Recommendations

### No Routine Monitoring Required
- Based on clinical trial data, routine liver enzyme monitoring not necessary
- No documented cases of hepatotoxicity

### Consider Monitoring If:
1. **Using with CYP 3A4 substrates**:
   - Monitor for signs of drug toxicity
   - Consider therapeutic drug monitoring for narrow therapeutic index medications

2. **Using with anticoagulants**:
   - Monitor INR if on warfarin
   - Watch for signs of bleeding

3. **Organ transplant recipients**:
   - **Contraindicated** - avoid use due to immunomodulatory effects

4. **Long-term use (>6 months)**:
   - Insufficient long-term data
   - Consider periodic clinical assessment

## Summary

### Excellent Safety Profile
- **LiverTox Score E**: Unlikely cause of liver injury
- **No hepatotoxicity** in clinical trials
- **No serious adverse events** across multiple trials (total >200 patients)
- **Minor side effects only**: Occasional GI upset, nausea, diarrhea

### Key Contraindications
1. Pregnancy
2. Lactation
3. Organ transplant recipients

### Key Drug Interaction
- **CYP 3A4 inhibition**: Use caution with CYP 3A4 substrates
- **Particular concern**: Immunosuppressants, narrow therapeutic index drugs

### Duration-Tested Safety
- **Up to 52 weeks**: Safe in clinical trials
- **300 mg/day**: Safe for multiple chemotherapy cycles
- **Long-term (>6 months)**: Research gap, limited data

### Special Populations
- **Cancer patients**: Generally safe, may reduce chemotherapy-induced neutropenia
- **RA patients on DMARDs**: Safe in combination therapy (52-week trial)
- **Autoimmune conditions**: Use with caution, monitor closely
