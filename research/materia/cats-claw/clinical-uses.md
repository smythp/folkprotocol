# Cat's Claw - Clinical Uses

## Rheumatoid Arthritis

**Study**: Mur et al. 2002 (PMID: 11950006)
- **Sample Size**: 40 participants with active RA
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, two-phase trial
- **Duration**: 52 weeks total (24-week phase 1, 28-week phase 2)
- **Population**: Patients already receiving sulfasalazine or hydroxychloroquine
- **Dosing**: Pentacyclic alkaloid-chemotype extract of Uncaria tomentosa, 30-60 mg daily
- **Results**:
  - **Phase 1 (24 weeks)**: Painful joints reduced 53.2% vs 24.1% placebo (p = 0.044)
  - **Phase 2 (28 weeks)**:
    - Painful joints reduction (p = 0.003)
    - Swollen joints reduction (p = 0.007)
    - Ritchie Index reduction (p = 0.004)
- **Safety**: Only minor side effects observed
- **Conclusion**: Modest benefit to tender joint count in active RA patients on concurrent conventional therapy

## Osteoarthritis

**Study**: Piscoya et al. 2001 (PMID: 11603848)
- **Sample Size**: 45 participants (30 treatment, 15 placebo)
- **Design**: Randomized controlled trial, multicenter
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Intervention**: Freeze-dried Uncaria guianensis extract
- **Results**:
  - Pain associated with activity significantly reduced
  - **Benefits observed within first week of treatment**
  - Medical and patient assessment scores significantly reduced
  - Resting pain and nighttime pain NOT significantly reduced during 4-week trial
  - Knee circumference NOT significantly reduced
- **Safety**:
  - No deleterious effects on blood or liver function vs placebo
  - No significant side effects vs placebo
- **Additional Finding**: U. guianensis and U. tomentosa showed equivalent activity in DPPH radical quenching and TNF-alpha inhibition

## Cancer Care

### Advanced Solid Tumors (Quality of Life)

**Study**: de Paula et al. 2015 (PMID: 25495394)
- **Sample Size**: 51 participants
- **Design**: Phase II clinical trial
- **Demographics**: Median age 64 (range 33-85), 47% female
- **Dosing**: 100 mg dry extract three times daily (300 mg/day total)
- **Results**:
  - Overall quality of life improved (p = 0.0411) per EORTC QLQ C-30
  - Social functioning improved (p = 0.0341)
  - Fatigue reduced (p = 0.0496) per Chalder Fatigue Questionnaire
  - No tumor response per RECIST criteria
  - Disease stabilization >8 months in 4 participants
- **Safety**: Well tolerated by most patients, no serious adverse events

### Breast Cancer (Adjuvant to Chemotherapy)

**Study**: Araújo et al. 2012 (PMID: 22811748)
- **Population**: Patients with Invasive Ductal Carcinoma - Stage II
- **Dosing**: 300 mg dry Uncaria tomentosa extract daily
- **Chemotherapy**: FAC regimen (Fluorouracil, Doxorubicin, Cyclophosphamide)
- **Duration**: 6 chemotherapy cycles
- **Results**:
  - Reduced neutropenia caused by chemotherapy
  - Restored cellular DNA damage
- **Conclusion**: Positive protective effects against chemotherapy-induced hematologic toxicity

### Colorectal Cancer (Adjuvant to Chemotherapy)

**Study**: Farias et al. 2012 (PMID: 21869902)
- **Sample Size**: 43 participants
- **Dosing**: 300 mg daily
- **Chemotherapy**: FOLFOX4 (5-Fluorouracil/leucovorin + oxaliplatin)
- **Duration**: 6 cycles
- **Results**: **NEGATIVE TRIAL**
  - 300 mg daily Uncaria tomentosa during 6 cycles FOLFOX4 did not change analyzed parameters
  - No significant differences in hematological parameters, oxidative stress markers, antioxidant enzyme activity, or immunologic parameters between groups
- **Safety**: No toxic effects observed
- **Conclusion**: No benefit observed for colorectal cancer patients, but confirmed safety

## Anti-Inflammatory and Immunomodulatory Effects

**Systematic Review**: Arado et al. 2024 (PMID: 38881881)
- **Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of in vivo studies
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42023450869
- **Studies**: 24 included (from 523 screened)
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Scopus (search date: 2023-07-26)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Statistically significant reductions in **IL-6**: SMD -0.72 (p = 0.001)
  - Statistically significant reductions in **NF-κB**: SMD -1.19 (p = 0.001)
  - Non-significant changes for IL-1, IL-10, and TNF-α
  - Extracts of stem bark, roots, and leaves exhibited anti-inflammatory and/or immunomodulatory activities with **low toxicity**
- **Note**: First systematic review and meta-analysis of anti-inflammatory effects in animal models

## Anticancer Activity (In Vitro Evidence)

**Systematic Review**: Lopes et al. 2025 (PMID: 40438596)
- **Type**: Systematic review of cytotoxic effects
- **Studies**: 13 included
- **Databases**: PubMed, Embase, Scielo (search date: 2025-01)
- **Key Findings**:
  - **POA-rich extracts** showed highest anticancer potential, particularly against prostate cancer and leukemia
  - Crude aqueous bark extracts (72h incubation) effective against squamous cell carcinoma
  - TOA and PAC fractions showed limited cytotoxic effects
  - Most extracts non-toxic to normal cell lines
  - Crude aqueous bark extract toxic to keratinocytes (IC50 < 10 μg/mL on HaCaT cells)
  - Cytotoxic effects vary considerably depending on extraction method and chemical composition
- **Conclusion**: Highlights importance of extract standardization for anticancer research

## Antiviral Activity (In Vitro)

**Study**: de Lima et al. 2024 (PMID: 38543735)
- **Virus**: Chikungunya virus (Brazilian strain)
- **Cell Line**: Vero cells
- **Methods**: RT-qPCR, flow cytometry, plaque reduction assay, MTT cytotoxicity
- **Results**:
  - At 100 μg/mL: antiviral activity >90% via plaque reduction assay
  - Reduced viral cytopathic effect at non-cytotoxic dosage
  - Significant virucidal effects at 24h and 48h post-infection at 100 μg/mL
  - Dose-dependent inhibition of CHIKV infection
- **Note**: First report on antiviral activity of UT against CHIKV infection

## Evidence Quality

**Strongest Evidence**:
- Rheumatoid arthritis (modest benefit, RCT)
- Osteoarthritis (pain with activity, RCT)
- Quality of life in cancer patients (Phase II trial)
- Reducing chemotherapy-induced neutropenia in breast cancer (RCT)

**Insufficient Evidence**:
- Colorectal cancer chemotherapy side effects (negative RCT)
- Aromatase inhibitor-induced arthralgia

## Key Considerations

1. Extract standardization is critical - pentacyclic alkaloid (POA) chemotype preferred
2. Wide dosing range in studies (30-300 mg/day)
3. Benefits may appear within first week (osteoarthritis data)
4. Effects strongest for inflammatory conditions
5. Mixed results in cancer - QOL benefits but not tumor response
6. Mechanism may be independent of alkaloid content (comparative studies suggest polyphenols/other compounds contribute)
