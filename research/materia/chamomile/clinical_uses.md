# Chamomile - Clinical Uses

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

### First RCT (2009)
- **Study**: Amsterdam et al., 2009
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Sample**: 57 participants with mild-moderate GAD (28 chamomile, 29 placebo)
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Primary outcome**: Hamilton Anxiety Rating (HAM-A) scores
- **Results**: Significantly greater reduction in mean total HAM-A score (P = 0.047)
- **Safety**: 1 patient per group discontinued due to adverse events; no significant difference in AE frequency (P = 0.417)
- **Significance**: First controlled clinical trial of chamomile extract for GAD

### Open-Label Moderate-Severe GAD (2016)
- **Study**: Keefe et al., 2016
- **Sample**: 179 subjects with moderate-severe GAD
- **Dosage**: 1500 mg/day pharmaceutical-grade chamomile extract
- **Duration**: Up to 8 weeks
- **Results**:
  - Response rate: 58.1% met clinical response criteria
  - GAD-7 change: β=-8.4 (95% CI=-9.1 to -7.7) - significant improvement
  - Statistically significant and clinically meaningful reductions across HAM-A, Beck Anxiety Inventory, Psychological General Well Being Index
- **Safety**:
  - Treatment-emergent adverse events: 11.7% of subjects
  - Serious adverse events: None
  - Premature discontinuation: 15.6%
- **Conclusion**: Meaningful symptom reduction comparable to conventional anxiolytic medications with favorable safety profile

### Long-Term GAD Prevention (2016)
- **Study**: Mao et al., 2016
- **Design**: Two-phase RCT (open-label Phase 1, double-blind placebo-substitution Phase 2)
- **Sample**: 179 enrolled, 93 Phase 1 completers/responders randomized to Phase 2 (46 chamomile, 47 placebo)
- **Dosage**: 1500 mg daily (500 mg capsules, three times daily)
- **Duration**: Phase 1: 12 weeks, Phase 2: 26 weeks (38 weeks total)
- **Extract**: Pharmaceutical grade with HPLC fingerprinting confirming apigenin-7-glucoside and 7-methoxycoumarin
- **Primary outcome**: Time to symptom relapse (Cox proportional hazards)
- **Results**:
  - Relapse rate chamomile: 15.2% (7/46)
  - Relapse rate placebo: 25.5% (12/47)
  - Mean time to relapse chamomile: 11.4 ± 8.4 weeks
  - Mean time to relapse placebo: 6.3 ± 3.9 weeks
  - Hazard ratio: 0.52 (95% CI, 0.20-1.33; P=0.16) - non-significant
- **Safety**: Both treatments had similar low adverse event rates

### Meta-Analysis: Anxiety & Sleep (2019)
- **Study**: Truong Hong Hieu et al., 2019
- **Included**: 12 RCTs
- **Generalized Anxiety Disorder findings**:
  - HAM-A Week 2: MD = -1.43, P = 0.007
  - HAM-A Week 4: MD = -1.79, P = 0.0097
  - State anxiety: Three trials showed no significant difference
- **Safety**: Mild adverse events reported by only 3 RCTs; favorable safety profile

### Systematic Review: Anxiety (2024)
- **Study**: Saadatmand et al., 2024
- **Included**: 10 clinical trials
- **Results**: 9 of 10 studies concluded chamomile effective in reducing anxiety
- **Mechanism**: Active compounds like apigenin may modulate hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenocortical axis
- **Conclusion**: Chamomile potentially has anxiolytic effect; effective and less dangerous compared to pharmaceutical anxiety treatments

## Depression and Comorbid Conditions

### Comorbid Anxiety & Depression (2012)
- **Study**: Amsterdam et al., 2012
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Sample**: 57 total (19 comorbid depression, 16 past depression, 22 anxiety only)
- **Dosage**: 220 mg capsules of pharmaceutical-grade chamomile extract standardized to 1.2% apigenin
- **Results**:
  - HAM-D overall: Significant reduction vs placebo (P < .05)
  - HAM-D comorbid trend: Clinically meaningful but nonsignificant trend for greater reduction in comorbid depression group (P = .062)
  - HAM-D core mood: Significant improvement on core mood items vs placebo (P < .05)
- **Safety**: No adverse effects explicitly reported

## Sleep Disorders

### Chronic Primary Insomnia (2011)
- **Study**: Zick et al., 2011
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot trial
- **Sample**: 34 patients (ages 18-65) with DSM-IV primary insomnia
- **Dosage**: 270 mg chamomile extract twice daily (540 mg/day total)
- **Duration**: 28 days
- **Primary outcomes**: No significant differences in total sleep time, sleep efficiency, sleep latency, wake after sleep onset, sleep quality, number of awakenings
- **Secondary outcomes**: Modest advantage on daytime functioning (not statistically significant); small-to-moderate effect sizes; moderate favorable effects on sleep latency and fatigue severity
- **Safety**: No differences in adverse events between chamomile and placebo groups

### Elderly Sleep Quality (2017)
- **Study**: Adib-Hajbaghery & Mousavi, 2017
- **Design**: Single-blind randomized controlled trial
- **Sample**: 60 elderly participants (aged 60+)
- **Dosage**: 200 mg chamomile extract capsules twice daily (400 mg/day)
- **Duration**: 28 consecutive days
- **Measurement**: Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index at baseline, 2 weeks post-start, immediately post-intervention, 2 weeks post-completion
- **Results**: After intervention, sleep quality in treatment group significantly better than controls (P<0.05)
- **Safety**: Can be used as safe modality for promoting elderly sleep; no detailed adverse events reported

### Meta-Analysis: Sleep Effects (2019)
- **Study**: Truong Hong Hieu et al., 2019
- **Included**: 12 RCTs
- **Results**:
  - Sleep quality: Significant improvement, SMD -0.73 (95% CI [-1.23, -0.23], P < 0.005)
  - Insomnia: One trial found no change in insomnia severity
- **Safety**: Mild adverse events reported by only 3 RCTs; favorable safety profile

### Meta-Analysis: Sleep (2024)
- **Study**: Kazemi et al., 2024
- **Included**: 10 clinical trials, 772 participants
- **Results**:
  - PSQI score: Significant reduction, WMD: -1.88 (95% CI: -3.46, -0.31) across 5 studies
  - Sleep onset: Improved in 3 of 4 studies
  - Awakenings: Improved number of awakenings in 2 of 3 studies
  - No effect on: Sleep duration, sleep efficiency percentage, daytime functioning measures
- **Safety**: No adverse events reported in any studies

## Women's Health

### Menopausal Symptoms (2025)
- **Study**: Mohsenzadeh-Ledari et al., 2025
- **Design**: Triple-blind randomized controlled trial
- **Sample**: 80 postmenopausal women (ages 47-62)
- **Dosage**: 100 mg capsules containing 1.2% apigenin, 4 capsules daily (400 mg/day)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Results**:
  - Overall symptom reduction: -10.36 points (P < 0.001)
  - Vasomotor symptoms: -2.25 points (P < 0.001)
  - Psychological symptoms: -3.74 points (P < 0.001)
  - Locomotor symptoms: -2.70 points (P < 0.001)
  - Urological symptoms: -1.89 points (P < 0.001)
- **Safety**: Two participants experienced adverse effects (mouth sores, skin spots, itching) leading to discontinuation; use warrants caution due to associated side effects

### Cyclic Mastalgia (2018)
- **Study**: Saghafi et al., 2018
- **Design**: Double-blind randomized controlled trial
- **Sample**: 60 patients (30 chamomile, 30 placebo)
- **Dosage**: 5 drops liquid extract three times daily
- **Duration**: 2 consecutive months
- **Measurement**: Visual Analogue Scale (VAS) and Breast Pain Chart (BPC) at 8 weeks
- **Results**:
  - Chamomile decline: p<0.0001
  - Placebo decline: p=0.048
  - Between-group difference: p=0.007
- **Safety**: Well-tolerated, secure and effective; no adverse events detailed

### Premenstrual Syndrome (2014)
- **Study**: Sharifi et al., 2014
- **Design**: Randomized controlled trial, double-blind
- **Sample**: 90 students in dormitories (Iran)
- **Dosage**: 100 mg capsules, 3x daily (300 mg/day)
- **Comparison**: Mefenamic acid 250 mg, 3x daily
- **Duration**: Two cycles
- **Results**:
  - Emotional symptom reduction chamomile: 30.1 ± 26.6% and 33.4 ± 25.3% after two cycles
  - Emotional symptom reduction mefenamic acid: 11.6 ± 25.7% and 10.7 ± 26.8%
  - Emotional symptoms: p < 0.001
  - Physical symptoms: No significant difference (p > 0.05)
- **Safety**: No adverse effects reported

### Systematic Review: PMS (2019)
- **Study**: Bostani Khalesi et al., 2019
- **Included**: 8 RCTs
- **Conclusion**: Chamomile is effective for the treatment of PMS
- **Mechanisms**: Anti-inflammatory, anti-spasmodic, anti-anxiety effects

### Dysmenorrhea (Systematic Review, 2021)
- **Study**: Niazi & Moradi, 2021
- **Included**: 7 clinical trials, 1033 participants
- **Study breakdown**: 2 studies pain only, 2 studies bleeding only, 3 studies both
- **Conclusion**: Chamomile can be considered effective treatment for primary dysmenorrhea and reducing menstrual bleeding
- **Mechanism**: Ceases production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes

### Pediatric Enuresis (2017)
- **Study**: Sharifi et al., 2017
- **Design**: Double-blind randomized placebo-controlled trial, parallel design
- **Sample**: 80 children with monosymptomatic nocturnal or daytime enuresis
- **Dosage**: Topical chamomile oil application
- **Duration**: 6 weeks
- **Results**: Mean frequency of enuresis lower in intervention group at first (P < .001), second (P = .03), and third 2 weeks (P < .001)
- **Safety**: No report of any adverse event in study groups

## Other Conditions

### Hyperprolactinemia (2019)
- **Study**: Kabiri et al., 2019
- **Design**: Randomized controlled clinical trial
- **Sample**: 56 women with idiopathic hyperprolactinemia
- **Dosage**: 5 mL chamomile syrup twice daily
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Primary outcome**: Serum prolactin levels at baseline and end of 4 weeks
- **Results**: Prolactin reduction in chamomile group statistically significant (p <0.0001); cabergoline demonstrated greater reduction (p <0.0001 between groups)
- **Safety**: Adverse events recorded; no safety concerns highlighted in abstract

### Oral Mucositis (Systematic Review, 2022)
- **Study**: Dantas et al., 2022
- **Included**: 6 RCTs, 492 patients total
- **Results**: Topical chamomile effective in prevention/treatment of oral mucositis in 4 of 6 studies
- **Dosing**: Concentration range 1% to 2.5%, frequency single to 4 times daily, topical application

## Anti-Inflammatory Effects

### Meta-Analysis (2025)
- **Study**: Valmy et al., 2025
- **Included**: 11 RCTs
- **Results**:
  - Pain reduction: Statistically significant, pooled MD -0.61 (95% CI: -0.76 to -0.46)
  - Mucosal inflammation: Statistically significant reduction, pooled MD -0.70 (95% CI: -1.25 to -0.15)
  - Pain bleeding combined: Non-significant, pooled MD -1.57
  - Antimicrobial: No significant effect, pooled MD -3.90
- **Conclusion**: Low-risk antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory agent; warrants larger future studies

## Glycemic Effects

### Meta-Analysis (2024)
- **Study**: Akhgarjand et al., 2024
- **Included**: 4 human RCTs, 8 animal studies
- **Results (Humans)**:
  - Fasting blood glucose: Favorable effect, SMD -0.65 (95% CI: -1.00 to -0.29, p<0.001)
  - HbA1c: Favorable effect, SMD -0.90 (95% CI: -1.39 to -0.40, p<0.001)
- **Results (Animals)**:
  - Blood glucose: SMD -4.37 (95% CI: -5.76 to -2.98, p<0.001)
  - Dose response: Each 100 mg/d increase lowered glucose ~54.35 mg/dL (p<0.001)
- **Safety**: No adverse events reported

## Dosing Summary by Indication

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder
- **Typical dose**: 1500 mg/day
- **Formulation**: 500 mg capsules, 3x daily
- **Standardization**: Pharmaceutical-grade extract; 1.2% apigenin; HPLC fingerprinting
- **Duration**: 8-38 weeks in clinical trials

### Sleep Disorders
- **Range**: 270-400 mg/day
- **Elderly dose**: 200 mg twice daily (400 mg/day)
- **Insomnia dose**: 270 mg twice daily (540 mg/day)
- **Duration**: 28 days typical

### Menopausal Symptoms
- **Dose**: 400 mg/day
- **Formulation**: 100 mg capsules with 1.2% apigenin, 4x daily
- **Duration**: 12 weeks

### Premenstrual Syndrome
- **Dose**: 100 mg capsules, 3x daily (300 mg/day)
- **Duration**: 2 menstrual cycles

### Topical Applications

**Oral Mucositis**:
- Concentration: 1-2.5%
- Frequency: Single to 4x daily

**Enuresis**:
- Formulation: Chamomile oil, topical
- Duration: 6 weeks

**Mastalgia**:
- Formulation: 5 drops liquid extract, 3x daily
- Duration: 2 months
