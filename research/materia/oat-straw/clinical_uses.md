# Oat Straw - Clinical Uses

## Evidence Summary

Oat straw (Avena sativa L., herba — aerial parts) has the strongest clinical evidence base among nervine herbs for cognitive function. Seven RCTs (n=20–161) consistently demonstrate acute benefits on attention, working memory, executive function, and processing speed using standardized green oat herb extracts (Neuravena®, cognitaven®). Chronic supplementation at 4 weeks shows cognitive and stress-physiological benefits; at 12 weeks, cerebrovascular endpoints improve substantially (+42% cerebrovascular responsiveness, +41% brachial flow-mediated dilatation) while cognitive outcomes in cognitively normal older adults do not show significance. One systematic review (Jibril et al. 2023) covers 6 RCTs (n=287) and confirms the acute cognitive benefit; chronic evidence remains inconsistent.

---

## Systematic Review

### Jibril et al. 2023 — Systematic Review of RCTs (PMID 36657906)

**Jibril AT, Arero AG, Kankam SB, Fuseini M. "Effect of Avena sativa (Oats) on cognitive function: A systematic review of randomized controlled trials." Clin Nutr ESPEN. 2023;53:144–150.**

- **Design**: Systematic review of RCTs; 6 trials included (3 crossover, 3 parallel; 4 acute, 2 chronic)
- **Total participants**: 287 across all trials
- **Interventions**: Green oat herb extracts (Neuravena®, cognitaven®); doses 800–2500 mg
- **Acute outcomes**: Positive cognitive influence in 4 of 6 trials; improvements in attention, spatial memory, working memory, and executive function
- **Chronic outcomes**: Significant improvement in 1 study (shorter-term); no benefit at 12 weeks
- **Conclusion**: Acute supplementation may improve cognitive function in healthy volunteers; evidence for chronic supplementation is inconsistent; insufficient trial numbers for meta-analysis

## Cognitive Function — Acute Effects

### Kennedy et al. 2017 — Acute Dose-Comparison RCT (PMID 26618715)

**Kennedy DO, Jackson PA, Forster J, et al. "Acute effects of a wild green-oat (Avena sativa) extract on cognitive function in middle-aged adults." Nutritional Neuroscience. 2017;20(2):135–151.**

- **Extract**: Neuravena® (ELFA®955, Frutarom Switzerland Ltd.)
- **Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled, counterbalanced crossover RCT
- **Population**: Healthy middle-aged adults aged 40–65 reporting subjective memory decline
- **Doses**: 800 mg vs 1600 mg vs placebo (single dose each arm)
- **Duration**: Single dose per arm

**Outcomes:**
- **800 mg**: Significantly increased speed of performance on timed cognitive tasks (p<0.05); improved delayed word recall accuracy; enhanced executive function; increased working memory span
- **1600 mg**: Less consistent effects than 800 mg — inverse dose-response pattern above 800 mg
- **Conclusion**: 800 mg is the apparent optimal acute dose for this population; cognitive benefits are acute and extend to processing speed, recall, and executive function

---

### Martinez-Horta et al. 2021 — Neurophysiology RCT (PMID 34658781)

**Martinez-Horta S, Ivanir E, Perrinjaquet-Moccetti T, et al. "Effects of a Green Oat Herb Extract on Cognitive Performance and Neurophysiological Activity." Front Neuroscience. 2021;15:748188.**

- **Extract**: Neuravena® 800 mg
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; single acute dose
- **Participants**: 20 healthy adults
- **Duration**: Single dose

**Outcomes:**
- **ERP neurophysiology**: Significantly reduced N2 component (enhanced inhibitory control); reduced error-related negativity (ERN) — optimized neural error processing; modulated P3a and P3b components (attention and working memory networks)
- **Behavioral**: Faster reaction times with maintained accuracy; active group did not show fatigue-related performance decline over the session, while placebo group did
- **Conclusion**: 800 mg Neuravena® acutely optimizes neural processing resources, prevents fatigue-induced performance decline, and enhances executive function and processing speed

---

### Berry et al. 2011 — Stroop Test RCT (PMID 21711204)

**Berry NM, Robinson MJ, Bryan J, et al. "Acute effects of an Avena sativa herb extract on responses to the Stroop Color-Word test." J Altern Complement Med. 2011;17(7):635–637.**

- **Extract**: Neuravena® (oat herb extract)
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover
- **Population**: Elderly volunteers with below-average cognitive performance
- **Doses**: 1600 mg vs 2400 mg vs placebo (3 arms)
- **Duration**: Single dose per arm; weekly washout

**Outcomes:**
- **1600 mg**: Significantly fewer errors during Stroop color-naming task vs placebo and 2400 mg: F(1,36)=18.85, p<0.001; significantly improved Stroop interference scores in cognitively impaired subgroup: F(1,34)=2.40, p<0.01
- **2400 mg**: No significant improvement vs placebo on primary outcomes — inverse dose-response confirmed above 1600 mg
- **Blood pressure**: No effect at either active dose
- **Conclusion**: 1600 mg improves attention and concentration acutely in older adults with below-average cognition; hormetic dose-response observed (1600 mg > 2400 mg)

---

### Dimpfel et al. 2011 — EEG Neurophysiology (PMID 21563962)

**Dimpfel W, Storni C, Verbruggen M. "Ingested oat herb extract (Avena sativa) changes EEG spectral frequencies in healthy subjects." J Altern Complement Med. 2011;17(5):427–434.**

- **Extract**: Neuravena® (oat herb extract)
- **Design**: Double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled crossover
- **Doses**: 1250 mg vs 2500 mg vs placebo
- **Method**: CATEEM® brain mapping (quantitative EEG)

**Outcomes:**
- **Resting EEG**: Significant lowering of spectral delta power (p<0.01) vs placebo — shift from slow-wave to alert-state brain activity
- **Concentration task**: Significant enhancement of spectral theta power in left frontotemporal region (p<0.05) — region implicated in cognitive processing
- **Mental arithmetic**: Enhanced theta power (trend p=0.115)
- **Conclusion**: Neurophysiological changes consistent with improved cognitive readiness; neuroimaging corroboration of behavioral findings from other RCTs

## Cognitive Function — Chronic Effects

### Kennedy et al. 2020 — Dose-Ranging Chronic RCT (PMID 32485993)

**Kennedy DO, Bonnländer B, Lang SC, et al. "Acute and Chronic Effects of Green Oat (Avena sativa) Extract on Cognitive Function and Mood during a Laboratory Stressor in Healthy Adults." Nutrients. 2020;12(6):1598.**

- **Extract**: cognitaven® (green oat extract)
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-groups, dose-ranging
- **Participants**: 132 healthy adults aged 35–65
- **Doses**: 430 mg/day, 860 mg/day, 1290 mg/day vs placebo
- **Duration**: 29 days (acute assessment Day 1; chronic assessment Day 29)

**Outcomes:**
- **Acute (Day 1)**: All three active doses significantly improved Corsi Blocks working memory (p<0.05 vs placebo) and multitasking performance
- **Chronic (Day 29)**:
  - 430 mg and 1290 mg: significantly improved working memory and multitasking vs placebo
  - 1290 mg: decreased electrodermal activity (physiological stress marker) after 4 weeks
  - 860 mg: intermediate; did not reach significance on all endpoints
- **Conclusion**: Dose-dependent cognitive benefits persist with 4-week supplementation; 1290 mg most effective; physiological stress modulation confirmed at highest dose
- **Note**: Largest dose-ranging oat straw extract RCT identified; supports both acute and 4-week chronic use

---

### Wong et al. 2012 — 12-Week Chronic RCT (PMID 22690320)

**Wong RHX, Howe PRC, Bryan J, et al. "Chronic effects of a wild green oat extract supplementation on cognitive performance in older adults." Nutrients. 2012;4(5):331–342.**

- **Extract**: Wild green oat extract (WGOE, Neuravena®)
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover
- **Participants**: 37 cognitively normal healthy older adults (mean age 67±0.8 years)
- **Dose**: 1500 mg/day
- **Duration**: 12 weeks per arm with washout
- **Battery**: Stroop colour-word, letter cancellation, rule-shift, computerised multi-tasking, trail-making

**Outcomes:**
- No significant improvement on any cognitive measure vs placebo at 12 weeks
- **Conclusion**: Acute cognitive benefits do not persist with 12-week supplementation in cognitively normal older adults; diverges from Kennedy et al. 2020 (benefit at 4 weeks), suggesting benefit may diminish or plateau between 4 and 12 weeks in this population

## Cerebrovascular and Vascular Outcomes

### Wong et al. 2013 — Cerebrovascular RCT (PMID 23221935)

**Wong RHX, Howe PRC, Coates AM, et al. "Chronic consumption of a wild green oat extract (Neuravena) improves brachial flow-mediated dilatation and cerebrovascular responsiveness in older adults." J Hypertension. 2013;31(1):192–200.**

- **Extract**: Wild green oat extract (Neuravena®)
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover (companion study to Wong et al. 2012)
- **Participants**: 37 healthy older adults over age 60
- **Dose**: 1500 mg/day
- **Duration**: 12 weeks per arm

**Outcomes:**
- **Cerebrovascular responsiveness (CVR)**: +42% vs placebo (p<0.01) — clinically meaningful improvement
- **Brachial flow-mediated dilatation (FMD)**: +41% vs placebo (p<0.01)
- **Resting blood pressure**: No significant change
- **CVR and FMD improvements were uncorrelated**, suggesting independent cerebral and systemic vascular mechanisms
- **Conclusion**: 1500 mg/day chronic supplementation produces large, significant improvements in vascular endothelial function in both systemic and cerebrovascular circulation; clinically relevant to dementia risk (reduced CVR is associated with cognitive decline)

## Stress, Quality of Life, and Sleep

### Friling et al. 2024 — Smoking Cessation RCT (PMID 38962436)

**Friling M, García-Muñoz AM, Lavie A, et al. "Dietary supplementation with a wild green oat extract (Avena sativa L.) to improve wellness and wellbeing during smoking reduction or cessation." Front Nutr. 2024;11:1405156.**

- **Extract**: Neuravena® (wild green oat extract)
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Participants**: 161 healthy smokers (74M, 87F; mean age 29.6 years); GOE n=79, placebo n=82
- **Dose**: 900 mg/day
- **Comparator**: 519 mg maltodextrin
- **Duration**: 8 weeks + 4-week follow-up

**Outcomes:**
- **Quality of life — Physical health domain**: GOE +4.2 points vs placebo −1.2 points (p=0.006)
- **Quality of life — Psychological domain**: GOE +3.9 points vs placebo −1.2 points (p=0.008)
- **Smoking reduction**: Successful cessation/reduction (>20% reduction): GOE 66.7% vs placebo 49.3% (p=0.034)
- **Sleep (WASO)**: Wake after sleep onset: GOE improved −0.7 min vs placebo worsened +5.4 min (p=0.047)
- **Perceived stress**: Significantly greater decline in GOE group vs placebo (p<0.05)
- **Conclusion**: 900 mg/day significantly improves quality of life, stress, and sleep during smoking reduction; supports cessation outcomes

## Clinical Dosing Summary

### Acute Single-Dose Evidence

| Dose | Studies | Key Outcomes |
|------|---------|--------------|
| 800 mg | Kennedy 2017 (PMID 26618715); Martinez-Horta 2021 (PMID 34658781) | Working memory, executive function, processing speed, ERP optimization; appears to be near-optimal acute dose |
| 1250–1600 mg | Berry 2011 (PMID 21711204); Dimpfel 2011 (PMID 21563962) | Stroop attention/concentration; EEG theta enhancement; 1600 mg > 2400 mg (inverse dose-response) |
| 2400–2500 mg | Berry 2011; Dimpfel 2011 | Less effective than 1600 mg; optimal window appears below 2000 mg for single doses |

### Chronic Daily-Dose Evidence

| Dose | Duration | Study | Cognitive Outcome | Vascular Outcome |
|------|----------|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 430 mg/day | 29 days | Kennedy 2020 (PMID 32485993) | Significant working memory and multitasking improvement | — |
| 860 mg/day | 29 days | Kennedy 2020 | Intermediate; not significant on all endpoints | — |
| 900 mg/day | 8 weeks | Friling 2024 (PMID 38962436) | Quality of life, stress, sleep (non-cognitive measures) | — |
| 1290 mg/day | 29 days | Kennedy 2020 | Strongest cognitive benefit + physiological stress reduction | — |
| 1500 mg/day | 12 weeks | Wong 2012 (PMID 22690320) | No cognitive benefit (cognitively normal older adults) | +42% CVR, +41% FMD (both p<0.01) |

### Optimal Dose Assessment

Based on the RCT literature: **800–1290 mg/day** for cognitive and mood endpoints. Acute optimal dose ~800 mg (Neuravena® standardized extract). An inverse dose-response at very high single doses (>1600 mg) has been observed in two studies. Cerebrovascular benefits confirmed at 1500 mg/day at 12 weeks. No dose-finding study for sleep or anxiolytic indications published.

## Effect Size Summary

### Cognitive Function (Acute)
- **Working memory (Corsi Blocks)**: Significant improvement vs placebo at 430–1290 mg (p<0.05), all dose groups, Day 1 (Kennedy 2020, n=132)
- **Stroop attention**: F(1,36)=18.85, p<0.001 at 1600 mg vs placebo (Berry 2011)
- **ERP neural efficiency**: Significant reduction in N2 and ERN amplitudes at 800 mg (Martinez-Horta 2021, n=20)
- **EEG theta power**: Significant enhancement in left frontotemporal region (p<0.05) at 1250 mg (Dimpfel 2011)

### Cerebrovascular (Chronic — 12 weeks, 1500 mg/day, n=37)
- **Cerebrovascular responsiveness**: +42% vs placebo (p<0.01)
- **Brachial FMD**: +41% vs placebo (p<0.01)

### Stress/QoL (Chronic — 8 weeks, 900 mg/day, n=161)
- **QoL physical**: +4.2 points vs −1.2 placebo (p=0.006)
- **Smoking cessation**: 66.7% vs 49.3% (p=0.034); ~17.4 percentage point improvement

---

## Research Gaps

- No RCTs specifically targeting sleep or anxiety (EMA traditional-use indications) using oat straw extract
- Long-term (>12 weeks) cognitive RCTs with dose-optimized protocols lacking
- No head-to-head comparison of Neuravena® vs cognitaven® preparations
- No RCTs in populations with anxiety disorders, insomnia, or cognitive impairment (most enroll healthy adults)
- Mechanistic studies confirming MAO-B and PDE-4 inhibition in vivo at clinical doses are absent
