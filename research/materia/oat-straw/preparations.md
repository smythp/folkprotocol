# Oat Straw - Preparations

## Source Standards

Primary clinical evidence: RCTs using **Neuravena®** (ELFA®955, Frutarom Switzerland Ltd., now IFF) and **cognitaven®** (Gencor) — standardized green oat herb extracts

Traditional dosing authority: **British Herbal Pharmacopoeia (BHP)**; ESCOP monograph traditions

Regulatory standards: **EMA HMPC** Community Herbal Monograph on Avenae herba (EMEA/HMPC/202966/2007)

---

## Standardized Extracts (Clinical Evidence Base)

### Neuravena® (ELFA®955)

- **Manufacturer**: Frutarom Switzerland Ltd. (now IFF)
- **Also known as**: Wild green oat extract (WGOE); ELFA®955
- **Source material**: Green oat herb (Avenae herba, aerial parts harvested before flowering)
- **Standardization**: Flavonoids (vitexin, isovitexin) and triterpene saponins (avenacosides A and B)
- **Clinical trial doses used**:
  - 800 mg: Kennedy 2017, Martinez-Horta 2021
  - 900 mg/day: Friling 2024 (8 weeks)
  - 1250 mg, 1500 mg, 1600 mg, 2400 mg: Berry 2011; Wong 2012/2013
- **Optimal acute dose from evidence**: 800 mg (appears near-optimal; 1600 mg effective but 2400 mg less so — inverse dose-response)
- **Optimal chronic dose from evidence**: 1290 mg/day at 4 weeks (Kennedy 2020); 1500 mg/day at 12 weeks for cerebrovascular endpoints

### cognitaven®

- **Manufacturer**: Gencor / licensed ingredient
- **Standardization**: Green oat extract (exact marker percentages not published in accessible literature)
- **Clinical trial doses used**: 430 mg/day, 860 mg/day, 1290 mg/day (Kennedy 2020, 29-day trial)

## Standardized Extract — Recommended Clinical Dosing

Based on available RCT evidence:

### Acute / On-Demand Use (Cognitive Focus, Mental Performance)

| Dose | Evidence Base | Use Case |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **800 mg** | Kennedy 2017 (n≈30 crossover); Martinez-Horta 2021 (n=20) | Near-optimal single acute dose for cognitive performance |
| 1250–1600 mg | Berry 2011 (n≈36); Dimpfel 2011 | Effective for attention/EEG; 1600 mg shows Stroop improvement (F=18.85, p<0.001) |
| > 1600 mg (single dose) | Berry 2011 | Less effective than 1600 mg; hormetic inverse dose-response; not recommended |

### Chronic Daily Use

| Dose | Duration | Evidence Base | Primary Outcome |
|------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **900 mg/day** | 8 weeks | Friling 2024 (n=161) | QoL, stress, sleep improvement |
| **1290 mg/day** | 29 days | Kennedy 2020 (n=132) | Working memory, multitasking, stress physiology — strongest cognitive chronic response |
| **1500 mg/day** | 12 weeks | Wong 2012/2013 (n=37) | Cerebrovascular responsiveness +42%, brachial FMD +41%; no cognitive benefit in normal older adults |
| 430 mg/day | 29 days | Kennedy 2020 | Significant working memory improvement; lowest effective chronic dose identified |

**Suggested clinical dose range**: 800–1290 mg/day for cognitive and mood outcomes; 1500 mg/day for cerebrovascular indications.

## Traditional Preparations

### Infusion (Tea)

- **Part used**: Dried aerial parts (Avenae herba) — comminuted
- **Dose**: 1–3 g dried herb per cup
- **Preparation**: Steep in boiling water 5–10 minutes; strain
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Total daily dose**: 3–9 g dried herb
- **Source**: BHP; ESCOP traditions; EMA HMPC covers this preparation
- **Traditional indication**: Mental stress, nervous fatigue, insomnia

### Tincture (1:5, 25% Ethanol)

- **Dose**: 3–5 mL per dose
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Total daily dose**: 9–15 mL
- **Notes**: 25% ethanol is standard for herba extracts; higher ethanol may over-extract lipophilic constituents; lower may under-extract flavonoids
- **Traditional indication**: Nervine tonic, stress, sleep support

### Fluid Extract (1:1)

- **Dose**: 3–5 mL per dose
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Total daily dose**: 9–15 mL
- **Notes**: More concentrated than 1:5 tincture; adjust dose accordingly; ensure consistent liquid equivalent
- **Traditional indication**: Nervine tonic, exhaustion

### Milky Oats (fresh plant tincture — North American tradition)

- **Part used**: Avena sativa immature grain at "milky stage" — harvested when contents of grain are milky white
- **Preparation**: Fresh plant tincture (1:2 or 1:3, 60% ethanol) made immediately after harvest to preserve milky latex
- **Dose**: 2–4 mL
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Traditional indication**: Deep nervous system restoration, chronic exhaustion, burn-out, recovery from nervous system depletion
- **Note**: Distinct preparation from dried oat straw; no standardized extract form; no clinical RCT data; recognized in North American herbal tradition (Michael Moore, David Hoffman, others)

## Topical Preparations (Commission E Approved)

### Oat Straw Bath (Stramentum avenae)

- **Commission E approval**: Inflammatory and seborrhoeic skin diseases, especially with itching
- **Preparation**: Steep 100 g dried oat straw in 2 L boiling water for 20–30 minutes; strain and add to full bath
- **Frequency**: As needed; typically 1–2× daily for acute flares
- **Safety**: Well tolerated; no side effects documented by Commission E

## Standardization Considerations

### Marker Compounds for Quality Assessment

| Compound | Class | Role in Quality |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| Vitexin | Flavone C-glycoside | Primary identity/potency marker (Neuravena®) |
| Isovitexin | Flavone C-glycoside | Primary identity/potency marker (Neuravena®) |
| Avenacoside A | Triterpenoid saponin | Secondary marker (Neuravena®) |
| Avenacoside B | Triterpenoid saponin | Secondary marker (Neuravena®) |

**Quality gap**: Neither Neuravena® nor cognitaven® disclose specific minimum percentage content of marker compounds in the accessible peer-reviewed literature. Products labeled "green oat extract" without specification of standardization to vitexin/isovitexin/avenacosides may not be equivalent to the preparations used in RCTs.

### Formulation and Bioavailability

- **Liquid vs solid**: Avenanthramide bioavailability is higher from liquid preparations (Armeni et al. 2025, PMID 40926245); this may also apply to other oat bioactives — liquid extracts and fluid extracts may offer better bioavailability than hard capsules
- **Extraction solvent**: Water/ethanol extractions are used in clinical preparations; pure water infusions retain polar flavonoids and saponins; ethanol improves extraction of less-polar phenolics
- **Encapsulated extract**: All published RCTs used encapsulated standardized extract (Neuravena® capsules); direct comparison to traditional liquid preparations has not been studied

## Part Used

| Tradition | Part | Stage of Harvest |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| Clinical trials (Neuravena®, cognitaven®) | Aerial parts (Avenae herba) | Before/at early flowering |
| Traditional Western herbal | Aerial parts (Avenae herba) | Before or just after grain fill |
| North American (milky oats) | Immature grain/seed | Milky stage, before grain ripens |
| German Commission E (topical) | Dried stem/straw | Post-harvest straw |

The most clinically validated preparation is standardized herba extract (Neuravena® type) from aerial parts harvested before flowering, standardized to flavonoids and avenacosides.
