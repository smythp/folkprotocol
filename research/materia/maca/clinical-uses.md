# Maca (Lepidium meyenii) - Clinical Uses

## Sexual Health

### SSRI-Induced Sexual Dysfunction
**Study:** Dording et al. 2008 (double-blind RCT)
**Sample:** 20 participants (16 in ITT analysis), mean age 36±13 years, 17 women
**Dosing:**
- **Effective dose:** 3,000 mg/day gelatinized maca for 12 weeks
- **Ineffective dose:** 1,500 mg/day (no significant changes)
- **Administration:** Divided doses with meals

**Results (3,000 mg/day group):**
- ASEX score: 22.8±3.8 → 16.9±6.2 (z=-2.20, P=0.028)
- MGH-SFQ score: 24.1±1.9 → 17.0±5.7 (z=-2.39, P=0.017)
- Both doses improved libido (P<0.05)

**Clinical significance:** Minimum effective dose for SSRI sexual dysfunction is 3 g/day; 1.5 g/day insufficient.

### Sexual Desire (General Population)
**Study:** Gonzales et al. 2002 (double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT)
**Sample:** Adult healthy men
**Dosing:**
- Group 1: 1,500 mg/day gelatinized maca
- Group 2: 3,000 mg/day gelatinized maca
- **Duration:** 12 weeks

**Results:**
- Sexual desire improvements observed starting at **week 8**
- Effect independent of testosterone (P=NS for serum testosterone vs placebo)
- Effect independent of estradiol levels (P=NS)
- Effect independent of depression and anxiety scores

**Mechanism:** Non-hormonal pathway; does NOT affect testosterone or estrogen

### Erectile Dysfunction (Mild)
**Study:** Zenico et al. 2009 (double-blind RCT)
**Sample:** 50 Caucasian men with mild ED
**Dosing:** 2,400 mg/day maca dry extract for 12 weeks

**Results:**
- IIEF-5 score increase: +1.6±1.1 (maca) vs +0.5±0.6 (placebo), P<0.001
- Psychological well-being: +9±6 (maca) vs +6±5 (placebo), P<0.05
- Physical performance: +7±6 (maca, P<0.05) vs no significant change (placebo)
- Social performance: +7±6 (maca, P<0.05) vs no significant change (placebo)

**Clinical significance:** 2,400 mg/day produces measurable improvements in erectile function and overall well-being.

### Late-Onset Hypogonadism (LOH)
**Study:** Shin et al. 2023 (double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT)
**Sample:** 80 participants (41 maca, 39 placebo)
**Dosing:** 6,000 mg/day (1,000 mg capsules × 2 pills × 3 times daily, before meals) for 12 weeks

**Results:**
- AMS score: significantly improved (p<0.05)
- IIEF score: significantly improved (p<0.05)
- IPSS score: significantly improved (p<0.05)
- ADAM positive rate: significantly decreased (p<0.0001)
- No significant changes in laboratory tests or vital signs

**Safety:** Well tolerated with no adverse laboratory changes

### Sexual Dysfunction in Postmenopausal Women
**Study:** Brooks et al. 2008 (double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover trial)
**Sample:** 14 postmenopausal women
**Dosing:** 3,500 mg/day powdered maca for 6 weeks per phase (12 weeks total crossover)

**Results:**
- Greene Climacteric Scale: significant reduction in psychological symptoms (P<0.05)
- Anxiety subscale: improved vs baseline and placebo (P<0.05)
- Depression subscale: improved vs baseline and placebo (P<0.05)
- Sexual dysfunction measures: significantly reduced vs placebo (P<0.05)
- **No changes in serum estradiol, FSH, LH, or SHBG** between treatment and placebo

**Mechanism:** Effects NOT mediated by hormonal changes

## Menopausal Symptoms

### Perimenopausal Women
**Study:** Meissner et al. 2006a (double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover)
**Sample:** 18 women aged 41-50 years (completed)
**Dosing:** 2,000 mg/day pre-gelatinized maca (500 mg capsules × 2, twice daily with meals) for 4 months

**Results:**
- 74-87% of participants reported symptom alleviation (Kupperman's Index)
- Hot flushes: highly significant improvement (P<0.01)
- Excessive sweating: highly significant improvement (P<0.01)
- Interrupted sleep: highly significant improvement (P<0.01)
- FSH levels: significantly increased (P<0.01) after 2 months
- Systolic blood pressure: significantly reduced (P<0.05)
- Diastolic blood pressure: significantly reduced (P<0.05)
- Body weight: significantly decreased (P<0.05)
- HDL cholesterol: significantly increased (P<0.01)
- Serum iron: significantly increased (P<0.05)

### Early Postmenopausal Women (Hormone Balancing)
**Study:** Meissner et al. 2006b (double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover)
**Sample:** 34 early-postmenopausal Caucasian women aged 49-58 years (22 in primary crossover, 12 in bone density pilot)
**Dosing:** 2,000 mg/day pre-gelatinized organic maca (500 mg capsules × 2, twice daily with meals) for 4 months

**Hormonal Changes at 2 Months:**
- Estradiol (E2): significantly increased (P<0.05)
- FSH: suppressed (P<0.05)
- LH: reduced (P<0.05)
- Thyroid T3: suppressed (P<0.05)
- Cortisol: decreased (P<0.05)
- ACTH: suppressed (P<0.05)

**Clinical Outcomes:**
- BMI: significantly reduced (P<0.05)
- Menopausal symptoms: alleviated (P<0.001)
- Bone density markers: noticeably increased
- LDL cholesterol: increased (P<0.01)
- Iron: increased (P<0.01)
- Kupperman's Index: highly significant reductions
- Hot flushes and night sweating: relieved

**Clinical significance:** Pre-gelatinized maca modulates multiple hormone axes distinct from HRT.

## Physical Performance

**Meta-Analysis:** Ojeda et al. 2024 (21 studies systematic review, 16 in meta-analysis)

**Effect Sizes (Large):**
- Forced swimming test: 2.26
- Rota-rod test (coordination): 6.26
- Grip strength test: 5.23
- Blood lactate reduction: -1.70

**Key Findings:**
- Macamides increase physical performance
- Greater effect at higher doses (dose-dependent)
- Dose-response relationship exists

## Antioxidant Effects

**Meta-Analysis:** 2024 Antioxidants journal (11 studies systematic review, 8 in meta-analysis)

**Standardized Mean Differences (SMD):**
- Glutathione peroxidase: 0.96 (large effect)
- Reduced glutathione: 0.89 (small effect)
- Superoxide dismutase: 0.68 (moderate effect)
- Malondialdehyde: -0.53 (moderate reduction)

**Key Findings:**
- Higher doses generate greater antioxidant effect
- Macamides (phytochemical compounds) effectively control cellular oxidative stress

## Metabolic & Cardiovascular Effects

### Glucose Metabolism
**Study:** Gonzales-Arimborgo et al. 2016 (double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT)
**Sample:** 175 participants (94 low altitude, 99 high altitude)
**Dosing:** 3,000 mg/day spray-dried extracts (black or red maca) for 12 weeks

**Results:**
- **Black maca:** Reduced blood glucose levels
- **Red maca:** Better effects on mood, energy, chronic mountain sickness score
- Fatty acids and macamides: higher in black maca than red maca
- Both extracts: good acceptability, no serious adverse effects

### Mood & Energy
**Same study findings:**
- Improved mood, energy, and health status
- Reduced chronic mountain sickness (CMS) score
- Red maca superior for mood/energy effects

## Dosing Summary by Condition

| Condition | Effective Dose | Duration | Form |
|-----------|---------------|----------|------|
| SSRI sexual dysfunction | 3,000 mg/day | 12 weeks | Gelatinized powder |
| Sexual desire | 1,500-3,000 mg/day | 12 weeks (onset: 8 weeks) | Gelatinized powder |
| Mild erectile dysfunction | 2,400 mg/day | 12 weeks | Dry extract |
| Late-onset hypogonadism | 6,000 mg/day | 12 weeks | Capsules (before meals) |
| Postmenopausal sexual dysfunction | 3,500 mg/day | 6-12 weeks | Powdered maca |
| Perimenopausal symptoms | 2,000 mg/day | 4 months | Pre-gelatinized (with meals) |
| Early postmenopausal symptoms | 2,000 mg/day | 4 months | Pre-gelatinized organic |
| Physical performance | Higher doses preferred | Dose-dependent | Various |
| Antioxidant effects | Higher doses preferred | Dose-dependent | Various |
| Glucose metabolism | 3,000 mg/day | 12 weeks | Black maca spray-dried |

**General Guidelines:**
- Minimum effective dose: 1,500-3,000 mg/day
- Typical therapeutic range: 2,000-3,500 mg/day
- High dose: 6,000 mg/day
- Administration: Divided into 2-3 doses daily with meals
- Onset of effects: Typically 8 weeks
- Most studies: 12-week duration

## Evidence Quality Notes

**Strong Evidence (Multiple RCTs):**
- Menopausal symptoms (perimenopausal and postmenopausal)
- Sexual desire improvement
- Physical performance

**Moderate Evidence (Single/Small RCTs):**
- SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction
- Mild erectile dysfunction
- Late-onset hypogonadism symptoms
- Antioxidant effects

**Insufficient Evidence:**
- Fertility/semen quality (meta-analysis found no significant effect on sperm concentration: WMD 2.22, 95% CI -2.94 to 7.37, p=0.4)
- Testosterone enhancement (consistently no effect across studies)
- Bone density (preliminary positive signals, needs confirmation)

**Systematic Review Conclusions:**
- Shin 2010 (sexual function): Only 2 of 4 RCTs showed positive effects; evidence too limited for firm conclusions
- Lee 2022 (semen quality): Unclear effects due to insufficient evidence

## Key Mechanisms

1. **Non-hormonal sexual health effects:** Improvements in sexual function occur WITHOUT changes in testosterone or estrogen levels
2. **Hormone axis modulation in menopause:** May involve FSH, LH, cortisol, ACTH modulation (distinct from HRT)
3. **Dose-dependent effects:** Physical performance and antioxidant effects show clear dose-response relationships
4. **Macamides as primary bioactives:** Higher macamide content (black maca) correlates with specific effects (glucose, performance)
5. **Phenotype-specific effects:** Black maca (glucose), red maca (mood/energy), different therapeutic profiles
