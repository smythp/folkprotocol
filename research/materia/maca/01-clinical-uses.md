# Maca (Lepidium meyenii) - Clinical Uses

## SSRI-Induced Sexual Dysfunction

### Dording et al. 2008 (CNS Neuroscience & Therapeutics)
- **Study Design**: Double-blind, randomized, parallel group, pilot dose-finding study
- **Sample**: 20 enrolled, 16 in ITT analysis, 10 completed; Mean age 36±13 years; 17 women
- **Duration**: 12 weeks

**Dosing Protocols**:
- **Low dose**: 1.5 g/day maca
- **High dose**: 3.0 g/day maca

**Results**:
- **High-dose (3.0 g/day)** showed significant improvement:
  - ASEX score: 22.8±3.8 → 16.9±6.2 (z=-2.20, P=0.028)
  - MGH-SFQ score: 24.1±1.9 → 17.0±5.7 (z=-2.39, P=0.017)
- **Low-dose (1.5 g/day)**: No statistically significant changes
- **Both groups**: Libido improvements (P<0.05)

**Clinical Conclusion**: 3.0 g/day effective; 1.5 g/day ineffective for SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction

## Sexual Desire in Healthy Adults

### Gonzales et al. 2002 (Andrologia)
- **Study Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized parallel trial
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Assessment timepoints**: 4, 8, 12 weeks

**Dosing Protocols**:
- **Group 1**: 1,500 mg gelatinized maca daily
- **Group 2**: 3,000 mg gelatinized maca daily
- **Control**: Placebo

**Results**:
- Sexual desire improvements observed **starting at week 8**
- **No significant differences** in serum testosterone between maca and placebo (P=NS)
- **No significant differences** in estradiol levels (P=NS)
- Maca had **independent effect** on sexual desire at 8 and 12 weeks
- Effect **not mediated by depression, anxiety, or hormone changes**

**Mechanism**: Effect on libido NOT mediated by testosterone changes; independent non-hormonal mechanism

## Mild Erectile Dysfunction

### Zenico et al. 2009 (Andrologia)
- **Study Design**: Randomized, double-blind controlled trial
- **Sample**: 50 Caucasian men with mild erectile dysfunction
- **Duration**: 12 weeks

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 2,400 mg maca dry extract daily

**Results**:
- **IIEF-5 score**: Maca group +1.6±1.1 vs placebo +0.5±0.6 (P<0.001)
- **Psychological well-being**: Maca +9±6 vs placebo +6±5 (P<0.05)
- **Physical performance**: Maca +7±6 (P<0.05), placebo no significant improvement
- **Social performance**: Maca +7±6 (P<0.05), placebo no significant improvement

**Clinical Significance**: 2,400 mg/day maca dry extract produces measurable improvements in erectile function and well-being

## Late-Onset Hypogonadism (LOH)

### Shin et al. 2023 (World Journal of Men's Health)
- **Study Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial
- **Sample**: 80 total (41 maca, 39 placebo)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Assessment timepoints**: Baseline, 4 weeks, 12 weeks

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 1,000 mg gelatinized maca per pill
- **Frequency**: Two pills, three times daily = **3,000 mg/day total**
- **Timing**: Before food intake

**Results**:
- **AMS score**: Significantly improved (p<0.05)
- **IIEF score**: Significantly improved (p<0.05)
- **IPSS score**: Significantly improved (p<0.05)
- **ADAM positive rate**: Significantly decreased (p<0.0001)
- **Safety**: No significant changes in laboratory tests or vital signs

**Clinical Significance**: Treatment improved LOH symptoms without significant adverse effects; well tolerated

## Menopausal Symptoms - Perimenopausal Women

### Meissner et al. 2006a (IJBS)
- **Study Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover, randomized pilot trial
- **Sample**: 20 enrolled, 18 completed; Age range 41-50 years
- **Duration**: 4 months (2 months per phase, crossover)

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 2,000 mg daily (two 500 mg capsules twice daily with meals)
- **Form**: Pre-gelatinized maca

**Results**:
- **74-87% of participants** reported symptom alleviation per Kupperman's Index
- **Hot flushes**: Highly significant improvement (P<0.01)
- **Excessive sweating**: Highly significant improvement (P<0.01)
- **Interrupted sleep**: Highly significant improvement (P<0.01)
- **FSH levels**: Significantly increased (P<0.01) after 2 months
- **Blood pressure**: Systolic and diastolic both significantly reduced (P<0.05)
- **Body weight**: Significantly decreased (P<0.05)
- **HDL cholesterol**: Significantly increased (P<0.01)
- **Serum iron**: Significantly increased (P<0.05)

## Menopausal Symptoms - Postmenopausal Women

### Brooks et al. 2008 (Menopause)
- **Study Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover trial
- **Sample**: 14 postmenopausal women
- **Duration**: 12 weeks total (6 weeks per phase, crossover)

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 3.5 g/day powdered maca

**Results**:
- **Greene Climacteric Scale**: Significant reduction in psychological symptoms (P<0.05)
- **Anxiety subscale**: Improved vs baseline and placebo (P<0.05)
- **Depression subscale**: Improved vs baseline and placebo (P<0.05)
- **Sexual dysfunction**: Significant reduction vs placebo (P<0.05)
- **Hormone levels**: No differences in serum estradiol, FSH, LH, or SHBG between treatment and placebo

**Mechanism**: Effects NOT mediated by hormonal changes; non-hormonal mechanism

### Meissner et al. 2006b (IJBS)
- **Study Design**: Double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled crossover trial
- **Sample**: 34 early-postmenopausal Caucasian women total
  - Primary crossover: 22 women (11 per group)
  - Pilot bone density: 12 women (6 per group)
  - Age range: 49-58 years
- **Duration**: 4 months (2 months per phase, crossover)

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 2,000 mg daily (two 500 mg capsules twice daily with meals)
- **Form**: Pre-gelatinized organic maca

**Hormonal Changes at 2 Months**:
- **Estradiol (E2)**: Significantly increased (P<0.05)
- **FSH**: Suppressed (P<0.05)
- **LH**: Reduced (P<0.05)
- **Thyroid T3**: Suppressed (P<0.05)
- **Cortisol**: Decreased (P<0.05)
- **ACTH**: Suppressed (P<0.05)

**Clinical Outcomes**:
- **BMI**: Significantly reduced (P<0.05)
- **Menopausal symptoms**: Alleviated (P<0.001)
- **Bone density markers**: Noticeably increased
- **LDL cholesterol**: Increased (P<0.01)
- **Iron**: Increased (P<0.01)
- **Kupperman's Index**: Highly significant reductions (hot flushes, night sweating)

**Clinical Significance**: Pre-gelatinized maca modulates multiple hormone axes in postmenopausal women; demonstrates hormone-balancing effects distinct from HRT

## Physical Performance and Anti-Fatigue

### Ojeda et al. 2024 Meta-Analysis (Nutrients)
- **Study Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies**: 21 in systematic review, 16 in meta-analysis

**Effect Sizes (Standardized)**:
- **Forced swimming test**: 2.26 (large effect)
- **Rota-rod test**: 6.26 (large effect)
- **Grip strength test**: 5.23 (large effect)
- **Blood lactic acid**: -1.70 (reduced)

**Key Findings**:
- Macamides increase physical performance
- **Greater effect at higher doses** (dose-response relationship exists)

### Zhu et al. 2021 (Food Funct) - Mechanistic Study
- **Study Type**: In vivo (mice) and in vitro (C2C12 muscle cells)

**Performance Outcomes**:
- Significantly enhanced mouse leg **grip-strength**
- Increased **exercise endurance** in rota-rod test
- Cleared accumulation of **blood lactic acid (BLA)** after forced swimming
- Cleared accumulation of **blood urea nitrogen (BUN)**
- Cleared accumulation of **reactive oxygen species (ROS)**

**Mechanism**:
- Upregulated **mitochondrial biogenesis and function**
- Protected against oxidative stress-induced muscle damage
- Increased **NAD+/NADH ratios** in muscle cells treated with oxidative stress
- Activation of energy metabolism in skeletal muscle

## Antioxidant Effects

### Antioxidant Meta-Analysis 2024 (Antioxidants, MDPI)
- **Study Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies**: 11 in systematic review, 8 in meta-analysis
- **Published**: August 28, 2024

**Effect Sizes (Standardized Mean Differences)**:
- **Reduced glutathione**: SMD = 0.89 (small effect)
- **Glutathione peroxidase**: SMD = 0.96 (large effect)
- **Superoxide dismutase**: SMD = 0.68 (moderate effect)
- **Malondialdehyde**: SMD = -0.53 (moderate effect, reduced)

**Key Findings**:
- **Higher dose** of maca generated **greater antioxidant effect** (dose-dependent)
- Phytochemical compounds (mainly **macamides**) effectively controlled cellular oxidative stress

## Metabolic Effects

### Gonzales-Arimborgo et al. 2016 (Pharmaceuticals)
- **Study Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial
- **Sample**: 175 total (94 low altitude, 99 high altitude)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 3 g daily
- **Forms**: Spray-dried extracts of black maca or red maca

**Results**:
- **Red maca**: Better effects on mood, energy, and chronic mountain sickness (CMS) score
- **Black maca**: Reduced blood glucose levels
- **Both**: Improved mood, energy, and health status
- **Safety**: Good acceptability with no serious adverse effects

**Phytochemical Differences**:
- **Black maca**: Higher fatty acids and macamides than red maca
- **Red maca**: Lower macamides than black maca

**Clinical Significance**: Phenotype-specific effects; red maca superior for mood/energy; black maca superior for glucose metabolism

## Semen Quality (Insufficient Evidence)

### Lee et al. 2022 Meta-Analysis (Frontiers in Pharmacology)
- **Study Type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies**: 5 RCTs reviewed

**Results**:
- **3 RCTs in infertile men**: Mixed efficacy for improving semen quality
- **Meta-analysis**: No significant effect on sperm concentration vs placebo
  - Weighted Mean Difference: 2.22
  - 95% CI: -2.94 to 7.37
  - p=0.4
- **2 RCTs in healthy men**: Mixed effects on various parameters

**Conclusion**: **Unclear effects** of maca on semen quality parameters due to **insufficient evidence**

### Gonzales et al. 2001 (Asian J Androl) - Early Clinical Trial
- **Study Type**: Clinical trial
- **Sample**: 9 adult men (ages 24-44 years)
- **Duration**: 4 months

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 1,500 or 3,000 mg/day
- **Preparation**: Maca tablets

**Results**:
- Increased seminal volume
- Increased sperm count per ejaculum
- Increased motile sperm count
- Increased sperm motility
- **Serum hormones not modified** (LH, FSH, prolactin, testosterone, estradiol)
- Improvement **not dose-dependent**

**Mechanism**: Independent of major reproductive hormones

**Note**: Small sample size (n=9) limits interpretation; contradicts later meta-analysis

## Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) - Preclinical

### Gasco et al. 2007 (Phytomedicine) - Animal Study
- **Study Type**: Rats with testosterone-induced BPH
- **Preparation**: Freeze-dried aqueous extract of **red maca**

**Dosing (Dose-Response)**:
- **Red maca**: 0, 0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.5 g/kg body weight
- **Finasteride control**: 0.6 mg/kg body weight

**Results**:
- **Prostate weight**: Diminished in **dose-dependent fashion**
- **Effect**: Better than finasteride
- **Serum testosterone**: Not affected
- **Mechanism**: Post-DHT conversion (different from finasteride)
- **Did NOT reduce seminal vesicle weight** (unlike finasteride)
- **Benzyl glucosinolate content**: 0.639%

**Clinical Significance**: Suggests **red maca-specific benefit** for BPH via different mechanism than finasteride; human trials needed

## Spermatogenesis (Preclinical)

### Gonzales et al. 2006 (Andrologia) - Animal Study
- **Study Type**: Male rats
- **Preparation**: **Black maca** extract

**Results (Rapid Onset)**:
- **Day 1**: Increased epididymal sperm count
- **Day 3**: Sperm count increased in vas deferens
- **Day 7**: Increased daily sperm production
- **Testicular testosterone**: Unchanged after 1 week

**Mechanism**: Direct effect on sperm transport and production; **not hormonal**

**Clinical Significance**: Demonstrates **rapid onset** of action on spermatogenesis (as early as 1 day)

## Key Clinical Insights

1. **Dose-Response**: Higher doses (3.0 g/day) consistently more effective than lower doses (1.5 g/day) for sexual dysfunction; dose-dependent effects for physical performance and antioxidant activity

2. **Non-Hormonal Mechanisms**: Sexual and menopausal benefits often occur **independent of testosterone or estrogen changes** (Gonzales 2002, Brooks 2008)

3. **Onset of Action**: Typically **8 weeks** for sexual desire effects; earlier effects possible for other outcomes

4. **Phenotype Differences**:
   - **Black maca**: Superior for glucose metabolism
   - **Red maca**: Superior for mood, energy; potential BPH benefits

5. **Safety**: Well tolerated across all studies; no significant adverse effects at doses up to 3.5 g/day for 12 weeks

6. **Insufficient Evidence**: Semen quality improvement claims not supported by meta-analysis (Lee et al. 2022)
