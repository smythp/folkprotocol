# Maca (Lepidium meyenii) - Preparations & Dosing

## Forms Used in Clinical Research

### Pre-Gelatinized Powder

**Description:**
- Industrial process: starch gelatinization via heating in water
- Breaks down starch structure for claimed improved digestibility
- Most commonly studied form in clinical trials

**Evidence Status:**
- ⚠ **NO STUDIES validate that gelatinization improves bioavailability** despite widespread claims
- Most trials used gelatinized forms for practical reasons (stability, standardization), not proven superiority
- Traditional preparation was drying/powdering, NOT gelatinization

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**

| Study | Condition | Dose | Duration | Results |
|-------|-----------|------|----------|---------|
| Gonzales 2002 | Sexual desire (men) | 1,500-3,000 mg/day | 12 weeks | Effective at 8 weeks |
| Dording 2008 | SSRI sexual dysfunction | 3,000 mg/day (1,500 ineffective) | 12 weeks | Significant improvement |
| Meissner 2006a | Perimenopausal symptoms | 2,000 mg/day (500mg × 2, twice daily with meals) | 4 months | 74-87% symptom relief |
| Meissner 2006b | Early postmenopause | 2,000 mg/day (500mg × 2, twice daily with meals) | 4 months | Hormonal & symptom improvements |

**Administration (from trials):**
- Divided doses: 2-3 times daily
- Timing: With meals
- Capsule size: Typically 500-1,000 mg capsules

### Dry Extract (Standardized Extract)

**Description:**
- Concentrated extract from dried maca roots
- Solvent extraction followed by drying
- May be standardized to specific compounds (rare)

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**

| Study | Condition | Dose | Duration | Results |
|-------|-----------|------|----------|---------|
| Zenico 2009 | Mild erectile dysfunction | 2,400 mg/day dry extract | 12 weeks | IIEF-5 +1.6±1.1 vs placebo +0.5±0.6 (P<0.001) |

**Advantages:**
- Potentially higher concentration of bioactives
- Lower pill burden (smaller doses of extract vs. powder)
- May allow standardization to macamides (if done)

**Disadvantages:**
- Extraction solvent/method affects compound profile
- Less studied than powders overall
- Standardization often not disclosed

### Spray-Dried Extract (Phenotype-Specific)

**Description:**
- Aqueous extraction followed by spray drying
- Used in studies differentiating black vs. red maca phenotypes
- Preserves heat-sensitive compounds better than some methods

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**

| Study | Condition | Dose | Duration | Phenotype Results |
|-------|-----------|------|----------|-------------------|
| Gonzales-Arimborgo 2016 | Multiple outcomes, high/low altitude | 3,000 mg/day spray-dried extract | 12 weeks | Red: better mood/energy/CMS; Black: better glucose |

**Phenotype Differences:**
- **Black maca extract:** Higher macamides/fatty acids, reduces blood glucose
- **Red maca extract:** Better mood, energy, chronic mountain sickness scores
- **Yellow maca extract:** Most common variety, intermediate properties

**Administration (from trial):**
- Single daily dose or divided doses
- 3 g/day total

### Raw Maca Powder

**Description:**
- Dried maca roots ground into powder
- Minimal processing
- Traditional preparation method

**Evidence Status:**
- **Least studied** in modern clinical trials
- Most trials used gelatinized or extracted forms
- Traditional Peruvian use was dried/powdered roots

**Theoretical Advantages:**
- Preserves full phytochemical profile
- No processing-induced degradation
- Closest to traditional use

**Theoretical Disadvantages:**
- Starch may be harder to digest (unvalidated claim)
- Variable composition batch-to-batch
- May have shorter shelf life

**Note:** Claims that raw maca is "harder to digest" are unvalidated; no studies compare digestibility.

## Dosing by Indication (Evidence-Based)

### Sexual Health

**SSRI-Induced Sexual Dysfunction:**
- **Dose:** 3,000 mg/day (1,500 mg/day ineffective)
- **Form:** Gelatinized powder
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Administration:** Divided doses
- **Evidence:** 1 RCT (n=20)

**Sexual Desire (General):**
- **Dose:** 1,500-3,000 mg/day (both effective)
- **Form:** Gelatinized powder
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Onset:** 8 weeks
- **Administration:** Divided doses
- **Evidence:** 1 RCT (adult healthy men)

**Mild Erectile Dysfunction:**
- **Dose:** 2,400 mg/day
- **Form:** Dry extract
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Administration:** Daily dose
- **Evidence:** 1 RCT (n=50)

**Late-Onset Hypogonadism:**
- **Dose:** 6,000 mg/day (1,000 mg × 2 pills × 3 times/day)
- **Form:** Capsules (form not specified)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Administration:** Before meals, three times daily
- **Evidence:** 1 RCT (n=80)

**Postmenopausal Sexual Dysfunction:**
- **Dose:** 3,500 mg/day
- **Form:** Powdered maca
- **Duration:** 6-12 weeks
- **Administration:** Daily dose
- **Evidence:** 1 crossover RCT (n=14)

### Menopausal Symptoms

**Perimenopausal Symptoms:**
- **Dose:** 2,000 mg/day (500 mg capsules × 2, twice daily)
- **Form:** Pre-gelatinized powder
- **Duration:** 4 months
- **Administration:** With meals, twice daily
- **Target symptoms:** Hot flushes, night sweats, sleep disturbance
- **Evidence:** 1 crossover RCT (n=18)

**Early Postmenopausal Symptoms:**
- **Dose:** 2,000 mg/day (500 mg capsules × 2, twice daily)
- **Form:** Pre-gelatinized organic maca
- **Duration:** 4 months
- **Administration:** With meals, twice daily
- **Target symptoms:** Hot flushes, night sweats, psychological symptoms
- **Additional effects:** Hormone axis modulation (FSH↓, LH↓, E2↑)
- **Evidence:** 1 crossover RCT (n=34)

### Physical Performance

**Athletic Performance/Anti-Fatigue:**
- **Dose:** Higher doses more effective (dose-dependent)
- **Form:** Various (powders, extracts)
- **Duration:** Variable
- **Evidence:** Meta-analysis of 21 studies
- **Effect sizes:** Large (2.26-6.26 for various performance metrics)

**Note:** Specific optimal dose for performance not established; meta-analysis shows dose-response relationship.

### Antioxidant Effects

**Cellular Oxidative Stress:**
- **Dose:** Higher doses generate greater effect (dose-dependent)
- **Form:** Various
- **Evidence:** Meta-analysis of 11 studies
- **Effect sizes:** Small to large (SMD 0.53-0.96) depending on marker

### Metabolic Effects

**Blood Glucose Reduction:**
- **Dose:** 3,000 mg/day
- **Form:** Black maca spray-dried extract
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Evidence:** 1 RCT (n=175)

**Mood/Energy (High Altitude):**
- **Dose:** 3,000 mg/day
- **Form:** Red maca spray-dried extract (superior to black for this indication)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Evidence:** 1 RCT (n=175)

## Dose Ranges Summary

**Minimum Effective Dose:** 1,500-3,000 mg/day (condition-dependent)
- Sexual desire: 1,500 mg/day effective
- SSRI sexual dysfunction: 3,000 mg/day required (1,500 ineffective)

**Typical Therapeutic Range:** 2,000-3,500 mg/day
- Most menopausal studies: 2,000-3,500 mg/day
- Most sexual health studies: 2,400-3,000 mg/day

**High Dose:** 6,000 mg/day
- Late-onset hypogonadism: 6,000 mg/day
- Well tolerated with no significant adverse effects

**Maximum Studied:** 6,000 mg/day for 12 weeks
- No serious adverse events at this dose
- Appears to be safe upper limit based on current evidence

## Administration Guidelines

### Timing

**With Meals:**
- Most studies: Administered with meals
- Meissner studies: Specifically "with meals" twice daily
- Shin 2023 (LOH): "Before food intake" three times daily

**Recommendation:** With or shortly before meals appears most common and may improve tolerance.

### Frequency

**Twice Daily:**
- Menopausal studies: 500 mg × 2 capsules, twice daily = 2,000 mg/day total
- Practical for 2,000-4,000 mg/day range

**Three Times Daily:**
- Late-onset hypogonadism: 1,000 mg × 2 capsules, three times daily = 6,000 mg/day
- Necessary for higher total doses

**Once Daily:**
- Less common in trials
- May be practical for lower doses (1,500-2,400 mg/day)
- Compliance may be better but pharmacokinetics unknown

**Recommendation:** Divided doses (2-3 times daily) preferred based on clinical trial evidence.

### Duration

**Onset of Effects:**
- Sexual desire: 8 weeks (Gonzales 2002)
- Most outcomes: Effects emerge around 8 weeks

**Typical Trial Duration:**
- 12 weeks most common
- Range: 6 weeks to 4 months

**Recommendation:**
- Minimum trial: 8-12 weeks before assessing efficacy
- Can continue up to 4 months based on safety data
- Beyond 4 months: Monitor lipids, thyroid (see safety.md)

## Tincture/Extract Ratios

**Critical Gap:** No clinical trials using tinctures or liquid extracts found in bibliography.

**Implications:**
- Cannot provide evidence-based tincture dosing
- All clinical evidence uses powders or dry extracts
- Tincture bioavailability unknown relative to powders

**Theoretical Calculation (Unvalidated):**
If a 1:5 tincture (1 part maca : 5 parts solvent):
- 2,000 mg maca powder equivalent ≈ 10 ml tincture (theoretical)
- 3,000 mg maca powder equivalent ≈ 15 ml tincture (theoretical)

**⚠ WARNING:** These calculations assume equivalent extraction efficiency, which is unproven. Macamide solubility in alcohol/water unknown. **Use powders or extracts matching clinical trial forms for evidence-based dosing.**

## Tea/Decoction Preparations

**Critical Gap:** No clinical trials using tea or decoctions found in bibliography.

**Traditional Use Note:** Traditional preparation details (decoction methods, if used) not documented in available research literature.

**Implications:**
- Cannot provide evidence-based tea dosing
- Unknown if decoction extracts macamides effectively
- Heat effects on macamide stability unknown

**Theoretical Approach (Unvalidated):**
- Simmer 2-3 grams dried maca root powder in water
- Strain and consume
- Repeat 1-2 times daily to approximate 2,000-6,000 mg/day

**⚠ WARNING:** This is speculative. No evidence validates tea/decoction efficacy. Macamides may not extract well in water alone. **Stick to forms used in clinical trials** (powders, capsules, dry extracts).

## Standardized Extract Dosing

**Current State:**
- Few products standardized to macamide content
- No consensus on standardization markers
- USP-NF monographs in development but not yet published

**If Standardized to Macamides (Rare):**
- No clinical trials report specific macamide content doses
- Research gap: correlation between macamide mg and clinical effects unknown

**If Standardized as "X:1 Extract":**
Example: 4:1 extract means 4 kg raw maca → 1 kg extract
- To approximate 2,000 mg raw maca equivalent: 500 mg of 4:1 extract
- To approximate 3,000 mg raw maca equivalent: 750 mg of 4:1 extract

**⚠ CAUTION:** Extraction ratios don't specify bioactive content. Two different 4:1 extracts may have vastly different macamide levels depending on extraction method, solvent, and raw material quality.

**Recommendation:** If using extracts, prefer those disclosing:
1. Extraction ratio (e.g., 4:1)
2. Phenotype (black/red/yellow maca)
3. Geographic origin (Peruvian)
4. Third-party testing for identity/purity

Better yet: Use forms matching clinical trials (gelatinized powder, spray-dried phenotype extracts).

## Product Selection Guidance

### Evidence-Based Product Characteristics

**Best Supported by Research:**
1. **Pre-gelatinized maca powder, 2,000-3,000 mg/day**
   - Most commonly studied form
   - Menopausal symptoms, sexual health evidence
   - Divide into 2-3 doses daily with meals

2. **Phenotype-specific spray-dried extracts**
   - Black maca: Glucose metabolism (3,000 mg/day)
   - Red maca: Mood, energy (3,000 mg/day)
   - Yellow maca: General use (most common)

3. **Standardized dry extracts**
   - If macamide content specified (rare but ideal)
   - Erectile dysfunction: 2,400 mg/day dry extract

### What to Look For on Labels

**Positive Indicators:**
- ✓ Peruvian maca (geographic origin)
- ✓ Phenotype specified (black/red/yellow)
- ✓ Processing method disclosed (gelatinized, spray-dried, etc.)
- ✓ Dose per capsule clear (e.g., 500 mg, 1,000 mg)
- ✓ Third-party testing (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab)
- ✓ Organic certification (optional but reduces pesticide risk)
- ✓ Macamide content specified (rare, excellent if available)

**Red Flags:**
- ✗ "Proprietary blend" without specific maca content
- ✗ Claims like "boosts testosterone" (contradicts research)
- ✗ No information on origin or processing
- ✗ Unrealistic claims ("miracle cure")
- ✗ No third-party verification

### Practical Dosing Recommendations by Goal

**Sexual Health (General):**
- Start: 1,500 mg/day gelatinized powder
- If ineffective after 8 weeks: Increase to 3,000 mg/day
- Divide into 2 doses with meals
- Trial duration: 12 weeks

**SSRI Sexual Dysfunction:**
- Start: 3,000 mg/day gelatinized powder (1,500 ineffective)
- Divide into 3 doses with meals
- Trial duration: 12 weeks

**Erectile Dysfunction (Mild):**
- 2,400 mg/day dry extract OR 3,000 mg/day gelatinized powder
- Single or divided doses
- Trial duration: 12 weeks

**Menopausal Symptoms:**
- 2,000-3,500 mg/day pre-gelatinized powder
- 500-1,000 mg capsules, twice daily with meals
- Trial duration: 8-12 weeks minimum, up to 4 months

**Physical Performance/Anti-Fatigue:**
- Higher doses more effective (dose-dependent)
- 3,000-6,000 mg/day
- Divide into 2-3 doses
- Consider black maca (higher macamides)

**Glucose Metabolism:**
- 3,000 mg/day black maca spray-dried extract
- Trial duration: 12 weeks

**Mood/Energy:**
- 3,000 mg/day red maca spray-dried extract preferred
- Alternative: 2,000-3,000 mg/day yellow maca
- Trial duration: 12 weeks

## Capsule Size & Pill Burden

**Common Capsule Sizes:**
- 500 mg: Most common
- 1,000 mg: Available but less common

**Pill Burden Examples:**

For 2,000 mg/day:
- With 500 mg capsules: 4 capsules/day (2 twice daily)
- With 1,000 mg capsules: 2 capsules/day (1 twice daily)

For 3,000 mg/day:
- With 500 mg capsules: 6 capsules/day (2-3 three times or 3 twice)
- With 1,000 mg capsules: 3 capsules/day (1-2 three times)

For 6,000 mg/day:
- With 500 mg capsules: 12 capsules/day (4 three times)
- With 1,000 mg capsules: 6 capsules/day (2 three times)

**Compliance Considerations:**
- Higher doses (6 g/day) = significant pill burden
- Larger capsules (1,000 mg) reduce pill count
- Divided dosing may improve tolerance but increases inconvenience
- Consider compliance when recommending higher doses

## Storage & Stability

**Research Gap:** No stability studies found in bibliography.

**General Supplement Guidance (Apply to Maca):**
- Store in cool, dry place
- Protect from light (use opaque containers)
- Avoid moisture (causes degradation)
- Refrigeration may extend shelf life (unvalidated for maca)

**Macamide Stability:**
- Formed during post-harvest drying (enzymatic process)
- May degrade over time (rate unknown)
- Suggests fresh products preferred

**Shelf Life:**
- Manufacturer dating should be followed
- Likely 1-2 years for powders if properly stored
- Extracts may have different stability

## Cost Considerations

**General Pricing Factors:**
- Pre-gelatinized powder: Typically moderate cost
- Spray-dried phenotype extracts: Often higher cost (specialized)
- Raw powder: Often lowest cost
- Standardized extracts (if macamide content): Premium pricing

At $20 for 100 grams pre-gelatinized powder:
- 2,000 mg/day = 60 grams/month = $12/month
- 3,000 mg/day = 90 grams/month = $18/month

**Capsule vs. Bulk Powder:**
- Capsules: Convenience premium (10-50% more expensive)
- Bulk powder: Economical but less convenient
- Can fill own capsules to save money

## Preparation Methods for Bulk Powder

**If Using Loose Powder:**

**Mix in Liquids:**
- Smoothies: 1-3 teaspoons (2-6 g) in fruit smoothie
- Juice: Stir into juice (earthy/nutty taste)
- Water: Mix in water (taste may be challenging)

**Mix in Foods:**
- Yogurt: Stir into yogurt
- Oatmeal: Mix into hot or cold oatmeal
- Energy balls: Combine with nut butters, dates, etc.

**Encapsulation:**
- Purchase empty capsules (size 00 holds ~500 mg)
- Fill with powder using capsule machine
- Cost-effective for high doses

**Note:** Maca has earthy, slightly nutty taste. Some find it pleasant in smoothies; others prefer capsules.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Evidence-based dosing requires 2,000-6,000 mg/day** depending on indication
2. **Most studies used gelatinized powder or spray-dried extracts** - these forms have best evidence
3. **Divided dosing (2-3 times daily) with meals** most common in trials
4. **Minimum 8-week trial** before assessing efficacy; 12 weeks typical
5. **Tinctures and teas lack clinical evidence** - stick to powders/capsules/extracts
6. **Phenotype matters:** Black for glucose, red for mood, yellow general use
7. **Standardization to macamides ideal** but rarely available commercially
8. **Higher doses generally more effective** (dose-dependent for performance, antioxidants)

**Bottom Line:** Use pre-gelatinized powder (2,000-3,000 mg/day divided with meals) or phenotype-specific spray-dried extracts (3,000 mg/day) for evidence-based maca supplementation. Trial for 12 weeks minimum. Avoid forms without clinical evidence (tinctures, teas).
