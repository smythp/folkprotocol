# Maca (Lepidium meyenii) - Preparations and Dosing

## Clinical Trial Preparation Methods

### 1. Gelatinized Maca Powder

**Definition**: Maca root processed through starch gelatinization via heating in water

**Characteristics**:
- **Most commonly studied form** in clinical trials
- Industrial processing method (not traditional)
- Claimed to improve digestibility and shelf stability
- **Evidence for superiority**: None (no studies compare gelatinized vs raw for bioactivity)

**Clinical Trials Using Gelatinized Maca**:

#### Dording et al. 2008 (SSRI-Induced Sexual Dysfunction)
- **Form**: Gelatinized maca (form not explicitly stated but standard)
- **Effective dose**: 3.0 g/day
- **Ineffective dose**: 1.5 g/day
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Frequency**: Likely divided doses (not specified)

#### Gonzales et al. 2002 (Sexual Desire)
- **Form**: Gelatinized maca (explicitly stated)
- **Doses**: 1,500 mg or 3,000 mg daily
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Assessment**: 4, 8, 12 weeks
- **Effect onset**: Week 8

#### Shin et al. 2023 (Late-Onset Hypogonadism)
- **Form**: Gelatinized maca (explicitly stated)
- **Dose**: 1,000 mg per pill
- **Frequency**: Two pills, three times daily = **3,000 mg/day total**
- **Timing**: Before food intake
- **Duration**: 12 weeks

#### Meissner et al. 2006a (Perimenopausal Symptoms)
- **Form**: Pre-gelatinized maca
- **Dose**: 2,000 mg daily
- **Frequency**: Two 500 mg capsules twice daily with meals
- **Duration**: 2 months per phase (4 months crossover)

#### Meissner et al. 2006b (Postmenopausal Symptoms)
- **Form**: Pre-gelatinized organic maca
- **Dose**: 2,000 mg daily
- **Frequency**: Two 500 mg capsules twice daily with meals
- **Duration**: 2 months per phase (4 months crossover)

**Standard Gelatinized Dosing**:
- **Range**: 1,500-3,500 mg/day
- **Frequency**: Divided into 2-3 doses daily
- **Timing**: With meals
- **Duration**: 8-12 weeks for optimal effects

---

### 2. Maca Dry Extract

**Definition**: Concentrated extract standardized to specific markers (theoretically)

**Characteristics**:
- More concentrated than powder
- May be standardized to macamides or glucosinolates
- Less commonly used in trials than gelatinized powder

**Clinical Trial Using Dry Extract**:

#### Zenico et al. 2009 (Erectile Dysfunction)
- **Form**: Maca dry extract
- **Dose**: 2,400 mg daily
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Results**: Significant improvement in IIEF-5 score (+1.6±1.1 vs placebo +0.5±0.6, P<0.001)

**Extract Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 2,400 mg/day dry extract
- **Equivalent to**: Unknown amount of whole root (concentration ratio not provided)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks

---

### 3. Spray-Dried Extract

**Definition**: Maca extract processed by spray-drying technology

**Characteristics**:
- Preserves bioactive compounds
- Allows phenotype differentiation (black vs red)
- Used in comparative phenotype studies

**Clinical Trial Using Spray-Dried Extract**:

#### Gonzales-Arimborgo et al. 2016 (Phenotype Comparison)
- **Forms**: Spray-dried extracts of **black maca** or **red maca**
- **Dose**: 3 g daily
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Findings**:
  - Black maca: Higher fatty acids and macamides
  - Red maca: Better for mood and energy
  - Black maca: Superior for glucose metabolism

**Spray-Dried Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 3,000 mg/day
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Phenotype selection**: Choose based on therapeutic target

---

### 4. Powdered Maca (Whole Root)

**Definition**: Dried maca root ground into powder without further processing

**Characteristics**:
- Closest to traditional preparation
- May have lower digestibility than gelatinized (claimed but unproven)
- Less commonly used in clinical trials

**Clinical Trial Using Powdered Maca**:

#### Brooks et al. 2008 (Postmenopausal Sexual Dysfunction)
- **Form**: Powdered maca (not gelatinized)
- **Dose**: 3.5 g/day
- **Duration**: 6 weeks per phase (12 weeks crossover)
- **Results**: Significant improvements in psychological symptoms and sexual dysfunction

**Powdered Maca Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 3,500 mg/day
- **Duration**: 6-12 weeks
- **Note**: Effective despite not being gelatinized (challenges claims of gelatinization superiority)

---

### 5. Freeze-Dried Aqueous Extract

**Definition**: Water extraction followed by freeze-drying

**Characteristics**:
- Preserves water-soluble compounds
- Used in some preclinical studies
- Standardization to specific compounds possible

**Preclinical Study Using Freeze-Dried Extract**:

#### Gasco et al. 2007 (BPH, Rat Study)
- **Form**: Freeze-dried aqueous extract of **red maca**
- **Doses**: 0, 0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.5 g/kg body weight
- **Benzyl glucosinolate content**: 0.639%
- **Results**: Dose-dependent reduction in prostate weight; better than finasteride

**Note**: Animal dosing; human equivalent dose not established

---

### 6. Maca Tablets

**Definition**: Compressed maca powder or extract in tablet form

**Characteristics**:
- Convenient dosing
- May contain binders and excipients
- Used in some early clinical trials

**Clinical Trial Using Tablets**:

#### Gonzales et al. 2001 (Semen Parameters)
- **Form**: Maca tablets
- **Doses**: 1,500 or 3,000 mg/day
- **Duration**: 4 months
- **Results**: Increased seminal volume, sperm count, motility

**Tablet Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 1,500-3,000 mg/day
- **Duration**: 4 months for semen parameters

## Dosing by Clinical Indication

### Sexual Dysfunction

#### SSRI-Induced Sexual Dysfunction
- **Effective dose**: **3.0 g/day** gelatinized maca
- **Ineffective dose**: 1.5 g/day
- **Form**: Gelatinized powder
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Frequency**: Divided doses (specific schedule not provided)
- **Evidence**: Dording et al. 2008 (n=16 ITT)

#### Sexual Desire (Healthy Adults)
- **Dose**: **1.5-3.0 g/day** gelatinized maca
- **Form**: Gelatinized powder
- **Duration**: 12 weeks (effects start at week 8)
- **Frequency**: Once daily
- **Evidence**: Gonzales et al. 2002

#### Mild Erectile Dysfunction
- **Dose**: **2.4 g/day** maca dry extract
- **Form**: Dry extract
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Frequency**: Not specified
- **Evidence**: Zenico et al. 2009 (n=50)

#### Late-Onset Hypogonadism
- **Dose**: **3.0 g/day** gelatinized maca
- **Form**: Gelatinized powder (1,000 mg pills)
- **Frequency**: 2 pills × 3 times daily = 6 pills/day
- **Timing**: Before food intake
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Evidence**: Shin et al. 2023 (n=80)

---

### Menopausal Symptoms

#### Perimenopausal Women
- **Dose**: **2.0 g/day** pre-gelatinized maca
- **Form**: Pre-gelatinized powder in capsules
- **Frequency**: 500 mg × 2 capsules × 2 times daily (with meals)
- **Timing**: With meals
- **Duration**: 2-4 months
- **Evidence**: Meissner et al. 2006a (n=18)

#### Postmenopausal Women (Psychological/Sexual)
- **Option 1**: **3.5 g/day** powdered maca
  - Form: Whole root powder (not gelatinized)
  - Duration: 6 weeks (crossover study)
  - Evidence: Brooks et al. 2008 (n=14)

- **Option 2**: **2.0 g/day** pre-gelatinized organic maca
  - Form: Pre-gelatinized powder in capsules
  - Frequency: 500 mg × 2 capsules × 2 times daily (with meals)
  - Duration: 2-4 months
  - Evidence: Meissner et al. 2006b (n=34)

---

### Physical Performance and Anti-Fatigue

#### Athletic Performance
- **Meta-analysis finding**: **Higher doses more effective** (dose-dependent)
- **Form**: Various (gelatinized powder, extracts)
- **Typical range**: 2.0-3.5 g/day
- **Duration**: Variable (4-12 weeks in studies)
- **Evidence**: Ojeda et al. 2024 (meta-analysis, 21 studies)

**Specific Dosing**:
- No single standardized dose established
- Dose-response relationship exists (higher = better effect)
- Practical upper limit: ~3.5 g/day based on trial data

---

### Metabolic Effects

#### Blood Glucose Reduction
- **Dose**: **3.0 g/day** spray-dried **black maca** extract
- **Form**: Spray-dried extract (phenotype-specific)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Evidence**: Gonzales-Arimborgo et al. 2016 (n=175)

#### Mood and Energy
- **Dose**: **3.0 g/day** spray-dried **red maca** extract
- **Form**: Spray-dried extract (phenotype-specific)
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Evidence**: Gonzales-Arimborgo et al. 2016 (n=175)

## Tincture Preparations (Traditional Herbalism)

**Status in Research**: **No clinical trials** using maca tinctures found in bibliography

**Theoretical Tincture Ratios** (Based on standard herbalism practices, NOT evidence-based for maca):

### Alcohol Tincture (Theoretical)
- **Ratio**: 1:5 (herb:menstruum) or 1:3 for fresh root
- **Alcohol content**: 40-60% ethanol
- **Dose**: Unknown; not studied clinically
- **Macamide solubility**: Likely good (lipophilic compounds)
- **Glucosinolate solubility**: Poor in alcohol

**Limitation**: No clinical trials validate tincture efficacy or dosing

### Glycerite (Theoretical)
- **Ratio**: 1:4 or 1:5
- **Solvent**: Glycerin or glycerin/water blend
- **Dose**: Unknown; not studied
- **Bioactive extraction**: Unknown

**Clinical Recommendation**: **Use studied forms** (gelatinized powder, dry extracts) rather than tinctures due to lack of evidence

## Tea Preparations (Traditional)

**Status in Research**: **No clinical trials** using maca tea/decoctions found in bibliography

**Traditional Use**: Likely consumed as food/drink in Peru, but specific preparation not documented in modern research

**Theoretical Tea Preparation**:
- **Amount**: 3-5 g dried root
- **Water**: 200-250 mL boiling water
- **Steep time**: 10-15 minutes (decoction)
- **Dose**: Unknown efficacy

**Limitations**:
- **No clinical validation**
- **Unknown bioactive extraction** in water
- **Macamides**: Lipophilic; may not extract well in water
- **Glucosinolates**: Water-soluble; may extract better

**Note**: Gasco et al. 2007 used **freeze-dried aqueous extract** (water extraction), suggesting water-soluble compounds have bioactivity

**Clinical Recommendation**: Tea/decoction not studied; use **gelatinized powder** or **dry extract** for evidence-based dosing

## Standardized Extract Specifications

### Current Standardization Status

**USP-NF Monographs** (In Development Since 2009):
- **Products**: Maca root, maca root powder, maca root dry extract
- **Parameters**: Identity, composition, purity, contaminant limits
- **Bioactive markers**: Glucosinolate determination methods under development

**Commercial Standardization Claims**:
- **Macamide content**: Some products claim % macamides
- **Glucosinolate content**: Rarely standardized
- **Phenotype**: Black, red, or yellow maca specified

**Reality**: **69-2738 μg/g macamide variation** in commercial products (near 40-fold range)

### Recommended Standardization Targets (Not Yet Official)

**For Quality Products**:
1. **Minimum macamide content**: ≥500 μg/g (proposed; not official)
2. **Benzyl glucosinolate content**: 0.5-1.0% (based on Gasco 2007: 0.639%)
3. **Phenotype verification**: Chemical fingerprinting for black vs red vs yellow
4. **Heavy metals**: Meets USP limits (in development)
5. **Microbial limits**: Meets USP limits (in development)

**Consumer Guidance**: Look for **third-party testing** (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab) until official standards established

## Preparation Recommendations

### Evidence-Based Forms (Recommended)
1. **Gelatinized maca powder**: 2.0-3.5 g/day (most studied)
2. **Maca dry extract**: 2.4 g/day (less common but effective)
3. **Spray-dried extract**: 3.0 g/day (for phenotype-specific effects)

### Traditional Forms (Not Clinically Validated)
- **Tinctures**: No clinical trials; dosing unknown
- **Teas/decoctions**: No clinical trials; bioactive extraction uncertain
- **Raw powder**: Effective (Brooks 2008) but less studied than gelatinized

### Phenotype Selection
- **Black maca**: Glucose metabolism, spermatogenesis (preclinical)
- **Red maca**: Mood, energy, chronic mountain sickness; BPH (preclinical)
- **Yellow maca**: General use; intermediate profile

---

## Timing and Administration

### Optimal Timing
- **With meals**: Meissner studies used meals timing
- **Before meals**: Shin 2023 used before food intake
- **Conclusion**: **Either timing effective**; no head-to-head comparison

### Divided vs Single Dose
- **Divided doses** (2-3×/day): More common in trials
- **Single dose**: Likely acceptable but less studied
- **Recommendation**: **Divide into 2-3 doses** based on trial protocols

### Duration to Effect
- **Sexual desire**: **8 weeks** (Gonzales 2002)
- **Menopausal symptoms**: **2 months** (Meissner 2006)
- **Sexual dysfunction (SSRI)**: **12 weeks** (Dording 2008)
- **Physical performance**: Variable; dose-dependent

**Clinical Guidance**: **Trial for at least 8 weeks** before assessing efficacy

## Storage and Stability

**Research Gap**: No stability studies found in bibliography

**General Recommendations** (Standard for botanicals):
- **Cool, dry place**: Protect from heat and moisture
- **Light protection**: Amber/opaque containers
- **Airtight**: Minimize oxidation
- **Shelf life**: Unknown for bioactive retention

**Note**: Postharvest drying affects macamide formation; storage conditions likely also impact bioactive content

## Preparation Quality Checklist

When selecting a maca product:

✅ **Third-party tested** (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab, or equivalent)
✅ **Phenotype specified** (black, red, or yellow) if targeting specific effects
✅ **Processing method disclosed** (gelatinized, spray-dried, or raw)
✅ **Macamide content listed** (if available; ≥500 μg/g proposed target)
✅ **Source/origin disclosed** (geographical origin affects quality)
✅ **Heavy metals tested** (lead, cadmium, mercury, arsenic)
✅ **Microbial testing** (total plate count, coliforms, pathogens)
✅ **Pesticide screening** (especially for non-organic products)

**Avoid**:
❌ Products without third-party verification
❌ Products with undisclosed phenotype or processing
❌ Products making unsupported claims (e.g., "increases testosterone" - contradicted by evidence)
❌ Products with macamide content <100 μg/g (very low quality)

## Summary: Optimal Preparation and Dosing

**Evidence-Based Recommendation**:
- **Form**: **Gelatinized maca powder** (most studied, well-tolerated)
- **Dose**: **2.0-3.0 g/day** for most indications; up to 3.5 g/day if needed
- **Frequency**: **Divided into 2-3 doses daily**
- **Timing**: **With meals** (enhances tolerability)
- **Duration**: **8-12 weeks** minimum trial
- **Phenotype**: **Black maca** for glucose; **red maca** for mood; **yellow maca** for general use

**Avoid Unvalidated Forms**:
- Tinctures (no clinical data)
- Teas/decoctions (no clinical data)
- Products without quality verification
