# Boswellia serrata - Clinical Uses

## Osteoarthritis

### Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses

**Yu et al. 2020** - Meta-analysis of 7 trials, 545 patients
- **VAS pain**: Decreased 8.33 points (P<0.00001)
- **WOMAC pain**: Dropped 14.22 points (P=0.0006)
- **WOMAC stiffness**: Improved 10.04 points (P=0.0007)
- **WOMAC function**: Improved 10.75 points (P<0.00001)
- **Lequesne Index**: Improved 2.27 points (P<0.00001)
- **Duration**: Minimum 4 weeks recommended
- **Conclusion**: Effective and safe treatment option for OA patients
- **Source**: [PMID: 32680575](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32680575/)

**Dubey et al. 2024** - Aflapin® meta-analysis, 9 trials, 712 patients
- **Aflapin® vs other BS extracts**:
  - VAS reduction: -16.09 vs -4.68
  - Lequesne Functional Index: -3.81 vs -2.01
  - WOMAC pain: -18.68 vs -7.07
  - WOMAC stiffness: -14.25 vs -3.78
  - WOMAC function: -14.99 vs -8.41
- **Overall BS effect**:
  - VAS reduced -10.71 (p<0.00001)
  - LFI reduced -2.99 (p<0.00001)
  - All WOMAC subscales significantly improved
- **Quality**: All studies except one rated as good quality
- **Source**: [PMID: 38365549](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38365549/)

### Randomized Controlled Trials

**Majeed et al. 2024** - 105 patients enrolled, 98 completed
- **Design**: Double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled, three-arm, multi-center
- **Extract**: Boswellin® Super (standardized Boswellia serrata extract)
- **Groups**: 35 per group
  - Placebo
  - BSE 150mg twice daily
  - BSE 300mg twice daily
- **Duration**: 90 days
- **Results at Day 90**:
  - VAS pain reduced 45.3% (150mg) and 61.9% (300mg)
  - WOMAC total score improved 68.5% (150mg) and 73.6% (300mg)
  - EQ-5D quality of life scores improved 56.9-62.9%
  - Six-minute walk distance increased 21.2-21.9%
  - Inflammatory markers decreased: TNF-α, hs-CRP, IL-6
- **Time to effect**: Measurable improvements at day 5, continued through day 90
- **Safety**: No significant adverse events
- **Source**: [PMID: 39092235](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39092235/)

**Majeed et al. 2019** - 48 patients, pilot trial
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Extract**: Boswellin® containing 30% 3-acetyl-11-keto-β-boswellic acid (AKBBA) with β-boswellic acid (BBA)
- **Dose**: 169.33 mg tablet twice daily
- **Duration**: 120 days
- **Results**:
  - Significantly reduced pain and stiffness vs placebo
  - Improved physical function (WOMAC scores)
  - Enhanced walking ability
  - Reduced VAS pain scores
  - Improved knee joint gap and reduced osteophytes on radiographic imaging
  - Decreased high-sensitive C-reactive protein levels
- **Safety**: No serious adverse events
- **Source**: [PMID: 30838706](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30838706/)

**Karlapudi et al. 2023** - 70 enrolled, 67 completed
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Extract**: Aflapin® (standardized to 20% 3-O-acetyl-11-keto-β-boswellic acid)
- **Dose**: 100 mg daily
- **Duration**: 30 days
- **Groups**: 35 per group
- **Results at Day 30**:
  - VAS pain scores decreased 45%
  - Lequesne Functional Index improved 40.9%
  - WOMAC pain scores reduced 44.4%
  - WOMAC stiffness decreased 66.3%
  - WOMAC function improved 44.4%
  - Inflammatory markers reduced: MMP-3, TNFα, hsCRP, C2C
- **Early response**: Significant improvements as early as five days
- **Safety**: No significant adverse effects
- **Source**: [PMID: 35512759](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35512759/)

**Pérez-Piñero et al. 2023** - 120 patients, four-arm trial
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, controlled
- **Population**: Adults aged 40+ with persistent knee pain (VAS >3)
- **Groups**:
  - Boswellia only: 29 patients
  - Omega-3 only: 31 patients
  - Combined: 30 patients
  - Placebo: 30 patients
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Results**:
  - Omega-3 achieved statistically significant pain reduction vs placebo
  - Combined treatment improved quality of life (WOMAC index) and muscle strength
  - Boswellia alone improved sleep latency
- **Safety**: AvailOm® was safe and effective
- **Source**: [PMID: 37686880](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37686880/)

## Spondylitis

**Mamatha et al. 2025** - 105 enrolled, 94 completed
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, three-arm parallel group
- **Population**: Moderate spondylitis patients
- **Groups**: 35 per group
  - Placebo
  - Full-spectrum Boswellia extract (F-BSE)
  - Boswellia with curcumin co-delivery (C-BSE)
- **Dose**: 400 mg/day
- **Technology**: FenuMat® delivery technology (water-soluble, non-covalent complex)
- **Duration**: 28 days
- **Results**:
  - Both F-BSE and C-BSE showed significant reductions in pain and stiffness by day 14, continuing through day 28
  - NLRP3 inflammasome and IL-1β levels significantly reduced by days 14 and 28
  - C-BSE showed superior effects compared to F-BSE
  - Improved efficacy at lower dose (400 mg/day) attributed to enhanced bioavailability
- **Source**: [PMID: 40667507](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40667507/)

## Ulcerative Colitis

**Gupta et al. 1997** - Controlled clinical trial
- **Design**: Controlled clinical trial vs Sulfasalazine
- **Dose**: 350 mg three times daily
- **Duration**: 6 weeks
- **Remission Rates**:
  - Boswellia: 82% went into remission
  - Sulfasalazine: 75% went into remission
- **Improvements**: All measured parameters improved including:
  - Stool properties
  - Hemoglobin
  - Serum iron, calcium, phosphorus, protein levels
  - Total leukocytes and eosinophils
- **Mechanism**: Inhibits 5-lipoxygenase, the key enzyme of leukotriene biosynthesis
- **Safety**: No adverse effects mentioned
- **Source**: [PMID: 9049593](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9049593/)

## Crohn's Disease

**Holtmeier et al. 2011** - 82 randomized, 66 analyzed
- **Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized, parallel-group, multicenter
- **Extract**: Boswelan
- **Dose**: 3×2 capsules daily (400 mg each)
- **Duration**: 52 weeks
- **Groups**:
  - Active treatment: 42 patients
  - Placebo: 40 patients
- **Primary Outcome**: Maintaining remission throughout study period
- **Secondary Outcomes**: Time to relapse, changes in CDAI and IBDQ scores
- **Results**:
  - **Efficacy**: 59.9% of actively treated patients and 55.3% of placebo group stayed in remission (P=0.85, no significant difference)
  - **Safety**: Good tolerability in long-term treatment with no disadvantages compared to placebo
- **Conclusion**: Good safety profile but lack of efficacy for maintaining remission
- **Source**: [PMID: 20848527](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20848527/)

## Bronchial Asthma

**Gupta et al. 1998** - 80 patients
- **Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT
- **Sample Size**:
  - Treatment group: 40 patients (23 males, 17 females)
  - Control group: 40 patients (16 males, 24 females)
  - Mean asthma duration: 9.58 ± 6.07 years
- **Treatment**: 300 mg gum resin thrice daily
- **Control**: 300 mg lactose thrice daily
- **Duration**: 6 weeks
- **Results**:
  - **Treatment group**: 70% showed improvement
    - Disappearance of dyspnea and rhonchi
    - Reduced number of attacks
    - Increased FEV₁, FVC, and PEFR
    - Decreased eosinophilic count and ESR
  - **Control group**: Only 27% showed improvement
- **Source**: [PMID: 9810030](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9810030/)

**Yugandhar et al. 2018** - 36 patients
- **Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Product**: LI13109F (marketed as AlvioLife®)
- **Composition**: Boswellia serrata and Aegle marmelos extracts
- **Dose**: 200 mg daily
- **Population**: Mild to moderate asthma
- **Duration**: 56 days
- **Clinical Trial ID**: CTRI/2016/10/007393
- **Results at Day 14**:
  - Emotional function improved (p=0.0305)
  - Asthma symptom scores improved (p=0.0002)
- **Results at Day 56**:
  - Serum IFN-γ increased significantly (p=0.0014)
  - IL-4 levels reduced (p=0.0497)
- **Safety**: No serious adverse events or abnormal laboratory observations
- **Source**: [PMID: 29210124](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29210124/)

## Peritumoral Brain Edema

**Kirste et al. 2011** - 44 patients
- **Design**: Prospective, randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind pilot trial
- **Population**: Patients with primary or secondary malignant cerebral tumors
- **Dose**: 4200 mg/day Boswellia serrata vs placebo
- **Duration**: Throughout radiotherapy course
- **Results**:
  - **Edema reduction >75%**: Found in 60% of BS patients vs 26% of placebo patients (P=0.023)
  - Boswellic acids detected in patient serum samples
  - No significant differences in dexamethasone requirements between groups
- **Safety**:
  - No severe adverse events in either group
  - Minor gastrointestinal discomfort reported by 6 patients in treatment group
- **Regulatory**: EU orphan drug designation (2002)
- **Source**: [PMID: 21287538](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21287538/)

## Clinical Dosing Summary

### Standardized Extracts

**Aflapin®** (20% AKBA)
- 100 mg daily (Karlapudi 2023)
- Enhanced bioavailability formulation

**Boswellin® Super**
- 150 mg twice daily (Majeed 2024)
- 300 mg twice daily (Majeed 2024)

**Boswellin® AKBBA** (30% AKBBA with BBA)
- 169.33 mg twice daily (Majeed 2019)

**Enhanced Bioavailability (FenuMat®)**
- 400 mg/day (Mamatha 2025)
- Water-soluble, non-covalent complex

### Crude Gum Resin

- **Ulcerative colitis**: 350 mg three times daily
- **Asthma**: 300 mg three times daily
- **Brain edema**: 4200 mg/day

### General Recommendations

- **Standard dosing**: 250-500 mg two or three times daily
- **Minimum duration**: At least 4 weeks for osteoarthritis
- **Administration frequency**: Every 6 hours based on pharmacokinetics
- **Time to steady state**: Approximately 30 hours
- **Early response**: Possible as early as 5 days in some studies

## Clinical Applications Summary

### Established Uses (Strong Evidence)

1. **Osteoarthritis**
   - Evidence level: Strong (multiple RCTs and meta-analyses)
   - Typical outcomes: Pain reduction 40-60%, function improvement 40-70%

2. **Ulcerative colitis**
   - Evidence level: Moderate (RCT showing 82% remission rate)
   - Comparator: Similar to sulfasalazine (75% remission)

3. **Bronchial asthma**
   - Evidence level: Moderate (RCT showing 70% improvement rate)
   - Typical outcomes: Improved FEV₁, FVC, PEFR; reduced attack frequency

4. **Peritumoral brain edema**
   - Evidence level: Moderate (RCT showing 60% with >75% edema reduction)
   - Regulatory: EU orphan drug designation (2002)

### Emerging Uses (Limited Evidence)

1. **Crohn's disease**
   - Evidence level: Limited (one negative RCT for maintenance therapy)
   - Notes: Good safety profile but lack of efficacy for maintaining remission

2. **Rheumatoid arthritis**
   - Evidence level: Preliminary (preclinical and pilot studies)
   - Notes: Promising anti-inflammatory effects but limited clinical trial data

## Research Gaps

- Limited head-to-head comparisons between different Boswellia extracts and formulations
- Need for more clinical trials in rheumatoid arthritis
- Optimal dosing and duration studies for specific conditions
- Long-term safety data (>1 year)
- Pediatric safety and efficacy data
- Pregnancy and lactation safety data
