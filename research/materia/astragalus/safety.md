# Astragalus - Safety Profile

## General Safety Assessment

**Overall Safety**: Astragalus is considered generally safe without major adverse effects based on:
- Clinical trial data (no serious adverse events reported)
- Animal toxicology studies (excellent safety margins)
- Traditional use history (over 2,000 years in TCM)
- LiverTox database: No significant hepatotoxicity recorded

## Clinical Trial Safety Data

### Human Studies - Adverse Events

**General Findings**:
- Few human studies reporting serum enzyme elevations during therapy
- No mention of serious adverse events
- No clinically apparent hepatotoxicity
- Clinical trials show doses 30 mg to 7.5 g daily for 1-3 months generally safe
- Up to 60 grams per day for as long as 4 months appears safe

### Cancer Patients

**Li et al. 2025** - Meta-analysis of astragalus polysaccharides in malignant tumors:
- 31 trials, 2,648 participants
- **Adverse events**: Low incidence of hand-foot numbness and weakness
- **Overall safety**: High safety profile
- **Comparison**: Lower incidence of adverse drug reactions compared to chemotherapy-only treatment (Cheng et al. 2021 gastric cancer study)

## Animal Toxicology Studies

### 1. Cycloastragenol Safety (Derivative Compound)

**Szabo 2014** - Subchronic toxicity and genotoxicity study
- **Species**: Sprague-Dawley rats
- **Duration**: ≥91 consecutive days
- **Dosing levels**:
  - Oral gavage: 0, 40, 80, or 150 mg/kg/day
  - Bacterial assay: Up to 5,000 μg/plate
  - Micronucleus assay: 2,000 mg/kg intraperitoneal injection

**Results**:
- **NOAEL** (No Observed Adverse Effect Level): >150 mg/kg/day in both male and female rats
- **Mortality**: No treatment-related mortalities
- **Cardiac effects**: None identified
- **Statistically significant changes**: None were adverse
- **Bacterial mutagenicity**: Negative across all doses
- **Chromosome aberration**: Moderately positive response at one intermediate concentration; negative elsewhere
- **Micronucleus testing**: No clastogenicity observed in peripheral erythrocytes
- **Conclusion**: High safety profile at doses exceeding typical dietary levels

### 2. Radix Astragali Extract Toxicity

**Yu et al. 2007** - Subchronic toxicity study
- **Species**: Sprague-Dawley rats and beagle dogs
- **Duration**: Three consecutive months
- **Dosing levels**:
  - Rats: 5.7-39.9 g/kg
  - Dogs: 2.85-19.95 g/kg

**Results**:
- **Toxicity**: No distinct toxicity demonstrated
- **Body weight**: Normal between experimental and control groups
- **Organ weights**: Normal for principal organs
- **Hematology**: Comparable results between groups
- **Histopathology**: Comparable results between groups
- **Safety margin**: ~70× human equivalent dose in rats, ~35× in dogs with no adverse effects
- **Conclusion**: Excellent safety profile at doses far exceeding human use levels

### 3. HT042 Mixture Safety (Multi-Herb Preparation)

**Song et al. 2017** - Preclinical toxicity assessment
- **Composition**: Astragalus membranaceus root + Eleutherococcus senticosus stem + Phlomis umbrosa root
- **Species**: Sprague-Dawley rats
- **Dosing levels**:
  - Acute study: Single doses up to 5,000 mg/kg
  - Subchronic study: Repeated doses up to 4,000 mg/kg/day

**Results**:
- **Approximate lethal dose (LD50)**: >5,000 mg/kg
- **NOAEL**: 4,000 mg/kg/day in both male and female rats
- **Acute toxicity**: No deaths; no indication of toxicity
- **Subchronic toxicity**: No deaths; no changes in clinical signs
- **Treatment-related alterations**: Minor changes remained within normal ranges or recovered during observation periods
- **Conclusion**: Favorable safety profile

## Hepatotoxicity

**LiverTox Database Assessment**:
- Not associated with serum enzyme elevations
- No clinically apparent liver injury
- No significant hepatotoxicity recorded

**Clinical Trial Data**:
- Few human studies reporting serum enzyme elevations during therapy
- No mention of hepatotoxicity in systematic reviews and meta-analyses

## Contraindications

### 1. Pregnancy and Lactation

**Status**: Use with caution
- **Evidence**: Insufficient safety data in pregnancy and lactation
- **Traditional contraindication**: TCM traditionally uses caution in pregnancy
- **Recommendation**: Avoid unless under professional guidance

### 2. Autoimmune Conditions

**Theoretical Concern**: Due to immunostimulatory effects
- **Mechanism**: Astragalus enhances immune function (CD3, CD4, NK cells)
- **Potential issue**: Could theoretically exacerbate autoimmune conditions
- **Evidence**: No clinical studies specifically examining autoimmune contraindications
- **Recommendation**: Use caution in autoimmune diseases; professional guidance advised

**Note**: This is a theoretical concern based on mechanism of action rather than reported adverse events.

### 3. Acute Infections (Traditional TCM Contraindication)

**TCM Principle**: Avoid "tonifying" during acute illness
- **Traditional view**: Astragalus strengthens "exterior" but may "trap pathogen" during active acute infection
- **Modern relevance**: Unclear clinical significance
- **Recommendation**: Follow traditional guidance; use after acute phase resolves

### 4. "Excess" Patterns in TCM

**Traditional contraindications**:
- "Excess heat" patterns
- "Yin deficiency with heat"
- These are TCM diagnostic concepts without direct Western equivalents

## Drug Interactions

**Status**: Understudied
- **Known interactions**: None well-documented in literature
- **Theoretical concerns**:
  - Potential interaction with immunosuppressants (due to immune-enhancing effects)
  - Potential blood sugar effects (caution with diabetes medications, though clinical studies show adjuvant benefit)
- **Research gap**: Interactions with other herbs and conventional medications understudied
- **Recommendation**: Inform healthcare providers of use, especially if on immunosuppressants or diabetes medications

## Dosing Safety Ranges

### Clinical Trial Dosing
- **Standardized extracts**: 30 mg to 7.5 g daily for 1-3 months - generally safe
- **Higher doses**: Up to 60 g/day for up to 4 months appears safe
- **Most common**: 500-1,000 mg/day standardized extracts

### Traditional Dosing
- **Chinese Pharmacopoeia**: 9-30 g/day (decoction)
- **History**: Over 2,000 years of use at these doses

### Animal Safety Margins
- **Rats**: 70× human equivalent dose for 3 months with no adverse effects
- **Dogs**: 35× human equivalent dose for 3 months with no adverse effects

## Limitations in Safety Data

### Research Gaps

1. **Long-term effects not examined**:
   - Longest human studies: 12 months (Li et al. 2018 diastolic dysfunction)
   - Most studies: 1-3 months
   - No data beyond 4 months at high doses

2. **Lack of information on adverse effects in combination**:
   - Interactions with other herbs understudied
   - Interactions with conventional medications limited
   - Polypharmacy safety unclear

3. **Pregnancy/lactation data insufficient**:
   - No controlled human studies
   - Animal studies not specifically focused on reproductive toxicity
   - Default to caution

4. **Autoimmune disease safety unclear**:
   - No specific studies in autoimmune populations
   - Theoretical concern based on mechanism
   - Clinical significance unknown

5. **Pediatric safety**:
   - Limited to HT042 study in 6-8 year olds (Lee et al. 2018)
   - 24 weeks, favorable safety profile
   - Broader pediatric data lacking

6. **Geriatric populations**:
   - Some studies in older adults (e.g., postmenopausal women)
   - No systematic safety assessment in elderly
   - Likely safe based on general profile

## Special Populations

### Cancer Patients
- **Evidence**: Multiple trials in cancer populations
- **Safety**: High safety profile
- **Adverse events**: Lower incidence than chemotherapy alone
- **Immune effects**: Beneficial (elevated CD3+, CD4+, NK cells)
- **Recommendation**: Appears safe as adjuvant therapy

### Diabetic Patients
- **Evidence**: Multiple trials in type 2 diabetes
- **Safety**: No safety concerns noted
- **Efficacy**: Improved glycemic control
- **Recommendation**: May be beneficial as adjuvant; monitor blood glucose

### Cardiovascular Patients
- **Evidence**: Trial in hypertensive postmenopausal women
- **Safety**: No adverse effects noted over 12 months
- **Efficacy**: Improved diastolic function
- **Recommendation**: Appears safe; professional guidance advised

### Athletes
- **Evidence**: RCT in elite athletes (Latour et al. 2021)
- **Safety**: No adverse effects
- **Efficacy**: Maintained immune function during intense training
- **Recommendation**: Safe for athletic populations

## Quality and Contamination Concerns

### Potential Issues (Not Specific to Studies Reviewed)
1. **Heavy metal contamination**: Possible in herbs from certain regions
2. **Pesticide residues**: Quality control important
3. **Adulteration**: Ensure proper species identification
4. **Standardization variability**: Product quality varies

### Recommendations
- Source from reputable suppliers
- Look for third-party testing (heavy metals, pesticides)
- Verify botanical identity (A. membranaceus or A. membranaceus var. mongholicus)
- Choose products with standardization (e.g., % isoflavonoids or astragalosides)

## Clinical Recommendations

1. **Generally safe for most adults** at typical doses (500-1,000 mg standardized extract or 9-30 g traditional preparation)

2. **Use caution or avoid in**:
   - Pregnancy and lactation (insufficient data)
   - Autoimmune conditions (theoretical concern)
   - During acute infections (traditional contraindication)

3. **Monitor when using with**:
   - Immunosuppressant medications
   - Diabetes medications (though may be beneficial)
   - Other herbs and supplements

4. **Quality matters**:
   - Source from reputable suppliers
   - Look for standardization and third-party testing
   - Verify botanical identity

5. **Professional guidance advised for**:
   - Long-term use (>4 months)
   - Use in special populations (pregnant, autoimmune, immunosuppressed)
   - Combination with multiple medications

6. **No significant safety concerns** for:
   - Short to medium-term use (1-4 months)
   - Doses within clinical trial ranges
   - Generally healthy adults
