# Astragalus - Clinical Uses

## Evidence-Based Clinical Applications

### 1. Immunomodulation

#### Exercise-Induced Immunosuppression (Athletes)
- **Study**: Latour et al. 2021 (RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled)
- **Sample**: 18 Polish rowing team members (10 intervention, 8 placebo)
- **Dosage**: 500 mg daily standardized extract (0.5% 3-hydroxy 7-methoxy isoflavonoids)
- **Duration**: 6 weeks (intensive training camp)
- **Results**:
  - IL-2 post-exertion decrease: Effect size d = 1.22, p = 0.02
  - IL-2/IL-10 ratio after recovery: Effect size d = 1.61, p < 0.01
  - Maintained baseline natural killer cells and regulatory T cells (placebo group declined)
  - Stabilized immune system by restoring Th1 response balance

#### General Immune Function (Meta-Analysis)
- **Study**: Zhang et al. 2023 - Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies**: 19 trials, 1,094 total participants
- **Findings**:
  - **Humoral immunity**: Proinflammatory cytokines (IL-2, IL-4, IL-6, IL-10, TNF-α, IFN-γ) reduced by SMD -2.8765 [95% CI: -3.2385, -2.5145], p < 0.0001
  - **Cellular immunity**: CD3 levels and CD4/CD8 ratio increased by SMD 2.4629 [95% CI: 1.9598, 2.9661]
- **Conclusion**: Significant immunomodulatory effects on both humoral and cellular immune response
- **Limitation**: Substantial heterogeneity among studies

### 2. Type 2 Diabetes (Adjuvant Treatment)

**Study**: Hong et al. 2024 - Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies**: 20 RCTs, 953 total participants
- **Key Outcomes**:
  - **HbA1c reduction**: -0.93% [95% CI: -1.22, -0.64], p < 0.001
  - **Fasting plasma glucose**: -0.67 mmol/L [95% CI: -1.13, -0.20], p = 0.005
  - **Postprandial glucose (2h)**: -0.67 mmol/L [95% CI: -1.13, -0.20], p = 0.005
  - **Insulin sensitivity index**: +0.42 [95% CI: 0.13, 0.72], p = 0.004
  - **Overall effectiveness**: Risk ratio 1.33 [95% CI: 1.26, 1.40], p < 0.001
- **Conclusion**: Astragalus may benefit T2DM patients as adjuvant treatment, though certainty of evidence is not optimal

### 3. Diabetic Nephropathy (Stage III)

**Study**: Lin et al. 2024 - Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies**: 32 trials, 2,462 total participants (1,244 intervention, 1,218 control)
- **Intervention**: Astragalus combined with renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system blockers
- **Key Outcomes**:
  - **Total effective rate**: Odds ratio 3.63 [95% CI: 2.59, 5.09], p < 0.00001
  - **Urinary protein excretion**: Mean difference -24.76 [95% CI: -27.12, -22.39], p < 0.00001
  - **Serum creatinine**: Mean difference -3.50 [95% CI: -4.91, -2.09], p < 0.00001
  - **Blood urea nitrogen**: Mean difference -0.35 [95% CI: -0.50, -0.19], p < 0.00001
  - **HbA1c**: Mean difference -0.17 [95% CI: -0.27, -0.07], p = 0.0006
- **Conclusion**: Combination safe and improves outcomes; larger rigorous trials needed

### 4. Cancer-Related Fatigue

**Study**: Sheng et al. 2025 - Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Studies**: 8 RCTs
- **Key Outcomes**:
  - **Fatigue reduction**: SMD -1.63 [95% CI: -1.90, -1.36], p < 0.00001
  - **Fatigue efficacy rate**: Risk ratio 1.55 [95% CI: 1.19, 2.02], p = 0.001
  - **Quality of life improvement**: SMD 0.86 [95% CI: 0.17, 1.55], p = 0.01
  - **QOL efficacy rate**: Risk ratio 1.57 [95% CI: 1.10, 2.23], p = 0.01
- **Limitations**: Small sample sizes, low study quality, inconsistent cancer types
- **Conclusion**: Results supportive but insufficient to strongly recommend clinical use at this time

### 5. Advanced Gastric Cancer (With Chemotherapy)

**Study**: Cheng et al. 2021 - Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Intervention**: Astragalus-containing TCM combined with platinum-based chemotherapy
- **Key Outcomes**:
  - **Objective response rate**: Risk ratio 1.24 (increased tumor response)
  - **Disease control rate**: Risk ratio 1.10
  - **1-year survival rate**: 41% relative increase
  - **2-year survival rate**: Relative increase 3.13
  - **Quality of life**: Risk ratio 2.03 (substantially better outcomes)
  - **Immune function**: Elevated CD3+ T cells, CD4+ T cells, natural killer cells
  - **Safety**: Lower incidence of adverse drug reactions vs chemotherapy-only
- **Evidence Quality**: Low to moderate; additional high-quality research recommended

### 6. Malignant Tumors (General)

**Study**: Li et al. 2025 - Systematic review and meta-analysis of astragalus polysaccharides
- **Studies**: 31 RCTs, 2,648 total participants
- **Key Outcomes**:
  - **Treatment efficacy**: Risk ratio 1.30 [95% CI: 1.21, 1.39], p < 0.01
  - **CD3+ T cells**: Mean difference +8.79 [95% CI: 6.15, 11.43]
  - **CD4+ T cells**: Mean difference +7.94 [95% CI: 5.27, 10.61]
  - **CD4/CD8 ratio**: Mean difference +0.40 [95% CI: 0.28, 0.52]
  - **CD8+ T cells**: No significant difference (p = 0.242)
  - **Safety**: Low incidence of hand-foot numbness and weakness; high safety profile

### 7. Post-COVID-19 Chronic Fatigue

**Study**: Banihashemi et al. 2025 - Triple-blind RCT
- **Sample**: 64 Iranian nurses with post-COVID-19 chronic fatigue (32 intervention, 32 control)
- **Dosage**: 500 mg astragalus root extract twice daily (1,000 mg/day total)
- **Timepoints**: Before intervention, at conclusion, 1 month post-intervention
- **Results**:
  - **Fatigue reduction at end**: 13.8% in intervention group
  - **Fatigue reduction at 1-month follow-up**: 17.2% in intervention group
  - **Control group**: 72.2% still had chronic fatigue (p = 0.003)
  - **Risk factors identified**:
    - Underlying disease: OR 2.33, p = 0.04
    - Control group assignment: OR 12.36, p = 0.01
    - Time duration (protective): OR 0.50, p = 0.0001
- **Conclusion**: Astragalus root extract reduces chronic fatigue in post-COVID-19 patients

### 8. Cardiovascular - Diastolic Dysfunction

**Study**: Li et al. 2018 - Prospective, open-label RCT
- **Sample**: 154 postmenopausal hypertensive women with metabolic syndrome
- **Duration**: 12 months
- **Dosing Groups**:
  - Control: Conventional medical therapy only
  - AM Group 1: Conventional therapy + 5 g/day astragalus capsules
  - AM Group 2: Conventional therapy + 10 g/day astragalus capsules
- **Results** (AM Group 2 vs Control):
  - **Early diastolic mitral velocity (E')**: 0.065 ± 0.007 m/s vs 0.061 ± 0.008 m/s, p = 0.014
  - **E/E' ratio**: 10.70 ± 1.30 vs 11.37 ± 1.73, p = 0.031
- **Conclusion**: Conventional medical therapy plus astragalus improved diastolic function

### 9. Pediatric Growth (Mild Short Stature)

**Study**: Lee et al. 2018 - Multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT
- **Sample**: 129 children aged 6-8 years with height below 25th percentile
- **Product**: HT042 extract mixture (Astragalus membranaceus + Eleutherococcus senticosus + Phlomis umbrosa)
- **Duration**: 24 weeks
- **Results**:
  - **Height gain (all children)**: +0.29 cm [95% CI: 0.01, 0.57], p = 0.027
  - **Height gain (below 10th percentile)**: +0.45 cm [95% CI: 0.04, 0.87], p = 0.031
  - **Bone age advancement**: Lower in treatment group
  - **Serum IGF-1 and IGFBP-3**: Significantly increased within HT042 group
- **Conclusion**: HT042 supplementation helped increase height growth without skeletal maturation; more effective in much shorter children

## Evidence Quality Summary

**Strong Evidence (Multiple large RCTs/Meta-analyses)**:
- Type 2 diabetes adjuvant treatment
- Diabetic nephropathy (with RAAS blockers)
- Immunomodulation (general)

**Moderate Evidence (Some RCTs, needs more research)**:
- Exercise-induced immunosuppression
- Post-COVID fatigue
- Cancer-related fatigue
- Malignant tumor adjuvant treatment

**Preliminary Evidence (Small studies, mixed quality)**:
- Cardiovascular diastolic function
- Pediatric growth support

**Limitations Across Studies**:
- Substantial heterogeneity in many meta-analyses
- Small sample sizes in some trials
- Low to moderate quality evidence in cancer studies
- Limited long-term safety data (>4 months)
- Standardization varies across studies
