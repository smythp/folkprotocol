# Andrographis - Active Compounds & Bioavailability

## Primary Active Constituents

### Diterpene Lactones

**Primary marker compound**:
- **Andrographolide** - Major bioactive diterpene lactone used for standardization

**Secondary diterpene lactones**:
- 14-deoxyandrographolide
- Neoandrographolide
- 14-deoxy-11,12-didehydroandrographolide

## Standardization

### USP Reference Standard
**United States Pharmacopeia specification**:
- **Reference Standard**: USP-1034873
- **CAS Number**: 90244-84-1
- **Quality specification**: 14-deoxy-11,12-didehydroandrographolide not more than 15% of total diterpene lactones
- **Monographs published**: Andrographis, Powdered Andrographis, and Powdered Andrographis Extract in USP Dietary Supplements Compendium

### Common Standardization Level
**Typical commercial standardization**: 30% total andrographolides
- Used in clinical trials (e.g., ParActin®, Paractin)
- Provides consistent andrographolide content across batches
- Improves quality consistency compared to crude herb

### Commercial Standardized Products

**Kan Jang**:
- Standardization: SHA-10 standardized extract
- Multiple RCTs conducted
- Fixed combination with Eleutherococcus senticosus in some formulations

**ParActin®**:

## Pharmacokinetics

### Human Studies - Standard Dosing

**Panossian 2000** - Kan Jang fixed combination in humans:
- **Single dose**: 4 tablets (20 mg andrographolide equivalent)
- **Routine regimen**: 3 × 4 tablets/day (~1 mg/kg/day)
- **Cmax**: ~393 ng/mL (~1.12 μM)
- **Tmax**: 1.5-2 hours (peak plasma concentration time)
- **Half-life**: 6.6 hours
- **Mean residence time**: 10.0 hours
- **Steady-state concentration**: ~660 ng/mL (1.9 μM)
- **Plasma protein binding**: 55% (rat studies)

**Tee 2025** - Pharmacokinetics in healthy volunteers (fasting):
- **Doses tested**: 1,000 mg and 2,000 mg

**1,000 mg dose**:
- Andrographolide: Cmax 10.15 ng/mL, Tmax 1.5 hours
- Deoxyandrographolide: Cmax 7.02 ng/mL, Tmax 1.5 hours
- Neoandrographolide: Cmax 58.45 ng/mL, Tmax 1.5 hours

**2,000 mg dose**:
- Broader metabolic shifts
- Enhanced fatty acid and amino acid metabolism

### Human Studies - High-Dose Regimens

**Songvut 2023** - High-dose aqueous extract:
- **Single doses**: 60 mg and 120 mg andrographolide
- **Multiple doses**: 180 mg/day (60 mg × 3) and 360 mg/day (120 mg × 3)
- **Dose proportionality**: Twofold increase in dose → twofold higher plasma concentrations of four major diterpenoids and metabolites
- **Linearity**: Dose-proportional behavior observed
- **Safety**: Occasional adverse events at high doses were mild, infrequent, and reversible

**Songvut 2025** - Non-linear bioavailability study:
- **Doses**: 60 mg or 120 mg andrographolide per dose, every 8 hours
- **Duration**: 7 days
- **Total daily doses**: 180 mg/day or 360 mg/day

**Single-dose plasma concentrations**:
- 60 mg: Cmax 6.44 μg/L
- 120 mg: Cmax 6.97 μg/L (only marginal increase despite doubled dose)

**Repeated-dose plasma concentrations (Day 7)**:
- 60 mg × 3 daily: Cmax 11.62 μg/L
- 120 mg × 3 daily: Cmax 15.03 μg/L

**Key finding**: Non-linear proportionality - doubling the dose does NOT proportionally increase blood levels; low plasma levels of major diterpenoids even at high doses

**Suriyo 2017** - Very high crude herb dose:
- **Dose**: 12 capsules daily (4.2g total A. paniculata)
- **Schedule**: 4 capsules of 1.4g, three times daily at 8-hour intervals
- **Duration**: 3 consecutive days
- **Clinical parameters**: All within normal ranges after treatment
- **WBC and neutrophils**: Induction observed
- **Alkaline phosphatase**: Plasma reduction
- **Urine pH**: Elevation
- **Blood pressure**: Rapid, transient reduction at 30 minutes post-administration; significant mean systolic BP decrease
- **Safety**: No serious adverse events

## Bioavailability Challenges

### Poor Oral Bioavailability
- **Rat bioavailability**: ~1.19% (extremely low)
- **Issue**: Low water solubility limits absorption
- **Non-linear dose response**: Doubling dose does not double blood levels (Songvut 2025)
- **Panossian 2000 rat data**: Four-fold decrease in bioavailability at higher doses

### Peak Absorption Time
- **Tmax**: Consistently 1.5-2 hours across multiple studies
- **Absorption**: Rapid when it occurs
- **Half-life**: ~6.6 hours in humans

## Bioavailability Enhancement Strategies

**Songvut 2024** - Beagle dog study comparing formulations:

**Control**: A. paniculata powder alone (baseline)

**Enhanced formulations tested**:
1. A. paniculata with 50% w/w β-cyclodextrin
2. A. paniculata with 1% w/w sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS)
3. A. paniculata with 1% w/w SDS plus 10% piperine

**Results**:
- **β-cyclodextrin (50%)**: Increased systemic andrographolide exposure by 131.01%
- **SDS (1%)**: Increased systemic andrographolide exposure by up to 196.05%
- **Combined approach**: Solubilizing agents (β-cyclodextrin or SDS) significantly improve bioavailability versus plant powder alone

**Mechanism**: Improved solubility through complexation (β-cyclodextrin) and surfactant action (SDS)

## Clinical Implications

1. **Standardization matters**: 30% andrographolide standardization provides consistency across products
2. **Bioavailability is poor**: Low blood levels even with high doses suggest efficacy may involve:
   - Local gastrointestinal effects
   - Gut microbiome interactions
   - Active metabolites not measured in standard assays
   - Tissue distribution beyond plasma measurements
3. **More is not necessarily better**: Non-linear dose response means increasing dose does not proportionally increase systemic exposure
4. **Formulation strategies**: β-cyclodextrin or surfactant-enhanced formulations may improve clinical outcomes by increasing bioavailability
5. **Rapid onset**: Peak levels at 1.5-2 hours support traditional use for acute conditions
6. **Moderate duration**: 6.6-hour half-life requires multiple daily doses for sustained effect
