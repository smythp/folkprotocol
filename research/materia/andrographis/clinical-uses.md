# Andrographis - Clinical Uses

## Upper Respiratory Tract Infections (URTI)

### Meta-Analysis Evidence

**Hu 2017** - Systematic review and meta-analysis of 33 RCTs, 7,175 participants (PMID: 28783743):
- **Cough improvement**: SMD -0.39, 95% CI [-0.67, -0.10], n=596 participants
- **Sore throat improvement**: SMD -1.13, 95% CI [-1.37, -0.89], n=314 participants
- Adverse events mainly gastrointestinal, minimal
- **Limitation**: Poor study quality and heterogeneity limit confidence

**Poolsup 2004** - Systematic review of 4 RCTs, 433 participants analyzed (PMID: 14748896):
- **Andrographis alone**: Mean difference symptom score 10.85, 95% CI [10.36, 11.34], p<0.0001
- **Combination with Acanthopanax**: Mean difference 2.13, 95% CI [1.00, 3.26], p=0.0002
- **Conclusion**: May be appropriate alternative treatment of uncomplicated acute URTI

**Coon 2004** - Systematic review of 7 RCTs, 896 participants (PMID: 15095142):
- Superior to placebo in alleviating subjective URTI symptoms
- Adverse events generally mild and infrequent
- Preliminary evidence for preventative benefits

### Clinical Trial Dosing - URTI

**Melchior 2000** - Phase III RCT, 179 participants (PMID: 11081985):
- **Dose**: Kan Jang standardized extract (SHA-10), three times daily
- **Duration**: 3 days
- **Results**: Highly significant total symptom score improvement, p≤0.0006; diagnosis score p=0.003
- **Best response**: Throat symptoms showed most significant improvement in both pilot and Phase III studies

**Melchior 1997** - Pilot RCT, 50 participants (PMID: 23195187):
- **Product**: Kanjang® tablets (standardized extract)
- **Results**: Significantly reduced subjective symptoms, symptom duration, and sick leave period
- **Note**: Used in Nordic countries for 12+ years prior to publication with positive results

**Okonogi 2023** - RCT, 60 participants with acute viral pharyngitis (PMID: 37821383):
- **Dose**: Andrographis paniculata spray
- **Treatment response duration**: 1.9 ± 0.7 days (andrographis) vs 2.5 ± 1.2 days (chamomile), p=0.049
- **Symptom reduction**: Significant in both groups (p < 0.05)
- **Safety**: No adverse events noted in either group

### WHO Recommended Dosing - URTI

**WHO 2002 Monograph** (ISBN: 9789241545372):
- **Common cold**: 1.5-3.0g powdered crude drug, three times daily, after meals and at bedtime
- **Pyrexia**: 3g crude drug decoction, twice daily
- **Uncomplicated sinusitis, bronchitis, pharyngotonsillitis**: Same as common cold dosing

## COVID-19

### Meta-Analysis Evidence

**Prabhakornritta 2025** - Systematic review and meta-analysis (DOI: 10.3389/fphar.2025.1598255):
- **Trials**: 6 RCTs, 660 total participants; meta-analysis on 2 RCTs with 228 participants
- **Database search**: PubMed, EMBASE, CENTRAL, EBSCO Open Dissertations (January 2020 - October 2024)
- **Fever resolution**: RR 1.12, 95% CI [0.90, 1.38] - no significant difference vs antivirals
- **Cough resolution**: RR 0.98, 95% CI [0.74, 1.31] - no significant difference vs antivirals
- **Inflammatory markers**: Serum CRP mean difference -0.04, 95% CI [-0.26, 0.18]; IL-6 comparable
- **Symptom improvement**: Early improvements within 7 days diminished by day 14 in individual studies
- **Adverse events**: Mild, primarily transient liver enzyme elevations, no serious complications
- **Conclusion**: Trends indicating possible benefits as adjunctive COVID-19 therapy, but evidence remains inconclusive

### Individual COVID-19 Trials

**Kanokkangsadal 2023** - Double-blind RCT, 165 participants (PMID: 39005565):
- **Sample**: 83 treatment, 82 placebo
- **Dose**: 60 mg andrographolide three times daily (180 mg/day total) from ethanolic extract
- **Duration**: 5 days
- **Disease progression**: No significant reduction
- **Symptom relief**:
  - Headache significantly relieved at Day 1
  - Olfactory loss significantly relieved at Day 2
- **Recovery**: 80.7% total recovery after treatment
- **Safety**: Mild diarrhea (most common, resolved within days); no hepatic or renal toxicity

**Siripongboonsitti 2023** - Double-blind RCT, 146 participants (PMID: 37625206):
- **Sample**: 73 treatment, 73 placebo
- **Dose**: 180 mg/day andrographis extract plus favipiravir
- **Comparator**: Placebo plus favipiravir
- **Clinical progression prevention (Day 4)**: 98.63% (treatment) vs 97.26% (placebo), p=1.000
- **Interleukin-1β**: Significantly lower in extract group throughout study
- **Clinical benefit**: No additional clinical or virological benefits demonstrated beyond favipiravir
- **Safety**: No substantial adverse events

## Multiple Sclerosis

### MS Fatigue (Relapsing-Remitting)

**Bertoglio 2016** - Double-blind placebo-controlled pilot RCT (PMID: 27215274):
- **Sample**: 25 participants, 22 analyzed
- **Dose**: 170 mg dried extract twice daily (340 mg/day total)
- **Duration**: 12 months continuous use
- **Fatigue Severity Score**: **44% reduction** at 12 months versus placebo (significant)
- **Relapse rate**: No statistically significant differences
- **EDSS scores**: No statistically significant differences
- **New lesions**: Trend toward reduction in treatment group
- **Safety**: One patient experienced mild, transient skin rash managed with antihistamines
- **Tolerability**: Well tolerated overall over 12 months

### MS Brain Atrophy (Progressive MS)

**Ciampi 2020** - Prospective exploratory double-blind placebo-controlled RCT (PMID: 32380977):
- **Sample**: 44 participants (23 andrographolide, 21 placebo)
- **Condition**: Not active primary or secondary progressive MS
- **ClinicalTrials.gov**: NCT02273635
- **Dose**: 140 mg andrographolide twice daily (280 mg/day total)
- **Duration**: 24 months
- **Brain atrophy (annualized percentage brain volume change)**:
  - Andrographolide: -0.679%
  - Placebo: -1.069%
  - **Relative reduction**: 36.5%
  - p=0.08 (trending toward significance)
- **Disability progression**: 30% (andrographolide) vs 41% (placebo)
- **EDSS change**: -0.025 (andrographolide) vs +0.352 (placebo), p=0.042 ✓ significant
- **Safety**: Mild rash and dysgeusia (taste disturbance)
- **Tolerability**: Well tolerated
- **Conclusion**: Potential effect in reducing brain atrophy and disability progression; larger trial needed

## Rheumatoid Arthritis

**Burgos 2009** - Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT, 60 participants (PMID: 19408036):
- **Dose**: Andrographis extract tablets (Paractin), 30% total andrographolides, three times daily
- **Duration**: 14 weeks (with 2-week washout period before starting)
- **Tender joint count**: Decrease 0.13 per week, p=0.001
- **Swollen joint count**: Decrease 0.15 per week, p=0.02
- **Health Assessment Questionnaire**: Improvement 0.52 points, p<0.001
- **Quality of Life (SF-36)**: Improved, p<0.001
- **Biomarkers**: Reductions in rheumatoid factor, IgA, and C4
- **Conclusion**: Could be useful natural complement; larger trials needed

## Osteoarthritis

**Hancke 2019** - Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT, 103 participants (PMID: 30968986):
- **Condition**: Grade I-II osteoarthritis of knee joint
- **Doses**: ParActin® 300 mg/day (low dose) or 600 mg/day (high dose)
- **Duration**: 84 days (measurements at Days 28, 56, 84)
- **Pain reduction**: Significant in both treatment groups versus placebo at all timepoints
- **WOMAC stiffness**: Improved significantly in both treatment groups
- **Physical function**: Improved significantly in both treatment groups
- **Fatigue scores**: Meaningful improvement versus placebo
- **Quality of life**:
  - SF-36: Improved significantly
  - FACIT: Improved significantly
- **Conclusion**: Effective and safe in reducing pain in mild to moderate knee osteoarthritis

## Ulcerative Colitis

**Sandborn 2013** - Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT, 224 participants (PMID: 23044768):
- **Condition**: Mild-to-moderate ulcerative colitis
- **Doses**: 1,200 mg/day, 1,800 mg/day, or placebo (HMPL-004 extract)
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Clinical response (1,800 mg)**: **60%** (treatment) vs **40%** (placebo), p=0.0183
- **Clinical remission (1,800 mg)**: 38% (treatment) vs 25% (placebo)
- **Adverse events**: Comparable across all groups (53-60%)
- **Note**: Higher dose (1,800 mg/day) showed statistical significance; 1,200 mg/day did not

## Hypertriglyceridemia

**Phunikhom 2015** - Randomized controlled trial, 60 participants (PMID: 26434249):
- **Condition**: Hypertriglyceridemia
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Low-dose APE**: 71.64-72.36 mg/day andrographolide - **not statistically significant**
- **High-dose APE**: 119.64-120.36 mg/day andrographolide
  - **Triglyceride reduction**: 41.6 ± 86.3 mg/dL, p=0.0442 (significant)
- **Gemfibrozil comparator**: 300 mg/day
  - **Triglyceride reduction**: 57.1 ± 94.9 mg/dL, p=0.0145 (significant)
- **Conclusion**: High-dose APE (120 mg/day andrographolide) significantly reduced triglyceride levels, demonstrating potential lipid-lowering effects at high doses

## Immunomodulation (Healthy Adults)

**Rajanna 2021** - Open-label clinical study, 30 healthy adults (PMC: PMC8377179):
- **Dose**: 200 mg daily (two 100 mg capsules - morning and evening)
- **Duration**: 30 days
- **Clinic visits**: Baseline, Day 3, Day 7, Day 30

**Immune cell changes**:
- **NK cells**: Trend increase (p=0.45, not significant)
- **T cells (CD3+)**: 1915.2 → 2134.4 cells/μL (p=0.06, trending)
- **T helper cells (CD3+CD4+)**: 1058.4 → 1199.8 cells/μL (p=0.087, trending)

**Cytokine changes (highly significant)**:
- **IFN-γ**: 7.39 → 20.82 pg/mL (p=0.0001) ✓ highly significant increase
- **IL-4**: 1.55 → 3.46 pg/mL (p=0.0007) ✓ highly significant increase
- **IL-2**: 2.7 → 0.9 pg/mL (p<0.0001) ✓ highly significant decrease

**Safety**: No treatment-related adverse effects

**Conclusion**: Demonstrates balanced Th1/Th2 immunomodulatory effects (both IFN-γ and IL-4 increased) without adverse events

## Safety Profile Across Clinical Trials

### General Safety Meta-Analysis

**Worakunphanich 2021** - Meta-analysis of 13 studies (10 RCTs, 3 monitoring studies) (PMID: 33372366):
- **Serious adverse events**: 0.02 per 1,000 patients, 95% CI [0.0, 0.5] (extremely rare)
- **Non-serious adverse events**: 102.6 per 1,000 patients, 95% CI [10.7, 256.1] (frequent but mild)
- **Common side effects**: Gastrointestinal disorders, skin and subcutaneous disorders
- **Conclusion**: May be generally safe, though patients require close monitoring

### Herbal vs Injectable Forms

**Shang 2022** - Meta-analysis of 262 studies (PMID: 35153776):
- **Studies**: 125 RCTs, 23 non-randomized trials, 6 case series, 108 case reports

**Herbal preparations (oral)** - 10 studies, 9 formulations:
- **Safety profile**: Essentially safe
- **Adverse events**: Predominantly mild to moderate gastrointestinal and skin issues

**Injectable andrographolide derivatives** - 9,490 participants:
- **Overall ADR rate**: 4.04% (383 cases)
- **Specific rates**:
  - Andrographolide sulfonate: 5.48%
  - Potassium sodium dehydroandrographolide succinate: 3.69%
  - Potassium dehydroandrographolide succinate: 5.33%
- **Serious life-threatening events**:
  - Anaphylactic shock: **55 cases**
  - Deaths: **3 deaths**
- **Critical recommendation**: ADRs of andrographolide derivative medications are few but can be life-threatening. **AVOID INJECTABLE FORMS**. Herbal preparations are essentially safe.

### Pharmacological Safety from Animal Studies

**Tian 2022** - Comprehensive pharmacodynamics and toxicity testing in rodents and dogs (PMID: 35166368):
- **Acute toxicity**: Single dose of 10 g/kg produced no death or abnormal consequences; maximum tolerated dose 10 g/kg
- **Repeated dose toxicity**: 1 g/kg daily for 8 weeks showed no adverse effects in rodents and dogs
- **Mechanism of action**: Primary anti-inflammatory via IL-8 pathway; targets AR, AKT, BAX pathways
- **Conclusion**: Safe at therapeutic doses while supporting anti-inflammatory applications

### Genotoxicity Assessment

**Chandrasekaran 2009** - KalmCold standardized extract safety study (PMID: 19447157):
- **Ames test**: 5000-16 µg/mL - did not induce mutations with or without S9 activation
- **Chromosome aberration**: 80-5.1 µg/mL - no clastogenic effects in cultured cells
- **Micronucleus test**: No genotoxic effects
- **Acute oral toxicity**: 5000 mg/kg in female rats, 14-day observation - no treatment-related toxic effects
- **Conclusion**: KalmCold is genotoxically safe and well-tolerated in acute exposure

**Worasuttayangkurn 2019** - Acute oral toxicity (OECD Guideline No. 420) (PMID: 31193040):
- **Test article**: Standardized first true leaf ethanolic extract (FTLEE)
- **Doses**: 0, 300, 2000, 5000 mg/kg body weight (single oral administration)
- **Survival**: All treated animals survived
- **Adverse effects**: No apparent adverse effects observed
- **Organ examination**: No lesions detected
- **LD50**: Not established; **no toxicity at maximum tested dose (5000 mg/kg)**
- **Conclusion**: Standardized FTLEE demonstrates high safety margin in acute exposure

## Evidence Quality Summary

**Most Robust Evidence**:
1. **URTI symptom relief** - Multiple RCTs, meta-analyses, 7,175 participants total
2. **Safety profile** - Meta-analysis of 13 studies showing extremely rare serious AEs
3. **Anti-inflammatory effects** - Supported by biomarker data across multiple conditions

**Emerging Evidence**:
1. **Rheumatoid arthritis** - 1 RCT (60 participants), significant joint improvement
2. **Ulcerative colitis** - 1 large RCT (224 participants), significant clinical response
3. **Osteoarthritis** - 1 RCT (103 participants), significant pain reduction
4. **MS fatigue** - 1 pilot RCT (22 participants), 44% fatigue reduction
5. **MS brain atrophy** - 1 RCT (44 participants), 36.5% reduction in atrophy, EDSS improvement p=0.042
6. **Immunomodulation** - 1 open-label study (30 participants), significant cytokine changes

**Inconclusive Evidence**:
1. **COVID-19** - Meta-analysis of 6 RCTs shows trends but evidence remains inconclusive
2. **Preventive use** - Preliminary evidence mentioned but not well-established

**Research Gaps Identified** (Zeng 2022 review):
- Current extensive clinical data lack sufficient rigor and documented details
- Poor study quality and heterogeneity in many trials limit confidence
- Higher-quality investigations required before full clinical integration
- Need for larger, standardized RCTs
- Long-term safety data needed (especially nephrotoxicity and reproductive toxicity concerns)
