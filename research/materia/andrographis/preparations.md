# Andrographis - Preparations & Dosing

## Crude Herb Preparations

### Decoction (Boiled Tea)

**WHO 2002 Traditional Dosing**:

**For Pyrexia (Fever)**:
- **Amount**: 3g crude dried herb
- **Preparation**: Decoction (boiled in water)
- **Frequency**: Twice daily
- **Total daily dose**: 6g crude herb

**For Acute Diarrhea**:
- **Amount**: 3-9g crude dried herb
- **Preparation**: Decoction (boiled in water)
- **Frequency**: As single dose, as needed
- **Note**: Wide dose range allows titration based on severity

### Powdered Crude Herb

**For Common Cold (WHO 2002)**:
- **Amount**: 1.5-3.0g powdered crude drug
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Timing**: After meals and at bedtime
- **Total daily dose**: 4.5-9.0g

**Preparation method**: Dried aerial parts ground to fine powder; can be taken in capsules, mixed with water, or as tea.

### Simple Tablet Form (Crude Herb)

**For Diarrhea (WHO 2002 alternative)**:
- **Strength**: 500mg per tablet
- **Dose**: 2 tablets
- **Frequency**: Four times daily
- **Timing**: After meals
- **Total daily dose**: 4g (8 tablets)

**Preparation**: Compressed powdered crude herb or minimally processed extract.

## Standardized Extracts

### General Standardization Level
**Industry standard**: 30% total andrographolides
- Provides 300mg andrographolides per 1g extract
- Used in most modern clinical trials
- Ensures batch-to-batch consistency

### Upper Respiratory Tract Infections

**Low-dose standardized extract**:
- **Product example**: Kan Jang (SHA-10 standardized extract)
- **Dose**: 20 mg andrographolide per dose
- **Frequency**: Three times daily (4 tablets per dose in Panossian 2000 study)
- **Total daily andrographolide**: ~60 mg
- **Duration**: Typically 3-8 days
- **Evidence**: Multiple RCTs (Melchior 1997, 2000; Panossian 2000)

**Moderate-dose standardized extract**:
- **Dose**: 60 mg andrographolide per dose
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Total daily andrographolide**: 180 mg
- **Duration**: 5 days (COVID-19 studies); 3-8 days (URTI)
- **Evidence**: Kanokkangsadal 2023, Siripongboonsitti 2023

**High-dose for acute use**:
- **Dose**: 120 mg andrographolide per dose
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Total daily andrographolide**: 360 mg
- **Duration**: 5-7 days
- **Evidence**: Songvut 2023, Songvut 2025
- **Safety**: Tolerated without serious adverse events

### Topical Spray - Pharyngitis

**Okonogi 2023 formulation**:
- **Type**: Andrographis paniculata spray
- **Application**: Direct throat spray
- **Condition**: Acute viral pharyngitis
- **Results**: Treatment response in 1.9 ± 0.7 days (faster than chamomile control)
- **Safety**: No adverse events noted

**Preparation details**: Not specified in study; likely liquid extract suspended in spray base.

### Multiple Sclerosis - Fatigue

**Bertoglio 2016 protocol**:
- **Form**: Dried extract tablet
- **Dose**: 170 mg per dose
- **Frequency**: Twice daily
- **Total daily dose**: 340 mg dried extract
- **Duration**: 12 months (long-term use)
- **Standardization**: Not specified; likely 30% andrographolides = ~102 mg andrographolides/day
- **Safety**: Well tolerated over 12 months; one mild transient skin rash

### Rheumatoid Arthritis

**Burgos 2009 protocol (Paractin)**:
- **Form**: Extract tablets
- **Standardization**: 30% total andrographolides
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Duration**: 14 weeks
- **Washout**: 2-week washout period before starting
- **Total daily andrographolides**: Not specified (dose per tablet not given)

### Osteoarthritis

**Hancke 2019 protocol (ParActin®)**:
- **Form**: Standardized extract
- **Low dose**: 300 mg/day
- **High dose**: 600 mg/day
- **Standardization**: Proprietary (ParActin®); likely 30% andrographolides
- **Duration**: 84 days (12 weeks)
- **Assessment timepoints**: Days 28, 56, 84
- **Results**: Both doses effective; no clear dose-response difference

### Ulcerative Colitis

**Sandborn 2013 protocol (HMPL-004)**:
- **Form**: Standardized extract capsules
- **Low dose**: 1,200 mg/day
- **High dose**: 1,800 mg/day
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Effective dose**: 1,800 mg/day showed statistical significance
- **Clinical response**: 60% at 1,800 mg vs 40% placebo (p=0.0183)
- **Standardization**: HMPL-004 proprietary formulation

## Tincture Preparations

**Note**: No clinical trials in the reviewed literature used tincture (alcohol extract) preparations. All trials used either:
- Aqueous extracts (water-based)
- Ethanolic extracts (ethanol-based, then dried to powder/tablet)
- Crude herb decoctions or powders

**Theoretical tincture ratios** (based on traditional practice, not clinical evidence):
- **Fresh herb tincture**: 1:2 ratio (herb:menstruum), 75-95% alcohol
- **Dried herb tincture**: 1:5 ratio (herb:menstruum), 60-75% alcohol
- **Dose**: 2-4 mL, three times daily
- **Note**: Extremely bitter; may be challenging for patient compliance

**Why tinctures are uncommon**: Andrographolide has poor water solubility but is alcohol-soluble. However, standardized dried extracts are preferred in modern practice for:
- Precise dosing of andrographolides
- Better patient compliance (tablets vs bitter liquid)
- Clinical evidence base (trials use tablets/capsules)

## Tea Preparations (Infusion vs. Decoction)

**Traditional method**: Decoction (boiling)
- **Reason**: Andrographolides are not highly water-soluble; boiling extracts more compounds than steeping
- **Method**: Simmer 3g dried herb in 250-500 mL water for 15-20 minutes
- **Frequency**: Twice to three times daily
- **Taste**: Extremely bitter (common name: "King of Bitters")

**Simple infusion (steeping)**: Less traditional but simpler
- **Method**: Steep 1.5-3g dried herb in boiled water for 10-15 minutes
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Efficacy**: Likely lower andrographolide extraction than decoction
- **Note**: No clinical trials validate infusion vs. decoction

## Bioavailability-Enhanced Formulations

**Songvut 2024 experimental formulations** (beagle dog study):

**β-Cyclodextrin complex**:
- **Ratio**: 50% w/w β-cyclodextrin with A. paniculata powder
- **Bioavailability increase**: +131.01%
- **Mechanism**: Complexation improves solubility
- **Status**: Experimental; not yet in commercial products

**Sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS) formulation**:
- **Ratio**: 1% w/w SDS with A. paniculata powder
- **Bioavailability increase**: +196.05%
- **Mechanism**: Surfactant improves dissolution
- **Status**: Experimental; not yet in commercial products

**SDS + Piperine combination**:
- **SDS**: 1% w/w
- **Piperine (bioenhancer)**: 10%
- **Status**: Under investigation
- **Rationale**: Combined solubilization and bioenhancement

**Clinical implications**: Future formulations may incorporate these strategies to improve efficacy at lower doses.

## Practical Preparation Guidelines

### For Acute Respiratory Infections (Short-term use, ≤7 days):

**Option 1 - Standardized extract (preferred)**:
- 60 mg andrographolide, three times daily (180 mg total)
- Take after meals to reduce GI upset
- Continue for 3-5 days or until symptoms resolve

**Option 2 - Crude herb decoction**:
- 3g dried aerial parts
- Simmer in 250 mL water for 15-20 minutes
- Strain and drink twice daily
- May add honey to offset extreme bitterness

**Option 3 - Powdered crude herb in capsules**:
- 1.5-3.0g in capsules
- Three times daily, after meals
- Total daily dose: 4.5-9g

### For Chronic Inflammatory Conditions (Long-term use, >4 weeks):

**Standardized extract tablets**:
- 300-600 mg extract (30% andrographolides = 90-180 mg)
- Once or divided into 2-3 doses daily
- Take with food
- Monitor for GI effects, skin reactions
- Consider kidney function monitoring if using >3 months

### For Acute Diarrhea:

**Crude herb decoction**:
- 3-9g dried herb (start with 3g)
- Boil in water for 15-20 minutes
- Single dose, repeat as needed
- Do NOT exceed 9g per dose

**Alternative - tablets**:
- 500mg crude herb tablets
- 2 tablets, four times daily after meals
- Total daily: 4g (8 tablets)

## Storage and Shelf Life

**Crude dried herb**:
- Store in airtight container, cool, dark, dry location
- Shelf life: 1-2 years if properly stored
- Signs of degradation: Loss of bitter taste, color fading, musty smell

**Standardized extract tablets/capsules**:
- Store in original container, cool, dry location
- Shelf life: Typically 2-3 years (check manufacturer)
- Andrographolide content may degrade over time if exposed to heat/moisture

**Decoctions and infusions**:
- Prepare fresh daily
- Do not store >24 hours (refrigerated)
- Microbial contamination risk if stored

## Patient Compliance Considerations

**Taste challenge**: Andrographis is extremely bitter
- **Solutions**: Capsules/tablets preferred over teas
- Add honey or mix with fruit juice if using liquid
- Take with meals to mask taste and reduce GI upset

**Dosing frequency**: Three times daily can be challenging
- **Solutions**: Set alarms, link to mealtimes
- Twice-daily formulations exist (e.g., Bertoglio 2016: 170 mg BID)
- Higher-dose once-daily not yet validated in trials

**Duration expectations**:
- **Acute URTI**: 3-5 days (short commitment)
- **Chronic conditions**: 8-14 weeks minimum (requires motivation)
- **Set realistic expectations**: Symptom relief may take days to weeks
