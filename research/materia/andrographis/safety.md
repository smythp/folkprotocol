# Andrographis - Safety Profile

## Contraindications

### Absolute Contraindications

**WHO 2002 Monograph**:
1. **Pregnancy** - Contraindicated
2. **Lactation** - Contraindicated

**Evidence basis**: Traditional safety concerns and lack of adequate safety data in pregnant/lactating populations.

## Adverse Events Profile

### Meta-Analysis - General Safety

**Worakunphanich 2021** - Systematic review and meta-analysis:
- **Studies included**: 13 studies (10 RCTs, 3 monitoring studies)

**Serious adverse events**:
- **Incidence**: 0.02 per 1,000 patients
- **95% CI**: [0.0, 0.5]
- **Interpretation**: Extremely rare

**Non-serious adverse events**:
- **Incidence**: 102.6 per 1,000 patients
- **95% CI**: [10.7, 256.1]
- **Interpretation**: Frequent but mild

**Common side effects**:
- Gastrointestinal disorders (most common)
- Skin and subcutaneous disorders

**Conclusion**: May be generally safe, though patients require close monitoring.

### Injectable Derivatives - SERIOUS SAFETY CONCERNS

**Shang 2022** - Meta-analysis of 262 studies:

**Herbal preparations (oral)** - 10 studies:
- **Safety profile**: Essentially safe
- **Adverse events**: Predominantly mild to moderate gastrointestinal and skin issues

**Andrographolide derivative injections** - 9,490 participants:
- **Overall ADR rate**: 4.04% (383 cases)

**Specific injectable forms**:
- Andrographolide sulfonate: 5.48% ADR rate
- Potassium sodium dehydroandrographolide succinate: 3.69% ADR rate
- Potassium dehydroandrographolide succinate: 5.33% ADR rate

**Serious life-threatening events**:
- **Anaphylactic shock**: 55 cases
- **Deaths**: 3 deaths attributed to injectable derivatives

**Critical recommendation**: ADRs of andrographolide derivative medications are few but can be life-threatening. **AVOID INJECTABLE FORMS**. Herbal preparations of Andrographis paniculata are essentially safe.

## Common Adverse Effects (Oral Herbal Preparations)

### Gastrointestinal Effects
- **Most common**: Gastrointestinal disorders
- **Specific**: Mild diarrhea
- **Characteristics**: Resolves within days, generally mild intensity

**Kanokkangsadal 2023 COVID-19 trial**:
- Mild diarrhea was most common adverse effect
- Resolved within days without intervention

**Sandborn 2013 ulcerative colitis trial**:
- Adverse events 53-60% across all groups (including placebo)
- Comparable rates between treatment and placebo groups

### Dermatological Effects
- **Type**: Skin and subcutaneous disorders
- **Characteristics**: Generally mild and transient

**Bertoglio 2016 MS fatigue trial**:
- One patient: mild, transient skin rash
- Management: Antihistamines
- Resolution: Complete

### Cardiovascular Effects

**Suriyo 2017** - High-dose study (4.2g/day crude herb × 3 days):
- **Blood pressure**: Rapid, transient reduction at 30 minutes post-administration
- **Effect**: Significant mean systolic BP decrease
- **Duration**: Transient (short-lived)
- **Clinical significance**: All clinical parameters remained within normal ranges

## High-Dose Safety Data

**Songvut 2023** - 180-360 mg/day andrographolide:
- **Adverse events**: Occasional, mild intensity, infrequent, reversible
- **Conclusion**: High doses tolerated without serious adverse events

**Kanokkangsadal 2023** - 180 mg/day × 5 days:
- **Hepatic toxicity**: None observed
- **Renal toxicity**: None observed
- **Adverse events**: Mild diarrhea (most common)

**Suriyo 2017** - 4.2g/day crude herb × 3 days:
- **Clinical parameters**: All within normal ranges after treatment
- **White blood cells**: Induction of WBC and neutrophil count (immunomodulatory effect, not adverse)
- **Alkaline phosphatase**: Plasma reduction
- **Urine pH**: Elevation
- **Serious adverse events**: None during treatment period

## Easily Overlooked Safety Concerns

**Zeng 2022** - Comprehensive review of 226 references:

**Nephrotoxicity**:
- **Status**: "Easily ignored" concern
- **Evidence**: Noted in comprehensive reviews
- **Recommendation**: Monitoring kidney function in long-term use may be prudent

**Reproductive toxicity**:
- **Status**: "Easily ignored" concern
- **Context**: Aligns with traditional contraindication in pregnancy
- **Evidence**: Preclinical data suggest reproductive concerns
- **Clinical relevance**: Supports absolute contraindication in pregnancy and lactation

**Clinical data quality**:
- "Current extensive clinical data do not have sufficient rigor and documented details"
- "Higher-quality investigations required before full clinical integration"

## Special Populations

### HIV Patients
**Safety concern noted**:
- One anaphylactic reaction reported in HIV trial
- Trial interrupted at 6 weeks
- **Context**: Unclear if reaction related to herb, HIV status, or drug interactions

## Drug Interactions

**No specific drug interactions documented in reviewed clinical trials.**

**Theoretical concerns** (based on pharmacology):
- **Anticoagulants**: Unknown interaction potential; traditional bitter herbs sometimes affect coagulation
- **Antihypertensives**: Transient BP-lowering effect observed (Suriyo 2017); monitor if combining
- **Immunosuppressants**: Immunomodulatory effects (WBC increase) could theoretically interact; clinical significance unknown

**Note**: Absence of documented interactions does NOT mean interactions don't exist - reflects lack of systematic study rather than proven safety.

## Laboratory Effects (Not Adverse)

**Suriyo 2017** findings that may appear on labs but are not adverse:
- **WBC and neutrophil increase**: Likely immunomodulatory effect, not infection
- **Alkaline phosphatase reduction**: Clinical significance unclear
- **Urine pH elevation**: Clinical significance unclear

## Monitoring Recommendations

Based on safety profile:

1. **Gastrointestinal symptoms**: Expected in ~10% of patients; generally mild and self-limiting
2. **Skin reactions**: Rare; manage with antihistamines if occur
3. **Kidney function**: Consider monitoring in long-term use (>3 months) due to nephrotoxicity concerns
4. **Liver function**: Generally safe; COVID-19 trials showed no hepatotoxicity, but long-term data limited
5. **Blood pressure**: Transient reduction possible; monitor in patients on antihypertensives

## Absolute Avoidance

**NEVER use**:
1. Injectable andrographolide derivatives (high risk of anaphylaxis and death)
2. During pregnancy (traditional contraindication + reproductive toxicity concerns)
3. During lactation (traditional contraindication + lack of safety data)

## Safety Comparison: Oral vs. Injectable

**Oral herbal preparations**:
- Serious AEs: 0.02 per 1,000 (extremely rare)
- Deaths: 0 in reviewed studies
- Profile: Mild GI and skin effects, generally safe

**Injectable derivatives**:
- Overall ADR: 4.04% (40.4 per 1,000)
- Anaphylactic shock: 55 cases
- Deaths: 3
- Profile: Life-threatening reactions possible

**Risk ratio**: Injectable forms are approximately **2,000 times more likely** to cause serious adverse events than oral herbal preparations.

## Overall Safety Assessment

**Short-term use (≤3 months)**:
- Generally safe in non-pregnant, non-lactating adults
- Mild, self-limiting adverse effects in ~10% of users
- Serious adverse events extremely rare with oral herbal preparations

**Long-term use (>3 months)**:
- Limited data available
- Consider monitoring kidney function
- One 12-month trial (Bertoglio 2016 MS study) showed good tolerability

**High-quality evidence needs**:
- Systematic drug interaction studies
- Long-term safety monitoring (>1 year)
- Clarification of nephrotoxicity and reproductive toxicity concerns
- Safety in special populations (elderly, children, immunocompromised)
