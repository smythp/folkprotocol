# Skullcap - Clinical Uses

**Species note:** Two distinct species are commonly sold as "skullcap":
- *Scutellaria lateriflora* (American skullcap) — primarily studied for anxiety/sleep/mood
- *Scutellaria baicalensis* (Chinese/Baical skullcap, Huang Qin) — primarily TCM anti-inflammatory use

Clinical RCT evidence is largely limited to *S. lateriflora*. Most *S. baicalensis* evidence comes from preclinical studies and TCM practice.

---

## Sleep Disorders (S. lateriflora)

### Di Minno et al. 2025 — Primary Insomnia RCT
- **Sample:** 66 adults with mild to moderate primary insomnia (29F/37M, mean age 44±15 years)
- **Dose:** 400 mg/day hydroethanolic extract standardized to 10% baicalin (BlueCALM)
- **Comparator:** Placebo
- **Duration:** 56 days per arm + 28-day washout
- **Design:** Single-center, randomized, crossover, double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **PMID:** 40362800

**Primary Outcome — PSQI Total Score:**
- Supplement: decreased from 13.0±2.9 (baseline) to 9.5±3.5 (endpoint)
- Placebo: deteriorated from 11.5±3.0 to 13.3±3.0
- Between-group difference: p<0.001

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- **Sleep Onset Latency:** reduced 0.82 min with supplement vs increased 0.37 min with placebo
- **Sleep Efficiency:** increased ~9% with supplement vs minimal change with placebo
- **Total Sleep Time:** increased 1.22 hours with supplement
- **Visual Analog Scale:** significantly improved (p<0.001)

**Safety:** No adverse effects reported; physicians assessed supplement as well-tolerated.

**Clinical Significance:** The most methodologically robust RCT for *S. lateriflora*; demonstrates clinically meaningful sleep improvements in insomnia. Effect size substantial: 3.5-point PSQI reduction (vs 1.8-point threshold for clinical significance) and +1.22 hours total sleep time.

## Mood and Anxiety (S. lateriflora)

### Brock et al. 2014 — Mood in Healthy Volunteers
- **Sample:** 43 healthy volunteers (81% with mild anxiety or less at baseline)
- **Dose:** 350 mg *S. lateriflora* three times daily (1050 mg/day)
- **Comparator:** Placebo
- **Duration:** 2 weeks per arm
- **Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover
- **PMID:** 23878109

**Primary Outcome — Total Mood Disturbance (POMS):**
- Skullcap: highly significant decrease (p<0.001)
- Placebo: no significant change (p=0.072)

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- **Anxiety (BAI):** No significant difference between treatment and placebo (p=0.191)
- **Energy/Cognition:** No reduction — herb improved mood without sedation

**Limitations:**
- Carryover effects possible (crossover design)
- Low baseline anxiety (81% mild or less) reduced statistical power for anxiety measures
- Baseline group differences in anxiety levels (p=0.022)

**Clinical Significance:** Significant global mood enhancement without sedation or cognitive impairment. Anxiolytic effect was not demonstrated on validated anxiety scales, suggesting primary benefit is mood rather than anxiety per se.

---

### Wolfson & Hoffmann 2003 — Anxiolytic Effects
- **Sample:** Healthy subjects (exact n not specified in abstract)
- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover
- **PMID:** 12652886

**Key Findings:**
- Demonstrated noteworthy anxiolytic effects in healthy subjects
- Reviewed published literature on *S. lateriflora* putative toxicity

**Clinical Significance:** Earliest published RCT on *S. lateriflora*; established initial evidence for anxiolytic activity; full methodological detail requires full-text review.

## Oral Health / Gingivitis (S. lateriflora)

### Di Minno et al. 2024 — Gingivitis RCT
- **Sample:** 60 adults with gingivitis (30 per group, aged 18–70 years)
- **Intervention:** Two chewing gums daily containing 160 mg combined *S. lateriflora* + *Cistus × incanus* hydroalcoholic extract (administered at 11 AM and 5 PM)
- **Comparator:** Placebo chewing gum
- **Duration:** 3 months
- **Design:** Monocentric, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group
- **PMID:** 38542772

**Primary Outcomes:**
- **QGBI (gingival bleeding index):** Significantly decreased in treatment group (β = 0.6, p<0.001)
- **MGI (modified gingival index):** Significantly decreased (β = 0.87, p<0.001)
- **OH-15 (oral health quality of life):** Improved (β = 5.3, p=0.038)

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- **VAS (pain):** Significantly reduced (β = 3.16, p<0.001)
- **CGI-S (clinical global impression):** Improved (β = 1.09, p<0.001)

**Safety:** No adverse reactions reported; well tolerated over 3 months.

**Limitations:** Combination product (*S. lateriflora* + *Cistus × incanus*); cannot isolate effect of *S. lateriflora* alone. Novel oral-health indication with no prior precedent for this species.

## Stress, Mood, and Sleep (S. baicalensis)

### Dodd et al. 2025 — Stressed Adults RCT
- **Sample:** 35 analyzed (43 enrolled); stressed healthy adults, aged 18–75 years
- **Intervention:** Two tablets daily containing *S. baicalensis* root extract + *Crataegus laevigata* fruit extract + magnesium + chromium
- **Comparator:** Placebo
- **Duration:** 15 days per arm plus acute dosing phase
- **Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover
- **PMID:** 41194549

**Acute dose outcomes:**
- Reduced serial 3's subtraction errors (improved attention/working memory)

**15-day chronic outcomes:**
- Increased correct serial 7's subtractions during stressor task
- Improved mood; reduced trait anxiety scores
- Reduced total mood disturbance and anger/hostility
- Enhanced total sleep time and overall sleep quality

**Safety:** No serious adverse effects reported.

**Limitations:** Combination product; individual *S. baicalensis* dose not specified; effects cannot be attributed to *S. baicalensis* alone. Small sample (n=35).

## Headache / Migraine (S. baicalensis) — Epidemiological

### Liao et al. 2021 — Taiwan NHIRD Database Analysis
- **Database:** Taiwan National Health Insurance Research Database (LHID 2000 — 1,000,000-person random sample, 1997–2013)
- **Design:** Epidemiological database analysis + preclinical animal study
- **PMID:** 33505504

**Database findings:**
- *S. baicalensis* prescribed for headache: **N=4,849 cases** (2.51% of all clinical applications in database)
- **4th most common clinical indication** for *S. baicalensis* in real-world Taiwan TCM practice
- Confirms frequent use for migraine/headache across 16 years of NHIRD data

**Preclinical component:**
- Nitroglycerin-induced rat migraine model (NTG 10 mg/kg i.p.)
- Pretreatment with 1.0 g/kg *S. baicalensis* reduced migraine-associated behavioral changes (locomotor, tactile sensitivity)

**Limitations:** No randomized clinical component; database analysis provides real-world usage epidemiology only, not efficacy data.

## Overall Clinical Evidence Assessment

### Strengths
1. 2025 RCT (n=66) is well-designed with crossover and objective sleep measures
2. Consistent signal for mood/anxiety benefit across multiple small studies
3. No sedation-related impairment documented in trials
4. Good short-term tolerability in all RCTs

### Limitations
1. **Very small evidence base:** Only 2-3 RCTs total; largest n=66
2. **No long-term RCTs** (longest is 56-day crossover)
3. **Healthy volunteer populations** — limited applicability to clinical populations
4. **Dosing inconsistency** across studies
5. **No systematic review** specifically for *S. lateriflora* RCTs identified
6. **No pharmacokinetic data** for whole herb in humans

### Clinical Bottom Line

**Sleep (Primary Insomnia):** 400 mg/day standardized extract (10% baicalin) for 56 days: PSQI decreased 3.5 points, total sleep time increased 1.22 hours (p<0.001, n=66). Best-evidenced indication.

**Global Mood:** 1050 mg/day (350 mg × 3/day) for 2 weeks: significant improvement in Total Mood Disturbance without cognitive impairment (n=43).

**Anxiety:** Evidence for anxiolytic effect exists but is not robust on validated anxiety measures; primary signal appears to be mood enhancement rather than anxiety reduction per validated tools.

**Oral Health:** 3-month RCT (n=60) demonstrates gingivitis improvement with *S. lateriflora* combination chewing gum, but combination product design prevents species-specific attribution. Novel indication.

**Stress/Mood/Sleep (S. baicalensis combination):** 15-day RCT (n=35) with *S. baicalensis* combination product showed improvements across mood, anxiety, and sleep; individual herb contribution unclear.

**Headache (S. baicalensis TCM):** Taiwan NHIRD data (N=4,849) confirms headache/migraine as the 4th most common real-world indication — epidemiological context only, not efficacy evidence.

**Evidence grade:** Limited but positive for sleep and mood (*S. lateriflora*); inadequate evidence to confirm anxiolytic effect on standardized measures; new indications (oral health, headache) require dedicated RCTs.
