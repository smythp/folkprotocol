# Skullcap - Preparations and Dosing

---

## S. lateriflora (American Skullcap)

### Traditional Dosing

**Dried herb (aerial parts):**
- 1–2 g, three times daily
- Daily total: 3–6 g
- Use: teas, capsules, or tablets

**Tincture (1:5, 45% ethanol):**
- 2–4 mL, three times daily
- Daily total: 6–12 mL
- Traditional Western herbalism standard

**Fluid extract (1:1):**
- 2–4 mL, three times daily
- Daily total: 6–12 mL
- Stronger preparation; equivalent to more crude herb per dose

### Practitioner Survey Dosing (Brock et al. 2012)
Survey of UK/Ireland registered herbal practitioners (NIMH members, n=62):
- Commonly prescribed as both standalone and in formula
- Primary use: anxiety, stress, spasms, digestive disorders, hypertension
- Exact dosing not standardized across practitioners

---

## Clinical Trial Dosing (S. lateriflora)

### Sleep (Di Minno et al. 2025)
- **Product:** BlueCALM — hydroethanolic extract, standardized to 10% baicalin
- **Dose:** 400 mg/day (single daily dose)
- **Duration:** 56 days
- **Outcome:** PSQI reduced 3.5 points; total sleep time increased 1.22 hours (p<0.001)
- **Note:** 400 mg is substantially lower than traditional crude herb doses; reflects concentration in standardized extract

### Mood in Healthy Adults (Brock et al. 2014)
- **Product:** *S. lateriflora* whole herb capsules (dried)
- **Dose:** 350 mg, three times daily (1050 mg/day total)
- **Duration:** 2 weeks
- **Outcome:** Significant improvement in Total Mood Disturbance (POMS, p<0.001)

## S. baicalensis (Chinese Skullcap / Huang Qin)

### Traditional TCM Dosing

**Dried root (decoction):**
- 3–9 g per dose (standard TCM range)
- WHO monograph standard: 3–9 g
- Typically prepared as multi-herb decoction

**Extended decoction:**
- 9–15 g for more intensive use
- Boiled 20–30 minutes with other herbs

**Note:** TCM dosing is formula-dependent; *S. baicalensis* is rarely used alone in classical practice.

### Clinical Trial Dosing (S. baicalensis Whole Herb)

**Hepatic safety study (Puri et al. 2019):**
- 1335 mg dried root daily
- Duration: average 444 days (~14.8 months)
- No LFT abnormalities in 17 participants

---

## Isolated Constituent Dosing (Baicalein from S. baicalensis)

### Phase I Safety Range (Li et al. 2014)
- Single dose: 100–2800 mg oral baicalein
- All doses safe and well-tolerated in Phase I study
- 72 healthy subjects

### Clinical Multiple Dose Studies
- 200, 400, 800 mg daily (Pang et al. 2016 — 200, 400, 800 mg once days 1+10; twice daily days 3–9)
- 200, 400, 600 mg daily (Li et al. 2021 — once days 1+10; three-times-daily days 4–9)
- All doses well-tolerated; steady state achieved by day 8
- No serious adverse events in any dose group

## Standardization

### S. lateriflora Standardization

**Primary marker:** Baicalin
- Clinical use: 10% baicalin (BlueCALM extract, Di Minno 2025)
- Other common markers: scutellarin, baicalein, wogonoside

**Authentication (critical for quality):**
- Germander (*Teucrium canadense*) adulteration is a historical problem
- AHP HPLC method: ajugapitin absent in all 7 authenticated *S. lateriflora* samples
- Adulteration with germander may be responsible for some reported hepatotoxicity
- Purchase only from suppliers with authentication certificates

**Bioavailability consideration:**
- In vitro data suggests oroxylin A glucuronide may be the primary systemically bioavailable constituent
- Standardization to baicalin may not reflect the most pharmacologically active fraction
- Human PK studies for whole *S. lateriflora* herb are absent — this is a major evidence gap

### S. baicalensis Standardization

**Primary marker:** Baicalin
- European Pharmacopoeia method available since 2010
- WHO Monograph Volume 3 (2007) provides quality standards

**Bioavailability consideration:**
- Baicalein aglycone is more bioavailable than baicalin glucuronide
- Low aqueous solubility of both compounds limits absorption
- Preparations formulated as baicalein or with bioavailability enhancers may be superior to baicalin-standardized products

## Preparation Selection Guide

| Preparation | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dried herb tea (S. lateriflora) | 1–2 g × 3/day | Traditional; pleasant to drink; cost-effective |
| Tincture 1:5, 45% EtOH (S. lateriflora) | 2–4 mL × 3/day | Easy dose adjustment; fast absorption |
| Fluid extract 1:1 (S. lateriflora) | 2–4 mL × 3/day | More concentrated; useful for lower volume |
| Standardized extract 10% baicalin (S. lateriflora) | 400 mg/day | Evidence-based for sleep (Di Minno 2025); reliable dosing |
| Whole herb capsules (S. lateriflora) | 350 mg × 3/day | Evidence-based for mood (Brock 2014) |
| Dried root (S. baicalensis) | 3–9 g/day as decoction | TCM traditional preparation |
| Baicalein tablets | 200–800 mg/day | Isolated constituent; superior bioavailability to baicalin |

## Tea Preparation

### S. lateriflora Infusion
- **Herb quantity:** 1–2 g dried aerial parts per cup (approximately 1 teaspoon)
- **Water:** 200–250 mL just-boiled water
- **Steep time:** 10–15 minutes covered
- **Frequency:** 2–3 cups daily
- **Daily dose equivalent:** 2–6 g dried herb
- **Notes:** Mild, slightly bitter taste; may be combined with valerian or lemon balm for synergistic sleep/anxiety effect

### S. baicalensis Decoction
- **Herb quantity:** 3–9 g dried root
- **Water:** 300–400 mL water
- **Method:** Bring to boil, simmer 20–30 minutes covered
- **Yield:** Approximately 150–200 mL
- **Frequency:** Once or divided twice daily
- **Notes:** Typically combined with other TCM herbs; strong bitter taste

## Combination Use

### Traditional Western Combinations (S. lateriflora)
- **With valerian:** Synergistic sleep support; commonly combined
- **With passionflower:** Anxiety and nervous restlessness; complementary nervines
- **With lemon balm:** Nervous tension, stress; gentle combination
- **With hops:** Sleep; traditional bedtime formula

### TCM Combinations (S. baicalensis)
- *Xiao Chai Hu Tang* (with bupleurum, ginger, ginseng): liver/gallbladder conditions
- *Huang Lian Jie Du Tang* (with coptis, phellodendron, gardenia): severe Heat toxin
- *Yin Qiao San* derivative formulas: febrile/upper respiratory conditions

## Dosing Duration

**Short-term (evidence-based):**
- 2 weeks: mood studies (Brock 2014)
- Up to 56 days: sleep studies (Di Minno 2025)

**Medium-term (traditional):**
- Ongoing as needed for anxiety/stress symptoms
- No maximum duration established in traditional sources

**Long-term safety data:**
- 444 days (*S. baicalensis* 1335 mg/day): no LFT changes in 17 participants (Puri 2019)
- Monitoring recommended regardless of duration given hepatotoxicity risk

---

## Dosing Notes and Cautions

1. **Species verification:** Confirm whether product is *S. lateriflora* or *S. baicalensis* — these are different herbs with different indications
2. **Authentication:** Source from manufacturers who provide authentication testing (germander adulteration risk)
3. **Starting dose:** Consider beginning at lower end of range (1 g dried herb or equivalent) and titrating
4. **Liver monitoring:** Baseline and 4–8 week LFTs recommended for ongoing use
5. **Drug interactions:** Review all medications, particularly warfarin, phenytoin, NSAIDs before initiating
6. **No dose established for:** Specific conditions beyond sleep and mood; evidence does not support use for other specific indications currently
7. **Pregnancy/lactation:** Contraindicated; no safe dose established
