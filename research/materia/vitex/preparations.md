# Vitex agnus-castus - Preparations and Dosing

## Overview

Vitex agnus-castus is prepared as standardized extracts, tinctures, and dried fruit preparations. Standardization to marker compounds (casticin or agnuside) is essential for clinical efficacy and consistency.

---

## Standardized Extract Products

### Ze 440

**Type**: Standardized dry extract

**Extraction Details**:
- **Solvent**: 60% ethanol
- **Extraction ratio**: 6-12:1 (6-12 grams dried fruit → 1 gram extract)
- **Standardization marker**: Casticin

**Dosing**:
- **Optimal dose**: 20mg daily
- **Dry fruit equivalent**: 120-240mg
- **Timing**: Once in morning with liquid
- **Duration**: Minimum 3 menstrual cycles for PMS

**Clinical Evidence**:
- **Dose-finding study** [Schellenberg et al. 2012, 162 participants]:
  - Doses tested: 8mg, 20mg, 30mg
  - **20mg optimal**: Significantly better than placebo and 8mg
  - 30mg showed no additional benefit over 20mg
- **PMS placebo-controlled trial** [Schellenberg 2001, 170 participants]:
  - 52% responder rate vs 24% placebo
  - Main efficacy improvement P<0.001
- **Meta-analysis inclusion** [Csupor et al. 2019]: Ze 440 met strict standardization criteria

**Forms**: Tablets or capsules containing 20mg standardized extract.

---

### BNO 1095 (Agnucaston / Cyclodynon)

**Type**: Standardized liquid or dry extract

**Extraction Details**:
- **Solvent**: 70% ethanol, 30% water
- **Extraction ratio**: 10:1 (10 grams dried fruit → 1 gram extract)
- **Standardization**: Casticin or agnuside

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 4mg daily
- **Dry fruit equivalent**: 40mg
- **Timing**: Once in morning
- **Duration**: 3 menstrual cycles for PMS treatment

**Clinical Evidence**:
- **Meta-analysis inclusion** [Csupor et al. 2019]: BNO 1095 met strict standardization criteria
- Studied in multiple European clinical trials
- Long history of use in German phytotherapy

**Trade Names**:
- Agnucaston
- Cyclodynon

**Forms**: Tablets, liquid extract

---

### Prefemin®

**Type**: Standardized extract

**Dosing**:
- **Dose**: 20mg daily
- **Timing**: Once daily
- **Duration**: Minimum 3 menstrual cycles

**Clinical Evidence**:
- **Phase 3 trial in Japanese population** [Momoeda et al. 2014, 69 participants]:
  - After first cycle: Significant decrease in total VAS score (P<0.001)
  - 91% responder rate by cycle 3
  - Almost all patients without symptoms or mild symptoms only
  - 8 minor adverse events; well-tolerated

**Form**: Tablets

**Market**: Approved in Japan; available in other markets

## Official Dosing Recommendations

### German Commission E

**Approved Indications**:
- Menstrual cycle irregularities
- Premenstrual syndrome
- Mastalgia
- Menopausal disorders

**Dosing Recommendations**:
- **Dried fruit extract**: 30-40mg daily
- **Standardization**: 0.6% casticin OR 0.6% agnuside
- **Alternative forms**:
  - Dry native extract: 2.6-4.2mg daily
  - Tincture: 40 drops daily
- **Timing**: Once in morning with liquid
- **Duration**: Several months consecutively

**Philosophy**: Long-term hormonal balancing herb rather than fast-acting remedy.

---

### European Medicines Agency (EMA/HMPC)

**Indication**: Treatment of premenstrual syndrome

**Status**: Traditional herbal medicinal product based on long-standing use

**Preparation**:
- **Extract type**: Liquid or dried extracts
- **Extraction solvent**: Ethanol 50-70% V/V
- **Daily dosage**: 30-40mg herbal substance

**Duration**: Minimum 3 menstrual cycles

---

### European Pharmacopoeia

**Reference**: 01/2015:2147

**Botanical Definition**: Whole, ripe, dried fruit of Vitex agnus-castus L.

**Standardization**: Minimum 0.08% casticin (C19H18O8; Mr 374.3) in dried drug

**Application**: Quality standard for raw material; extracts are typically standardized higher (0.6%).

## Tincture Preparations

### Traditional Tincture

**Definition**: Alcohol extraction of dried fruit taken as liquid.

**German Commission E Recommendation**:
- **Dose**: 40 drops daily
- **Timing**: Once in morning with liquid
- **Duration**: Several months consecutively

### Tincture Ratios (Typical)

Based on clinical products and traditional use:

**Standard ratios**:
- 1:5 (w/v) - 1 part herb to 5 parts menstruum
- 1:10 (w/v) - 1 part herb to 10 parts menstruum

**Menstruum** (extraction solvent):
- 50-70% ethanol in water
- Higher ethanol content extracts lipophilic diterpenes and casticin
- Water component extracts iridoids like agnuside

**Dosing Calculation**:
- If 40 drops = therapeutic dose (German Commission E)
- Typical drop volume: 0.04-0.05mL
- 40 drops ≈ 1.6-2.0mL
- This should deliver equivalent of 30-40mg standardized extract or dried fruit

**Standardization**: Should contain 0.6% casticin or agnuside for consistency with clinical evidence.

---

### Tincture Dosing from Clinical Products

**Halaska et al. 1999 (Mastalgia Study)**:
- **Dose**: 2 × 30 drops/day (total 60 drops)
- **Results**: 33.7mm pain reduction vs 20.3mm placebo after 2 cycles (p=0.006)
- **Form**: Solution containing Vitex agnus castus extract (VACS)

**Implication**: Higher tincture doses (60 drops) used successfully for mastalgia vs. 40 drops standard for PMS.

## Whole Dried Fruit (Tea or Powder)

### German Commission E Base Recommendation
- **Dose**: 30-40mg dried fruit daily
- **Standardization**: Should contain minimum 0.08% casticin (European Pharmacopoeia)

### Tea Preparation (Traditional)

**Not commonly recommended** because:
- Diterpenes are lipophilic (not well-extracted in water alone)
- Standardization difficult in tea form
- Clinical trials use alcohol extracts (ethanol extracts diterpenes efficiently)

**If using tea**:
- **Dose**: Approximately 500-1000mg dried fruit (to achieve equivalent extraction)
- **Preparation**: Steep in hot water 10-15 minutes
- **Limitation**: Suboptimal extraction of active diterpenes without alcohol

### Powder Form
- **Dose**: 30-40mg if standardized extract powder
- **Alternative**: 500-1000mg whole dried fruit powder (equivalent to higher extract doses)
- **Form**: Capsules or mixed in liquid
- **Limitation**: Bioavailability may be lower than extracts; absorption issues

**Recommendation**: Standardized extracts preferred over raw powder for consistent clinical effects.

## Dosing by Condition

### Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS)

**Standardized Extract**:
- **Ze 440**: 20mg once daily
- **BNO 1095**: 4mg once daily
- **Generic standardized extract**: 30-40mg daily (0.6% casticin or agnuside)

**Tincture**:
- **Dose**: 40 drops once daily in morning

**Duration**: Minimum 3 menstrual cycles

**Timing**: Once in morning with liquid; taken continuously (not cyclically)

**Expected Response**:
- Improvement may begin after first cycle
- Optimal response by 3rd cycle
- 52-91% responder rates in clinical trials

---

### Cyclic Mastalgia (Breast Pain)

**Standardized Extract**:
- **Dose**: 20-40mg daily (0.6% casticin or agnuside)
- **Higher end** (40mg) may be more effective for mastalgia

**Tincture**:
- **Dose**: 2 × 30 drops/day (60 drops total) [based on Halaska et al. 1999]
- **Alternative**: 40 drops once daily (standard)

**Duration**: 3 treatment cycles (typically 3 months)

**Timing**: Can be taken once in morning or divided (e.g., morning and evening for tincture)

**Expected Response**:
- Pain reduction may begin after 1 cycle
- Significant reduction by 2 cycles
- Maximum benefit by 3 cycles
- 33.7mm pain reduction on VAS scale (Halaska et al. 1999)

---

### Hyperprolactinemia and Luteal Phase Defects

**Standardized Extract**:
- **Dose**: 20mg daily (based on Milewicz et al. 1993)
- **Standardization**: 0.6% casticin or agnuside

**Tincture**:
- **Dose**: 40 drops once daily (equivalent)

**Duration**: 3 months minimum

**Timing**: Once in morning

**Clinical Monitoring**:
- **Prolactin levels**: Check baseline and every 3 months
- **Basal body temperature**: Track luteal phase length
- **Menstrual cycle**: Document cycle regularity

**Expected Response**:
- Prolactin reduction within 3 months
- Luteal phase normalization
- Improved progesterone synthesis
- Possible fertility enhancement (2 pregnancies in Milewicz study)

---

### Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD)

**Standardized Extract**:
- **Dose**: Likely same as PMS (20mg Ze 440 or 30-40mg generic)
- **Evidence**: Limited; based on Atmaca et al. 2003 study

**Duration**: 2-3 months

**Expected Response**:
- 57.9% response rate (comparable to fluoxetine 68.4%)
- Better for physical symptoms
- SSRIs may be better for psychological symptoms

**Note**: PMDD is severe condition; medical supervision recommended.

---

### Menopausal Symptoms

**Standardized Extract**:
- **Dose**: 30-40mg daily (standard recommendation)
- **Specific dose in Naseri et al. 2019**: Not specified; likely standard

**Duration**: 8 weeks minimum (based on Naseri et al. 2019)

**Expected Response**:
- Reduced anxiety
- Reduced vasomotor symptoms (hot flashes, night sweats)
- No significant effect on depression or sexual dysfunction

**Note**: Limited evidence; more research needed.

## Administration Guidelines

### Timing

**Optimal**: Once in morning with liquid

**Rationale**:
- Consistent daily administration
- Morning timing follows traditional and clinical trial protocols
- Liquid aids absorption

**Continuous vs. Cyclical**:
- **Take continuously** (every day), not just during luteal phase
- Clinical trials used continuous daily dosing
- Hormonal balancing requires sustained intake

### With or Without Food

**Not specified** in most clinical trials; likely either way is acceptable.

**Recommendation**: Take consistently (either always with food or always without) for steady absorption.

### Duration of Use

**Minimum**: 3 menstrual cycles (approximately 3 months)

**Typical**: 3-6 months for initial treatment

**Long-term use**: German Commission E recommends "several months consecutively"

**When to expect results**:
- Some improvement after 1 cycle possible
- Optimal response by 3rd cycle
- If no improvement after 3 cycles, reassess

## Preparation Quality and Selection

### Choose Medicinal-Grade Products

**Why**:
- Dietary supplements show high variability [Sogame et al. 2019]
- 2 of 10 supplements lacked marker compounds entirely
- Possible species contamination in some products
- Poor disintegration (not releasing actives) in some

**What to look for**:
1. **Standardization**: 0.6% casticin OR 0.6% agnuside
2. **Extraction ratio**: 6-12:1 or 10:1 (concentrated extract)
3. **Extraction solvent**: 50-70% ethanol (ensures diterpene extraction)
4. **Third-party testing**: HPLC verification of marker compounds
5. **Compliance**: European Pharmacopoeia or similar standards

### Researched Brands

**With clinical evidence**:
- Ze 440 (various manufacturers using this standardized extract)
- BNO 1095 / Agnucaston / Cyclodynon
- Prefemin®

### Red Flags (Avoid)

- No standardization information
- "Proprietary blend" without specific content
- Dietary supplements with vague labeling
- Very low price (suggesting poor quality raw material)
- No casticin or agnuside content stated
- Absence of third-party testing claims

## Special Preparation Considerations

### Bioavailability Issues

**Current Limitation**:
- Agnuside has only 0.7% oral bioavailability in mice
- Human bioavailability of diterpenes unknown
- May explain need for relatively high doses

**Emerging Solutions**:
- **Nanoemulsion formulations** under development to improve bioavailability
- Could potentially allow lower doses with equal or better efficacy
- Not yet commercially available

### Standardization Philosophy

**Why casticin and agnuside**:
- **Casticin**: Marker compound, easily quantified by HPLC
- **Agnuside**: Secondary marker, iridoid glycoside
- **Diterpenes**: True active compounds, but harder to quantify

**Implication**: Casticin/agnuside are standardization markers, not necessarily the primary active compounds; products should be standardized to these to ensure presence of diterpenes.

## Comparison: Extract vs. Tincture vs. Whole Herb

### Standardized Extract (Preferred)

**Advantages**:
- Consistent dose of marker compounds
- Clinical trial evidence
- Concentrated (smaller dose needed)
- Ethanol extraction captures diterpenes
- Tablet/capsule form convenient

**Disadvantages**:
- More expensive than whole herb
- Processing required

**Best for**: Clinical conditions (PMS, mastalgia, hyperprolactinemia) where consistent dosing is essential.

---

### Tincture (Traditional)

**Advantages**:
- Traditional preparation method
- Ethanol extracts diterpenes effectively
- Liquid form may absorb faster
- Dose adjustable (drops)

**Disadvantages**:
- Alcohol content (for those avoiding alcohol)
- Standardization variable unless commercial product
- Taste (bitter)
- Less convenient to carry

**Best for**: Those preferring traditional liquid preparations; can be effective if properly standardized.

---

### Whole Dried Fruit (Least Preferred)

**Advantages**:
- Least processed
- Lower cost
- Contains full spectrum of compounds

**Disadvantages**:
- Difficult to standardize dose
- Lower bioavailability (no extraction step)
- Larger quantity needed
- Water-based preparations (tea) poorly extract diterpenes
- No clinical trial evidence with raw herb

**Best for**: Not recommended for clinical conditions; extract or tincture preferred for reliable results.

## Practical Dosing Recommendations Summary

### For PMS
**Standardized extract**: 20mg Ze 440 OR 30-40mg generic (0.6% casticin) once daily in morning for minimum 3 cycles.

**Tincture**: 40 drops once daily in morning for minimum 3 cycles.

### For Cyclic Mastalgia
**Standardized extract**: 20-40mg (0.6% casticin) once daily for 3 cycles.

**Tincture**: 2 × 30 drops/day (or 40 drops once daily) for 3 cycles.

### For Hyperprolactinemia
**Standardized extract**: 20mg daily (0.6% casticin) for 3 months; monitor prolactin levels.

**Tincture**: 40 drops once daily for 3 months; monitor prolactin levels.

### For PMDD
**Standardized extract**: 20mg Ze 440 or 30-40mg generic for 2-3 months; consider medical supervision.

### For Menopausal Symptoms
**Standardized extract**: 30-40mg daily (0.6% casticin) for 8+ weeks.

## Dosing Safety Notes

### Do Not Exceed
- German Commission E maximum: 40mg dried extract daily (or equivalent)
- Clinical trials: Up to 30mg Ze 440 studied (but 20mg optimal)
- Higher doses not shown to improve efficacy and may increase side effects

### Minimum Effective Dose
- 8mg Ze 440 suboptimal in dose-finding study
- 20mg significantly better than 8mg
- Recommendation: Use 20-40mg standardized extract range

### Duration Before Expecting Results
- **Minimum**: 1 menstrual cycle (may see some improvement)
- **Optimal**: 3 menstrual cycles
- **If no improvement**: Reassess after 3 cycles; may not be responsive

## Storage and Shelf Life

### Standardized Extracts (Tablets/Capsules)
- **Storage**: Cool, dry place; protect from light and moisture
- **Temperature**: Room temperature (avoid heat)
- **Shelf life**: Typically 2-3 years (check expiration date)

### Tinctures
- **Storage**: Dark glass bottle; cool, dry place
- **Shelf life**: 2-5 years if stored properly (alcohol preserves)
- **Signs of degradation**: Cloudiness, off smell, sediment

### Whole Dried Fruit
- **Storage**: Airtight container; cool, dry, dark place
- **Shelf life**: 1-2 years
- **Signs of degradation**: Loss of aroma, discoloration, moisture

## Cost Considerations

### Standardized Extracts
- **Most expensive** per dose
- **Most cost-effective** for clinical conditions (higher success rate, consistent dosing)

### Tinctures
- **Moderate cost**
- Cost varies by quality and standardization

### Whole Dried Fruit
- **Least expensive** per gram
- **Potentially more expensive per effective dose** (need larger quantity, lower bioavailability)

**Recommendation**: Invest in quality standardized products for reliable clinical results rather than saving money on low-quality or unstandardized products.

## Summary: Preparation Selection Guide

### Best Evidence: Standardized Extracts
- **Ze 440**: 20mg daily (strongest evidence, dose-finding study)
- **BNO 1095**: 4mg daily (meta-analysis inclusion)
- **Generic standardized**: 30-40mg daily (0.6% casticin or agnuside)

### Traditional: Tincture
- 40 drops daily (standard)
- 2 × 30 drops daily (for mastalgia, based on clinical study)
- Ensure standardization to 0.6% casticin or agnuside

### Not Recommended: Whole Dried Fruit
- Tea or powder form has poor extraction of active diterpenes
- Difficult to standardize dose
- No clinical evidence for efficacy

### Selection Criteria
1. Choose medicinal-grade over dietary supplements
2. Verify standardization (0.6% casticin or agnuside)
3. Check extraction solvent (50-70% ethanol)
4. Look for third-party testing
5. Prefer brands with clinical evidence (Ze 440, BNO 1095, Prefemin)
