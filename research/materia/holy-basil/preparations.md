# Holy Basil (Ocimum sanctum/tenuiflorum) - Preparations

## Overview

This document covers preparation methods for holy basil based on clinical trial data and traditional use. Clinical trials primarily used standardized ethanolic extracts in capsule form, while traditional Ayurvedic use involved fresh leaves, dried leaf preparations, and water-based decoctions. The bibliography does not include specific tincture ratios or tea dosing protocols; information provided here is extrapolated from clinical and traditional contexts.

---

## CLINICAL PREPARATIONS (EVIDENCE-BASED)

### 1. Standardized Ethanolic Extracts - Capsules

These are the preparations with the strongest clinical evidence.

---

#### HolixerTM

**Form:** Capsule
**Dosage per Capsule:** 125 mg
**Recommended Dose:** 125 mg twice daily (250 mg/day total)
**Timing:** Two capsules twice daily (formulation details from study)
**Duration:** 8 weeks (studied duration)

**Standardization:** Proprietary (not disclosed in bibliography)

**Evidence:**
- Stress reduction: 37% decrease in Perceived Stress Scale vs 19% placebo (p = 0.003)
- Sleep improvement: 48% reduction in Athens Insomnia Scale vs 27% placebo (p = 0.025)
- Cortisol reduction: Hair cortisol significantly lower (p = 0.025); salivary cortisol/amylase lower (p = 0.001)

**Clinical Applications:** Stress, anxiety, sleep disturbances, cortisol dysregulation

**Reference:** Lopresti et al., 2022 (PMID: 36185698)

---

#### OciBest™

**Form:** Capsule
**Dosage per Capsule:** 400 mg
**Recommended Dose:** 1200 mg actives daily (likely 3× 400 mg capsules)
**Duration:** 6 weeks (studied duration)

**Standardization:**
- Ociglycoside-I: >0.1% w/w
- Rosmarinic acid: >0.2% w/w
- Triterpene acids: >2.5% w/w

**Evidence:**
- 39% more effective than placebo for stress symptom management
- Forgetfulness: 70% improvement
- Sexual dysfunction: 62% improvement
- Exhaustion: 39% improvement
- Sleep disturbance: 37% improvement
- Overall: 1.6× greater improvement than placebo

**Safety:** No adverse events; non-genotoxic

**Clinical Applications:** Generalized stress, cognitive symptoms of stress, exhaustion, sleep problems

**Reference:** Saxena et al., 2012 (PMID: 21977056)

---

#### Generic Ethanolic Leaf Extract (300 mg)

**Form:** Capsule
**Dosage:** 300 mg daily
**Duration:** 30 days (cognitive); 4 weeks (immune)
**Extraction:** Ethanolic leaf extract

**Standardization:** Not specified beyond extraction method

**Evidence:**
- **Cognitive (30 days):** Improved reaction time, error rates, P300 latency, continued improvement beyond day 15
- **Immune (4 weeks):** Increased IFN-γ (p=0.039), IL-4 (p=0.001), T-helper cells (p=0.001), NK-cells (p=0.017)

**Clinical Applications:** Cognitive enhancement, immune support

**References:**
- Cognitive: Sampath et al., 2015 (PMID: 26571987)
- Immune: Mondal et al., 2011 (PMID: 21619917)

---

#### Generic Ethanolic Extract (250 mg Capsules)

**Form:** Capsule
**Dosage per Capsule:** 250 mg
**Recommended Dose:** 250 mg twice daily (500 mg/day)
**Timing:** On empty stomach
**Duration:** 8 weeks (studied duration)

**Evidence:**
- Metabolic improvements in overweight/obese subjects
- Triglycerides: 12.14% decrease
- LDL-C: 11.73% decrease
- HDL-C: 21.74% increase
- Plasma insulin: 28.49% decrease
- HOMA-IR: 24.79% decrease
- BMI: 1.98% decrease

**Clinical Applications:** Metabolic syndrome, obesity, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia

**Reference:** Satapathy et al., 2017 (PMID: 28811698)

---

#### Higher Dose Extract (500 mg Capsules)

**Form:** Capsule
**Dosage per Capsule:** 500 mg
**Recommended Dose:** 500 mg twice daily (1000 mg/day)
**Timing:** After meals
**Duration:** 60 days (studied duration)

**Evidence:**
- Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) significantly attenuated (p<0.001)
- Associated stress and depression attenuated (p<0.001)
- Willingness to adjustment improved (p<0.001)
- Attention improved (p<0.001)

**Clinical Applications:** Generalized anxiety disorder, clinical anxiety

**Reference:** Bhattacharyya et al., 2008 (PMID: 19253862)

---

#### Ethanolic Extract Standardized to 5% Eugenol

**Standardization:** 5% eugenol content
**Evidence:** Gastroprotective effects, dose-dependent ulcer protection
**Clinical Applications:** Gastric protection, ulcer prevention

**Note:** Specific dosing protocol not detailed in bibliography

## TRADITIONAL PREPARATIONS

**Important Note:** The bibliography focuses on clinical trials using ethanolic extracts. Traditional Ayurvedic preparations are mentioned but not detailed. The following represents extrapolation from traditional use patterns and typical Ayurvedic preparation methods.

---

### 1. Fresh Leaf Preparations

**Traditional Use:** 3000+ years in Ayurveda
**Plant Parts:** Fresh leaves primarily

#### Method 1: Fresh Leaves (Chewed Directly)
**Dose:** Traditionally 5-10 fresh leaves daily
**Timing:** Morning on empty stomach (common traditional practice)
**Preparation:** Wash leaves thoroughly, chew and swallow

**Traditional Applications:** General health maintenance, respiratory support, stress

**Modern Clinical Equivalent:** Approximately equivalent to lower dose range (250-300 mg extract), though not directly comparable due to different phytochemical profiles

---

#### Method 2: Fresh Leaf Juice
**Dose:** 1-2 teaspoons (5-10 mL) fresh juice
**Preparation:**
1. Harvest fresh, clean leaves
2. Crush or grind leaves
3. Extract juice through cloth or fine strainer
4. Consume immediately (oxidizes quickly)

**Traditional Applications:** Acute respiratory issues, digestive complaints, fever

**Timing:** Morning on empty stomach, or as needed for acute conditions

---

### 2. Dried Leaf Preparations

#### Dried Leaf Powder

**Preparation:**
1. Harvest leaves at peak potency (before flowering ideal)
2. Dry in shade to preserve volatile oils
3. Grind to fine powder
4. Store in airtight container away from light

**Traditional Dose:** ½ to 1 teaspoon (approximately 1-3 grams) daily
**Administration:** Mixed with water, honey, or warm milk

**Modern Note:** Traditional powder dose higher by weight than ethanolic extract dose due to lower concentration of actives

---

### 3. Tea/Infusion Preparations

**Important Note:** Clinical trials used ethanolic extracts, not water-based infusions. Tea preparations have different phytochemical profiles (water-soluble compounds extracted; volatile oils may be lost or reduced).

---

#### Basic Holy Basil Tea

**Preparation:**
1. Use 1-2 teaspoons dried leaves per cup (approximately 2-4 grams)
2. Pour boiling water over leaves
3. Cover and steep 10-15 minutes (cover retains volatile oils)
4. Strain and drink

**Dose:** 1-3 cups daily

**Timing:**
- Morning: General tonic, stress support
- Before meals: Digestive support
- Evening: Sleep/relaxation support

**Traditional Applications:** Daily tonic, mild stress, respiratory support, digestive support

**Clinical Note:** Tea extraction method differs significantly from ethanolic extraction; may not deliver same compound profile or potency as clinical extracts

---

#### Strong Decoction

**Preparation:**
1. Use 2-3 teaspoons dried leaves per cup (approximately 4-6 grams)
2. Simmer (do not boil vigorously) for 10-15 minutes
3. Cover while simmering to retain volatile oils
4. Strain and drink

**Dose:** 1-2 cups daily

**Traditional Applications:** More acute conditions, stronger therapeutic effect

**Note:** Prolonged heat may degrade some volatile oils (particularly eugenol); covering pot helps retain aromatics

---

### 4. Tincture Preparations

**Note:** The bibliography does not include tincture preparations in clinical trials. The following represents standard herbal tincture ratios used in Western herbalism, not specific to holy basil clinical evidence.

---

#### Standard Tincture (Extrapolated)

**Typical Ratio:** 1:5 (1 part herb to 5 parts menstruum)
**Menstruum:** 40-60% alcohol (ethanol)
**Rationale:** Clinical trials used ethanolic extracts; alcohol extracts both polar and non-polar compounds (volatile oils, triterpene acids, phenolics)

**Preparation (Folk Method):**
1. Fill jar ½ to ⅔ full with dried holy basil leaves
2. Cover completely with 40-60% alcohol
3. Seal and shake daily
4. Macerate 4-6 weeks
5. Strain through cloth, bottle in dark glass

**Typical Dose (Extrapolated from Herbalism, Not Clinical Trials):**
- Standard: 2-4 mL (40-80 drops) three times daily
- Equivalence: Difficult to compare directly to clinical capsule doses due to extraction differences

**Clinical Context:** This is speculative; clinical trials used capsules with standardized extracts, not tinctures with variable alcohol percentages and herb ratios

---

#### Strong Tincture (Extrapolated)

**Typical Ratio:** 1:2 or 1:3 (more concentrated)
**Menstruum:** 50-70% alcohol
**Dose (Extrapolated):** 1-2 mL (20-40 drops) three times daily

**Note:** Higher concentration may better approximate clinical extract potency, but still speculative without clinical validation

---

### 5. Seed Preparations

**Preparation Methods (Traditional, Not Clinically Validated):**

## PREPARATION COMPARISON

### Ethanolic Extract (Clinical) vs. Traditional Preparations

| Aspect | Ethanolic Extract (Clinical) | Traditional Water-Based | Fresh Leaves |
|--------|------------------------------|-------------------------|--------------|
| **Extraction** | Ethanol (alcohol) | Hot water | None (direct consumption) |
| **Compounds Extracted** | Polar + non-polar (comprehensive) | Primarily water-soluble | All compounds (as-is) |
| **Volatile Oils** | Preserved if extracted properly | Partially lost (heat, evaporation) | Fully present (if fresh) |
| **Triterpene Acids** | Well-extracted | Poorly extracted (not water-soluble) | Present but bioavailability? |
| **Standardization** | Yes (marker compounds) | No (variable) | No (variable by plant) |
| **Potency** | Consistent, concentrated | Variable, less concentrated | Variable, moderate |
| **Evidence Base** | Strong (24 RCTs) | Traditional use (3000+ yrs) | Traditional use |
| **Convenience** | High (capsules) | Moderate (requires preparation) | Low (requires fresh plant) |

## PREPARATION STABILITY AND STORAGE

### Fresh Preparations
**Fresh Leaves:** Use immediately; refrigerate up to 1-2 days in sealed container
**Fresh Juice:** Use immediately; oxidizes quickly (minutes to hours)

### Dried Preparations
**Dried Leaves:** Store in airtight container away from light; use within 1 year for optimal potency
**Powder:** Store in airtight container away from light and moisture; use within 6 months (volatile oils degrade)

### Liquid Preparations
**Tea/Infusion:** Prepare fresh daily; refrigerate unused portion up to 24 hours
**Decoction:** Prepare fresh; can refrigerate 24-48 hours in sealed container
**Tincture:** Store in dark glass bottle; stable for 3-5 years if properly made and stored

### Commercial Extracts
**Capsules:** Follow manufacturer expiration date; typically 2-3 years if stored properly (cool, dry, away from light)

## QUALITY CONSIDERATIONS

### Selecting Commercial Products

**Look For:**
1. **Standardization:** Products standardized to marker compounds (eugenol, rosmarinic acid, triterpene acids, ociglycoside)
2. **Extraction Method:** Ethanolic extraction (matches clinical evidence)
3. **Third-Party Testing:** Independent verification of purity and potency
4. **Dosing:** Clear labeling of active content (not just "500 mg holy basil" but "500 mg extract containing X% actives")
5. **Clinical Brands:** HolixerTM and OciBest have specific clinical evidence; generic equivalents should match standardization profiles

**Avoid:**
- Unstandardized products with no marker compound information
- Products with fillers, binders, or additives (unless necessary for capsule stability)
- Products without clear extraction method
- Products with vague labeling ("proprietary blend," unclear active content)

### Sourcing Herb for Home Preparation

**Plant Identification:**
- Botanical name: Ocimum sanctum (syn. Ocimum tenuiflorum)
- Common names: Holy basil, Tulsi
- Note: Different from culinary basil (Ocimum basilicum)

**Growing Your Own:**
- Annual herb (in most climates)
- Prefers warm weather
- Harvest leaves before flowering for maximum potency
- Dry in shade to preserve volatile oils

**Purchasing Dried Herb:**
- Source from reputable herbal suppliers
- Look for organic certification (reduces pesticide exposure)
- Check for freshness (color, aroma)
- Store properly upon receipt

## PRACTICAL NOTES

### Transitioning from Traditional to Clinical Preparations

**Scenario:** Using traditional tea or fresh leaves but need stronger therapeutic effect for clinical condition

**Approach:**
1. Acknowledge traditional preparations may be insufficient for clinical conditions
2. Transition to standardized ethanolic extract at evidence-based dose
3. Continue for duration studied in clinical trials (4-13 weeks)
4. Can maintain traditional preparations alongside for additional support, if desired

---

### Transitioning from Clinical to Maintenance

**Scenario:** Completed clinical protocol (e.g., 8 weeks of 500 mg/day for stress), want to maintain benefits

**Approach:**
1. Consider transitioning to lower maintenance dose (e.g., 250-300 mg/day)
2. Or transition to traditional preparations (tea, fresh leaves) for ongoing gentle support
3. Monitor symptoms; if condition recurs, return to clinical dosing protocol

---

## SUMMARY

### Evidence-Based Preparations (Clinical)
- **Form:** Capsules containing ethanolic extracts standardized to marker compounds
- **Dose Range:** 250-1200 mg/day depending on indication
- **Evidence:** Strong (24 RCTs in systematic review)
- **Applications:** Clinical conditions (stress, anxiety, metabolic disorders, cognitive support, immune support)

### Traditional Preparations
- **Forms:** Fresh leaves, tea/infusion, decoction, powder, tincture (Western), seeds
- **Dose:** Variable (5-10 fresh leaves, 1-3 cups tea, ½-1 tsp powder)
- **Evidence:** Traditional use (3000+ years); lacks modern clinical validation
- **Applications:** General wellness, mild stress, daily tonic, respiratory support

### Key Distinction
**Clinical preparations (ethanolic extracts) have strong evidence for clinical conditions; traditional preparations have empirical/historical support for general wellness but may not achieve therapeutic effects seen in clinical trials.**

### Recommendation
- **For clinical conditions:** Use standardized ethanolic extracts at evidence-based doses
- **For general wellness:** Traditional preparations (tea, fresh leaves, powder) are reasonable and cost-effective
- **Quality matters:** Choose standardized products from reputable sources with third-party testing
