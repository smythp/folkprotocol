# Holy Basil (Ocimum sanctum/tenuiflorum) - Clinical Uses

## Overview

Holy basil has been studied in 24 clinical trials across metabolic, neurocognitive, and immune domains (Jamshidi & Cohen 2017, systematic review). Clinical dosing ranges from 250 mg to 1200 mg daily depending on indication and extract standardization, with study durations of 2-13 weeks.

---

## 1. STRESS AND ANXIETY

### Generalized Stress - HolixerTM Extract

**Dosage:** 125 mg twice daily (250 mg/day total)
**Duration:** 8 weeks
**Sample size:** 100 participants (85 completers)
**Age range:** 18-65 years

**Primary Outcome:**
- Perceived Stress Scale (PSS): 37% decrease in treatment group vs 19% in placebo (p = 0.003)

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- Athens Insomnia Scale: 48% reduction vs 27% placebo (p = 0.025)
- Hair cortisol: Significantly lower in treatment group (p = 0.025)
- Salivary cortisol during MAST stress test: Lower (p = 0.001)
- Salivary amylase during MAST: Lower (p = 0.001)
- Blood pressure during stress: Lower in treatment group

**Safety:** No serious adverse events; 83% reported no adverse effects

**Reference:** Lopresti et al., 2022 (PMID: 36185698)

---

### Generalized Stress - OciBest Extract

**Dosage:** 1200 mg actives daily (400 mg capsules)
**Standardization:** Ociglycoside-I >0.1%, rosmarinic acid >0.2%, triterpene acids >2.5%
**Duration:** 6 weeks
**Sample size:** 150 participants (79 placebo, 71 treatment)
**Location:** Two centers in Lucknow, India
**Study period:** April 2008 - September 2009

**Outcomes (Effect Sizes):**
- Forgetfulness: 70% improvement (p ≤ 0.05)
- Sexual dysfunction: 62% improvement (p ≤ 0.05)
- Exhaustion: 39% improvement (p ≤ 0.05)
- Sleep disturbance: 37% improvement (p ≤ 0.05)

**Overall:** OciBest 39% more effective than placebo for stress symptom management; overall improvement 1.6 times greater than placebo

**Safety:** No adverse events reported in either group

**Reference:** Saxena et al., 2012 (PMID: 21977056)

---

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**Dosage:** 500 mg capsule twice daily (1000 mg/day)
**Timing:** After meals
**Duration:** 60 days
**Sample size:** 35 participants (21 male, 14 female)
**Mean age:** 38.4 years

**Assessment:** Hamilton's Brief Psychiatric Rating Scale (BPRS); assessments at baseline (day 0), mid-term (day 30), final (day 60)

**Outcomes:**
- Generalized anxiety: Significantly attenuated (p < 0.001)
- Associated stress and depression: Significantly attenuated (p < 0.001)
- Willingness to adjustment: Significantly improved (p < 0.001)
- Attention: Significantly improved (p < 0.001)

**Conclusion:** May be useful in treatment of GAD; promising anxiolytic agent

**Reference:** Bhattacharyya et al., 2008 (PMID: 19253862)

## 2. COGNITIVE FUNCTION

**Dosage:** 300 mg daily
**Formulation:** Ethanolic leaf extract capsules
**Duration:** 30 days
**Study type:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial

**Outcomes (all significant vs placebo):**
- Sternberg test reaction time (RT): Significant improvement
- Sternberg test error rate (ER): Significant improvement
- Neutral task RT: Significant improvement
- P300 latency: Significant improvement (intergroup difference)
- Salivary cortisol: Intra-group improvement
- Anxiety measures: Intra-group improvement

**Key Finding:** Improvement stabilized after day 15 in placebo group; extract group showed continued improvement

**Safety:** No adverse effects; heart rate and skin conductance unchanged

**Reference:** Sampath et al., 2015 (PMID: 26571987)

---

## 3. METABOLIC DISORDERS

### Type 2 Diabetes (NIDDM)

**Dosage:** Holy basil leaves (Ocimum sanctum and Ocimum album)
**Study type:** Randomized placebo-controlled crossover trial, single-blind

**Outcomes:**
- Fasting blood glucose: Decreased by 21.0 mg/dl (17.6% reduction, p < 0.001)
- Postprandial blood glucose: Decreased by 15.8 mg/dl (7.3% reduction, p < 0.02)
- Urine glucose: Similar improvement patterns
- Total cholesterol: Mild reduction during basil treatment

**Recommendation:** May be prescribed as adjunct to dietary therapy and drug treatment in mild to moderate NIDDM

**Reference:** Agrawal et al., 1996 (PMID: 8880292)

---

### Metabolic Syndrome - Overview (Systematic Review)

**Studies reviewed:** 17 trials focusing on metabolic disorders
**Dosing range:** 250 mg to 3000 mg daily
**Duration range:** 12-13 weeks

**Metabolic Outcomes:**
- Fasting blood glucose: Improved 1.2-2.2 fold over 12-13 weeks
- Postprandial glucose: Improved 1.5-6.0 fold
- HbA1c: Improved 1.5-3.2 fold

**Reference:** Jamshidi & Cohen 2017 (PMID: 28400848)

---

### Obesity and Metabolic Parameters

**Dosage:** 250 mg capsule twice daily (500 mg/day)
**Timing:** On empty stomach
**Duration:** 8 weeks
**Sample size:** 30 participants (16 intervention, 14 control)
**Age range:** 17-30 years
**Population:** Overweight/obese subjects

**Lipid Outcomes:**
- Triglycerides: 12.14% decrease (p = 0.019)
- LDL-C: 11.73% decrease (p = 0.001)
- HDL-C: 21.74% increase (p = 0.001)
- VLDL-C: 12.14% decrease (p = 0.019)

**Body Composition:**
- BMI: 1.98% decrease (p = 0.005)

**Insulin and Glucose:**
- Plasma Insulin: 28.49% decrease (p = 0.021)
- HOMA-IR (Insulin Resistance): 24.79% decrease (p = 0.049)

**Safety:** No adverse effects on liver enzymes

**Reference:** Satapathy et al., 2017 (PMID: 28811698)

## 4. IMMUNE FUNCTION

**Dosage:** 300 mg capsules
**Formulation:** Ethanolic Tulsi leaf extract
**Duration:** 4 weeks
**Sample size:** 22 participants (24 enrolled)
**Population:** Healthy volunteers
**Study type:** Double-blinded randomized controlled cross-over trial

**Immunological Outcomes:**
- IFN-γ levels: Statistically significant increase (p = 0.039)
- IL-4 levels: Significant increase (p = 0.001)
- T-helper cells (percentage): Increased (p = 0.001)
- NK-cells (percentage): Increased (p = 0.017)

**Key Findings:**
- Clear immunomodulatory role demonstrated
- IFN-γ known for antiviral, antibacterial, anti-proliferative properties
- Significant increases in both cell-mediated and humoral immunity markers

**Conclusion:** Observations clearly ascertain the immunomodulatory role of Tulsi leaves extract on healthy volunteers

**Reference:** Mondal et al., 2011 (PMID: 21619917)

## SYSTEMATIC REVIEW SUMMARY

**Title:** The Clinical Efficacy and Safety of Tulsi in Humans: A Systematic Review of the Literature

**Scope:** 24 clinical trials (from 1553 initially screened)

**Domains:**
- Metabolic disorders: 17 trials
- Neurocognitive/mood effects: 4 trials
- Immune function/viral infections: 5 trials

**Overall Dosing Range:** 250 mg to 3000 mg daily
**Overall Duration Range:** 2-13 weeks

**Safety Across All 24 Trials:** All studies reported favorable clinical outcomes with no significant adverse events; only one trial documented mild transient nausea

**Quality:** Systematic review of human clinical trials

**Need:** Larger, longer-duration studies with standardized formulations

**Reference:** Jamshidi & Cohen 2017 (PMID: 28400848)

---

## CLINICAL APPLICATIONS SUMMARY

### Metabolic Disorders
**Conditions:** Type 2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome, dyslipidemia, obesity
**Evidence:** 17 trials showing improvements in glucose control, lipid profile, insulin resistance

### Stress and Mood
**Conditions:** General stress, generalized anxiety disorder, sleep disturbances
**Evidence:** Multiple RCTs showing significant reductions in stress, anxiety, improved sleep
**Mechanism:** Reduced cortisol, HPA axis modulation

### Cognitive Function
**Conditions:** Cognitive performance, memory, attention
**Evidence:** RCT showing improved reaction time, error rates, P300 latency

### Immune Function
**Conditions:** Immune support, antiviral, antibacterial
**Evidence:** RCT showing increased IFN-γ, IL-4, T-helper cells, NK-cells

### Antimicrobial
**Conditions:** Viral encephalitis, Candida infections, bacterial infections
**Evidence:** Clinical trials and systematic review on anti-candidal effects

---

## RESEARCH GAPS

- Need for larger, longer-duration studies with standardized formulations
- Independent replications of single studies
- Standardization of dosing protocols across indications
- Long-term safety studies (>8 weeks)
- Comparative effectiveness studies against standard treatments
- Optimal extraction methods and standardization markers

---

## NOTES

- Strong evidence base from systematic review of 24 RCTs
- Standardized extracts (HolixerTM, OciBest) show consistent efficacy and safety
- Excellent safety profile across all clinical trials
- Applications span metabolic, neuropsychiatric, immune, and antimicrobial domains
- Traditional use in Ayurveda for 3000+ years supported by modern clinical evidence
