## Traditional Preparations

**Note**: The bibliography does not contain specific information about traditional preparation methods (decoctions, tinctures, dried powder doses, etc.). Clinical trials used modern standardized extracts rather than traditional preparations.

### Modern Standardized Forms Used in Research
(These represent modern clinical research, not traditional preparations):

1. **Dry powder** (Mori 2009): 96% H. erinaceus dry powder at 3,000 mg/day
2. **Mycelia extracts** (Li 2020): Capsules containing mycelia enriched with erinacine A
3. **Fruiting body extracts** (Saitsu 2019, Surendran 2025): Supplements and 10:1 extracts
4. **Food incorporation** (Nagano 2010): H. erinaceus cookies

The bibliography primarily contains **modern clinical research** (2008-2025) rather than historical or ethnographic documentation of traditional uses. The single review article (Khan 2013) provides only a brief mention of TCM history without citing primary historical sources.

For a comprehensive traditional uses section, additional research into classical texts and ethnographic sources is required.
