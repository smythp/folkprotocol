# Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus) - Clinical Uses

## Cognitive Impairment & Dementia

### Mild Cognitive Impairment (Mori 2009)
- **Population**: Men and women aged 50-80 years with mild cognitive impairment
- **Sample size**: 30 participants (15 per group)
- **Dosage**: 3,000 mg/day (four 250 mg tablets, three times daily)
- **Content**: 96% H. erinaceus dry powder
- **Duration**: 16 weeks treatment + 4 weeks observation
- **Effect sizes**:
  - Significantly increased cognitive function scale scores at weeks 8, 12, and 16 vs placebo
  - Dose-response relationship: improvements increased with longer intake duration
  - Scores declined significantly 4 weeks after cessation
- **Safety**: No adverse effects observed in laboratory testing

### Early Alzheimer's Disease (Li 2020)
- **Population**: Patients with mild Alzheimer's Disease
- **Sample size**: Not specified
- **Dosage**: 1,050 mg/day (three 350 mg capsules daily)
- **Content**: H. erinaceus mycelia containing 5 mg/g erinacine A (~5.25 mg erinacine A daily)
- **Duration**: 49 weeks (plus 3-week screening period)
- **Effect sizes**:
  - Significant improvement in MMSE score in treatment group vs decline in placebo
  - Significant IADL score difference between groups favoring intervention
  - Enhanced contrast sensitivity in intervention arm vs placebo
- **Adverse events**: 4 subjects dropped out due to abdominal discomfort, nausea, and skin rash. No other adverse events reported.

### General Cognitive Function (Saitsu 2019)
- **Population**: Not specified
- **Sample size**: Not specified
- **Preparation**: Supplements containing fruiting body of H. erinaceus
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Effect sizes**:
  - MMSE showed significant improvement in cognitive functions
  - Prevented cognitive deterioration
- **Conclusion**: Safe and convenient method for dementia prevention

### Systematic Review Evidence (Menon 2025)
- **Studies included**: 5 RCTs, 3 pilot clinical trials, 15 laboratory studies, 1 cohort study, 1 case report, 1 computer analysis
- **Effect sizes**: Combined weighted mean increase of 1.17 in MMSE scores from one RCT and one PCT
- **Mechanisms**: Enhanced BDNF production demonstrated for mood benefits
- **Safety**: Potential side effects include stomach discomfort, headache, and allergic reactions (commonly unreported)

## Acute Cognitive Effects (Young Adults)

### Acute Single Dose - Stroop Task (Docherty 2023)
- **Population**: Healthy adults aged 18-45 years
- **Sample size**: 41 participants
- **Dosage**: 1,800 mg single dose
- **Assessment timing**: 60 minutes post-dose
- **Chronic protocol**: 28 days at 1,800 mg/day
- **Effect sizes**:
  - Acute: Improved speed on Stroop task performance (p = 0.005) at 60 minutes
  - Chronic: Trend toward reduced subjective stress (p = 0.051) after 28 days
- **Limitations**: Null and limited negative findings also observed; recommend further investigation in larger sample sizes

### Acute Single Dose - Pegboard Task (Surendran 2025)
- **Population**: Healthy participants aged 18-35 years
- **Sample size**: 18 participants
- **Dosage**: 3,000 mg single dose (10:1 fruiting body extract)
- **Assessment timing**: 90 minutes post-consumption
- **Effect sizes**:
  - No significant effect on global cognitive function and mood composite measures
  - Improved performance on pegboard testing at 90 minutes
- **Conclusion**: Acute supplementation benefits may be task or domain specific

## Mood, Depression, and Anxiety

### Depression and Anxiety in Women (Nagano 2010)
- **Population**: 30 females
- **Sample size**: 30 participants
- **Preparation**: H. erinaceus cookies
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Effect sizes**:
  - Depression scores (CES-D) significantly decreased following treatment
  - Anxiety and related complaints notably reduced vs baseline
  - Two symptom categories ('insentive' and 'palpitatio') showed significantly lower scores in HE group vs placebo
  - Three measures ('concentration,' 'irritating,' 'anxious') demonstrated trends toward improvement

### Systematic Review - BDNF Mechanism (Menon 2025)
- **Mechanism**: Enhanced BDNF production demonstrated for mood benefits in depression, anxiety, and sleep disorders
- **Preclinical findings**: Dose-dependent benefits in motor, cognitive, and depression-like behaviours in animal models (Spangenberg 2025)

## Anti-Cancer Activity

### Gastric Cancer (Menon 2025)
- **Active compound**: Erinacine A
- **Mechanism**: Inhibited gastric cancer cell invasiveness and activated apoptotic pathways
- **Study type**: Laboratory studies (included in systematic review)

## Clinical Interpretation Notes

1. **Cognitive benefits**: Most robust evidence exists for mild cognitive impairment and early Alzheimer's disease populations
2. **Dose-response**: Evidence suggests improvements increase with longer intake duration
3. **Persistence**: Benefits decline after cessation (Mori 2009 showed significant decline 4 weeks post-treatment)
4. **Acute vs chronic**: Acute effects may be task-specific; chronic use shows broader cognitive benefits
5. **Safety profile**: Generally well-tolerated with mild GI side effects in <10% of participants
6. **Standardization**: Clinical trials used varied preparations (fruiting body powder, mycelia extracts, erinacine-enriched extracts), making direct comparisons difficult
