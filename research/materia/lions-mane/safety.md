# Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus) - Safety Profile

## Toxicology Studies

### 28-Day Sub-Acute Toxicity (Li 2014)

**Study Design**:
- **Species**: Sprague-Dawley rats
- **Duration**: 28 days repeated oral administration
- **Test article**: H. erinaceus mycelium enriched with 5 mg/g erinacine A
- **Administration**: Oral gavage

**Dose Groups**:
- 1,000 mg/kg body weight/day
- 2,000 mg/kg body weight/day
- 3,000 mg/kg body weight/day
- Control: Distilled water

**Results**:
- **Survival**: 100% survival; all animals completed 28-day study
- **Clinical signs**: No abnormal changes observed
- **Laboratory parameters**: No treatment-related differences in urinalysis, blood chemistry, or hematology
- **Pathology**: Absence of gross and histological pathological findings between treatment and control groups
- **NOAEL**: Greater than 3,000 mg/kg body weight/day (highest dose tested)

### 13-Week Subchronic Toxicity (Lee 2019)

**Study Design**:
- **Species**: Sprague-Dawley rats
- **Duration**: 13 weeks
- **Test article**: Erinacine A-enriched lion's mane mycelia

**Dose Groups**:
- 0 mg/kg (control)
- 875 mg/kg body weight
- 1,750 mg/kg body weight
- 2,625 mg/kg body weight

**Results**:
- **Mortality**: No mortalities observed
- **Toxicological effects**: No noticeable toxicological effects during study period
- **Body weight & feed consumption**: Remained unaffected
- **Hematology & biochemistry**: No significant differences between treatment and control groups
- **Histopathology**: No treatment-related changes
- **Conclusion**: EAHE mycelia could be relatively unharmful when used over extended period
- **Implied NOAEL**: 2,625 mg/kg body weight (highest tested dose)

### 90-Day β-Glucan Extract Toxicity & Genotoxicity (Chen 2022)

**Study Design**:
- **Species**: Rats
- **Duration**: 90 days
- **Administration**: Gavage
- **Test article**: H. erinaceus β-glucan extract preparation

**Dose Groups**:
- 0 mg/kg/day (control)
- 500 mg/kg/day
- 1,000 mg/kg/day
- 2,000 mg/kg/day

**Results**:
- **Toxicity**: No toxicologically significant treatment-related changes in clinical observations, body weights, organ weights, or pathology
- **Genotoxicity**: No mutagenic effects in genotoxicity testing
- **NOAEL**: 2,000 mg/kg body weight/day (highest tested dose)

## Clinical Safety Data

### LiverTox Clinical Monograph (2024)

**Source**: National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases

**Safety Classification**:
- **General status**: Generally recognized as safe
- **Likelihood score**: E (unlikely cause of clinically apparent liver injury)
- **Hepatotoxicity**: Has not been linked to serum enzyme elevations during therapy nor to episodes of clinically apparent liver injury
- **Registry data**: No reported cases in major hepatotoxicity registries or systematic reviews

**Adverse Effects Profile**:
- **Gastrointestinal effects**: Mild complaints in <10% of treated subjects
  - Abdominal discomfort
  - Nausea
  - Diarrhea
- **Hypersensitivity**: One acute hypersensitivity reaction documented
- **Discontinuation**: Most adverse events do not require discontinuation

### Clinical Trial Adverse Events

#### Li 2020 (49-Week Alzheimer's Trial)
- **Dropouts**: 4 subjects due to:
  - Abdominal discomfort
  - Nausea
  - Skin rash
- **Other adverse events**: None reported

#### Mori 2009 (16-Week MCI Trial)
- **Safety findings**: No adverse effect of Yamabushitake observed in laboratory testing
- **Sample size**: 30 participants (15 per group)

#### Menon 2025 (Systematic Review)
- **Potential side effects**:
  - Stomach discomfort
  - Headache
  - Allergic reactions
- **Reporting**: Commonly unreported in studies

## Contraindications

**Note**: The bibliography does not contain specific studies on contraindications. The following are inferred from general safety data:

### Documented Contraindications
- **Known hypersensitivity**: One documented case of acute hypersensitivity reaction (LiverTox 2024)
- **Allergy to mushrooms**: General precaution (allergic reactions noted as potential side effect in Menon 2025)

### Theoretical Contraindications (Not Evidence-Based from Bibliography)
The current bibliography does not provide evidence for or against use in:
- Bleeding disorders (no data on anticoagulant effects)
- Upcoming surgery (no data on bleeding risk)
- Autoimmune conditions (immune-modulating effects noted but safety not assessed)

## Drug Interactions

**Critical Gap**: The bibliography contains **no studies** specifically examining drug interactions with lion's mane.

**Theoretical Considerations** (based on mechanisms, not clinical evidence):
- **Immune modulators**: β-glucans have immune-modulating effects (Khan 2013), but no interaction studies exist
- **Anticoagulants**: No bleeding studies or interaction data
- **CNS medications**: No pharmacodynamic interaction studies despite cognitive effects

**Research Priority**: Drug interaction studies are completely absent from the current evidence base.

## Special Populations

### Pediatric Use
**Evidence**: No studies in bibliography
**Status**: Safety and efficacy not established

### Geriatric Use
**Evidence**: Clinical trials included elderly populations (Mori 2009: ages 50-80; Li 2020: Alzheimer's patients)
**Safety findings**: No age-specific safety concerns identified in these trials
**Note**: Trials were conducted in target populations (cognitive impairment in elderly) with good tolerability

### Hepatic Impairment
**Evidence**: No specific studies
**Hepatotoxicity data**: No cases of liver injury reported (LiverTox 2024)
**Recommendation**: Insufficient data

### Renal Impairment
**Evidence**: No specific studies
**Excretion data**:
- Erinacine A: Primary elimination via feces (Tsai 2021)
- Erinacine S: Only 0.1% eliminated unchanged in urine/feces (Hu 2019)
**Recommendation**: Insufficient data, but low renal excretion suggests lower risk

## Long-Term Safety

**Longest human trial**: 49 weeks (Li 2020)
- No safety concerns at 1,050 mg/day for 49 weeks
- 4 dropouts due to GI symptoms and rash

**Longest animal toxicology**: 90 days (Chen 2022)
- NOAEL 2,000 mg/kg/day
- No genotoxic effects

**Gap**: No studies >49 weeks in humans

## Safety Monitoring Recommendations

Based on available evidence, the following monitoring may be prudent (not evidence-based requirements):

1. **Baseline**: Medical history for mushroom allergies
2. **During treatment**:
   - Monitor for GI symptoms (most common adverse effect)
   - Discontinue if hypersensitivity symptoms develop
3. **Laboratory monitoring**: Not required based on current evidence (no liver or kidney toxicity observed)

## Overall Safety Conclusion

**Based on available evidence**:
- **General safety**: Good safety profile in clinical trials and toxicology studies
- **Most common adverse effects**: Mild GI symptoms in <10% of users
- **Serious adverse events**: Rare (hypersensitivity reaction)
- **Hepatotoxicity**: No evidence of liver injury
- **NOAEL**: Well above therapeutic doses used in clinical trials
- **Major gaps**: Drug interactions, pregnancy/lactation, long-term use >1 year

**Risk-benefit assessment**: For cognitive impairment and mood disorders in adults, available evidence suggests favorable risk-benefit ratio, but significant evidence gaps exist for special populations and long-term use.
