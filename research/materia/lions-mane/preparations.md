# Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus) - Preparations & Dosing

## Clinical Trial Preparations

### Dried Powder (Whole Fruiting Body)

#### Mori 2009 - Cognitive Impairment Protocol
- **Preparation**: 96% Yamabushitake (H. erinaceus) dry powder
- **Form**: Tablets
- **Dosing regimen**: Four 250 mg tablets, three times daily
- **Total daily dose**: 3,000 mg (3 g)
- **Duration used**: 16 weeks treatment + 4 weeks observation
- **Population**: Mild cognitive impairment, ages 50-80
- **Outcomes**: Significantly increased cognitive function scores at weeks 8, 12, and 16

### Enriched Mycelia Extracts

#### Li 2020 - Alzheimer's Prevention Protocol
- **Preparation**: H. erinaceus mycelia enriched with erinacine A
- **Standardization**: 5 mg/g erinacine A content
- **Form**: Capsules (350 mg each)
- **Dosing regimen**: Three capsules daily
- **Total daily dose**: 1,050 mg mycelia (containing ~5.25 mg erinacine A)
- **Duration used**: 49 weeks (plus 3-week screening)
- **Population**: Mild Alzheimer's Disease
- **Outcomes**: Significant MMSE and IADL improvements

### Fruiting Body Supplements

#### Saitsu 2019 - Cognitive Function Protocol
- **Preparation**: Supplements containing fruiting body of H. erinaceus
- **Form**: Not specified
- **Dosing regimen**: Not specified in bibliography
- **Duration used**: 12 weeks
- **Outcomes**: Significant MMSE improvement, prevented deterioration

#### Surendran 2025 - Acute Dose Protocol
- **Preparation**: 10:1 fruiting body extract
- **Form**: Not specified
- **Dosing regimen**: Single acute dose
- **Total dose**: 3,000 mg (equivalent to 30 g fresh fruiting body)
- **Assessment timing**: 90 minutes post-consumption
- **Population**: Healthy adults ages 18-35
- **Outcomes**: Improved pegboard testing performance at 90 minutes

### Acute Dosing Studies

#### Docherty 2023 - Stroop Task Protocol
- **Preparation**: H. erinaceus (standardization not specified)
- **Form**: Not specified
- **Acute dose**: 1,800 mg single dose
- **Chronic dose**: 1,800 mg daily for 28 days
- **Assessment timing**: 60 minutes post-acute dose
- **Population**: Healthy adults ages 18-45
- **Outcomes**: Improved Stroop task speed (p=0.005) at 60 minutes; trend toward reduced stress after 28 days

### Food-Based Preparations

#### Nagano 2010 - Depression/Anxiety Protocol
- **Preparation**: H. erinaceus incorporated into cookies
- **Dosing**: Not specified in bibliography
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Population**: 30 females
- **Outcomes**: Significantly decreased depression (CES-D) and anxiety scores

## Standardization Markers

### For Mycelia Products
- **Erinacine A**: Primary marker
  - Typical enrichment: 5 mg/g (Li 2020)
  - High-quality production: 42.16 mg/g achievable with strain HeG (Liu 2024)
- **Extraction purity**: >95% erinacine A achievable using HSCCC (Liu 2024)

### For Fruiting Body Products
- **Extract ratios**: 10:1 used in Surendran 2025
- **Hericenones**: Markers mentioned but not quantified in clinical trials
- **β-glucan content**: Mentioned as primary therapeutic constituent (Khan 2013) but not quantified in trials
- **Total polysaccharides**: Not specified in clinical trials

### Quality Control Methods
- **LC-MS/MS**: Validated method for erinacine A quantification (Tsai 2021)
- **HSCCC**: High-purity isolation method (Liu 2024)
- **HPLC**: Referenced as established method (bibliography notes)

## Preparation Considerations

### Mycelia vs. Fruiting Body

**Mycelia (Underground)**:
- **Primary compounds**: Erinacines (A, C, S)
- **Bioavailability**:
  - Erinacine A: 24.39% (Tsai 2021)
  - Erinacine S: 15.13% (Hu 2019)
- **Clinical evidence**: Li 2020 (Alzheimer's), Lee 2019 (toxicology)
- **Standardization**: Erinacine A content (mg/g)

**Fruiting Body (Visible Mushroom)**:
- **Primary compounds**: Hericenones (C, D, E), β-glucans
- **Clinical evidence**: Mori 2009 (MCI), Saitsu 2019 (cognitive), Surendran 2025 (acute)
- **Standardization**: Less commonly standardized; extract ratios (e.g., 10:1) used

**Note**: Both show clinical efficacy; optimal form may depend on therapeutic target.

### Bioavailability Enhancement

**Timing for Brain Penetration**:
- **BBB crossing**: Both erinacine A and S cross blood-brain barrier within 1 hour
- **Peak brain concentration**: 8 hours post-oral dose (both compounds)
- **Implication**: For acute cognitive effects, assess at 60-90 minutes (used in Docherty 2023, Surendran 2025); for brain accumulation, longer duration may be beneficial

**Food Effects**: Not studied in bibliography

**Absorption Enhancers**: Not studied in bibliography

## Dosing Regimens by Indication

### Cognitive Impairment & Dementia

**Mild Cognitive Impairment**:
- **Dose**: 3,000 mg/day
- **Form**: Dried powder (96% purity)
- **Schedule**: Four 250 mg tablets, three times daily
- **Duration**: Minimum 16 weeks for benefit
- **Evidence**: Mori 2009 (n=30, RCT)
- **Note**: Improvements increased with longer duration; benefits declined 4 weeks after cessation

**Early Alzheimer's Disease**:
- **Dose**: 1,050 mg/day
- **Form**: Erinacine A-enriched mycelia (5 mg/g)
- **Erinacine A content**: ~5.25 mg/day
- **Schedule**: Three 350 mg capsules daily
- **Duration**: 49 weeks
- **Evidence**: Li 2020 (pilot RCT)

**General Prevention**:
- **Dose**: Not standardized
- **Form**: Fruiting body supplements
- **Duration**: 12 weeks minimum
- **Evidence**: Saitsu 2019 (RCT)

### Acute Cognitive Enhancement (Healthy Adults)

**Protocol 1** (Docherty 2023):
- **Acute dose**: 1,800 mg single dose
- **Assessment**: 60 minutes post-dose
- **Effect**: Improved Stroop task speed (p=0.005)
- **Chronic use**: 1,800 mg/day for 28 days showed trend toward stress reduction

**Protocol 2** (Surendran 2025):
- **Acute dose**: 3,000 mg single dose
- **Form**: 10:1 fruiting body extract
- **Assessment**: 90 minutes post-dose
- **Effect**: Improved pegboard testing
- **Note**: Task-specific benefits

### Depression & Anxiety

**Protocol** (Nagano 2010):
- **Form**: H. erinaceus cookies
- **Dose**: Not specified
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Evidence**: Significant reduction in CES-D depression scores and anxiety
- **Population**: Females (n=30)

### Anti-Cancer (Experimental)

**Note**: Only laboratory evidence (Menon 2025); no clinical dosing protocols

## Administration Timing

### With or Without Food
- **Not studied**: Bibliography contains no food-interaction studies
- **Clinical trials**: Timing relative to meals not specified

### Time of Day
- **Not studied**: Bibliography contains no chronotherapy studies
- **Suggestion from pharmacokinetics**: Peak brain concentration at 8 hours suggests morning dosing for daytime cognitive benefits

### Dosing Frequency

**Three times daily**: Mori 2009 (3,000 mg/day)
- Four tablets × three times = 1,000 mg per meal

**Once daily**: Li 2020 (1,050 mg/day)
- Three capsules, timing not specified

**Single acute dose**: Docherty 2023, Surendran 2025
- Effects observed 60-90 minutes post-dose

## Adverse Effects by Dose

### Clinical Doses (1,050-3,000 mg/day)
- **GI symptoms**: <10% (abdominal discomfort, nausea, diarrhea)
- **Skin rash**: Rare (4 dropouts in Li 2020)
- **Discontinuation rate**: Low (most adverse events do not require discontinuation)

### High Doses (Animal Studies)
- **NOAEL**: >3,000 mg/kg/day (28 days, rats) = ~30 g/day human equivalent
- **Safety margin**: Clinical doses well below NOAEL

## Duration of Treatment

### Acute Benefits
- **Onset**: 60-90 minutes for task-specific cognitive enhancement
- **Evidence**: Docherty 2023, Surendran 2025

### Chronic Benefits
- **Minimum duration**: 4 weeks for mood effects (Nagano 2010)
- **Cognitive improvement timeline**:
  - 8 weeks: Initial significant improvement (Mori 2009)
  - 12 weeks: Continued improvement (Mori 2009, Saitsu 2019)
  - 16 weeks: Maintained improvement (Mori 2009)
  - 28 days: Trend toward stress reduction (Docherty 2023)
  - 49 weeks: Maintained MMSE/IADL improvement (Li 2020)

### Persistence After Cessation
- **Mori 2009**: Scores declined significantly 4 weeks after stopping 16-week treatment
- **Implication**: Chronic use may be required for sustained benefits

## Special Considerations

### Pediatric Dosing
- **Evidence**: None
- **Status**: Not established

### Geriatric Dosing
- **Evidence**: Clinical trials conducted in ages 50-80
- **Doses used**: 1,050-3,000 mg/day
- **Safety**: Good tolerability in this age group
- **No dose adjustment**: Required based on age alone

### Renal/Hepatic Impairment
- **Evidence**: No specific studies
- **Excretion**: Primarily fecal (Tsai 2021); minimal renal excretion (Hu 2019: 0.1%)
- **Hepatotoxicity**: None observed (LiverTox 2024)
- **Recommendation**: Insufficient data for dosing adjustments

## Therapeutic Dose Rationale

### Based on Erinacine A Content (Mycelia)
- **Li 2020**: 5.25 mg erinacine A/day effective for Alzheimer's
- **Bioavailability**: 24.39% (Tsai 2021)
- **Brain delivery**: ~1.28 mg erinacine A reaches systemic circulation
- **BBB crossing**: Confirmed in preclinical models

### Based on Total Extract (Fruiting Body)
- **Mori 2009**: 3,000 mg/day (96% dry powder) effective for MCI
- **Extract ratio**: Surendran 2025 used 10:1 (3 g extract = 30 g fresh fruiting body)
- **Active compounds**: Hericenones + β-glucans (mechanisms partially characterized)

## Preparation Recommendations Summary

**For Cognitive Impairment**:
- Either mycelia (1,050 mg/day, standardized to erinacine A) or fruiting body (3,000 mg/day, whole powder)
- Minimum 12-16 weeks duration
- Consider chronic use due to decline after cessation

**For Acute Cognitive Enhancement**:
- 1,800-3,000 mg single dose
- Assess at 60-90 minutes
- Effects may be task-specific

**For Mood/Anxiety**:
- Form and dose not well-established
- Minimum 4 weeks treatment
- Female population primarily studied
