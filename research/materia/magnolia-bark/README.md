# Magnolia Bark Research Summaries

Detailed research summaries extracted from `bibliographies/magnolia-bark.yaml` (846 lines, 34 PubMed-indexed sources).

## Files

### [01-clinical-uses.md](01-clinical-uses.md)
**178 lines** - Clinical trial evidence for specific conditions

- **Stress & Anxiety**: 3 RCTs with Relora® (500-750 mg/day, 4-6 weeks)
  - 18% cortisol reduction, 42% anger reduction (Talbott 2013, n=56)
  - Significant anxiety relief (Kalman 2008, n=26)
  - Prevented stress-related weight gain (Garrison 2006, n=28)

- **Menopausal Symptoms**: 2 RCTs (634 and 89 participants, 12-24 weeks)
  - 60 mg/day magnolia extract superior for insomnia, anxiety, mood
  - 66.7% wellbeing improvement (Mucci 2006, n=89)

- **Postpartum Depression**: 1 RCT (143 participants, 3 weeks)
  - Pure magnolia tea improved sleep, reduced fatigue and depression

- **Alzheimer's Disease**: Preclinical only (28 studies, no human trials yet)

### [02-traditional-uses.md](02-traditional-uses.md)
**91 lines** - Historical use and traditional medicine documentation

- Chinese Pharmacopoeia (2015, 2010): Houpo (厚朴), thousands of years of use
- Traditional indications: Anxiety, asthma, depression, GI disorders, headache, phlegm retention
- Pharmacopoeial standard: Minimum 2.0% total honokiol + magnolol
- Modern validation: Anxiety and depression well-supported; GI/headache not evaluated in modern RCTs
- No WHO or Commission E monographs (primarily Asian medicinal plant)

### [03-active-compounds.md](03-active-compounds.md)
**187 lines** - Phytochemistry, bioavailability, mechanisms

- **Primary actives**: Honokiol (1-5% natural) and Magnolol (2-10% natural)
- **Standardization**: 90-98% combined in commercial extracts
- **Bioavailability paradox**: Extremely low oral BA (0.7-4.9% in rats), yet clinically effective
- **Mechanisms**:
  - GABA_A receptor: Benzodiazepine site modulation (Chen 2012)
  - Novel allosteric site: 350-400% increase in tonic currents (Alexeev 2012)
  - CB1 receptor: Agonism with neuroprotective effects (Borgonetti 2021)
- **Human PK data**: Practically none available (critical research gap)
- **Enhanced formulations**: Nanoemulsions improve BA 3-fold (preclinical only)

### [04-safety.md](04-safety.md)
**234 lines** - Safety profile, contraindications, drug interactions

- **Overall**: Excellent safety profile (Sarrica 2018: 145 articles reviewed)
- **Human trials**: No adverse events in stress/anxiety RCTs; 1.2% AE rate in menopausal trials (= control)
- **Duration**: Safe up to 1 year; beyond 1 year unknown (research gap)
- **Animal toxicology**: NOAEL >240 mg/kg/day (Liu 2007, 90-day rat study)
- **Genotoxicity**: None detected (in vitro + in vivo)
- **Contraindications**: Pregnancy and lactation (insufficient data)
- **Drug interactions**: 
  - CNS depressants: Most plausible concern (GABA_A mechanism)
  - CYP3A4 substrates: Theoretical (unstudied)
  - Aristolochic acid: AVOID (magnolia potentiates toxicity)
- **Hepatotoxicity**: Hepatoprotective at therapeutic doses (400 mg/day reduced liver fat in NAFLD trial)

### [05-preparations.md](05-preparations.md)
**274 lines** - Dosing protocols, formulations, preparation methods

- **Stress/Anxiety**: 500-750 mg/day Relora®, 4-6 weeks
- **Menopause**: 60 mg/day magnolia extract (with isoflavones, minerals), 12-24 weeks
- **Postpartum**: Pure magnolia tea, 3 weeks
- **Traditional TCM**: 3-10 g dried bark/day (decoctions)
- **Modern extracts**: 45-90× more concentrated than traditional preparations
- **Quality control**: HPLC-DAD (industry standard), ¹H NMR (can purify to >98% in <1 hour)
- **Enhanced formulations**: Nanoemulsions, liposomes under development (not yet clinical)
- **Product selection**: Look for ≥1.5% honokiol standardization, third-party testing

## Key Findings

### Strongest Clinical Evidence
1. **Stress/cortisol reduction**: 3 RCTs, 18% cortisol ↓
2. **Menopausal mood/sleep**: 2 RCTs, 723 participants total
3. **Postpartum depression**: 1 RCT, 143 participants (pure tea)

### Critical Research Gaps
1. **Human pharmacokinetics**: "Practically no clinical studies published in English"
2. **Single-ingredient trials**: Most use combinations (Relora®, Estromineral)
3. **Sleep disorder RCTs**: None, despite strong GABA mechanism
4. **Long-term safety**: No data beyond 1 year
5. **Drug interactions**: Theoretical only; no systematic studies

### The Bioavailability Paradox
- Oral bioavailability extremely low (0.7-4.9%)
- Yet clear clinical efficacy demonstrated
- Possible explanations: Tissue accumulation, BBB penetration, active metabolites, traditional processing
