# Magnolia Bark - Clinical Uses

## Stress, Anxiety, and Mood

### Relora® Studies (Magnolia + Phellodendron)

**Talbott 2013 (PMID: 23924268)**
- **Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT
- **Participants**: 56 subjects (35 men, 21 women) with moderate stress
- **Dose**: 500 mg/day Relora® (standardized to ≥1.5% honokiol, 0.1% berberine)
- **Duration**: 4 weeks
- **Results**:
  - Salivary cortisol: **18% reduction** (p<0.05)
  - Overall stress: **11% reduction** (p<0.05)
  - Tension: **13% reduction** (p<0.05)
  - Depression: **20% reduction** (p<0.05)
  - Anger: **42% reduction** (p<0.05)
  - Fatigue: **31% reduction** (p<0.05)
  - Confusion: **27% reduction** (p<0.05)
  - Vigor: **18% increase** (p<0.05)
  - Global mood state: **11% improvement** (p<0.05)
- **Safety**: No adverse events reported

**Kalman 2008 (PMID: 18426577)**
- **Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot RCT
- **Participants**: 40 enrolled, 26 completed (healthy, overweight premenopausal women who eat more during stress)
- **Dose**: 750 mg/day Relora® (250 mg three times daily; standardized to ≥1.5% honokiol, 0.1% berberine)
- **Duration**: 6 weeks
- **Results**:
  - **Significantly reduced temporary, transitory anxiety** vs placebo (Spielberger STATE anxiety questionnaire)
  - No significant effect on trait anxiety
  - No significant changes in salivary cortisol or amylase
  - No significant changes in sleep quality/latency
  - No changes in appetite or body composition
- **Safety**: No adverse events occurred
- **Conclusion**: Effective for acute anxiety but not chronic trait anxiety

**Garrison 2006 (PMID: 16454147)**
- **Design**: Double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot RCT
- **Participants**: 28 overweight (BMI 25-34.9) premenopausal women aged 20-50 who ate more during stress
- **Dose**: 750 mg/day (250 mg three times daily)
- **Duration**: 6 weeks
- **Results**:
  - Placebo group: **Gained average 1.5 kg** (p<0.01)
  - Treatment group: **No significant weight change** (p=0.89)
  - Weight gain frequency: 75% placebo vs **37% treatment** gained ≥1 kg (p<0.04)
  - Evening cortisol: **Marginally significant reduction** (p=0.06)
- **Safety**: Well-tolerated, no adverse effects
- **Conclusion**: Prevented stress-related weight gain

## Menopausal Symptoms

### Magnolia Extract + Isoflavone Combination Studies

**Agosta 2011 (PMID: 21311416)**
- **Design**: Multicenter, parallel-group, placebo-controlled RCT
- **Participants**: 634 menopausal women (mean age 53.1 years, BMI 25.2 kg/m²)
- **Centers**: 91 centers
- **Dose**: 60 mg/day magnolia bark extract (in combination with isoflavones 60 mg, Lactobacillus sporogenes, calcium, vitamin D3)
- **Frequency**: One tablet nightly
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Assessment**: Weeks 0, 4, 8, 12
- **Results**:
  - Magnolia group **superior for**:
    - Insomnia
    - Irritability
    - Anxiety
    - Depressed mood
    - Asthenia (weakness/fatigue)
    - Loss of libido
  - Physician wellbeing assessment: **>70% of both groups** showed positive well-being
  - Adverse events: 1.2% (magnolia group) vs 1% (control group)
- **Conclusion**: Magnolia bark extract enhances benefits beyond isoflavones alone for menopausal psychological symptoms

**Mucci 2006 (PMID: 16957676)**
- **Design**: Multicenter controlled RCT
- **Participants**: 89 menopausal women (mean age 53.8 years)
- **Dose**: 60 mg/day magnolia bark extract (in combination with soy isoflavones 60 mg, lactobacilli 500 million spores, calcium 141 mg, vitamin D3 5 mcg, magnesium 50 mg)
- **Frequency**: 1 tablet daily
- **Duration**: 24 weeks
- **Control**: Calcium + vitamin D3 only
- **Results**:
  - **Significant decrease from week 4 onward** in:
    - Insomnia
    - Anxiety
    - Mood depression
    - Irritability
  - Wellbeing rated good/very good: **66.7% (treatment)** vs 20% (control)
  - Efficacy judgments: **72.7% (treatment)** vs 17.1% (control)
  - Acceptability: **93.9% (treatment)** vs 31.4% (control)
- **Conclusion**: Magnolia extract and magnesium effective for psycho-affective and sleep disturbances in menopause

## Postpartum Depression and Sleep

**Xue 2020 (PMID: 32180964)**
- **Design**: Randomized controlled pilot study
- **Participants**: 143 postpartum women
- **Intervention**: **Pure magnolia tea** (single ingredient - rare in research)
- **Duration**: 3 weeks
- **Control**: Standard postpartum care
- **Results**:
  - Sleep quality: **Physical sleep inefficiency improved** at 3 weeks (t=-2.10, p=0.03)
  - Fatigue reduction:
    - 3 weeks: t=-2.02, p=0.04
    - 6 weeks: t=-1.99, p=0.04
  - Depression (Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale):
    - 3 weeks: t=-2.38, p=0.02
    - 6 weeks: t=-2.13, p=0.02
  - **Benefits sustained at 6-week follow-up**
- **Significance**: One of the few studies using pure magnolia (not combination product)
- **Conclusion**: Single-ingredient magnolia tea has positive effects on postpartum depression, fatigue, and sleep

## Alzheimer's Disease (Preclinical)

**Li 2024 (PMID: 38955265) - Systematic Review**
- **Studies**: 28 preclinical studies (from 1,172 screened)
- **Methodology**: PRISMA guidelines; searched through March 1, 2024
- **Active compounds**: Honokiol, magnolol, 4-O-methylhonokiol, obovatol
- **Effects in AD animal models**:
  - Alleviated behavioral abnormalities
  - Improved learning and cognitive function
  - Inhibited Aβ generation/aggregation
  - Reduced neuroinflammation
  - Inhibited acetylcholinesterase activity
  - Promoted microglial phagocytosis
  - Antioxidative stress
  - Alleviated mitochondrial dysfunction
- **Pathways**: NF-κB, ERK, AMPK/mTOR/ULK1 signaling
- **Limitation**: **Pre-clinical studies only** - no human trials
- **Conclusion**: Promising preclinical evidence; human studies needed for exact targets, pharmacokinetics, safety, and clinical efficacy

## Clinical Dosing Summary

### Stress, Anxiety, Mood
- **Product**: Relora® (Magnolia + Phellodendron)
- **Dose**: 500-750 mg/day (250 mg three times daily)
- **Standardization**: ≥1.5% honokiol, 0.1% berberine
- **Duration**: 4-6 weeks
- **Evidence**: 3 RCTs, 56-40 participants
- **Key outcomes**: 18% cortisol reduction, 42% anger reduction, anxiety relief, prevented stress-related weight gain

### Menopause (Insomnia, Mood, Anxiety)
- **Dose**: 60 mg/day magnolia bark extract
- **Frequency**: Once nightly
- **Duration**: 12-24 weeks
- **Combination ingredients**: Typically with isoflavones (60 mg), lactobacilli, calcium, vitamin D3, magnesium
- **Evidence**: 2 RCTs, 634 and 89 participants
- **Key outcomes**: Superior for insomnia, anxiety, irritability, depressed mood; 66.7% wellbeing improvement

### Postpartum Depression
- **Product**: Pure magnolia tea
- **Duration**: 3 weeks (benefits sustained at 6 weeks)
- **Specific dosing**: Not detailed in abstract
- **Evidence**: 1 RCT, 143 participants
- **Key outcomes**: Improved sleep efficiency, reduced fatigue and depression

## Research Gaps

- **No large-scale RCTs** for sleep disorders despite strong GABA receptor evidence
- **Most studies use combination products** - difficult to isolate magnolia-specific effects
- **Xue 2020 is rare exception** using pure magnolia tea
- **No Phase 2 dose-ranging trials** to establish optimal dosing
- **Limited populations studied**: Primarily stressed women and menopausal women
- **No human trials for Alzheimer's disease** despite promising preclinical evidence
- **No long-term safety data beyond 1 year** in humans
