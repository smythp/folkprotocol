# Schisandra - Safety and Toxicology

## General Safety Assessment

**Overall Profile:** Generally considered safe at moderate doses; appears entirely free of toxicity in recommended dosage range

**Study Limitations:** Not well studied in humans; long-term safety data limited

**Source:** Safety assessment from multiple clinical trials and toxicology studies

---

## Adverse Events in Clinical Trials

### Serious Adverse Events
**Frequency:** Rare to none reported in clinical trials

**Clinical Trials with Zero Serious Adverse Events:**
- Park & Kim, 2016 (n=36, menopausal symptoms) - None reported
- Park et al., 2020 (n=45, muscle strength) - None reported
- Chiu et al., 2013 (n=40, liver function) - None reported
- Song et al., 2015 (n=28, obesity/microbiota) - None reported
- Yoon et al., 2022 (n=80, hyperglycemia) - No clinically significant changes in any safety parameter
- Aslanyan et al., 2010 (n=40, cognitive function) - None reported

---

### Non-Serious Adverse Events

**Common Mild Effects (Infrequent):**
1. **Gastrointestinal:**
   - Heartburn
   - Upset stomach
   - Decreased appetite
   - Stomach pain

2. **Dermatological:**
   - Skin rash
   - Itching

3. **Other:**
   - Sleepiness (observed in both treatment and placebo groups in Aslanyan et al., 2010)
   - Cold extremities (observed in both treatment and placebo groups in Aslanyan et al., 2010)

**Frequency:** Mild and infrequent

---

### Overdose Symptoms

**Reported Effects at High Doses:**
- Restlessness
- Insomnia
- Dyspnea (difficulty breathing)

**Note:** Overdose symptoms suggest CNS stimulation at excessive doses

---

## Toxicology Studies

### Acute Toxicity (Mice)

**Oral Route:**
- **Minimum Toxic Dose:** 3.6 g/kg

**Intraperitoneal Route:**
- **Convulsions:** ED₅₀ = 175 mg/kg
- **Paresis:** ED₅₀ = 370 mg/kg
- **Effects on Normal Parameters:** No effects on blood pressure, breath, or motility

**Clinical Significance:** Very wide margin between therapeutic doses (~10-20 mg/kg human equivalent for 1000 mg in 60 kg person) and toxic doses (3600 mg/kg oral in mice)

---

### Repeated Dose Toxicity

**Study Design:**
- **Species:** Sprague-Dawley (SD) Rat
- **Duration:** 13 weeks
- **Route:** Oral

**No Observed Adverse Effect Level (NOAEL):**
- **NOAEL:** 250 mg/kg/day

**Clinical Significance:**
- For 60 kg human, equivalent to ~15 g/day (using allometric scaling)
- Typical human doses (1-6 g crude drug or 200-1000 mg extract) far below NOAEL
- Suggests good safety margin with standard dosing

---

## Contraindications

### Pregnancy
**Classification:** POSSIBLY UNSAFE

**Mechanism:** May cause uterine contractions

**Risk:** Leading to miscarriage

**Recommendation:** **Avoid use during pregnancy**

**Evidence Level:** Preclinical/traditional knowledge basis (human studies lacking)

---

### Lactation
**Classification:** Insufficient data

**Safety Data:** Limited

**Recommendation:** **Avoid use during lactation** due to insufficient safety data

**Note:** Unknown whether lignans pass into breast milk or effects on infant

---

## Drug Interactions

### CYP450 Enzyme Interactions

**Enzymes Inhibited:**
1. **CYP1A2**
2. **CYP3A4** (primary)
3. **CYP3A5**

**Interaction Type:** Inhibition

**Clinical Significance:** May reduce effectiveness of some drugs or increase adverse effects of others

**Affected Drug Classes (Partial List):**
- Immunosuppressants
- Statins
- Calcium channel blockers
- Benzodiazepines
- Many others metabolized by CYP3A4

**General Precaution:** Exercise caution with any CYP3A4 substrate, especially narrow therapeutic index drugs

---

### P-glycoprotein (P-gp) Interaction

**Interaction Type:** Inhibition

**Clinical Significance:** May increase risk of side effects of P-gp substrate drugs

**Mechanism:** Reduced efflux leads to increased systemic exposure

**Affected Drugs (Partial List):**
- Digoxin
- Dabigatran
- Colchicine
- Many chemotherapy agents

---

### Tacrolimus Interaction (Well-Documented)

**Study:** Xin et al., 2007 (British Journal of Clinical Pharmacology)
- **Sample:** 12 healthy male volunteers

**Schisandra Dosing:**
- **Extract:** Schisandra sphenanthera extract (SchE)
- **Dose:** Three capsules twice daily
- **Duration:** 13 days

**Tacrolimus Dosing:**
- **Dose:** 2 mg oral (single dose)

**Pharmacokinetic Changes:**
| Parameter | Change | 95% CI |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| AUC | **+164.2%** | [70.1, 258.4] |
| Cmax | **+227.1%** | [155.8, 298.4] |
| Tmax | +36.8% | [13.4, 60.2] |
| CL/F | **-49.0%** | [31.1, 66.9] |
| V/F | -53.7% | [40.1, 67.4] |

**Mechanism:**
- Inhibition of CYP3A-mediated metabolism
- Inhibition of P-gp-mediated efflux
- Results in increased oral bioavailability

**Clinical Significance:**
- Blood tacrolimus concentrations occasionally **markedly increased** in transplant recipients receiving concomitant SchE
- **Risk of toxicity** from tacrolimus overdose (nephrotoxicity, neurotoxicity, infection risk)

**Clinical Action Required:**
- **Blood level monitoring required** when combining
- **Avoid combination** unless close monitoring available
- Consider **dose reduction** of tacrolimus if schisandra initiated

**Affected Population:**
- Liver transplant patients
- Kidney transplant patients
- Any patient on tacrolimus immunosuppression

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17506780/

---

### Other Potential Drug Interactions

**CYP3A4 Substrates (Theoretical Risk Based on Tacrolimus Data):**

**High Risk (Narrow Therapeutic Index):**
- Cyclosporine (immunosuppressant)
- Everolimus, sirolimus (immunosuppressants)
- Warfarin (anticoagulant, also CYP2C9)
- Theophylline (bronchodilator, also CYP1A2)

**Moderate Risk:**
- Statins (simvastatin, atorvastatin, lovastatin)
- Calcium channel blockers (felodipine, amlodipine, nifedipine)
- Benzodiazepines (alprazolam, midazolam, triazolam)
- Sildenafil and related drugs
- Many antidepressants
- Many chemotherapy agents

**CYP1A2 Substrates:**
- Caffeine (may increase stimulant effects)
- Clozapine
- Theophylline
- Some antidepressants

**CYP2C9 Substrates:**
- Warfarin
- Phenytoin
- Many NSAIDs

**General Recommendation:** Exercise caution when combining schisandra with any medication metabolized by CYP1A2, CYP2C9, or CYP3A4. Monitor for increased drug effects or side effects. Consider therapeutic drug monitoring when available.

---

## Long-Term Safety Concerns

### Data Limitations

**Current Evidence:**
- Data on long-term safety, particularly with concentrated extracts or lignan-rich supplements, remains **limited**
- Most clinical trials duration: 6-12 weeks
- Longest trial: 5 months (Chiu et al., 2013)

**Concerns with Prolonged High-Dose Use:**
1. **Potential adverse effects** from prolonged consumption at high doses
2. **CYP450 enzyme modulation** over extended periods
3. **Accumulation potential** (low excretion noted in pharmacokinetic studies)
4. **Drug interaction risks** increase with chronic use

**Research Needed:**
- Comprehensive toxicological studies with extended duration
- Pharmacokinetic studies evaluating accumulation
- Clinical trials >6 months duration
- Post-market surveillance data

---

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy and Lactation
**Recommendation:** **Avoid use**
- Risk of uterine contractions and miscarriage
- Insufficient safety data during lactation
- Unknown effects on fetal development
- Unknown passage into breast milk

---

### Children
**Data:** No specific safety data in pediatric populations

**Recommendation:** Avoid use in children due to insufficient safety data

---

### Elderly
**Clinical Trial Data:**
- Included in some studies (Aslanyan et al., 2010: ages 20-68)
- No specific safety concerns identified in older adults
- Monitor closely due to likely polypharmacy and drug interaction risks

---

### Hepatic Impairment
**Theoretical Considerations:**
- Primary site of metabolism and distribution
- May accumulate in liver disease
- CYP450 function may be altered in hepatic impairment

**Paradox:**
- Used traditionally and clinically for liver support
- Yet liver is primary site of metabolism and accumulation

**Recommendation:**
- Use with caution in severe hepatic impairment
- Monitor liver enzymes
- Consider lower doses in advanced liver disease
- More data needed

---

### Renal Impairment
**Considerations:**
- Kidney is secondary site of accumulation
- Renal excretion is primary route (for Schisandrol A)

**Recommendation:**
- Use with caution in severe renal impairment
- Monitor renal function
- No specific dosing adjustments established

---

## Surgery and Anesthesia

**CYP450 Interaction Concerns:**
- Many anesthetic agents metabolized by CYP3A4
- May alter drug levels during surgery

**Recommendation:**
- **Discontinue 2 weeks before scheduled surgery** to allow CYP450 enzyme normalization
- Inform anesthesiologist of schisandra use

---

## Monitoring Recommendations

### When Using Schisandra

**Baseline Assessment:**
- Medication list review for CYP3A4/CYP1A2/CYP2C9/P-gp substrates
- Liver function tests (if using for liver support or long-term)
- Pregnancy test if applicable (avoid in pregnancy)

**Ongoing Monitoring:**
- **If on interacting medications:**
  - Therapeutic drug monitoring when available (e.g., tacrolimus, warfarin, theophylline)
  - Watch for signs of increased drug effects or toxicity
  - Monitor for reduced efficacy of affected drugs

- **Long-term use (>3 months):**
  - Periodic liver function tests
  - Assessment for adverse effects (GI symptoms, skin reactions, sleep disturbances)

- **Discontinuation:**
  - Monitor for 2 weeks after stopping (CYP450 normalization period)
  - Adjust interacting medication doses as needed

---

## Risk Stratification

### Low Risk Populations
- Healthy adults
- Not pregnant or lactating
- Not on CYP3A4/CYP1A2/CYP2C9/P-gp substrate medications
- Short-term use (<3 months)
- Moderate doses (1-2 g crude drug or equivalent)

**Action:** Standard dosing, routine monitoring

---

### Moderate Risk Populations
- Elderly
- Mild-moderate hepatic or renal impairment
- On medications with some CYP450 interaction potential but wide therapeutic index
- Long-term use (>3 months)

**Action:** Lower doses initially, closer monitoring, regular follow-up

---

### High Risk Populations (Contraindicated or Use Only with Close Monitoring)
- Pregnant or lactating
- On narrow therapeutic index drugs (tacrolimus, warfarin, theophylline, etc.)
- Severe hepatic or renal impairment
- Scheduled for surgery within 2 weeks
- History of uterine conditions (if female)

**Action:**
- **Avoid in pregnancy/lactation**
- **Avoid or use only with close monitoring** if on narrow therapeutic index drugs
- Consider alternative therapies
- If used, intensive monitoring required

---

## Summary: Key Safety Points

### Generally Safe When:
1. ✅ Used at moderate doses (1-6 g crude drug; 200-1000 mg extract daily)
2. ✅ Not pregnant or lactating
3. ✅ Not on interacting medications
4. ✅ Short to moderate duration (<3-6 months)
5. ✅ No severe organ impairment

### Exercise Caution When:
1. ⚠️ Using long-term (>6 months) - limited safety data
2. ⚠️ On any CYP3A4/CYP1A2/CYP2C9/P-gp substrate medications
3. ⚠️ Hepatic or renal impairment
4. ⚠️ Elderly or polypharmacy
5. ⚠️ Scheduled for surgery

### Avoid When:
1. ❌ Pregnant (risk of miscarriage)
2. ❌ Lactating (insufficient data)
3. ❌ On tacrolimus or other narrow therapeutic index immunosuppressants without close monitoring
4. ❌ Within 2 weeks of surgery
5. ❌ Children (insufficient data)
