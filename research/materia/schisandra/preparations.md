# Schisandra - Preparations and Dosing

## Traditional Dosing

### Crude Drug Decoction (Traditional Chinese Medicine)

**Dose Range:** 1.5-6 g/day

**Preparation:**
- Dried ripe fruit of *Schisandra chinensis* (Turcz.) Baill.
- Boiled in water to make decoction
- Traditional aqueous extraction method

**Source:** Traditional Chinese Medicine references; WHO Monograph, 2007

---

### Powdered Crude Drug (Boiled Tea)

**Dose Range:** 500-1500 mg/day

**Preparation:**
- Ground dried schisandra berries
- Prepared as boiled tea
- Water-based extraction

**Source:** Clinical dosing references

---

### Extract (Traditional Reference)

**Dose:** 200 mg/day

**Form:** Extract (preparation method not specified in traditional sources)

**Source:** Clinical references

---

## Clinical Trial Dosing (Evidence-Based)

### 1. Menopausal Symptoms

**Study:** Park & Kim, 2016 (Climacteric)

**Preparation:**
- **Extract:** BMO-30 extract from *Schisandra chinensis*
- **Dose:** Not specified in study
- **Duration:** 6 weeks treatment, 12 weeks follow-up
- **Sample Size:** 36 participants (18 treatment, 18 placebo)

**Outcomes:**
- Kupperman Index scores significantly lower (p=0.042)
- Particularly effective for hot flushes, sweating, heart palpitations

**Standardization:** Not specified

**Safety:** No serious adverse events

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27763802/

---

### 2. Muscle Strength and Exercise Performance

**Study:** Park, Han & Park, 2020

**Preparation:**
- **Dose:** **1000 mg/day extract** (500 mg twice daily)
- **Standardization:** **4.92 ± 0.06 mg/g schizandrin content**
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Sample Size:** 45 healthy post-menopausal women (24 treatment, 21 placebo)
- **Placebo:** 1000 mg/day starch

**Outcomes:**
- Quadriceps muscle strength: 7.7% improvement (p<0.001)
- Resting lactate level: Significantly decreased (p=0.045)

**Safety:** No adverse events; 7 dropouts unrelated to safety

**Calculation for Standardization:**
- 1000 mg extract × 4.92 mg/g = **~4.92 mg schizandrin per 1000 mg extract**
- Daily schizandrin intake: **~4.92 mg/day**

**Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7177795/

---

### 3. Liver Function Improvement

**Study:** Chiu et al., 2013 (Phytotherapy Research)

**Preparation:**
- **Formulation:** Schisandra fruit extract + sesamin mixture
- **Dose:** Four tablets daily
- **Duration:** 5 months
- **Sample Size:** 40 subjects with borderline high liver enzymes (20 treatment, 20 placebo)

**Outcomes:**
- ALT and AST clearly reduced
- Glutathione peroxidase and reductase activity significantly increased
- Fatty liver significantly improved

**Standardization:** Not specified; combination formula limits specific schisandra dosing information

**Safety:** No adverse events

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22610748/

---

### 4. Athletic Performance (Historical Reference)

**Dose per Tablet:**
- **Extract amount:** 91.1 mg
- **Standardization:** 3.1 mg schizandrin + gamma-schizandrin per tablet

**Calculation:**
- Schizandrin concentration: 3.1 mg / 91.1 mg = **~3.4% total schizandrins**

**Source:** Referenced in clinical dosing literature

---

### 5. Hepatitis (Clinical Reference)

**Dose:**
- **Lignan content:** 20 mg/day
- **Crude drug equivalence:** 20 mg lignans ≈ **1.5 g crude schisandra**

**Calculation Basis:**
- If crude schisandra contains ~1.3% lignans: 1.5 g × 1.3% ≈ 19.5 mg lignans

**Source:** Clinical dosing references

---

### 6. Hyperglycemia

**Study:** Yoon et al., 2022 (Nutrients)

**Preparation:**
- **Formulation:** Omija (*Schisandra chinensis*) extract + soybean mixture (OSM)
- **Dose:** Not specified
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Sample Size:** 80 participants with fasting plasma glucose 100-140 mg/dL

**Outcomes:**
- Significant decreases in fasting plasma glucose, postprandial glucose, insulin AUC, fructosamine, LDL cholesterol

**Standardization:** Not specified; combination formula

**Safety:** No clinically significant changes in any safety parameter

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35956334/

---

### 7. Gut Microbiota/Obesity

**Study:** Song et al., 2015 (Nutritional Research)

**Preparation:**
- **Material:** *Schisandra chinensis* fruit (SCF)
- **Dose:** Not specified
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Sample Size:** 28 obese women

**Outcomes:**
- Microbiota changes (Bacteroides increased, Ruminococcus decreased)
- Greater decreases in waist circumference, fat mass, glucose, triglycerides, liver enzymes (not statistically significant vs. placebo)

**Conclusion:** SCF not sufficient alone for significant obesity parameter changes

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26048342/

---

### 8. Cognitive Function (Combination Formula)

**Study:** Aslanyan et al., 2010 (Phytomedicine)

**Preparation:**
- **Formulation:** ADAPT-232 (combination of *Rhodiola rosea*, *Schisandra chinensis*, and *Eleutherococcus senticosus* extracts)
- **Dose:** 270 mg tablet (single dose)
- **Sample Size:** 40 healthy females, ages 20-68

**Outcomes:**
- d2 Test of Attention: Significant improvement (p<0.05)
- Increased speed and accuracy, reduced errors

**Note:** Cannot determine specific schisandra dose from combination formula

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20374974/

---

## Pharmacokinetic Study Dosing

### Drug Interaction Study (Tacrolimus)

**Study:** Xin et al., 2007

**Preparation:**
- **Extract:** *Schisandra sphenanthera* extract (SchE)
- **Dose:** Three capsules twice daily (total: **6 capsules/day**)
- **Note:** Specific mg dose per capsule not provided
- **Duration:** 13 days
- **Sample Size:** 12 healthy male volunteers

**Purpose:** Evaluate effect on tacrolimus pharmacokinetics

**Results:**
- Tacrolimus AUC increased 164.2%
- Tacrolimus Cmax increased 227.1%
- Demonstrates significant CYP3A and P-gp inhibition

**Clinical Dosing Reference (from same study context):**
- Common dosing for interaction studies: **300 mg twice daily** (600 mg/day total)

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17506780/

## Practical Dosing Guidelines

### Starting Doses (Conservative Approach)

**For Crude Drug/Tea:**
- **Start:** 1.5 g/day (lower end of traditional range)
- **Target:** 3-6 g/day based on response
- **Preparation:** Decoction (boil 10-15 minutes) or tea

**For Standardized Extract:**
- **Start:** 500 mg/day
- **Target:** 1000 mg/day (based on Park et al., 2020 muscle strength study)
- **Standardization:** Prefer ≥4-5 mg/g total schizandrins (0.4-0.5%)
- **Frequency:** Divide into 2 doses (morning and afternoon/evening)

**For Lignan-Standardized Products:**
- **Target:** 20 mg total lignans/day (based on hepatitis clinical reference)
- **Equivalence:** Approximately 1.5 g crude schisandra

---

### Dose Adjustments

**Sex Considerations:**
- Females may have higher bioavailability (Schisandrin B: 55% vs 19% in rats)
- Consider lower starting doses in females and titrate based on response
- More research needed for sex-specific dosing

**Duration:**
- **Short-term (acute effects):** Single dose to 6 weeks
- **Medium-term:** 12 weeks (most common in trials)
- **Long-term:** Up to 5 months documented in trials
- **Caution:** Long-term safety data >6 months limited

**Timing:**
- **With food:** May improve absorption due to poor water solubility and lipophilicity
- **Divided doses:** Twice daily may optimize bioavailability given first-pass metabolism
- **Avoid late evening:** May cause restlessness/insomnia in some individuals

## Tincture Preparations

### Traditional Tincture Ratios

**Note:** Specific tincture dosing not well-documented in clinical literature. The following are based on general herbal tincture preparation standards:

**Typical Ratios:**
- **1:5 ratio** (1 part herb to 5 parts menstruum)
- **Menstruum:** 25-40% alcohol (ethanol/water mixture)

**Estimated Tincture Dosing:**
- If using 1:5 tincture to achieve 1.5 g crude drug equivalent:
  - 1.5 g herb ÷ 1:5 ratio = 7.5 mL tincture
- If using 1:5 tincture to achieve 6 g crude drug equivalent:
  - 6 g herb ÷ 1:5 ratio = 30 mL tincture

**Typical Tincture Dose Range:** 2-8 mL, 2-3 times daily

**Limitation:** Tincture preparations not used in clinical trials; dosing extrapolated from crude drug equivalents

## Tea Preparation

### Traditional Tea Method

**Ingredients:**
- Dried schisandra berries: 1.5-6 g

**Preparation:**
1. Add berries to 1-2 cups (250-500 mL) water
2. Bring to boil
3. Simmer 10-15 minutes
4. Strain and drink

**Frequency:** 1-2 times daily

**Taste:** All five flavors (sour, sweet, salty, bitter, pungent) - may be challenging for some palates

---

### Powdered Tea Method

**Dose:** 500-1500 mg powdered berries

**Preparation:**
1. Add powder to hot water
2. Steep 5-10 minutes
3. Drink (may consume powder or strain)

**Frequency:** 1-2 times daily

## Quality Control and Product Selection

### What to Look For

**Standardized Extracts:**
1. **HPLC verification** of lignan content
2. **Certificate of Analysis (CoA)** available
3. **Schisandrol A ≥0.40%** (pharmacopoeial standard)
4. **Total schizandrins ≥4-5 mg/g** (based on Park et al., 2020)

**Crude Herb:**
1. **Botanical verification:** *Schisandra chinensis* (not adulterated with *S. sphenanthera* unless specified)
2. **Pharmacopoeial grade** when possible
3. **Proper storage:** Dried, protected from moisture
4. **Recent harvest:** Lignans may degrade over time

**Combination Products:**
1. Verify schisandra content and standardization
2. Check for interaction potential with other ingredients
3. Ensure each ingredient is appropriately dosed

---

### Red Flags (Avoid)

❌ **No standardization information**
❌ **No species identification** (just "schisandra")
❌ **No CoA available**
❌ **Unclear extract ratio or method**
❌ **Unrealistic claims** (cure-all, extreme effects)
❌ **Very low price** (suggests low quality or adulteration)

---

## Administration Tips

### Optimizing Absorption

1. **Take with fatty meal:** Poor water solubility suggests fat may enhance absorption
2. **Divide doses:** Twice daily may overcome first-pass metabolism limitations
3. **Consistent timing:** Same time each day for steady-state effects

### Monitoring Response

**What to Track:**
- **Target symptoms:** Energy, mental clarity, menopausal symptoms, muscle strength (depending on indication)
- **Adverse effects:** GI symptoms, skin reactions, sleep disturbances
- **Drug interactions:** Changes in effectiveness or side effects of other medications

**Timeline for Effects:**
- **Acute effects (mental/physical energy):** May notice within hours to days
- **Menopausal symptoms:** 6 weeks in trials
- **Muscle strength/exercise:** 12 weeks in trials
- **Liver function:** 5 months in trials

---

## Special Population Dosing

### Elderly
- **Start low:** Use lower end of dose range
- **Monitor closely:** Higher risk of polypharmacy and drug interactions
- **No specific adjustments:** Clinical trials included up to age 68 without specific dose changes

### Hepatic Impairment
- **Caution advised:** Liver is primary site of metabolism
- **Consider lower doses:** Accumulation risk in severe impairment
- **Monitor liver enzymes:** Regular testing advised

### Renal Impairment
- **Caution advised:** Kidney is site of accumulation and excretion route
- **Consider lower doses:** In severe impairment
- **No established adjustments:** Insufficient data

### Sex Differences
- **Females:** May have higher bioavailability; consider lower starting doses
- **Males:** Standard dosing
- **Monitor response:** Adjust based on individual response

---

## Duration of Use Recommendations

**Short-Term (Acute Use):**
- Single dose to 6 weeks
- For acute fatigue, mental performance, menopausal symptom relief

**Medium-Term:**
- 12 weeks (3 months)
- Most clinical trials use this duration
- Good safety data at this length

**Long-Term:**
- Beyond 3 months
- Up to 5 months documented in one trial (Chiu et al., 2013)
- **Caution:** Limited long-term safety data
- **Recommendation:** Periodic breaks (e.g., 1 week off every 3 months) may be prudent
- **Monitoring:** Regular liver function tests if used >3 months

---

## Summary: Practical Dosing Protocol

### Recommended Evidence-Based Approach

**For General Use (Fatigue, Adaptogenic Support):**
- **Form:** Standardized extract
- **Dose:** 500-1000 mg/day (divided into 2 doses)
- **Standardization:** ≥4-5 mg/g schizandrins
- **Timing:** With breakfast and lunch (avoid evening if sleep-sensitive)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks, then reassess

**For Menopausal Symptoms:**
- **Form:** BMO-30 extract or equivalent standardized product
- **Duration:** 6 weeks minimum, continue if effective
- **Note:** Specific product dosing may vary; follow manufacturer's evidence-based recommendations

**For Muscle Strength/Exercise:**
- **Form:** Standardized extract
- **Dose:** 1000 mg/day (500 mg twice daily)
- **Standardization:** ~4.92 mg/g schizandrin
- **Duration:** 12 weeks minimum
- **Timing:** Pre-workout and evening, or as divided dose

**For Liver Support:**
- **Form:** Lignan-standardized extract
- **Dose:** 20 mg lignans/day (or equivalent to 1.5 g crude schisandra)
- **Duration:** 5 months in study; ongoing use may be appropriate with monitoring
- **Monitoring:** Liver function tests every 3 months

**For Traditional Use (Tea/Decoction):**
- **Dose:** 1.5-6 g dried berries
- **Preparation:** Decoction (boil 10-15 minutes)
- **Frequency:** 1-2 times daily
- **Duration:** As needed; traditional use suggests ongoing use is safe at these doses
