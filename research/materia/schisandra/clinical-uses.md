# Schisandra - Clinical Uses

## Evidence-Based Clinical Applications

### Menopausal Symptoms
**Evidence Grade: B** (based on single RCT)

**Study:** Park & Kim, 2016 (Climacteric)
- **Sample:** 36 participants (18 treatment, 18 placebo)
- **Duration:** 6 weeks treatment, 12 weeks follow-up
- **Intervention:** BMO-30 extract from Schisandra chinensis (dose not specified)
- **Primary Outcome:** Kupperman Index (KI) total scores significantly lower in treatment group (p=0.042)
- **Symptoms Most Improved:**
  - Hot flushes (particularly effective)
  - Sweating (particularly effective)
  - Heart palpitations (particularly effective)
- **Safety:** No serious adverse events reported
- **Conclusion:** Safe and effective complementary medicine for menopausal symptoms

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27763802/

---

### Muscle Strength and Exercise Performance
**Evidence Grade: B** (based on single RCT)

**Study:** Park, Han & Park, 2020 (Int J Environ Res Public Health)
- **Sample:** 45 healthy post-menopausal women (24 treatment, 21 placebo)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Dose:** 1000 mg/day extract (500 mg twice daily)
- **Standardization:** 4.92 ± 0.06 mg/g schizandrin content
- **Primary Outcomes:**
  - Quadriceps muscle strength: **7.7% improvement** (p<0.001)
  - Resting lactate level: **Significantly decreased** (p=0.045)
- **Safety:** No adverse events reported; 7 dropouts unrelated to safety (adherence/consent withdrawal)
- **Conclusion:** Safety confirmed through institutional review

**Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7177795/

---

### Liver Function Improvement
**Evidence Grade: B** (based on RCT but combination formula)

**Study:** Chiu et al., 2013 (Phytotherapy Research)
- **Sample:** 40 subjects with borderline high liver enzymes (20 treatment, 20 placebo)
- **Duration:** 5 months
- **Intervention:** Four tablets daily (schisandra fruit extract + sesamin mixture)
- **Outcomes:**
  - **ALT:** Clearly reduced
  - **AST:** Clearly reduced
  - **Bilirubin:** Remained unchanged
  - **Antioxidant markers:**
    - Glutathione peroxidase activity: Significantly increased
    - Glutathione reductase activity: Significantly increased
    - Free radical levels: Decreased
    - LDL oxidation lag time: Increased
  - **Imaging:** Fatty liver significantly improved
- **Safety:** No adverse events reported

**Note:** Combination formula limits attribution solely to schisandra

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22610748/

### Mental Fatigue and Cognitive Function
**Evidence Grade: B** (nonrandomized clinical trials)

**Basis:**
- 7 trials (N=1,712) single dose administration
- 1 trial (n=665) repeated administration
- Evidence level: IIa to III

**Outcomes:** Improvement in endurance and mental performance

**RCT on Attention (Combination Formula):**
**Study:** Aslanyan et al., 2010 (Phytomedicine)
- **Sample:** 40 healthy females, ages 20-68
- **Intervention:** ADAPT-232 single dose (270 mg tablet: combination of Rhodiola rosea, Schisandra chinensis, and Eleutherococcus senticosus extracts)
- **Assessment:** Baseline and 2 hours post-treatment
- **Outcome:**
  - d2 Test of Attention (TN-E scores): Significant difference (p<0.05)
  - Improved attention, increased speed and accuracy
  - Tendency to reduce error percentages
- **Safety:** No serious adverse events; sleepiness and cold extremities in both groups

**Note:** Combination formula limits specific attribution to schisandra

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20374974/

### Hyperglycemia Management
**Evidence:** Single RCT (combination formula)

**Study:** Yoon et al., 2022 (Nutrients)
- **Sample:** 80 participants with fasting plasma glucose 100-140 mg/dL
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Intervention:** Omija (Schisandra chinensis) extract + soybean mixture (OSM) - dose not specified
- **Outcomes (All Significant):**
  - Fasting plasma glucose (FPG): Decreased
  - Postprandial glucose at 30 min: Decreased
  - Postprandial glucose at 60 min: Decreased
  - Postprandial insulin at 60 min: Decreased
  - Insulin area under curve (AUC): Decreased
  - Fructosamine: Decreased
  - LDL cholesterol: Decreased
- **Safety:** No clinically significant changes in any safety parameter
- **Conclusion:** Safe and effective for improving hyperglycemia

**Note:** Combination formula (schisandra + soybean)

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35956334/

### Gut Microbiota Modulation in Obesity
**Evidence:** Single RCT with modest effects

**Study:** Song et al., 2015 (Nutritional Research)
- **Sample:** 28 obese women
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Intervention:** Schisandra chinensis fruit (SCF) - dose not specified
- **Anthropometric/Metabolic Results:** Greater decreases in SCF group but **not statistically significant**:
  - Waist circumference
  - Fat mass
  - Fasting blood glucose
  - Triglycerides
  - Liver enzymes (AST, ALT)
- **Microbiota Changes:**
  - **Increased:** Bacteroides and Bacteroidetes (negative correlation with fat mass and liver markers)
  - **Decreased:** Ruminococcus (negative correlation with blood glucose and HDL cholesterol)
- **Conclusion:** SCF not sufficient to induce significant changes in obesity-related parameters compared with placebo despite microbiota modulation
- **Safety:** No adverse events reported

**Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26048342/

## Preclinical Evidence (Animal Studies)

### Liver Injury Protection
**Meta-Analysis:** Huang et al., 2025 (Frontiers in Pharmacology)

**Scope:**
- 54 animal studies
- 1,314 animals total
- 8 databases searched

**Animal Models:**
- C57BL/6 mice (13 studies)
- ICR mice (20 studies)
- Kunming rats (5 studies)
- Sprague-Dawley rats (6 studies)
- BALB/c mice (1 study)
- Wistar rats (1 study)

**Liver Injury Types:**
- Acetaminophen-induced
- CCl4-induced
- D-galactosamine-induced
- Ethanol-induced

**Liver Enzyme Reduction (All p<0.001):**
- **ALT:** SMD -4.74 [95% CI: -5.42, -4.06] (I²=90.8%)
- **AST:** SMD -5.10 [95% CI: -5.84, -4.37] (I²=91.7%)
- **ALP:** SMD -3.11 [95% CI: -4.86, -1.37] (I²=87.0%)

**Oxidative Stress Markers (All p<0.001):**
- **SOD increase:** SMD 4.37 [95% CI: 3.38, 5.37] (I²=89.3%)
- **MDA decrease:** SMD -3.42 [95% CI: -4.16, -2.69] (I²=89.7%)
- **GSH increase:** SMD 3.07 [95% CI: 2.35, 3.80] (I²=84.0%)

**Inflammatory Markers (All p<0.001):**
- **TNF-α reduction:** SMD -2.85 [95% CI: -3.73, -1.96] (I²=76.3%)
- **IL-6 reduction:** SMD -3.30 [95% CI: -4.43, -2.17] (I²=86.7%)
- **IL-1β reduction:** SMD -2.41 [95% CI: -3.75, -1.07] (I²=85.4%)

**Conclusion:** Schisandra chinensis bioactive compounds significantly reduced liver injury markers across multiple animal models

**Limitations:**
- High heterogeneity in studies
- Limited human clinical trial data

**Source:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pharmacology/articles/10.3389/fphar.2025.1627081/full

## Research Gaps and Future Directions

### Critical Needs:
1. **Large-Scale RCTs:** Well-designed trials for liver disease applications
2. **Long-Term Safety:** Studies with standardized extracts over extended periods
3. **Dose-Response Studies:** To establish optimal dosing across indications
4. **Bioavailability Enhancement:** Human studies on absorption and delivery systems
5. **Precise Molecular Mechanisms:** Especially for hepatoprotection
6. **Sex-Dependent Effects:** Mechanisms underlying bioavailability differences
7. **Comprehensive Drug Interaction Profiles:** Beyond tacrolimus

### Quality Control Needs:
- Standardization protocols for lignan content
- Quality control methods for commercial products
- Advanced drug delivery systems to enhance bioavailability

## Summary of Evidence Quality

**Strong Evidence (Grade B):**
- Mental fatigue (multiple trials, N=1,712-665)
- Menopausal symptoms (single well-designed RCT)
- Muscle strength/exercise performance (single well-designed RCT)

**Moderate Evidence (Grade B/C):**
- General fatigue, asthenia, weakness (5 trials, N=406, non-randomized)
- Liver function improvement (single RCT with combination formula)

**Preliminary Evidence:**
- Hyperglycemia management (single RCT, combination formula)
- Gut microbiota modulation (single RCT, non-significant metabolic outcomes)

**Preclinical Only:**
- Liver injury protection (strong animal data, 54 studies, limited human translation)
