# Black Walnut - Preparations

## Overview

All therapeutic preparations use the **unripe green hull (pericarp)** of Juglans nigra. This is the only preparation approved by Health Canada's NHPID. The hull must be harvested while still green (unripe) — juglone content and biological activity decline significantly as the hull ripens and blackens.

Other plant parts (leaf, bark, nut meat, inner shell) have different phytochemical profiles and distinct traditional applications not covered by regulatory approval.

---

## Regulatory-Approved Preparation

### Health Canada NHPID (2022) — Traditional Vermifuge Use

- **Part**: Unripe hull (pericarp)
- **Forms permitted**: Dried hull, powdered hull, or non-standardized ethanolic extract (tincture, fluid extract, dry extract)
- **Dose**: **0.65–2.75 g/day** (expressed as dried hull equivalent)
- **Population**: Adults 18 years and older
- **Duration**: Occasional use; consult practitioner if exceeding 14 days
- **Standardization**: Non-standardized only (juglone content not specified or required)

## Tincture Preparations

### Tincture 1:4 at 25% Ethanol
- **Source**: Naturopathic herbalist tradition; practitioner use
- **Dose**: 5–10 drops, 3× daily
- **Weekly maximum**: 10–15 mL/week
- **Cycling**: 2 weeks on / 2 weeks off (mutagenicity risk management)
- **Note**: Practitioner-only in some jurisdictions; restricted use due to mutagenicity concerns
- **Conversion**: At 1:4 ratio, 5–10 drops (~0.25–0.5 mL per dose) corresponds to 0.06–0.12 g hull per dose; 3× daily = 0.18–0.36 g/day hull equivalent

### Fresh Plant Liquid Extract 1:1 (Green Hull)
- **Source**: Traditional Western herbalism; practitioner use
- **Dose**: 1–10 drops, 1–3× daily in water
- **Note**: Most potent commercially available form; restricted to trained practitioners
- **Conversion**: At 1:1 ratio, 1–10 drops in water is a very low dose by volume; practitioners adjust by individual tolerance
- **Key feature**: Fresh plant extraction preserves juglone in reduced (active) form before oxidation to the blackened quinone

### Liquid Extract 1:2 (Green Hull)
- **Source**: Southern Cross University (SCU) / Australian naturopathic herbalism
- **Dose**: 1.5–5.5 mL/day
- **Therapeutic dose range**: 1.5 mL/day (low/maintenance) to 5.5 mL/day (active treatment)
- **Note**: Traditional practitioner dosing; not clinically validated
- **Conversion**: At 1:2 ratio, 1.5–5.5 mL corresponds to 0.75–2.75 g hull equivalent/day — aligns with Health Canada dose range

## Infusion / Decoction

### Hull or Bark Infusion
- **Source**: SCU monograph; traditional use
- **Material**: 30 g dried hull or bark material
- **Preparation**: Add to 500 mL cold water; bring to gentle simmer; steep 15–20 minutes; strain
- **Dose**: 200 mL, 3× daily (600 mL total / day)
- **Calculation**: 30 g in 600 mL daily = 30 g herb/day — significantly above Health Canada's 2.75 g/day maximum for internal use
- **Important caveat**: Juglone is relatively insoluble in water and degrades in acidic/gastric conditions; aqueous infusions may have substantially reduced juglone bioavailability compared to ethanolic extracts. EMA data for J. regia leaf infusions indicates juglone is present at ~0.1% and degrades under acidic conditions.
- **Traditional use context**: High-tannin bark decoctions have primarily topical/gargle applications in traditional practice; internal use at these doses is outside modern safety guidelines

### Bark Decoction for External Use
- **Preparation**: 30 g inner bark in 500 mL water; simmer 20 minutes; cool
- **Application**: Topical wash for skin conditions, ringworm, athlete's foot; gargle for gum disease
- **Dose**: As needed topically
- **Safety note**: External use avoids systemic juglone absorption concerns

## Capsule / Powder

### Dried Hull Powder (Capsule Form)
- **Source**: Commercial products; Health Canada-compliant form
- **Dose**: **0.65–2.75 g/day** (Health Canada NHPID)
- **Typical commercial capsule sizes**: 250–500 mg per capsule
- **Example dosing at 500 mg capsules**: 2–5 capsules per day for anthelmintic use (1.0–2.5 g/day)
- **Duration**: 14 days maximum for self-directed use
- **Standardization**: No pharmacopoeial standard; "non-standardized" product quality varies by manufacturer

---

## Topical Applications

### Undiluted Tincture (Topical)
- Applied directly to affected skin for fungal infections (athlete's foot, nail fungus, ringworm)
- Juglone staining of skin is inevitable; warn users
- Contact sensitization risk from repeated application

### Hull Juice (Topical)
- Fresh green hull squeezed or crushed; juice applied to skin lesions
- Strongest topical preparation; significant staining
- Contact dermatitis risk — test small area first
- **Handling safety**: Use gloves when processing fresh hulls; juglone penetrates latex gloves; use nitrile gloves

## Standardization

**No pharmacopoeial standardization exists for black walnut hull.**

### Proposed Standardization Markers
- **Primary**: Juglone content (most pharmacologically active but unstable)
- **Secondary**: Total ellagic acid content
- **Tertiary**: Total hydrolysable tannin content

### Why Standardization Is Absent
1. **Juglone instability**: The naphthoquinone undergoes rapid oxidation during processing, drying, and storage; standardized juglone content cannot be reliably maintained in commercial products
2. **Toxicity concerns**: Standardizing to a specific juglone dose would be problematic given its mutagenicity; Health Canada explicitly permits "non-standardized" ethanolic extracts only
3. **No pharmacopoeia entry**: Without USP, BP, or EP monograph, there is no official reference standard to comply with

### Quality Considerations for Commercial Products
- Source material should be harvested from unripe (green) hull; ripe/blackened hulls have lower juglone content
- Ethanolic extraction better preserves juglone than aqueous extraction
- Nitrogen-purged or refrigerated storage reduces juglone oxidation
- Products showing significant brown/black discoloration may indicate juglone oxidation and reduced potency

---

## Handling Safety

- **Gloves required**: Nitrile gloves for processing fresh hulls (juglone penetrates latex)
- **Eye protection**: Fresh hull juice can cause ocular irritation
- **Staining**: Juglone permanently stains wood, concrete, fabric, and skin brown/black
- **Outdoor processing recommended**: Juglone in fresh hull preparations can damage nearby plants (allelopathic)
