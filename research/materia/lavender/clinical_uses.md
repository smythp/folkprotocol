# Lavandula angustifolia (Lavender) - Clinical Uses

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

### Oral Silexan vs Placebo and Paroxetine
**Study:** Kasper et al., 2014 (PMID: 24456909)
- **Sample:** 539 participants with DSM-5 GAD, HAMA ≥18
- **Duration:** 10 weeks
- **Dosing:**
  - Silexan 160mg daily
  - Silexan 80mg daily
  - Paroxetine 20mg daily
  - Placebo
- **Results:**
  - **Silexan 160mg:** 14.1 ± 9.3 point HAMA reduction (p < 0.01 vs placebo)
  - **Silexan 80mg:** 12.8 ± 8.7 point HAMA reduction (p < 0.01 vs placebo)
  - **Paroxetine:** 11.3 ± 8.0 point HAMA reduction (p = 0.10 vs placebo)
  - **Placebo:** 9.5 ± 9.0 point HAMA reduction
- **Response rates (≥50% reduction):**
  - Silexan 160mg: 60.3%
  - Silexan 80mg: 51.9%
  - Paroxetine: 43.2%
  - Placebo: 37.8%
- **Safety:** Adverse event incidence density per day:
  - Silexan 160mg: 0.006
  - Silexan 80mg: 0.008
  - Paroxetine: 0.011
  - Placebo: 0.008

### Oral Silexan vs Lorazepam
**Study:** Woelk & Schläfke, 2010 (PMID: 19962288)
- **Condition:** Generalized anxiety disorder
- **Duration:** 6 weeks
- **Dosing:**
  - Silexan: Oral lavender oil capsule (standardized preparation)
  - Lorazepam: Benzodiazepine comparator
- **Results:**
  - **Silexan:** 11.3 ± 6.7 point HAMA reduction (45% reduction)
  - **Lorazepam:** 11.6 ± 6.6 point HAMA reduction (46% reduction)
  - Comparable improvements in both groups
- **Safety:** Silexan showed no sedative effects and no potential for drug abuse

### Meta-Analysis: Anxiety Effects
**Study:** Donelli et al., 2019 (PMID: 31655395)
- **Trials included:** 90 studies (65 RCTs, 25 non-randomized)
- **Participants:** 9,193 total (7,993 in RCTs + 1,200 in non-randomized); 3,964 in meta-analysis
- **Results:**
  - **Lavender inhalation for anxiety:** Hedges' g = -0.73 [95% CI -1.00 to -0.46], p < 0.00001
  - **State anxiety (STAI-State):** Mean difference = -5.99 [95% CI -9.39 to -2.59], p < 0.001
  - **Trait anxiety (STAI-Trait):** Mean difference = -8.14 [95% CI -14.44 to -1.84], p < 0.05
  - **Massage with lavender oil:** Hedges' g = -0.66 [95% CI -0.97 to -0.35], p < 0.0001
  - **Oral Silexan 80mg:** Showed benefit on Hamilton Anxiety Scale

**Study:** Kang et al., 2019 (PMID: 31743795)
- **Trials included:** 22 RCTs
- **Results:**
  - **Self-rated anxiety:** Hedges' ĝ = -0.65 (95% CI, -0.84 to -0.46)
  - **Systolic blood pressure:** ĝ = -0.22 (95% CI, -0.43 to -0.02)
  - **Heart rate:** ĝ = -0.53 (95% CI, -0.74 to -0.32)
  - **Salivary cortisol:** ĝ = -1.29 (95% CI, -2.23 to -0.35)
  - **Chromogranin A levels:** ĝ = -2.29 (95% CI, -3.24 to -1.34)

**Study:** Kim et al., 2021 (PMID: 34775136)
- **Results:**
  - **Anxiety reduction:** Hedges' ĝ = -0.72 (95% CI -0.90 to -0.55, p <.001)
  - **Depression reduction:** Hedges' ĝ = -0.43 (95% CI, -0.59 to -0.27, p <.001)
  - **Systolic blood pressure:** Hedges' ĝ = -0.23 (95% CI, -0.41 to -0.05, p = .01)
  - Treatment sessions accounted for 13.2% of heterogeneity in anxiolytic effects

## Subsyndromal Anxiety

**Study:** Kasper et al., 2010 (PMID: 20512042)
- **Sample:** 221 adults with subsyndromal anxiety disorder
- **Sites:** 27 general and psychiatric practices
- **Duration:** 10 weeks with visits every 2 weeks
- **Dosing:**
  - Silexan: 80mg daily, oral
  - Placebo: Matched placebo
- **Results:**
  - **Hamilton Anxiety Scale (HAMA):**
    - Silexan: 16.0 ± 8.3 point decrease (59.3% reduction)
    - Placebo: 9.5 ± 9.1 point decrease (35.4% reduction)
  - **Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI):**
    - Silexan: 5.5 ± 4.4 point decrease (44.7%)
    - Placebo: 3.8 ± 4.1 point decrease (30.9%)
  - **Response rate:** 76.9% vs 49.1% placebo
  - **Remission rate:** 60.6% vs 42.6% placebo
- **Safety:** Alleviated anxiety-related disturbed sleep without unwanted sedative or drug-specific effects

## Mixed Anxiety-Depression

**Study:** Kasper et al., 2016 (PMID: 26718792)
- **Sample:** 318 adult outpatients with mixed anxiety-depression
- **Duration:** 70 days
- **Dosing:**
  - Silexan: 80mg daily, oral
  - Placebo: Matched placebo
- **Results:**
  - **Hamilton Anxiety Rating Scale (HAMA):**
    - Silexan: 10.8 ± 9.6 point decrease
    - Placebo: 8.4 ± 8.9 point decrease (p<0.01)
  - **Montgomery Åsberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS):**
    - Silexan: 9.2 ± 9.9 point decrease
    - Placebo: 6.1 ± 7.6 point decrease (p<0.001)
- **Outcomes:** Better overall clinical outcome with improved daily functioning and quality of life
- **Adverse events:** Eructation was primary adverse event with higher incidence in Silexan group

## Major Depression (Mild-to-Moderate)

### Oral Silexan vs Sertraline
**Study:** Kasper et al., 2025 (PMID: 38558147)
- **Sample:** 498 patients with mild-to-moderate major depression
  - Silexan n=170
  - Sertraline n=171
  - Placebo n=157
- **Duration:** 56 days (8 weeks)
- **Dosing:**
  - Silexan: 80mg once daily, oral
  - Sertraline: 50mg daily
  - Placebo: Matched placebo
- **Results:**
  - **MADRS reduction difference vs placebo:**
    - Silexan: 2.17 points (95% CI: 0.58-3.76; p<0.01)
    - Sertraline: 2.59 points (95% CI: 1.02-4.17; p<0.01)
  - **Sheehan Disability Scale:** Silexan improved by 2.40 points vs placebo (p<0.001)
- **Adverse events:** Eructation most frequent adverse effect; both treatments well tolerated

### Meta-Analysis: Depression Effects
**Study:** Firoozeei et al., 2021 (PMID: 33549687)
- **Trials included:** 17 RCTs from 342 screened
- **Results:**
  - **Overall effect:** pooled SMD = -0.66 (95% CI: -0.85 to -0.46; P < 0.001)
  - **Diagnosed depression subgroup:** pooled SMD = -0.62 (95% CI: -1.26 to 0.01, P = 0.055)
  - **Other diseases with depressive symptoms:** pooled SMD = -0.65 (95% CI: -1.84 to -0.46, P < 0.001)
  - **Oral administration route:** pooled SMD = -0.56 (95% CI: -1.07 to -0.05, P = 0.032)
- **Safety:** Lavender has less serious side effects compared to conventional antidepressants

## Stress Reduction

### Meta-Analysis
**Study:** Ghavami et al., 2022 (PMID: 35429599)
- **Trials included:** 21 studies
- **Participants:** 791 in intervention groups, 804 in control groups
- **Results:**
  - **Overall stress reduction:** SMD 0.63 ± 0.13 (95% CI), P < 0.001
  - **L. angustifolia species showed highest effect:** SMD 0.73 ± 0.22
  - **Student populations demonstrated strongest results:** SMD 2.27 ± 1.34
  - **Perceived Stress Scale measurement:** SMD 0.82 ± 0.42
- **Conclusion:** Lavender significantly reduces stress and could be integrated into stress management programs

## Sleep Enhancement

### Inhalation for Sleep Issues
**Study:** Lillehei et al., 2015 (PMID: 26133206)
- **Sample:** 79 college students with self-reported sleep issues
- **Duration:** 5 nights with baseline, post-intervention, and 2-week follow-up
- **Dosing:**
  - Lavender: Chest inhalation patches with 55 μl lavender essential oil, worn nightly
  - Control: Blank patches
  - Both groups: Good sleep hygiene practices
- **Results:**
  - **Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI):**
    - Post-intervention: p=0.01
    - Follow-up: p<0.001
  - **PROMIS sleep disturbance:**
    - Post-intervention: p=0.04
    - Follow-up: p=0.007
  - **"Waking feeling refreshed":** p=0.01
  - Sleep quantity: No significant differences
- **Adverse events:** None reported

### Meta-Analysis: Sleep Quality
**Study:** Shen et al., 2025 (PMID: 40600743)
- **Trials included:** 11 RCTs
- **Participants:** 628 total
- **Results:**
  - **Sleep enhancement:** SMD = -0.56 (95% CI [-0.96, -0.17], P = .005)
  - Significant improvement in sleep quality with lavender essential oil
- **Note:** Conclusion requires verification in more high-quality studies due to constraints in quantity and quality

## Anxiety and Sleep in Hypertensive Population

**Study:** Wang et al., 2025 (PMID: 41177368)
- **Sample:** 58 hypertensive adults aged 40-60 years
  - Intervention n=30
  - Placebo n=28
- **Duration:** Single acute session plus 7 consecutive days
- **Dosing:**
  - Lavender: Lavender essential oil inhaled via magnetic face mask clip, 15 minutes daily
  - Placebo: Sweet almond oil
- **Results:**
  - **Anxiety (STAI):**
    - Immediately post-intervention: -15.7 vs -0.9 (p<.001)
    - After 7 days: -15.6 vs -0.6 (p<.001)
  - **Sleep Quality (VHS):**
    - After 7 days: +165.0 vs +4.7 (p<.001)
  - Additional: Fatigue (CFQ), systolic/diastolic blood pressure measured
- **Adverse events:** None reported

## Route of Administration Comparison

### Network Meta-Analysis
**Study:** Sayed et al., 2020 (PMID: 32088511)
- **Trials included:** 40 studies analyzed (32 in quantitative analysis)
- **Routes compared:** Aromatherapy/inhalation, oral (Silexan 80mg), topical/massage, foot bath
- **Results:**
  - **Short-term (first week):** Aromatherapy superior
    - SMD = -0.57 (95% CI -1.14 to 0.01), P-score = 0.72
  - **Long-term:** Silexan (oral) 80mg most favorable
    - First rank probability = 0.116
  - **At study endpoint:** Massage + foot bath most effective
    - SMD = -1.10 (95% CI -7.41 to 5.21), P-score = 0.65
- **Conclusions:**
  - Lavender aromatherapy clinically superior for short-duration
  - Silexan (oral lavender) 80mg preferable for long-term anxiety treatment

## Clinical Pharmacology Review

**Study:** Kasper et al., 2018 (PMID: 28511598)
- **Trials reviewed:** 7 clinical trials, 3 preclinical publications
- **Treatment duration:** 6-10 weeks
- **Formulation:** Lavender oil preparation in 80-mg capsules
- **Clinical efficacy:**
  - Anxiolytic effects appeared after 2 weeks
  - HAM-A total score reductions significantly superior to placebo in subthreshold anxiety
  - Effects comparable to lorazepam or paroxetine in GAD patients
  - Benefits extended to impaired sleep, somatic complaints, comorbid depression, decreased quality of life
- **Safety:** Mild gastrointestinal symptoms only; no sedation or withdrawal symptoms at 80-160 mg daily
- **Drug interactions:** Did not cause drug interactions

## Summary Dosing by Indication

### Anxiety (Generalized Anxiety Disorder)
- **Oral Silexan:** 80-160mg daily for 6-10 weeks
- **Aromatherapy:** 55 μl patches or 15-minute sessions daily for 5-7 days (short-term)
- **Onset:** Oral effects appear after 2 weeks; aromatherapy effects immediate to within hours

### Subsyndromal Anxiety
- **Oral Silexan:** 80mg daily for 10 weeks
- **Response rate:** 76.9% (221 participants, 59.3% HAMA reduction)

### Mixed Anxiety-Depression
- **Oral Silexan:** 80mg daily for 70 days
- **Results:** Significant improvements in both anxiety and depression scales

### Depression (Mild-to-Moderate)
- **Oral Silexan:** 80mg once daily for 8 weeks
- **Comparable to:** Sertraline 50mg daily

### Stress Reduction
- **Route:** Various (inhalation, oral)
- **Effect size:** SMD 0.63 (L. angustifolia SMD 0.73)
- **Best response:** Student populations (SMD 2.27)

### Sleep Enhancement
- **Inhalation:** 55 μl patches worn nightly for 5 nights minimum
- **Oral Silexan:** 80mg daily (demonstrated sleep improvement in anxiety trials)
- **Effect size:** SMD -0.56

### Hypertension with Anxiety
- **Inhalation:** 15 minutes daily for 7 days
- **Results:** 27.9% anxiety reduction (15.7 point STAI decrease, 58 participants)
