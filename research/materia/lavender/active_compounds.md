# Lavandula angustifolia (Lavender) - Active Compounds & Bioavailability

## Primary Active Compounds

### Major Constituents of Lavender Essential Oil

**Source:** German Commission E Monograph (1984/1990), European Pharmacopoeia standards

**Main components:**
1. **Linalyl acetate** (ester)
2. **Linalool** (alcohol)
3. **Camphor**
4. **β-ocimene** (terpene)
5. **1,8-cineol** (eucalyptol)

**Additional constituents:**
- Approximately 12% tannins unique to Lamiaceae family (in dried flowers)

### Linalool and Linalyl Acetate

**Significance:** These two compounds are the primary active constituents responsible for lavender's therapeutic effects.

**Enantiomer Specificity:**
- European Pharmacopoeia specifications include specific requirements for linalool and linalyl acetate enantiomers
- The specific stereochemistry of these compounds is important for activity

**Safety Profile:**
**Study:** Nataf et al., 2023 (PMID: 37906319)
- **Study type:** In vitro endocrine activity assessment
- **Components tested:** Linalool and linalyl acetate (main lavender components)
- **Findings:** No significant estrogenic or androgenic activity detected
- **Quality:** 2023 publication, proactive testing strategy addressing historical concerns

**Cytotoxicity Considerations:**
**Study:** Prashar et al., 2004 (PMID: 15144499)
- **Study type:** In vitro cytotoxicity study
- **Cell types:** Human endothelial cells and fibroblasts
- **Findings:**
  - Lavender oil cytotoxic to human skin cells at 0.25% (v/v) concentration in all cell types tested
  - Linalool may be the active component responsible for cytotoxicity
  - In vitro findings; clinical relevance unclear
- **Implications:** Concerns about potential irritant or allergenic skin reactions with topical use at high concentrations

**Standardization:**
- Consistent chemical composition across batches
- Pharmaceutical-grade quality control
- Reproducible therapeutic effects

**Key Constituents:**
- Linalyl acetate
- Linalool

- β-ocimene
- 1,8-cineol

**Capsule Strength:** 80 mg (standard dose)

**Manufacturer Quality:** Pharmaceutical-grade standardization ensures batch-to-batch consistency, which is critical for clinical efficacy and safety.

**Clinical Validation:** Extensive RCT evidence base supports the efficacy of this specific standardized preparation (see clinical_uses.md for details).

**Study Reference:** Kasper et al., 2018 (PMID: 28511598)
- **Review type:** Clinical and preclinical review of Silexan
- **Formulation:** Lavender oil preparation in 80-mg capsules
- **Treatment duration in trials:** 6-10 weeks
- **Mechanism:** Preclinical investigations support anxiolytic use
- **Drug interactions:** Did not cause drug interactions

## Bioavailability

### Oral Lavender Oil (Silexan)

**Study Reference:** Kasper et al., 2018 (PMID: 28511598)

**Notes:**
- Bioavailability depends on formulation
- Capsule formulation standardized for consistency
- Active constituents: linalyl acetate, linalool, camphor, β-ocimene, 1,8-cineol
- No significant first-pass metabolism issues reported

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Onset of anxiolytic effects: 2 weeks after initiation of 80-160 mg daily oral Silexan
- This relatively rapid onset suggests adequate bioavailability and CNS penetration

**Pharmacokinetics:**
- Specific pharmacokinetic parameters (Cmax, Tmax, half-life) not detailed in available clinical literature
- Efficacy data suggests clinically relevant CNS concentrations are achieved with oral dosing

### Experimental Formulations (Increased Bioavailability)

#### Lavender Oil-Based Lipid Nanocapsules
**Reference:** PMID: 36839811

**Enhancement:**
- Transdermal delivery showed 4-fold increase in Cmax vs oral suspension
- Bioavailability increased up to 52%
- Sustained release for 3 days

**Clinical Status:** Experimental, not in clinical use

**Significance:** Demonstrates potential for enhanced delivery systems, though current clinical evidence is based on conventional formulations.

#### Lavender Oil Nanoemulsion
**Reference:** PMID: 34060397

**Enhancement:**
- Nearly twice the permeability of standard formulation
- Relative bioavailability 180% vs marketed cream

**Clinical Status:** Investigational formulation

**Significance:** Research-stage technology; not yet validated in clinical trials.

### Inhalation/Aromatherapy Route

**Bioavailability Considerations:**
- Volatile compounds (linalool, linalyl acetate, camphor, 1,8-cineol) readily enter the respiratory system
- Direct absorption through nasal mucosa and lungs
- Likely some systemic absorption, though olfactory/limbic system effects may be primary mechanism
- Rapid onset of effects (minutes to hours) suggests efficient delivery via this route

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Immediate to short-term effects (first week) documented in clinical trials
- Effects on anxiety (STAI scores), heart rate, blood pressure, salivary cortisol demonstrate systemic bioactivity

**Study Reference:** Wang et al., 2025 (PMID: 41177368)
- **Dosing:** 15 minutes daily inhalation
- **Immediate effects:** STAI anxiety reduction of 15.7 points (58 participants)
- Demonstrates rapid bioavailability and CNS effects

### Topical/Massage Route

**Bioavailability Considerations:**
- Percutaneous absorption of volatile oil constituents
- Linalool and linalyl acetate are small lipophilic molecules capable of skin penetration
- Combined with massage, may enhance local and systemic absorption

**Safety Consideration:**
- Cytotoxicity at 0.25% (v/v) concentration in vitro (Prashar et al., 2004, PMID: 15144499)
- Clinical formulations typically use lower concentrations (1-3% dilution in carrier oil)
- Contact dermatitis possible in sensitized individuals

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Meta-analysis (Donelli et al., 2019, PMID: 31655395): Massage with lavender oil showed Hedges' g = -0.66 for anxiety reduction
- Suggests clinically relevant systemic effects via topical route

## Drug Metabolism and Interactions

### CYP450 Enzyme Interactions

**Study:** Doroshyenko et al., 2013 (PMID: 23401474)
- **Study design:** Double-blind, randomized, 2-fold crossover trial
- **Sample size:** 16 healthy male and female Caucasians
- **Intervention:** Silexan 160 mg once daily for 11 days
- **Enzymes tested:** CYP1A2, CYP2C9, CYP2C19, CYP2D6, CYP3A4
- **Probe drugs:** Cocktail of probe substrates for each CYP enzyme

**Key Findings:**
- Repeated silexan (160 mg/day) has no clinically relevant inhibitory or inducing effects on CYP1A2, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, and 3A4 enzymes in vivo
- No significant interactions with most enzyme phenotyping metrics
- Omeprazole (CYP2C19) showed minor variability, likely inherent pharmacokinetic fluctuation

**Clinical Significance:** Low risk of drug-drug interactions via major CYP pathways

**Implications:**
- Lavender (Silexan) can be safely co-administered with most medications metabolized by CYP450 enzymes
- Does not induce or inhibit major drug metabolism pathways
- No dose adjustments needed for co-administered drugs

## Summary of Active Compounds

### Primary Active Constituents
1. **Linalyl acetate** - ester, major constituent
2. **Linalool** - alcohol, major constituent
3. **Camphor** - ketone
4. **β-ocimene** - terpene
5. **1,8-cineol** (eucalyptol) - ether

### Standardization
- **Silexan:** Pharmaceutical-grade, 80mg capsules, batch-consistent
- **Essential oil:** European Pharmacopoeia standards with enantiomer specifications
- **Dried flower:** Minimum 13 ml/kg essential oil (Ph. Eur.) or 1.5% (v/w) (Commission E)

### Bioavailability
- **Oral (Silexan):** Adequate bioavailability, 2-week onset for anxiolytic effects, no significant first-pass issues
- **Inhalation:** Rapid onset (minutes to hours), direct respiratory and olfactory absorption
- **Topical:** Percutaneous absorption, combined with massage for enhanced effect
- **Experimental formulations:** Up to 4-fold increase in Cmax and 52-180% bioavailability enhancement (not yet in clinical use)

### Drug Interactions
- **CYP450 enzymes:** No clinically significant interactions with CYP1A2, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, 3A4
- **Clinical significance:** Low risk of drug-drug interactions, safe co-administration with most medications

### Safety
- **Linalool/linalyl acetate:** No significant estrogenic or androgenic activity (Nataf et al., 2023)
- **Cytotoxicity:** In vitro cytotoxicity at 0.25% (v/v); clinical relevance unclear
- **Contact sensitization:** Possible with topical use; patch test recommended
