# Lavandula angustifolia (Lavender) - Preparations & Dosing

## Standardized Oral Preparations (Evidence-Based)

### Silexan (Pharmaceutical-Grade Standardized Extract)

**Description:** Proprietary standardized lavender oil preparation used in clinical trials

**Formulation:**
- Lavender oil in pharmaceutical-grade capsules
- Standardized for consistent chemical composition
- Key constituents: linalyl acetate, linalool, camphor, β-ocimene, 1,8-cineol

**Capsule Strength:** 80 mg (standard); 160 mg (studied in some trials)

**Clinical Evidence Base:** Extensive RCT validation with multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses

---

### Dosing by Indication

#### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
**Standard Dose:** 80 mg once daily
**Higher Dose (studied):** 160 mg once daily
**Duration:** 6-10 weeks (typical trial duration)
**Onset of effect:** 2 weeks

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Kasper et al., 2014 (PMID: 24456909)** - 539 participants
  - Silexan 160mg: 14.1 ± 9.3 point HAMA reduction (60.3% response rate)
  - Silexan 80mg: 12.8 ± 8.7 point HAMA reduction (51.9% response rate)
  - Comparable or superior to paroxetine 20mg
- **Woelk & Schläfke, 2010 (PMID: 19962288)**
  - Silexan: 11.3 ± 6.7 point HAMA reduction (45% reduction)
  - Comparable to lorazepam (11.6 ± 6.6 point reduction)

#### Subsyndromal Anxiety
**Standard Dose:** 80 mg once daily
**Duration:** 10 weeks (with visits every 2 weeks)
**Onset of effect:** 2 weeks

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Kasper et al., 2010 (PMID: 20512042)** - 221 participants
  - 16.0 ± 8.3 point HAMA decrease (59.3% reduction)
  - Response rate: 76.9%
  - Remission rate: 60.6%
  - Also improved sleep (PSQI): 5.5 ± 4.4 point decrease (44.7%)

#### Mixed Anxiety-Depression
**Standard Dose:** 80 mg once daily
**Duration:** 70 days
**Onset of effect:** Progressive over 70 days

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Kasper et al., 2016 (PMID: 26718792)** - 318 participants
  - HAMA: 10.8 ± 9.6 point decrease
  - MADRS: 9.2 ± 9.9 point decrease
  - Improved daily functioning and quality of life

#### Major Depression (Mild-to-Moderate)
**Standard Dose:** 80 mg once daily
**Duration:** 56 days (8 weeks)
**Onset of effect:** Progressive over 8 weeks

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Kasper et al., 2025 (PMID: 38558147)** - 498 participants
  - MADRS reduction: 2.17 points vs placebo (p<0.01)
  - Comparable to sertraline 50mg (2.59 points vs placebo)
  - Sheehan Disability Scale: 2.40 point improvement vs placebo

---

### Administration Notes for Oral Silexan

**Timing:** Typically once daily (specific time of day not critical; morning or evening acceptable)

**With/Without Food:** Not specified in clinical trials; likely can be taken with or without food

**Duration of Treatment:**
- **Acute treatment:** 6-10 weeks (typical trial duration)
- **Maintenance:** Longer-term use not extensively studied but no safety signals suggest problems
- **Discontinuation:** No tapering required; no withdrawal symptoms

**Adverse Effects:** Mild gastrointestinal symptoms (eructation/belching) most common; generally well-tolerated

**Drug Interactions:** None significant (no CYP450 interactions)

**Availability:** Silexan is a proprietary preparation marketed in various countries (check local availability)

## Essential Oil Preparations (Evidence-Based)

### Inhalation/Aromatherapy

#### Method 1: Inhalation Patches
**Formulation:** Lavender essential oil on adhesive patches
**Dose:** 55 μl lavender essential oil per patch
**Administration:** Apply to chest, worn nightly
**Duration:** 5 nights minimum (effects sustained at 2-week follow-up)

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Lillehei et al., 2015 (PMID: 26133206)** - 79 college students
  - Improved PSQI (p<0.001 at follow-up)
  - Improved PROMIS sleep disturbance (p=0.007 at follow-up)
  - "Waking feeling refreshed" (p=0.01)
  - No adverse events

**Indications:** Sleep issues, sleep quality improvement

---

#### Method 2: Direct Inhalation (Face Mask/Clip)
**Formulation:** Lavender essential oil via magnetic face mask clip
**Dose:** 15-minute inhalation session
**Frequency:** Daily
**Duration:** Single session (acute effects) to 7 consecutive days

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Wang et al., 2025 (PMID: 41177368)** - 58 hypertensive adults
  - Immediate anxiety reduction: STAI -15.7 points (p<.001)
  - After 7 days anxiety reduction: STAI -15.6 points (p<.001)
  - After 7 days sleep quality: VHS +165.0 (p<.001)
  - No adverse events

**Indications:** Acute anxiety, anxiety in hypertensive patients, sleep quality

---

#### Method 3: Diffusion/Ambient Inhalation
**Formulation:** Lavender essential oil diffused in air
**Dose:** Variable (dependent on diffuser, room size)
**Duration:** Acute sessions (minutes to hours)

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Multiple studies in meta-analyses used diffusion methods
- **Donelli et al., 2019 (PMID: 31655395)** - Meta-analysis
  - Lavender inhalation: Hedges' g = -0.73 for anxiety reduction
  - State anxiety (STAI-State): mean difference = -5.99 points
  - Trait anxiety (STAI-Trait): mean difference = -8.14 points

**Indications:** Acute anxiety, stress reduction, relaxation

**Note:** Specific dosing (drops, duration) varies across studies; general approach is 10-30 minute inhalation sessions

---

### Route Comparison (Network Meta-Analysis)

**Study:** Sayed et al., 2020 (PMID: 32088511) - 40 studies

**Findings:**
- **Short-term (first week):** Aromatherapy/inhalation superior (SMD = -0.57, P-score = 0.72)
- **Long-term:** Oral Silexan 80mg most favorable (first rank probability = 0.116)
- **Study endpoint:** Massage + foot bath most effective (SMD = -1.10, P-score = 0.65)

**Clinical Recommendation:**
- Use inhalation/aromatherapy for **acute, short-term anxiety relief** (immediate to 1 week)
- Use oral Silexan for **long-term anxiety management** (weeks to months)

---

### Topical/Massage Preparations

#### General Formulation
**Formulation:** Lavender essential oil diluted in carrier oil
**Typical Dilution:** 1-3% lavender essential oil in carrier oil
**Example:** 10-30 drops lavender essential oil per 1 oz (30 ml) carrier oil

**Carrier Oils:**
- Sweet almond oil
- Jojoba oil
- Coconut oil (fractionated)
- Grapeseed oil
- Any neutral carrier oil suitable for massage

**Application:** Topical massage to areas of tension (shoulders, neck, back) or general relaxation massage

---

#### Clinical Evidence
**Meta-Analysis:**
- **Donelli et al., 2019 (PMID: 31655395)**
  - Massage with lavender oil: Hedges' g = -0.66 for anxiety reduction (p < 0.0001)

**Indications:** Anxiety reduction, relaxation, stress relief

**Safety Considerations:**
- Patch test recommended for individuals with sensitive skin or allergy history
- Lavender is an uncommon but documented contact sensitizer
- In vitro cytotoxicity at 0.25% (v/v); clinical formulations at 1-3% generally well-tolerated
- Avoid undiluted essential oil application

## Traditional Preparations

### Lavender Flower Tea (Historical/Traditional)

**Botanical Source:** Dried flowers of Lavandula angustifolia (Lavandulae flos)

**Quality Standards:**
- European Pharmacopoeia Ph. Eur. 01/2008:1534: Not less than 13 ml/kg essential oil (dried material basis)
- German Commission E: At least 1.5% (v/w) essential oil

**Traditional Preparation Method:**
1. Use 1-2 teaspoons dried lavender flowers per cup (approximately 2-4 grams)
2. Pour boiling water over flowers
3. Steep for 5-10 minutes
4. Strain and drink

**Traditional Dosing:**
- 1-3 cups daily
- Often consumed before bedtime for sleep support

**Traditional Indications (Commission E, 1984/1990):**
- Restlessness and difficulties in sleeping
- Functional disorders of upper abdomen
- Irritable stomach and intestinal disorders of nervous origin

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Limited modern clinical trials using tea preparations
- Most clinical evidence is for standardized oral extracts (Silexan) or essential oil inhalation
- Traditional use recognized by Commission E, EMA, WHO

**Note:** Tea preparations are not standardized and provide variable amounts of active constituents. For evidence-based dosing, standardized preparations (Silexan) or measured essential oil inhalation are preferred.

---

### Lavender Tincture (Historical/Traditional)

**Botanical Source:** Lavandula angustifolia flowers and/or essential oil

**Traditional Preparation Method (Dried Flowers):**
1. Fill jar 1/3 to 1/2 with dried lavender flowers
2. Cover with 80-proof alcohol (40% ethanol) or higher
3. Seal and store in cool, dark place
4. Shake daily
5. Strain after 4-6 weeks

**Traditional Preparation Method (Essential Oil):**
1. Dilute lavender essential oil in alcohol base
2. Typical ratio: 1 part essential oil to 4-5 parts alcohol (1:4 or 1:5)

**Traditional Dosing:**
- 2-4 ml (approximately 40-80 drops) 2-3 times daily
- Often taken in small amount of water

**Traditional Indications:**
- Similar to tea: restlessness, sleep difficulties, nervous tension, nervous stomach

**Clinical Evidence:**
- No modern clinical trials specifically testing tincture preparations
- Most evidence is for standardized capsules or inhalation
- Traditional use recognized by herbal medicine systems

**Note:** Tinctures provide alcohol extraction of lavender constituents. Standardization and potency are variable. For evidence-based dosing, use standardized preparations.

---

### Essential Oil (Lavandulae aetheroleum) - Pharmaceutical Grade

**Source:** Steam distillation of Lavandula angustifolia flowering tops

**Quality Standards:** European Pharmacopoeia Ph. Eur. 10.0 (07/2018)

**Key Constituents (specified percentages):**
- Linalyl acetate
- Linalool (with enantiomer ratios specified)

**EMA Recognition:** Community Herbal Monograph, 2012
- **URL:** https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/medicines/herbal/lavandulae-aetheroleum
- Recognized for traditional medicinal use

**Uses:**
- Inhalation/aromatherapy (see inhalation section above)
- Topical application when diluted (see topical section above)
- **Not for internal use** unless in pharmaceutical-grade capsule form (e.g., Silexan)

**Storage:** Store in dark glass bottle, away from light and heat, tightly sealed

**Safety:** Use diluted for topical applications; patch test recommended; do not ingest undiluted essential oil

## Clinical Practice Recommendations

### First-Line Recommendations (Evidence-Based)

**For Chronic Anxiety/Depression (weeks to months):**
- **Oral Silexan 80 mg once daily** for 6-10 weeks minimum
- Strong clinical evidence, comparable to SSRIs and benzodiazepines
- Excellent safety profile, no drug interactions
- Onset: 2 weeks

**For Acute Anxiety/Stress (immediate to 1 week):**
- **Lavender inhalation/aromatherapy**
  - 15-minute sessions daily, OR
  - 55 μl patches worn nightly, OR
  - 10-30 minute diffusion sessions as needed
- Rapid onset (minutes to hours)
- No adverse effects reported in trials

**For Sleep Issues:**
- **Option 1:** Oral Silexan 80 mg once daily (if comorbid anxiety)
- **Option 2:** Lavender inhalation patches 55 μl nightly
- Both show evidence for sleep improvement

**For Relaxation/Massage Therapy:**
- **1-3% lavender essential oil in carrier oil**
- Apply via massage to areas of tension
- Patch test recommended for sensitive individuals

---

### Second-Line/Complementary Options

**Traditional Preparations (tea, tincture):**
- May be used for mild symptoms or as adjunct
- Less reliable potency and standardization
- Recognized by traditional medicine authorities (Commission E, EMA, WHO)
- Use when standardized preparations not available or for cultural/patient preference reasons

## Quality Assurance

### Selecting Quality Products

**For Oral Use:**
- Prefer pharmaceutical-grade standardized extracts (Silexan or equivalent)
- Look for products with clinical trial backing
- Check for third-party quality testing and certification

**For Essential Oil (Inhalation/Topical):**
- European Pharmacopoeia Ph. Eur. 10.0 compliant oils preferred
- Look for:
  - "Lavandula angustifolia" (not Lavandula x intermedia or "lavandin")
  - "Steam distilled"
  - Third-party testing (GC-MS analysis)
  - Dark glass bottles
  - Clear constituent percentages (linalyl acetate, linalool)

**For Dried Flowers (Tea):**
- European Pharmacopoeia compliant: Not less than 13 ml/kg essential oil
- Organic certification preferred to minimize pesticide exposure
- Store in airtight container away from light and moisture

---

## Special Considerations

### Pregnancy and Lactation
- **Insufficient evidence** for safety in pregnancy and lactation with therapeutic doses
- **Food-use amounts** (culinary) generally recognized as safe (FDA GRAS)
- **Clinical recommendation:** Caution advised; consult healthcare provider before therapeutic use

### Children
- No endocrine disruption risk (recent large population studies)
- Dosing in children not extensively studied in clinical trials
- Inhalation/aromatherapy likely lower risk than oral preparations
- Consult pediatric healthcare provider for therapeutic use

### Elderly
- No specific contraindications
- May be preferred over benzodiazepines (no sedation, no fall risk)
- Standard adult dosing appears appropriate

### Drug Interactions
- **No clinically significant CYP450 interactions**
- Safe to combine with SSRIs, benzodiazepines, and most medications
- No dose adjustments needed for co-administered drugs

## Preparation Storage and Shelf Life

**Silexan/Capsules:**
- Store in cool, dry place
- Follow manufacturer expiration dates
- Typically 2-3 years shelf life

**Essential Oil:**
- Store in dark glass bottle
- Keep tightly sealed
- Store in cool, dark location (not refrigerated unless opened frequently)
- Shelf life: 2-3 years unopened; 1-2 years after opening
- Oxidation reduces potency; discard if aroma changes significantly

**Dried Flowers:**
- Store in airtight container
- Keep away from light, heat, and moisture
- Shelf life: 1-2 years
- Potency decreases over time; fresher is better

**Tinctures:**
- Store in dark glass bottle
- Keep tightly sealed
- Alcohol preserves; shelf life 3-5 years or longer
- Minimal degradation if stored properly

## Summary

**Strongest Clinical Evidence:**
1. **Oral Silexan 80-160 mg daily** for anxiety, depression, mixed presentations (6-10 weeks)
2. **Lavender inhalation/aromatherapy** for acute anxiety, stress, sleep (immediate to 7 days)

**Traditional Preparations:**
- Tea, tinctures recognized by regulatory authorities but lack standardized clinical trials
- Appropriate for mild symptoms or when standardized preparations unavailable

**Route Selection:**
- **Oral (Silexan):** Long-term anxiety/depression management
- **Inhalation:** Acute anxiety, stress, sleep support
- **Topical/Massage:** Relaxation, anxiety reduction, complementary therapy

**Safety:**
- Excellent safety profile across all routes
- Minimal adverse effects (mild GI symptoms with oral use)
- No drug interactions
- Preferred over conventional anxiolytics/antidepressants in many cases due to superior safety

**Quality:**
- Use pharmaceutical-grade standardized preparations for oral use
- Use European Pharmacopoeia-compliant essential oils for inhalation/topical
- Ensure proper storage to maintain potency
