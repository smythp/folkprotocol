# Turmeric (Curcuma longa) - Clinical Uses

## Evidence Overview

Clinical evidence for turmeric comes from 103 randomized controlled trials with 7,216 participants (Jafari 2024). Of 42 analyzed outcomes, 55% showed statistically significant improvements. High-certainty evidence exists for four primary outcomes: fasting blood sugar, C-reactive protein, HDL cholesterol, and weight.

## Glycemic Control

**Evidence Level**: High-certainty

**Key Findings** (Jafari 2024, 103 RCTs, 7,216 participants):
- Significant improvements in fasting blood sugar (FBS)
- Effects on glycemic indices demonstrated across multiple studies

**Typical Dosing**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin or curcuminoids
**Treatment Duration**: 4-36 weeks

## Inflammatory Conditions

### General Anti-Inflammatory Effects

**Evidence Level**: Moderate (mixed results across studies)

**Positive Findings** (Dehzad 2023, 66 RCTs):
- C-reactive protein (CRP): reduced by 0.58 mg/L
- Tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α): reduced by 3.48 pg/mL
- Interleukin-6 (IL-6): reduced by 1.31 pg/mL
- Interleukin-1 beta (IL-1β): no significant change

**Contradictory Findings** (White 2019, 19 RCTs, 1,344 participants):
- CRP: -2.71 mg/L (95% CI -5.73 to 0.31, p=0.08) - not significant
- hsCRP: -1.44 mg/L (95% CI -2.94 to 0.06, p=0.06) - not significant
- IL-1β: -4.25 pg/mL (95% CI -13.32 to 4.82, p=0.36) - not significant
- IL-6: -0.71 pg/mL (95% CI -1.68 to 0.25, p=0.15) - not significant
- TNF-α: -1.23 pg/mL (95% CI -3.01 to 0.55, p=0.18) - not significant

**Note**: Heterogeneity in study designs, populations, and formulations may explain contradictory results.

**Typical Dosing**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin
**Treatment Duration**: 4-16 weeks

## Antioxidant Support

**Evidence Level**: Moderate

**Key Findings** (Dehzad 2023, 66 RCTs):
- Total antioxidant capacity (TAC): improved by 0.21 mmol/L
- Malondialdehyde (MDA): reduced by 0.33 µmol/L
- Superoxide dismutase (SOD): increased by 20.51 U/L

**Typical Dosing**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin
**Treatment Duration**: 4-16 weeks

## Arthritis (All Types)

**Evidence Level**: Moderate

**Types Studied**: Osteoarthritis (OA), Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA), Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS), Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA), Gout/Hyperuricemia

**Key Findings** (Zeng 2022, 29 RCTs, 2,396 participants):
- Improved severity of inflammation
- Reduced pain levels
- Safe across all 29 studies

**Dosing Range**: 120-1500 mg/day curcumin or Curcuma longa extract
**Treatment Duration**: 4-36 weeks
**Safety**: Well-tolerated across all trials

## Musculoskeletal Health

**Evidence Level**: Moderate to High

**Key Findings** (Doyle 2023, 21 RCTs):
- Turmeric extract and curcumin effective as adjuvants for musculoskeletal health
- Statistical significance (P < 0.0001) for both musculoskeletal and joint health outcomes
- Low incidence of adverse events

**Most Effective Formulation**: Water-dispersible turmeric extract (WDTE60N)
**Optimal Dose**: 250 mg/day
**Evidence**: 7 RCTs on skeletal muscle health, 14 RCTs on joint health

### Knee Osteoarthritis (Theracurmin)

**Formulation-Specific Finding**:
- 75.6% treatment effectiveness with Theracurmin formulation
- Dose: 180 mg/day
- Duration: 6 months

## Cardiovascular Health

**Evidence Level**: Moderate

### Blood Pressure

**Key Findings** (Dehzad 2024, 35 RCTs):
- Systolic blood pressure: reduced by 2.02 mmHg
- Diastolic blood pressure: reduced by 0.82 mmHg

### Endothelial Function

**Key Findings** (Dehzad 2024, 35 RCTs):
- Flow-mediated vasodilation: improved by 2.00%
- Vascular cell adhesion molecule-1: improved
- No significant change in ICAM-1 or pulse-wave velocity

### Lipid Profile

**Evidence Level**: High-certainty

**Key Findings** (Jafari 2024):
- HDL cholesterol: significant improvement (high-certainty evidence)
- Effects on other lipid parameters demonstrated

**Typical Dosing**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin
**Treatment Duration**: Variable

## Weight Management

**Evidence Level**: High-certainty

**Key Findings** (Jafari 2024, 103 RCTs, 7,216 participants):
- Significant effects on body weight (high-certainty evidence)

**Typical Dosing**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin
**Treatment Duration**: Variable

## Standardized Extract Performance

### BCM-95 (Biocurcumax)
- **Bioavailability**: 6.93× higher free curcumin AUC vs standard curcumin
- **Applications**: Multiple myeloma, multiple sclerosis, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, prediabetic conditions, knee osteoarthritis
- **Safety**: Well-tolerated with reduced GI side effects compared to diclofenac or paracetamol
- **Dosing**: Variable by indication

### Meriva (Curcumin-Phospholipid Complex)
- **Bioavailability**: 29× higher total curcuminoid absorption (18× in human volunteers)
- **Applications**: Joint health, inflammation management
- **Safety**: Well-tolerated
- **Dosing**: Variable by indication

### Theracurmin
- **Bioavailability**: 185× higher compared to unformulated powder; 11× higher than BCM-95, 4.6× higher than Meriva in head-to-head trials
- **Applications**: Knee osteoarthritis, general inflammatory conditions
- **Dosing**: 180 mg/day for osteoarthritis
- **Safety**: Safe and well-tolerated at doses from 2 g/day to 12 g/day for 6 months to 1 year

## Dosing Summary by Condition

**Arthritis**: 120-1500 mg/day curcumin or extract, 4-36 weeks

**Musculoskeletal Health**: 250 mg/day water-dispersible extract (most effective); or 180 mg/day Theracurmin

**Cardiovascular Support**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin, variable duration

**Inflammatory Conditions**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin, 4-16 weeks

**Glycemic Control**: 500-2000 mg/day curcumin, 4-36 weeks

## Critical Notes

1. **Bioavailability is crucial**: Unformulated curcumin has extremely poor bioavailability. Enhanced formulations (BCM-95, Meriva, Theracurmin) or adjuvants (piperine) are necessary for therapeutic effects.

2. **Study heterogeneity**: Large variation in study designs, populations, formulations, and dosing makes direct comparisons difficult.

3. **Industry bias concerns**: Critical analyses (Khosravi 2024, 293 studies reviewed) raise concerns that positive bioavailability results are primarily obtained in studies "in proximity to pharmaceutical industry."

4. **Need for longer trials**: Most trials are 4-36 weeks. Long-term safety and efficacy data is limited.
