# Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) - Preparations and Dosing

## Evidence-Based Preparation Forms

### 1. Elderberry Syrup

**Clinical trial formulation:**

**Zakay-Rones 2004 (Positive RCT):**
- Form: Elderberry syrup (Sambucol)
- Dose: 15 ml four times daily
- Duration: 5 days
- Population: Adults 18-54 years
- Outcome: Symptoms relieved 4 days earlier than placebo

**Macknin 2020 (Negative RCT):**
- Form: Elderberry syrup
- Dose (Adults 13+): 15 ml four times daily
- Dose (Children 5-12): 15 ml twice daily
- Duration: 5 days
- Population: Ages 5+ with influenza
- Outcome: NO significant benefit (conflicts with earlier study)

**Zakay-Rones 1995 (Positive RCT):**
- Form: Sambucol elderberry extract
- Outcome: 93.3% improvement within 2 days vs 6 days for placebo

**Standardization:** Not specified in syrup formulations from bibliography

**Volume per dose:** 15 ml
**Frequency:** 2x/day (children 5-12) to 4x/day (adults)
**Total daily volume:** 30-60 ml/day

### 2. Standardized Extract Capsules

**Tiralongo 2016 (Positive RCT - Prevention):**
- Form: Capsules containing standardized elderberry extract
- Standardization:
  - 22% polyphenols
  - 15% anthocyanins
  - Total extract per capsule: 300 mg
  - Anthocyanin content per capsule: 45 mg

**Dosing protocol:**
- **Priming phase (10 days pre-travel):**
  - 2 capsules daily (600 mg extract)
  - 90 mg anthocyanins/day

- **Treatment phase (travel + 4-5 days post-arrival):**
  - 3 capsules daily (900 mg extract)
  - 135 mg anthocyanins/day

- **Total duration:** 15-16 days

**Outcome:**
- 2-day reduction in cold duration
- 50% reduction in symptom severity
- 57 vs 117 cold episode days (p=0.02)

### 3. Elderberry Juice/Concentrate

**Wu 2002 (Bioavailability study):**
- Form: Elderberry juice concentrate
- Dose: 150 ml single dose
- Anthocyanin content: 3.57 g total anthocyanins
- Note: This was a bioavailability study, not a clinical efficacy trial

**Vlachojannis 2015 (Standardization study):**
- Form: Elderberry concentrate
- Required volume: Only 11 ml daily
- Note: Highly concentrated products more practical than large-volume juices

**Comparison:**
- Elderberry concentrate: 11 ml/day adequate
- Chokeberry juice: 100 ml/day required (lower concentration)

### 4. Elderflower Tea/Infusion (Traditional)

**Traditional preparation (WHO 2002, German Commission E):**
- Plant part: Elderflower (Flos), NOT berry
- Form: Hot infusion/tea
- Traditional use: Diaphoretic (sweat-inducing), expectorant
- Indications: Fever, chills, upper respiratory inflammation

**Dosing:** Not specified with precision in bibliography

**Note:** Traditional use focuses on FLOWER, not fruit. Modern clinical research focuses on FRUIT extracts, which represent a departure from traditional preparations.

**Quality standard:**
Elderflower defined in European Pharmacopoeia (Ph. Eur.)

## Standardization Requirements

### Anthocyanin Content (Critical Marker)

**Clinical trial standardization:**
- Target: 15-22% anthocyanins
- Daily anthocyanin dose: 90-135 mg in positive trials

**Minimum effective doses (Vlachojannis 2015):**
- Metabolic syndrome: 110 mg/day
- Influenza: 3.5 g/day (26-39x higher than typical trial doses)

**Major anthocyanins to verify:**
1. Cyanidin-3-glucoside
2. Cyanidin-3-sambubioside

### Polyphenol Content

**Tiralongo 2016 standardization:**
- 22% polyphenols
- Used alongside 15% anthocyanin standardization

### Analytical Verification

**Recommended method:** HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography)

**Vlachojannis 2015 findings:**
- Analyzed 9 products using HPLC
- Three products inappropriate for clinical use (insufficient anthocyanin content)
- Significant variability among commercial products

**Implication:** Not all elderberry products are equivalent. Verify analytical testing.

## Dosing by Indication

### Active Upper Respiratory Infection - Treatment

**Influenza (positive trials - 1995, 2004):**
- Form: Elderberry syrup
- Dose: 15 ml four times daily
- Duration: 5 days
- Timing: Initiate within 48 hours of symptom onset
- Expected outcome: Symptom relief 2-4 days earlier than placebo

**Common cold (Tiralongo 2016 - positive):**
- Form: Standardized capsules (15% anthocyanins)
- Dose: 900 mg extract daily (3 capsules, 135 mg anthocyanins)
- Duration: During illness episode
- Expected outcome: 2-day reduction in duration, 50% symptom reduction

**NOTE:** Macknin 2020 found NO benefit with 15 ml syrup 2-4x/day, contradicting earlier studies

### Prevention (Pre-exposure Prophylaxis)

**Air travelers/high-risk exposure (Tiralongo 2016):**
- Form: Standardized capsules (15% anthocyanins)
- Priming dose: 600 mg extract daily (90 mg anthocyanins) for 10 days before travel
- Active dose: 900 mg extract daily (135 mg anthocyanins) during and after travel
- Total duration: 15-16 days
- Outcome: 50% reduction in symptom severity, 2-day shorter duration if cold develops

### Dosing by Age

**Adults (≥13 years):**
- Syrup: 15 ml four times daily (60 ml/day total)
- Capsules: 600-900 mg extract daily (90-135 mg anthocyanins)
- Duration: 5 days treatment, up to 16 days prevention

**Children (5-12 years):**
- Syrup: 15 ml twice daily (30 ml/day total)
- Duration: 5 days
- Note: Based on Macknin 2020 (which found NO benefit)

**Children (<5 years):**
- No clinical trial data available
- Safety not established
- NOT recommended

## Preparation Safety Requirements

### Processing Requirements (Essential)

**MUST USE COOKED/PROCESSED BERRIES:**
- Cooked berries: Safe (no cyanide risk)
- Raw berries: May contain cyanogenic glycosides
- Unripe berries: AVOID (contain toxic constituents)

**Plant parts to NEVER use:**
- Bark: Contains sambunigrin (cyanogenic glycoside)
- Roots: Contains sambunigrin
- Leaves: Contains sambunigrin
- Risk: Cyanide poisoning

### Quality Control Markers

**When selecting products, verify:**
1. **Anthocyanin standardization:** 15-22% (or 90-135 mg/day)
2. **HPLC testing:** Analytical verification of content
3. **GMP certification:** Good manufacturing practices
4. **Processed berries only:** No raw plant parts
5. **Third-party testing:** Independent verification

**Red flags:**
- No standardization information
- "Whole berry" without anthocyanin content specified
- Raw or unprocessed berry products
- No analytical testing documentation

## Tea/Infusion Dosing

**Traditional elderflower tea:**
- Form: Dried elderflowers (Flos)
- Preparation: Hot water infusion
- Traditional uses: Diaphoretic, expectorant
- Precise dosing: NOT specified in bibliography

**Note:** Traditional preparations use FLOWER, not fruit. Modern clinical efficacy data focuses on fruit extracts. These are distinct preparations with different traditional indications.

**EMA 2014 finding:**
Elderberry FRUIT could not establish traditional use monograph due to insufficient data on traditional posology (dosing) and strength.

## Cost-Effectiveness Considerations

**Elderberry concentrate vs chokeberry juice (Vlachojannis 2015):**
- Elderberry: 11 ml/day required (high concentration)
- Chokeberry: 100 ml/day required (lower concentration)

**Implication:**
Concentrated elderberry products more practical and potentially more cost-effective due to lower volumes needed.

**Zakay-Rones 2004 conclusion:**
"Elderberry extract seems to offer an efficient, safe and cost-effective treatment for influenza"

## Preparation Selection Algorithm

**For treatment of active infection:**
1. Choose standardized product (15-22% anthocyanins)
2. Verify HPLC testing
3. Calculate anthocyanin dose (target 90-135 mg/day)
4. Select form:
   - Syrup: 15 ml 2-4x/day (depending on age)
   - Capsules: 900 mg extract (135 mg anthocyanins) daily
5. Duration: 5 days
6. Timing: Within 48 hours of symptom onset

**For prevention (high-risk exposure):**
1. Use standardized capsules (15% anthocyanins)
2. Priming: 600 mg extract daily (90 mg anthocyanins) for 10 days before exposure
3. Active: 900 mg extract daily (135 mg anthocyanins) during and after exposure
4. Total: 15-16 days

**For traditional diaphoretic/expectorant use:**
1. Use elderflower (Flos), not fruit
2. Preparation: Hot infusion/tea
3. Note: Precise dosing not established; modern clinical data focuses on fruit, not flower

## Critical Dosing Insights

### What Works (Based on Positive Trials):
- 15 ml elderberry syrup 4x/day for 5 days (Zakay-Rones 2004)
- 600-900 mg standardized extract (90-135 mg anthocyanins) daily for 15-16 days (Tiralongo 2016)
- Initiation within 48 hours of symptom onset (Harnett 2020)

### What Didn't Work:
- 15 ml syrup 2-4x/day in Macknin 2020 (NO benefit, contradicts earlier studies)

### Unresolved Questions:
- Why did identical dosing (15 ml 4x/day) work in 2004 but not 2020?
- Possible factors: Product variability, different patient populations, different viral strains
- Optimal anthocyanin dose ranges from 110 mg to 3.5 g/day across different sources

## Bioavailability-Informed Dosing

**Given low bioavailability (<0.5%):**
- Multiple daily doses necessary (4x/day in positive trials)
- Short half-life (1.74-2.57 hours) requires frequent dosing
- Single daily dose likely insufficient
- Split dosing (2-4x/day) maintains metabolite levels

**Implication:**
Once or twice daily dosing may not be adequate despite convenience. Positive trials used 4x/day dosing for adults.

## Research Gaps in Preparation Science

1. **Optimal preparation method:** Which extraction method preserves anthocyanins best?
2. **Tincture dosing:** No clinical trial data on alcohol-based tinctures
3. **Combination products:** Effects when combined with other herbs/supplements unknown
4. **Long-term dosing:** Safety and efficacy beyond 2-3 weeks not established
5. **Elderflower dosing:** Traditional use recognized but precise dosing not documented
6. **Stability:** How do different preparation methods affect shelf life and potency?

## Bottom Line Recommendations

**Evidence-based preparation choices:**
1. **First choice:** Standardized extract capsules (15-22% anthocyanins, 90-135 mg/day)
2. **Second choice:** Elderberry syrup with verified anthocyanin content
3. **Avoid:** Unstandardized products, raw preparations, products without analytical testing

**Dosing strategy:**
- Treatment: Multiple doses per day (2-4x depending on age)
- Prevention: Daily dosing 10 days before through exposure period
- Duration: Limit to 5 days (treatment) or 2-3 weeks (prevention)
- Timing: Within 48 hours of symptom onset for best results

**Critical caveat:**
Most recent RCT (Macknin 2020) found NO benefit with standard dosing, contradicting earlier positive studies. This unresolved conflict means evidence quality is UNCERTAIN despite multiple positive trials.
