# Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) - Preparations and Dosing

## Standardized Extract Preparations

### Dry Extracts (Capsules/Tablets)

**European Pharmacopoeia Specification:**
- **Solvents:** Ethanol (40-90% V/V), methanol (60% V/V), or acetone (40% V/V)
- **Minimum content:** 2.0% flavonoids expressed as vitexin
- **Form:** Powder for encapsulation or tableting

**Commercial Standardization:**
- **Typical:** 3.5% flavonoids as vitexin
- **Alternative:** 2.5-4% flavonoids as isovitexin
- **Higher than minimum** to ensure consistent activity

---

### Clinical Trial Doses (Standardized Extracts)

**Generalized Anxiety Disorder:**
- **Akhondzadeh et al. 2001:** 45 drops daily liquid extract
  - Duration: 4 weeks
  - Efficacy comparable to oxazepam 30 mg
  - Extract ratio not specified

**Preoperative/Surgical Anxiety:**
- **Movafegh et al. 2008:** 500 mg orally (Passipy IranDarouk)
  - Timing: 90 minutes before surgery
  - Single dose
  - No sedation, effective anxiolysis

**Dental Anxiety:**
- **Dantas et al. 2017:** 260 mg orally
  - Timing: 30 minutes pre-surgery
  - Single dose
  - Comparable to midazolam 15 mg without amnesia

**Opioid Withdrawal:**
- **Akhondzadeh et al. 2001:** 60 drops daily liquid extract
  - Duration: 14 days
  - Adjuvant to clonidine
  - Improved mental symptoms

**Sleep/Insomnia:**
- **Harit et al. 2024:** 600 mg daily SIVI extract at bedtime
  - Duration: 30 days
  - Improved sleep time and stress
  - No adverse effects
- **Lee et al. 2020:** Passionflower extract (dose not fully specified)
  - Duration: 2 weeks
  - Increased total sleep time by ~23 minutes (polysomnography)

---

### Standardized Extract Dosing Summary

**For Anxiety (ongoing):**
- **Dose:** 45-60 drops liquid extract daily, or 260-600 mg standardized dry extract daily
- **Frequency:** Divided doses (2-3 times daily) or single daily dose
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks typical

**For Acute Anxiety (procedures):**
- **Dose:** 260-500 mg single dose
- **Timing:** 30-90 minutes before procedure
- **Note:** Higher end (500 mg) for ambulatory surgery; lower (260 mg) effective for dental procedures

**For Sleep:**
- **Dose:** 600 mg at bedtime
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks for best results
- **Alternative:** Lower doses (equivalent to 2-4 g crude herb) for mild sleep disturbances

## Tincture Preparations

### Traditional Tincture Ratios

**1:8 Ratio (45% alcohol) - British Herbal Pharmacopoeia 1976**
- **Preparation:** 1 part herb to 8 parts 45% ethanol
- **Dose:** 0.5-2 ml, three times daily
- **Daily total:** 1.5-6 ml
- **Equivalent:** Each ml ≈ 0.125 g crude herb; daily dose ≈ 0.19-0.75 g crude herb

**1:8 Ratio - WHO Monograph**
- **Dose:** 1.0-4.0 ml, three to four times daily
- **Daily total:** 3-16 ml
- **Equivalent:** Daily dose ≈ 0.375-2 g crude herb

**1:1 Ratio (25% alcohol) - British Herbal Pharmacopoeia**
- **Preparation:** 1 part herb to 1 part 25% ethanol (stronger extract)
- **Dose:** 0.5-1 ml, three times daily
- **Daily total:** 1.5-3 ml
- **Equivalent:** Each ml ≈ 1 g crude herb; daily dose ≈ 1.5-3 g crude herb

---

### Tincture Dosing by Indication

**Nervous Restlessness/Anxiety:**
- **1:8 tincture:** 1-2 ml, 3-4 times daily (British/WHO tradition)
- **1:1 tincture:** 0.5-1 ml, 3 times daily
- **Timing:** Spread throughout day

**Sleep Support:**
- **1:8 tincture:** 2-4 ml at bedtime
- **1:1 tincture:** 1-2 ml at bedtime
- **Optional:** Smaller dose (half) mid-afternoon if early waking is issue

**Acute Anxiety (adapted from drops studies):**
- **45 drops ≈ 2.25 ml** (assuming 20 drops/ml)
- If using 1:1 extract: 2-3 ml single dose, 30-60 minutes before stressful event
- If using 1:8 extract: May need higher dose (4-5 ml) for acute effects

---

### Tincture Preparation Instructions (Herbalist)

**Folk Method (1:5 ratio, 40% alcohol):**
1. Fill jar 1/5 full with dried Passiflora incarnata aerial parts
2. Cover completely with 40% (80 proof) alcohol (vodka or brandy)
3. Ensure all plant material submerged
4. Cap tightly, label with herb name and date
5. Shake daily for 2-4 weeks
6. Strain through cheesecloth/muslin
7. Store in amber glass bottles

**Dose:** 2-4 ml (40-80 drops), 3 times daily for anxiety; 3-5 ml at bedtime for sleep

**Weight-to-Volume Method (1:5, 40% alcohol, more precise):**
1. Weigh 200 g dried passionflower aerial parts
2. Add 1000 ml of 40% ethanol
3. Follow steps 3-7 above

---

### Converting Between Tincture Ratios

**Equivalence (crude herb basis):**
- 1 ml of 1:1 tincture ≈ 8 ml of 1:8 tincture ≈ 1 g crude herb
- 1 ml of 1:5 tincture ≈ 0.2 g crude herb

**Example:**
- Commission E dose: 4-8 g crude herb daily
- **Using 1:8 tincture:** 32-64 ml daily (impractically large)
- **Using 1:1 tincture:** 4-8 ml daily (practical)
- **Using 1:5 tincture:** 20-40 ml daily (borderline practical)

**Practical Implication:** Stronger tinctures (1:1 or 1:5) are more practical for achieving therapeutic doses equivalent to crude herb recommendations.

## Infusion (Tea) Preparations

### Traditional European Tea Methods

**Commission E / Hagers Handbuch:**
- **Dose:** 15 g crude drug in 150 ml water
- **Preparation:** Pour boiling water over herb, steep 10-15 minutes, strain
- **Frequency:** Not specified (likely 1-2 cups daily)
- **Note:** This is a strong tea (10% concentration)

**WHO Monograph:**
- **Dose:** 2.5 g aerial parts as infusion
- **Frequency:** Three to four times daily
- **Daily total:** 7.5-10 g
- **Preparation:** Standard infusion method

**British Herbal Pharmacopoeia 1976:**
- **Dose:** 0.25-1 g per infusion
- **Frequency:** Three times daily
- **Daily total:** 0.75-3 g
- **Note:** Lower dose range than WHO

---

### Tea Preparation Instructions

**Standard Infusion (WHO method):**
1. Measure 2.5 g (approximately 1-2 teaspoons) dried passionflower aerial parts
2. Place in cup or teapot
3. Pour 200-250 ml (8 oz) boiling water over herb
4. Cover and steep for 10-15 minutes
5. Strain
6. Drink 3-4 times daily for anxiety; 1-2 times (including bedtime) for sleep

**Strong Infusion (German tradition):**
1. Measure 15 g (approximately 1-2 tablespoons) dried aerial parts
2. Place in cup or small pot
3. Pour 150 ml boiling water over herb
4. Cover and steep 15 minutes
5. Strain
6. Drink 1-2 times daily

---

### Tea Dosing by Indication

**Mild Anxiety/Nervous Restlessness:**
- 2.5 g infusion, 3 times daily (WHO standard)
- Total daily: 7.5 g crude herb

**Sleep Support:**
- 2.5-5 g infusion, 1 hour before bed
- Optional second cup mid-evening if needed
- **Ngan & Conduit 2011:** Low-dose passionflower tea for 7 days improved subjective sleep quality (t(40) = 2.70, p < 0.01)

**Nervous Stomach:**
- 0.25-1 g infusion (British tradition), 3 times daily with meals
- Lighter dose appropriate for digestive applications

---

### Tea vs. Extract Potency

**Comparison:**
- Tea is less concentrated than tinctures or dry extracts
- Flavonoid extraction in water is less efficient than alcohol
- Tea suitable for mild symptoms; extracts for moderate-severe

**Evidence:**
- Ngan & Conduit 2011 used low-dose tea successfully for subjective sleep quality
- Most clinical trials showing anxiolytic equivalence to benzodiazepines used concentrated extracts, not tea

**Clinical Implication:**
- Tea appropriate for maintenance, mild symptoms, or traditional use
- Standardized extracts preferred for documented clinical efficacy in moderate-severe anxiety or insomnia

## Crude Herb Dosing (Regulatory Standards)

### Commission E Recommendation
- **Dose:** 4-8 g daily
- **Indication:** Nervous restlessness
- **Note:** Can be taken as tea, tincture, or extract equivalent

### WHO Recommendations
- **Sedative dose:** 0.5-2.0 g of aerial parts, three to four times daily
- **Daily total:** 1.5-8 g
- **Forms:** Infusion, tincture, or extract

### Hagers Handbuch
- **Daily dose:** 4-8 g crude drug or corresponding extract
- **Tea preparation:** 15 g per 150 ml water

## Equivalent Dosing Across Preparation Types

### To Achieve 4-8 g Crude Herb Daily Equivalent

**Crude Herb (Tea):**
- 4-8 g per day as infusion

**Tincture 1:8:**
- 32-64 ml per day (impractical volume)

**Tincture 1:1:**
- 4-8 ml per day (practical)

**Liquid Extract (Drops, 1:1 assumed):**
- 45-60 drops = ~2.25-3 ml = 2.25-3 g crude herb equivalent (if 1:1)
- Falls on lower end of Commission E range

**Dry Extract (Standardized to 3.5% flavonoids as vitexin):**
- **Assumption:** Extract concentration ~5:1 to 10:1 (5-10 g crude herb → 1 g extract)
- 400-800 mg dry extract daily
- **Clinical trial doses:** 260-600 mg (align with this estimate)

## Combination Formulas

### Traditional Sleep Combinations

**Passionflower + Valerian:**
- **Ratio:** Often 1:1 or 2:1 (passionflower:valerian)
- **Rationale:** Complementary mechanisms (passionflower GABAergic, valerian GABAergic + adenosine)
- **Dose:** Passionflower 200-400 mg + Valerian 300-600 mg at bedtime

**Passionflower + Lemon Balm + Hops:**
- **Traditional "calming tea" blend**
- **Ratios:** Variable, often equal parts
- **Preparation:** Tea infusion or tincture blend

**Passionflower + Chamomile:**
- **Use:** Nervous stomach with anxiety
- **Preparation:** Tea infusion
- **Dose:** 1-2 g each herb, 3 times daily

---

### Combination Cautions

**Do Not Combine With:**
- Prescription sedatives (benzodiazepines, Z-drugs) without medical supervision
- Other strongly GABAergic herbs in excessive amounts (avoid oversedation)

**Safe Combinations:**
- Passionflower + gentle nervines (lemon balm, chamomile)
- Passionflower + adaptogens for stress (ashwagandha, rhodiola) - monitor for sedation
- Passionflower + hawthorn for "nervous heart" (traditional combination)

## Pediatric Dosing

**Status:** Not established

**Recommendation:** Not recommended for children without medical supervision

**Traditional Use:** Primarily adult use; pediatric traditional use not well-documented

**Clark's Rule (if used under supervision):**
- Pediatric dose = (Child's weight in lbs / 150) × Adult dose
- Example: 50 lb child would receive 1/3 of adult dose
- **Use with extreme caution; not evidence-based for passionflower**

---

## Dosing by Age and Weight

### Adults (Average 70 kg / 154 lbs)
- **Standard dose:** As per regulatory recommendations (4-8 g crude herb equivalent)
- **Anxiety:** 45-60 drops liquid extract or 260-600 mg standardized extract
- **Sleep:** 600 mg at bedtime or 4-8 ml 1:1 tincture

### Elderly or Low Body Weight (<50 kg / 110 lbs)
- **Start low:** 50-75% of standard dose
- **Titrate up:** Based on response and tolerability
- **Monitor:** For excessive sedation, dizziness (fall risk)

### High Body Weight (>90 kg / 200 lbs)
- **Standard dose:** Typically sufficient (no weight-based dosing established)
- **May increase:** Up to upper end of ranges if no response at standard dose

---

## Timing of Doses

### For Anxiety (Ongoing)

**Divided Dosing (Traditional):**
- Morning, afternoon, evening (3× daily)
- Maintains steady levels throughout day

**Example:**
- 1:1 tincture: 1.5 ml at breakfast, 1.5 ml mid-afternoon, 2 ml at bedtime (5 ml total)
- Standardized extract: 200 mg morning, 200 mg afternoon, 200 mg evening (600 mg total)

---

### For Sleep

**Bedtime Dosing:**
- 1-2 hours before bed (allows time for absorption and effect)
- Single dose

**Example:**
- 1:1 tincture: 4-6 ml, 1 hour before bed
- Standardized extract: 600 mg, 1-2 hours before bed
- Tea: 5 g infusion, 1 hour before bed

**Optional:** Small dose (1/3 of bedtime dose) mid-afternoon if early morning waking is issue.

---

### For Acute Anxiety (Situational)

**Pre-Event Dosing:**
- 30-90 minutes before stressful event
- Single dose

**Evidence:**
- Movafegh 2008: 500 mg, 90 minutes before surgery
- Dantas 2017: 260 mg, 30 minutes before dental extraction

**Example:**
- Public speaking: 300-500 mg standardized extract, 60 minutes before
- Dental appointment: 260 mg, 30 minutes before
- Flight anxiety: 300-400 mg, 1 hour before boarding

---

## Duration of Use

### Short-Term (Acute)
- **Duration:** Single dose to 7 days
- **Use case:** Acute anxiety events, short-term stress, situational insomnia
- **Evidence:** Single-dose studies (Movafegh, Dantas), 7-day tea study (Ngan)

### Medium-Term
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks
- **Use case:** GAD, adjustment disorder, insomnia
- **Evidence:** Akhondzadeh 2001 (4 weeks), Lee 2020 (2 weeks), Harit 2024 (30 days)
- **Regulatory:** EMA/WHO suggest medical consultation if symptoms persist >2 weeks (to evaluate underlying condition, not due to safety concern)

### Long-Term
- **Duration:** >4 weeks
- **Evidence:** Limited clinical trial data
- **Traditional use:** Suggests long-term use acceptable
- **Safety:** No documented cumulative toxicity
- **Recommendation:** Periodic reassessment of need; consider tapering to see if still necessary

---

## Titration and Adjustment

### Starting Dose
- **Conservative:** Begin at low end of dose range (e.g., 260 mg standardized extract or 2 ml 1:1 tincture)
- **Assess:** Response after 3-5 days

### Increasing Dose
- **If insufficient response:** Increase by 25-50% every 3-5 days
- **Maximum:** Upper end of traditional ranges (e.g., 600 mg extract or 8 ml 1:1 tincture)

### Decreasing Dose
- **If excessive sedation:** Reduce by 25-50%
- **If effective at lower dose:** Maintain lowest effective dose

### Discontinuation
- **Tapering:** Not necessary (no documented withdrawal syndrome)
- **Abrupt discontinuation:** Appears safe based on clinical trial data
- **Recommendation:** Can stop abruptly if needed, or taper gradually if preferred

---

## Quality Considerations for Dosing

### Standardization Matters

**Problem:** Not all products are equivalent
- Non-standardized products have variable flavonoid content
- May require higher doses of non-standardized products to achieve effects

**Solution:**
- Use products standardized to 3.5% flavonoids as vitexin (or 2.5-4% as isovitexin)
- Minimum European Pharmacopoeia standard: 1.5% for crude herb, 2.0% for extracts

---

### Certificate of Analysis

**Should Include:**
- Flavonoid content (% vitexin or isovitexin)
- HPLC fingerprint
- Contaminant testing (heavy metals, pesticides, microbial)
- Identity confirmation (correct species)

**Dosing Implication:** Only products with CoA allow confident dosing based on clinical trial evidence.

---

### Form Selection by Use Case

**For Convenience and Standardization:**
- Capsules/tablets (dry extract)
- Pre-measured, easy to dose
- Preferred for evidence-based dosing

**For Flexibility and Tradition:**
- Tinctures (liquid extracts)
- Adjustable dosing
- Faster onset (sublingual absorption possible)

**For Mild Symptoms and Enjoyment:**
- Tea (infusion)
- Ritual and sensory experience
- Lower potency, suitable for mild symptoms

---

## Special Preparation Notes

### Sublingual Administration
- **Tinctures** can be held under tongue for 30-60 seconds before swallowing
- **May enhance:** Speed of onset (bypasses first-pass metabolism)
- **Note:** No clinical trials used sublingual route; effect is theoretical

### With or Without Food
- **Clinical trials:** Did not specify food timing
- **Recommendation:** Can be taken with or without food
- **Consideration:** Taking with food may slow onset but could reduce any mild GI upset (rare)

---

## Storage and Stability

### Dried Herb
- **Storage:** Cool, dry, dark place in airtight container
- **Shelf life:** 1-2 years
- **Degradation signs:** Loss of color (fading), loss of aroma

### Tinctures
- **Storage:** Amber glass bottles, cool location
- **Shelf life:** 3-5 years (alcohol preserves)
- **Degradation:** Minimal if stored properly

### Dry Extracts (Capsules/Tablets)
- **Storage:** Cool, dry place, away from moisture
- **Shelf life:** 2-3 years (check expiration date)
- **Degradation:** Moisture exposure can reduce potency

---

## Preparations Bottom Line

**Most Evidence-Based:** Standardized dry extract, 260-600 mg daily (divided or single dose depending on indication), standardized to 3.5% flavonoids as vitexin.

**Most Traditional:** Tincture 1:8 (45% alcohol), 1-4 ml 3-4 times daily, or crude herb tea 4-8 g daily.

**For Acute Anxiety:** 260-500 mg standardized extract, 30-90 minutes before stressful event.

**For Sleep:** 600 mg standardized extract at bedtime, or 4-6 ml 1:1 tincture 1 hour before bed.

**For Ongoing Anxiety:** 45-60 drops liquid extract daily or 400-600 mg standardized extract divided 2-3× daily for 2-4 weeks.

**Quality Requirement:** Use products standardized to flavonoid content (minimum 1.5-2.0% vitexin) with Certificate of Analysis.

**Conversion:** 1 ml 1:1 tincture ≈ 1 g crude herb; 1 ml 1:8 tincture ≈ 0.125 g crude herb; 300-600 mg standardized dry extract ≈ 3-6 g crude herb (approximately).
