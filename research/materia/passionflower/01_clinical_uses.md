# Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) - Clinical Uses

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder

### Akhondzadeh et al. 2001 - GAD Study
- **Sample:** 36 outpatients with generalized anxiety disorder
- **Dose:** 45 drops daily of Passiflora incarnata extract
- **Comparator:** Oxazepam 30 mg daily
- **Duration:** 4 weeks
- **Design:** Double-blind RCT

**Results:**
- No significant difference in overall efficacy between passionflower and oxazepam
- Oxazepam showed faster therapeutic onset
- Passionflower group had significantly fewer job performance impairment problems compared to oxazepam group
- Comparable anxiolytic efficacy without cognitive side effects

**Clinical Significance:** Passionflower demonstrated benzodiazepine-level efficacy without impairment of work performance.

---

## Preoperative/Surgical Anxiety

### Movafegh et al. 2008 - Ambulatory Surgery
- **Sample:** 60 ambulatory surgery patients (30 per group)
- **Dose:** 500 mg Passiflora incarnata (Passipy IranDarouk) orally
- **Timing:** 90 minutes before surgery
- **Comparator:** Placebo
- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT

**Results:**
- Anxiety scores significantly lower in treatment group vs control (P < 0.001)
- No sedation induced
- Psychomotor recovery comparable between groups
- Discharge times similar
- Anxiolytic effect without impairment

**Clinical Significance:** Effective anxiolytic for outpatient procedures without sedative effects that would delay discharge.

---

## Dental Anxiety

### Dantas et al. 2017 - Dental Extraction
- **Sample:** 40 volunteers undergoing mandibular third molar extraction
- **Dose:** 260 mg Passiflora orally, 30 minutes pre-surgery
- **Comparator:** Midazolam 15 mg orally, 30 minutes pre-surgery
- **Design:** Double-blind, crossover RCT (same participants received both)

**Results:**
- Over 70% of volunteers felt quiet or slightly anxious under both protocols
- No significant differences in blood pressure, heart rate, or oxygen saturation
- Midazolam caused amnesia in 20% of participants
- Passiflora showed minimal memory effects
- Anxiolytic effect similar to midazolam

**Clinical Significance:** As effective as midazolam for conscious sedation without amnesia side effects.

### Kaviani et al. 2013 - Periodontal Treatment
- **Sample:** 63 patients undergoing periodontal treatment (3 groups of 21)
- **Intervention:** Passion flower extract drops as premedication
- **Design:** Randomized, single-blind clinical trial

**Results:**
- Pre-treatment anxiety: 12.09 ± 2.42
- Post-treatment (passionflower): 8.47 ± 2.58
- Post-treatment (placebo): 10.52 ± 2.11
- Post-treatment (control): 11.23 ± 2.34
- **Reduction:** 30.0% decrease from baseline (3.62 point reduction)
- Significant difference (p < 0.0001) before and after passionflower

**Clinical Significance:** Significant anxiolytic effect for dental procedures with 30% reduction in anxiety scores.

## Opioid Withdrawal

### Akhondzadeh et al. 2001 - Opiate Withdrawal
- **Sample:** 65 opioid-dependent adults (predominantly male, DSM-IV criteria)
- **Treatment group:** 60 drops daily Passiflora extract + clonidine (max 0.8 mg daily in 3 divided doses)
- **Control group:** Clonidine + placebo drops (same dosing)
- **Duration:** 14 days
- **Design:** Double-blind RCT

**Results:**
- Both groups equally effective for physical withdrawal symptoms
- Passiflora + clonidine significantly superior for mental symptoms compared to clonidine alone
- Adjuvant benefit specifically for psychological aspects of withdrawal

**Clinical Significance:** May be useful adjuvant for managing psychological symptoms during opioid withdrawal.

---

## Sleep Disorders

### Lee et al. 2020 - Polysomnographic Study
- **Sample:** 110 adults with insomnia disorder (mean age 40.47 ± 11.68 years; 53.6% female)
- **Intervention:** Passionflower extract
- **Comparator:** Placebo
- **Duration:** 2 weeks
- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT
- **Measurement:** Polysomnography (objective sleep parameters)

**Results:**
- **Total sleep time (TST):** Increased 23.05 ± 54.26 minutes in passionflower group vs -0.16 ± 53.12 in placebo (P = 0.049)
- Sleep efficiency showed improvement (not statistically significant vs placebo)
- Wake after sleep onset (WASO) improved (not statistically significant vs placebo)

**Clinical Significance:** Objective improvement in total sleep time measured by polysomnography. Effect size approximately 23 minutes of additional sleep.

### Ngan & Conduit 2011 - Subjective Sleep Quality
- **Sample:** 41 healthy adults ages 18-35 (10 underwent polysomnography)
- **Intervention:** Passionflower herbal tea (low dose)
- **Comparator:** Placebo tea
- **Duration:** 7 days
- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled, counterbalanced crossover with 1-week washout

**Results:**
- Sleep quality significantly better for passionflower: t(40) = 2.70, p < 0.01
- Subjective improvements in sleep rating

**Clinical Significance:** Low-dose tea effective for mild sleep quality fluctuations in healthy adults.

### Harit et al. 2024 - Stress and Sleep
- **Sample:** 65 participants with stress and sleep problems (32 treatment, 33 placebo)
- **Dose:** 600 mg daily SIVI (Passiflora incarnata aerial parts extract) at bedtime
- **Duration:** 30 days
- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT

**Results:**
- Statistically significant reduction in stress scores vs placebo by day 30
- Significant increase in mean total sleep time at day 15 and day 30 vs placebo
- General health improved significantly (GHQ-12) on days 15 and 30
- No adverse effects during study period

**Clinical Significance:** 600 mg daily dose effective for both stress reduction and sleep improvement with good safety profile.

## Systematic Review Evidence

### Janda et al. 2020 - Neuropsychiatric Disorders
- **Review scope:** 9 clinical trials on neuropsychiatric disorders
- **Findings:**
  - Majority reported reduced anxiety following Passiflora administration
  - Effect less evident in people with mild anxiety symptoms
  - Study durations: 1-30 days in participants 18+
  - No adverse effects including memory loss or psychometric function collapse
  - High heterogeneity prevented meta-analysis

**Conclusion:** Passionflower preparations helpful for stress-related conditions like anxiety, insomnia, and depression.

### Miroddi et al. 2013 - Clinical Applications
- **Findings:**
  - Good safety profile
  - Preclinical evidence: anxiolytic, sedative, antitussive, antiasthmatic, antidiabetic activities
  - Clinical trial weaknesses: poor extract ratio details, limited samples, inadequate blinding/randomization descriptions

**Recommendation:** More rigorous clinical trials needed for anxiety and sleep disorders.

## Typical Doses Used in Clinical Trials

### Liquid Extracts
- **45-60 drops daily** (Akhondzadeh studies)
- Usually divided into multiple doses throughout day

### Standardized Extracts
- **260 mg orally** (Dantas 2017 - dental anxiety)
- **500 mg orally** (Movafegh 2008 - preoperative anxiety)
- **600 mg daily at bedtime** (Harit 2024 - stress/sleep)

### Crude Herb
- **4-8 g daily** (regulatory recommendations)

### Duration
- **Acute use:** Single dose 30-90 minutes before procedure (dental, surgery)
- **Short-term:** 7-14 days (sleep quality, withdrawal)
- **Medium-term:** 2-4 weeks (GAD, insomnia, stress)
- **Extended:** Up to 30 days studied (stress/sleep)

---

## Clinical Strengths

1. **Multiple RCTs** demonstrate anxiolytic effects comparable to benzodiazepines (oxazepam, midazolam)
2. **No memory impairment** unlike benzodiazepines
3. **No job performance impairment** compared to pharmaceutical anxiolytics
4. **Effective for perioperative anxiety** without excessive sedation or discharge delays
5. **Good safety profile** with minimal adverse effects in most studies
6. **Objective sleep improvements** documented via polysomnography
7. **Versatile applications:** GAD, dental anxiety, surgical anxiety, opioid withdrawal, insomnia, stress

---

## Clinical Limitations

1. **High heterogeneity** prevents meta-analysis across studies
2. **Small sample sizes** in many trials (36-65 participants typical)
3. **Poor methodological details** in some trials (extract ratio, blinding, randomization)
4. **Lack of standardization** across products used in different studies
5. **Optimal dose and frequency** not firmly established
6. **Limited long-term safety data** (most studies ≤30 days)
7. **No pharmacokinetic data** available
8. **Effect less evident** in mild anxiety symptoms (needs moderate-severe symptoms)

---

## Clinical Bottom Line

**Generalized Anxiety:** 45 drops daily liquid extract comparable to oxazepam 30 mg without cognitive impairment.

**Acute Anxiety (procedures):** 260-500 mg single dose 30-90 minutes before effective without sedation.

**Sleep Disorders:** 600 mg at bedtime for 2-4 weeks increases total sleep time by ~23 minutes (objective measure).

**Opioid Withdrawal:** 60 drops daily as adjuvant to clonidine improves psychological symptoms.

**Safety Profile:** Generally well-tolerated with minimal adverse effects; rare cardiac effects reported in single case.
