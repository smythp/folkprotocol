# Dosing Protocols and Expected Timelines

## Dosing by Herb

### Bacopa monnieri (Brahmi)

**Standardized Extract (50% bacosides):**
- Standard: 300 mg daily
- Optimal: 450 mg daily
- Maximum studied: 600 mg daily
- Timing: Once or twice daily (split if >300mg)

**Powder (whole herb):**
- 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon with warm water
- Once or twice daily
- Traditional preparation

**Fresh Juice:**
- 1-4 teaspoons every morning
- Traditional Ayurvedic use

**Clinical Onset:**
- Effects typically emerge at 8 weeks
- Full benefits by 12 weeks
- Requires chronic dosing for cognitive benefits

**Safety:**
- Only 2.3% reported mild side effects
- Effect size: d=0.42 (small-medium)
- Well-tolerated long-term

**Key Insight:**
Bacopa is NOT an acute nootropic. Patience required for cholinergic system changes and BDNF enhancement to manifest.

---

### Ginkgo biloba (EGb 761)

**Standardized Extract:**
- Standard dose: 240 mg daily
- Take as single dose
- Standardization: 24% flavonoid glycosides, 6% terpene lactones

**Safety Maximum:**
- 240 mg daily maximum for acceptable drug interaction profile
- Higher doses may interact with anticoagulants

**Clinical Onset:**
- Cerebral blood flow: Dose-related response within days
- Effects lasting at least 6 days after dosing
- Cognitive benefits in MCI/dementia: Weeks to months

**Timing:**
- Single daily dose (no need to split)
- Consistency important for blood flow maintenance

**Drug Interactions:**
- May interact with anticoagulants
- Consult healthcare provider if on blood thinners
- Stay at or below 240 mg daily

---

### Rhodiola rosea

**Dosage Range:** 170-576 mg daily

**By Indication:**

**Low Dose (170 mg daily):**
- Population: Night shift workers
- Effects: Reduced fatigue, improved mental performance
- Use: Mild mental fatigue, occasional stress

**Moderate Dose (350-400 mg daily):**
- General stress-related fatigue
- Concentration improvement

**High Dose (576 mg daily):**
- Population: Patients with stress-related fatigue
- Effects: 30% reduction in fatigue symptoms
- Duration: 28 days
- Use: Severe mental fatigue, chronic stress

**Clinical Onset:**
- 4 weeks for 30% fatigue reduction (576 mg dose)
- Effects on mental performance: Days to weeks
- Adaptogenic effects build over time

**Dosing Strategy:**
- Start low (170 mg)
- Increase to 350-400 mg if inadequate response after 2 weeks
- Maximum 576 mg for severe cases
- Take in morning or early afternoon (may interfere with sleep)

**Limitations:**
- Evidence contradictory in some studies
- More rigorous research needed
- Individual response variable

---

### Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)

**Cognitive Enhancement:**
- Standard: 225 mg daily
- Optimal: 300-350 mg daily
- Maximum studied: 400 mg daily
- Duration: 30 days minimum

**ADHD in Children (ages 5-12):**
- Duration: 56 days in trial
- Dosing: Not specified; professional guidance required
- Result: p<0.001 improvement vs placebo

**Clinical Onset:**
- Cognitive effects: 30 days
- Stress/cortisol reduction: 2-4 weeks
- ADHD benefits: 56 days
- Adaptogenic effects: Progressive over weeks

**Timing:**
- Can take morning or evening
- Evening: May support sleep
- Morning: May support daytime stress resilience

**Synergy:**
- Works synergistically with Bacopa
- Combined timeline: 8-12 weeks for full assessment

---

### Polygala tenuifolia (Yuan Zhi)

**Clinical Dosage:**
- 100 mg per dose, three times daily
- Total: 300 mg/day

**Dosage Range:**
- Minimum: 50 mg daily
- Standard: 300 mg daily (100mg x 3)
- Maximum: 500 mg daily

**Dosing Strategy:**
- Start low (50-100 mg once daily)
- Gradually increase to 100mg three times daily
- Allows individual tolerance assessment

**Clinical Onset:**
- Timeline not specified in research
- Traditional use suggests chronic dosing
- Monoamine modulation likely progressive

**Best For:**
- Overall cognition (network meta-analysis winner)
- Focus, attention, working memory
- Motivation support

---

### L-Theanine + Caffeine

**Standard Ratio:** 2:1 (L-theanine : caffeine)

**Dosing Options:**

**Standard:**
- 100 mg L-theanine
- 50 mg caffeine
- Most commonly researched

**Moderate (Demanding Tasks):**
- 97 mg L-theanine
- 40 mg caffeine
- Evidence: Helps focus during demanding cognitive tasks

**Higher Dose:**
- 200 mg L-theanine
- 160 mg caffeine
- For those with high caffeine tolerance

**Clinical Onset:**
- **Acute effects: 30-60 minutes**
- Peak effects: 1-2 hours
- Duration: 4-6 hours (depending on caffeine metabolism)

**Frequency:**
- As needed for acute attention tasks
- Daily use: 1-3 times per day
- Avoid late afternoon/evening (caffeine disrupts sleep)

**Mechanism Timeline:**
- Alpha wave modulation: Within 30 minutes
- Behavioral effects: 30-60 minutes
- Synergistic attention enhancement peaks around 1-2 hours

**Key Insight:**
This is the ONLY truly acute nootropic in the focus protocol. All others require days to weeks.

---

### American Ginseng (Panax quinquefolius)

**Dosage Range:** 100-400 mg

**By Effect:**

**Low Dose (100 mg):**
- Effects: Improved choice reaction time accuracy, increased calmness
- Best for: Working memory, subtle enhancement
- Can be taken daily

**Moderate Dose (200 mg):**
- General cognitive enhancement
- Balanced effects

**Upper Dose (400 mg):**
- Mental fatigue reduction
- Working memory enhancement
- Attentional performance

**Clinical Onset:**
- Acute effects possible (reaction time, calmness)
- Chronic benefits: Not well-specified
- Traditional use: Both acute and chronic

**Population:**
- Healthy adults
- Schizophrenia patients (working memory and attention benefits)

**Timing:**
- Can take as needed or daily
- Morning preferred (ginseng can be stimulating for some)

---

### Rosemary (Salvia rosmarinus)

**Students/Adults:**
- 500 mg twice daily (1,000 mg total)
- Duration: 1 month in RCT
- Effects: Enhanced memory, reduced anxiety/depression, improved sleep

**Elderly (Mean Age 75):**
- **Optimal: 750 mg dried leaf powder once daily**
- Evidence: Statistically significant beneficial effects (p=0.01)

**WARNING - Dose-Response Curve:**
- 750 mg: Beneficial (p=0.01)
- 6,000 mg: Significant cognitive IMPAIRMENT (p<0.01)
- **Lower doses are optimal**

**Clinical Onset:**
- 1 month in student trial
- Effects likely progressive over weeks

**Key Compounds:**
- Carnosic acid (most potent AChE inhibitor of 35 tested phenolics)
- Carnosol
- Rosmarinic acid

**Breakthrough Development:**
- diAcCA (stable synthetic carnosic acid) by Scripps Research
- Alzheimer's mice: Memory improvement, increased synaptic density
- FDA GRAS status: Easier path to trials

**Dosing Principle:**
Less is more with rosemary. Very high doses impair cognition.

### Melissa officinalis (Lemon Balm)

**Recommendation:**

**Effects:**

**Mechanisms:**

## Acute vs Chronic Dosing Strategies

### Acute Focus Needs (Within Hours)

**Only One Option:**
```
L-Theanine 100 mg + Caffeine 50 mg
Timeline: 30-60 minutes to effect
Duration: 4-6 hours
Frequency: As needed, 1-3x daily
```

**Other Possibilities (Less Evidence):**
```
American Ginseng 100 mg
Timeline: Acute effects possible (reaction time, calmness)
Less robust acute evidence than L-theanine+Caffeine
```

**Key Insight:**
If you need focus in the next few hours, your options are very limited. Most herbal nootropics require days to weeks.

## Dosing Optimization Strategies

### Start Low, Go Slow

**Principle:**
Begin at lower end of dosing range, increase gradually if inadequate response.

**Examples:**
```
Rhodiola:
- Week 1-2: 170 mg daily
- Week 3-4: 350 mg daily (if needed)
- Week 5+: 576 mg daily (if needed)

Bacopa:
- Week 1-4: 300 mg daily
- Week 5-8: 450 mg daily (if tolerated, inadequate response)
- Week 9-12: 600 mg daily (maximum)

Polygala:
- Week 1-2: 50-100 mg once daily
- Week 3-4: 100 mg twice daily
- Week 5+: 100 mg three times daily
```

**Rationale:**
- Assess individual tolerance
- Minimize side effects
- Find minimum effective dose

---

### Timing Within Day

**Morning Dosing:**
- Rhodiola (may interfere with sleep)
- American Ginseng (can be stimulating)
- L-Theanine + Caffeine (avoid after 2pm)
- Rosemary
- Polygala

**Evening/Bedtime Dosing:**
- Shankhpushpi (4-6g for sleep support)
- Ashwagandha (can support sleep, though also taken AM)

**Twice Daily (Split Dosing):**
- Bacopa >300 mg (e.g., 300mg AM, 300mg PM for 600mg total)
- Rosemary (500mg AM, 500mg PM)

**Three Times Daily:**
- Polygala (100mg with each meal for 300mg total)

**Rationale:**
- Some herbs stimulating → AM dosing
- Some support sleep → PM dosing
- Higher doses → split to improve absorption and reduce GI upset

---

### Pulsing vs Continuous Dosing

**Continuous Dosing (Most Herbs):**
- Bacopa: Requires chronic dosing for cholinergic system changes
- Ashwagandha: Adaptogenic effects build over time
- Rhodiola: Anti-fatigue effects with chronic use
- Ginkgo: Blood flow maintenance requires consistency

**As-Needed Dosing:**
- L-Theanine + Caffeine: Acute attention enhancement
- American Ginseng: Can use acutely or chronically

**Cycling (Less Evidence):**
- Some practitioners suggest 5 days on, 2 days off
- Or 3 months on, 1 month off
- Limited research support for necessity

## Factors Affecting Timeline

### Individual Variation
- Genetics (neurotransmitter systems, metabolism)
- Baseline cognitive function (ceiling effects)
- Age (elderly may respond differently)
- Stress levels (adaptogens work better under stress)

### Severity of Impairment
- Mild symptoms: May see benefits sooner
- Severe ADHD: Full 12 weeks likely needed
- Alzheimer's/MCI: Very long timelines (12+ months)

### Dosing Adequacy
- Suboptimal dose: No effect or delayed onset
- Optimal dose: Expected timeline
- Excessive dose: May impair (rosemary example)

### Combination Effects
- Synergistic: May see benefits sooner (L-theanine+Caffeine)
- Additive: Timeline matches longest component
- Insufficient interaction: No added benefit

### Consistency
- Daily dosing: Expected timeline
- Inconsistent: Delayed or no effect
- Missed doses: Setbacks, especially with chronic herbs

## Clinical Recommendations

### For Practitioners:

1. **Set realistic expectations:**
   - Most herbal nootropics take weeks, not days
   - Bacopa: "Wait 12 weeks before judging efficacy"
   - Acute needs: Very limited options (L-theanine+Caffeine)

2. **Start with appropriate timeline herb:**
   - Acute (<24hr): L-theanine+Caffeine only
   - Short-term (1-4 weeks): Rhodiola, Ashwagandha, Rosemary
   - Medium-term (8-12 weeks): Bacopa, combinations
   - Long-term (12+ months): Polyherbal, Rasayanas

3. **Monitor and adjust:**
   - 4-week check-in: Assess tolerance, minor adjustments
   - 8-week check-in: Early effects should emerge (Bacopa)
   - 12-week check-in: Full assessment, decide continue/adjust/stop

4. **Don't give up too early:**
   - Bacopa at 4 weeks: Too soon
   - Combination at 6 weeks: May need full 12
   - Elderly with polyherbal: May need 12+ months

5. **Don't continue ineffective treatment indefinitely:**
   - If no response by 12 weeks (Bacopa, combinations): Reassess
   - Consider different herb, higher dose, or combination
   - Rule out other causes (sleep, nutrition, medical)
