# Traditional Combination Formulas for Anxiety

## Chinese Medicine Formulas

### Jiu Wei Zhen Xin (Jiuwei Zhenxin Granules)

**Name:** 九味镇心 (Nine Ingredients to Settle the Heart)

**Origin:**
- Tai Ping He pharmacy prescriptions, Northern Song Dynasty
- Modern revision: 1990s by Professor TuYa (Beijing University) and Professor Xiaolan Cui (Institute of Pharmacology)

**Regulatory Status:** Recognized by China Food and Drug Administration

**Indication:** Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**Functions:**
- Nourish and regulate heart and spleen
- Replenish Qi (vital energy)
- Tranquilize the mind
- Replenish blood

**Applications:**
- Depression
- Anxiety disorders
- Bipolar disorders
- Prevention and treatment of psychiatric conditions in China

**Clinical Evidence:**

*Meta-Analysis (Wang 2018, PMID: PMC5863357):*
- Title: "Efficacy and Safety of JWZX for GAD: A Meta-Analysis"
- DOI: 10.1155/2018/9090181
- Conclusion: Effective for GAD treatment

*Mechanism Study (2022, PMID: PMC9168090):*
- Network pharmacology analysis of TCM
- Title: "Analysis of Underlying Mechanism via Network Pharmacology"

**Dosing:** Per traditional TCM dosing or modern granule preparations

**Timeline:** Months for constitutional balancing (typical TCM approach)

**Why It Works:**
- Multi-herb formula addresses multiple aspects of anxiety
- Balances constitution rather than suppressing symptoms
- Synergistic effects from herb combinations

### Jieyu Anshen Decoction/Granules

**Name:** 解郁安神 (Resolve Depression, Calm the Spirit)

**Status:** Classical TCM with antidepressant and anxiolytic activity

**Indication:** Anxiety and depression, post-stroke depression

**Regulatory:** Widely implemented in China for anxiety and depression treatment

**Usage:** Alone or in combination with conventional antidepressants

**Clinical Evidence:**

*Systematic Review 2024 (PMID 40311928):*
- Title: "Jieyu Anshen Decoction monotherapy or combination with Western medicine for mental disorders"
- **95 RCTs, 8,188 participants**

**Key Findings:**

1. **Combined with Paroxetine for Anxiety:**
   - RR = 1.30 (95% CI: 1.17–1.44)
   - **30% BETTER EFFECTIVENESS than paroxetine alone**

2. **HAMA Improvements:**
   - Significant improvements vs Western medicine alone

3. **Combination Therapy:**
   - Enhanced effectiveness when combined with paroxetine
   - Better safety profiles vs Western medicine monotherapy

*Animal Model (2020, DOI: 10.1155/2020/7469068):*
- Title: "Effects on Poststroke Depression in Rats"
- Demonstrated antidepressant and anxiolytic effects

**Why It Works:**
- Enhances pharmaceutical effectiveness when combined
- Addresses root causes alongside symptom management
- Fewer side effects than medication alone

### Xiao Yao San (Free and Easy Wanderer)

**Names:**
- Chinese: 逍遥散
- Pinyin: Xiaoyao San
- Japanese Kampo: Kamishoyosan / Kami-Shoyo-San (TJ-24)
- Korean: Gami-Shoyo-San

**Origin:** Tai Ping Hui Min He Ji Ju Fang (Song Dynasty, 1078-1085 AD)

**Status:** Most frequently used TCM formula in anxiety/depression studies

**Composition (8 Herbs):**
1. Bupleuri radix (Chai Hu)
2. Angelicae radix (Dang Gui / Dong Quai)
3. Paeoniae radix alba (Bai Shao)
4. Atractylodis rhizome macrocephalae (Bai Zhu)
5. Poria cocos (Fu Ling)
6. Zingiberis siccatum rhizome (Sheng Jiang)
7. Menthae haplocalycis (Bo He)
8. Glycyrrhizae radix (Gan Cao / Licorice)

**Modified Version:** Jia Wei XYS (Augmented Rambling Powder / Dan Zhi Xiao Yao San)
- Original 8 herbs + 2 additional herbs
- Indications: Anxiety, mood disturbances, fatigue associated with climacteric syndrome

**Clinical Evidence:**

*Multicenter RCT (192 participants, 8 weeks):*
- **Jia Wei XYS vs Sertraline** (SSRI)

**Outcomes:**
- **HAMD (Depression):** Similar improvements
- **HAMA (Anxiety):** Similar improvements
- **CGI (Clinical Global Impression):** Similar improvements
- **Side Effects:** **LOWER rates in Jia Wei XYS arm at 12-week follow-up**

**Conclusion:** Equal efficacy to SSRI with better tolerability

**Dosing:** Traditional decoction or modern granule preparations

**Why It Works:**
- Addresses liver Qi stagnation (TCM pathology for anxiety/depression)
- Balances multiple organ systems simultaneously
- Gentler side effect profile than pharmaceuticals
- Constitutional approach rather than symptom suppression

**Sources:**
- [ASCO Post](https://ascopost.com/issues/april-10-2022/use-of-traditional-chinese-medicine-herbal-formula-xiao-yao-san-to-relieve-depression-and-anxiety/)
- [Frontiers in Pharmacology](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pharmacology/articles/10.3389/fphar.2023.1169292/full)
- PMC10613521, PMC10230634, PMC8556059

## Ayurvedic Formulas

**Traditional Use:** Fighting depression, anxiety, and chronic stress

**Individual Herbs:**

**Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri):**
- Traditional uses: Memory improvement, insomnia, epilepsy, anxiolytic
- Clinical studies: Verbal learning, delayed word recall, memory acquisition improvements
- Anxiety evidence: 12 weeks, 300 mg daily → **significantly lowered anxiety levels**
- Adaptogenic: Increases resistance to stress and strain
- Cortisol: Modern research suggests improves mood by lowering cortisol levels

**Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera):**
- Adaptogenic: Helps body cope with stress
- Cognitive: Supports cognitive function, reduces anxiety/depression symptoms

**Synergistic Effects:**
"When Ashwagandha and Brahmi combined, synergistic effects provide even greater benefits for mental health"

**Roles:**
- Ashwagandha: Adaptogenic properties help body cope with stress
- Brahmi: Supports cognitive function and reduces anxiety/depression symptoms
- Overall: Create potent formula to combat depression, anxiety, and chronic stress

**Why It Works:**
- Complementary mechanisms: HPA axis (Ashwagandha) + cognitive support (Brahmi)
- Both are adaptogens but work through different pathways
- Traditional pairing with centuries of Ayurvedic use

**Sources:**
- NCBI NBK589635

## Western Herbal Combinations

### "Three Sisters of Sleep" - Valerian + Hops + Passionflower

**Traditional Name:** Three Sisters of Sleep / Hops & Valerian with Passion Flower

**Synergy:**
- All three affect gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) levels
- Valerian's effects best potentiated by hops and passionflower
- Safe and synergistic approach

**Effects:**
- Aid sleep onset
- Reduce overall anxiety
- Shorten time to fall asleep
- Lengthen duration of sleep

**Clinical Evidence:**

*Comparison Study:*
"Combination of valerian, hops and passion flower is safe and effective alternative to zolpidem"
- At least for short-term treatment of insomnia

*Studied Doses:*
- **Valerian:** 300 mg extract (standardized to 0.8% total valerenic acid)
- **Passionflower:** 80 mg extract (standardized to 4% isovitexin)
- **Hops:** 30 mg extract (standardized to 0.35% rutin)

**Regulatory:**
European Union: Valeriana officinalis, Melissa officinalis, and Passiflora incarnata traditionally used for:
- Treatment of nervous tension
- Relief of mild symptoms of mental stress
- Aid sleep

**Mechanisms:**
- Hops + Valerian: Commonly combined for beneficial, synergistic effect in sleep disorders
- Action: Comparable to efficacy of benzodiazepines
- Passionflower chrysin: Natural flavone with anxiolytic effects equal to benzodiazepines

**Why It Works:**
- Triple GABA modulation through different mechanisms
- Valerian: Direct GABA system modulation
- Hops: GABA-ergic + phytoestrogenic sedation
- Passionflower: GABA uptake inhibition + receptor affinity

**Best For:**
- Anxiety WITH sleep disturbance
- Alternative to prescription sleep aids
- Benzodiazepine withdrawal support

**Sources:**
- PMC7317844
- PMC3608291
- [Psychology Today](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/rhythms-of-recovery/201707/how-the-3-sisters-of-sleep-can-help)

### L-Lysine + L-Arginine - STRONGEST SYNERGY EVIDENCE

**Evidence Level:** STRONG - systematic review conclusion

**Systematic Review (Lakhan 2010, PMID 20929532):**
"Strong evidence for L-lysine and L-arginine combinations treating anxiety disorders"

**Clinical Trial (Smriga 2007, PMID 17510493):**
- Title: "Oral L-lysine and L-arginine reduce anxiety and basal cortisol in healthy humans"
- Participants: Humans with high trait anxiety
- Dose: 2.64 g per day of each amino acid
- Duration: One week

**Key Findings:**

1. **Trait Anxiety:** Normalized hormonal stress responses
2. **State Anxiety:** Significantly reduced stress-induced state anxiety
3. **Cortisol:** Decreased basal levels of salivary cortisol (males)
4. **Chromogranin A:** Decreased basal levels (males)

**Mechanism:**
- **L-Lysine:** 5-HT4 receptor antagonist → lessens stress reaction
- **Cortisol lowering:** Observed cortisol level reduction
- **Synergistic:** Effects greater than individual amino acids

**Dosing:**
- Clinical trial: 2.64 g daily of each
- Recommended: 2-4 grams daily of each

**Side Effects:** Virtually no reported side effects

**Herbal Combinations:**
Can be combined with:
- Passionflower + L-lysine + L-arginine
- Kava + L-lysine + L-arginine

**Note:** Amino acids enhance herbal anxiolytic effects

**Why It Works:**
- Addresses neurotransmitter (5-HT4) and hormonal (cortisol) pathways simultaneously
- Synergistic effect - combination superior to monotherapy
- Rapid effect (one week)
- Excellent safety profile

**Best For:**
- High trait anxiety
- Stress-induced state anxiety
- Combining with herbal anxiolytics for enhanced effect
- Those seeking pharmaceutical-grade evidence for natural treatment

### Magnesium + Vitamin B6 - SYNERGISTIC FOR PMS ANXIETY

**Evidence:** Synergistic effect demonstrated

**Population:** Women with premenstrual anxiety

**Result:**
- Greatest relief from combination vs magnesium alone
- **Magnesium monotherapy similar to placebo**
- **Synergy crucial** - combination superior to individual nutrients

**Why It Works:**
- B6: Cofactor for neurotransmitter synthesis (serotonin, GABA, dopamine)
- Magnesium: NMDA receptor antagonist, GABA-A receptor modulation
- Together: Enable proper neurotransmitter synthesis AND receptor function

**Best For:**
- Premenstrual anxiety and mood disturbances
- Anxiety with possible magnesium deficiency (stress depletes magnesium)

**Dosing:** Follow standard supplementation guidelines for each

## Research Gaps in Combinations

**Current Status:**
"Kitchen sink approach common in practice, but **no clear evidence combinations superior to adequate doses of effective single herbs**"

**Exceptions (Proven Synergy):**
1. L-lysine + L-arginine - STRONG evidence for synergy
2. Magnesium + B6 - STRONG evidence for premenstrual anxiety
3. Jieyu Anshen + Paroxetine - 30% better than medication alone

**Traditional Combinations (Clinical Use but Limited Research):**
- Valerian + Hops + Passionflower
- Valerian + Lemon balm
- Brahmi + Ashwagandha
- TCM multi-herb formulas
- Ayurvedic multi-herb formulas

**Need:**
- Rigorous trials comparing combination formulas vs monotherapy at adequate doses
- Synergy question: Do combinations provide additive/synergistic benefit or just increased cost/complexity?
- Head-to-head: Multi-herb formula vs best single herb at optimal dose

## When to Use Combinations vs Single Herbs

### Choose Combination When:

1. **Proven Synergy Exists:**
   - L-lysine + L-arginine (high trait anxiety)
   - Magnesium + B6 (premenstrual anxiety)
   - Jieyu Anshen + medication (treatment-resistant)

2. **Multiple Symptoms:**
   - Anxiety + sleep disturbance → Three Sisters (Valerian + Hops + Passionflower)
   - Anxiety + depression → TCM formulas or Xiao Yao San
   - Stress + cognitive issues → Brahmi + Ashwagandha

3. **Traditional Medicine Approach:**
   - Constitutional treatment (TCM formulas)
   - Long-term balancing (Ayurvedic formulas)
   - Prefer holistic over reductionist approach

4. **Single Herb Inadequate:**
   - Tried optimal doses of best single herbs
   - Partial response, need enhancement

### Choose Single Herb When:

1. **Clear Primary Symptom:**
   - Pure anxiety without comorbidities → Silexan
   - Pure stress/cortisol issue → Ashwagandha

2. **First-Line Treatment:**
   - Start simple, add complexity only if needed
   - Easier to identify what's working

3. **Cost/Accessibility:**
   - Single herbs generally more affordable
   - Easier to source quality products

4. **Monitoring Response:**
   - Clearer cause-effect relationship
   - Easier to adjust dosing
