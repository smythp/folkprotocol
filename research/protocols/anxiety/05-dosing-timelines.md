# Dosing and Timelines for Anxiety Treatments

## Acute vs Chronic Anxiety Dosing

### Acute Anxiety (Situational/Performance)

**Definition:** Short-term anxiety related to specific events, situations, or stressors

#### Fast-Acting Options (Minutes to Hours)

**1. Lavender Aromatherapy**
- **Form:** Essential oil inhalation
- **Dose:** As needed
- **Onset:** Minutes
- **Duration:** Hours
- **Use case:** Immediate relief before stressful events
- **Method:** Direct inhalation, diffuser, or applied to pulse points

**2. L-Lysine + L-Arginine (Acute Stress Response)**
- **Dose:** 2.64 g of each
- **Onset:** Within hours for acute stress response
- **Study finding:** "Significantly reduced stress-induced STATE anxiety"
- **Duration:** Effects persist during stressor
- **Use case:** Pre-event dosing (1-2 hours before)

#### Rapid-Acting Options (Days to 1-2 Weeks)

**3. Kava**
- **Dose:** Per medical guidance (due to liver concerns)
- **Onset:** Days to 1-2 weeks
- **Duration:** Hours after single dose; cumulative with regular use
- **Use case:** Short-term anxiety episodes, social anxiety
- **CAUTION:** Requires medical supervision (hepatotoxicity)
- **Timeline:** Can work relatively quickly with regular dosing

**4. Passionflower**
- **Dose:** Standardized extract or traditional tea
- **Onset:** Days to 1-2 weeks with regular use; may work acutely
- **Duration:** Several hours per dose
- **Use case:** Situational anxiety, pre-event anxiety
- **Comparison:** Oxazepam worked faster initially, but passionflower equally effective by 4 weeks

**5. Valerian (if sleep component)**
- **Dose:** Standardized extract
- **Timing:** Evening dosing (sedative properties)
- **Onset:** Variable, some immediate sedation
- **Use case:** Anxiety with sleep disturbance
- **Note:** May cause morning grogginess

---

### Chronic Anxiety (Ongoing Management)

**Definition:** Persistent anxiety requiring long-term management

#### Standard Timeline (2-4 Weeks)

**1. Silexan (Lavender Oil) - FIRST LINE**
- **Dose:** 80-160 mg daily
- **Onset:** 2-4 weeks for full effect
- **Maintenance:** Continue at same dose
- **Duration of use:** Can be used long-term (excellent safety profile)
- **Use case:** Generalized anxiety, ongoing management
- **Dosing schedule:** Once daily with meal
- **Note:** Specific Silexan preparation studied in clinical trials

**2. Chamomile**
- **Dose:** Tea (1-4 cups daily) OR standardized extract
- **Onset:** 2-4 weeks
- **Maintenance:** Continue at same dose
- **Duration of use:** Generally safe for short to medium-term
- **Use case:** Mild chronic anxiety, excellent safety needed
- **Dosing schedule:** Tea throughout day or extract once-twice daily

---

#### Extended Timeline (4-13 Weeks for Full Effect)

**3. Ashwagandha - BEST FOR HPA AXIS**
- **Dose:** 250-500 mg daily (withanolide-standardized)
- **Onset:** 4-13 weeks for full HPA axis effects
- **Maintenance:** 250-500 mg daily ongoing
- **Duration of use:** Can continue long-term
- **Use case:** Stress-related anxiety, high cortisol, chronic anxiety
- **Dosing schedule:** Once or twice daily with food
- **Standardization:** Withanolide content (typically 1.5-5%)

**Clinical Timeline Detail:**
- Week 1-2: Minimal effects
- Week 4: Beginning to see stress reduction
- Week 8-13: Full cortisol reduction (23-33%)
- Ongoing: Sustained HPA axis balancing

**4. St. John's Wort (if depression component)**
- **Dose:** 300 mg three times daily
- **Onset:** 4-6 weeks for mood effects
- **Maintenance:** 900 mg daily (divided doses)
- **Duration of use:** Typically 6 months minimum for depression
- **Use case:** Anxiety with depression
- **Standardization:** Hypericin and/or hyperforin content
- **MAJOR CONCERN:** Drug interactions (CYP450 inducer)
- **Dosing schedule:** 300 mg three times daily with meals

---

#### Constitutional/Long-Term Balancing (Months)

**5. TCM Formulas**
- **Formulas:** Jiu Wei Zhen Xin, Xiao Yao San, Jieyu Anshen
- **Dose:** Per traditional TCM dosing or modern granule preparations
- **Onset:** Weeks to months
- **Timeline:** Months for constitutional balancing
- **Duration of use:** 3-6 months minimum, often longer
- **Use case:** Chronic anxiety, constitutional imbalance, long-term management
- **Approach:** Holistic balancing vs symptom suppression

**TCM Timeline Philosophy:**
- Immediate: Symptom relief begins
- Weeks 1-4: Initial rebalancing
- Months 2-3: Constitutional changes
- Months 3-6: Deep systemic balancing
- Ongoing: Maintain balance, prevent relapse

**6. Ayurvedic Formulas**
- **Formulas:** Saraswatarishta, Ashwagandharishta, Brahmi Vati
- **Dose:** Traditional dosing (e.g., arishta 15-30 ml twice daily)
- **Onset:** Weeks to months for full effect
- **Duration of use:** 3-6 months or longer
- **Use case:** Constitutional treatment, nervous system nourishment
- **Approach:** Long-term nervous tissue rejuvenation

## Dosing by Preparation Type

### Standardized Extracts

**Advantages:**
- Consistent active constituent content
- Dosing based on clinical trials
- Reliable effects

**Silexan (Lavender Oil):**
- **Dose:** 80-160 mg daily
- **Standardization:** Specific Silexan preparation
- **Form:** Soft gel capsules
- **Timing:** Once daily

**Ashwagandha:**
- **Dose:** 250-500 mg daily
- **Standardization:** Withanolide content (1.5-5%)
- **Form:** Capsules or powder
- **Timing:** Once daily or divided (morning and evening)
- **With food:** Improves absorption

**St. John's Wort:**
- **Dose:** 300 mg three times daily (900 mg total)
- **Standardization:** 0.3% hypericin OR 3-5% hyperforin
- **Form:** Tablets or capsules
- **Timing:** Three times daily with meals

**Kava:**
- **Dose:** Per medical guidance
- **Standardization:** Kavalactone content (typically 30-70%)
- **Form:** Capsules or tincture
- **Timing:** As directed by healthcare provider
- **Quality:** Water-extracted preparations may be safer than alcohol extracts

**Saffron:**
- **Dose:** 30 mg daily
- **Standardization:** Crocin and safranal content
- **Form:** Capsules
- **Timing:** Once daily
- **Note:** Expensive; limited accessibility

**Ginkgo:**
- **Dose:** Typically 120-240 mg daily (based on cognitive studies)
- **Standardization:** EGb 761 or similar (24% flavone glycosides, 6% terpene lactones)
- **Form:** Tablets or capsules
- **Timing:** Once or twice daily
- **Note:** Anxiety dosing less established than cognitive enhancement

---

### Traditional Preparations

**Chamomile Tea:**
- **Dose:** 1-4 cups daily
- **Preparation:** 1-2 teaspoons dried flowers per cup, steep 5-10 minutes
- **Timing:** Throughout day, including before bed
- **Onset:** 2-4 weeks with regular use
- **Acute use:** Can provide mild calming within 30-60 minutes

**Passionflower Tea:**
- **Dose:** 1-3 cups daily
- **Preparation:** 1-2 teaspoons dried herb per cup, steep 10 minutes
- **Timing:** As needed or regular schedule
- **Note:** Less potent than kava/valerian but useful

**Lemon Balm Tea:**
- **Dose:** 1-3 cups daily
- **Preparation:** 1-2 teaspoons dried leaves per cup, steep 5-10 minutes
- **Timing:** Throughout day
- **Effect:** Mild calming, digestive support

**Valerian:**
- **Dose:** Standardized extract (timing: evening)
- **Tea form:** Less palatable but traditional
- **Timing:** 30-60 minutes before bed
- **Note:** Sedative properties - avoid daytime use

**Holy Basil (Tulsi) Tea:**
- **Dose:** 2-3 cups daily
- **Preparation:** Traditional tea preparation
- **Timing:** Throughout day
- **Duration:** Weeks to months for adaptogenic effects

---

### TCM Decoctions and Granules

**Traditional Decoctions:**
- **Preparation:** Herbs boiled in water 30-60 minutes
- **Dose:** 1-3 cups daily (divided doses)
- **Timing:** Between meals or as directed
- **Customization:** TCM practitioner adjusts formula based on presentation

**Modern Granule Preparations:**
- **Form:** Concentrated powdered extracts
- **Dose:** 1-3 teaspoons dissolved in warm water
- **Timing:** 2-3 times daily
- **Advantages:** Convenience, standardized dosing, shelf-stable

**Examples:**
- Jiu Wei Zhen Xin: Per traditional or granule dosing
- Xiao Yao San: Typically 2-3 times daily
- Jieyu Anshen: Per practitioner guidance

---

### Ayurvedic Fermented Preparations (Arishta)

**Saraswatarishta:**
- **Dose:** 15-30 ml twice daily
- **Dilution:** Mix with equal parts water
- **Timing:** After meals
- **Duration:** 3-6 months for full effect
- **Alcohol content:** Self-generated through fermentation (3-10%)

**Ashwagandharishta:**
- **Dose:** 15-30 ml twice daily
- **Dilution:** Mix with equal parts water
- **Timing:** After meals
- **Duration:** 3-6 months

**Advantages of Arishta:**
- Enhanced bioavailability through fermentation
- Self-generated alcohol aids absorption
- Traditional formulation for long-term use

---

### Ayurvedic Ghee Preparations (Ghrita)

**Brahmi Ghrita:**
- **Dose:** 1-2 teaspoons daily
- **Timing:** Morning on empty stomach or with warm milk
- **Duration:** Weeks to months
- **Advantages:** Lipid base enhances absorption, nourishes nervous tissue

**Vijaya Ghrita (where legal):**
- **Dose:** As directed by Ayurvedic practitioner
- **Timing:** Evening typically
- **Use case:** Severe anxiety, nervous tension
- **Note:** Contains cannabis - legal status varies

## Amino Acid and Nutrient Combinations

**L-Lysine + L-Arginine:**
- **Dose:** 2.64 g of each daily (clinical trial dose)
- **Recommended range:** 2-4 g of each daily
- **Form:** Powder or capsules
- **Timing:** Once daily or divided doses
- **With food:** Can take with or without
- **Onset:** One week in clinical trial
- **Duration:** Continue as needed
- **Side effects:** Virtually none reported

**Magnesium + Vitamin B6:**
- **Dose:** Follow standard supplementation guidelines
  - Magnesium: Typically 200-400 mg elemental
  - B6: Typically 50-100 mg (P5P form preferred)
- **Timing:** With meals
- **Use case:** Premenstrual anxiety
- **Synergy:** Combination superior to magnesium alone
- **Note:** Magnesium monotherapy similar to placebo

## Timeline Expectations by Condition

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**First-Line:**
- **Silexan:** 2-4 weeks → Full effect
- **Ashwagandha:** 4-13 weeks → Full HPA axis modulation
- **Passionflower:** Days to 4 weeks → Equal to benzodiazepine by week 4

**Comparison to Pharmaceuticals:**
- SSRIs: 4-6 weeks for full effect
- Benzodiazepines: Immediate but tolerance/dependence issues
- **Herbs:** Similar timeline to SSRIs, faster than full SSRI effect

---

### Acute Situational Anxiety

**Fastest Options:**
- **Lavender aromatherapy:** Minutes
- **L-lysine + L-arginine (pre-dosed):** Hours
- **Kava (with regular use):** Days to 1-2 weeks cumulative effect

**Strategy:**
- Start regular dosing days before known stressor
- Use aromatherapy for immediate relief
- Combine approaches for maximum effect

---

### Stress-Related Anxiety (High Cortisol)

**Best Timeline:**
- **Ashwagandha:**
  - Week 1-2: Minimal
  - Week 4: Stress reduction begins
  - Week 8-13: Full cortisol reduction (23-33%)
  - Ongoing: Sustained HPA axis balancing

**Adjuncts:**
- **L-lysine + L-arginine:** 1 week → Cortisol reduction
- **Rhodiola:** Similar to ashwagandha (weeks to months)

**Monitoring:**
- Track cortisol levels (salivary or serum) at baseline and week 8-12
- Assess subjective stress (Perceived Stress Scale)

---

### Anxiety with Sleep Disturbance

**Immediate (same day):**
- **Valerian:** Variable onset, some immediate sedation

**Short-term (1-2 weeks):**
- **Passionflower:** Days to 1-2 weeks
- **Three Sisters combination:** Comparable to zolpidem for short-term

**Longer-term (4-13 weeks):**
- **Ashwagandha:** Reduces sleeplessness over 4-13 weeks

**Strategy:**
- Start with Valerian or Three Sisters for immediate sleep support
- Add Ashwagandha for long-term HPA axis regulation
- Taper acute sleep aids as long-term herbs take effect

---

### Anxiety + Depression Comorbidity

**Timeline:**
- **Saffron:** 4-6 weeks → Mood and anxiety improvement
- **St. John's Wort:** 4-6 weeks → Full antidepressant effect
- **Xiao Yao San (TCM):** 8 weeks in RCT, months for full constitutional effect
- **Jieyu Anshen:** Weeks to months

**Comparison:**
- **SSRIs:** 4-6 weeks minimum, 8-12 weeks for full effect
- **Herbs:** Comparable timeline, often better tolerability

## Dose Adjustments and Optimization

### Starting Doses vs Maintenance

**Conservative Approach (Recommended):**

**Ashwagandha:**
- Start: 250 mg daily for 1 week
- Increase: 500 mg daily if well-tolerated and needed
- Maximum studied: 500 mg daily

**Chamomile:**
- Start: 1 cup tea daily
- Increase: Up to 4 cups daily as needed

**Passionflower:**
- Start: Lower end of standardized extract range or 1 cup tea
- Increase: As needed and tolerated

**Silexan:**
- Start: 80 mg daily
- Increase: 160 mg daily if needed after 2-4 weeks
- Studied range: 80-160 mg

---

### When to Increase Dose

**Criteria:**
1. **Inadequate Response:**
   - After adequate trial at starting dose (2-4 weeks for most herbs)
   - Partial benefit but not full relief
   - Using validated scales (HAMA, GAD-7) to track objectively

2. **Good Tolerability:**
   - No adverse effects at current dose
   - Body has adjusted to herb

3. **Within Studied Range:**
   - Don't exceed doses studied in clinical trials
   - Higher ≠ better; risk of side effects increases

---

### When to Decrease or Discontinue

**Decrease Dose:**
1. Side effects emerge (GI upset, sedation, etc.)
2. Symptoms resolved and trialing lower maintenance dose
3. Adding other herbs/medications (interaction concerns)

**Discontinue:**
1. No benefit after adequate trial (4-8 weeks at therapeutic dose)
2. Intolerable side effects
3. Symptoms fully resolved (can trial discontinuation after 3-6 months)
4. Need for medication that interacts (esp. St. John's Wort, Kava)

**Tapering:**
- **Most herbs:** Can stop abruptly (not physically addictive)
- **Exception - Long-term St. John's Wort:** Taper over 1-2 weeks to avoid discontinuation symptoms
- **Recommendation:** Gradual reduction generally gentler on system

## Combination Dosing Strategies

### Sequential Addition (Recommended Approach)

**Step 1: Start with Single Best Herb (4-8 weeks)**
- Choose based on primary symptom:
  - General anxiety → Silexan 80-160 mg
  - Stress/cortisol → Ashwagandha 250-500 mg
  - Need benzodiazepine alternative → Passionflower

**Step 2: Assess Response**
- Full response → Continue monotherapy
- Partial response → Add synergistic combination
- No response → Try different single herb

**Step 3: Add Proven Synergistic Combination (if needed)**
- **L-lysine + L-arginine:** 2.64 g each daily
- **Magnesium + B6:** Standard doses (if premenstrual component)

**Step 4: Consider Traditional Formula (if still inadequate)**
- TCM formula (Xiao Yao San, Jiu Wei Zhen Xin)
- Ayurvedic formula (Brahmi + Ashwagandha)
- Three Sisters (if sleep component)

---

### Simultaneous Combination (Traditional Approach)

**TCM/Ayurvedic Practice:**
- Start with complete formula from beginning
- All herbs dosed together
- Rationale: Synergistic effects from day one
- Duration: 3-6 months minimum

**Western Combination:**
**Three Sisters for Anxiety + Sleep:**
- Valerian 300 mg + Hops 30 mg + Passionflower 80 mg
- All dosed together in evening
- Start together, not sequentially

## Special Populations

### Elderly

**Considerations:**
- Start lower doses
- Longer titration periods
- More sensitive to sedation (caution with Valerian, Passionflower)
- Drug interactions more likely

**Recommended:**
- **Ashwagandha:** Start 125 mg, increase slowly
- **Chamomile:** Excellent safety, start 1 cup daily
- **Avoid:** Kava (hepatotoxicity risk higher), St. John's Wort (polypharmacy interactions)

---

### Pregnancy and Lactation

**AVOID Most Herbs (Insufficient Safety Data):**
- Ashwagandha
- Passionflower
- Kava
- St. John's Wort
- Most standardized extracts

**Generally Considered Safer:**
- **Lavender aromatherapy** (external use)
- **Chamomile tea** (moderate amounts - 1-2 cups daily)

**Recommendation:** Consult healthcare provider before using ANY herbs during pregnancy/lactation

---

### Children and Adolescents

**Research Gap:** Very limited pediatric anxiety research

**General Principle:** Avoid most herbs in children due to lack of safety data

**Possible Exceptions (with medical supervision):**
- Chamomile tea (mild, traditional use)
- Lavender aromatherapy

**Recommendation:** Pediatric anxiety should be managed by qualified healthcare provider

---

### Hepatic Impairment

**ABSOLUTELY AVOID:**
- **Kava** (hepatotoxicity)

**Caution:**
- St. John's Wort (CYP450 effects)
- Any herbs metabolized by liver

**Safer Options:**
- Lavender aromatherapy (topical/inhalation, minimal liver metabolism)
- Chamomile (with caution)

**Recommendation:** Medical supervision essential

---

### Renal Impairment

**Generally Safe:**
- Most herbal anxiolytics (primarily hepatic metabolism)

**Monitor:**
- Magnesium supplementation (avoid in severe renal impairment)

**Recommendation:** Consult nephrologist before starting herbs

---

## Monitoring Response

### Validated Assessment Tools

**Hamilton Anxiety Rating Scale (HAMA):**
- Used in most research studies
- Clinician-administered
- 14 items
- Track at baseline, 4 weeks, 8 weeks, 12 weeks

**GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item scale):**
- Self-administered
- Quick and easy
- Can track weekly or biweekly
- Free and validated

**Subjective Tracking:**
- Sleep quality (1-10 scale)
- Daytime anxiety (1-10 scale)
- Panic attacks (frequency)
- Avoidance behaviors (frequency)
- Physical symptoms (tension, GI, etc.)

---

### When to Expect Improvement

**Rapid Response Herbs (Days to 2 Weeks):**
- Kava
- Passionflower
- Lavender aromatherapy

**Expected improvement timeline:**
- Week 1: May notice mild effects
- Week 2: Clear benefit if herb is effective
- Week 4: Maximal benefit

**Moderate Response Herbs (2-4 Weeks):**
- Silexan (Lavender oral)
- Chamomile

**Expected timeline:**
- Week 2: Beginning to notice effects
- Week 4: Clear benefit
- Week 6-8: Maximal benefit

**Sustained Response Herbs (4-13 Weeks):**
- Ashwagandha
- St. John's Wort
- TCM formulas

**Expected timeline:**
- Week 4: Initial effects
- Week 8: Substantial benefit
- Week 12-13: Maximal benefit

---

### If Inadequate Response

**After Adequate Trial (Timeline + Therapeutic Dose):**

1. **Verify Product Quality:**
   - Using standardized extract?
   - Reputable manufacturer?
   - Proper storage?

2. **Assess Dosing:**
   - At therapeutic dose from clinical trials?
   - Taking consistently?
   - Correct timing (with food, etc.)?

3. **Consider Different Herb:**
   - Different mechanism (GABA → serotonin → HPA axis)
   - Silexan → Ashwagandha → Passionflower

4. **Add Synergistic Combination:**
   - L-lysine + L-arginine
   - Magnesium + B6 (if applicable)

5. **Reassess Diagnosis:**
   - Is it truly GAD vs other anxiety disorder?
   - Comorbid depression?
   - Medical causes (thyroid, etc.)?

6. **Consider Conventional Treatment:**
   - May require pharmaceutical anxiolytic or antidepressant
   - Herbs can be adjunct

## Summary: Dosing Quick Reference

### First-Line Recommendations

**General Anxiety:**
- **Silexan:** 80-160 mg daily, 2-4 weeks

**Stress-Related Anxiety:**
- **Ashwagandha:** 250-500 mg daily, 4-13 weeks

**Acute Anxiety:**
- **Lavender aromatherapy:** As needed, immediate
- **L-lysine + L-arginine:** 2.64 g each, 1 week

**Anxiety + Sleep:**
- **Three Sisters:** Valerian 300 mg + Hops 30 mg + Passionflower 80 mg, evening

**Chronic/Constitutional:**
- **TCM formula:** Per practitioner, 3-6 months
- **Ashwagandha:** 250-500 mg daily, 4-13 weeks

### Timeline Expectations

- **Fastest:** Aromatherapy (minutes), Kava (days-2 weeks)
- **Standard:** Silexan, Chamomile (2-4 weeks)
- **Extended:** Ashwagandha, St. John's Wort (4-13 weeks)
- **Constitutional:** TCM/Ayurvedic formulas (months)

### Safety Monitoring

- **Excellent safety:** Can use long-term without intensive monitoring
  - Silexan, Chamomile, Lemon Balm, Passionflower

- **Requires monitoring:** Medical supervision essential
  - **Kava:** Liver function tests
  - **St. John's Wort:** Drug interaction monitoring
