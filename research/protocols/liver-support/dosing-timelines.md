# Dosing Guidelines and Expected Timelines

## Clinical Trial Dosages by Herb

---

## 1. Silymarin (Milk Thistle)

### Standard Dosing
- **Range:** 140-800 mg/day
- **Typical dose:** 420 mg/day divided into 2-3 doses
- **Standardization:** 70-80% silymarin content (calculated from whole extract)

### Timing
- **With or without food:** Generally taken with meals for better absorption
- **Frequency:** 2-3 times daily (divided doses may be more effective than single dose)

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **Minimum effective duration:** 8 weeks
- **Typical trial duration:** 8-24 weeks
- **Long-term use:** Safe in studies of alcoholic cirrhosis (chronic use documented)

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Liver Enzymes (ALT/AST):**
- **Initial response:** 4-8 weeks
- **Significant reduction:** 12-24 weeks
- **Note:** "Context-dependent" - most notable in NAFLD patients

**Liver Histology (biopsy or imaging):**
- **Timeline:** 6+ months for structural improvements
- **Evidence:** Improves liver histology in NAFLD patients

**Mortality/Major Outcomes:**
- **Timeline:** Long-term use (6+ months to years)
- **Evidence:** Liver-related mortality benefit seen in chronic alcoholic cirrhosis

### Acute vs Chronic Use
- **Chronic use appropriate:** Silymarin has extensive safety data for long-term use
- **Acute use (weeks):** May provide symptom relief but structural benefits take months

---

## 2. Artichoke (Cynara scolymus)

### Standard Dosing
- **Typical dose:** 2700 mg extract daily
- **Standardization:** Standardized to caffeoylquinic acids (especially cynarin)

### Timing
- **With meals:** Typically recommended
- **Frequency:** Often divided into 3 doses (900mg three times daily)

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **Typical trial duration:** 2-3 months
- **Minimum for effect:** 8 weeks

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Liver Enzymes:**
- **ALT/AST reduction:** Significant by 2 months (60 days)
- **Study finding:** 2700mg daily for 2 months improved liver enzymes

**Lipids (Cholesterol, Triglycerides):**
- **Timeline:** 2 months
- **Co-benefit:** Cholesterol and triglyceride reduction alongside liver enzyme improvement

### Acute vs Chronic Use
- **Duration studied:** 2-3 months primarily
- **Long-term data:** Limited beyond 3 months
- **Consideration:** May be used intermittently (2-3 month courses) or continuously

---

## 3. Green Tea Extract (EGCG)

### Standard Dosing
- **EGCG range:** 300-800 mg/day
- **Green tea extract:** 500 mg (typically providing ~250-300mg EGCG)
- **Dosing studied:**
  - 500mg tablet daily for 90 days
  - 100mg extract, 2 tablets, 3 times/day = 600mg daily
  - 300mg EGCG total (from gummy tablets)

### Timing
- **With food:** Recommended to reduce GI upset and potential hepatotoxicity
- **Avoid very high doses fasting:** Risk of liver enzyme elevation

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **Range:** 8-24 weeks
- **Typical:** 12 weeks (90 days)
- **Extended:** 6 months in some trials

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Liver Enzymes (ALT, AST):**
- **Initial response:** 8-12 weeks
- **Significant improvement:** 12 weeks

**Metabolic Parameters:**
- **Insulin resistance:** 12 weeks
- **Lipid metabolism:** 12 weeks
- **Body composition (BMI, visceral fat):** 12-24 weeks

**Liver Imaging:**
- **NAFLD grade improvement (ultrasound):** 12-24 weeks
- **Liver/spleen ratio improvement:** 6 months
- **Note:** One study showed 78.9% improvement in NAFLD grade

**Inflammatory Markers:**
- **hs-CRP reduction:** 6 months

### Acute vs Chronic Use
- **Chronic use preferred:** Metabolic and structural benefits require sustained use
- **Duration:** 3-6 months minimum for full liver benefits
- **Safety note:** Stay within 300-800mg EGCG range; higher doses (>800mg) may cause liver enzyme elevation

## 4. N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)

### Standard Dosing

**For NAFLD/General Liver Support:**
- **Dose:** 600 mg every 12 hours (1200 mg/day total)
- **Alternative range:** 600-1800 mg/day
- **Safety tested:** Up to 3 g/day with no significant adverse effects

**For Acute Liver Failure/Drug-Induced Injury (Hospital Protocol):**
- **Loading dose:** 150 mg/kg over 1 hour
- **Maintenance:** 12.5 mg/kg/h for 4 hours, then 6.25 mg/kg/h for remaining 67 hours
- **Application:** Acetaminophen overdose, non-acetaminophen acute liver failure

### Timing
- **Frequency:** Every 12 hours (twice daily)
- **With or without food:** Can be taken either way
- **Note:** May have sulfur odor/taste

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **NAFLD trials:** 3 months minimum
- **Acute drug-induced injury:** 72-hour hospital protocol

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Liver Enzymes (ALT):**
- **Timeline:** 3 months
- **Study finding:** NAC 600mg BID for 3 months resulted in decreased ALT

**Spleen Size (indication of portal hypertension):**
- **Timeline:** 3 months
- **Study finding:** Decreased spleen size vs vitamin C control

**Acute Liver Failure:**
- **Timeline:** Within 72 hours (acute protocol)
- **Outcomes:** Reduced mortality, shortened hospital stay

**Bilirubin and Albumin:**
- **Timeline:** Variable (weeks to months)
- **Systematic review:** NAC may have favorable effects on albumin and total bilirubin

### Acute vs Chronic Use

**Acute use (Drug overdose, acute liver injury):**
- Hospital IV protocol, 72 hours
- Immediate life-saving intervention

**Chronic use (NAFLD, general support):**
- Oral dosing, 600mg BID
- Minimum 3 months for liver benefits
- Safe for long-term use (up to 3g/day studied)

## 5. Curcumin/Turmeric

### Standard Dosing
- **Range:** 500-3000 mg/day
- **Typical:** 1000-1500 mg/day
- **Important:** Enhanced bioavailability formulations required
  - With piperine (black pepper extract)
  - Phospholipid complexes
  - Nanoparticle formulations

### Timing
- **With food:** Fat-containing meal improves absorption (curcumin is fat-soluble)
- **Frequency:** Usually 1-3 times daily depending on formulation

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **Minimum:** 8 weeks
- **Typical:** 12 weeks
- **Range studied:** 8-24 weeks

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Liver Enzymes:**
- **AST:** Mean reduction -3.90 U/L
- **ALT:** Mean reduction -5.61 U/L
- **Timeline:** 8-12 weeks

**Liver Imaging (Ultrasound):**
- **NAFLD severity reduction:** 12+ weeks
- **Steatosis resolution:** 78.9% improvement in one study
- **Timeline:** 12-24 weeks for imaging changes

**Cholesterol:**
- **Total cholesterol reduction:** 12 weeks
- **Co-benefit with liver improvement**

### Acute vs Chronic Use
- **Chronic use needed:** Benefits accumulate over 8-12+ weeks
- **Bioavailability challenge:** Standard turmeric powder likely ineffective; need enhanced formulations

### Evidence Quality Note
- **AST/ALT evidence:** Low quality
- **GGT evidence:** Very low quality
- **Limitations:** Small sample sizes, methodological issues in many studies

## 6. Schisandra chinensis

### Standard Dosing
- **Traditional dose:** 1.5-9 g dried fruit daily
- **Extract dosing:** Variable (often used in combination formulas)
- **Note:** Specific monotherapy dosing less established in modern trials

### Timing
- **Traditional use:** Decoction or tea, 1-3 times daily
- **Modern extracts:** Follow product standardization

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **Preclinical data:** Extensive (54 animal studies in systematic review)
- **Human data:** Limited specific duration guidelines
- **Traditional use:** Sustained/chronic use for adaptogenic benefits

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Liver Enzymes (from animal data):**
- **ALT, AST, ALP reduction:** Documented in preclinical models
- **Timeline:** Extrapolating from animal data suggests weeks to months

**Antioxidant Enzyme Upregulation:**
- **Glutathione, SOD, catalase increases:** Likely within 4-8 weeks based on mechanism
- **Nrf2 pathway activation:** Ongoing with sustained use

**Detoxification Enhancement:**
- **CYP450 upregulation (CYP3A, CYP2E1):** Improves hepatic clearance
- **Timeline:** Likely 2-4 weeks for enzyme induction

### Acute vs Chronic Use
- **Traditional adaptogenic use:** Chronic/sustained (weeks to months)
- **Liver support:** Often combined with other herbs; used long-term
- **Note:** Many formulas position schisandra as complementary rather than stand-alone

## 7. Licorice (Glycyrrhizin)

### Standard Dosing
- **Dose:** 1,000 mg/day licorice root extract
- **Duration:** 12 weeks in NAFLD trial

### Timing
- **Frequency:** Typically once or twice daily
- **Monitoring:** Blood pressure and potassium levels

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **NAFLD:** 12 weeks
- **Chronic hepatitis C:** Used for 20+ years clinically in China/Japan (long-term use established)

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Acute Protective Effects (During Alcohol Consumption):**
- **Immediate:** Prevented AST, ALT, GGT elevation in RCT
- **Timeline:** Concurrent with alcohol exposure

**NAFLD Improvements:**
- **Liver enzymes, hepatic steatosis, metabolic parameters:** 12 weeks
- **Oxidative stress reduction:** 12 weeks

**Fat Accumulation Reversal:**
- **Alcohol-fed models:** Significantly reversed fat accumulation
- **Timeline:** Weeks to months

### Acute vs Chronic Use

**Acute/Short-term:**
- Protective during alcohol consumption
- Use for specific hepatotoxic exposures

**Chronic use:**
- Established for chronic hepatitis C (20+ years of use)
- **Caution:** Monitor for hypertension and hypokalemia
- **Limit duration or use intermittently** due to mineralocorticoid effects

### Safety Monitoring
- **Blood pressure:** Weekly initially, then monthly
- **Potassium levels:** Baseline and monthly
- **Discontinue if:** Hypertension or hypokalemia develops

## 8. Picrorhiza kurroa (Kutki)

### Standard Dosing
- **Clinical trial dose:** 500 mg standardized extract
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Experimental dose (animal):** 400 mg/kg (not directly translatable to humans)

### Timing
- **Traditional Ayurvedic use:** With meals
- **Frequency:** Typically 1-2 times daily

### Duration from Clinical Trials
- **Human pilot study:** 12 weeks for mild chronic hepatitis
- **Note:** Limited human data available

### Expected Timeline for Improvements
- **Mild chronic hepatitis:** Significant improvement at 12 weeks (pilot study)
- **Preclinical comparisons:** Comparable to silymarin in animal toxicity models
- **Need:** Larger human trials to establish timelines

### Acute vs Chronic Use
- **Studied duration:** 12 weeks (chronic use)
- **Traditional use:** Long-term for liver conditions

## 9. Phyllanthus amarus

### Standard Dosing
- **Highly variable across studies**
- **Typical duration:** 30 days for hepatitis B trials
- **Note:** Dosing not well-standardized

### Expected Timeline for Improvements

**Hepatitis B markers:**
- **HBsAg clearance:** 30 days in positive studies (but highly variable - 59% vs 6%)
- **HBeAg conversion:** 3 months (42.3% conversion rate)
- **HBV DNA disappearance:** 3 months (47.8% clearance)

### Acute vs Chronic Use
- **Typical trials:** 30 days to 3 months
- **Note:** Variable results across populations

## General Dosing Principles

### Starting Dosages
- **Begin at lower end of range** to assess tolerance
- **Titrate up** to therapeutic dose over 1-2 weeks if well-tolerated

### Divided vs Single Daily Dosing
- **Silymarin, artichoke:** Divided doses (2-3x daily) common in trials
- **NAC:** Every 12 hours (twice daily)
- **Green tea, curcumin:** 1-3 times daily depending on formulation
- **Rationale:** Maintains more consistent blood levels

### With or Without Food
- **Fat-soluble compounds (curcumin):** With fatty meal improves absorption
- **Green tea (EGCG):** With food reduces GI upset and hepatotoxicity risk
- **NAC, silymarin, artichoke:** Generally with food for tolerability

## Expected Timeline Summary by Outcome

### Liver Enzymes (ALT, AST)
- **Initial response:** 4-8 weeks
- **Significant reduction:** 8-12 weeks
- **Optimal effect:** 12-24 weeks
- **Herbs with fastest effect:** NAC, artichoke (significant at 8-12 weeks)

### Liver Imaging Changes (Ultrasound, FibroScan)
- **Initial changes:** 12 weeks minimum
- **Significant improvement:** 12-24 weeks
- **Steatosis resolution:** 12-24 weeks (curcumin, green tea showed 78.9% improvement)
- **Fibrosis reversal:** 6+ months (slow process)

### Metabolic Parameters (Insulin, Lipids, Weight)
- **Insulin resistance:** 12 weeks
- **Cholesterol/triglycerides:** 8-12 weeks
- **Body composition (BMI, visceral fat):** 12-24 weeks
- **Best herbs for metabolic benefits:** Green tea EGCG, artichoke

### Symptom Improvements (Fatigue, Right Upper Quadrant Discomfort)
- **Timeline:** Variable, often 4-8 weeks
- **Note:** Symptomatic improvement less well-studied than objective markers

### Mortality/Major Clinical Outcomes
- **Timeline:** 6+ months to years
- **Evidence:** Silymarin in alcoholic cirrhosis (long-term mortality benefit)

## Acute vs Chronic Treatment Strategies

### Acute Liver Injury/Toxin Exposure
**Protocol:**
- **NAC:** First-line for drug overdose (72-hour hospital protocol)
- **Silymarin:** For general toxin blockade (start immediately)
- **Duration:** Days to weeks depending on injury severity

**Expected timeline:**
- **Acute drug injury:** Hours to days (NAC protocol)
- **Toxin exposure recovery:** 1-4 weeks with hepatoprotective herbs

---

### Chronic Liver Conditions (NAFLD, Cirrhosis, Chronic Hepatitis)
**Protocol:**
- **Silymarin, artichoke, green tea, curcumin:** Primary options
- **Duration:** Minimum 12 weeks, ideally 6+ months
- **Long-term use:** Appropriate for chronic conditions (silymarin has long-term safety data)

**Expected timeline:**
- **Enzyme normalization:** 8-24 weeks
- **Structural improvements (imaging):** 12-24 weeks
- **Fibrosis regression:** 6+ months to years
- **Metabolic improvements:** 12-24 weeks

**Monitoring frequency:**
- **Baseline:** Liver enzymes, imaging if available
- **4-8 weeks:** Recheck enzymes to assess response
- **12 weeks:** Comprehensive reassessment
- **Ongoing:** Every 3-6 months once stable

---

### Preventive/Supportive Use (No Active Disease)
**Protocol:**
- **Lower doses or intermittent use** may be appropriate
- **Options:** Artichoke (bile flow), NAC (glutathione support), green tea (antioxidant)
- **Duration:** Intermittent courses (2-3 months) or continuous at lower doses

**Expected benefit:**
- **Enzyme stability:** Ongoing
- **Detoxification support:** Ongoing
- **General hepatoprotection:** Ongoing

## Special Populations

### Elderly
- **Start low, go slow:** Begin at lower end of dosing range
- **Consider:** Reduced hepatic clearance may affect herb metabolism
- **Monitoring:** More frequent enzyme checks

### Pediatric
- **Data gap:** Most trials in adults; pediatric dosing inadequately studied
- **Approach:** Weight-based dosing if used, but evidence limited

### Pregnancy/Lactation
- **Contraindication:** Insufficient safety data for most herbs
- **Exception:** NAC has some pregnancy safety data (used for acetaminophen overdose in pregnancy)

### Advanced Liver Disease (Cirrhosis, Severe Hepatitis)
- **Medical oversight essential**
- **Silymarin:** Long-term safety in cirrhosis established
- **Caution with:** Herbs metabolized by liver may accumulate
- **Avoid:** Bupleurum in active hepatitis B (safety concern)

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Patience required:** Liver healing is slow - most benefits take 8-24 weeks
2. **Consistency matters:** Daily dosing more important than high occasional doses
3. **Monitoring essential:** Check enzymes at 4-8 weeks to assess response
4. **Chronic conditions need chronic treatment:** 3-6+ months minimum for structural improvements
5. **Acute emergencies (overdose) need immediate medical care:** NAC hospital protocol for acetaminophen overdose
6. **Enhanced formulations for curcumin:** Standard turmeric powder unlikely to be effective
7. **Silymarin has longest safety track record** for chronic use
8. **Green tea EGCG offers metabolic benefits** alongside liver protection (ideal for NAFLD with metabolic syndrome)
