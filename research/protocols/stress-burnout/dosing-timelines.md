# Dosing and Treatment Timelines: Stress-Burnout Protocol

## General Treatment Principles

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:508-512

**General Timeline:**
> "Most adaptogens require **4-12 weeks** of consistent use for full benefits on cortisol regulation and stress markers. Acute effects on mood and stress reactivity may be seen sooner (days to weeks) with some herbs like ashwagandha and holy basil."

**Two-Phase Response:**
1. **Acute effects** (days to weeks): Mood, stress reactivity
2. **Full HPA axis regulation** (4-12 weeks): Cortisol normalization, sustained benefits

---

## Herb-Specific Dosing and Timelines

### 1. Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)

**Standard Dose:** 300mg twice daily (600mg total daily)

**Duration for Full Effect:** 56-60 days

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:499

**Evidence:**
- Systematic review of 25 RCTs (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:10-16)
- Significantly decreased serum cortisol and Perceived Stress Scale scores after 56-60 days

**Timeline Breakdown:**
- **Days to weeks:** Calming effect felt (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:150)
- **56-60 days:** Full cortisol reduction and stress scale improvements

**Clinical Note:**
- Start noticing anxiety relief relatively quickly
- Full cortisol normalization requires nearly 2 months
- Sleep-promoting effects may appear earlier than cortisol changes

---

### 2. Rhodiola rosea

**Standard Dose:** 576mg daily (SHR-5 extract)

**Minimum Duration:** 4 weeks

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:500

**Evidence:**
- Phase III RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:108-118): 4 weeks showed significant effects on burnout scale and attention
- German study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:120-128): 8 weeks showed considerable alleviation of burnout symptoms

**Timeline Breakdown:**
- **4 weeks:** Anti-fatigue effect, improved concentration
- **8 weeks:** Considerable alleviation of exhaustion, depression, insomnia, fatigue, performance drop

**Burnout-Specific Timeline (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:488-493):**
- **Population:** 330 patients with burnout symptoms
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Result:** Considerable alleviation of complaints

**Clinical Note:**
- Mental performance benefits may be noticed within first month
- Burnout symptoms require longer treatment (8 weeks)
- Energizing effect noted (dose in morning)

---

### 3. Holy Basil / Tulsi (Ocimum tenuiflorum)

**Standard Dose:** 125mg twice daily (250mg total)

**Duration:** 8 weeks

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:501

**Evidence:**
- 2022 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:164-178): 100 volunteers, 8 weeks

**Timeline Breakdown:**
- **Acute:** Lower cortisol and subjective stress after stress test (within weeks)
- **8 weeks:** Significantly lower hair cortisol (long-term HPA dampening), improved Perceived Stress Scale (p=0.003), improved Athens Insomnia Scale (p=0.025)

**Clinical Note:**
- Acute stress response improves relatively quickly
- Hair cortisol (reflects long-term cortisol exposure) requires 8 weeks
- Sleep benefits measurable by 8 weeks

---

### 4. Panax Ginseng (Korean Red Ginseng)

**Standard Dose:** 2g/day

**Duration:** 6-8 weeks

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:502

**Evidence:**
- 2019 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:233-242): 6 weeks, HPA axis stabilization
- 2023 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:244-254): 8 weeks, prevented serotonin decrease and cortisol increase

**Timeline Breakdown:**
- **6 weeks:** CNS, ANS, HPA axis stabilization
- **8 weeks:** Neurotransmitter protection (serotonin, cortisol regulation)

**Clinical Note:**
- Longer timeline than some adaptogens
- Full HPA stabilization requires 6+ weeks
- Mental acuity benefits may appear earlier

---

### 5. Eleuthero / Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus)

**Standard Dose:** 300-4,000mg/day (1,000mg/day standard effective)

**Duration:** 2 months

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:503

**Evidence:**
- 2004 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:211-220): 96 patients, 2 months for chronic fatigue

**Timeline:**
- **2 months:** Significant reductions in fatigue severity, duration, mental fatigue

**Clinical Note:**
- Chronic fatigue requires full 2-month course
- Do not expect quick results
- Concentration and reaction time improvements within this timeframe

---

### 6. Bacopa monnieri

**Standard Dose:** 300mg/day

**Minimum Duration:** 8-12 weeks for stress/cognitive benefits

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:504

**Unique Acute Dosing (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:270):**
- **Single dose:** 320mg or 640mg
- **Acute effect:** Improved mood and decreased cortisol after 1 dose (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:271-272)

**Evidence:**
- 2008 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:275-285): 12 weeks in elderly (300mg/day)
- 2021 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:287-297): 28 days (150mg twice daily) for sleep

**Timeline Breakdown:**
- **1 dose:** Acute stress reactivity improvement
- **28 days (4 weeks):** Sleep quality improvement
- **12 weeks:** Full cognitive and anxiety/depression benefits in elderly

**Clinical Note:**
- **Unique dual timeline:** Acute effects + long-term benefits
- Can use acutely for high-stress events
- Chronic use required for sustained cognitive benefits

---

### 7. Reishi (Ganoderma lucidum)

**Dose Range:** 1,800-5,400mg/day

**Duration:** 6-8 weeks

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:505-506

**Evidence:**
- Neurasthenia study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:324-330): 1,800mg three times daily (5,400mg total), 8 weeks
- Fibromyalgia study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:313-322): 6g/day, 6 weeks
- 2025 combo study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:301-311): 6 weeks

**Timeline:**
- **6-8 weeks:** Improved clinician ratings, reduced fatigue (neurasthenia)

**Clinical Note:**
- High doses used in studies (up to 6g/day)
- Sleep benefits (serotonin pathway) may appear within weeks
- Full neurasthenia/burnout benefits require 6-8 weeks

---

### 8. Schisandra chinensis

**Dose:** Often combined with other adaptogens; single dose studies show acute benefits

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:506

**Evidence:**
- 2016 HPA axis study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:182-189): Markedly decreased CRH and cortisol
- Combination studies (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:392-396): Reduces fatigue, improves mental accuracy

**Clinical Note:**
- Most research in animals (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:591-593)
- Human trials needed for burnout-specific timeline
- Typically used in combination formulas (Rhodiola + Schisandra + Eleuthero)

## Acute vs. Chronic Stress Dosing

### Acute Stress (Exams, Presentations, High-Pressure Events)

**Best Evidence: Bacopa**
- **Single dose:** 320-640mg (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:270)
- **Effect:** Improved mood, decreased cortisol response after 1 dose (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:271-272)
- **Best for:** Multitasking stress reactivity (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:273)

**Strategy for Acute Events:**
1. **Baseline support (4-8 weeks before):** Start Rhodiola or Ashwagandha
2. **Day-of:** Add Bacopa for acute reactivity support

**Limitation:**
- Other adaptogens (Ashwagandha, Rhodiola) require weeks to months for full effect
- Not suitable for "day-of" acute stress dosing

---

### Chronic Stress / Burnout

**Timeline Required: 4-12 weeks minimum**

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:508-512

**Why Longer Duration:**
- Full cortisol regulation requires sustained HPA axis normalization
- Glucocorticoid receptor sensitivity restoration takes time (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:443-446)
- Neurotransmitter rebalancing (serotonin, GABA, dopamine) is gradual (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:467-469)

**Herb Selection for Chronic Stress:**
1. **Ashwagandha:** 56-60 days for cortisol normalization
2. **Rhodiola:** 4-8 weeks for burnout
3. **Holy Basil:** 8 weeks for HPA dampening
4. **Eleuthero:** 2 months for chronic fatigue

**Clinical Note:**
> "Most adaptogens require 4-12 weeks of consistent use for full benefits on cortisol regulation and stress markers." (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:508-510)

## Traditional Chinese Medicine Timeline

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:560-563

**TCM Expectation:**
> "Herbal tonic formulas help rebuild what has been depleted over years. Can restore wellness and core vitality by restoring organ systems like adrenals and kidneys. Takes **2 months or more** before noticeable changes in vitality may be felt."

**TCM Formulas:**
- **Xiao Yao San:** For burnout (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:400-406)
- **Bu-Zhong-Yi-Qi-Tang:** For chronic fatigue (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:408-412)
- **Sijunzi Decoction:** Fundamental fatigue formula (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:414-422)

**TCM Timeline vs. Modern Adaptogens:**
- **TCM:** 2+ months for vitality changes
- **Modern (e.g., Ashwagandha):** Days to weeks for calming, 56-60 days for cortisol
- **Modern (e.g., Rhodiola):** 4 weeks minimum

**Clinical Implication:**
- TCM formulas aim for deeper constitutional restoration
- Requires patience
- May be slower onset but potentially more comprehensive

## Long-Term Use and Safety

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:596

**Research Gap:**
> "Long-term safety data (>12 weeks) limited for most adaptogens"

**Safety Notes (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:567-574):**
- Holy Basil: Well tolerated, no significant adverse effects in 8-week trial
- Ashwagandha: Generally well-tolerated in clinical trials
- Rhodiola: Good safety profile in clinical studies
- Bacopa: Safe in elderly populations over 12 weeks

**Licorice Exception (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:576-580):**
- Can cause pseudohyperaldosteronism if long-term/high dose
- Not suitable for unsupervised long-term use

**Clinical Guidance:**
- Most studies 4-12 weeks
- Longer-term use (6+ months) should be monitored
- Periodic breaks may be prudent (though not well-studied)
- TCM tradition uses tonics for extended periods (months to years)
