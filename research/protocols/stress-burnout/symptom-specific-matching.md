# Symptom-Specific Matching: Stress-Burnout Herbs

## Clinical Decision Framework

**Key Principle:** Match herb energetics to symptom pattern
- **Calming herbs** for anxiety-dominant stress
- **Energizing herbs** for fatigue-dominant stress
- **Balanced herbs** when both are present

---

## Symptom Pattern 1: High Anxiety + Sleep Disturbance

### Primary Herbs

**1. Ashwagandha** (First-line)
- **Cortisol:** Significantly decreases serum cortisol (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:15-16)
- **Anxiety:** Significantly outperforms other herbs for anxiety reduction (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:148-149)
- **Sleep:** Sleep-promoting effects (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:159-160)
- **Timeline:** Calming effect within **days to weeks** (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:150)

**Dosing:** 300mg twice daily (600mg total) for 56-60 days (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:499)

**2. Holy Basil** (Add-on or alternative)
- **HPA axis:** Hair cortisol significantly lower (long-term dampening) (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:172-173)
- **Sleep:** Improved Athens Insomnia Scale scores (p=0.025) (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:177-178)
- **Acute stress:** Lower cortisol, blood pressure, subjective stress after stressor (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:174-176)
- **Effect:** Balanced (not overly sedating)

**Dosing:** 125mg twice daily (250mg total) for 8 weeks (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:501)

**3. Reishi** (Sleep-specific add-on)
- **Sleep mechanism:** Shortens sleep latency, prolongs sleep time via serotonin/gut microbiota (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:337-339)
- **Sedative:** Analgesic and sedative effects (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:321)

**Dosing:** 1,800-5,400mg/day for neurasthenia/burnout (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:505-506)

### Combination Strategy
- **Start:** Ashwagandha alone
- **If sleep persists:** Add Reishi at bedtime
- **If need balanced effect:** Switch to or add Holy Basil

## Symptom Pattern 2: Mental Fatigue + Performance Demands

### Primary Herbs

**1. Rhodiola** (First-line)
- **Mental performance:** Strongest evidence for attention, concentration under stress (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:529-531)
- **Energizing:** Noted energizing effect (vs. calming) (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:138, 159)
- **Cortisol:** Decreases cortisol response to awakening stress (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:117-118)
- **Effect:** Anti-fatigue effect increases mental performance, ability to concentrate (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:116-118)

**Dosing:** 576mg daily (SHR-5 extract), minimum 4 weeks (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:500)

**2. Bacopa** (Memory-specific add-on)
- **Cognitive:** Best for memory and cognitive function, especially elderly (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:531)
- **Multitasking:** Acute effects on multitasking stress reactivity (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:273)
- **Acute benefit:** Improved mood, decreased cortisol after **1 dose** (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:271-272)

**Dosing:** 300mg/day for 8-12 weeks (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:504)

**3. Panax Ginseng** (Concentration add-on)
- **HPA axis:** Stabilizes CNS, ANS, HPA axis (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:241-242)
- **Mental acuity:** Concentration and mental acuity under stress (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:533)
- **Neurotransmitters:** Prevents serotonin decrease in stressed individuals (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:251-253)

**Dosing:** 2g/day for 6-8 weeks (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:502)

### Combination Strategy
- **Start:** Rhodiola alone (morning)
- **If memory issues:** Add Bacopa
- **If concentration poor:** Add Panax Ginseng
- **Triple combo:** Rhodiola + Schisandra + Eleuthero (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:392-396)

## Symptom Pattern 3: Burnout (Exhaustion + Depression + Insomnia + Performance Drop)

### Primary Herb

**Rhodiola** (Strong burnout-specific evidence)

**German Primary Care Study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:488-493):**
- **Population:** 330 patients with burnout symptoms
- **Symptoms treated:** Exhaustion, depression, insomnia, fatigue, drop in performance
- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Result:** "Considerable alleviation of complaints"

**Phase III RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:108-118):**
- **Population:** 60 individuals with stress-related fatigue
- **Findings:** Significant effects on Pines' burnout scale and attention indices
- **Duration:** 4 weeks at 576mg daily

### Add-on Based on Dominant Symptom

**If anxiety is prominent:** Add Ashwagandha
- Creates balanced calm-focus effect (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:374-376)

**If depression is prominent:** Consider Schisandra
- BDNF/TrkB/CREB signaling upregulation (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:196-198)
- Antidepressant-like effects in corticosterone-induced depression (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:195-196)

**If insomnia is severe:** Add Reishi
- Sleep promotion via serotonin pathway (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:337-339)

### Traditional Alternative

**Xiao Yao San (Free and Easy Wanderer)**
- "One of most used herbs in TCM, **often used for burnout**" (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:404-405)
- Soothes liver (associated with anger, burnout symptom) (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:405-406)

## Symptom Pattern 4: Chronic Fatigue Syndrome

### Primary Herb

**Eleuthero / Siberian Ginseng** (Strongest CFS evidence)

**2004 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:211-220):**
- **Population:** 96 patients with moderate fatigue
- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled (University of Iowa)
- **Duration:** 2 months
- **Results:** Significant reductions in fatigue severity, duration, mental fatigue vs. placebo

**2009 Systematic Review (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:50-55):**
- Good evidence for increasing endurance and mental performance in mild fatigue/weakness
- Improved concentration, mental acuity, reaction time under stress-related fatigue (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:228-229)

**Dosing:** 300-4,000mg/day (1,000mg/day standard effective dose) (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:503)

### Add-ons

**Rhodiola:** For mental performance component
- Strong evidence for cognitive function in fatigue (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:50-52)

**Schisandra:** For synergy
- Studies show Schisandra + Rhodiola + Eleuthero reduces fatigue, improves mental accuracy (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:392-396)

### Traditional TCM Alternative

**Sijunzi Decoction**
- "Fundamental formula for fatigue" in China (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:419-420)
- Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (2024) (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:416-418)
- Improves fatigue via gut microbiota changes (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:415-416)

**Bu-Zhong-Yi-Qi-Tang**
- RCT in 38 CFS patients (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:408-412)
- 18 patients able to resume normal work/activity
- 16 additional had symptoms relieved

## Symptom Pattern 5: Need for Both Calm AND Focus

### "Balanced" Herb Approach

**Holy Basil** (Best single-herb option)
- **Not overly sedating** (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:177-178)
- **Calming:** Lower cortisol, subjective stress (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:174-176)
- **Supportive:** Improved Perceived Stress Scale, sleep, but maintains function (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:177-178)

**Dosing:** 125mg twice daily for 8 weeks (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:501)

### Combination Approach

**Rhodiola + Ashwagandha** (Synergistic)

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:367-384

**Rationale:**
> "**Complementary adaptogens**—ashwagandha more calming, rhodiola more energizing—together supporting steady, calm focus under stress." (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:374-376)

**Combined Benefits:**
- Enhanced stress reduction
- Mood regulation
- Energy and vitality
- Improved cognitive function (memory + concentration)
- **Calm, focused, stabilized energy throughout day** (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:383-384)

**Strategy:**
- Take Rhodiola in morning (energizing)
- Take Ashwagandha in evening (calming, sleep support)
- Or both together if tolerated

## Symptom Pattern 6: Acute Stress Reactivity (Multitasking, Exams, High-Pressure Events)

### Acute-Acting Herbs

**1. Bacopa** (Fastest evidence)
- **Timeline:** Improved mood and decreased cortisol after **1 dose** (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:271-272)
- **Multitasking:** Acute effects on multitasking stress reactivity (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:273)

**Dosing:** 320mg or 640mg (acute study doses) (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:270)

**2. Rhodiola** (4-week minimum for full effects)
- But acute stress response may improve sooner based on mechanism

**Strategy for Acute Events:**
- **Baseline support:** Start Rhodiola or Ashwagandha 4-8 weeks before event
- **Day-of:** Bacopa for acute reactivity support

## Symptom Pattern 7: Low Cortisol States (Suspected Adrenal Insufficiency)

### Special Case: Licorice

**Mechanism (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:343-363):**
- Inhibits 11β-HSD2 (enzyme converting cortisol → cortisone)
- **Unique cortisol-sparing mechanism** (slows breakdown vs. increasing production)
- Allows naturally produced cortisol to remain active longer (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:349-350)

**Indications:**
- Mild adrenal insufficiency
- Low aldosterone output
- Hyperkalemia
- **Under professional supervision only** (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:578-580)

**Safety Warning (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:576-580):**
- Can cause pseudohyperaldosteronism
- May elevate blood pressure
- Can cause electrolyte imbalances
- Not for long-term unsupervised use

**Clinical Note:**
- Most other adaptogens work by **normalizing HPA axis response**
- Licorice works by **sparing existing cortisol**
- Different mechanism, different indication

## Mental Fatigue vs. Physical Fatigue

**Source:** bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:473-484

**Physical Fatigue:**
- Tiredness from repeated muscle movements
- **Improves with rest**

**Mental Fatigue:**
- Decreased attention, reduced concentration, somnolence
- **Persists even after sleep** if brain hasn't recovered from stress
- Adaptogens work on underlying stress mechanisms rather than targeting specific symptoms

**Herb Selection:**
- **Physical fatigue with stress:** Eleuthero, Cordyceps, Astragalus (qi tonics)
- **Mental fatigue with stress:** Rhodiola, Bacopa, Panax Ginseng
- **Both:** Rhodiola (strongest evidence for both)

## Age-Specific Considerations

### Elderly Population

**Bacopa** (Best evidence in elderly)
- 2008 RCT in participants 65+ (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:275-285)
- Decreased depression scores, anxiety scores, heart rate
- Improved cognitive performance
- **Duration:** 12 weeks at 300mg/day

**Eleuthero** (Quality of life in elderly)
- Randomized trial showing QOL benefits (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:222-229)

## Summary: Symptom → Herb Matching

| Symptom Pattern | First-Line Herb | Add-on Options | Timeline |
|----------------|----------------|----------------|----------|
| High anxiety + sleep issues | Ashwagandha | Holy Basil, Reishi | Days to weeks (calming); 56-60 days (full cortisol) |
| Mental fatigue + performance | Rhodiola | Bacopa, Panax Ginseng | 4 weeks minimum |
| Burnout (multi-symptom) | Rhodiola | Ashwagandha (if anxious), Schisandra (if depressed) | 4-8 weeks |
| Chronic fatigue syndrome | Eleuthero | Rhodiola, Schisandra | 2 months |
| Need calm + focus | Holy Basil OR Rhodiola+Ashwagandha | — | 8 weeks (Holy Basil); 4-8 weeks (combo) |
| Acute stress reactivity | Bacopa (acute) | Rhodiola or Ashwagandha (baseline) | 1 dose (Bacopa); 4-8 weeks (baseline) |
| Low cortisol | Licorice (supervised) | — | — |

**General Principle:**
> "Adaptogens work on underlying stress mechanisms rather than targeting specific symptoms." (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:482-484)

**Timeline Expectation (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:508-512):**
- **Most adaptogens:** 4-12 weeks for full cortisol/HPA axis benefits
- **Acute effects:** Days to weeks for mood/stress reactivity (Ashwagandha, Holy Basil, Bacopa)
- **TCM approach:** 2+ months before noticeable vitality changes (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:560-563)
