# Evidence Ranking: Herbs for Stress and Burnout

## Strongest Evidence

### 1. Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)
**Evidence Grade: A+**

**Key Studies:**
- 2023 systematic review of 25 RCTs (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:10-16)
- 2024 meta-analysis on stress/anxiety (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:18-25)
- Multiple RCTs showing cortisol reduction after 56-60 days

**Primary Benefits:**
- **Cortisol reduction**: Significantly decreased serum cortisol levels
- **Anxiety relief**: Significantly outperforms Rhodiola for anxiety reduction
- **Sleep support**: Sleep-promoting effects noted
- **Perceived stress**: Remarkable improvements in Perceived Stress Scale scores

**Timeline:**
- Calming effect felt within **days to weeks** of consistent use
- Full cortisol normalization: **56-60 days**

**Best For:**
- High anxiety with stress
- Sleep disturbances from stress
- Need for calming effect
- Cortisol dysregulation

---

### 2. Rhodiola rosea
**Evidence Grade: A**

**Key Studies:**
- 2009 Phase III RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:108-118): 60 individuals, stress-related fatigue
- 2022 German primary care study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:120-128): 330 patients with burnout
- 2018 review of >70 human clinical trials (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:130-138)

**Primary Benefits:**
- **Mental performance**: Strongest evidence for attention, concentration under stress
- **Burnout**: "Considerable alleviation" of exhaustion, depression, insomnia, fatigue, performance drop
- **Energizing**: Noted energizing effect (vs. ashwagandha's calming)
- **Cortisol**: Decreases cortisol response to awakening stress

**Timeline:**
- Minimum **4 weeks** (576mg daily SHR-5 extract)
- Burnout symptoms: **8 weeks** for considerable alleviation

**Best For:**
- Mental fatigue and burnout
- Need for energy/alertness
- Performance under stress
- When calming herbs make you too sedated

---

### 3. Holy Basil / Tulsi (Ocimum tenuiflorum)
**Evidence Grade: A**

**Key Studies:**
- 2022 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:164-178): 100 volunteers, 8 weeks, 125mg 2x/day
- 2017 systematic review of 24 studies (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:57-66)

**Primary Benefits:**
- **Hair cortisol**: Significantly lower (long-term HPA dampening)
- **Acute stress response**: Lower salivary cortisol (p=0.001), amylase, blood pressure
- **Sleep**: Improved Athens Insomnia Scale scores (p=0.025)
- **Balanced effect**: Both calming and supportive (not overly sedating)

**Timeline:**
- **8 weeks** for full HPA axis dampening
- Acute stress benefits measurable within weeks

**Best For:**
- Balanced approach (not too stimulating/sedating)
- Sleep issues with stress
- Long-term HPA axis regulation
- Those wanting lifestyle medicine approach

---

## Good Evidence

### 4. Panax Ginseng (Korean Red Ginseng)
**Evidence Grade: B+**

**Key Studies:**
- 2019 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:233-242): 63 high-stress individuals, 2g/day, 6 weeks
- 2023 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:244-254): 90 men, 8 weeks
- 2017 review on HPA axis effects (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:77-84)

**Primary Benefits:**
- **HPA axis stabilization**: Stabilizes CNS, ANS, and HPA axis
- **Neurotransmitters**: Prevents serotonin decrease, cortisol increase in stressed individuals
- **Mental acuity**: Concentration under stress
- **Cortisol prevention**: Ginsenoside Rd dampens stress-triggered cortisol

**Timeline:**
- **6-8 weeks** at 2g/day

**Best For:**
- HPA axis dysregulation
- Mental concentration under stress
- Those preferring traditional Asian medicine

---

### 5. Schisandra chinensis
**Evidence Grade: B**

**Key Studies:**
- 2016 study with Rhodiola (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:182-189): Decreased CRH and cortisol
- 2016 BDNF/TrkB/CREB pathway study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:191-198)
- 2011 combo study with Rhodiola (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:200-207)

**Primary Benefits:**
- **HPA axis balancing**: Markedly decreased stress-induced CRH and cortisol
- **Neuroprotection**: BDNF/TrkB/CREB signaling upregulation
- **Synergy**: Works well in combination with Rhodiola, Eleuthero

**Limitation:**
- Most research in animals; human trials for burnout needed

**Best For:**
- Combination formulas
- Those with depression from chronic stress
- Preference for multi-herb approach

---

### 6. Eleuthero / Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus)
**Evidence Grade: B**

**Key Studies:**
- 2004 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:211-220): 96 patients, chronic fatigue
- 2009 systematic review (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:46-55): Good evidence for endurance and mental performance

**Primary Benefits:**
- **Chronic fatigue**: Significant reductions in severity, duration, mental fatigue
- **Mental acuity**: Improved concentration, reaction time under stress-related fatigue
- **Endurance**: Mental performance in mild fatigue/weakness

**Timeline:**
- **2 months** for chronic fatigue improvement

**Best For:**
- Chronic fatigue syndrome
- Mental fatigue under stress
- Those needing reaction time/acuity

---

### 7. Bacopa monnieri
**Evidence Grade: B**

**Key Studies:**
- 2013 acute study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:266-273): Single dose improved mood, decreased cortisol
- 2008 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:275-285): 12 weeks in elderly
- 2021 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:287-297): 150mg 2x/day, 28 days, poor sleep

**Primary Benefits:**
- **Acute effects**: Improved mood and decreased cortisol after **1 dose**
- **Anxiety/depression**: Decreased combined anxiety scores, depression scores
- **Sleep**: Improved sleep quality in poor sleepers
- **Cognitive**: Best for memory, cognitive function in elderly

**Timeline:**
- Acute: **1 dose** for mood/cortisol
- Full benefits: **8-12 weeks**

**Best For:**
- Acute stress reactivity
- Elderly with stress/cognitive decline
- Multitasking stress
- Poor sleep with stress

---

### 8. Reishi (Ganoderma lucidum)
**Evidence Grade: B-**

**Key Studies:**
- 2025 RCT (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:301-311): 499 adults, 6 weeks, combo with ashwagandha
- Neurasthenia study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:324-330): 132 adults, 1,800mg 3x/day
- 2020 fibromyalgia study (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:313-322): 6g/day, 6 weeks

**Primary Benefits:**
- **Neurasthenia/burnout**: Improved clinician ratings, reduced fatigue
- **Sleep**: Shortens sleep latency, prolongs sleep time (via serotonin/gut microbiota)
- **Fatigue reduction**: 28.3% decrease reported

**Timeline:**
- **6-8 weeks** for neurasthenia/burnout

**Best For:**
- Neurasthenia (fatigue, poor rest, low resilience)
- Sleep promotion in burnout
- Those preferring mushroom medicine

## Promising but Limited Evidence

### 9. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)
**Evidence Grade: C+**

**Mechanism (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:343-363):**
- Inhibits 11β-HSD2 enzyme (converts cortisol → cortisone)
- Slows cortisol breakdown → naturally produced cortisol remains active longer
- **Unique cortisol-sparing mechanism** vs. other adaptogens

**Best For:**
- Low cortisol states (mild adrenal insufficiency)
- Low aldosterone
- Hyperkalemia
- **Adjunctive therapy under professional supervision**

**Safety Warning:**
- Can cause pseudohyperaldosteronism (high BP, electrolyte imbalances)
- Not for long-term unsupervised use

## Summary: Evidence Quality

**Tier 1 (Strongest):** Ashwagandha, Rhodiola, Holy Basil
**Tier 2 (Good):** Panax Ginseng, Schisandra, Eleuthero, Bacopa, Reishi
**Tier 3 (Promising/Traditional):** Licorice (special use), TCM formulas

**Research Gaps (bibliographies/stress-burnout-protocol.yaml:589-596):**
1. No direct head-to-head comparison trials between major adaptogens
2. Variability in study designs limits comparability
3. Schisandra needs more human trials for burnout
4. TCM formulas need better diagnostic standards
5. Optimal combination ratios not established
6. Long-term safety data (>12 weeks) limited
