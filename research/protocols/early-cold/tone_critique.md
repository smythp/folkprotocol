# Tone Critique: Early Cold & Immune Support Protocol

## 1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT

**Score: 4/10** (Too clinical/encyclopedic)

This reads more like a literature review than a friend helping you think through your options. While the information is solid and well-organized, it lacks the warm, decision-support framing that makes protocol pages actionable. The voice is primarily third-person academic rather than second-person guidance.

## 2. OVERVIEW SECTION

**Status: NEEDS WORK - Clinical definition style**

CURRENT:
> "Early cold refers to the initial stages of upper respiratory tract infection, characterized by symptoms such as scratchy throat, sniffles, mild fatigue..."

PROBLEM: This is a clinical definition. It tells you what an early cold IS, not how to think about it or what you're dealing with.

NEEDED: Lead with empathy - "If you're feeling that first tickle in your throat..." framing.

## 3. EVIDENCE TIERS

**Status: NEEDS MAJOR REWRITE - Literature review style**

The entire "Landscape" section reads like a research summary:
- "A 2024 meta-analysis of 30 trials with 5,652 participants found..."
- "A 2017 systematic review of 33 RCTs with 7,175 participants found..."
- Lists statistics without guidance on what to DO

NEEDED: Transform from "here's what studies found" to "here's what you should try first, and why"

## 4. SPECIFIC PASSAGES TO FIX

### Passage 1: Overview Opening
- **SECTION:** Overview, paragraph 1
- **CURRENT TEXT:** "Early cold refers to the initial stages of upper respiratory tract infection, characterized by symptoms such as scratchy throat, sniffles, mild fatigue, and the general sense that you're 'coming down with something.'"
- **PROBLEM:** Clinical definition opening
- **SUGGESTED DIRECTION:** Start with "If you're feeling that first scratchy throat, the sniffles starting, that 'I think I'm getting sick' feeling - here's how to think about your options and what might actually help."

### Passage 2: Evidence Presentation
- **SECTION:** The Landscape - Strong Evidence
- **CURRENT TEXT:** "For prevention, [[materia/echinacea]] has the strongest evidence. A 2024 meta-analysis of 30 trials with 5,652 participants found echinacea reduced monthly respiratory tract infection occurrence by 32% (RR 0.68), reduced the number of people developing infections by 25% (RR 0.75)..."
- **PROBLEM:** Leads with study details, uses statistical notation (RR), no guidance
- **SUGGESTED DIRECTION:** Lead with the recommendation: "For prevention, echinacea is your strongest bet. Taking it throughout cold season cuts your chances of catching a cold by roughly a third..."

### Passage 3: Elderberry Evidence Dump
- **SECTION:** The Landscape - Elderberry
- **CURRENT TEXT:** "A 2019 meta-analysis found a large effect size (1.717) for reducing upper respiratory symptoms [3]. Clinical trials show elderberry reduces illness duration by 2-4 days compared to placebo [4,5]. One trial found symptoms relieved 4 days earlier..."
- **PROBLEM:** Effect size (1.717) is statistical jargon; lists multiple trial results without synthesis
- **SUGGESTED DIRECTION:** "Elderberry can shorten a cold by 2-4 days - but here's the catch: you have to start it within 48 hours of first symptoms. Miss that window and it's less helpful."

### Passage 4: Core Protocol Format
- **SECTION:** Core Protocol - For Acute Treatment
- **CURRENT TEXT:** "**Primary approach: Elderberry** Elderberry syrup: 15 ml (1 tablespoon) four times daily for 5 days [4]. Alternatively, capsules: 600-900 mg daily, standardized to 15-22% anthocyanins (90-135 mg anthocyanins daily) [5]."
- **PROBLEM:** Recipe-card format, technical standardization details, no "here's how to think about this"
- **SUGGESTED DIRECTION:** "My first suggestion: elderberry syrup. Take a tablespoon four times a day for 5 days. The key is getting started fast - the 48-hour window is real."

### Passage 5: Andrographis Introduction
- **SECTION:** The Landscape - Andrographis
- **CURRENT TEXT:** "Andrographis paniculata ('Indian echinacea') also has strong acute treatment evidence. A 2017 systematic review of 33 RCTs with 7,175 participants found it beneficial for relieving symptoms..."
- **PROBLEM:** Third-person, study-focused
- **SUGGESTED DIRECTION:** "If you can't get elderberry or want something different, andrographis (sometimes called 'Indian echinacea') works well for acute colds too. 33 trials back this up."

### Passage 6: Astragalus Warning
- **SECTION:** Moderate Evidence - Astragalus

- **PROBLEM:** Third-person, passive
- **SUGGESTED DIRECTION:** "Important: don't take astragalus when you're already sick. It's a prevention herb, not a treatment herb. Traditional Chinese medicine is very clear on this - stop taking it at first symptoms and resume after you've recovered."

## 5. STAT NOTATION AUDIT

Statistical notation that needs plain-language translation:

1. "RR 0.68" → "about a third fewer infections"
2. "RR 0.75" → "25% fewer people getting sick"
3. "RR 0.60" → "40% reduction in repeat infections"
4. "OR 0.42" → "58% fewer colds"
5. "effect size (1.717)" → DELETE or say "substantial improvement"
6. "SMD -5.11" → "significant reduction"
7. "10+ point symptom improvement" → OK, but could be clearer

## 6. VOICE AUDIT

**'You/your' usage:** ~12 instances in body text (should be 15+)
- Most are in N=1 section
- Almost none in The Landscape or Core Protocol sections

**'Participants/patients/studies found' usage:** HIGH - appears throughout
- "A 2024 meta-analysis of 30 trials with 5,652 participants found..."
- "A 2017 systematic review of 33 RCTs with 7,175 participants found..."
- "Clinical trials show elderberry..."
- "A 2019 systematic review concluded..."

**'If you only do one thing' recommendation:** MISSING
- No clear single primary recommendation
- No "start here" framing
- Tables are helpful but lack prose interpretation

## 7. MISSING GUIDANCE

### Decision Points Without Clear Recommendations:

1. **Elderberry vs Andrographis for acute treatment**: Both listed as options without clear guidance on how to choose
   - NEEDED: "If you have to pick one, I'd go with elderberry because..."

2. **Kitchen sink vs single herb**: Not addressed
   - NEEDED: "Should you take multiple herbs or just one? Here's how to think about it..."

3. **What to do if you miss the 48-hour window**: Mentioned that elderberry is less effective, but no alternative recommendation
   - NEEDED: "If you're past 48 hours, here's what to try instead..."

4. **Prevention: Echinacea alone vs with astragalus**: Listed as "optional addition" but no clear guidance
   - NEEDED: "Is it worth adding astragalus? Here's when I would..."

5. **What symptoms each herb helps most**: Tables exist but lack narrative guidance
   - NEEDED: "If congestion is your main issue, focus on... If it's more fatigue and aches..."

### Missing Sections:

- No "If you only do one thing" framing
- No "If that doesn't work" decision tree
- No trade-off explanations (faster vs gentler, stronger evidence vs personal preference)
- Comparison tables lack prose interpretation

## SUMMARY OF REQUIRED CHANGES

1. **Rewrite Overview** with empathy opening ("If you're feeling...")
2. **Transform Landscape section** from literature review to decision support
3. **Add clear primary recommendation** ("If you only do one thing...")
4. **Convert all statistical notation** to plain language
5. **Add decision guidance** between options (elderberry vs andrographis)
6. **Reframe Core Protocol** from recipe cards to friendly guidance
7. **Add "If that doesn't work" alternatives**
8. **Increase you/your usage** throughout (target 15+)
9. **Remove or translate** study details (participants, effect sizes)
10. **Add prose interpretations** to all tables
