### [01-evidence-ranking.md](./01-evidence-ranking.md)
**Which herbs show the strongest evidence?**

Rankings by evidence level:
1. **Butterbur (75mg bid)** - 48% reduction, Level A evidence ⚠️ Safety concerns
2. **Ginger** - Acute treatment (RR=1.79 pain-free at 2h)
3. **Peppermint/Menthol (10% topical)** - 52% improvement rate
4. **Curcumin (500mg bid)** - CGRP reduction p<0.001
5. **Chuan Xiong** - #1 most-used herb in TCM (59.8% of formulas)
6. **Mag + Riboflavin + CoQ10** - Mitochondrial approach
7. **Feverfew** - Weak monotherapy (p=0.26), better in combinations
8. **Lavender** - Acute aromatherapy (VAS reduction 3.6)
9. **Tian Ma + Chuan Xiong** - Classical herb pair (4:1 ratio)

Use case rankings:
- **Acute attack:** Ginger, Peppermint, Lavender
- **Prevention:** Curcumin, Mag+B2+CoQ10, TCM formulas
- **Safety-first:** Ginger, Peppermint, Lavender, Magnesium combo

### [02-comparative-effectiveness.md](./02-comparative-effectiveness.md)
**How do herbs compare head-to-head and vs placebo?**

Direct comparisons:
- **Butterbur vs Placebo:** 48% vs 26% reduction (p=0.0012) - strongest effect

- **Feverfew vs Placebo:** -0.6 attacks/month (p=0.26, non-significant)

Network meta-analysis rankings:
- **Migraine frequency:** CoQ10 + L-carnitine
- **Attack duration:** Curcumin (#1)
- **Migraine severity:** Cinnamon

TCM clinical frequency (real-world use):
- **Most prescribed formula:** Chuan-Xiong-Cha-Tiao-San

Mechanism comparisons:
- **CGRP pathway:** Curcumin > Chuan Xiong > Butterbur > Feverfew
- **COX-2 inhibition:** Butterbur, Ginger (dual COX-2+LOX), Chuan Xiong
- **Neuroprotection:** Chuan Xiong + Tian Ma > others

### [04-traditional-combinations.md](./04-traditional-combinations.md)
**Why do combinations work better than single herbs?**

Classical TCM formulas:
1. **Chuan-Xiong-Cha-Tiao-San** (#1 prescribed)
   - Wind-dispelling layer (Bo He, Jing Jie, Fang Feng) → anti-inflammatory
   - Channel-guiding (Bai Zhi, Qiang Huo) → directs to head
   - Blood-moving core (Chuan Xiong) → CGRP, cerebral blood flow
   - **Synergy:** Multi-pathway (wind + blood + channel guidance)

2. **Da Chuanxiong Formula** (4:1 Chuan Xiong:Tian Ma)
   - Chuan Xiong (4 parts) → ligustilide: CGRP, COX-2, BBB protection
   - Tian Ma (1 part) → polysaccharides: neuroprotection, calms liver
   - **Why 4:1?** High Chuan Xiong for therapeutic effect; Tian Ma moderates/anchors

3. **Jia-Wei-Xiao-Yao-San** (#2 prescribed, stress-triggered)
   - Liver qi regulation (Chai Hu) + blood nourishment (Dang Gui, Bai Shao)
   - Heat clearing (Moutan, Gardenia) + spleen support (Bai Zhu, Fu Ling)
   - **Synergy:** Emotional regulation + inflammatory modulation

Western combinations:
- **Mag + Riboflavin + CoQ10:**
  - Riboflavin → Complex I/II function
  - CoQ10 → Electron carrier I/II → cytochrome C
  - Magnesium → ATP synthase cofactor + NMDA antagonism
  - **Synergy:** Entire mitochondrial pathway (upstream + downstream)

- **Curcumin + Omega-3** (theoretical):
  - Curcumin → COX-2/iNOS gene suppression
  - Omega-3 → Eicosanoid substrate shift (↓PGE2, ↑resolvins)
  - **Synergy:** Transcriptional + substrate-level anti-inflammatory

Why combinations outperform monotherapy:
1. **Multi-pathway targeting:** Migraine is multi-mechanism disease
2. **Synergistic enhancement:** 1 + 1 > 2 (Chuan Xiong blood-moving + Tian Ma neuroprotection)
3. **Balancing side effects:** TCM "emperor-minister-assistant-envoy" design
4. **Personalization:** Pattern diagnosis allows tailoring
5. **Temporal dynamics:** Acute + preventive components in same formula

Mechanism convergence:
- Multiple herbs hit CGRP (feverfew, butterbur, curcumin, chuan xiong) → validates importance
- Use herbs with DIFFERENT mechanisms (curcumin + ginger) for broader coverage
- OR use traditional formulas (validated combinations over centuries)

### [05-dosing-timelines.md](./05-dosing-timelines.md)
**What doses? How long until it works?**

Acute treatment dosing:
- **Ginger:** 500-2000mg at onset → 30min-2h onset, peak 2h
- **Peppermint 10% topical:** Apply to forehead/temples → immediate onset
- **Lavender:** 2-4 drops, 15-min inhalation → 5-15min onset

Preventive dosing:
- **Curcumin:** 500mg bid → 8 weeks minimum (CGRP reduction at 8 wks)
- **Magnesium:** 600mg daily → 3 months (tissue repletion slow)
- **Riboflavin:** 400mg daily → 3 months (mitochondrial incorporation)
- **CoQ10:** 150mg daily → 3 months (lipid-soluble, slow distribution)
- **Butterbur:** 75mg bid → 4 months (leukotriene pathway remodeling)
- **Feverfew:** 50-150mg daily → 8-12 weeks (weak, variable response)

TCM formulas:
- **CXCTS:** Decoction 2×/day or granules 2-3g 2-3×/day → days to weeks
- **Da Chuanxiong:** 4:1 ratio, decoction 2×/day → days to weeks
- **Practitioner-dosed:** Customized, reassess monthly

Why these timelines?
- **Acute (minutes-hours):** Direct receptor activation (TRPM8), rapid systemic absorption (ginger)
- **Preventive (weeks-months):** Gene expression changes (curcumin 8 wks), mitochondrial biogenesis (mag+B2+CoQ10 3 mos), inflammatory pathway remodeling (butterbur 4 mos)

Optimization:
- **Acute combo:** Peppermint (immediate) + Ginger (2h) + Lavender (15min) → staggered onset
- **Preventive staged:** Month 1-2 single agent → Month 3 add second if <50% response

Special populations:
- **Pregnancy:** Ginger safe, magnesium safe; butterbur/feverfew contraindicated
- **Children:** Riboflavin 200-400mg studied; magnesium safe (lower doses)
- **Renal impairment:** Avoid magnesium (accumulates)
- **Hepatic impairment:** Avoid butterbur (contraindicated)

### [06-clinical-integration.md](./06-clinical-integration.md)
**How to implement in practice?**

Treatment selection algorithm:
1. **Classify:** Episodic (<15 days/mo) vs Chronic (≥15 days/mo)
2. **Identify triggers:** Stress, hormonal, weather, food, sleep, hypertension
3. **Assess contraindications:** Liver disease, kidney disease, pregnancy, medications
4. **Check interactions:** Anticoagulants, antihypertensives, immunosuppressants

Practical protocols:

**Protocol 1: Acute Attack Kit**
- Ginger capsules + 10% menthol + lavender oil
- At onset: Ginger oral, peppermint topical, lavender inhalation, dark room
- Timeline: 0min (peppermint) → 15min (lavender) → 2h (ginger peak)

**Protocol 2: Prevention Stack (Frequent Episodic)**
- Month 1-2: Curcumin 500mg bid OR Mag+B2+CoQ10
- Month 3: Assess; if <50% add second agent
- Month 6: Reassess; if <50% consider TCM or medical preventive

**Protocol 3: Chronic Migraine**
- Daily: Curcumin 500mg bid + Mag 600mg + B2 400mg + CoQ10 150mg
- Optional (after 3 mos if insufficient): Add butterbur 75mg bid ⚠️
- Acute rescue: Limited to ≤10 days/month

**Protocol 4: TCM Pattern-Based**
- Consult licensed acupuncturist/TCM practitioner
- Pattern diagnosis → formula selection
- Monthly reassessment, formula adjustment

Integration with conventional medicine:
- **As monotherapy:** Mild-moderate episodic, patient preference
- **As adjunct:** Partial response to Rx preventive, reduce Rx dose
- **As bridge:** Start Rx (slow titration) + herbs (faster onset)
- **Transition:** Herbs → medical preventive if insufficient after 6 months

Monitoring:
- **Daily:** Migraine diary (frequency, severity, duration, triggers, treatments)
- **Monthly:** Summarize headache days, severity, acute med use
- **Quarterly:** % reduction, responder status (≥50%)
- **Adjust:** <50% at 8-12 weeks → add agent; ≥75% → continue

Safety:
- Third-party testing (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab)
- Standardization (curcumin ≥95%, feverfew 0.2-0.4% parthenolide, butterbur PA-free)
- TCM: Licensed practitioner, GMP-certified suppliers

Patient education:
- Timeline: "Acute = hours, preventive = months"
- Efficacy: "Goal 50% reduction, not 100% cure"
- Lifestyle: "Herbs augment sleep/stress management, don't replace it"
- Red flags: Thunderclap headache, fever+stiff neck, neurological symptoms → ER

## Key Insights

### Strongest Evidence
1. **Butterbur 75mg bid** historically strongest (48% reduction) but safety concerns limit use (PA-free only, LFT monitoring)
2. **Ginger** best-evidenced acute treatment (RR=1.79 pain-free at 2h)
3. **Curcumin** emerging as top preventive (direct CGRP targeting, p<0.001)
4. **Chuan Xiong** most-used TCM herb (59.8% of formulas) with mechanism validation

### Combination Rationale
- Migraine is multi-pathway disease → single herbs limited
- Traditional formulas target wind + blood + channels simultaneously
- Modern combos (Mag+B2+CoQ10) address entire metabolic pathway
- 1+1 > 2 (synergistic enhancement demonstrated)

### Safety First
- Safest: Ginger (GRAS), peppermint topical, lavender, Mag+B2+CoQ10
- Caution: Butterbur (hepatotoxicity), feverfew (pregnancy), ginger (anticoagulants)
- Always: Check contraindications, drug interactions, quality sourcing

### Timeline Expectations
- Acute: Minutes to hours (topical > aromatherapy > oral)
- Preventive: 2-4 months minimum (don't assess early, patience required)
- Pattern-based TCM: Variable (days for acute symptoms, weeks-months for root pattern)

### When to Escalate
- <50% reduction after 6-month herbal trial → medical preventive
- Chronic migraine (≥15 days/month) → aggressive multi-modal approach
- Red flag symptoms → emergency evaluation (not herbal treatment)
