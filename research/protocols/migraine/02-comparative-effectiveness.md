# Comparative Effectiveness: Head-to-Head Analysis

## Direct Comparisons

### Butterbur vs Placebo (Gold Standard RCT)
**Study:** Neurology 2004, n=245, 4-month trial

| Treatment | Migraine Reduction | P-value | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|----------------|
| Butterbur 75mg bid | 48% | 0.0012 | **Highly effective** |
| Butterbur 50mg bid | 36% | 0.127 | Non-significant trend |
| Placebo | 26% | — | Baseline |

**Key Finding:** Clear dose-response relationship; 75mg bid significantly superior

**Status:** AAN Level A recommendation (2004) → **Withdrawn 2015** (hepatotoxicity)

---

### Ginger vs Placebo (Meta-Analysis)
**Study:** Meta-analysis 2021, 3 RCTs

| Outcome | Ginger | Statistical Result | Interpretation |
|---------|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Pain-free at 2h | Higher | RR=1.79 [1.04-3.09], p=0.04 | 79% more likely to be pain-free |
| Pain score at 2h | Lower | MD=-1.27 [-1.46,-1.07], p<0.00001 | Highly significant reduction |

**Use Case:** Acute treatment (not prevention)

---

### Curcumin vs Placebo (Recent RCT)
**Study:** Double-blind RCT 2022, n=44, 8 weeks

| Biomarker/Outcome | P-value | Effect |
|-------------------|---------|--------|
| CGRP reduction | <0.001 | **Highly significant** |
| IL-6 reduction | 0.041 | Significant |
| Headache severity | 0.001 | **Highly significant** |
| Headache duration | 0.007 | **Highly significant** |

**Dose:** 500mg twice daily
**Mechanism:** Direct CGRP pathway modulation

---

### Magnesium + Riboflavin + CoQ10 vs Placebo
**Study:** Multicenter RCT 2015, n=130

| Outcome | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Migraine frequency | Trend toward significance (p not reaching 0.05) |
| Symptom burden | **Statistically significant reduction** vs placebo |

**Composition:** 400mg B2 + 600mg Mg + 150mg CoQ10 daily
**Duration:** 3 months minimum

---

### Peppermint/Menthol vs Placebo
**Study:** RCT 2010, topical 10% menthol

| Outcome | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Pain-free at 2h | Statistically superior to placebo |
| Pain relief at 2h | Statistically superior to placebo |
| Sustained effects | Maintained at follow-up |
| Associated symptoms | Effective for nausea, photophobia, phonophobia |
| Improvement rate | 52% (≥1 significant improvement at 2h) |

**Intranasal Comparison:**
- Peppermint 1.5% ≈ Lidocaine 4% (similar efficacy)

---

### Feverfew vs Placebo (Meta-Analysis)
**Study:** Meta-analysis 2020, 6 RCTs, n=561

| Metric | Result | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Reduction in attacks/month | -0.6 | Small, non-significant |
| 95% CI | [-0.52, 0.14] | Crosses zero |
| P-value | 0.26 | Not significant |
| Cohen's d | -0.19 | Small effect size |
| Evidence quality | Low (Cochrane) | Study heterogeneity |

**Conclusion:** Weak as monotherapy; better in combinations

---

### Lavender vs Placebo (Liquid Paraffin Control)
**Study:** RCT 2012, n=47

| Outcome | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Headache severity (VAS) | Mean reduction 3.6 ± 2.8 |
| Efficacy | Effective and safe |
| Application | 15-minute inhalation |

## Cross-Study Effectiveness Summary

### By Primary Outcome

#### **Migraine Frequency Reduction**
1. **Butterbur 75mg bid:** 48% reduction (strongest)
2. CoQ10 + L-carnitine: Top-ranked in network meta-analysis
3. Magnesium + B2 + CoQ10: Trend toward significance
4. Feverfew: Non-significant (-0.6 attacks/month)

#### **Acute Pain Relief (2-hour endpoint)**
1. **Ginger:** RR=1.79 for pain-free (79% increased likelihood)
2. **Peppermint 10% topical:** 52% improvement rate
3. Lavender aromatherapy: Mean VAS reduction 3.6

#### **CGRP Pathway Modulation** (Key Migraine Mediator)
1. **Curcumin:** p<0.001 (highly significant CGRP reduction)
2. Butterbur: Inhibits CGRP release from meningeal afferents
3. Chuan Xiong (ligustilide): High affinity for 5-HT receptors → reduces CGRP
4. Feverfew (parthenolide): Blocks CGRP release/vasodilatation

#### **Attack Duration Reduction**
1. **Curcumin:** Top-ranked in network meta-analysis
2. Curcumin RCT: p=0.007 for duration reduction

### Mechanism-Based Comparison

#### **Anti-Inflammatory Potency** (COX-2/Leukotriene Inhibition)
1. **Butterbur:** COX-2 + leukotriene biosynthesis inhibition (dual pathway)
2. **Ginger:** COX-2 + LOX inhibition (dual pathway, may reduce side effects)
3. Chuan Xiong: COX-2 inhibition + NF-κB down-regulation
4. Curcumin: COX-2 + iNOS suppression (especially with omega-3)

**Winner:** Butterbur (if PA-free) / Ginger (best safety-efficacy ratio)

---

#### **CGRP Pathway Targeting**
1. **Curcumin:** Direct CGRP reduction (p<0.001 in RCT)
2. Chuan Xiong: High 5-HT receptor affinity → regulates CGRP release
3. Butterbur: Inhibits CGRP from meningeal afferents
4. Feverfew: Blocks CGRP release and vasodilatation

**Winner:** Curcumin (quantified biomarker reduction in humans)

---

#### **Neuroprotection**
1. **Chuan Xiong (ligustilide):** Prevents apoptosis, regulates mitochondria, maintains BBB
2. Tian Ma (polysaccharides): Mobilizes neuroprotective genes, inhibits stress proteins
3. Magnesium: NMDA antagonist (regulates excitability)

**Winner:** Chuan Xiong + Tian Ma combination (Da Chuanxiong formula)

## Key Insights: Why Some Herbs Outperform Others

### Butterbur's Superiority (Pre-2015)
- **Multi-pathway:** Leukotrienes + COX-2 + CGRP + calcium channels
- **High potency:** 48% vs 26% placebo (nearly double)
- **But:** Safety killed clinical utility

### Ginger's Acute Advantage
- **Dual inhibition:** COX-2 + LOX (broader than single-pathway NSAIDs)
- **Rapid onset:** 2-hour efficacy
- **Safety:** GRAS status, no hepatotoxicity
- **Limitation:** Preventive evidence weaker than acute

### Curcumin's Emerging Role
- **Direct CGRP targeting:** Hits primary migraine mediator
- **Quantifiable:** Biomarker reductions (CGRP, IL-6)
- **Modern RCT:** 2022 study with rigorous design
- **Network meta-analysis:** Top-ranked for duration

### Chuan Xiong's Traditional Dominance
- **Clinical validation:** 59.8% of classical formulas (centuries of use)
- **Multi-target:** CGRP, neuroprotection, BBB, anti-inflammatory
- **Synergy:** Works in combinations (Da Chuanxiong, CXCTS)
- **Limitation:** Less RCT data in Western literature

### Feverfew's Puzzle
- **Theoretical promise:** TRPA1, CGRP, serotonin pathways
- **Clinical reality:** Meta-analysis non-significant (p=0.26)
- **Hypotheses:**
  - Dosing inconsistency (parthenolide content varies)
  - Better as adjuvant than monotherapy
  - Study quality issues (Cochrane: "low quality evidence")

## Bottom Line Comparisons

### If You Had to Choose ONE Herb...

**For Acute Attack:**
- **Ginger** (strongest evidence, safest, fastest)
- Alternative: Peppermint topical (if prefer non-oral)

**For Prevention:**
- **Curcumin 500mg bid** (best modern RCT data, CGRP targeting, safe)
- Alternative: Mag+B2+CoQ10 (mitochondrial approach, very safe)
- Butterbur historically strongest but safety risk too high

**For TCM Pattern-Based:**
- **Chuan Xiong** (alone or in formula)
- Most validated by centuries of clinical use + modern mechanism studies

**Safety-First Approach:**
- **Magnesium + Riboflavin + CoQ10** (excellent safety, moderate efficacy)
