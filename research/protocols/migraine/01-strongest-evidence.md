# Migraine: Herbs with Strongest Evidence

## Top-Tier Evidence (Meta-analyses + RCTs)

### 1. **Butterbur (Petasites hybridus)** - STRONGEST for Prevention
**Evidence Level**: Level A (with safety caveat)

**Key Findings**:
- **48% reduction** in migraine frequency at 75mg bid (p=0.0012 vs placebo)
- 50mg bid showed 36% reduction (non-significant, p=0.127)
- Placebo only 26% reduction
- American Headache Society Level A recommendation (highest level)

**Study**: RCT, 245 participants, 4 months duration (Neurology 2004)

**CRITICAL SAFETY CONCERN**:
- **Hepatotoxicity risk** from pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs)
- AAN stopped recommending in 2015 due to liver toxicity
- **Only use PA-free standardized extracts** with medical supervision
- Monitor liver function if using

**Mechanisms**:
- Inhibits leukotriene biosynthesis (blocks LTB4, cysteinyl-LTs)
- COX-2 inhibition → reduces PGE2
- Blocks calcium influx into neutrophils/eosinophils
- Inhibits CGRP release from meningeal afferents
- L-type voltage-dependent Ca2+ channel inhibition

**Clinical Reality**: Most effective herb for prevention but safety concerns severely limit use. Consider safer alternatives first.

---

### 2. **Ginger (Zingiber officinale)** - STRONGEST for Acute Treatment
**Evidence Level**: Strong (meta-analysis + RCTs)

**Key Findings**:
- **Pain-free at 2 hours**: RR 1.79 [1.04-3.09], p=0.04
- **Pain score reduction**: Mean difference -1.27 [-1.46, -1.07], **p<0.00001**
- **63% pain relief at 2h** when combined with feverfew (p=0.002)

**Study**: Meta-analysis of 3 RCTs (2021) + multiple head-to-head trials

**Mechanisms**:
- **Dual COX-2 and LOX inhibition** (advantage over NSAIDs that only block COX)
- Reduces prostaglandin production (PGE2)
- Suppresses inflammatory cytokines (IL-1β, TNF)
- Inhibits NF-κB activation
- Active compounds: 6-, 8-, 10-gingerols and 6-shogaol

**Dosing**: Based on RCT protocols, take at migraine onset
**Safety**: GRAS (Generally Recognized As Safe)

**Clinical Advantage**: Works for **acute treatment** (not just prevention), comparable to sumatriptan in some trials, extremely safe

---

### 3. **CoQ10 (Coenzyme Q10)** - STRONGEST for Prevention
**Evidence Level**: Strong (meta-analysis + RCTs)

**Key Findings**:
- **47.6% responder rate** (≥50% reduction) vs 14.4% placebo
- **NNT = 3** (number needed to treat - excellent efficacy)
- Frequency reduction: **-56.7%** vs -41.9% placebo (women's RCT)
- Severity reduction: **-46.6%** vs -30.1% placebo
- Duration reduction: **-60.0%** vs -37.8% placebo
- Superior to placebo in third treatment month

**Studies**:
- Meta-analysis 2020 (multiple RCTs)
- Women's RCT 2017 (84 participants)
- Network meta-analysis 2024: **Ranked #1 for migraine frequency** (CoQ10 + L-carnitine)

**Mechanisms**:
- Electron carrier in mitochondrial electron transport chain
- Transfers electrons from Complex I/II to cytochrome C
- Potent anti-inflammatory properties
- Reduces oxidative stress
- Improves mitochondrial energy metabolism (addresses core migraine pathology)

**Dosing**: 300-400mg daily
**Safety**: Excellent safety profile, well-tolerated

**Clinical Reality**: One of the safest AND most effective options for prevention. Should be first-line consideration.

---

### 4. **Peppermint/Menthol (Mentha × piperita)** - STRONGEST for Rapid Acute Relief
**Evidence Level**: Strong (multiple RCTs)

**Key Findings**:
- **10% topical menthol** superior to placebo for pain-free at 2h (p<0.001)
- **52%** experienced significant improvement at 2h
- **Equivalent to intranasal lidocaine 4%** in head-to-head trial
- Alleviates nausea/vomiting and photophobia/phonophobia

**Studies**:
- RCT 2010: Topical menthol vs placebo
- RCT 2019: Intranasal peppermint 1.5% vs lidocaine (comparable efficacy)

**Mechanisms**:
- **TRPM8 channel activation** (cold receptors)
- Generates long-lasting cooling effect
- Inhibits pain signal transmission
- Alters calcium channels
- GWAS revealed **TRPM8 genetic association** with migraine susceptibility

**Application**:
- Topical: 10% menthol solution to forehead/temples
- Intranasal: 1.5% peppermint essential oil
- Timing: At onset, rapid relief

**Safety**: Very safe, no significant adverse effects

**Clinical Advantage**: **Fastest onset** for acute treatment, non-oral route bypasses GI upset during migraine

---

### 5. **Curcumin/Turmeric (Curcuma longa)** - STRONG for Prevention
**Evidence Level**: Strong (multiple meta-analyses + RCTs)

**Key Findings - Standard Curcumin**:
- **Migraine severity**: Hedges's g = -0.75 [-1.44, -0.07], **p=0.03**
- CGRP reduction: **p<0.001** (RCT 2022)
- IL-6 reduction: p=0.041
- Headache severity: p=0.001
- Headache duration: p=0.007
- Network meta-analysis: **Ranked #1 for attack duration**

**Key Findings - Nano-Curcumin** (enhanced bioavailability):
- Migraine attacks: SMD **-0.77**
- Migraine severity: SMD **-0.92**
- Migraine duration: SMD **-0.63**
- **Combined with Omega-3 + CoQ10**: SMD **1.19 [0.90-1.48]** (synergistic enhancement)

**Studies**:
- Meta-analysis 2024 (standard curcumin)
- Scoping review/meta-analysis 2024 (nano-curcumin)
- RCT 2022 (44 participants, 8 weeks)

**Mechanisms**:
- **CGRP inhibition** (key migraine pathway)
- Anti-inflammatory (multiple pathways)
- Antioxidative
- Analgesic
- With omega-3: suppresses COX-2 and iNOS gene expression

**Dosing**:
- Standard: 500mg twice daily (1000mg/day total)
- Nano-curcumin: 80mg daily for 2 months (enhanced absorption)

**Safety**: Well-tolerated in clinical trials

**Clinical Reality**: Very effective for prevention, **nano-curcumin formulations show enhanced effects**. Works even better when combined with omega-3 and CoQ10.

---

### 6. **Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)** - STRONG for Both Acute + Prevention
**Evidence Level**: Strong (multiple RCTs)

**Key Findings - Acute Treatment**:
- **15 minutes inhalation** reduces headache severity by 3.6 ± 2.8 points (VAS)
- Effective and safe for acute management (RCT 2012, 47 participants)

**Key Findings - Prophylaxis**:
- **3-month aromatherapy** significantly reduced MIDAS score (p<0.05)
- No adverse effects reported
- RCT 2016 (double-blind, placebo-controlled)

**Mechanisms**:
- **Inhibits leukotriene biosynthesis** (anti-inflammatory cascade)
- GABA receptor modulation
- Linalool affects glutamatergic NMDA receptors
- Inhibits serotonin transporter (SERT)
- Antagonizes NMDA receptor
- Affects 5-HT-1A receptor in specific brain areas
- Anxiolytic/sedative properties (addresses stress/anxiety triggers)
- Modulates limbic system via olfactory pathways

**Application**:
- Acute: Inhalation for 15 minutes at onset
- Prophylaxis: Regular aromatherapy for 3 months

**Safety**: Very safe, no complaints or adverse effects in trials

**Clinical Advantage**: **Dual-use** (both acute and preventive), extremely safe, pleasant application method

---

### 7. **Gastrodin/Tianma (Gastrodia elata)** - STRONG in TCM Context
**Evidence Level**: Moderate-Strong (meta-analysis of TCM trials)

**Key Findings**:
- Meta-analysis 2022: "Favorable results for migraine treatment"
- 10 electronic databases searched
- Gastrodin-containing preparations effective

**Mechanisms**:
- **Inhibits CGRP expression** (key migraine pathway)
- **Crosses blood-brain barrier** (widely distributed in tissues)
- With Chuanxiong: Regulates MMP-9 to reduce BBB permeability
- Maintains BBB integrity in migraine rats
- Anticonvulsant, sedative, analgesic
- Neuroprotective, antioxidative, immunomodulatory

**Traditional Use**:
- Extensively combined with Chuanxiong, Angelica, Uncaria
- Rank #5 most common single herb in classical migraine literature
- Da Chuanxiong Formula: Chuan Xiong (4 parts) + Tian Ma (1 part)

**Active Compounds**:
- Gastrodin (phenolic glycoside)
- Polysaccharides (neuroprotective)
- β-sitosterol, chrysophanol, vanillin

**Clinical Reality**: Strong evidence **within TCM context**, especially when combined with Chuan Xiong. Limited head-to-head trials vs Western herbs.

## Comparative Rankings Summary

### **For Acute Treatment (Fastest → Slowest)**:
1. **Peppermint/Menthol 10% topical** - Rapid onset via TRPM8
2. **Ginger** - 2-hour pain relief (p<0.00001)
3. **Lavender inhalation** - 15 minutes
4. **Chamomile topical** - Acute relief

### **For Prevention (Strongest → Moderate)**:
1. **Butterbur 75mg bid** - 48% reduction (⚠️ safety concerns)
2. **CoQ10 400mg/day** - NNT=3, excellent safety
3. **Curcumin 1000mg/day** - Severity g=-0.75, p=0.03
4. **Nano-curcumin 80mg/day** - SMD -0.92 for severity
5. **Lavender aromatherapy** - 3-month prophylaxis
6. **Magnesium + Riboflavin + CoQ10 combo** - Mitochondrial support

### **Safety Profile (Safest → Caution)**:
1. **Ginger, Peppermint, Lavender, CoQ10, Magnesium** - Excellent safety
2. **Curcumin, Feverfew** - Well-tolerated
3. **Butterbur** - ⚠️ Hepatotoxicity risk, PA-free only, monitor liver

### **Network Meta-Analysis Rankings (2024)**:
- **Migraine frequency**: CoQ10 + L-carnitine #1
- **Migraine days**: Vitamin D3 #1
- **Attack duration**: Curcumin #1
- **Migraine severity**: Cinnamon #1

## Clinical Decision Framework

**Start with safest, most effective options**:
1. **CoQ10 400mg/day** for prevention (NNT=3, excellent safety)
2. **Ginger powder** for acute attacks (p<0.00001, GRAS)
3. **Peppermint 10% topical** for rapid acute relief
4. **Curcumin 1000mg/day** for prevention (or nano-curcumin 80mg)

**Consider combinations** (evidence shows synergy):
- Nano-curcumin + Omega-3 + CoQ10 (SMD 1.19 [0.90-1.48])
- Magnesium 600mg + Riboflavin 400mg + CoQ10 150mg (mitochondrial support)
- Feverfew + Ginger sublingual (63% vs 39%, p=0.002)

**Reserve for refractory cases** (with caution):
- Butterbur 75mg bid (most effective BUT hepatotoxicity risk)
- Only use PA-free extracts
- Monitor liver function
- Medical supervision required

**Dual-action options** (acute + preventive):
- Lavender (inhalation acute, aromatherapy prevention)
- Consider rotating acute treatments to prevent tolerance

## Expected Timelines

**Acute Treatment** (symptom relief):
- Peppermint topical: **Minutes** (rapid onset)
- Lavender inhalation: **15 minutes**
- Ginger: **2 hours** for pain-free state

**Preventive Treatment** (reduction in frequency/severity):
- CoQ10: Effective by **third month**
- Curcumin: **8 weeks** for preventive effect
- Butterbur: Minimum **4 months** for full effect
- Magnesium + Riboflavin + CoQ10: **3 months**
- Lavender prophylaxis: **3 months**

**Key Principle**: Prevention requires **consistent daily use for 2-4 months** before judging efficacy. Acute treatments work within **minutes to hours**.
