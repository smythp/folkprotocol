### 5. dosing-timelines.md
**Dosing for acute vs chronic, expected timelines**

Evidence-based dosing:
- Berberine: 1200mg daily (divided)
- Fenugreek: 5-10g seed or 500-1000mg extract
- Curcumin: 500-1500mg with piperine (essential)
- Cinnamon: 600mg-6g daily
- Jinlida: Traditional formula dosing

Acute vs Chronic strategies:
- **Acute** (postprandial): α-glucosidase inhibitors before meals
- **Chronic** (HbA1c): Consistent daily dosing for 12+ weeks

Expected timelines:
- **Short-term** (days-2 weeks): Postprandial effects, early fasting glucose changes
- **Medium-term** (2-6 weeks): Fasting glucose plateau, insulin sensitivity improvements
- **Long-term** (8-16 weeks): HbA1c reduction (primary endpoint), lipid changes
- **Very long-term** (years): Prevention benefits (Jinlida 41% over ~3.5 years)

Timing throughout day:
- With meals: Berberine, Curcumin, Fenugreek, Milk Thistle
- Before meals: α-glucosidase inhibitors, ACV
- Divided doses preferred for short half-life herbs

### 6. mechanisms-of-action.md
**How herbs work: molecular targets and pathways**

Six primary mechanisms:
1. **Alpha-glucosidase inhibition** (Cinnamon, Mulberry, Fenugreek)
2. **Enhanced insulin secretion/sensitivity** (Cinnamon, Mulberry, Ginseng)
3. **Glucose uptake enhancement** (GLUT4, PI3K-Akt pathway)
4. **Inhibition of hepatic glucose production** (Berberine: AMPK activation)
5. **Anti-inflammatory effects** (Curcumin, ALA: reduce TNF-α, IL-6, CRP)
6. **Antioxidant activity** (Curcumin, ALA, Milk Thistle: reduce oxidative stress)

Additional mechanism:
7. **PPARγ agonism** (Milk Thistle: insulin sensitizer)

Multi-target herbs:
- **Berberine**: 4-5 mechanisms (AMPK, insulin receptors, GLUT4, anti-inflammatory, microbiota)
- **Curcumin**: 4 mechanisms (anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, insulin sensitizing, lipid modulation)
- **Cinnamon**: 4 mechanisms (α-glucosidase, insulin receptors, GLUT4, glycogen synthase)

Why combinations work:
- Non-overlapping mechanisms
- Multi-organ targeting (liver, muscle, intestine, pancreas)
- Synergistic bioavailability

## Key Research Findings

### Strongest Individual Herbs

**Fasting Glucose:**
1. Fenugreek: -20.32 mg/dL
2. Berberine: -14.8 mg/dL
3. Curcumin: -6 to -11 mg/dL

**HbA1c:**
1. Berberine: -0.63%
2. Fenugreek: -0.54%
3. Curcumin: -0.31 to -0.54%

**Postprandial:**
1. Berberine: -20.9 mg/dL

**Insulin Resistance:**
1. Milk Thistle: Significant HOMA-IR reduction
2. American Ginseng: Significant HOMA-IR reduction

### Traditional Formulas

**Jinlida Granules:**
- Prevention: 41% lower diabetes risk (FOCUS trial)
- Add-on to metformin: -0.92% HbA1c vs -0.53% placebo
- Glycemic variability: Only intervention showing significant reduction

### Proven Combinations

**Berberine + Cinnamon (RCT):**
- 12 weeks, 1200mg + 600mg daily
- Significant reductions: FBG (P=0.031), HbA1c (P=0.013), LDL-C (P=0.039)
- Synergistic effects confirmed

## Research Limitations

1. Most studies are 12-16 weeks (long-term data limited except Jinlida)
2. Head-to-head trials rare (mostly placebo-controlled)
3. Extract standardization varies across studies
4. Publication bias toward positive results likely
5. Optimal dosing not established for many herbs

## Future Directions

Research gaps identified:
- More head-to-head comparative trials
- Higher quality RCTs for polyherbal formulations
- Standardization of extracts and dosing
- Long-term safety studies (>1 year)
- Bioavailability enhancement strategies
- Molecular mechanism validation
