# PMS Protocol: Symptom-Specific Treatments

## Psychological Symptoms

### Depression / Low Mood

**Most Effective:**
1. **Saffron (Crocus sativus)**
   - Evidence: As effective as fluoxetine for PMDD
   - Dose: 30 mg/day (15 mg twice daily)
   - Duration: Two menstrual cycles minimum
   - Mechanism: Inhibits reuptake of dopamine, norepinephrine, serotonin
   - Statistical significance: P < 0.001 vs placebo

2. **Vitex agnus-castus**
   - Evidence: Helps with psychological symptoms (complementary to fluoxetine)
   - Best for: Overall mood improvement as part of full PMS symptom reduction
   - Mechanism: Dopaminergic; reduces prolactin, increases progesterone

**Supporting Herbs:**
- **Lemon balm**: Depression SMD -0.47 (95% CI: -0.73 to -0.21; p = 0.0005)
- **Bupleurum formulas** (Chaihu Shugan Powder): Especially for PMS-related depression

**Avoid:**
- St. John's wort (ineffective for depressive symptoms in PMS RCT)

### Anxiety

**Most Effective:**
1. **Lemon balm (Melissa officinalis)**
   - Evidence: Anxiety SMD -0.98 (95% CI: -1.63 to -0.33; p = 0.003)
   - Dose: 1200 mg daily
   - Timing: First to last day of menstrual cycle
   - Duration: Three consecutive cycles
   - Mechanism: GABAergic (increases GABA concentration)

2. **Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata)**
   - Evidence: Equal to oxazepam (benzodiazepine) for generalized anxiety
   - Mechanism: GABAergic pathways (binds to GABAA receptors)
   - Note: General anxiety studies; specific PMS trials lacking

3. **Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)**
   - Evidence: Significant reduction in generalized anxiety with long-term treatment
   - Additional benefit: Also addresses physical symptoms
   - Mechanism: Modulates dopamine and serotonin actions

**Supporting:**
- Saffron (reduced anxiety scores in both follicular and luteal phases)

### Irritability / Mood Swings

**Most Effective:**
1. **Vitex agnus-castus**
   - Evidence: Efficacy specifically found for irritability and mood swings
   - Broad symptom coverage

2. **Saffron**
   - Evidence: Effective for PMDD emotional symptoms

### Insomnia / Sleep Disturbances

**Most Effective:**
1. **Valerian (Valeriana officinalis)**
   - Evidence: Improves sleep quality, decreases sleep onset time, avoids frequent waking
   - Dose: 2 pills daily (300-600 mg range)
   - Timing: Last seven days of menstrual cycle
   - Duration: 3 cycles
   - Mechanism: Inhibits GABA catabolizing enzymes, increases GABA in brain

2. **Lemon balm**
   - Evidence: Insomnia reduction 42% (p < 0.01) in adolescent study
   - Additional benefits: Also addresses anxiety and depression

## Physical Symptoms

### Breast Pain / Mastalgia / Breast Tenderness

**Most Effective:**
1. **Vitex agnus-castus**
   - Evidence: Dopaminergic compounds specifically improve premenstrual mastodynia
   - Mechanism: Reduces prolactin (elevated prolactin associated with breast pain)
   - Best established evidence for this symptom

2. **Ginkgo biloba (EGb 761)**
   - Evidence: Statistical significance vs placebo for breast symptoms (n=165)
   - Dose: 40 mg three times daily (120 mg total)
   - Timing: From 16th day of cycle to 5th day of next cycle
   - Effectiveness: Particularly for breast pain/tenderness

**Supporting:**
- White Peony (in combinations): Reduces prolactin 21-28%

### Menstrual Pain / Dysmenorrhea

**Most Effective:**
1. **Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare)**
   - Evidence: Meta-analysis of 12 RCTs; similar effect to conventional drugs
   - Effect size: SMD -0.632 (CI: -0.827 to -0.436; p<0.001) vs placebo
   - Vs conventional drugs: SMD 0.07 (95% CI: -0.08 to 0.21) - non-inferior
   - Dose: 30 mg extract every 4 hours
   - Timing: 3 days before menstruation until 5th day
   - Duration: 3 months
   - Additional benefits: Also helps lethargy and depression (P < 0.05)

2. **Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)**
   - Evidence: Systematic review of 7 RCTs; MORE effective than placebo AND NSAIDs
   - Unique position: Outperforms standard medical treatment
   - Mechanism: Interrupts cyclooxygenase and lipoxygenase, stops prostaglandin production
   - Additional benefits: Antispasmodic, relieves painful cramps

**Supporting:**
- **Curcumin**: Analgesic properties, anti-inflammatory
- **Ginger**: Traditionally for pelvic circulation (in systematic review list)

### Congestive Symptoms (Bloating, Water Retention, Swelling)

**Most Effective:**
1. **Ginkgo biloba (EGb 761)**
   - Evidence: Statistical significance vs placebo (p < 0.001)
   - Particularly effective for congestive symptoms
   - Dose: 40 mg three times daily
   - Timing: From 16th day of cycle to 5th day of next cycle

**Supporting:**
- **Calcium 1200 mg/day**: Significant reductions in water retention (nutrient, not herb)

## Behavioral Symptoms

### General Behavioral Symptoms

**Most Effective:**
1. **St. John's wort (Hypericum perforatum)**
   - Evidence: Improved behavioral symptoms vs placebo (statistically superior)
   - Dose: 900 mg/day standardized extract
   - Duration: Two menstrual cycles
   - Note: Effective for behavioral but NOT for mood symptoms

2. **Valerian**
   - Evidence: Significant improvement P < 0.001 for behavioral symptoms
   - Broad coverage: Also effective for emotional and physical

## Multi-Domain Symptoms (Physical + Psychological)

### Overall PMS (Mixed Symptom Profile)

**Most Effective for Multi-Domain:**
1. **Vitex agnus-castus**
   - Evidence: Most robust for overall PMS (13 of 14 studies positive)
   - 2.57x more likely to achieve remission vs placebo
   - Addresses: Physical, psychological, hormonal symptoms

2. **Chamomile**
   - Evidence: Alleviated general physical AND psychological PMS symptoms
   - Superior to mefenamic acid for psychological/behavioral
   - Equal to mefenamic acid for physical

3. **Curcumin**
   - Evidence: 6 of 10 RCTs positive
   - Significant reductions in mood, behavioral, AND physical symptoms
   - Dose: 500 mg curcuminoid + piperine daily
   - Timing: 7 days before until 3 days after menstruation
   - Duration: Three consecutive cycles

4. **Valerian**
   - Evidence: P < 0.001 for emotional, physical, AND behavioral symptoms
   - Reduced all symptom categories in double-blind trial

5. **JWXYS (Jia-Wei-Xiao-Yao-San) formula**
   - Evidence: p < 0.01 for both physical and psychological symptoms
   - Most prescribed TCM formula for PMS (37.5% of prescriptions)

## Traditional Chinese Medicine Pattern-Based Selection

### Liver Qi Stagnation Pattern

**Symptoms:**
- Rib or side pain
- Emotional depression
- Frequent sighing
- Irregular periods
- Breast tenderness
- Irritability

**Primary Herbs/Formulas:**
1. **Bupleurum (Chai Hu)** - "King Herb for relieving Liver Qi Stagnation"
2. **Jia-Wei-Xiao-Yao-San (JWXYS)**
3. **Chaihu Shugan Powder**

**Principle:** Soothe liver, promote smooth flow of Qi

### Liver-Spleen Disharmony Pattern

**Symptoms:**
- Fatigue
- Digestive issues
- Emotional symptoms
- Blood deficiency signs

**Primary Formula:**
- **Xiao Yao San** (base formula, variations include JWXYS)

**Functions:**
- Soothe liver
- Relieve depression
- Nourish blood
- Strengthen spleen

## Hormonal Imbalance Patterns

### High Prolactin / Low Progesterone

**Most Effective:**
1. **Vitex agnus-castus**
   - Mechanism: Reduces prolactin, increases progesterone
   - Reverses LH suppression, develops corpus luteum

2. **White Peony (Paeonia lactiflora)**
   - Evidence: Reduces prolactin 21-28% from baseline
   - Mechanism: Increases progesterone, lowers prolactin
   - Often combined with licorice for synergy

### PCOS-Related PMS

**Most Effective:**
- **White Peony + Licorice combination**
- Evidence: Normalized menstrual cycles, improved hormonal balance
- Also: Improved ovulation induction

## Symptom-Specific Selection Strategy

**Step 1: Identify Primary Symptom Domain**
- Psychological (depression, anxiety, mood)
- Physical (pain, breast tenderness, congestive)
- Behavioral
- Mixed

**Step 2: Select Evidence-Based Herb for That Domain**
- Use tables above for strongest evidence in each category

**Step 3: Consider Secondary Benefits**
- Many herbs have multi-domain effects
- Example: Chamomile addresses both pain AND psychological symptoms

**Step 4: Assess for TCM Pattern Match (Optional)**
- If symptoms fit Liver Qi Stagnation: Consider JWXYS or Bupleurum formulas
- If hormonal imbalance suspected: Consider Vitex or White Peony

**Step 5: Monitor and Adjust**
- Track symptom changes by domain
- If insufficient response in one domain, add symptom-specific herb
- Minimum 3 cycles before changing strategy
