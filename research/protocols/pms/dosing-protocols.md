# PMS Protocol: Dosing for Acute vs Chronic PMS

## General Timing Principles

### When to Start Taking Herbs

**Luteal Phase Dosing (Most Common):**
- Start: 7-14 days before expected menstruation
- Continue: Through first 1-3 days of period
- Rationale: Targets symptom window when PMS occurs

**Specific Timing Approaches:**

1. **Mid-cycle approach:**
   - Start: 10-14 days from mid-cycle
   - Continue: Until a day or two after period begins
   - Best for: Symptoms that start predictably mid-luteal phase

2. **Week-before approach:**
   - Start: 7 days before expected menstruation
   - Continue: Through menstruation or until symptoms resolve
   - Best for: Symptoms that appear closer to menstruation

3. **Full-cycle approach:**
   - Dosing: Entire menstrual cycle (first to last day)
   - Best for: Severe symptoms, hormonal regulation focus
   - Examples: Lemon balm studies used this approach

### Acute vs Chronic Dosing Strategy

**Acute PMS (Episodic, Mild-Moderate):**
- Duration: 1-3 cycles of symptoms
- Strategy: Luteal phase dosing only
- Goal: Symptom relief during active PMS window
- Suitable herbs: Most herbs can be used this way

**Chronic PMS (Recurrent, Moderate-Severe):**
- Duration: Multiple cycles, established pattern
- Strategy: Either luteal phase dosing OR full-cycle dosing
- Goal: Hormonal regulation and pattern resolution
- Duration: Minimum 3 months, ideally 3-6 months
- Suitable herbs: Vitex, White Peony, JWXYS formula

## Herb-Specific Dosing Protocols

### Vitex agnus-castus (Chasteberry)

**Standard Dosing:**
- Form: Standardized extract (dopaminergic compounds)
- Timing: Typically daily, full-cycle OR luteal phase only
- Note: Specific dosing not provided in search results, but most clinical trials used standardized extracts

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Luteal phase dosing may provide symptom relief
- **Chronic**: Full-cycle daily dosing preferred for hormonal regulation
- **Duration**: Minimum 3 cycles; clinical trials used 2+ months

**Mechanism Consideration:**
- Works on prolactin regulation and progesterone production
- Effects accumulate over time
- Best results with consistent daily dosing

---

### Saffron (Crocus sativus)

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 30 mg/day (15 mg twice daily)
- **Timing**: Not specified if luteal-only or full-cycle
- **Duration**: Minimum two menstrual cycles
- **Frequency**: Twice daily (morning and evening)

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Likely effective for acute PMDD symptoms
- **Chronic**: Clinical trials used 2+ cycles, suggesting chronic dosing
- **Duration**: Start with 2 cycles, continue if effective

**Evidence:**
- P < 0.001 vs placebo after 2 cycles
- As effective as fluoxetine for PMDD

---

### Ginkgo biloba (EGb 761)

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 40 mg leaf extract, three times daily (120 mg total)
- **Timing**: From 16th day of menstrual cycle to 5th day of next cycle
- **Frequency**: Three times daily
- **Duration**: Studies used multiple cycles

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Luteal phase dosing (day 16 to day 5) specifically studied
- **Chronic**: Repeat luteal dosing each cycle for recurrent symptoms
- **Duration**: Effects seen within 1 cycle for congestive symptoms

**Best For:**
- Breast pain/tenderness
- Congestive symptoms
- Symptom-specific rather than hormonal regulation

---

### Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare)

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 30 mg fennel extract capsules
- **Frequency**: Every 4 hours as needed
- **Timing**: 3 days before menstruation until 5th day of period
- **Duration**: 3 months

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Can be used for dysmenorrhea as needed (every 4 hours)
- **Chronic**: 3-month protocols showed enhanced benefits over time
- **Flexibility**: "As needed" dosing possible for pain

**Best For:**
- Menstrual pain (dysmenorrhea)
- Pelvic pain
- Lethargy and depression associated with menstruation

---

### Curcumin

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 500 mg curcuminoid + piperine, daily
- **Timing**: From 7 days before menstruation until 3 days after menstruation
- **Duration**: Three consecutive menstrual cycles
- **Form**: Curcuminoid extract with piperine (enhances absorption)

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Luteal phase dosing (7 days before to 3 days after)
- **Chronic**: Repeat for 3+ cycles for full benefit
- **Duration**: Clinical trials used 3 cycles minimum

**Mechanism Consideration:**
- Anti-inflammatory effects accumulate
- Neuroprotective effects develop over time
- Best results with consistent luteal dosing over multiple cycles

---

### Lemon balm (Melissa officinalis)

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 1200 mg daily
- **Timing**: First to last day of menstrual cycle (full-cycle dosing)
- **Duration**: Three consecutive cycles
- **Frequency**: Typically divided into 2-3 doses per day

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Full-cycle approach means no "acute" option in clinical trials
- **Chronic**: Designed for chronic PMS with 3-month protocol
- **Duration**: Minimum 3 cycles; benefits increased in months 2-3

**Evidence:**
- Month 1: -55.5 point reduction
- Month 2: -57.3 point reduction
- Cumulative benefits suggest chronic dosing optimal

---

### Valerian (Valeriana officinalis)

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 2 pills daily (300-600 mg range typical)
- **Timing**: Last seven days of menstrual cycle
- **Duration**: 3 cycles
- **Frequency**: Daily during luteal phase

**Acute Cramping Protocol:**
- **Dose**: 1/2 teaspoon tincture
- **Frequency**: Every 2-3 hours as needed
- **Duration**: Until cramping subsides
- **Form**: Tincture for acute use

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Tincture every 2-3 hours for acute cramping episodes
- **Chronic**: Daily pills last 7 days of cycle for 3+ cycles
- **Flexibility**: Can use both strategies (daily + acute as needed)

**Best For:**
- Chronic: Emotional, behavioral, physical symptoms (all domains)
- Acute: Sleep disturbances, acute cramping

---

### Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

**General Dosing:**
- **Tea**: 1 teaspoon chopped herbs per cup boiling water
- **Steep**: 20-30 minutes
- **Frequency**: 1 cup 2-3 times daily before meals
- **Timing**: Studies varied; likely effective both luteal-only and full-cycle

**Tincture Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 1 teaspoon per cup water, 2-3 times daily
- **Alternative**: Approximately 30 drops 2-3 times per day

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Can be used as needed for pain episodes (tea or tincture)
- **Chronic**: Daily dosing during luteal phase for 2-3+ cycles
- **Flexibility**: Gentle herb, can use both strategies

**Evidence:**
- Systematic review of 8 RCTs showed effectiveness
- More effective than NSAIDs for dysmenorrhea

---

### St. John's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

**Clinical Trial Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 900 mg/day
- **Standardization**: 0.18% hypericin, 3.38% hyperforin
- **Timing**: Not specified, likely full-cycle
- **Duration**: Two menstrual cycles minimum

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Chronic**: Daily dosing required (antidepressant-like mechanism)
- **Duration**: 2+ cycles minimum for effect
- **Note**: NOT effective for mood symptoms in PMS (only physical/behavioral)

**Limitation:**
- SSRIs preferred for PMDD
- Use only if psychological symptoms not primary concern

## Traditional Formula Dosing

### Jia-Wei-Xiao-Yao-San (JWXYS)

**Forms Available:**
- Decoction (tea): Traditional preparation
- Pills/tablets: Concentrated extract
- Granules: Instant tea preparation

**General Dosing:**
- **Timing**: Can be used full-cycle OR luteal phase only
- **Frequency**: 2-3 times daily (varies by form)
- **Duration**: Minimum 3 cycles, typically 3-6 months

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Acute**: Less suitable; works on pattern resolution over time
- **Chronic**: Ideal for chronic PMS with liver Qi stagnation pattern
- **Duration**: 3-6 months for pattern resolution

**TCM Approach:**
- Practitioner may adjust dosing based on response
- May modify herbs in formula over time
- Full-cycle dosing more common for hormonal regulation

---

### Chaihu Shugan Powder (CSP)

**Dosing:**
- Similar to JWXYS
- Available as decoction, pills, granules
- Timing and duration: Follow JWXYS guidelines

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Chronic**: Best for recurrent PMS-related depression
- **Duration**: 3-6 months typical

## Nutrient Dosing (For Comparison)

### Calcium

**Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 1200 mg daily
- **Timing**: Daily, full-cycle
- **Duration**: At least three cycles to be beneficial
- **Evidence**: Large, rigorous, double-blind trial

**Acute vs Chronic:**
- **Chronic only**: Requires 3+ cycles for benefit
- Not suitable for acute/episodic use

---

### Magnesium

**Dosing:**
- **Dose**: 250 mg daily
- **Timing**: Full-cycle or luteal phase
- **Evidence**: Mixed results, 2 positive and 2 nonpositive studies

**Combination:**
- **Magnesium + Vitamin B6**: Highest effectiveness
- Better than either alone

---

### Vitamin B6

**Dosing:**
- **Evidence**: Conflicting results (5 positive, 5 nonpositive)
- **Combination**: Calcium + B6 showed enhanced benefits

## Treatment Duration Guidelines

### Minimum Duration by Condition Severity

**Mild PMS (Minimal Impact on Function):**
- **Minimum**: 1 menstrual cycle
- **Recommended**: 2-3 cycles to assess full effect
- **Strategy**: Luteal phase dosing

**Moderate PMS (Noticeable Impact on Function):**
- **Minimum**: 3 menstrual cycles
- **Recommended**: 3-6 cycles
- **Strategy**: Luteal phase or full-cycle dosing

**Severe PMS / PMDD (Significant Functional Impairment):**
- **Minimum**: 3 months (3 cycles)
- **Recommended**: 6-12 months for lasting change
- **Strategy**: Full-cycle daily dosing for hormonal herbs (Vitex, White Peony)
- **Strategy**: Consistent luteal dosing for neurotransmitter herbs (Saffron, Lemon balm)

### Clinical Study Ranges
- **Range**: 1 menstrual cycle to 12 months
- **Most common**: 2-3 cycles
- **For lasting change**: Ideally 3 months minimum

- 1/2 to 1 teaspoon tincture before bed

## Chronic PMS Management Protocols

### Hormonal Regulation Strategy (Full-Cycle Dosing)

**For hormonal imbalances, irregular cycles, high prolactin:**
- **Vitex**: Daily, full-cycle, 3-6 months
- **White Peony (+ Licorice)**: Daily, full-cycle, 3-6 months
- **JWXYS formula**: Daily, full-cycle, 3-6 months

**Rationale:**
- Hormonal effects accumulate over time
- Regulates menstrual cycle overall, not just symptoms
- Best for root cause resolution

### Symptom Suppression Strategy (Luteal Phase Dosing)

**For predictable luteal phase symptoms:**
- **Saffron**: 30 mg/day, 7-14 days before period, 2+ cycles
- **Curcumin**: 500 mg/day, 7 days before to 3 days after, 3+ cycles
- **Ginkgo**: 120 mg/day, day 16 to day 5 of next cycle
- **Fennel**: 30 mg every 4 hours, 3 days before to day 5

**Rationale:**
- Targets symptom window
- Reduces cost and herb exposure
- Effective for symptom relief without full hormonal regulation

### Mixed Strategy

**Combine hormonal regulation + symptom targeting:**
- **Vitex**: Daily, full-cycle (hormonal base)
- **+ Ginkgo**: Luteal phase only (for breast pain)
- **+ Chamomile**: As needed (for acute pain)

**Rationale:**
- Addresses root cause with Vitex
- Adds symptom-specific support as needed
- Flexible and individualized

## Practical Dosing Decision Tree

**Step 1: Assess PMS Pattern**
- Acute/episodic (1-3 cycles) → Luteal phase dosing
- Chronic/recurrent (4+ cycles) → Consider full-cycle dosing for hormonal herbs

**Step 2: Identify Primary Goal**
- Symptom relief → Luteal phase dosing
- Hormonal regulation → Full-cycle dosing

**Step 3: Select Herbs Based on Symptoms**
- Use symptom-specific treatment guide
- Match dosing protocol to selected herb

**Step 4: Determine Duration**
- Mild: 2-3 cycles
- Moderate: 3-6 cycles
- Severe: 6-12 cycles

**Step 5: Add Acute Protocols as Needed**
- Even with chronic dosing, can add acute protocols for breakthrough symptoms
- Example: Daily Vitex + acute Valerian for cramping

---

## Monitoring and Adjustment

**Track Daily:**
- Symptom severity (scale 1-10)
- Timing of symptoms relative to cycle
- Sleep quality
- Mood changes
- Physical symptoms (pain, breast tenderness, bloating)

**Assess at 3 Cycles:**
- ≥50% improvement → Continue current protocol
- 25-49% improvement → Extend to 6 cycles or add second herb
- <25% improvement → Reassess herb selection and pattern

**Long-term Management:**
- After 6-12 cycles of improvement, can trial reducing dose or frequency
- Some women continue low-dose or luteal-only dosing long-term
- Others can stop after pattern resolution and resume if symptoms return
