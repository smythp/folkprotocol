# PMS Protocol: Evidence Hierarchy

## Which Herbs Show Strongest Evidence

### Tier 1: Highest Evidence (Multiple High-Quality RCTs)

**Vitex agnus-castus (Chasteberry)**
- **Evidence**: 4 trials with ~500 women; meta-analysis of 14 double-blind RCTs
- **Effectiveness**: 2.57x more likely to achieve symptom remission vs placebo (95% CI 1.52-4.35)
- **Positive results**: 13 of 14 studies reported positive effects on total premenstrual symptoms
- **Best for**: Overall PMS symptoms, especially physical symptoms and breast pain
- **Mechanism**: Dopaminergic compounds that reduce prolactin and increase progesterone

**Saffron (Crocus sativus)**
- **Evidence**: Multiple RCTs including head-to-head comparison with fluoxetine
- **Effectiveness**: As effective as fluoxetine (SSRI) for PMDD treatment
- **Significance**: P < 0.001 compared to placebo in student study (n=78)
- **Best for**: Psychological symptoms, depression, PMDD
- **Mechanism**: Inhibits reuptake of dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin

### Tier 2: Good Evidence (Positive RCTs)

**Curcumin**
- **Evidence**: 10 RCTs evaluated, 6 showed significant benefits
- **Effectiveness**: Significant reductions in mood, behavioral, and physical symptoms
- **Best for**: Overall PMS symptoms, particularly when inflammation is a factor
- **Mechanism**: Anti-inflammatory, modulates neurotransmitters, antioxidant properties

**Ginkgo biloba (EGb 761)**
- **Evidence**: Multiple RCTs including 165-person double-blind study
- **Effectiveness**: Statistical significance vs placebo for breast symptoms
- **Best for**: Breast pain/tenderness, congestive symptoms
- **Mechanism**: Improves circulation, reduces inflammation

**Lemon balm (Melissa officinalis)**
- **Evidence**: RCTs with 200 adolescents and other populations
- **Effectiveness**:
  - Anxiety reduction: SMD -0.98 (95% CI: -1.63 to -0.33; p = 0.003)
  - Depression reduction: SMD -0.47 (95% CI: -0.73 to -0.21; p = 0.0005)
  - Insomnia reduction: 42% (p < 0.01)
- **Best for**: Anxiety, depression, insomnia, behavioral symptoms
- **Mechanism**: GABAergic (increases GABA concentration)

### Tier 3: Promising Evidence (Positive Studies, Need More Research)

**Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare)**
- **Evidence**: Meta-analysis of 12 RCTs for dysmenorrhea
- **Effectiveness**: Similar effect to conventional drugs (SMD: 0.07, 95% CI: -0.08 to 0.21)
- **Best for**: Menstrual pain, pelvic pain, lethargy, depression
- **Statistical significance**: SMD -0.632 (CI: -0.827 to -0.436; p<0.001) vs placebo

**Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)**
- **Evidence**: Systematic review of 8 RCTs for PMS, 7 for dysmenorrhea
- **Effectiveness**: More effective than placebo AND NSAIDs for menstrual pain
- **Best for**: Menstrual pain, general physical and psychological symptoms
- **Unique finding**: More effective than mefenamic acid for psychological/behavioral symptoms

**Valerian (Valeriana officinalis)**
- **Evidence**: Double-blind trial with 100 female students
- **Effectiveness**: P < 0.001 for emotional, behavioral, and physical symptoms
- **Best for**: All symptom categories (emotional, behavioral, physical)
- **Mechanism**: GABAergic pathways, improves sleep quality

**White Peony (Paeonia lactiflora)**
- **Evidence**: RCTs for hormonal balance, especially in PCOS
- **Effectiveness**: Reduces prolactin 21-28% from baseline at 4 and 8 weeks
- **Best for**: Hormonal imbalances, high prolactin, PCOS-related symptoms
- **Mechanism**: Supports aromatase, increases progesterone, lowers prolactin

**Bupleurum (Chai Hu)**
- **Evidence**: Key component of most effective TCM formulas (JWXYS, CSP)
- **Best for**: Liver Qi stagnation pattern, PMS-related depression
- **Traditional status**: "King Herb for relieving Liver Qi Stagnation"

### Tier 4: Limited or Mixed Evidence

**St. John's wort (Hypericum perforatum)**
- **Evidence**: 36-person crossover RCT
- **Effectiveness**: Superior for physical and behavioral symptoms but NOT for mood symptoms
- **Limitation**: No significant effects on anxiety, depression, aggression, or impulsivity
- **Clinical recommendation**: SSRIs better first-line option for PMDD

**Dong Quai (Angelica sinensis)**
- **Evidence**: >2000 years traditional use but inconsistent clinical evidence
- **Limitation**: Results too similar to control groups to conclude direct effect
- **Mechanism**: Does not appear to have hormone-like actions (not a phytoestrogen)

**Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata)**
- **Evidence**: Effective for anxiety (equal to oxazepam in GAD trial)
- **Limitation**: No specific PMS/PMDD trials found
- **Application**: Used for painful menstruation but lack of rigorous PMS research

## Traditional Formulas with Best Evidence

**Jia-Wei-Xiao-Yao-San (JWXYS)**
- **Status**: Most commonly prescribed Chinese herbal formula for PMS
- **Prevalence**: 37.5% of all PMS prescriptions in Taiwan study
- **Evidence**: Clinical trial showing p < 0.01 for both physical and psychological symptoms
- **Components**: 8 herbs including Bupleurum, White Peony, Dong Quai, Atractylodes, Poria, Licorice, Moutan bark, Gardenia
- **TCM theory**: Addresses liver Qi stagnation and liver-spleen disharmony

**Chaihu Shugan Powder (CSP)**
- **Status**: Representative TCM formula for PMS
- **Evidence**: Widely used, especially effective for PMS-related depression
- **Components**: 7 traditional Chinese medicine herbs
- **Traditional use**: Liver detoxification and depression alleviation

## Summary Rankings by Evidence Quality

**Strongest Single Herb Evidence:**
1. Vitex agnus-castus (most trials, highest quality, meta-analysis support)
2. Saffron (head-to-head with fluoxetine, multiple RCTs)
3. Curcumin (10 RCTs, 6 positive)
4. Ginkgo biloba (multiple controlled trials)
5. Lemon balm (strong statistical significance for anxiety/depression)

**Best Traditional Formula Evidence:**
1. Jia-Wei-Xiao-Yao-San (clinical trials, 37.5% prescription prevalence)
2. Chaihu Shugan Powder (widely used, depression focus)

**Most Robust Overall:**
- Vitex agnus-castus has the most extensive, consistent evidence across multiple trials
- Saffron shows promise as potentially equal to SSRIs with better side effect profile
- Traditional formulas (especially JWXYS) have good evidence supporting combination approaches
