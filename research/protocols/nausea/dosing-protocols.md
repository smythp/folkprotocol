# Dosing Protocols and Formulations for Nausea

Practical dosing information extracted from clinical trials and systematic reviews.

## Evidence-Based Dosing by Herb

### Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

#### Standardized Doses from Clinical Trials

**Pregnancy NVP**:
- **Standard dose**: 1g daily (divided doses)
- **Typical regimen**: 250mg capsules 4x/day
- **Range studied**: 1-2g daily
- **Duration**: Throughout symptomatic period (typically weeks 6-16)
- **Onset**: 2-4 days for initial improvement

**CINV (Chemotherapy)**:
- **Standard dose**: 500-1000mg twice daily
- **Timing**: Start 5 days before chemotherapy, continue through 5 days after
- **Combined with**: Standard antiemetics (ondansetron, etc.)
- **Trial dose (ginger vs chamomile)**: 500mg capsules twice daily

**PONV (Post-operative)**:
- **Preventive dose**: 1g oral, given 2 hours preoperatively
- **Meta-analysis range**: Various preparations studied (powder, capsules, syrup)

**General Digestive Nausea**:
- **Standard dose**: 1-2g daily
- **Frequency**: Divided doses or single dose
- **Duration**: Ongoing as needed

**Motion Sickness** (weak evidence):
- **Dose studied**: 1g
- **Timing**: 30-60 minutes before travel
- **Note**: Mixed results; works for some, not others

#### Formulation-Specific Dosing

**Fresh ginger root**:
- **Dose**: 2-4g daily
- **Preparation**: Slice thinly, steep in hot water 5-10 minutes
- **Advantage**: Highest volatile oil content, fresh gingerols
- **Storage**: Refrigerate; use within 2-3 weeks

**Dried ginger powder**:
- **Dose**: 250-1000mg daily
- **Preparation**: Mix in water, juice, or encapsulate
- **Advantage**: Concentrated, shelf-stable
- **Note**: Higher shogaol content (gingerols convert to shogaols when dried)
- **Storage**: Airtight container, use within 6 months

**Ginger tea** (dried):
- **Dose**: 1-2g steeped in 8oz hot water
- **Steeping time**: 5-10 minutes
- **Frequency**: 1-3 cups daily

**Standardized extract capsules**:
- **Standardization**: 5% gingerols (common)
- **Dose**: Follow product label; typically 250-500mg capsules
- **Advantage**: Consistent potency, easy dosing
- **Quality**: Choose reputable brands (ConsumerLab tested preferred)

**Ginger essential oil** (aromatherapy):
- **Use**: Inhalation only (NOT oral)
- **Method**: 2-3 drops on tissue or cotton ball, inhale as needed
- **Alternative**: Diffuser with 3-5 drops
- **Onset**: 5-10 minutes

**Candied/crystallized ginger**:
- **Dose**: 1-2 pieces (approximately 500mg-1g ginger)
- **Note**: Contains sugar; consider for acute nausea when oral intake tolerable
- **Advantage**: Palatable, easy to carry

#### Special Populations

**Pregnancy**:
- **Safe dose**: 1g daily (per systematic reviews)
- **Maximum studied**: 2g daily
- **Recommendation**: Start low (500mg daily), increase if needed
- **Safety**: Multiple systematic reviews confirm safety

**Children**:
- **Age 2-6**: Consult pediatrician; typically 250mg daily MAX
- **Age 6-12**: 250-500mg daily
- **Age 12+**: Adult doses
- **Form**: Tea (diluted) or small amounts fresh ginger safest

**Elderly**:
- **Standard adult doses** generally safe
- **Caution**: Monitor if on anticoagulants (warfarin)
- **Start low**: 500mg daily, increase to 1-2g if tolerated

#### Drug Interactions and Contraindications

**Anticoagulants** (warfarin, heparin):
- **Concern**: Theoretical increased bleeding risk
- **Evidence**: Weak; mostly theoretical
- **Recommendation**: Use doses ≤2g daily; monitor INR if on warfarin
- **Note**: No documented serious interactions, but caution warranted

**Diabetes medications**:
- **Concern**: Ginger may lower blood sugar
- **Recommendation**: Monitor glucose if on insulin or sulfonylureas
- **May need**: Medication dose adjustment

**Antiplatelet drugs** (aspirin, clopidogrel):
- **Concern**: Theoretical additive effect
- **Evidence**: Weak
- **Recommendation**: Use standard doses (1-2g); avoid very high doses

**Pre-surgery**:
- **Some practitioners recommend**: Stop 1-2 weeks before surgery (bleeding concern)
- **Evidence**: Weak; no documented increased bleeding in surgical patients
- **Decision**: Discuss with surgeon; many allow continuation

### Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

#### Aromatherapy Dosing

**Postoperative/Chemotherapy Nausea**:
- **Method**: Inhalation of 100% pure peppermint essential oil
- **Application**: 2-3 drops on tissue, cotton ball, or gauze pad
- **Frequency**: Inhale as needed (every 15-30 minutes if severe)
- **Onset**: 5-10 minutes
- **Duration of effect**: 1-2 hours
- **Evidence**: "Most effective essential oil" per 2025 meta-analysis

**Pregnancy NVP**:
- **Method**: Same as above
- **Safety**: Generally safe; avoid oral essential oil
- **Alternative**: Peppermint tea (see below)

#### Oral Dosing

**Peppermint tea**:
- **Dose**: 1-2 cups as needed
- **Preparation**: 1-2 teaspoons dried leaves steeped 5-10 minutes
- **Frequency**: Up to 3-4 cups daily
- **Safety**: Generally safe; may worsen GERD in some

**Enteric-coated peppermint oil** (IBS/dyspepsia with nausea):
- **Dose**: 0.2-0.4mL (approximately 180-225mg) three times daily
- **Timing**: Between meals (30-60 min before or after)
- **Why enteric-coated**: Prevents release in stomach (which can cause heartburn); releases in intestines
- **Duration**: 4-6 weeks for IBS; ongoing for chronic dyspepsia
- **Evidence**: Multiple RCTs for IBS; extrapolated for nausea

**Fresh peppermint leaves**:
- **Dose**: 2-3g in tea
- **Advantage**: Pleasant taste, less concentrated than oil
- **Use**: Acute nausea, mild cases

#### Contraindications

**GERD/Hiatal hernia**:
- **Mechanism**: Peppermint relaxes lower esophageal sphincter
- **Effect**: May worsen acid reflux
- **Recommendation**: Avoid or use with caution; aromatherapy safer than oral

**Achlorhydria** (severe low stomach acid):
- **Recommendation**: Avoid

**Gallstones**:
- **Concern**: Theoretical cholagogue effect (stimulates bile flow)
- **Recommendation**: Use caution; consult provider

**Infants**:
- **Avoid**: Direct application of menthol/peppermint oil to face (respiratory distress risk)
- **Safe**: Dilute tea for older children

#### Special Populations

**Pregnancy**:
- **Tea**: Generally safe in moderate amounts (1-2 cups daily)
- **Essential oil**: Aromatherapy safe; avoid oral ingestion
- **Evidence**: Included in systematic reviews of safe herbs for NVP

**Lactation**:
- **Concern**: Very large amounts may reduce milk supply (theoretical)
- **Safe amounts**: 1-2 cups tea daily unlikely to cause issues
- **High doses**: Avoid concentrated oil/high-dose capsules while breastfeeding

### Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

#### Dosing from Clinical Trials

**CINV** (vs ginger RCT):
- **Dose**: 500mg Matricaria chamomilla extract capsules twice daily
- **Timing**: 5 days before chemotherapy through 5 days after
- **Combined with**: Routine antiemetic medications
- **Result**: Equal to ginger for vomiting frequency

**General Use**:
- **Tea**: 1-4 cups daily
- **Extract**: 500mg twice daily (standardized)

#### Formulations

**Chamomile tea** (most common):
- **Dose**: 1 heaping teaspoon (2-3g) dried flowers per 8oz water
- **Steeping**: 5-10 minutes, covered (preserve volatile oils)
- **Frequency**: 1-4 cups daily
- **Onset**: 30-60 minutes for digestive effects
- **Best for**: General nausea, anxiety-related nausea

**Standardized extract capsules**:
- **Dose**: 500mg twice daily (based on CINV trial)
- **Standardization**: Typically to apigenin content
- **Use**: More consistent dosing than tea

**Tincture**:
- **Dose**: 2-3mL three times daily
- **Dilution**: In water or juice
- **Advantage**: Concentrated, long shelf life

#### Safety and Contraindications

**Allergy to Asteraceae family**:
- **Plants in family**: Ragweed, chrysanthemums, marigolds, daisies
- **Risk**: Allergic reactions (rash, itching, rare anaphylaxis)
- **Recommendation**: Avoid if known allergy to any Asteraceae plant

**Pregnancy**:
- **Moderate amounts**: Generally safe (1-2 cups tea daily)
- **Excessive amounts**: Not recommended (theoretical uterine stimulant in very high doses)
- **Evidence**: Included in systematic reviews as safe for NVP

**Interactions**:
- **Anticoagulants**: Contains coumarins; theoretical interaction (weak evidence)
- **Sedatives**: May have additive effect (apigenin is mildly sedating)

### Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare)

#### Traditional Dosing

**Fennel seeds** (chewed or tea):
- **Dose**: 1-2 teaspoons crushed seeds
- **Preparation**: Chew after meals OR steep in hot water 5-10 min
- **Frequency**: After each meal or 2-3x daily
- **Use**: Bloating with nausea, gas-related discomfort

**Fennel extract**:
- **Dose**: Varies by preparation; follow product guidelines
- **Typical**: 100-200mg standardized extract 2-3x daily

#### CCF Tea (Ayurvedic formula)

**Composition**: Equal parts Cumin, Coriander, Fennel seeds
- **Dose**: 1 teaspoon total blend per 8oz water
- **Preparation**: Briefly sauté seeds (optional, enhances flavor), add water, boil 5 min, strain
- **Frequency**: 1 cup before each meal (2-3x daily)
- **Use**: Preventive digestive support, mild nausea

#### Safety

**Generally safe** as food/spice

**Estrogen-sensitive conditions** (theoretical concern):
- **Concern**: Anethole has weak estrogenic activity
- **Conditions**: Breast cancer, endometriosis, uterine fibroids
- **Recommendation**: Food/culinary amounts safe; avoid high-dose extracts

**Pregnancy**:
- **Culinary amounts**: Safe
- **Medicinal doses**: Not well-studied; avoid concentrated extracts

**Lactation**:
- **Traditional use**: Galactagogue (increases milk production)
- **Safety**: Generally considered safe

### Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum)

#### Dosing

**As culinary spice**:
- **Amount**: As desired in food/beverages
- **Traditional**: Chai tea, curries, rice dishes
- **Advantage**: Pleasant flavor, easy to incorporate

**Cardamom tea**:
- **Dose**: 2-4 pods, crushed
- **Preparation**: Steep in hot water 5-10 min
- **Frequency**: 1-3 cups daily

**Cardamom-Ginger-Fennel Tea** (Ayurvedic):
- **Composition**: 1/4 tsp cardamom seeds, 1/4 tsp fresh ginger (grated), 1/4 tsp fennel seeds
- **Preparation**: Boil in 8oz water for 5 minutes
- **Frequency**: 1-2 cups daily
- **Use**: Morning nausea, digestive support, circulation

**Extract/capsules**:
- **Dose**: Follow product guidelines (not well-standardized)
- **Typical**: 200-400mg 2-3x daily

#### Safety

**Generally safe** as culinary spice
**No well-established contraindications**
**Pregnancy/Lactation**: Safe as food/spice

### Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)

**Note**: Not an herb, but included due to evidence for pregnancy NVP

#### Dosing

**Pregnancy NVP**:
- **Standard dose**: 10-25mg three times daily
- **Total daily**: 30-75mg
- **Timing**: With meals to reduce GI upset
- **Evidence**: Comparable to ginger in most trials

**Maximum safe dose**:
- **Short-term (pregnancy)**: Up to 200mg daily considered safe
- **Long-term**: Limit to <100mg daily (chronic high doses can cause peripheral neuropathy)

#### Combination Products

**Diclegis/Diclectin** (prescription in US/Canada):
- **Composition**: Doxylamine (antihistamine) 10mg + Pyridoxine (B6) 10mg
- **Dose**: 2 tablets at bedtime; up to 4 tablets daily
- **Note**: Delayed-release formulation

**Over-the-counter combinations**:
- Various brands combine B6 + ginger
- Example: 25mg B6 + 200mg ginger per capsule

#### Safety

**Pregnancy**: Safe and recommended for NVP
**Lactation**: Safe (B6 needed for infant development)

**Interactions**:
- **Levodopa**: B6 reduces effectiveness (unless combined with carbidopa)
- **Phenytoin, phenobarbital**: May reduce B6 levels (supplementation may be needed)

**Toxicity**:
- **>200mg daily long-term**: Risk of peripheral neuropathy (numbness, tingling in extremities)
- **Pregnancy use**: 30-75mg daily safe, even long-term

## Combination Protocols

### Protocol 1: Pregnancy NVP (First Trimester)

**Tier 1 - Mild NVP**:
- **Morning**: Ginger 250mg + B6 25mg
- **Midday**: Ginger 250mg + B6 25mg
- **Evening**: Ginger 250mg + B6 25mg
- **Bedtime**: B6 25mg (or use Diclegis if prescribed)
- **As needed**: Peppermint or lemon aromatherapy

**Tier 2 - Moderate NVP** (if Tier 1 insufficient after 3-4 days):
- Increase ginger to 500mg per dose (2g total daily)
- Continue B6 25mg 3x daily
- Add chamomile tea 1-2 cups daily
- Peppermint aromatherapy regularly

**Tier 3 - Severe NVP** (if Tier 2 insufficient):
- Continue Tier 2 herbs
- Consult provider for prescription antiemetics (ondansetron, Diclegis)
- Consider acupressure at P6 point
- If unable to keep down liquids → seek medical care (hyperemesis gravidarum)

**Timeline**: Expect improvement in 2-4 days; maximum benefit 2-4 weeks

### Protocol 2: CINV (Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea)

**Starting 5 days before chemotherapy**:
- **Morning**: Ginger 500-1000mg
- **Evening**: Ginger 500-1000mg
- **As needed**: Peppermint aromatherapy

**Day of chemotherapy**:
- Standard antiemetics (ondansetron, etc.) AS PRESCRIBED
- Continue ginger 500-1000mg twice daily
- Peppermint aromatherapy available

**Days 1-5 post-chemotherapy** (acute phase):
- Continue all standard antiemetics AS PRESCRIBED
- Ginger 500-1000mg twice daily
- **Peppermint aromatherapy**: Especially effective at 6 hours post-chemo (per RCT evidence)
- Consider chamomile tea or capsules (500mg 2x daily) as alternative to ginger

**Days 6-10 post-chemotherapy** (delayed phase):
- Continue ginger if nausea persists
- Taper standard antiemetics as directed by oncologist
- Aromatherapy as needed

**Traditional option**: Xiao-Ban-Xia Decoction (consult TCM practitioner)

**Important**: Always inform oncologist about herb use; continue all prescribed medications

### Protocol 3: PONV (Post-Operative Nausea)

**Preoperative** (2 hours before surgery):
- Ginger 1g oral with small sip of water

**Postoperative**:
- **Immediately upon waking**: Peppermint essential oil aromatherapy (2-3 drops on gauze pad, inhale)
- **Every 15-30 min as needed**: Repeat aromatherapy
- **Once oral intake allowed**: Ginger tea or capsules 500mg
- **Alternative aromatherapy**: Ginger essential oil or blend (ginger + peppermint + lavender)

**First 24 hours**:
- Continue aromatherapy as needed
- Ginger 500-1000mg oral when able
- Use in addition to any prescribed antiemetics

**Thyroidectomy-specific** (based on RCT):
- Ginger aromatherapy superior to peppermint (Rhodes score 0.48 vs 1.11)
- Consider ginger as primary aromatherapy for thyroid surgery

### Protocol 4: IBS/Functional Dyspepsia with Nausea

**Daily maintenance**:
- **Before breakfast**: Peppermint oil (enteric-coated) 0.2-0.4mL OR CCF tea 1 cup
- **Before lunch**: Peppermint oil 0.2-0.4mL OR CCF tea 1 cup
- **Before dinner**: Peppermint oil 0.2-0.4mL OR CCF tea 1 cup
- **Throughout day**: Ginger 1-2g (divided doses or single dose)

**For acute flare**:
- Peppermint tea or aromatherapy (immediate relief)
- Ginger tea 1-2g
- Fennel seeds (chew 1 tsp or make tea) if bloating present

**Timeline**: Expect initial improvement 1-2 weeks, significant benefit 4-6 weeks, maximum 8-12 weeks

### Protocol 5: Acute Nausea (Digestive Upset, Food Poisoning, Virus)

**Immediate**:
- **Aromatherapy**: Peppermint essential oil, inhale 2-3 drops on tissue
- **Onset**: 5-10 minutes
- **Repeat**: Every 15-30 minutes as needed

**Once able to sip liquids**:
- **Ginger tea**: 2-4g fresh root steeped in hot water, sip slowly
- **Alternative**: Peppermint tea
- **Onset**: 15-30 minutes

**Once oral intake tolerable**:
- Ginger capsules 500-1000mg every 4-6 hours
- Chamomile tea if anxiety/tension present
- Fennel tea if bloating/gas present

**Rehydration**: Essential with vomiting
- Small sips water, electrolyte solution
- Ginger tea counts toward fluid intake

### Protocol 6: Motion Sickness (Weak Evidence)

**Preventive**:
- Ginger 1g 30-60 minutes before travel
- Consider repeat dosing every 4 hours if long travel

**During travel**:
- Peppermint aromatherapy as needed
- Ginger candy/crystallized ginger to suck on

**Note**: Evidence mixed; works for some people, not others. Consider pharmaceutical options (meclizine) if critical travel.

### Protocol 7: Chronic Functional Nausea (Pattern-Based TCM Approach)

**For "rebellious qi with phlegm" pattern** (nausea, vomiting, throat sensation, anxiety):
- **Formula**: Banxia Houpo Tang (consult TCM practitioner for proper preparation)
- **Frequency**: 2-3x daily
- **Duration**: 2-4 weeks minimum
- **Adjunct**: Chamomile tea for anxiety component

**For "spleen qi deficiency with dampness"** (nausea, poor appetite, fatigue, loose stools):
- **Formula**: Liu Jun Zi Tang OR CCF tea
- **Frequency**: Before meals
- **Duration**: 4-12 weeks (building approach)
- **Adjunct**: Ginger tea to strengthen digestion

## Traditional Tea Formulas

### Cardamom-Ginger-Fennel Tea (Ayurvedic)

**Ingredients** (per cup):
- 1/4 tsp cardamom seeds (crushed)
- 1/4 tsp fresh ginger root (grated)
- 1/4 tsp fennel seeds

**Preparation**:
1. Bring 8oz water to boil
2. Add all ingredients
3. Simmer 5 minutes
4. Strain
5. Optional: Add honey

**Use**: Morning nausea, digestive support
**Frequency**: 1-2 cups daily

### CCF Tea (Cumin-Coriander-Fennel)

**Ingredients** (per cup):
- 1/3 tsp cumin seeds
- 1/3 tsp coriander seeds
- 1/3 tsp fennel seeds

**Preparation**:
1. Optional: Dry roast seeds in pan 1-2 minutes (enhances flavor)
2. Add to 8oz boiling water
3. Simmer 5-10 minutes
4. Strain

**Use**: Preventive digestive support, mild nausea
**Frequency**: Before each meal (2-3 cups daily)
**Timeline**: Preventive benefits develop over 1-2 weeks

### Simple Ginger-Peppermint Tea

**Ingredients**:
- 2g fresh ginger root (sliced)
- 1 tsp dried peppermint leaves

**Preparation**:
1. Steep both in 8oz hot water 5-10 minutes
2. Strain

**Use**: Acute nausea, general digestive upset
**Frequency**: As needed

## Quality and Sourcing Considerations

### Ginger

**Fresh root**:
- Look for: Firm, smooth skin; heavy for size
- Avoid: Wrinkled, soft, moldy spots
- Storage: Refrigerator in paper towel, use within 2-3 weeks
- Peel: Not necessary if organic; peel if conventional

**Dried powder**:
- Organic preferred (fewer pesticides)
- Store airtight, cool, dark place
- Use within 6 months for best potency
- Color: Pale yellow-tan (dark brown = old)

**Capsules**:
- Standardization: 5% gingerols common
- Third-party testing: Look for USP, ConsumerLab, NSF certification
- Avoid: Fillers, additives, "proprietary blends" (unknown amounts)

### Peppermint

**Essential oil**:
- **100% pure** (no "fragrance oil" or diluted versions)
- Species: Mentha × piperita (not spearmint = Mentha spicata)
- Therapeutic grade (though term not regulated)
- Reputable brands: doTERRA, Young Living, Plant Therapy, NOW Foods
- Storage: Dark glass bottle, cool place

**Enteric-coated oil capsules**:
- Must be enteric-coated (releases in intestines, not stomach)
- Brands with evidence: IBgard, Heather's Tummy Tamers
- Standardization: 0.2mL (180-225mg) per capsule typical

**Dried leaves** (tea):
- Organic preferred
- Whole leaves > cut leaves > fannings (for potency)
- Aroma: Strong, fresh mint smell (weak = old/poor quality)
- Storage: Airtight, use within 1 year

### Chamomile

**Dried flowers**:
- Species: German chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) for GI use
- Organic preferred
- Whole flowers > broken flowers
- Color: Bright white petals with yellow centers (brown = old)
- Storage: Airtight, use within 1 year

**Extract/capsules**:
- Standardization: Often to apigenin content (not always specified)
- Look for: Third-party testing, reputable brand

### Fennel Seeds

**Quality**:
- Greenish-brown color (faded green-gray = old)
- Strong licorice-like aroma
- Organic preferred
- Storage: Airtight, cool, dark; whole seeds last 2-3 years

**Preparation tip**: Crush seeds just before use (mortar & pestle or back of spoon) to release volatile oils

### Cardamom

**Quality**:
- Green cardamom pods (Elettaria) preferred for digestive use
- Whole pods > ground (ground loses potency quickly)
- Pods should be plump, green (not brown/faded)
- Strong aromatic smell when crushed
- Storage: Whole pods in airtight container, 1-2 years

**Preparation**: Crush pods to release seeds; use seeds (discard green husk or leave for flavor)

## Practical Tips for Implementation

### Maximizing Effectiveness

**1. Start before nausea peaks**:
- Pregnancy: Start ginger at first sign of NVP (week 6)
- Chemotherapy: Start 5 days before treatment
- Surgery: Take ginger 2 hours pre-op

**2. Choose formulation based on speed needed**:
- **Fastest** (5-10 min): Aromatherapy
- **Fast** (15-30 min): Tea
- **Standard** (30-60 min): Capsules
- **Slowest** (hours): Enteric-coated (but longest-lasting for IBS)

**3. Use consistent dosing for chronic nausea**:
- Not "as needed" → scheduled dosing (e.g., 3x daily)
- Build therapeutic levels over days/weeks
- Prevent nausea rather than just treat it

**4. Combine mechanisms**:
- Ginger (5-HT3 antagonism) + Peppermint (calcium blockade) = synergy
- Ginger (oral) + Peppermint (aromatherapy) = different routes, additive effect

### Storage and Shelf Life

**Dried herbs** (ginger, chamomile, peppermint):
- Airtight container, cool, dark place
- Shelf life: 6 months to 1 year
- Signs of degradation: Faded color, weak aroma

**Essential oils**:
- Dark glass bottles (not plastic)
- Cool place (refrigerator OK but not necessary)
- Shelf life: 2-3 years if stored properly
- Signs of degradation: Cloudy, weak scent, rancid smell

**Fresh ginger**:
- Refrigerator in paper towel or breathable bag
- Shelf life: 2-3 weeks
- Freezer: 6+ months (grate frozen for tea)

**Capsules/tablets**:
- Follow expiration date
- Store cool, dry place (not bathroom - humidity degrades)
- Shelf life: Typically 2 years from manufacture

### When to Escalate Care

**Herbs not working after**:
- Acute nausea: 24-48 hours
- Pregnancy NVP: 1 week of consistent use
- PONV/CINV: If vomiting prevents oral intake
- Chronic nausea: 2-4 weeks of protocol

**Red flags** (seek immediate care):
- Unable to keep down liquids >12 hours
- Signs of dehydration
- Severe abdominal pain
- Bloody vomit
- High fever with nausea
- Pregnant and losing weight (hyperemesis gravidarum)
