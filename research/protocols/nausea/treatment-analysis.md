## 1. Strongest Evidence Herbs for Nausea

### Tier 1: High-Quality Evidence (Multiple Meta-Analyses)

**Ginger (Zingiber officinale)**
- **Evidence level**: 15+ meta-analyses reviewed
- **Pooled effect size**: OR 0.41 (95% CI: 0.22-0.79, P = 0.008)
- **Contexts validated**:
  - Pregnancy NVP (strongest evidence)
  - Postoperative nausea (PONV)
  - Chemotherapy-induced nausea (CINV)
  - General digestive nausea
  - Motion sickness (mixed evidence)
- **Key finding**: "Ginger is a promising antiemetic herbal remedy" across multiple systematic reviews since 2000

**Peppermint (Mentha × piperita)**
- **Evidence level**: Meta-analysis (2025) - most recent comprehensive review
- **Key finding**: "Most effective essential oil among all tested" for nausea/vomiting
- **Contexts validated**:
  - Postoperative nausea (PONV) - strongest evidence
  - Chemotherapy-induced nausea (CINV)
  - Pregnancy-related nausea (NVP)
- **Administration**: Primarily aromatherapy (essential oil inhalation)

### Tier 2: Strong Evidence (Multiple RCTs, Some Meta-Analyses)

**Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)**
- **Evidence level**: Multiple RCTs, included in systematic reviews
- **Contexts validated**:
  - Chemotherapy-induced nausea
  - Pregnancy NVP (safe and effective)
  - General digestive nausea
- **RCT data**: 500 mg twice daily equally effective as ginger for vomiting frequency in CINV

**Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)** *(pharmaceutical comparison)*
- **Evidence level**: Multiple meta-analyses for pregnancy NVP
- **Standard dose**: 10-25 mg three times daily
- **Key finding**: Ginger comparable or superior to B6 across multiple head-to-head trials

### Tier 3: Moderate Evidence (Multiple RCTs, Traditional Use)

**Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum)**
- **Evidence level**: RCT in aromatherapy blend (N=1,151); traditional use; mechanism studies
- **Contexts**: Pregnancy NVP, general nausea, gastroprotection

**Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare)**
- **Evidence level**: Systematic reviews for pregnancy NVP; traditional formulas
- **Contexts**: Digestive nausea with gas/bloating, pregnancy NVP

**Spearmint**
- **Evidence level**: Included in systematic reviews for pregnancy NVP; aromatherapy blend RCT
- **Administration**: Tea, syrup, aromatherapy

**Lemon**
- **Evidence level**: Systematic reviews for pregnancy NVP
- **Administration**: Aromatherapy, water infusion

## 2. Comparative Effectiveness: Head-to-Head Trials

### Peppermint vs Ginger

**Context varies effectiveness:**

**Chemotherapy aromatherapy (N=28, breast cancer patients):**
- **Winner**: Peppermint at 6 hours post-chemo
- Peppermint average score: 6.57
- Ginger average score: 10.29
- Statistical significance: p = 0.036
- **Conclusion**: "Peppermint more effective than ginger at 6 hours post-chemotherapy"

**Thyroidectomy PONV (RCT, aromatherapy):**
- **Winner**: Ginger (slightly)
- Rhodes score - Ginger: 0.48 ± 0.51
- Rhodes score - Peppermint: 1.11 ± 0.64
- Rhodes score - Control: 1.37 ± 0.49
- Statistical significance: P < 0.001
- **Conclusion**: "Ginger performed slightly better than peppermint for thyroidectomy PONV"

**TB patients with GI disturbances (N=200, 3-month trial):**
- Ginger (1 g daily): **No vomiting recurrence** in both intervention and washout phases
- Peppermint (3 mL daily): Effective but data not as striking
- **Conclusion**: Ginger showed superior sustained effect

### Ginger vs Chamomile

**CINV in breast cancer patients (N=65, RCT):**
- **Dose**: Both 500 mg capsules twice daily for 5 days before/after chemo
- **Vomiting frequency**: No significant difference (both equally effective)
- **Nausea frequency**: Ginger superior
- **Conclusion**: "Ginger superior for nausea frequency; both equally effective for vomiting"

### Ginger vs Vitamin B6

**Multiple RCTs for pregnancy NVP:**

**Smith 2004:**
- Ginger more effective than placebo
- Ginger comparable with vitamin B6

**Ensiyeh 2009:**
- Both effective
- **Ginger more effective than vitamin B6**

**Chittumma 2007:**
- **Nausea severity**: Ginger more effective than B6
- **Vomiting episodes**: Equally effective

**Sharifzadeh 2018:**
- Both led to significant reduction in MPUQE score
- No significant difference between groups

**Summary**: Ginger comparable or superior to vitamin B6 depending on study; both effective first-line options

### Essential Oil Meta-Analysis (2025)

**Oils tested**: Lavender, chamomile, peppermint, orange, ginger, damask rose, sage

**Winner**: Peppermint oil - "most effective in reducing nausea and vomiting across all essential oils"

### Placebo Comparisons

**Ginger vs Placebo (Pregnancy NVP):**
- All 7 studies in meta-analysis: Ginger more effective than placebo
- Significantly decreased nausea symptoms
- Significant effect on general NVP symptoms
- Significant in relieving severity of nausea
- **NOT significant** in reducing vomiting alone (nausea > vomiting effect)

**Herbal Medicine vs Placebo (Laparoscopic surgery PONV):**
- Vomiting incidence: RR = 0.43 (95% CI 0.32-0.57, P < 0.00001)
- Highly significant reduction

## 3. Symptom-Specific Benefits

### Nausea Predominant (vs Vomiting)

**Best herbs:**
1. **Ginger** - strongest evidence specifically for nausea reduction
   - Superior to chamomile for nausea frequency (CINV)
   - More effective than vitamin B6 for nausea severity (some trials)
   - Meta-analysis: significant for nausea, not significant for vomiting alone
2. **Peppermint** - especially aromatherapy
   - Most effective essential oil
   - Rapid onset via inhalation

**Mechanisms favoring nausea control:**
- 5-HT3 receptor antagonism (ginger)
- Serotonin receptor blockade in GI tract (peppermint)
- Smooth muscle relaxation (both)

### Vomiting Predominant

**Best herbs:**
1. **Ginger** - effective for vomiting frequency
   - No vomiting recurrence in TB study (1 g daily)
   - Equally effective as chamomile for vomiting in CINV
2. **Chamomile** - equally effective as ginger for vomiting
   - 500 mg twice daily in CINV RCT

**Traditional formulas specifically for vomiting:**
- **Banxia Houpo Tang** (TCM) - "addresses vomiting from rebellious qi and phlegm"
- **Xiao-Ban-Xia Decoction** (TCM) - "classical anti-emetic formula" for CINV
  - Modern mechanism: inhibits ferroptosis via Nrf2/SLC7A11/GPX4 pathway

### Nausea with Anxiety/Tension

**Best herbs:**
1. **Chamomile** - dual action
   - Antispasmodic (smooth muscle relaxation)
   - Anxiolytic via apigenin (GABA-A receptor binding)
   - Anti-inflammatory (NF-κB suppression, reduces IL-1β, TNF-α)
2. **Lavender** - aromatherapy for calming effect

**Traditional approach:**
- **Banxia Houpo Tang** (TCM) - addresses "plum-pit qi" (throat/chest tension + digestive symptoms)
  - Indication: "Nausea and vomiting from rebellious qi and phlegm"
  - Pattern: Emotional component + digestive distress

### Nausea with Bloating/Gas

**Best herbs (carminatives):**
1. **Fennel** - strongest carminative action
   - Anethole breaks down gas bubbles
   - Facilitates gas expulsion
   - Increases bile and gastric secretions
2. **Cardamom** - warming carminative
   - Gastroprotective (89.3% mucus increase)
   - 1,8-cineole reduces gastric acid secretion
3. **Peppermint** - cooling carminative
   - Antispasmodic
   - Relaxes GI smooth muscle

**Ayurvedic approach:**
- **CCF Tea** (Cumin-Coriander-Fennel) - gentle digestive support, reduces bloating/gas
- **Cardamom-Ginger-Fennel Tea** - circulation + digestion + "burns off morning mucous"

### Nausea with Acid Reflux/Hyperacidity (Pitta-type)

**Ayurvedic formulas:**
1. **Avipattikar Churna** - balances excess Pitta
   - Acidity, indigestion, bile reflux
   - Soothes stomach, reduces acid-related nausea
2. **Kamdudha Ras** - Pitta imbalance
   - Acid reflux, hyperacidity

**Note on Peppermint**: AVOID if GERD/hiatal hernia (relaxes lower esophageal sphincter, may worsen reflux)

## 4. Traditional Combinations & Synergy

### Why Combinations Work: Complementary Mechanisms

**Ginger + Peppermint + Cardamom + Spearmint** (RCT N=1,151)
- **Result**: Statistically significant reduction in nausea AND antiemetic medication requests
- **Mechanisms stack**:
  - Ginger: 5-HT3 antagonism, NK-1 antagonism, prokinetic
  - Peppermint: Calcium channel blockade, TRPM8 activation, smooth muscle relaxation
  - Cardamom: Gastroprotection, 1,8-cineole (antioxidant, lipoxygenase inhibition)
  - Spearmint: Similar to peppermint but gentler
- **Synergy noted**: "Synergistic plant combinations noted for enhanced efficacy and safety"

### Traditional Chinese Medicine Formulas

**Banxia Houpo Tang (Pinellia & Magnolia Bark Decoction)**
- **Source**: Jingui Yaolue (Han Dynasty, Zhang Zhongjing)
- **Composition**:
  1. Pinellia ternata (Ban Xia) - primary antiemetic
  2. Poria cocos (Fu Ling) - strengthens spleen, drains dampness
  3. Magnolia officinalis (Hou Po) - moves qi, transforms phlegm
  4. Perilla frutescens (Zi Su Ye) - regulates qi, harmonizes stomach
  5. **Zingiber officinale (Sheng Jiang) - GINGER** - warms stomach, stops vomiting
- **Why it works**:
  - Addresses ROOT CAUSE: rebellious qi + phlegm accumulation
  - Ginger provides antiemetic + warming action
  - Other herbs address underlying dampness/phlegm pattern
  - Multi-targeted approach: qi regulation + phlegm transformation + stomach warming

**Xiao-Ban-Xia Decoction (Minor Pinellia Decoction)**
- **Modern use**: CINV (chemotherapy-induced nausea/vomiting)
- **Mechanism discovered**: Inhibits ferroptosis (iron-dependent cell death)
  - Activates Nrf2/SLC7A11/GPX4 pathway
  - Protects cells from cisplatin-induced damage
- **Why it works**: Not just symptomatic relief - prevents cellular mechanism of chemo-induced nausea

### Ayurvedic Formulas

**CCF Tea (Cumin-Coriander-Fennel)**
- **Why it works**:
  - All three are carminatives (anti-gas)
  - Support digestion at different levels
  - Encourage gentle cleansing
  - Cumulative effect on bloating reduction
- **When to use**: Digestive nausea with sluggish digestion, gas, bloating

**Cardamom-Ginger-Fennel Tea**
- **Why it works**:
  - Cardamom: warming, circulation, gastroprotective
  - Ginger: antiemetic, prokinetic, anti-inflammatory
  - Fennel: carminative, digestive stimulant
  - Synergy: circulation improvement + antiemetic action + gas reduction
- **When to use**: Morning nausea, sluggish digestion, cold-type digestive issues

**Triphala (Three Fruits)**
- **Composition**: Amalaki + Bibhitaki + Haritaki
- **Why it works**: General digestive tonic
  - Regulates bowel movements (addresses constipation-related nausea)
  - Cleanses GI tract
  - Long-term digestive health
- **When to use**: Chronic digestive issues underlying nausea

### Western Aromatherapy Blends

**Multi-Oil Inhalation Therapy**
- **Principle**: Different oils target different receptors/pathways
- **Example**: Peppermint (TRPM8 + calcium channels) + Ginger (5-HT3) + Lavender (calming/GABA) + Cardamom (gastroprotection)
- **Why combinations work better**:
  - Broader receptor coverage
  - Multiple mechanisms simultaneously
  - Enhanced safety profile (lower dose of each component)
  - Olfactory synergy (more pleasant = better compliance)

## 5. Dosing: Acute vs Chronic Nausea

### Acute Nausea (Immediate Relief Needed)

**Ginger - Aromatherapy (Fastest Onset)**
- **Dose**: Inhalation of ginger essential oil
- **Onset**: Minutes
- **Context**: PONV, motion sickness, sudden nausea

**Peppermint - Aromatherapy (Fastest Acting Essential Oil)**
- **Dose**: Inhalation of peppermint essential oil as needed
- **Onset**: Minutes (meta-analysis shows rapid effect)
- **Context**: PONV (most effective), CINV, sudden nausea
- **AVOID**: GERD patients (may worsen reflux)

**Ginger - Fresh Root**
- **Dose**: 2-4 g fresh ginger (chewed or made into tea)
- **Onset**: 15-30 minutes
- **Preparation**: Slice fresh ginger, chew or steep in hot water

**Ginger - Capsules/Powder (For Predictable Events)**
- **PONV**: 1 g preoperatively (before surgery)
- **Motion sickness**: 1 g before travel
- **Onset**: 30-60 minutes (needs to be absorbed)

### Chronic/Recurrent Nausea (Daily Management)

**Pregnancy NVP (Nausea and Vomiting of Pregnancy)**

*Ginger Protocol:*
- **Dose**: 1 g daily (divided doses)
- **Formulation**: Capsules (250 mg x 4) or dried powder
- **Duration**: Throughout first trimester or until symptoms resolve
- **Expected improvement**: Gradual over 1-2 weeks
- **Safety**: Multiple meta-analyses confirm safety in pregnancy

*Vitamin B6 Protocol (Alternative or Combination):*
- **Dose**: 10-25 mg three times daily
- **Duration**: Daily throughout symptomatic period
- **Safety**: Safe for pregnancy; avoid >100 mg/day long-term

*Herbal Tea Approach (Milder, Multiple Mechanisms):*
- **Peppermint tea**: 1-2 cups daily
- **Chamomile tea**: 1-4 cups daily
- **Cardamom-Ginger-Fennel tea**: 1-2 cups daily
- **Expected improvement**: Gradual, supportive effect

**Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea (CINV)**

*Ginger Protocol:*
- **Dose**: 500-1000 mg twice daily
- **Timing**: Start 5 days BEFORE chemotherapy, continue 5 days AFTER
- **Duration**: 10-day cycle around each chemo session
- **Formulation**: Capsules
- **Combination**: WITH conventional antiemetics (not replacement)
- **Expected benefit**: Reduction in nausea severity, reduced antiemetic medication needs

*Chamomile Alternative/Addition:*
- **Dose**: 500 mg Matricaria chamomilla extract twice daily
- **Timing**: Same as ginger (5 days before + 5 days after)
- **Expected benefit**: Equally effective for vomiting; ginger superior for nausea frequency

*Peppermint Aromatherapy:*
- **Dose**: Inhalation of essential oil as needed during acute episodes
- **Timing**: During and after chemotherapy infusion
- **Expected benefit**: Rapid relief within minutes

*Xiao-Ban-Xia Decoction (TCM Approach):*
- **Use**: Classical TCM formula specifically for CINV
- **Mechanism**: Prevents ferroptosis, protects cells
- **Administration**: As prescribed by TCM practitioner
- **Expected benefit**: Addresses cellular mechanism, not just symptoms

**Postoperative Nausea (PONV) Prevention**

*Ginger Preoperative:*
- **Dose**: 1 g preoperatively
- **Timing**: 30-60 minutes before anesthesia
- **Expected benefit**: Network meta-analysis shows preventive effects

*Aromatherapy (Recovery Room):*
- **Peppermint oil**: Inhalation post-surgery as needed
- **Ginger oil**: Alternative or combination
- **Onset**: Minutes
- **Expected benefit**: Rapid relief in recovery period

**General Digestive Nausea (Ongoing)**

*Single Herb Approaches:*
- **Ginger**: 1-2 g daily (tea, capsules, or fresh)
- **Peppermint tea**: 1-2 cups daily
- **Chamomile tea**: 2-4 cups daily
- **Fennel seeds**: 1-2 teaspoons crushed, steeped as tea or chewed after meals

*Combination Tea Approaches:*
- **CCF Tea** (Ayurveda): 1-2 cups daily for digestive support
- **Cardamom-Ginger-Fennel**: 1 cup in morning for circulation + digestion
- **Expected improvement**: Gradual over 1-2 weeks of consistent use

*Ayurvedic Formulas (Pattern-Specific):*
- **Avipattikar Churna**: For acid-related nausea, as directed
- **Kamdudha Ras**: For Pitta-type nausea (heat/acid), as directed
- **Expected benefit**: Addresses underlying dosha imbalance over weeks-months

## 6. Expected Timelines for Improvement

### Acute Relief (Symptomatic Treatment)

**Aromatherapy (Peppermint, Ginger)**
- **Onset**: 2-5 minutes
- **Peak effect**: 5-15 minutes
- **Duration**: 30-60 minutes
- **Context**: Fastest option for immediate relief
- **Evidence**: Multiple RCTs show rapid reduction in nausea scores within minutes of inhalation

**Tea (Ginger, Peppermint, Chamomile)**
- **Onset**: 10-20 minutes
- **Peak effect**: 20-45 minutes
- **Duration**: 1-3 hours
- **Context**: Moderate-speed relief, longer duration than aromatherapy

**Fresh Ginger (Chewed/Eaten)**
- **Onset**: 10-30 minutes
- **Peak effect**: 30-60 minutes
- **Duration**: 2-4 hours
- **Context**: Sustained release, traditional use for motion sickness

**Capsules/Extracts (Oral)**
- **Onset**: 30-60 minutes (needs absorption)
- **Peak effect**: 1-2 hours
- **Duration**: 4-6 hours
- **Context**: Best for prevention rather than acute crisis

### Preventive Use (Before Triggering Event)

**Preoperative Ginger (PONV Prevention)**
- **Timing**: Give 1 g ginger 30-60 minutes before anesthesia
- **Expected benefit**: Reduced PONV incidence in recovery period
- **Evidence**: Network meta-analysis of various ginger preparations

**Pre-Chemotherapy Protocol (CINV Prevention)**
- **Start**: 5 days BEFORE first chemotherapy dose
- **Early benefit**: May reduce acute nausea (0-24 hours post-chemo)
- **Delayed benefit**: More effective for delayed nausea (24-120 hours post-chemo)
- **Full effect**: After 2-3 chemo cycles with consistent use
- **Evidence**: "Notable benefits in preventing delayed CINV" (Hezhong granules study)

**Motion Sickness Prevention**
- **Timing**: Take 1 g ginger 30-60 minutes before travel
- **Mixed evidence**: Some studies positive, others show no benefit
- **Note**: "Evidence for motion sickness remains much less certain than for other nausea types"

### Chronic Conditions (Daily Management)

**Pregnancy NVP (First Trimester)**
- **Initial improvement**: 3-7 days of daily ginger (1 g) or vitamin B6
- **Significant relief**: 1-2 weeks
- **Peak benefit**: 2-4 weeks of consistent use
- **Duration of use**: Throughout first trimester or until symptoms naturally resolve (typically 12-16 weeks gestation)
- **Evidence**: Meta-analyses show "significant reduction in nausea symptoms" with sustained use

**Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea (Multiple Cycles)**
- **First cycle**: Some benefit from ginger/chamomile (500-1000 mg BID starting 5 days prior)
- **Second cycle**: Enhanced benefit with continued use
- **Peak benefit**: After 2-3 cycles of consistent perichemotherapy use
- **Pattern**: Better control of delayed nausea than acute nausea
- **Evidence**: Studies using 5-day pre + 5-day post protocol each cycle

**General Digestive Nausea (Functional Dyspepsia, IBS-related)**
- **Acute symptom relief**: Within single dose (aromatherapy/tea)
- **Pattern improvement**: 1-2 weeks of daily use
- **Significant improvement**: 4-6 weeks of consistent herbal protocol
- **Root cause addressing**: 2-3 months with TCM/Ayurvedic formulas addressing underlying pattern
- **Example - Peppermint oil for IBS**: Studies show benefit within 2-4 weeks

**Ayurvedic Formulas (Dosha Imbalance)**
- **Initial effect**: 3-7 days (symptomatic relief)
- **Dosha rebalancing**: 4-8 weeks
- **Deep pattern shift**: 3-6 months of consistent use
- **Examples**:
  - Avipattikar Churna (Pitta/acid): Relief in days, full benefit in weeks
  - Triphala (digestive tonic): Bowel regulation in 1-2 weeks, deep cleansing over months

**TCM Formulas (Pattern-Based Treatment)**
- **Acute formulas** (e.g., Xiao-Ban-Xia for CINV):
  - Symptomatic relief: Within doses around chemo cycle
  - Mechanism (ferroptosis prevention): Cumulative over cycles
- **Chronic formulas** (e.g., Banxia Houpo Tang):
  - Qi regulation/phlegm transformation: 2-4 weeks
  - Pattern resolution: 4-12 weeks depending on chronicity

### Factors Affecting Timeline

**Faster Response:**
- Acute nausea (not chronic underlying condition)
- Aromatherapy delivery
- High-quality, fresh herbs
- Correct pattern matching (TCM/Ayurveda)
- Addressing trigger (remove causative factor + herb)

**Slower Response:**
- Chronic underlying digestive dysfunction
- Oral delivery (capsules need absorption)
- Low-quality or stale herbs
- Mismatch of herb to pattern
- Ongoing trigger (e.g., continuing chemotherapy)

**No Response Scenarios:**
- Wrong diagnosis (nausea from serious pathology needing medical treatment)
- Inadequate dose
- Poor quality product
- Herb-pattern mismatch (e.g., warming ginger for heat-type nausea)
- Severe underlying condition requiring pharmaceutical intervention

### Clinical Decision Timeline

**Try acute relief**: Aromatherapy or tea - expect response in minutes to 30 minutes

**If no benefit from acute trial**: Consider different herb or combination

**For chronic nausea**: Commit to 2-week trial of daily use before assessing effectiveness

**If 2 weeks shows partial benefit**: Continue for 4-6 weeks for full effect

**If 2 weeks shows no benefit**: Reassess diagnosis, herb choice, or dosing; consider medical evaluation

**For prevention protocols**: Follow full protocol duration (e.g., full 10 days around chemo) before judging effectiveness

**Traditional formulas (TCM/Ayurveda)**: Allow 4-8 weeks to address underlying pattern; reassess if no improvement

## Summary: Clinical Decision Framework

### Step 1: Classify Nausea Type
- **Acute vs Chronic**
- **With/without vomiting**
- **Associated symptoms** (anxiety, gas/bloating, acid reflux)
- **Triggering context** (pregnancy, chemo, post-op, digestive)

### Step 2: Select Primary Herb(s)

| Nausea Type | First Choice | Second Choice | Third Choice |
|-------------|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Acute (any cause)** | Peppermint aromatherapy | Ginger aromatherapy | Ginger fresh/tea |
| **Pregnancy NVP** | Ginger 1g daily | Vitamin B6 | Peppermint tea |
| **PONV (prevention)** | Ginger 1g pre-op | Peppermint aromatherapy (recovery) | Ginger + peppermint |
| **CINV** | Ginger 500-1000mg BID (5d pre/post) | Chamomile 500mg BID | Peppermint aromatherapy |
| **Nausea + anxiety** | Chamomile tea | Banxia Houpo Tang (TCM) | Lavender aromatherapy |
| **Nausea + gas/bloating** | Fennel seeds | CCF tea | Cardamom-Ginger-Fennel |
| **Nausea + acid reflux** | Chamomile tea | Avipattikar Churna | Ginger (AVOID peppermint) |
| **Vomiting predominant** | Ginger | Chamomile | Xiao-Ban-Xia (TCM) |

### Step 3: Choose Delivery Method by Urgency

- **Immediate relief needed**: Aromatherapy (minutes)
- **Moderate urgency**: Tea or fresh herb (15-30 min)
- **Prevention**: Capsules/extract started in advance (hours to days)
- **Chronic management**: Daily protocol (weeks)

### Step 4: Set Expectations

- **Acute**: Relief in minutes (aromatherapy) to 30-60 min (oral)
- **Chronic**: Initial improvement 3-7 days, significant relief 1-2 weeks, peak benefit 4-6 weeks
- **Prevention**: Start before trigger; assess after 2-3 exposures

### Step 5: Reassess

- **If no benefit at expected timeline**: Change herb, increase dose, or seek medical evaluation
- **If partial benefit**: Continue, consider combination approach
- **If full relief**: Continue as needed; may reduce dose/frequency once stable

## Key Takeaways

1. **Strongest overall evidence**: Ginger (15+ meta-analyses) and peppermint (most effective essential oil)

2. **Context matters**: Peppermint wins for aromatherapy/PONV; ginger wins for pregnancy/oral use

3. **Symptom targeting**:
   - Nausea > vomiting: Ginger, peppermint
   - Vomiting: Ginger, chamomile, TCM formulas
   - With anxiety: Chamomile, Banxia Houpo Tang
   - With gas: Fennel, cardamom, CCF tea
   - With acid: Chamomile, Avipattikar (AVOID peppermint)

4. **Traditional combinations work**: Multiple mechanisms = synergy (e.g., ginger + peppermint + cardamom + spearmint showed significant benefit in 1,151-patient RCT)

5. **Dosing strategy**:
   - Acute: Aromatherapy or tea (fast onset)
   - Chronic: Daily capsules/extract (sustained effect)
   - Prevention: Start before trigger

6. **Timeline expectations**:
   - Aromatherapy: Minutes
   - Tea: 15-30 minutes
   - Capsules: 30-60 minutes
   - Chronic benefit: 1-2 weeks of daily use

7. **Safety**: All major herbs (ginger, peppermint, chamomile, fennel, cardamom) safe for pregnancy and general use with few contraindications
