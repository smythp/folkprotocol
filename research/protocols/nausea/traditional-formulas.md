# Traditional Herbal Formulas for Nausea

Research compilation covering traditional formulas from Chinese, Japanese, Ayurvedic, and Western herbal traditions.

---

## Traditional Chinese Medicine Formulas

### Ban Xia Xie Xin Tang (半夏瀉心湯)
**Pinellia Decoction for Draining the Heart**

One of the most historically significant formulas for digestive disturbance, originating from the Han Dynasty text *Shang Han Lun*.

**Composition:**
| Herb | Pinyin | Amount | Function |
|------|--------|--------|----------|
| Pinellia rhizome | Ban Xia | 12g | Chief herb - dries dampness, harmonizes stomach, stops vomiting |
| Scutellaria root | Huang Qin | 9g | Clears heat, reduces inflammation |
| Dried ginger | Gan Jiang | 9g | Warms stomach, stops vomiting |
| Ginseng | Ren Shen | 9g | Tonifies qi, supports digestion |
| Coptis rhizome | Huang Lian | 3g | Clears heat, soothes stomach |
| Jujube dates | Da Zao | 4 pieces | Nourishes spleen, balances formula |
| Roasted licorice | Zhi Gan Cao | 9g | Harmonizes herbs, protects stomach |

**Indications:**
- Functional dyspepsia
- Gastroenteritis
- Epigastric distension with simultaneous heat and cold signs
- Nausea with borborygmus
- Oral mucositis (chemotherapy-induced)

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Systematic review and meta-analysis (PMC10235465) found efficacy for functional dyspepsia
- Used in Asia as complementary treatment when conventional options show insufficient efficacy
- Demonstrated gastric mucosal protection via mucin elevation and oxygen radical elimination

**Contraindications:** Avoid in qi stagnation without dampness, yin deficiency dry cough, blood disorders. Not suitable during pregnancy due to Pinellia's mild toxicity.

### Ban Xia Hou Po Tang (半夏厚朴湯)
**Pinellia and Magnolia Bark Decoction**

Classical formula from *Jingui Yaolue* (Essentials from the Golden Cabinet) by Zhang Zhongjing.

**Composition:**
| Herb | Pinyin | Function |
|------|--------|----------|
| Pinellia rhizome | Ban Xia | Chief - transforms phlegm, disperses binds |
| Magnolia bark | Hou Po | Deputy - moves qi downward, eliminates fullness |
| Poria | Fu Ling | Strengthens spleen, helps transform phlegm |
| Fresh ginger | Sheng Jiang | Disperses binds, harmonizes stomach, relieves vomiting |
| Perilla leaf | Zi Su Ye | Aromatic - moves qi, rectifies lung, soothes liver |

**Indications:**
- Phlegm-dampness obstructing middle jiao
- Nausea and vomiting with abdominal distension
- Gastroenteritis, gastritis
- Morning sickness
- Food poisoning
- Globus hystericus (plum-pit qi)

---

### Xiao Ban Xia Tang (小半夏湯)
**Minor Pinellia Decoction**

Simple but effective two-herb formula.

**Composition:**
- Ban Xia (Pinellia rhizome)
- Sheng Jiang (Fresh ginger)

**Actions:** Alleviates thin mucus, directs rebellious qi downward

**Indications:** Morning sickness, simple nausea without complex presentations

---

### Wu Zhu Yu Tang (吴茱萸汤)
**Evodia Decoction**

For cold-pattern vomiting with yang deficiency.

**Composition:**
- Wu Zhu Yu (Evodia fruit) - chief herb
- Ren Shen (Ginseng)
- Sheng Jiang (Fresh ginger)
- Da Zao (Jujube)

**Indications:**
- Vomiting clear fluids
- Severe upper abdominal pain with cold signs
- Cold extremities
- Desire for warm food/drink
- Vertex headaches with nausea

**Clinical Note:** Historical case studies show dose adjustment may be needed - one case required halving the dose and adding Huang Lian to negate excessive heat.

---

### Er Chen Tang (二陈汤)
**Two-Cured/Two-Aged Decoction**

Foundational phlegm-resolving formula from the Song Dynasty *Taiping Huimin Heji Jufang*.

**Composition:**
| Herb | Role | Function |
|------|------|----------|
| Ban Xia (Pinellia) | Sovereign | Transforms dampness, harmonizes middle burner |
| Chen Pi (Tangerine peel) | Minister | Resolves phlegm, promotes qi flow |
| Fu Ling (Poria) | Assistant | Strengthens spleen, drains dampness |
| Zhi Gan Cao (Licorice) | Envoy | Harmonizes formula |

**Indications:** Phlegm-dampness with nausea, heaviness, stagnant qi, poor digestion

---

### Liu Jun Zi Tang (六君子汤)
**Six Gentlemen Decoction**

Builds on Si Jun Zi Tang by adding Er Chen Tang components.

**Composition:**
- Ren Shen (Ginseng) - tonifies spleen qi
- Bai Zhu (Atractylodes) - dries dampness
- Fu Ling (Poria) - drains dampness
- Gan Cao (Licorice) - harmonizes
- Chen Pi (Citrus peel) - regulates qi
- Ban Xia (Pinellia) - transforms phlegm

**Indications:**
- Spleen qi deficiency with phlegm
- Loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting
- Chronic bloating
- Functional dyspepsia
- IBS (diarrhea-predominant)
- Chronic gastritis

## Japanese Kampo Formulas

### Hangeshashinto (半夏瀉心湯)
**Japanese adaptation of Ban Xia Xie Xin Tang**

**Composition:** Seven crude drugs - Pinellia tuber, Scutellaria root, processed ginger, Glycyrrhiza, jujube, ginseng, Coptis rhizome

**Mechanism:**
- Pinellia clears fluid retention, stops vomiting
- Coptis and Scutellaria clear GI inflammation
- Ginseng and ginger improve GI blood flow
- Increases plasma levels of somatostatin, motilin, gastrin, CGRP, substance P

**Clinical Evidence:**
- RCT with 41 NSCLC patients on cisplatin/CPT-11: significantly improved diarrhea grade
- Significantly reduced severe diarrhea (grade 3-4) with irinotecan
- Demonstrated gastric mucosal protection in stress and ethanol injury models
- Used for chemotherapy/radiotherapy-induced mucositis

**Safety Note:** Formulas containing Scutellaria root (including Hangeshashinto) account for ~70% of Kampo-associated lung injury cases.

---

### Rikkunshito (六君子湯)
**Japanese Six Gentlemen Decoction**

Known as Liu-Jun-Zi Tang in Chinese tradition.

**Composition:**
Eight crude drugs:
- Atractylodis lanceae rhizoma
- Ginseng radix
- Pinellia tuber
- Hoelen (Poria)
- Zizyphi fructus (Jujube)
- Aurantii nobilis pericarpium (Citrus)
- Glycyrrhizae radix
- Zingiberis rhizome

**Mechanism:** Potentiates ghrelin signaling, improves gastric emptying and GI motility

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Multiple RCTs demonstrate efficacy for functional dyspepsia
- Significantly improved epigastric discomfort and extra-gastric symptoms (fatigue)
- Gastroparesis in Parkinson's patients: 67% improved GI symptoms, significantly shortened gastric emptying time
- Effective for GERD in children: increased esophageal clearance, decreased acid exposure, relieved nausea
- Useful for chemotherapy-induced nausea/anorexia in lung and esophageal cancer patients
- Effects more pronounced in patients with "kyosho" (low energy constitution)

**Combination Use:** No significant interactions with Western drugs. Shows ameliorative effects on adverse reactions from anticancer drugs and PPIs.

---

### Goreisan (五苓散)
**Five-Ingredient Powder with Poria**

Traditional hydrostatic modulator.

**Composition:**
- Alisma rhizome
- Poria
- Polyporus
- Atractylodes rhizome
- Cinnamon twig

**Traditional Uses:** Edema, diarrhea, headache, nausea, dizziness

**Clinical Evidence for PONV:**
- 2013 single-blind RCT: Suggested efficacy for reducing postoperative nausea/vomiting
- 2017 double-blind placebo-controlled trial (UMIN-000011801): Found negligible effect in high-risk gynecological surgery patients
- Conclusion: Single administration insufficient for PONV prevention in high-risk populations

## Ayurvedic Formulas

### Avipattikar Churna

Classical polyherbal formulation for hyperacidity (Amlapitta).

**Composition (14 ingredients):**
| Ingredient | Sanskrit | Parts |
|------------|----------|-------|
| Ginger | Shunthi | 1 |
| Black pepper | Maricha | 1 |
| Long pepper | Pippali | 1 |
| Chebulic myrobalan | Haritaki | 1 |
| Belleric myrobalan | Vibhitaka | 1 |
| Indian gooseberry | Amalaki | 1 |
| Nutgrass | Musta | 1 |
| Black salt | Vida Lavana | 1 |
| False black pepper | Vidanga | 1 |
| Cardamom | Ela | 1 |
| Indian bay leaf | Patra | 1 |
| Clove | Lavanga | 11 |
| Indian jalap | Trivrit | 44 |
| Sugar candy | Sharkara | 66 |

**Primary Actions:** Neutralizes gastric acid, corrects Pitta imbalance

**Indications:** Hyperacidity with nausea, constipation, burning sensation, gastritis, IBS, bloating

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Rat study demonstrated anti-secretory and anti-ulcerogenic effects comparable to ranitidine
- Significantly decreased gastric content volume and ulcer scores
- Increased pH in gastric contents

**Best For:** Pitta-dominant presentations with burning and constipation

### Hingwashtak Churna

Eight-ingredient digestive formula.

**Composition:**
| Ingredient | Sanskrit |
|------------|----------|
| Cumin | Jeeraka |
| Ginger | Shunthi |
| Asafoetida | Hing |
| Long pepper | Pippali |
| Black pepper | Maricha |
| Ajwain | Yavani |
| Fennel | Saunf |
| Rock salt | Saindhava |

**Primary Actions:** Deepana (kindles digestive fire), Pachana (digests toxins)

**Indications:** Low digestion strength, flatulence, bloating, indigestion, Vata-type digestive issues

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Aqueous extract reduced gastric ulcer size in studies
- 2019 pilot study: 60% reduction in subjective bloating scores after 2 weeks
- 2021 open-label study: Improved stool consistency and reduced abdominal pain in IBS patients over 28 days

**Best For:** Vata-dominant presentations with weak digestion, used pre-Panchakarma

Classical formulation for Pitta-related digestive issues.

**Actions:** Soothes stomach, manages nausea related to acid reflux

**Indications:** Pitta imbalance, acid reflux-associated nausea

### Sutshekhar Ras

Herbal-mineral preparation for gastric calming.

**Actions:** Calms digestive fire, controls vomiting

**Indications:** Gastritis-related nausea, vomiting

---

### Triphala

Three-fruit rejuvenative formula.

**Composition:**
| Fruit | Latin | Dosha Affinity |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| Haritaki | Terminalia chebula | Vata |
| Bibhitaki | Terminalia bellerica | Kapha |
| Amalaki | Phyllanthus emblica | Pitta |

**Actions:** Digestive support, mild laxative, carminative, antispasmodic

**Clinical Applications:**
- GI motility disturbances with cramping, nausea, bloating
- Functional GI disorders
- May cause temporary nausea during cleansing as toxins release

**Evidence:** Peer-reviewed research supporting applications in functional GI disorders (PMC6052535)

---

### Trikatu

"Three Pungents" - heating digestive formula.

**Composition (equal parts):**
- Shunthi (Dry ginger)
- Maricha (Black pepper)
- Pippali (Long pepper)

**Actions:** Stimulates Agni (digestive fire), reduces Kapha, improves nutrient assimilation

**Indications:** Sluggish digestion, nausea with cold signs, bloating, Kapha imbalance

**Note:** Often combined with Triphala for comprehensive digestive support

## Western Herbal Tradition

### Classic Digestive Bitters Formula

Traditional European approach using bitter herbs to stimulate digestive function.

**Core Bitter Herbs:**
| Herb | Properties | Traditional Use |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| Gentian root | Intensely bitter | Premier digestive stimulant |
| Dandelion root | Bitter, cooling | Liver support, gentle digestion |
| Artichoke leaf | Bitter, cooling | Slow digestion, liver support |
| Wormwood | Very bitter | Appetite, gastric secretion |

**Warming Carminatives (paired with bitters):**
| Herb | Properties |
|------|------------|
| Ginger | Warming, anti-nausea |
| Fennel | Carminative, antispasmodic |
| Cardamom | Aromatic, digestive |
| Chamomile | Calming bitter, antispasmodic |

**Mechanism:**
1. Bitter taste activates nerve signal to brain
2. Vagus nerve stimulates entire digestive system
3. Triggers digestive enzyme production
4. Increases bile production
5. Balances HCl levels

**Common Formula Combinations:**
- Dandelion + Burdock + Gentian + Fennel + Ginger
- Chamomile + Lemon balm + Ginger + Artichoke
- Gentian + Chamomile + Fennel + Cardamom

**Indications:** Heartburn, nausea, indigestion, gas, bloating, slow digestion

**Pregnancy-Safe Options:** Chamomile, mint, fennel, ginger (check specific formulations)

**Traditional Morning Sickness Blend:**
Most commonly cited herbs in traditional sources:
- Ginger (55%)

**Additional Traditional Herbs:**
- Wild yam - reduces hollow organ irritation, calms stomach spasms

## Cross-Traditional Comparisons

### Shared Principles

| Principle | TCM | Kampo | Ayurveda | Western |
|-----------|-----|-------|----------|---------|
| Primary anti-emetic | Ban Xia (Pinellia) | Pinellia tuber | Ginger | Ginger |
| Digestive warming | Gan Jiang, Sheng Jiang | Zingiberis | Trikatu | Ginger, warming spices |
| Phlegm/dampness clearing | Fu Ling, Chen Pi | Poria, Citrus | Trikatu | Bitter herbs |
| Qi/energy support | Ren Shen | Ginseng | Ashwagandha | Gentian |

### Ginger as Universal Anti-Emetic

Present across all traditions:
- **TCM:** Sheng Jiang (fresh), Gan Jiang (dried)
- **Kampo:** Zingiberis rhizome
- **Ayurveda:** Shunthi (dry), Ardrak (fresh)
- **Western:** Zingiber officinale

Modern evidence confirms anti-nausea efficacy with minimal side effects.

## Clinical Evidence Summary

### Strongest Evidence (RCTs available)
1. **Rikkunshito** - Functional dyspepsia, gastroparesis, chemotherapy-induced symptoms
2. **Hangeshashinto** - Chemotherapy-induced diarrhea and mucositis
3. **Ban Xia Xie Xin Tang** - Functional dyspepsia (meta-analysis)
4. **Ginger** - Nausea and vomiting across contexts (multiple RCTs)
5. **Avipattikar Churna** - Anti-ulcerogenic activity (animal studies)

### Moderate Evidence (Clinical observation, smaller studies)
- Liu Jun Zi Tang / Six Gentlemen variants
- Hingwashtak Churna
- Western digestive bitters

### Traditional Use Evidence
- Ban Xia Hou Po Tang
- Wu Zhu Yu Tang
- Er Chen Tang
- Triphala/Trikatu

---

## Safety Considerations

### General Cautions
- **Pregnancy:** Many formulas contraindicated, especially those containing Pinellia (Ban Xia)
- **Scutellaria-containing formulas:** Associated with rare lung injury cases
- **Drug interactions:** Consult practitioner when combining with pharmaceuticals
- **Bitter herbs:** May be contraindicated in certain gastric conditions

### Practitioner Guidance Recommended
All traditional formulas should ideally be prescribed by qualified practitioners who can:
- Assess individual constitution
- Identify root pattern/cause
- Adjust dosing appropriately
- Monitor for adverse effects
