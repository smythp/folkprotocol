# Comparative Effectiveness: Herbs vs Herbs, Herbs vs Drugs, Herbs vs Placebo

## Direct Herb vs. Herb Comparisons

### Ginger vs. Peppermint vs. Caraway (Latif 2025)
**The only true 3-way herbal comparison found in literature**

- **Study**: Latif 2025, RCT with 189 completers
- **Population**: Pulmonary TB patients on anti-TB treatment with GI disturbances
- **Duration**: 3 months intervention + 1 month washout
- **Interventions**:
  - Ginger: 500 mg twice daily (1 g/day)
  - Peppermint: 1.5 mL twice daily (3 mL/day)
  - Caraway: 1,000 mg twice daily (2 g/day)
  - Placebo: 500 mg roasted starch twice daily

**RESULTS - WINNER: GINGER**
1. **Ginger** (best overall)
   - No vomiting recurrence
   - Highest phenolic content (1035.51 mgGAE/g)
   - Superior antioxidant capacity
   - Significant reduction in postprandial distress during washout period

2. **Peppermint** (second place)
   - Good overall efficacy
   - Effective for Rome IV criteria symptoms

3. **Caraway** (third place)
   - Least effective of the three

**Limitations**:
- Specific population (TB patients)
- Different dose amounts complicate direct comparison
- Doses not standardized by weight/bioactivity

**Clinical Significance**: First true head-to-head herbal comparison; establishes ginger as most effective for overall GI disturbances

### Peppermint-Caraway: High Dose vs. Low Dose (Freise 1999)

- **High dose**: 90 mg peppermint + 50 mg caraway (enteric-coated)
- **Low dose**: 36 mg peppermint + 20 mg caraway (enteric-soluble)

**Results**:
- Pain intensity: Both equivalent (-3.6 vs -3.3 points, p<0.001)
- Pain frequency: High dose SUPERIOR (p=0.04)
- Tolerability: High dose BETTER despite higher dose (due to enteric coating)

**Conclusion**: Higher dose (90/50 mg) superior for pain frequency with better tolerability

## Herbs vs. Pharmaceutical Drugs

### Curcumin vs. Omeprazole (PPI) - LANDMARK FINDING
**Kongkam 2023: Three-arm RCT**

- **Study**: 151 completers, 28 days treatment + 28 days follow-up
- **Interventions**:
  - Curcumin: 250 mg four times daily (1,000 mg/day)
  - Omeprazole: 20 mg once daily
  - Combination: Both agents at above doses

**Results (SODA pain scores)**:
- Curcumin: -8.07
- Omeprazole: -8.85
- Combination: -7.19
- **NO significant differences among groups**
- No serious adverse events in any group

**Clinical Significance**:
- **EQUIVALENT EFFICACY**: Herb matched pharmaceutical
- Curcumin at 1,000 mg/day appears viable alternative to PPIs for functional dyspepsia
- No synergistic benefit when combined

### Peppermint-Caraway vs. Cisapride (Prokinetic Drug)
**Madisch 1999: Equivalence Trial**

- **Study**: 118 analyzed, 4 weeks, functional dyspepsia
- **Interventions**:
  - Herbal: 90 mg peppermint + 50 mg caraway, 2 capsules daily
  - Drug: Cisapride 10 mg three times daily

**Results**:
- Pain reduction: 4.62 points (herbal) vs 4.60 points (drug)
- **p=0.021 (statistically equivalent)**
- Adverse events: 12 (herbal) vs 14 (drug)

**Clinical Significance**: Therapeutic equivalence demonstrated; herbal combination viable alternative to pharmaceutical prokinetic

### Chinese Herbal Medicine vs. Prokinetic Agents
**Chu 2018: Systematic Review of Systematic Reviews**

- **Finding**: CHM MORE EFFECTIVE than prokinetic agents for alleviation of global dyspeptic symptoms
- **Quality**: Mediocre quality of included systematic reviews
- **Context**: Functional dyspepsia treatment

### STW-5 vs. Cisapride
**Part of Multiple STW-5 Reviews**

- **Finding**: No significant difference between STW 5 and cisapride in RCT
- **Context**: Functional dyspepsia
- **Safety**: STW-5 superior tolerability (0.04% adverse drug reaction rate)

### Ginger vs. Metoclopramide (Anti-emetic Drug)
**Multiple systematic reviews on PONV**

- **Finding**: Ginger EQUALLY EFFECTIVE as metoclopramide
- **Context**: Postoperative nausea and vomiting
- **Summary relative risks**: 0.69 for nausea, 0.61 for vomiting
- **Clinical use**: Safe botanical alternative to pharmaceutical anti-emetic

## Herbs vs. Placebo Meta-Analyses

### Multi-Herb Reviews: Overall Effect Sizes

**Tan 2020 - Herbal Medicine for FGIDs**
- 49 trials, 7,396 participants
- **Overall vs placebo**: RR = 1.67 (95% CI 1.48-1.88)
- By condition:
  - Functional Dyspepsia: RR = 1.50
  - IBS: RR = 1.62
  - **Functional Constipation: RR = 3.83** (strongest effect)
- Herbal vs conventional drugs: RR = 1.10 (non-inferior)

**Billings 2020 - CAM for IBS**
- 66 trials, 6,764 participants
- Herbal therapy for abdominal pain: SMD=0.47 (95% CI: 0.20-0.75)
- Herbal therapy overall response: **RR=1.57 (NNT=5)**
- Dietary supplements: RR=1.95 (NNT=4)

**Herbal FD Meta-Analysis 2021**
- 49 trials, 6,987 participants
- Herbal remedies vs placebo:
  - SMD = -0.58 [-0.66 to -0.51], p<0.00001
  - RR = 1.73 [1.62-1.85], p<0.00001
- No significant difference in adverse events vs placebo

### Peppermint Oil Specific

**Ingrosso 2022**
- 10 trials, 1,030 participants
- Global IBS symptoms: RR of not improving = 0.65 (NNT=4)
- Abdominal pain: RR of pain not improving = 0.76 (NNT=7)
- Adverse events: RR=1.57 (higher with peppermint but mild)

**Alammar 2019**
- 12 trials, 835 participants
- Global symptoms: RR=2.39 [95% CI: 1.93, 2.97], p<0.00001
- Abdominal pain: RR=1.78 [95% CI: 1.43, 2.20], p<0.00001
- Note: Enteric-coated peppermint oil specifically

### Chinese Herbal Medicine Specific

**CHM for IBS Meta-Analysis 2021**
- CHM associated with higher proportion of adequate relief of global IBS symptoms vs placebo
- Trial Sequential Analysis confirmed findings with sufficient information size
- Higher adverse event rate than placebo but no serious events

## Summary: Comparative Effectiveness Rankings

### For Functional Dyspepsia
1. **Curcumin 1,000 mg/day = Omeprazole 20 mg** (equivalent efficacy, landmark finding)
2. **Peppermint-caraway 90/50mg = Cisapride** (equivalent efficacy)
3. **STW-5 = Cisapride** (equivalent efficacy, superior tolerability)
4. **CHM > Prokinetic agents** (systematic review finding)
5. **Herbs generally > Placebo** (RR=1.50-1.73 across reviews)

### For IBS
1. **Peppermint oil** (strongest evidence, NNT=4 for global symptoms)
2. **STW-5** (1.5 point reduction vs placebo, p<0.0004)
3. **CHM formulas** (positive but variable quality, higher adverse events)
4. **Herbs generally > Placebo** (RR=1.62)

### For Acute Vomiting
1. **Ginger ≤1 g/day = Metoclopramide** (equal efficacy for PONV)
2. **Ginger** (60% reduction in acute CINV vomiting)
3. **Ginger > Peppermint > Caraway** (3-way head-to-head for GI disturbances)

### For Functional Constipation
1. **Herbs generally** (RR=3.83 vs placebo - strongest herbal effect seen)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Drug-equivalent efficacy demonstrated** for:
   - Curcumin (= omeprazole)
   - Peppermint-caraway (= cisapride)
   - STW-5 (= cisapride)
   - Ginger (= metoclopramide)

2. **Only one true herb vs. herb comparison exists** (Latif 2025): Ginger won

3. **Herbs generally superior to placebo** with moderate effect sizes (RR 1.5-3.8 depending on condition)

4. **Herbs non-inferior to conventional drugs** (RR=1.10) with often better tolerability

5. **Biggest research gap**: Need more head-to-head herbal comparisons at equivalent doses
