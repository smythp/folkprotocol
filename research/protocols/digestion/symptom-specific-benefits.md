# Symptom-Specific Benefits: Which Herb for Which Symptom

## Gas and Bloating (Carminatives)

### First-Line: Peppermint Oil
- **Mechanism**: Calcium channel blockade → smooth muscle relaxation
- **Evidence**: 75% reduction in bloating in IBS studies (Fifi 2018)
- **Form**: Enteric-coated to avoid GERD exacerbation
- **Contraindication**: GERD/hiatal hernia

### First-Line: Fennel
- **Mechanism**: Volatile oils expel gas, relax smooth muscles
- **Active compounds**: Anethole, fenchone, estragole
- **Pediatric evidence**: Eliminated infantile colic in 65% vs 23.7% placebo
- **Traditional use**: Chewed before/after meals in Indian culture
- **Clinical**: Accelerated recovery of GI function postoperatively

### First-Line: Ginger
- **Mechanism**: Digestive enzyme stimulation, enhances motility
- **Evidence**: Winner in 3-way comparison for GI disturbances (Latif 2025)
- **No vomiting recurrence** in comparative trial
- **Highest antioxidant capacity** of compared herbs

### Alternative: Caraway
- **Mechanism**: Carminative, gas expulsion
- **Best in combination**: Peppermint 90mg + Caraway 50mg = drug-equivalent for dyspepsia
- **Solo**: Less effective than ginger or peppermint (Latif 2025)

### Multi-Herb: STW-5 (Iberogast)
- **Contains**: Peppermint, caraway, melissa, chamomile + others
- **Evidence**: 96.84% efficacy in pediatric bloating/gas
- **Onset**: Improvements within 5 minutes

## Abdominal Pain and Cramping (Anti-spasmodics)

### First-Line: Peppermint Oil
- **Evidence**: NNT=7 for abdominal pain relief in IBS (Ingrosso 2022)
- **Mechanism**: Calcium channel blockade prevents muscle contraction
- **Effect size**: 40-75% symptom reduction in adults, 75% pain severity reduction in children
- **Studies**: Guinea pig taenia coli showed marked attenuation of contractile responses

### First-Line: Chamomile
- **Mechanisms**:
  - Calcium channel blockade (myorelaxant effects)
  - Potassium channel activation (causes smooth muscle relaxation)
  - Histamine/acetylcholine antagonism
- **Active compound**: Bisabolol (sesquiterpene) + flavonoids
- **Evidence**: In vitro studies on rabbit jejunum and guinea pig ileum
- **Safety**: All adverse events self-limiting, non-serious (2,896 patients)

### Multi-Herb: STW-5
- **Evidence**: P=0.0009 for pain in IBS; OR: 0.22 for functional dyspepsia pain
- **Multi-target**: Different extracts act on different GI regions

### Multi-Herb: Peppermint-Caraway Combination
- **Evidence**: 4.62 point pain reduction (equivalent to cisapride drug)
- **Optimal dose**: 90mg peppermint + 50mg caraway twice daily

## Nausea

### First-Line: Ginger (for vomiting more than nausea)
- **Evidence**:
  - CINV: Did NOT significantly decrease nausea (but effective for vomiting)
  - PONV: RR=0.69 for nausea (vs metoclopramide)
  - Pregnancy: Shows promise for pregnancy-related nausea
- **Note**: **Effects on nausea less clear than effects on vomiting**
- **Mechanism**: Anti-inflammatory, prokinetic, no gastric irritation

### Alternative: STW-5
- **Evidence**: Improvements within 5 minutes for nausea in pediatric studies
- **Rikkunshito (Kampo)**: Effective for nausea in functional dyspepsia

### Alternative: Chamomile
- **Mechanism**: Spasmolytic effect, reduces acid reflux episodes
- **Traditional use**: Digestive upset and nausea

## Acute Vomiting

### First-Line: Ginger
**STRONGEST evidence for vomiting specifically**

**Chemotherapy-Induced (CINV)**:
- 60% reduction in acute vomiting with ginger ≤1 g/day for >3 days
- Did NOT significantly decrease delayed vomiting
- Specifically effective for acute vomiting, not delayed

**Postoperative (PONV)**:
- Equal to metoclopramide (pharmaceutical standard)
- RR=0.61 for vomiting (superior to placebo)

**Pregnancy-Related**:
- Evidence shows promise for alleviating pregnancy-related vomiting

**Dosing**: ≤1 g/day for >3 days

**Note**: "Ginger is safe botanical option for managing vomiting; effects on nausea less clear" (2023 overview of systematic reviews)

## Functional Dyspepsia (Postprandial Distress, Early Satiation, Epigastric Pain/Burning)

### First-Line: Curcumin
- **Evidence**: Equivalent to omeprazole 20mg (landmark finding)
- **Dose**: 1,000 mg/day (250mg four times daily)
- **SODA score reduction**: -8.07 (vs -8.85 for omeprazole, not significant difference)

### First-Line: Peppermint-Caraway
- **Evidence**: Equivalent to cisapride (prokinetic drug)
- **Dose**: 90mg peppermint + 50mg caraway twice daily
- **Pain reduction**: 4.62 points over 4 weeks

### First-Line: STW-5 (Iberogast)
- **Evidence**: OR: 0.22 for most bothersome GI symptom (P=0.001)
- **Pediatric**: 96.84% efficacy, 97.99% tolerability
- **Onset**: 5 minutes to improvement, >90% maximum effect by 1 hour

### Second-Line: Artichoke Extract
- **Evidence**: Superior to placebo (p<0.01)
- **Dose**: 640mg twice daily
- **Quality of life**: Significant improvement
- **Mechanism**: Bile production enhancement

### Second-Line: Ginger + Artichoke Combination
- **Evidence**: Significant improvement by day 14 (Giacosa 2015)
- **Mechanism**: Synergistic - artichoke bile stimulation + ginger prokinetic effects
- **Gastric emptying**: Made significant difference in delayed emptying

### Second-Line: Rikkunshito (Kampo)
- **Evidence**: 4 RCTs showing relief of subjective symptoms
- **Mechanism**: Improves gastric motility, reduces gastric hypersensitivity, facilitates ghrelin secretion

## Poor Appetite / Weak Digestion (Bitters)

### First-Line: Gentian
- **Mechanism**: T2R (bitter receptor) activation → vagus → gastrin → HCl/pepsin
- **Classic study**: Moorhead 1915 - marked increase in appetite and gastric secretion in cachectic dogs
- **Pathway**: T2R activation → vagal nerve stimulation → gastrin release → HCl and pepsin secretion → enhanced saliva, bile flow

### First-Line: Artichoke
- **Mechanism**: Bile production enhancement, gastric emptying acceleration
- **Evidence**: RCT in Phytomedicine 2003
- **Active compounds**: Cynaropicrin, cynarin

### Alternative: Dandelion
- **Category**: Bitter
- **Mechanism**: T2R activation, digestive secretion stimulation
- **Traditional use**: Liver and digestive support

### Multi-Herb: Traditional Bitters Formulas
**Components**: Gentian + Angelica + Artichoke + Dandelion + Fennel + Ginger + Orange peel + Cinnamon
**Mechanism**: Stimulate bitter receptors → increase saliva, gastric juice, bile → digestive enzyme delivery

### Multi-Herb: Liu Jun Zi Tang / Rikkunshito (TCM/Kampo)
- **Indication**: Chronic digestive weakness, poor appetite, fatigue
- **Evidence**: Meta-analysis showing improved gastric emptying vs placebo
- **Mechanism**: Strengthens "spleen Qi", improves motility

## GERD / Acid Reflux

### First-Line: Chamomile
- **Mechanism**: Spasmolytic effect reduces acid reflux episodes, alleviates esophageal spasms, promotes gastric emptying
- **Evidence**: 2025 systematic review (11 trials, 2,896 patients)
- **Safety**: 65 adverse events across 10 trials - all self-limiting, non-serious (mostly GI complications, drowsiness)

### First-Line: DGL (Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice)
- **Evidence**: Significant improvement in GERD-HRQL scores (p=0.014)
- **Mechanism**: Mucosal protection, decreasing relapse
- **Limitation**: Clinical trial support weak overall; individual trial showed 78% ulcer reduction vs 34% placebo

### AVOID: Peppermint Oil
- **Contraindication**: Worsens GERD/hiatal hernia
- **Reason**: Lower esophageal sphincter relaxation

## Slow Gastric Emptying / Gastroparesis (Prokinetics)

### First-Line: Ginger
- **Evidence**: Accelerates gastric emptying in healthy volunteers (PMC3016669)
- **Mechanism**: Prokinetic - accelerates gastric emptying, stimulates antral contractions
- **Active compounds**: Gingerols, shogaols

### First-Line: Ginger + Artichoke Combination
- **Evidence**: 2015 Prodigest trial - made significant difference in delayed gastric emptying
- **Mechanism**: Artichoke bile stimulation + ginger prokinetic/enzyme stimulation

### First-Line: STW-5 (Iberogast)
- **Mechanism**: Multi-target prokinetic action
- **Evidence**: Meta-analyses showing gastric motility improvement

### Alternative: Rikkunshito (Kampo)
- **Mechanism**: Improves impaired adaptive relaxation and delayed gastric emptying
- **Evidence**: 4 RCTs in functional dyspepsia

### Alternative: Dai-kenchu-to (Kampo)
- **Indication**: Post-operative ileus, chronic constipation, impaired motility
- **Mechanism**: Stimulates GI motility through cholinergic and 5-HT3 receptors, produces membrane depolarization in interstitial cells of Cajal
- **Evidence**: Enhanced intestinal transit after abdominal surgeries

## Diarrhea

### First-Line: Hange-shashin-to (Kampo)
- **Evidence**: Prevented CPT-11-induced diarrhea in cancer patients
- **Mechanism**: Attenuates PGE2 increase, promotes colonic water absorption
- **Clinical**: Confirmed preventive effect in advanced non-small-cell lung cancer

### Alternative: Chinese Herbal Medicine formulas
- **Note**: Most TCM evidence for diarrhea-predominant IBS
- **Higher adverse events** than placebo but mild

## Constipation

### First-Line: Herbal medicines (general)
- **Evidence**: RR=3.83 vs placebo (STRONGEST herbal effect seen for any digestive condition)
- **Note**: Specific herbs not detailed in included reviews

### First-Line: Triphala (Ayurveda)
- **Evidence**: Improved stool frequency (p<0.05), yield (p<0.001), consistency
- **Mechanism**: Mild laxative facilitating colonic transit, restores epithelium lining
- **Active compounds**: Polyphenols, vitamin C, tannins, phenolic acids

### Alternative: Dai-kenchu-to (Kampo)
- **Indication**: Chronic constipation, adhesive bowel obstruction
- **Mechanism**: Stimulates upper GI motility, CGRP production
- **Evidence**: Clinical trials showing enhanced intestinal transit

## Inflammatory Digestive Conditions (IBD, Gastritis)

### First-Line: Ginger + Turmeric Combination
- **Mechanism**: Synergistic anti-inflammatory
  - Both inhibit prostaglandin pathways
  - Curcumin inhibits COX-2
  - Combination inhibits NF-κB, TLR4/TRAF6, JNK/c-JUN
  - Enhanced Nrf2/HO-1 expression (antioxidant/anti-inflammatory)
- **Evidence**: RAW 264.7 cell studies showing enhanced cytokine suppression
- **Synergy**: Ginger-turmeric combo > individual herbs

### First-Line: Turmeric/Curcumin
- **Evidence**:
  - Ulcerative colitis: 6 out of 7 studies positive; adjuvant curcumin produced significant beneficial effect on clinical remission
  - Crohn's: 2 out of 4 studies positive
  - Peptic ulcer: Both studies positive
- **Mechanism**: COX-2 inhibition, PGE2 suppression, NF-κB inhibition

### Second-Line: Ginger
- **Mechanism**: COX-2 inhibition, NF-κB suppression, reduces TNF, IL-6, GM-CSF, MCP-1
- **Evidence**: No irritant effect on gastric mucosa; selectively reduces inflammatory cytokines
- **Synergy**: Enhanced when combined with turmeric

## Stress-Related Digestive Issues (Gut-Brain Axis)

### First-Line: Xiao Yao San (TCM)
- **Indication**: Digestive disorders with liver stagnation and stress component, IBS with emotional factors
- **Evidence**: Improves IBS symptoms AND anxiety/depressive states
- **Mechanism**: Improves GI motility by increasing motilin and 5-HT levels, decreases somatostatin

### First-Line: Rikkunshito (Kampo)
- **Mechanism**: Exhibits anti-stress effects, attenuates stress-induced exacerbation of gastric sensation
- **Evidence**: Significantly improved extra-gastric symptoms like general fatigue

### Alternative: Hange-shashin-to (Kampo)
- **Evidence**: Significantly suppressed increases in plasma ACTH and cortisol levels under stress
