### 2. [Comparative Effectiveness](./comparative-effectiveness.md)
**Focus**: Head-to-head comparisons - herb vs herb, herb vs drug, herb vs placebo

**Landmark Findings**:
1. **Ginger > Peppermint > Caraway** (Latif 2025 - only 3-way herbal comparison)
2. **Curcumin = Omeprazole** (Kongkam 2023 - herb matched PPI drug)
3. **Peppermint-Caraway = Cisapride** (Madisch 1999 - herb matched prokinetic drug)
4. **Ginger = Metoclopramide** (PONV - herb matched anti-emetic drug)
5. **Chinese Herbal Medicine > Prokinetic agents** (Systematic review)

**Comparative Effect Sizes**:
- Herbs vs placebo for functional dyspepsia: RR = 1.50-1.73
- Herbs vs placebo for IBS: RR = 1.62
- Herbs vs placebo for constipation: RR = 3.83 (strongest herbal effect)
- Herbs vs conventional drugs: RR = 1.10 (non-inferior)

- Direct herb-to-herb trials (very rare in literature)
- Equivalence trials (herbs vs pharmaceutical drugs)

### 5. [Dosing Protocols](./dosing-protocols.md)
**Focus**: Acute vs chronic dosing - how much, how often, what form

**Acute Dosing** (As-Needed, Short-Term):
- Acute vomiting: Ginger ≤1 g/day for >3 days
- Acute bloating: Peppermint 180-200mg enteric-coated as needed
- Acute cramping: Peppermint 180-200mg or chamomile tea
- Acute indigestion: Bitters 15-30 min before meals OR STW-5

**Chronic Dosing** (Daily, Long-Term):
- IBS: Peppermint 180-200mg enteric-coated 2-3x daily ongoing
- Functional dyspepsia: Curcumin 250mg 4x daily OR peppermint-caraway 90/50mg 2x daily
- Poor digestion: Bitters before all meals, ongoing
- GERD: Chamomile 2-3x daily, DGL 3x daily
- Constipation: Triphala 3-6g daily before bed
- IBD: Ginger + turmeric combo with piperine, ongoing

**Formulation Considerations**:
- Why enteric-coating matters (peppermint)
- Higher dose paradox (90mg peppermint + coating = better tolerability than 36mg without)
- Standardization importance
- Tincture vs tea vs capsule (mechanism-dependent)

**Special Populations**:
- Pediatric dosing
- Elderly adjustments
- Pregnancy/lactation safety
- Drug-herb interactions

## Key Insights Summary

### Strongest Overall Evidence
1. **Peppermint oil** (IBS) - NNT=4, multiple meta-analyses
2. **STW-5** (dyspepsia/IBS) - fastest onset (5 min), excellent safety
3. **Ginger** (acute vomiting) - 60% reduction, equal to pharmaceutical anti-emetics

### Landmark Herb-Drug Equivalence
1. **Curcumin = Omeprazole** (functional dyspepsia)
2. **Peppermint-Caraway = Cisapride** (functional dyspepsia)
3. **Ginger = Metoclopramide** (PONV)

### Only Herb vs Herb Head-to-Head
**Latif 2025**: Ginger > Peppermint > Caraway for GI disturbances

### Strongest Herbal Effect (Any Condition)
**Constipation**: RR=3.83 vs placebo (strongest effect size in all digestive research)

### Fastest-Acting Herbal Remedy
**STW-5**: 5 minutes to onset, >90% effect by 1 hour

### Most Robust Synergistic Combination
**Ginger + Turmeric**: Documented synergistic anti-inflammatory effects in RAW 264.7 cells

## Research Quality Considerations

### GRADE Assessments
- Most herbal evidence: **Low to Very Low**
- Reasons: High heterogeneity, unclear risk of bias, lack of standardization

### Despite Low GRADE, Strong Clinical Signals For
- Peppermint oil (IBS)
- STW-5 (dyspepsia/IBS)
- Ginger (acute vomiting)
- Peppermint-caraway (dyspepsia)

### Major Research Gaps
1. **Very few herb vs herb comparisons** (Latif 2025 is rare exception)
2. **No TCM formula vs formula trials**
3. **Short follow-up** (most trials 4-8 weeks, need 6-12 months)
4. **Lack of standardization** in preparations
5. **Bioequivalent dosing needed** (standardize by active constituents)
6. **More pediatric research** required
