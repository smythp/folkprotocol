# Dosing Protocols: Acute vs. Chronic Digestion

## Acute Digestive Issues (As-Needed, Short-Term)

### Acute Nausea/Vomiting

**Ginger - First Choice**
- **Acute vomiting dose**: ≤1 g/day
- **Duration**: >3 days for optimal effect
- **Forms**:
  - Fresh ginger: 1-2g per dose
  - Ginger tea: 1-2 cups as needed
  - Ginger capsules: 250-500mg per dose
- **Timing**: At onset of nausea; can take up to 4x daily
- **Evidence**: 60% reduction in acute vomiting (CINV studies)

**Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea/Vomiting (CINV)**:
- **Optimal**: Ginger ≤1 g/day for >3 days
- **Start**: Before chemotherapy
- **Continue**: Through acute phase (first 24-48 hours)
- **Target**: Acute vomiting specifically (not delayed vomiting)

**Postoperative Nausea/Vomiting (PONV)**:
- **Dose**: 1g ginger 1 hour before surgery
- **Efficacy**: Equal to metoclopramide
- **Safety**: No interaction with anesthesia documented

**Pregnancy Nausea/Vomiting**:
- **Dose**: 250mg ginger 4x daily OR 1g divided doses
- **Safety**: Evidence shows promise; consult provider
- **Duration**: As needed during symptomatic period

---

### Acute Bloating/Gas

**Peppermint Oil - First Choice**
- **Dose**: 0.2-0.4 mL (180-200mg) enteric-coated
- **Timing**: 1-3 times daily, between meals
- **Form**: MUST be enteric-coated to avoid heartburn
- **Duration**: As needed for acute episodes
- **Onset**: 1-3 hours for symptom relief
- **Contraindication**: GERD/hiatal hernia

**Fennel - Alternative**
- **Dose**: 5-7g crushed seeds as tea, or 0.1-0.6 mL essential oil
- **Timing**: After meals or as needed
- **Form**: Tea, capsules, or oil in water
- **Pediatric**: Fennel oil emulsion for infantile colic (consult pediatrician for dosing)

**Ginger - Alternative**
- **Dose**: 1-2g fresh or 250-500mg dried
- **Timing**: With or between meals
- **Form**: Tea, capsules, fresh grated

**STW-5 (Iberogast) - Multi-Symptom**
- **Dose**: 20 drops (1 mL) three times daily
- **Timing**: Before or with meals
- **Onset**: 5 minutes to improvement, >90% effect by 1 hour
- **Evidence**: 96.84% efficacy in pediatric bloating

---

### Acute Indigestion (Post-Meal Discomfort)

**Peppermint-Caraway Combination**
- **Dose**: 90mg peppermint + 50mg caraway
- **Timing**: Twice daily (or as needed after heavy meals)
- **Form**: Enteric-coated capsules
- **Duration**: Single dose or 2-3 days

**Bitters Formula**
- **Dose**: 1-2 mL (20-40 drops) tincture
- **Timing**: 15-30 minutes BEFORE meals (traditional aperitif use)
- **Form**: Alcohol tincture or glycerite
- **Purpose**: Stimulate digestive secretions preventatively

**Digestive Enzyme Herbs (Ginger-based)**
- **Dose**: 1g ginger before meals
- **Timing**: 15-30 minutes before eating
- **Purpose**: Stimulate enzyme production

---

### Acute Abdominal Cramping/Spasm

**Peppermint Oil**
- **Dose**: 0.2-0.4 mL enteric-coated
- **Timing**: At onset of cramping
- **Can repeat**: Every 4-6 hours as needed
- **Maximum**: 3-4 doses per day

**Chamomile Tea**
- **Dose**: 2-4g dried flowers (1-2 cups tea)
- **Timing**: At onset, can repeat every 2-3 hours
- **Form**: Tea infusion (steep 10-15 minutes)
- **Additional benefit**: Calming effect for stress-related cramping

## Chronic Digestive Issues (Daily, Long-Term)

### Functional Dyspepsia (Chronic)

**Curcumin - Drug-Equivalent Option**
- **Dose**: 250mg four times daily (total 1,000 mg/day)
- **Timing**: With meals to aid absorption
- **Duration**: 28 days minimum (based on Kongkam 2023 trial)
- **Evidence**: Equivalent to omeprazole 20mg
- **Enhance absorption**: Take with black pepper (piperine) or fats
- **Form**: Standardized curcumin extract

**Peppermint-Caraway Combination**
- **Dose**: 90mg peppermint + 50mg caraway, twice daily
- **Timing**: Morning and evening, between meals
- **Form**: Enteric-coated capsules
- **Duration**: 4-8 weeks typical trial period
- **Evidence**: Equivalent to cisapride; 4.62 point pain reduction in 4 weeks

**STW-5 (Iberogast)**
- **Dose**: 20 drops (1 mL) three times daily
- **Timing**: Before or with meals
- **Duration**: Long-term use appears safe (0.04% adverse reaction rate)
- **Evidence**: OR: 0.22 for symptom improvement (P=0.001)

**Artichoke Extract**
- **Dose**: 320mg twice daily (total 640mg/day)
- **Timing**: With meals
- **Duration**: 6 weeks minimum (Holtmann 2003 trial duration)
- **Evidence**: Significant improvement in symptoms and QoL (p<0.01)

**Rikkunshito (Kampo)**
- **Dose**: 7.5g daily (typically 2.5g three times daily)
- **Timing**: Before or between meals
- **Duration**: 4-8 weeks minimum
- **Evidence**: 4 RCTs showing symptom relief
- **Special benefit**: Also improves anorexia and fatigue

---

### Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

**Peppermint Oil - First-Line for IBS**
- **Dose**: 0.2-0.4 mL (180-200mg) enteric-coated, 2-3 times daily
- **Timing**: Between meals (30-60 minutes before eating)
- **Duration**: 2-8 weeks minimum; can continue long-term
- **Evidence**: NNT=4 for global symptoms, NNT=7 for abdominal pain
- **Form**: MUST be enteric-coated
- **Adverse events**: RR=1.57 vs placebo (mild, heartburn if not enteric-coated)

**STW-5 (Iberogast)**
- **Dose**: 20 drops (1 mL) three times daily
- **Duration**: Ongoing; safe for long-term use
- **Evidence**: 1.5 point IBS symptom score reduction (p<0.0004)

**Chinese Herbal Medicine Formulas**
- **Dose**: Varies by formula (typically 2-3 servings daily)
- **Duration**: 4-8 weeks minimum
- **Evidence**: RR=1.62 vs placebo for IBS
- **Note**: Higher adverse events than placebo but mild; best evidence for diarrhea-predominant IBS

**Xiao Yao San (for IBS with stress/emotional component)**
- **Dose**: 6-12g daily (typically 2-4g three times daily)
- **Timing**: Before or between meals
- **Duration**: 8-12 weeks for full effect
- **Evidence**: Improves IBS symptoms AND anxiety/depressive states
- **Mechanism**: Modulates gut-brain axis

---

### Poor Digestion / Weak Digestive Fire

**Bitters Formula - Daily Tonic**
- **Dose**: 1-2 mL (20-40 drops) tincture
- **Timing**: 15-30 minutes BEFORE each meal (critical timing)
- **Duration**: Ongoing; safe long-term
- **Mechanism**: Stimulates digestive secretions via bitter receptors
- **Traditional use**: Aperitif before meals

**Gentian Root**
- **Dose**: 1-2g dried root as tea, or 1-3 mL tincture
- **Timing**: 15-30 minutes before meals
- **Evidence**: Classic 1915 Moorhead study; historical validation
- **Mechanism**: T2R activation → vagus → gastrin → HCl/pepsin

**Trikatu (Ayurvedic "Digestive Fire")**
- **Dose**: 1-2g (¼ to ½ teaspoon) per day
- **Timing**: Before meals
- **Form**: Powder mixed with honey, ghee, or warm water
- **Duration**: Ongoing for chronic weak digestion
- **Evidence**: Himalaya studies for indigestion, bloating, belching
- **Benefit**: Also enhances bioavailability of other herbs

**Liu Jun Zi Tang / Rikkunshito (Digestive Tonic)**
- **Dose**: 7.5g daily (2.5g three times daily)
- **Duration**: Weeks to months for tonic effect
- **Evidence**: Meta-analysis showing improved gastric emptying
- **Indication**: Chronic digestive weakness with fatigue, poor appetite

---

### Gastroparesis / Delayed Gastric Emptying

**Ginger + Artichoke Combination**
- **Dose**:
  - Ginger: 1-2g per day (divided doses)
  - Artichoke: 320-640mg per day
- **Timing**: Before meals
- **Duration**: 4 weeks minimum (Giacosa 2015 trial: improvement by day 14)
- **Evidence**: 2015 Prodigest trial validated gastric emptying improvement

**Ginger Alone**
- **Dose**: 1-3g daily, divided doses
- **Timing**: 30-60 minutes before meals
- **Evidence**: Accelerates gastric emptying in healthy volunteers

**Rikkunshito (Kampo)**
- **Dose**: 7.5g daily (2.5g three times daily)
- **Duration**: 4-8 weeks
- **Evidence**: Improves impaired adaptive relaxation and delayed emptying
- **Mechanism**: Facilitates ghrelin secretion

---

### GERD / Chronic Acid Reflux

**Chamomile**
- **Dose**: 2-4g dried flowers as tea, 2-3 times daily
- **Timing**: Between meals and before bed
- **Duration**: Ongoing
- **Evidence**: 2025 systematic review - reduces acid reflux episodes
- **Safety**: All adverse events self-limiting (2,896 patients)

**DGL (Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice)**
- **Dose**: 760mg three times daily (based on trial showing 78% ulcer reduction)
- **Timing**: 20 minutes before meals and at bedtime
- **Form**: Chewable tablets (allows coating of esophagus/stomach)
- **Duration**: 8-16 weeks
- **Note**: Evidence mixed; may help mucosal protection and prevent relapse

**AVOID**:
- Peppermint oil (worsens GERD by relaxing lower esophageal sphincter)

---

### Chronic Constipation

**Triphala (Ayurvedic)**
- **Dose**: 3-6g daily (typically 1-2 teaspoons powder or 3-6 capsules)
- **Timing**: Before bed or upon waking on empty stomach
- **Duration**: Safe for long-term use as gentle tonic laxative
- **Evidence**: Improved stool frequency (p<0.05), yield (p<0.001), consistency
- **Additional benefits**: Restores epithelial lining, microbiome modulation

**Herbal Laxative Formulas (General)**
- **Evidence**: RR=3.83 vs placebo (strongest herbal effect for any digestive condition)
- **Note**: Use gentler formulas (like Triphala) for long-term; avoid senna/cascara long-term

**Dai-kenchu-to (Kampo) - For Severe Cases**
- **Dose**: Per practitioner guidance (typically 15g daily in divided doses)
- **Indication**: Chronic constipation, post-operative ileus, impaired motility
- **Evidence**: Clinical trials showing enhanced intestinal transit
- **Note**: Stronger prokinetic; for more severe cases

---

### Inflammatory Digestive Conditions (IBD, Gastritis)

**Ginger + Turmeric Combination - Synergistic Anti-Inflammatory**
- **Curcumin dose**: 500-1,000mg per day
- **Ginger dose**: 1-3g per day
- **Enhancement**: MUST include black pepper (5-10mg piperine) or take with fats
- **Timing**: With meals
- **Duration**: 8-12 weeks minimum; ongoing for chronic conditions
- **Evidence**:
  - Curcumin: 6/7 ulcerative colitis studies positive
  - Ginger + Turmeric: Synergistic cytokine suppression (RAW 264.7 cells)

**Turmeric/Curcumin Alone**
- **Dose**: 1,000-1,500mg curcumin per day
- **Timing**: Divided doses with meals
- **Bioavailability**: Critical to use with piperine or lipid formulation
- **Evidence**: Adjuvant curcumin produced significant beneficial effect on clinical remission in UC

## Dosing Adjustments

### Pediatric Considerations
- **Peppermint oil**: Evidence in children (75% pain reduction) - use pediatric formulations
- **STW-5**: 96.84% efficacy in 1,042 children - safe and effective
- **Fennel oil**: 65% eliminated infantile colic (use pediatric formulations)
- **General**: Most pediatric research lacking; consult pediatrician for dosing

### Elderly Considerations
- Start at lower end of dose range
- Monitor for drug-herb interactions (especially with PPIs, anticoagulants)
- Bitters may be particularly appropriate (age-related decrease in digestive secretions)

### Pregnancy/Lactation
- **Ginger**: Evidence shows promise for pregnancy nausea/vomiting; doses ≤1g/day
- **Most herbs**: Insufficient safety data; consult provider
- **AVOID high doses** of herbs during pregnancy

### With Medications
- **Curcumin + PPIs**: No interaction; actually equivalent efficacy
- **Peppermint + Antacids**: May dissolve enteric coating too early; separate by 2 hours
- **Turmeric + Anticoagulants**: May enhance anticoagulant effects; monitor
- **Bitters + Diabetes medications**: May enhance glucose-lowering; monitor

## Formulation Considerations

### Why Enteric-Coating Matters
**Peppermint oil**:
- Non-enteric: Releases in stomach → heartburn, GERD exacerbation
- Enteric-coated: Releases in small intestine → optimal effect, better tolerability
- **Critical**: Always use enteric-coated for IBS/dyspepsia

**Higher dose paradox**: 90mg peppermint (high) + enteric coating = BETTER tolerability than 36mg peppermint (low) without proper coating

### Standardization Importance
- **Curcumin**: Use standardized extract (95% curcuminoids) with bioavailability enhancer
- **Peppermint oil**: Standardized to 0.2-0.4 mL essential oil content
- **Artichoke**: Standardized to caffeoylquinic acids
- **Ginger**: Standardized to gingerols/shogaols content

### Tincture vs. Tea vs. Capsule
**Bitters**: Tincture > tea > capsule (must taste bitter to activate oral T2R receptors)
**Peppermint**: Capsule (enteric-coated) > tea (for IBS/dyspepsia)
**Chamomile**: Tea ≥ capsule (traditional preparation, added relaxation from ritual)
**Ginger**: Fresh = tea ≥ capsule (all effective)
**Curcumin**: Capsule with bioavailability enhancement (poor absorption otherwise)
