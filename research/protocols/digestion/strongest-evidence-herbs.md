# Strongest Evidence Herbs for Digestion

## Tier 1: Strongest Evidence (Multiple Systematic Reviews, RCTs)

### Peppermint Oil
- **Conditions**: IBS (especially pain and bloating), functional dyspepsia
- **Evidence Quality**: Strong - multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- **Key Studies**:
  - Ingrosso 2022: NNT=4 for global symptoms, NNT=7 for abdominal pain
  - Alammar 2019: RR=2.39 for global symptoms, RR=1.78 for abdominal pain
  - Fifi 2018: 75% reduction in pain severity in children, 40-75% symptom reduction in adults
- **Dosing**: Enteric-coated peppermint oil (see dosing document for specifics)
- **Contraindication**: Avoid in GERD/hiatal hernia
- **Adverse Events**: RR=1.57 vs placebo (more frequent but mild)

### STW-5 (Iberogast)
- **Conditions**: Functional dyspepsia, IBS
- **Evidence Quality**: Strong - well-established efficacy, equivalence to conventional drugs
- **Key Studies**:
  - 6 RCTs, 3 placebo-controlled in meta-analyses
  - Functional dyspepsia: P=0.001, OR: 0.22
  - IBS: 1.5 point reduction vs placebo (p < 0.0004)
  - Pediatric: 96.84% efficacy, 97.99% tolerability in 1,042 children
  - Onset: Improvements within 5 minutes, >90% maximum effect by 1 hour
  - Equivalent to cisapride (prokinetic drug)
- **Safety**: Adverse drug reaction incidence: 0.04%, no serious adverse events
- **Components**: Bitter candytuft, angelica, milk thistle, celandine, caraway, licorice, peppermint, melissa, chamomile

### Ginger
- **Conditions**: Acute vomiting (chemotherapy, postoperative, pregnancy)
- **Evidence Quality**: Strong - consistent findings across multiple reviews
- **Key Studies**:
  - CINV: 60% reduction in acute vomiting with ginger ≤1 g/day for >3 days
  - PONV: Equal to metoclopramide (RR: 0.69 for nausea, 0.61 for vomiting)
  - Pregnancy: Evidence shows promise for pregnancy-related nausea/vomiting
  - Latif 2025: Winner in 3-way head-to-head vs peppermint vs caraway for GI disturbances
- **Note**: Stronger evidence for vomiting than nausea specifically
- **Safety**: No irritant effect on gastric mucosa

## Tier 2: Moderate to Strong Evidence

### Peppermint-Caraway Combination
- **Conditions**: Functional dyspepsia
- **Evidence Quality**: Drug-equivalent efficacy
- **Key Study**: Madisch 1999 - equivalent to cisapride (pharmaceutical prokinetic)
- **Optimal Dose**: 90 mg peppermint + 50 mg caraway, twice daily
- **Results**: 4.62 point pain reduction (herbal) vs 4.60 points (drug), p=0.021 (equivalent)

### Curcumin (from Turmeric)
- **Conditions**: Functional dyspepsia, IBD
- **Evidence Quality**: Moderate - LANDMARK finding of drug equivalence
- **Key Study**: Kongkam 2023 - curcumin 1,000 mg/day EQUIVALENT to omeprazole 20 mg
- **Dosing**: 250 mg four times daily (total 1,000 mg/day)
- **Systematic Review 2024**:
  - IBS: 4 out of 7 studies showed benefit
  - Ulcerative colitis: 6 out of 7 studies positive
  - Peptic ulcer: Both studies positive
- **Limitation**: Most studies have high risk of bias, low quality of evidence

### Chinese Herbal Medicine (General)
- **Conditions**: Functional dyspepsia, IBS
- **Evidence Quality**: Moderate - positive meta-analyses but variable quality
- **Key Findings**:
  - Tan 2020: RR=1.67 vs placebo overall; RR=3.83 for functional constipation
  - More effective than prokinetic agents for dyspeptic symptoms
  - Trial Sequential Analysis confirmed findings
- **Limitation**: Higher adverse event rate than placebo (though mild)

## Tier 3: Emerging/Promising Evidence

### Artichoke Extract
- **Conditions**: Functional dyspepsia, delayed gastric emptying
- **Evidence Quality**: Promising - RCT evidence
- **Key Studies**:
  - Holtmann 2003: 640 mg twice daily superior to placebo (p<0.01)
  - Giacosa 2015: Artichoke + ginger significant improvement by day 14
- **Mechanisms**: Bile production enhancement, prokinetic effects

### Chamomile
- **Conditions**: GERD, digestive spasms
- **Evidence Quality**: Emerging - 2025 systematic review
- **Key Findings**: Spasmolytic effect reduces acid reflux episodes
- **Safety**: All adverse events self-limiting and non-serious (2,896 patients in 10 trials)

### DGL (Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice)
- **Conditions**: Peptic ulcer, GERD
- **Evidence Quality**: Mixed/Weak
- **Systematic Review 2024**:
  - Pooled OR not significant for pain or complete healing
  - Individual trial: 78% ulcer reduction vs 34% placebo with 760mg 3x/day
- **Conclusion**: May have role in mucosal protection and preventing relapse, but clinical support weak

## Evidence Quality Considerations

### Overall GRADE Assessments
Most herbal medicine evidence rates as **Low to Very Low** in formal GRADE assessments due to:
- High heterogeneity between studies
- Unclear risk of bias in many trials
- Lack of standardization in preparations/dosages
- Small sample sizes in many trials

### Despite Low GRADE, Clinical Signals Strong For:
1. **Peppermint oil** - most consistent findings across reviews
2. **STW-5** - well-established multi-herb formula
3. **Ginger** - particularly for vomiting (not nausea)
4. **Peppermint-caraway** - drug-equivalent efficacy demonstrated

### Research Gaps
- Very few true herb vs. herb head-to-head comparisons (Latif 2025 is rare exception)
- TCM formulas never compared against each other in RCTs
- Need for longer follow-up (most trials 4-8 weeks)
- Better adverse event monitoring needed
- More pediatric research required
