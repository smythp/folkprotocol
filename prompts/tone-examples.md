# Tone Examples - What to Avoid

## Blog-Speak Examples (From Real Output)

### ❌ BAD - Chatty/Blog Voice
"Can't make fresh cold infusions while traveling? Try these alternatives..."
"Herbs work, but they're not magic."
"What is it? A tickly cough—that maddening dry irritation that won't quit—is..."
"Bottom line: Marshmallow + honey + elevation will help most acute tickly coughs."

### ✅ GOOD - Clean, Direct
"Travel preparations: Pre-made syrup in 2oz bottles, marshmallow capsules, small honey jar."
"Herbs provide symptom relief, not cure of underlying conditions."
"A tickly cough is a dry, persistent throat irritation without mucus production."
"Primary interventions: marshmallow root, honey, sleep elevation, humidification."

## Medical Advice (From Real Output)

### ❌ BAD - Playing Doctor
"When to Seek Professional Help:
- Cough lasting >8 weeks
- Fever >101°F
- Coughing up colored mucus
- Chest pain or tightness"

"If you have asthma, COPD, heart disease..."
"Ask doctor for referral to speech-language pathologist"

### ✅ GOOD - Staying in Lane
"Timeline: Post-viral cough typically resolves in 3-8 weeks [1]"
"Contraindications: Licorice not recommended for those with hypertension [2]"

---

## Condescending Explanations

### ❌ BAD - Over-Explaining
"DO: Be patient—chronic cough is exhausting and frustrating
DON'T: Say 'just stop coughing' (it's involuntary, unhelpful)"

"This is NOT 'just stop coughing.' It's retraining a hypersensitive reflex through specific techniques."

"REALITY CHECK: Sometimes it just takes time. Support ≠ fixing."

### ✅ GOOD - Respectful
"Chronic cough often requires 8 weeks for resolution."
"Behavioral techniques can help retrain cough reflex [3]"

---

## Vague Traditional Claims

### ❌ BAD - Unsourced
"Used for centuries in traditional medicine"
"Ancient cultures have long known..."
"Traditionally used for cough"

### ✅ GOOD - Specific Attribution
"Gerard's Herbal (1597) recommends marshmallow for 'the chin-cough' [4]"
"The German Commission E approved marshmallow for irritation of oral mucosa (1989) [5]"
"Dioscorides prescribed althaea (marshmallow) for cough in De Materia Medica (70 CE) [6]"

---

## Buried Critical Information

### ❌ BAD - Preparations Hidden in Text
"The cold infusion is made by steeping 2-4 tablespoons of dried marshmallow root in a quart jar filled with cold water overnight. This extracts the mucilaginous polysaccharides better than hot water, which would extract starches..."

### ✅ GOOD - In Preparations Section
```yaml
preparations:
  - method: cold infusion
    dosing: "2-4 tbsp root per quart water"
    notes: "Steep 4-12 hours. Cold extraction preserves mucilage."
    taste: "Slightly sweet, slippery texture"
```

---

## Effect Sizes Without Context

### ❌ BAD - Numbers Without Meaning
"Cortisol reduced by 27.9%"
"Significant improvement (p<0.001)"
"Effect size of 1.55"

### ✅ GOOD - Numbers With Context
"In a trial of 64 stressed adults, cortisol decreased by 27.9% after 60 days of 600mg/day [7]"
"Sleep quality improved in 72% of treatment group vs 29% placebo (n=150) [8]"

---

## Kitchen Sink Syndrome

### ❌ BAD - Everything Including Partner Tips
"For Partners: Support Tips
- Keep honey, cold infusion, lozenges stocked
- Remind about elevation/humidifier
- Be patient..."

"Behavioral Cough Suppression: The Hidden Game-Changer
What is it? Speech therapy techniques..."

### ✅ GOOD - Focused on Herbs
Primary content: herbs, preparations, evidence
Skip: relationship advice, medical procedures, lifestyle coaching

---

## Fake Urgency

### ❌ BAD - NOW! ACT FAST!
"You or your partner has that annoying tickle starting. Do this NOW!"
"CRITICAL: Elevate bed head 6-8 inches"

### ✅ GOOD - Calm Guidance
"First interventions: marshmallow infusion, honey, bed elevation"
"Bed elevation of 6-8 inches reduces nocturnal cough [9]"
