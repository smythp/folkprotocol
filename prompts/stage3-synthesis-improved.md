<tone_guidelines>
## TONE: Accessible Wikipedia-Level (8th Grade Reading)

Write for an educated lay reader who has no medical training. Target: Wikipedia medical articles, NOT PubMed abstracts.

**Core rule:** Keep all citations, numbers, and scientific accuracy. Simplify the LANGUAGE, not the FACTS.

### Statistical Translations (Required)

**Study descriptions:**
❌ "A Cochrane review of 6 RCTs (n=899)"
✅ "A Cochrane review of 6 clinical trials with 899 participants"

**Effect sizes - translate to meaning:**
❌ "MD -1.05, 95% CI -1.48 to -0.62"
✅ "reduced by about one episode per day"

❌ "SMD -0.36, 95% CI -0.50 to -0.21"
✅ "small but consistent benefit" OR "measurable improvement across all studies"

**Consistency measures:**
❌ "I²=0% indicating minimal inter-study variance"
✅ "very consistent results across studies" OR "all studies showed similar benefits"

**Keep the numbers, explain what they mean:**
✅ "reduced cough frequency by about one episode per day (MD -1.05) [1]" - if precision matters
✅ "reduced cough frequency by about one episode per day [1]" - usually sufficient

### Medical Terminology Simplifications (Required)

**Mechanisms:**
❌ "Demulcent herbs containing polysaccharides form protective coatings over mucous membranes"
✅ "Demulcent herbs contain natural compounds (polysaccharides) that create a protective coating over the throat lining"

❌ "Mineralocorticoid-like effects causing hypokalemia, hypertension, cardiac arrhythmia, and myopathy"
✅ "affects hormones that control blood pressure and electrolytes, potentially causing low potassium, high blood pressure, irregular heart rhythm, and muscle problems"

**Anatomy:**
❌ "laryngopharyngeal and tracheobronchial stimulation"
✅ "throat and lung irritation"

❌ "mucous membranes"
✅ "throat lining" OR "throat and airways"

**Safety warnings:**
❌ "Contraindicated in hypertension, heart failure, pulmonary hypertension"
✅ "Not recommended for people with high blood pressure or heart conditions"

### Common Translations Table

| Academic | Accessible |
|----------|-----------|
| RCT | clinical trial, study |
| n=899 | 899 participants (first use), can use (n=899) after |
| Preclinical studies | animal studies, laboratory studies |
| Mineralocorticoid-like effects | affects hormones that control blood pressure |
| Laryngopharyngeal | throat |
| Tracheobronchial | lung, airway |
| Mucous membranes | throat lining, throat and airways |
| Contraindicated in | not recommended for people with |

| Hypertension | high blood pressure |

### Inline Definitions (Required for First Use)

Define technical terms inline using parentheses or brief clauses:

✅ "glycyrrhizin (licorice's active compound)"
✅ "demulcent herbs, which create protective coatings,"
✅ "polysaccharides (natural plant compounds)"
✅ "The European Medicines Agency (EMA)"

After first definition, can use the technical term alone.

### What to ALWAYS Keep

1. ✓ All citation numbers [n]
2. ✓ All specific numbers: 899 participants, 6 studies, 30-78% reduction
3. ✓ All dosages: 1.5g, 100mg/day, 2-4ml
4. ✓ Study sizes: can write "6 clinical trials (n=899)"
5. ✓ Percentages and effect magnitudes
6. ✓ Safety warnings (just make accessible)
7. ✓ Scientific accuracy

### What to Often Omit or Simplify

1. ✗ Statistical notation (CI ranges, p-values) unless central
2. ✗ Complex chemical names unless commonly known
3. ✗ Latin anatomical terms - use common names
4. ✗ Unexplained abbreviations - spell out first use
5. ✗ I² heterogeneity stats - translate to "consistent/varied"

### The Test: "Educated Dinner Guest"

Would you say this sentence to an educated friend over dinner who isn't a medical professional?
- If YES → Good accessible tone
- If NO → Needs simplification

### Examples of Good Balance

✅ "A Cochrane review of 6 clinical trials with 899 participants found honey reduced cough frequency by about one episode per day compared to no treatment [1]"
- Has numbers (6, 899), citation [1], plain language, meaningful translation

✅ "Animal studies found several compounds in licorice reduced cough frequency by 30-78% [9]"
- Simplified "preclinical", kept percentage range, has citation

✅ "Never give honey to infants under 12 months old due to botulism risk from bacterial spores [3]"
- Clear safety warning, specific age, simplified but accurate

### Red Flags - Fix These

🚩 "RCT (n=899)" → change to "clinical trial with 899 participants"
🚩 "MD -1.05, 95% CI..." without translation → add plain language meaning
🚩 Undefined medical jargon → define inline on first use
🚩 Latin anatomy without common name → use throat/lung/airways
🚩 Acronyms without explanation → spell out first use

## Citation Format Rules

**Correct formats:**
✅ "Marshmallow reduces cough frequency by 55% [2]"
✅ "A trial of 62 adults found significant improvement [2]"
✅ "Traditional dose is 1-2 tsp per cup [3]"
✅ "Gerard's Herbal (1597) recommends for 'chin-cough' [4]"

**Incorrect formats:**
❌ "Marshmallow reduces cough frequency." (no citation)
❌ "Studies show marshmallow works [2]" (too vague - what specifically?)
❌ "[2] shows marshmallow is effective" (citation at beginning)
❌ "Research suggests..." (which research? be specific)

### What the Evidence Says

#### Example GOOD Paragraph:
Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) root contains 5-10% mucilage polysaccharides that form a protective coating over irritated throat tissues [7]. A randomized controlled trial with 62 adults experiencing dry cough found a 55% reduction in cough frequency within 10 minutes of taking marshmallow root extract, compared to 25% in the placebo group [2]. The effect persisted for 4 hours post-administration [2]. A systematic review of 5 studies (n=313 total) found consistent improvement in throat irritation scores, with marshmallow performing comparably to dextromethorphan [4].

### Traditional Context
[Every claim needs attribution]

Marshmallow appears in Dioscorides' De Materia Medica (70 CE) for throat ailments [10]. Gerard's Herbal (1597) specifically recommends it for "chin-cough" in children, using a syrup preparation [9]. The German Commission E approved marshmallow leaf and root for irritation of oral and pharyngeal mucosa in 1989 [13].

### How to Try It

The traditional cold infusion maximizes mucilage extraction: 1-2 teaspoons dried root in one cup room-temperature water, steeped 4-8 hours [3]. Strain and drink up to 3 cups daily. Effects typically occur within 10 minutes for acute relief [2].
